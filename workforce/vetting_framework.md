# Operator Vetting Framework

> Minimum standards for security operators listed on the platform. This document defines what "vetted" means.

## Purpose

The platform connects clients with security operators. "Vetted" is the core trust signal. If vetting is weak, the brand is worthless. If a poorly vetted operator harms a client, the legal and reputational consequences are severe. This framework defines the minimum bar for an operator to appear on the platform.

This is a living document. The Architect and The Auditor review it quarterly.

## Vetting Tiers

| Tier | Label | Requirements | Use |
|------|-------|-------------|-----|
| Bronze | Listed | Identity verified, license confirmed, insurance confirmed | Minimum to appear on platform |
| Silver | Verified | Bronze + background check passed, references checked, interview completed | Default display tier for city pages |
| Gold | Approved | Silver + operational assessment passed, client feedback reviewed (3+ engagements) | Featured operator status |

Only Silver and Gold operators appear on city pages. Bronze operators are listed in the backend directory but not displayed to clients until they complete Silver requirements.

## Bronze: Identity and Compliance (Minimum Bar)

Every operator must provide:

### 1. Business Registration
- Registered business entity in operating country
- Business registration document (verified against national registry)
- Active status (not dissolved, suspended, or struck off)

### 2. Security Licensing
- Valid security operating license for each country/state claimed
- License number verified against issuing authority
- License covers the specific services offered (e.g. close protection license for bodyguard services, not just a generic security license)
- Expiry date tracked; operator flagged 60 days before expiry

### 3. Insurance
- Professional liability/indemnity insurance: minimum coverage appropriate to operating region
- Employer's liability insurance (where staff are employed)
- Insurance certificate verified as current
- Expiry date tracked; operator flagged 60 days before expiry

### 4. Identity Verification
- Director/principal identity verified (government ID)
- Company address verified (not just a PO box for primary address)

## Silver: Background and Competence

Everything in Bronze, plus:

### 5. Background Checks
- Criminal background check on company directors/principals
- No convictions for violent crime, fraud, theft, or sexual offences
- Checks completed by a recognised screening provider (not self-declared)
- Country-specific: UK (DBS), US (FBI/state), EU (national police certificate), other (equivalent local check)
- Refreshed every 2 years

### 6. References
- Minimum 3 professional references from previous clients or industry contacts
- At least 1 reference must be from a corporate or institutional client (not personal)
- References contacted and verified by platform staff or designated verification partner
- Reference checks documented

### 7. Qualifications and Training
- Close protection / executive protection training from recognised institution
- First aid certification (current)
- For security drivers: advanced/defensive driving qualification
- For armed services (where legal): firearms training certification and license
- Training certificates verified against issuing institution

### 8. Interview
- Video or in-person interview with platform vetting team
- Covers: experience, operational approach, emergency protocols, client handling, conflict de-escalation
- Interview scored against standard rubric
- Minimum pass score required

## Gold: Operational Excellence

Everything in Silver, plus:

### 9. Operational Assessment
- Simulated or observed operational scenario (close protection walkthrough, route planning exercise, or event security plan review)
- Assessed by platform vetting team or designated assessor
- Covers: advance planning, threat assessment, communication, client interaction, incident response
- Scored against standard rubric

### 10. Client Feedback
- Minimum 3 completed engagements through the platform
- Average client rating above threshold (defined per-region)
- No unresolved complaints
- At least 1 positive written testimonial (with client consent)

## Ongoing Compliance

Vetting is not a one-time event.

| Check | Frequency | Action if Failed |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| License validity | Every 6 months | Suspend listing until renewed |
| Insurance validity | Every 6 months | Suspend listing until renewed |
| Background check refresh | Every 2 years | Suspend listing until completed |
| Client complaint review | Per incident | Investigation; suspend if serious |
| Regulatory change review | Quarterly | Update requirements; notify affected operators |
| Random audit (10% of operators per quarter) | Quarterly | Re-verify all documents for selected operators |

## Suspension and Removal

An operator is immediately suspended (removed from all city pages) if:
- License expires and is not renewed within 14 days
- Insurance lapses
- A serious client complaint is received (pending investigation)
- Background check reveals new disqualifying information
- Operator is found to have misrepresented qualifications
- Operator is subject to regulatory action in any operating country

Suspension is reversible once the issue is resolved. Removal (permanent) is for:
- Fraud or deliberate misrepresentation
- Violent incident involving operator personnel
- Repeated serious complaints (3+ in 12 months)
- Regulatory ban in any operating country

## Country-Specific Considerations

Security regulations vary by country. This framework is the minimum global standard. Country-specific additions:

| Country/Region | Additional Requirement |
|----------------|----------------------|
| UK | SIA (Security Industry Authority) license mandatory for all CP operatives |
| US | State-level private security license (varies by state); some states require individual operator licensing |
| UAE | SIRA (Security Industry Regulatory Agency) approval in Dubai; specific requirements per emirate |
| EU | Country-specific licensing; mutual recognition may apply for some EU states |
| Nigeria | NSCDC registration; restrictions on foreign operators |
| Brazil | Federal Police registration for private security |
| Mexico | Federal and state licensing; restrictions on foreign operators |
| South Africa | PSIRA (Private Security Industry Regulatory Authority) registration |

The Geographer's country regulation data feeds this section. New countries added as coverage expands.

## Data Handling

- All operator vetting documents are stored securely (encrypted at rest)
- Access restricted to vetting team (The Architect + designated personnel)
- Documents retained for the duration of the operator relationship + 3 years
- Operator can request their data under GDPR/local equivalent
- No operator personal data (home address, personal ID numbers) is ever displayed on the public site
- City pages show only: operator count, certification tier breakdown, service capabilities, languages

## What "Vetted" Means on the Public Site

When the site says "vetted operators", it means:
- Business registration verified
- Security license confirmed with issuing authority
- Insurance confirmed as current
- Background checks completed by recognised screening provider
- Professional references checked
- Qualifications verified against issuing institution

This is what we can stand behind publicly. No more, no less. The Auditor verifies that no city page claims anything beyond what this framework guarantees.
