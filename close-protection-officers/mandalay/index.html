FCDO Travel Advice and Entry Context

FCDO advises against all but essential travel to Myanmar following the military coup of February 2021 and the ongoing armed conflict between the Myanmar Armed Forces and resistance coalition forces that has expanded across multiple regions since that date. Mandalay Division has experienced armed clashes, targeted attacks, and civil unrest that have disrupted normal commercial activity and significantly reduced the operating space for international visitors. Any business operation requiring personnel in Mandalay should be preceded by a formal risk acceptance process at the organisational level, with legal and duty-of-care obligations reviewed before deployment is authorised.

Regulatory Framework Post-Coup

The Myanmar Police Force nominally governs commercial security licensing, but the regulatory infrastructure for private security has been substantially disrupted since the February 2021 coup and the subsequent civil conflict. The military government (SAC) has issued directives affecting business operations, but enforcement is inconsistent and the legal environment for foreign-connected businesses carries significant uncertainty. Foreign security personnel cannot hold commercial operating licences under any functioning regulatory framework in Myanmar. Any close protection arrangement for Mandalay must be delivered through locally connected operators with current situational awareness, and the legal basis for their operation should be reviewed with counsel experienced in the Myanmar conflict-period legal environment.

Threat Assessment: Mandalay

Mandalay is Myanmar's second-largest city and an industrial and commercial hub, with the Chan Mya Thazi zone housing automotive and manufacturing operations connected to foreign investment. Since 2021, the city has experienced episodes of targeted assassinations of government-linked officials, improvised explosive device incidents, and civil unrest events. The Central Bank of Myanmar branch in Mandalay has been a symbolic target in the broader economic disruption campaign. Foreign nationals, particularly those perceived as connected to regime-linked business interests, face a heightened profile risk. Emergency services, including police response and hospital capacity, are operating under conflict-period constraints that reduce their reliability compared to pre-coup benchmarks.

Conflict-Environment Close Protection

Operations in Mandalay require officers with documented conflict-environment experience, not the standard corporate close protection skill set appropriate for lower-risk cities. Relevant experience includes prior deployments in active conflict or post-coup environments, familiarity with improvised explosive device awareness, the ability to operate without reliance on official emergency services, and the professional judgement to advise mission-abort when the threat assessment warrants it. Our network for Myanmar operations draws on specialists with relevant regional experience and current ground intelligence, and we do not deploy standard corporate-tier officers into this environment.

Communication and Intelligence Protocols

Reliable mobile and internet communications in Mandalay are subject to government-imposed restrictions, including periodic network shutdowns that have been documented by international press monitoring organisations since 2021. Close protection teams operating in this environment use redundant communication methods, including satellite communication devices, to maintain connectivity with the client organisation's security control function during movement periods. Daily intelligence briefs, drawing on both local network contacts and international conflict monitoring services, are provided to the client at the start of each operating day so that movement decisions are based on current situational awareness rather than static pre-trip planning.

Evacuation and Contingency Planning

Contingency planning is a non-negotiable element of any Mandalay security package. Evacuation options must be pre-planned against both civil unrest scenarios and medical emergencies, as Mandalay International Airport (MDL) has experienced service disruptions since 2021 and overland routes to the Thai border carry their own conflict-related risks. International SOS and regional medical evacuation providers maintain Myanmar contingency capabilities, though their ability to respond is subject to the access situation on the day. Clients' organisations should have a clear evacuation decision authority chain established before any deployment commences, so that the order to abort and extract can be given promptly without requiring escalations that cause delay.
