What this covers

Operational detail for Casablanca

Ministry of Interior Law 27-06 Licensing

Morocco's private security sector is regulated by the Ministry of Interior under Law 27-06. Security companies must hold current government licensing and individual operators require formal accreditation and background verification. Standard private security in Morocco operates on an unarmed basis. Our Casablanca CPO partners hold current Ministry of Interior authorisation, verified before each assignment. Gendarmerie coordination is available for higher-risk assignments requiring armed support.

Terrorism Situational Awareness

Morocco's counter-terrorism services regularly disrupt Daesh-inspired cells. The FCDO rates the terrorism threat in Morocco as likely. CPO briefings for Casablanca assignments include current terrorism awareness indicators, target-type awareness (hotels, transport hubs, high-footfall sites), and protocols for responding to a security incident in the city. The December 2018 Imlil murders by attackers with documented Daesh links demonstrated that the threat is real and extends to locations used by foreign visitors.

Low-Visibility Business Profile

For the majority of Casablanca corporate assignments in the CFC and Maarif business districts, a low-visibility CPO in business attire and unmarked vehicle is the appropriate and proportionate approach. Operating discreetly is also consistent with the cultural operating environment in Morocco, where an overt personal security profile draws unwanted attention and can complicate access to venues and official meetings.

Medina and Tourist Area Close Escort

Motorcycle drive-by theft in Casablanca's medina areas and busy commercial streets is fast, often involves physical force, and targets visible valuables. When principal itineraries include medina visits or tourist circuits, a close escort protocol is applied: no visible jewellery or bags, the CPO maintains close physical proximity, and the vehicle is positioned for immediate extraction if required.

Female Principal Protocol

Female principals visiting Casablanca face documented patterns of unwanted attention in Morocco, particularly outside formal business contexts. The CPO brief for female principals includes discreet close escort in all public areas, specific venue advance work addressing harassment risk, and principal briefing on dress and behaviour appropriate to the Moroccan cultural and legal environment. Female CPO deployment is available on request.

Mohammed V International Airport Operations

CPO meets the principal inside Mohammed V International Airport arrivals hall, manages baggage handling, and conducts the vehicle security check before the transfer to the principal's hotel or first meeting venue in the CFC or Maarif district. The 30-kilometre CMN to Casablanca centre route on the A7 motorway is standard; alternate route planning addresses typical congestion points.
