The CPO environment in Brazzaville

Brazzaville’s security environment is shaped by three distinct factors: urban robbery and carjacking risk in the capital’s peripheral districts, Pool region armed conflict to the south, and the logistical complexity of the Congo River crossing to Kinshasa. ENI and TotalEnergies anchor the international business community; the UN and a range of NGOs maintain a significant presence given regional humanitarian operations. For most corporate visitors, the airport transfer, hotel-to-office movement in the Plateau district, and the Beach port crossing are the operational segments where CPO management reduces risk most materially.

FCDO maintained a against-all-but-essential travel advisory for Brazzaville and advised against all travel to the Pool region as of 2026. All CPO planning incorporates these advisories as baseline parameters for route selection and movement scheduling.

Operational planning for Brazzaville assignments

CPO teams operating in Brazzaville are briefed on the current Pool region situation before deployment, even when the assignment is entirely urban, because principals sometimes seek to extend their itinerary to sites south of the capital. Pre-arrival route planning covers the Maya-Maya Airport corridor, the Plateau-Bacongo movement zones, and the Beach port if a Kinshasa connection is part of the trip.

Operational detail for Brazzaville

Interior Ministry Licensing Framework

Private security and close protection operations in the Republic of Congo are regulated by the Ministry of Interior, with all operating companies required to hold a current Ministry licence before conducting CPO work in Brazzaville. Individual armed operators require separate Ministry authorisation. Our Brazzaville CPO partners provide their Ministry licence documentation to clients before deployment. Gabon and DRC-licensed operators may not carry their authorisations across the border without separate Congolese Ministry approval.

Maya-Maya Airport Transfer

Maya-Maya International Airport (BZV) is approximately 4 kilometres north of central Brazzaville, making the airport transfer shorter than many African capitals but not without risk. The road from Maya-Maya to the Plateau district passes areas where vehicle-stop robbery has been reported. CPO airport protocol covers inside-terminal collection, pre-positioned vehicle, and transfer route assessment. The physical proximity to Kinshasa means some principals arrive via Kinshasa N'djili and cross the Congo River by ferry or speed boat; this cross-river transit requires its own CPO protocol.

Congo River and Kinshasa Proximity

Brazzaville sits directly across the Congo River from Kinshasa, DRC, creating a distinctive operational environment. The ferry and speed-boat crossings at the Beach port are a vulnerability point for international visitors: crowd management, baggage security, and pickpocketing are documented risks at the terminal. CPO teams manage the cross-river transit as a distinct operational segment with a pre-positioned vehicle on each bank and a port-side escort for the crossing.

Pool Region Exclusion Zone

FCDO advises against all travel to the Pool region south and southeast of Brazzaville, where armed militia activity linked to the Ninja rebel movement has persisted since the 2016-2017 conflict. CPO operations are structured around Brazzaville's urban zones; no CPO service is offered for Pool region travel regardless of stated purpose. Source: FCDO Republic of Congo travel advice, 2026.

Oil Sector Operations

ENI and TotalEnergies are the principal international oil operators in Republic of Congo, with Brazzaville serving as the headquarters base and Pointe-Noire as the production hub. CPO operations support senior executives based in or transiting through Brazzaville, covering hotel-to-office movement, government-ministry meetings, and the Brazzaville-to-Pointe-Noire domestic transfer, which requires its own security assessment.

Robbery and Carjacking Risk

Robbery targeting international visitors is the primary threat in Brazzaville's urban zones. Incidents have been recorded at night on routes between the Plateau district and residential areas in Bacongo and Makelekelé. After-dark movement outside the Plateau is the highest-risk period and CPO teams apply heightened vehicle protocols for any movement after 21:00. Source: FCDO Republic of Congo travel advice, 2026.
