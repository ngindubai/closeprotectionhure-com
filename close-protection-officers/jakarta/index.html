What this covers

Operational detail for Jakarta

POLRI Licensing: Perkapolri No. 24 Tahun 2007

Private security in Indonesia is regulated by POLRI (Kepolisian Negara Republik Indonesia, the Indonesian National Police) under Perkapolri No. 24 Tahun 2007 (Regulation of the Chief of Police No. 24 of 2007). Private security companies, known as Perusahaan Jasa Pengamanan (PJKP), must be registered with and licensed by POLRI. Individual security officers hold a Kartu Tanda Anggota Satpam (KTAS), a POLRI-issued membership and qualification card. Training standards for Satpam (security officers) are set by POLRI, covering basic, intermediate (Supervisor), and advanced (Manager) certification levels. All CPO personnel supplied for Jakarta engagements hold current KTAS cards and are employed by POLRI-registered PJKPs.

Terrorism Threat: FCDO Assessment and Historical Context

The FCDO Indonesia travel advice (updated 2024) rates the terrorism threat in Indonesia as high. Indonesia has experienced a series of significant Islamist terrorist attacks: the 2002 Bali bombings, the 2003 Jakarta Marriott bombing, the 2009 Jakarta Marriott and Ritz-Carlton attacks, and the 2016 Jakarta attacks in the Thamrin business district. These incidents are documented in FCDO travel advice as part of its threat baseline for Indonesia. BNPT (Badan Nasional Penanggulangan Terorisme, the National Counterterrorism Agency) annual reports document ongoing operations against extremist cells, indicating that the threat, though significantly disrupted, has not been eliminated.

Venue Pre-Advance and Terrorism-Aware Operations

The 2016 Jakarta attacks targeted the Thamrin business district, which includes multiple international hotels and the Sarinah shopping complex. This history means that venue pre-advance for Jakarta CPO operations includes assessment of blast-stand-off, vehicle-access controls, and security screening at entry points to hotels, office buildings, and commercial venues. CPO teams apply a terrorism-aware operational posture as standard in Jakarta, including attention to seated positions in public spaces, awareness of exits, and immediate-action drills for active-threat scenarios.

Urban Crime and Traffic-Related Security Considerations

Jakarta is one of the world's most densely populated urban environments, with chronic traffic congestion that creates predictable, slow-moving vehicle movements exploitable for vehicle-based crime. FCDO Indonesia travel advice notes petty crime and vehicle crime as documented risks for foreign nationals. CPO-managed transport in Jakarta accounts for traffic density in route planning, avoids predictable arterial routes during peak periods, and uses vehicles appropriate to both the traffic environment and the principal's profile. Ojek (motorcycle taxi) and informal transport should not be used by principals under CPO protection.

Cultural Sensitivity in a Muslim-Majority Environment

Indonesia has the world's largest Muslim population. Jakarta CPO operations require cultural awareness at all levels, including dress standards during visits to religious or government locations, behavioural norms in mixed social environments, and sensitivity to the political context of foreign commercial operations. Female principals should discuss requirements in advance, as female CPO team members are available and are standard for female principal assignments in culturally sensitive environments. CPO teams avoid behaviours or conduct that could draw adverse attention in conservative areas or during religious observances.

Integration with Jakarta's Corporate Security Infrastructure

Jakarta's major multinational employers, diplomatic missions, and international hotels operate their own security infrastructure, which CPO teams integrate with rather than duplicate. Pre-arrival liaison with hotel security managers, corporate campus security officers, and diplomatic mission security teams is standard for Jakarta CPO engagements. BNPT-issued threat bulletins and POLRI public advisories are monitored throughout any engagement to ensure CPO operational planning reflects the current threat picture.
