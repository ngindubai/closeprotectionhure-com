The CPO environment in Freetown

Sierra Leone’s security environment reflects the persistent consequences of the 1991 to 2002 civil war: elevated crime risk driven by economic marginalisation, a post-conflict security sector undergoing ongoing reform, and a political environment that has maintained stability but requires monitoring. FCDO advises a high degree of caution throughout Sierra Leone. The US State Department rates Sierra Leone at Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution). The Lungi estuary crossing and after-dark movement are the two primary operational risk windows; the Aberdeen and Hill Station corridors are the principal safe zones for international visitors. The NGO humanitarian sector, diamond and rutile mining, and development finance organisations form the core of the professional visitor community. Source: FCDO Sierra Leone travel advice, 2026; US State Dept Sierra Leone Level 2 advisory, 2026.

Operational detail for Freetown

Private Security Industry Act CPO Licensing

Private security operations in Sierra Leone are governed by the Private Security Industry Act and licensed by the Private Security Regulatory Authority (PSRA). CPO companies must hold a current PSRA operating licence, and individual operators require PSRA registration with completed vetting. Armed CPO operations require a separate Sierra Leone Police (SLP) firearms permit. Our Freetown CPO partners provide current PSRA documentation before deployment. Operators licensed in neighbouring Guinea or Liberia cannot carry those authorisations into Sierra Leone; a PSRA licence is mandatory for every in-country CPO deployment. Source: FCDO Sierra Leone travel advice, 2026.

Lungi Airport Estuary Crossing: The Primary Vulnerability

Lungi International Airport (FNA) is located across the Sierra Leone River estuary from Freetown. This geographical separation creates a unique CPO challenge in West Africa: there is no road connection between the airport and the city. Transfer options are helicopter (approximately 10 minutes), the Lungi-Freetown passenger ferry (approximately 30 to 45 minutes), or an overland road route via the Conakry highway and Mange ferry, which takes 3 to 4 hours under normal conditions. Helicopter is the standard protocol for high-value principals; ferry requires security assessment of embarkation and disembarkation zones. CPO planning for the Lungi crossing is the primary operational task for all Freetown airport transfers.

NGO and Mining Sector Team Configuration

Freetown hosts one of West Africa's largest NGO and humanitarian sectors -- UNHCR, WFP, MSF, Save the Children, and numerous bilateral donor implementers -- alongside the diamond, rutile, and bauxite mining sectors. NGO assignments use a low-profile 2-person CPO team coordinated with the host organisation's security framework; visible armed presence is avoided in most community-facing NGO settings. Mining sector assignments for executives visiting diamond processing facilities or rutile operations may use an armed escort configuration where the specific itinerary warrants. The configuration is assessed against the assignment profile and the client's own security policy.

Freetown Movement: Aberdeen and Hill Station Corridors

Freetown's movement corridors for international visitors concentrate on the Aberdeen peninsula (the principal hotel and NGO office cluster), Hill Station (where many expatriate residential compounds and some diplomatic missions are located), and the Lumley Beach area. The East End of Freetown -- the Kissy and Wellington areas -- carries elevated crime risk and is not on standard international visitor movement corridors. After-dark movement is significantly restricted: FCDO advises that robbery and carjacking risk increases substantially after dark across Freetown. CPO operations reduce night movement to essential transfers only, with operations controller coordination required for any after-dark journey.

Post-Conflict Security Context

Sierra Leone's civil war ended in 2002; the country has maintained political stability since, with credible elections in 2023. However, the post-conflict economic environment, high youth unemployment, and persistent poverty sustain an elevated robbery and opportunistic crime risk. FCDO advises a high degree of caution throughout Sierra Leone. The primary risks for international professionals in Freetown are armed robbery, carjacking, and theft targeting visible wealth. Political demonstrations can occur with limited advance warning. CPO protective intelligence covers current political conditions and known criminal activity patterns in the principal movement corridors.

Medical Evacuation: Dakar and Accra

Medical facilities in Freetown are severely limited. Choithram Hospital provides the best available private care in the city, but serious trauma and specialist treatment require evacuation. The primary medevac destinations are Dakar, Senegal (approximately 2 hours by air) and Accra, Ghana (approximately 1 hour 40 minutes by air), both offering substantially better medical infrastructure than Freetown. Casevac from a remote diamond or rutile mining site requires helicopter-to-Freetown staging before onward air evacuation. CPO deployment planning confirms medevac insurance coverage for Sierra Leone and pre-establishes the Dakar and Accra pathways before each assignment.
