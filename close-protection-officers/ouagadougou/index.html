The CPO environment in Ouagadougou

Burkina Faso’s security environment deteriorated sharply from 2019 onwards following the expansion of Sahel jihadist groups from Mali. The 2022 military coups accelerated the collapse of civilian security structures and the displacement of international security partners. The MPSR junta under Captain Ibrahim Traore has expelled French forces and sought Wagner Group (now Africa Corps) support, fundamentally altering the security architecture. JNIM and IS Sahel Province have conducted complex attacks in Ouagadougou’s commercial areas and hotels used by international visitors. CPO deployment in this environment requires specialist Sahel expertise, current intelligence, and pre-confirmed extraction planning – not standard corporate close protection. Source: FCDO Burkina Faso travel advice, 2026; US State Dept Burkina Faso Level 4 advisory, 2026.

What this covers

Operational detail for Ouagadougou

Extreme Risk Advisory and CPO Prerequisite

FCDO advises against all travel to Burkina Faso as of 2026. The US State Department classifies Burkina Faso at Level 4 (Do Not Travel). CPO cover in Ouagadougou is not a standard corporate security measure -- it is a precondition for any essential operations that cannot be avoided. The JNIM (Jama'at Nusrat al-Islam wal-Muslimin) and Islamic State Sahel Province operate active campaigns in Burkina Faso, including attacks in and around Ouagadougou. No CPO deployment should proceed without a specialist Sahel threat assessment, host-nation coordination, and explicit client approval for the residual risk profile. Source: FCDO Burkina Faso travel advice, 2026; US State Dept Burkina Faso Level 4 advisory, 2026.

Military Junta Licensing Context

Since the January 2022 and September 2022 coups, Burkina Faso has been governed by the military junta under Captain Ibrahim Traore and the Mouvement Patriotique pour la Sauvegarde et la Restauration (MPSR). Private security licensing under this junta government is subject to rapid change: the regulatory framework that applied under civilian government has been partially disrupted by the political transition. CPO operator credentials must be verified against the current Ministry of Territorial Administration framework. Operators without current junta-era documentation and established Ministry relationships are not appropriate for Ouagadougou deployments.

OUA Airport Transfer and Threat Assessment

Ouagadougou Airport (OUA) is approximately 5 kilometres from the city centre -- one of the shortest airport-to-city corridors in West Africa. The short transfer is not a security asset in this environment: the threat is not vehicle-targeting crime but armed group activity, suicide bombing, and complex attacks of the type recorded in Ouagadougou's commercial and diplomatic areas. CPO airport protocol covers coordinated inside-terminal collection with advance coordination with the airport security authority, low-profile non-descript vehicle, and an updated threat environment assessment current to within 24 hours of any principal movement.

Specialist Sahel Team Configuration

Standard corporate CPO team configurations are not appropriate for Ouagadougou. The threat environment requires operators with specific Sahel and counterterrorism-adjacent training, current local intelligence networks, government coordination capability, and experience operating in military junta political environments. Team size is assessed against the specific mission requirement and principal risk profile. Essential operations in Ouagadougou are typically conducted by organisations with their own pre-established security frameworks (mining companies, UN agencies, diplomatic missions) that integrate with the CPO team. Stand-alone commercial CPO deployment without pre-existing local infrastructure is not recommended.

Movement Restrictions and Safe Zone Constraints

Movement in Ouagadougou is severely constrained by the security environment. The city centre, the Koulouba district (government quarter), and the Ouaga 2000 development area are the primary zones for any essential international operations; movement outside these areas requires specific mission justification and elevated protocols. After-dark movement is avoided except for essential evacuations. Roadblocks and security force checkpoints are common across the city and on approaches to the capital. Movement to any area outside Ouagadougou itself is subject to FCDO against-all-travel advisories and requires specialist operator assessment. Source: FCDO Burkina Faso travel advice, 2026.

Emergency Extraction and Medevac

Emergency extraction from Ouagadougou requires pre-established protocols with the client's embassy or diplomatic mission, confirmed aircraft availability at OUA (noting that commercial flight schedules are disrupted by the security environment), and an alternative overland extraction route to Ghana or Ivory Coast as a contingency. Medical evacuation is to Accra (approximately 1 hour 30 minutes by air) or Abidjan (approximately 1 hour 15 minutes). CHU Yalgado Ouedraogo hospital is the main public medical facility but does not meet international standards. CPO deployment planning for Ouagadougou must include a pre-confirmed extraction plan, not just a medevac pathway.
