Operational detail for Port Moresby

Regulatory Framework and Licensing

Commercial security personnel in Papua New Guinea operate under the Security (Protection) Act 2004, administered by the PNG Police Force. All close protection officers must hold a valid security licence issued under this Act before undertaking any guarding or escort duties. Our provider network engages only fully licensed PNG-registered operators, verified against the Police Force licensing register prior to deployment. Clients visiting on temporary assignments should note that foreign-national security personnel working commercially in PNG must also satisfy PNG immigration work authorisation requirements.

Threat Assessment: Port Moresby

Port Moresby consistently records one of the highest violent crime rates in the Pacific region. Raskol gang networks operate across settlements adjacent to the CBD, Waigani, and Gordons, with carjacking incidents concentrated on arterial routes between the airport and the city centre. FCDO and US OSAC advisories both note that kidnapping for ransom has affected foreign nationals, particularly those perceived to have business wealth. Civil unrest events, including politically motivated demonstrations, can escalate rapidly in Waigani near government facilities. Business travellers should treat public transport as high risk and avoid displaying laptops, cameras, or premium mobile devices at any time.

Airport and Transit Security

Jacksons International Airport (POM) sits approximately 9 kilometres from the CBD via the Hubert Murray Highway, a route that FCDO travel advice consistently identifies as a higher-risk corridor, particularly after dark. Close protection teams meet clients airside where airline agreements permit, coordinate with airport police liaison officers, and use vetted vehicle and driver combinations for the transfer leg. Route planning accounts for known stop-and-rob locations and is reviewed before each movement. Clients should avoid kerb-side waiting at the terminal and accept officer guidance on vehicle positioning throughout.

Residential and Hotel Security

Acceptable accommodation in Port Moresby is largely confined to a small number of security-conscious properties in Harbour City and the Ela Beach corridor, several of which operate their own guard forces. Close protection teams conduct a hotel security assessment on arrival, verify room allocation practices, and establish primary and secondary evacuation routes. For extended assignments, residential security surveys covering perimeter integrity, access control, and domestic staff vetting protocols are available on request. The PNG Police Force can be contacted through hotel security desks for incident reporting, though response times outside the Waigani precinct can be lengthy.

Corporate and Meeting Venue Coverage

Business meetings in Port Moresby typically occur in the CBD (Town area), Waigani government precinct, or the Gordons commercial zone. Close protection officers provide close escort between the vehicle and the venue entrance, conduct advance checks of meeting rooms where time permits, and maintain a static presence in reception areas during sensitive discussions. Officers brief clients on duress signals and exit options before each appointment. For multi-day programmes, a day-by-day movement matrix is prepared in advance and shared with clients via secure channel.

Emergency Response and Evacuation Planning

Emergency medical capacity in Port Moresby is limited; Port Moresby General Hospital handles serious trauma but equipment and specialist capacity fall below regional standards documented in OSAC reporting. Close protection teams carry trauma first aid equipment and maintain standing contacts with international medical evacuation providers, including Pacific SOS and international air ambulance networks. In the event of civil unrest or a declared threat, teams operate pre-planned extraction routes to the airport or to designated warden assembly points as advised by the Australian High Commission or British High Commission duty officer.
