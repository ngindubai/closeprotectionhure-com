Operational detail for Munich

Germany CPO Licensing Framework

Close protection officers operating in Munich are licensed under the Trade Regulation Act (Gewerbeordnung) Section 34a. The supervising authority for Bavaria is the Bavarian State Criminal Police Office (BLKA - Bayerisches Landeskriminalamt). Our Munich CPO partners hold all required current credentials and IHK-recognised qualifications, with documentation available to clients before deployment.

Low-Visibility Operational Profile

For the majority of corporate and private assignments in Munich, a low-visibility CPO in business attire with an unmarked vehicle is the appropriate operational profile. This is particularly relevant in Maxvorstadt and Schwabing business and creative districts, and in the Bogenhausen private residence area, where an overt security footprint would attract unnecessary attention. The CPO maintains situational awareness while operating within the professional environment.

Munich Business and Embassy District Coverage

Our CPO teams are familiar with Munich's Maxvorstadt, Schwabing, and Bogenhausen districts, as well as the city's major corporate venues, Messe Munchen (trade fair grounds), and the BMW Group and Siemens headquarters corridors. Knowledge of venue access procedures, building security protocols, and the principal hotel cluster including Mandarin Oriental Munich, Hotel Vier Jahreszeiten Kempinski Munich, and Bayerischer Hof Munich is standard across all assigned operators.

Munich Airport (MUC) Arrival and Departure Operations

The CPO meets the principal inside Munich Airport Franz Josef Strauss (MUC) arrivals before the principal exits baggage claim. A vehicle check is conducted before the principal boards. The MUC-to-city transfer takes 40 to 45 minutes (approximately 28km) under normal conditions, with elevated transfer times during Oktoberfest in September and October. Counter-surveillance awareness is applied from the arrivals hall for principals with elevated threat profiles.

Threat Environment and Briefing

BfV (Bundesamt fur Verfassungsschutz, Germany's domestic intelligence service) classifies the abstract terrorism threat in Germany as high (2026). FCDO advises normal precautions for Germany (2026). US State Dept Level 2 advisory for Germany (2026). BLKA provides the operational security context used in Munich CPO threat assessment. Oktoberfest (September to October) creates elevated crowd-management and personal security considerations for principals attending the event or transiting Munich during this period.

Armed Capability

Armed close protection is available in Germany through properly licensed operators holding the required authorisation under Gewerbeordnung Section 34a and applicable firearms law. The majority of Munich corporate and private assignments are conducted unarmed, consistent with the low ambient risk. Armed capability is deployed where the pre-deployment threat assessment identifies a specific requirement.
