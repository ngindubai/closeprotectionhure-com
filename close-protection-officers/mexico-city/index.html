SEGOB licensing and federal compliance

Mexico’s federal law governing private security, the Ley Federal de Seguridad Privada published in 2006, requires all commercial private security companies to hold a federal operating licence from the Secretaria de Gobernacion (SEGOB). The SEGOB registration system covers company licensing, personnel vetting, and equipment authorisation including firearms. State-level private security regulations exist alongside the federal system, but SEGOB registration is the primary compliance requirement for any company providing services to foreign corporate clients.

CPO operations in Mexico City

Targeted executive kidnapping is addressed through information security discipline, vetted driver-only transportation, and counter-surveillance awareness during all ground movements.

Operational detail for Mexico City

SEGOB Federal Registration

Private security companies operating commercially in Mexico must register with the Secretaria de Gobernacion (SEGOB) under the Ley Federal de Seguridad Privada (2006). Operating companies require a federal operating licence from SEGOB. Individual CPOs are vetted and authorised under the employing company's licence. We engage only SEGOB-registered operators for Mexico City engagements.

Express Kidnap and Targeted Abduction Protocols

Mexico City CPO work is specifically designed around two distinct threat types: express kidnapping (secuestro expres) targeting any visible-wealth individual, and targeted kidnapping of senior executives and HNWI principals. Both are documented in OSAC (Overseas Security Advisory Council) and FCDO reporting. Operational planning includes information security around the principal's itinerary, vehicle and route protocols, and pre-determined emergency contacts.

Polanco, Santa Fe, and Lomas Coverage

Operational familiarity with Mexico City's corporate and HNWI zones: Polanco, Santa Fe financial district, Lomas de Chapultepec, and the Reforma corridor. Each area has its own ambient risk profile and established CPO operating protocols.

Armed CPO Options

Armed close protection is available in Mexico City through SEGOB-registered operators with the appropriate federal firearms authorisation. Armed CPOs carry under strict federal licensing requirements distinct from standard security guard arming. Armed cover is specified based on the threat assessment for the specific principal and engagement.

Vehicle Counter-Surveillance

Counter-surveillance during vehicle movements is a core CPO competency in Mexico City, where vehicle-based express kidnapping is the primary threat vector for foreign executives. This includes anti-surveillance driving techniques, recognition of vehicle-based surveillance, and protocols for suspected follow situations.

Emergency Extraction and KRE Contacts

For principals with the highest threat profiles, the CPO engagement plan includes Kidnap and Ransom Extraction (KRE) contacts, a pre-established emergency communication protocol, and coordination with the principal's corporate security and travel risk management provider.
