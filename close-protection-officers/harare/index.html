Operational detail for Harare

ZRP Licensing and Regulatory Framework

Private security companies operating in Zimbabwe are required to be licensed under the framework administered by the Zimbabwe Republic Police (ZRP), which serves as the primary authority overseeing both company-level and individual officer authorisations for the private security sector. All CPO providers deploying teams in Harare must hold current ZRP licensing, and personnel must meet the vetting and training standards set under the Private Security Act. Our Harare operations are delivered exclusively through ZRP-licensed partner companies whose staff have been verified against these requirements. The FCDO Zimbabwe advisory (2025) notes that the broader security and economic environment in Zimbabwe remains volatile, which has had a knock-on effect on the reliability of lower-tier security operators; verifying ZRP authorisation before engaging any provider is therefore a practical as well as a legal requirement. Clients should request sight of current licence documentation at the engagement stage and confirm it covers both the company entity and the individual officers deployed.

Robert Gabriel Mugabe International Airport Protocol

Robert Gabriel Mugabe International Airport (IATA: HRE) is Harare's primary international gateway, located approximately 15 kilometres south-east of the city centre. Our CPO conducts a pre-arrival advance of the arrivals hall and landside vehicle staging area, confirming the collection point, identifying the nearest alternative routes, and completing vehicle security checks before the principal's flight lands. The transfer from HRE to the CBD, Borrowdale, or Highlands areas involves a route assessment that accounts for current ZRP roadblock activity, traffic congestion, and any public order incidents. Departure protocols are briefed in advance, covering check-in timing, airside escort procedures, and the documentation required for a smooth ZRP checkpoint clearance on the airport approach roads. The operations controller maintains contact with the CPO team throughout the transfer and holds contingency routing in the event the primary approach road is blocked.

Carjacking and Economic Crime Counter-Protocol

The FCDO Zimbabwe advisory (2025) identifies carjacking and opportunistic vehicle crime as significant risks for international principals, driven in part by Zimbabwe's sustained economic instability and high unemployment. Our CPO counter-protocol for carjacking risk covers vehicle discipline: all vehicles are kept in motion where possible, doors locked and windows raised in urban traffic, and approach to static stopping points such as fuel stations is conducted with heightened observation. Anti-surveillance techniques are applied on all principal movements to identify and break any surveillance or follow pattern before it develops. Principals are briefed on immediate-action procedures if a vehicle is approached by hostile actors. For higher-risk assignments, armoured vehicle capability is available within the ZRP-licensed partner network. The Harare CBD requires particular attention at peak traffic hours when vehicle crime risk is elevated; Borrowdale and Highlands residential areas carry a different profile focused on residential approach and gate security, which is documented in the advance work for each venue.

CBD, Borrowdale, and Highlands Zone Operations

Harare's primary operating environment for international corporate principals spans three zones: the CBD, which hosts the major commercial banks, law firms, and government offices along Jason Moyo Avenue and Samora Machel Avenue; Borrowdale, the established residential suburb to the north that accommodates the majority of senior expatriate and NGO housing; and Highlands, an adjacent residential area also used by diplomatic and corporate communities. Zone-specific advance work for the CBD covers vehicle approach and extraction routes, footprint minimisation in the busy pedestrian areas around First Street, and identification of secure meeting venues. Borrowdale and Highlands advance work focuses on residential approach security, gate and guardroom protocols, and street-level surveillance indicators. ZRP roadblock activity is tracked as a routine element of all three zone operations, with daily intelligence updates informing route selection. Movement between zones is planned to avoid peak congestion windows and known high-incident street segments.

Emergency Planning and Medevac to Johannesburg

Medical infrastructure in Harare includes a small number of private clinics capable of initial stabilisation, but the FCDO Zimbabwe advisory makes clear that serious cases involving international principals are typically evacuated for definitive treatment. Our CPO teams carry trauma-capable first-aid kits and maintain direct liaison contacts with Harare's leading private medical facilities for stabilisation while medevac is arranged. The standard medevac routing for critical cases is to Johannesburg, South Africa, at approximately one hour by air, where multiple internationally accredited hospitals offer specialist emergency and surgical care. Medevac provider contacts are confirmed active before each principal arrives in country. The emergency plan for every Harare assignment defines trigger criteria for medevac activation, identifies the staging route to HRE for air evacuation, and documents all relevant medical and insurance contacts. Copies of the plan are held by the operations controller and shared with the principal's home organisation travel risk contact before the assignment begins.
