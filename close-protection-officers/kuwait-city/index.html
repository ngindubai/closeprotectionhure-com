What this covers

Operational detail for Kuwait City

MOI Licensing and Enforcement

Kuwait's Ministry of Interior formally enforces licensing requirements for all private security companies and individual security personnel. Every operator on our Kuwait City panel holds a current MOI licence. Individual CPO personnel carry MOI-issued authorisations. Licence documentation is provided to clients as part of the pre-deployment confirmation pack.

Armed Close Protection Under MOI Authorisation

Kuwait is unusual among Gulf states in that armed close protection is available under MOI licensing, primarily relevant for oil and gas executives, US government contractor staff, and principals whose threat assessment warrants it. Armed CPO deployment is assessed against the specific threat profile of the principal and requires advance authorisation through the MOI framework. This is confirmed at the scoping stage, not assumed.

Iran-Aligned Militia and Terrorism Threat Awareness

Kataib Hezbollah and other Iran-aligned militia groups have referenced US military installations at Camp Arifjan and Camp Buehring as targets. Disrupted ISIS plots in 2020 and 2024 confirm that domestic security services remain actively engaged. CPO teams operating for Western, oil sector, or US government contractor principals incorporate this threat picture into daily advance planning and movement decisions.

Gulf Road and Salmiya Operations Zone

The Gulf Road corridor and Salmiya hotel zone are the primary operating environment for international corporate principals in Kuwait City, with major international hotel properties and conference facilities concentrated in this area. Our advance officers are familiar with this zone, maintaining pre-advance hotel liaison and venue access protocols for all major properties.

Road Movement Risk Management

FCDO notes hazardous driving conditions across Kuwait: speeding, unpredictable lane changes, and red lights ignored as standard. Kuwait International Airport is approximately 16 kilometres from central Kuwait City. All CPO team movements use vetted, MOI-licensed drivers with route planning and operations controller monitoring as standard. No principal movement uses unvetted or ad hoc transport.

Low and High-Visibility Profiles

For most Kuwait City corporate assignments, a discreet, low-visibility CPO profile operating in unmarked vehicles is appropriate. Principals with elevated threat profiles, or those attending government, military, or diplomatic events, may require a visible, high-profile security presence. Profile is confirmed at the threat assessment stage and reviewed throughout the assignment.
