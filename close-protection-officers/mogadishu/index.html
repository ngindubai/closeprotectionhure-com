What this covers

Operational detail for Mogadishu

NISA and Somalia Police Force Regulatory Framework

Private security operations in Mogadishu operate within a framework shaped by the National Intelligence and Security Agency (NISA) and the Somalia Police Force (SPF). NISA holds primacy for intelligence and national security matters affecting foreign nationals, while the SPF provides the broader policing framework within which private security companies must operate. In practice, the private security sector in Mogadishu is heavily shaped by relationships with NISA and the Federal Government of Somalia's security institutions, and reputable CPO providers maintain active working relationships with both. Our Mogadishu operations are delivered through partner companies with established NISA and SPF frameworks and a documented track record supporting international organisations, diplomatic missions, and UN-system clients. The FCDO Somalia advisory (2025) advises against all travel to Somalia and rates the security situation as extremely high risk. Any principal whose programme requires travel to Mogadishu should receive a full travel risk assessment before confirmation, and the organisation's duty-of-care framework should be reviewed against the FCDO advisory.

Aden Adde International Airport Protocol

Aden Adde International Airport (IATA: MGQ) is Mogadishu's main international gateway and sits within a fortified perimeter on the Indian Ocean coast. The airport itself operates under heavy security, with multiple access control layers managed by Somali National Army and AMISOM-successor forces. Our CPO team coordinates the principal's arrival directly with the airport security authority and the host organisation's compound or secure facility to ensure a continuous security chain from the aircraft door. No unaccompanied movement occurs between the aircraft and the secure vehicle. The transfer from MGQ to the principal's fortified compound destination is conducted exclusively in armoured vehicles, with the route assessed against current intelligence on IED activity and Al-Shabaab targeting in Mogadishu. The FCDO Somalia advisory identifies the airport itself as a target for Al-Shabaab attacks, and exposure time in the airport environment is minimised accordingly. Departure protocols follow the same armoured vehicle and close co-ordination framework, with the team maintaining continuous contact with the operations controller.

Al-Shabaab IED and Targeted Killing Counter-Protocol

Al-Shabaab represents the primary security threat for international principals in Mogadishu, employing IED attacks, vehicle-borne IEDs (VBIEDs), complex attacks on fortified compounds, and targeted killings of international staff and Somali government officials, as documented in the FCDO Somalia advisory (2025). Our CPO counter-protocol for the Mogadishu environment is built around several non-negotiable operational baselines. Armoured vehicles are mandatory for all principal movements, with no exceptions. Route plans are updated against the most recent intelligence before every movement, with no fixed routes used on repeat journeys. Vehicle convoys for higher-profile assignments include a lead vehicle conducting a forward route check. The CPO team maintains constant radio communication with the operations controller and the principal's fortified compound security manager throughout all movements. Compound perimeter security is reviewed at the start of every assignment, and the CPO team conducts a walk-through of all emergency exit and extraction routes before the principal enters the compound. The team is briefed on VBIED recognition indicators and rapid extraction drills from vehicle and building environments.

Villa Somalia and Fortified Compound Zone Operations

The primary operating zones for international principals in Mogadishu are the areas around Villa Somalia, the seat of the Federal Government, and the fortified compound areas used by the UN system, AMISOM successor forces, major international NGOs, and diplomatic missions. UNSOM compound areas and the Halane camp provide the most secure base locations available to international visitors. Zone-specific operations focus on movement between fortified facilities, with each transfer treated as a high-risk event requiring full armoured vehicle, route-advance, and quick-reaction force (QRF) protocols. Principals do not move between facilities without a confirmed QRF capability in place: QRF availability is a contractual requirement for all Mogadishu assignments, not an optional enhancement. Footprint outside fortified perimeters is kept to the absolute minimum consistent with the principal's programme. Any meeting that can be conducted within a secure compound is held there rather than at an external venue; any external venue visit requires a full pre-advance and a dedicated extraction plan before the principal is committed.

Emergency Planning, QRF, and Medevac to Nairobi

Given the extreme risk environment in Mogadishu, emergency planning is one of the most intensive components of any assignment. Medical facilities in Mogadishu are limited, and for any serious trauma or medical contingency, medevac to Nairobi, Kenya, is the standard response, at approximately two hours by air. Our CPO teams carry advanced trauma first-aid capability including haemostatic dressings and tourniquet sets, and the principal's compound is assessed for a designated casualty collection point and staging area for medevac. Medevac provider contacts are confirmed active before each principal's arrival, and the operations controller holds the full activation chain throughout the deployment. Quick-reaction force provision is confirmed contractually before deployment commences: the QRF must have a defined response time and a pre-agreed rendezvous protocol with the CPO team. For compound compound-breach scenarios, extraction plans to the nearest allied security facility or the airport are documented and rehearsed with compound security managers before the principal arrives.
