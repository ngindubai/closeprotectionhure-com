Operational detail for Naypyidaw

SAC Military Oversight and Regulatory Framework

Myanmar's private security sector in Naypyidaw operates under the oversight of the Myanmar Naypyidaw Council and, since the February 2021 military coup, under the broader authority of the State Administration Council (SAC). The SAC exercises control over all security-related functions in the country, and any CPO operation in Naypyidaw must operate in full compliance with SAC directives and through locally approved security structures. Independent private security operations outside government-sanctioned frameworks are not permitted. Our Naypyidaw deployments are conducted exclusively through channels that have been pre-cleared with the relevant SAC security liaison structures, and all operational plans are shared with those liaison contacts before deployment commences. The FCDO Myanmar advisory (2025) rates the overall security situation as very high risk and strongly advises against all but essential travel to Myanmar. Clients should understand that CPO operations in Naypyidaw are subject to a level of regime oversight that does not apply in other markets, and that all movement must be planned within government-approved parameters.

Naypyidaw Airport Protocol

Naypyidaw Airport (IATA: NYT) is a large, purpose-built facility serving the capital. Due to the highly restricted movement environment, the airport transfer protocol in Naypyidaw differs significantly from standard CPO practice in other markets. All collection arrangements must be pre-coordinated with the SAC security liaison assigned to the principal's visit, and vehicles used for airport transfers are required to conform to SAC-approved transport provisions for international visitors. Our operations controller confirms liaison contact and transfer arrangements a minimum of 48 hours before the principal's arrival. The route from NYT into the government ministry and hotel zones is assessed against current SAC directives on permitted movement corridors. Departure protocols follow the same liaison coordination structure, with advance notification to the SAC security contact required before the principal is moved to the airport. CPO teams do not make independent routing decisions in Naypyidaw; all movements are documented and shared with the SAC liaison as part of the operational requirement.

Restricted Movement and SAC Security Liaison Protocol

The defining operational constraint for CPO operations in Naypyidaw is the complete restriction on independent movement. The FCDO Myanmar advisory (2025) notes that the SAC exercises total control over access and movement in the capital, and that international visitors are permitted to operate only within government-approved zones. CPO teams accompanying international principals do not conduct independent route advances or exploratory movements; all zone access is determined by SAC-approved itineraries. SAC security liaisons accompany all international principals throughout their time in Naypyidaw, and CPO operations are structured to complement rather than replace this official security presence. Pre-visit planning focuses on establishing the permitted zone parameters, confirming which hotel and meeting facilities are within approved areas, and identifying the SAC contact chain for any contingency requiring a change to the approved itinerary. Principals are fully briefed on these constraints before travel so that expectations are set correctly at the outset.

Government Zone and Ministry Area Operations

Naypyidaw was purpose-built as Myanmar's administrative capital and is divided into distinct zones, with the principal operating environment for international visitors concentrated in the Ministry Zone, the Hotel Zone, and the diplomatic compound areas. Movement between these zones is permitted within the government-approved itinerary parameters. The Ministry Zone hosts the headquarters of Myanmar's main government departments and is the primary location for official meetings with SAC representatives and government counterparts. The Hotel Zone provides the internationally managed accommodation options available to foreign visitors. Our CPO teams are briefed on the physical layout of each zone, the approved vehicle entry points, and the SAC security positions within each area. All meetings, venue visits, and movements within approved zones are documented in advance and shared with the SAC liaison. Any request to visit a location outside the approved zones is escalated immediately to the operations controller and the SAC liaison before any movement is made.

Emergency Planning and Medevac to Bangkok

Medical facilities in Naypyidaw are limited in scope and quality, and for any serious medical contingency involving an international principal, medevac out of Myanmar is the appropriate response. The standard medevac routing from Naypyidaw is to Bangkok, Thailand, at approximately two hours by air, where multiple internationally accredited hospitals offer specialist emergency and surgical care. Our CPO teams carry trauma-capable first-aid equipment and hold direct contacts at Naypyidaw's best-resourced clinic facilities for initial stabilisation. Medevac provider contacts are confirmed active before each principal's arrival, and medevac trigger criteria are documented in the emergency plan shared with the principal and their home organisation. Given the SAC oversight environment, all emergency movements including medevac must be co-ordinated with the SAC security liaison, and the emergency plan builds in the required SAC notification steps to avoid delays at the airport. Contingency documentation includes all medical records, insurance contacts, and medevac provider details held by the operations controller throughout the deployment.
