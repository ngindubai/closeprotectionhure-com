What this covers

Operational detail for San Jose

Ministerio de Seguridad Publica Licensing

Costa Rica's Ministry of Security (Ministerio de Seguridad Publica) administers the most developed private security regulatory framework in Central America. All CPO operators on our San Jose panel hold current ministry registration. Costa Rica's licensing environment provides genuine accountability and is a material differentiator from security service quality in some neighbouring countries.

Crime Management Focus

The primary close protection task in San Jose is crime management rather than political threat mitigation. Vehicle break-ins are extremely common across the city. Armed robbery occurs in peripheral districts and parts of the city centre. Drug trafficking spillover creates situational awareness requirements on transport routes, particularly after dark. CPO deployment for San Jose principals is calibrated to these specific risks.

Escazu and Santa Ana Corporate Zone

Escazu and Santa Ana, west of the city centre, form the primary multinational corporate zone and host the majority of international business activity. These areas carry lower street-level crime exposure than peripheral urban districts. Our CPO teams maintain pre-advance protocols at major corporate hotel properties and business centres in both zones.

Armed Close Protection Authorisation

Armed close protection is available in Costa Rica under Ministry of Security authorisation. Armed provision is not routinely required for corporate assignments in Escazu or Santa Ana given the overall lower risk profile, but can be arranged for principals with individual threat assessments that specify it, or for clients whose global security programmes require armed CPO as a standard. Authorisation timelines are confirmed at scoping.

Airport Transfer and Arrival Management

Juan Santamaria International Airport (SJO) is approximately 18 kilometres from central San Jose. The airport-to-city transfer corridor carries the principal vehicle security risks relevant to the San Jose environment. CPO collection inside the terminal, vetted and licensed drivers, and operations controller monitoring are standard for all SJO arrivals in the principal's programme.

NGO and Development Sector Support

San Jose is a regional hub for NGO operations, development finance institutions, and international organisations with Central American programmes. Our CPO teams have experience supporting NGO field staff, development finance executives, and visiting delegations from international institutions, with appropriate low-visibility profiles calibrated to the NGO operating context.
