Operational detail for Recife

Regulatory Framework and Licensing

Commercial security services in Brazil are regulated at the federal level under Federal Law 7.102/1983 (Lei de Seguranca Privada), with oversight provided by the Federal Police (Policia Federal). All private security companies must hold a Federal Police authorisation certificate, and individual security professionals must hold a Federal Police-issued identity card (identidade funcional) demonstrating completion of the legally required training programme. The Pernambuco State Secretary of Public Security (SSDS-PE) coordinates with the Federal Police on oversight of operators in the state. Our Recife network engages only Federal Police-authorised companies, and we verify the Federal Police certificate of each operator before engagement.

Threat Assessment: Recife

Recife carries one of the highest violent crime rates in Brazil, a country that itself records globally elevated homicide and robbery statistics. FCDO Brazil travel advice notes the importance of heightened vigilance in Recife and identifies the city as requiring particular caution regarding vehicle crime, express kidnapping, and armed robbery. Boa Viagem, the beachfront business hotel district, has experienced a pattern of robbery along its beachfront promenade, particularly at dawn and after dark. The Centro (CBD) requires close escort at all times and should not be navigated on foot without security support. Carjacking at traffic signals is a consistent risk across the city, and vehicles at stationary positions for more than a few seconds represent the most common physical vulnerability for business visitors.

Airport and Transit Security

Recife/Guararapes Gilberto Freyre International Airport (REC) is located approximately 12 kilometres south of the Centro in the Boa Viagem zone. The airport transfer is operationally straightforward but the route passes through areas of elevated street crime risk. Close protection teams position vehicles to minimise kerb-side exposure at the arrivals terminal, use vetted drivers with knowledge of the Recife road network, and monitor real-time traffic and incident intelligence to identify and avoid route disruptions. Air-conditioned private vehicles are strongly preferred over any form of public or semi-public transport. Under no circumstances should clients accept rides from unlicensed taxi operators at the terminal.

Express Kidnapping and Vehicle Crime

Express kidnapping (sequestro-relampago) is a documented crime type in Recife, involving the abduction of victims for short periods to force ATM withdrawals or transfer of valuables. Targets are typically selected based on visible wealth indicators or predictable routines. FCDO Brazil advice notes this crime type as affecting foreign nationals. Close protection officers address this risk through a combination of route variation, avoidance of predictable schedules, low-profile vehicle selection, and awareness protocols for identifying surveillance or following behaviour. Officers are trained to identify the early behavioural indicators of targeting and to initiate evasive action before a threat develops into a contact situation.

Business District and Hotel Security

Boa Viagem is the principal area for business hotels in Recife, with the main international and national chain properties concentrated within a few blocks of the beachfront on Avenida Boa Viagem. This location is well served for business meetings in the same district but requires close escort for any movement to the Centro or Espinheiro commercial areas. Officers conduct a hotel security assessment on arrival, identify primary and emergency exit routes, and brief clients on protocols for in-hotel security including safe opening procedures and vehicle staging. For multi-day assignments, a movement programme is prepared daily based on the client's confirmed schedule, incorporating current local intelligence.

Emergency Response and Medical Support

Recife has relatively developed medical infrastructure by Northeast Brazilian standards. Hospital das Clinicas da UFPE is the main public reference hospital, and there are several private facilities used by the business community, including Unimed Recife and Hospital Esperanca. For serious trauma, private hospital capacity in Boa Viagem provides faster access to specialist care than the public system. Brazilian emergency services use the number 190 for police (Policia Militar), 192 for SAMU ambulance, and 193 for fire and civil defence. Police response times in Boa Viagem are generally faster than in the Centro or peripheral neighbourhoods. For medical emergencies exceeding local capacity, medical evacuation to Sao Paulo or internationally is the next step, and teams maintain standing contacts with evacuation providers.

FCDO travel advice for Brazil specifically notes Recife as requiring heightened vigilance, and US OSAC Brazil reporting consistently identifies the city as a higher-risk location within the country. Business visitors face risks including armed robbery, vehicle crime, and express kidnapping. The professional profile of most business visitors, including accommodation in beach-district hotels, visible use of devices and branded luggage, and predictable movement schedules, creates a threat profile that close protection directly addresses through route discipline, vehicle security, and protective awareness.

Federal Law 7.102/1983 is the principal legislation governing private security in Brazil. It requires private security companies to obtain authorisation from the Federal Police (Policia Federal) to operate lawfully. Individual security professionals must complete a Federal Police-approved training programme and hold a Federal Police identity card confirming their authorisation. The law sets out minimum standards for firearms, training, and conduct applicable to commercial security workers. The Federal Police maintains a registry of authorised companies and individual security professionals, which can be checked to verify that an operator is in good standing. State-level authorities including SSDS-PE in Pernambuco coordinate with the Federal Police on enforcement.

Vehicle crime in Recife is concentrated at two points: stationary positions including traffic signals and locations where vehicles must slow or stop, and predictable routes between known destinations such as the airport and Boa Viagem hotels. Carjacking is the most common form, sometimes involving motorcyclists who approach vehicles from the rear or side at traffic signals. Close protection teams address this by keeping vehicles moving where possible (using known green-light routes during planning), maintaining situational awareness at all stationary positions, and briefing drivers on protocols for evading or responding to an approach. High-value vehicles and large SUVs with foreign or rental markings carry higher targeting risk and are avoided where possible in favour of local-spec vehicles.

Boa Viagem is the most security-conscious area of Recife for business visitors, with the international hotel properties operating their own guard forces, controlled parking, and reception security. Within the immediate hotel zone and along the main commercial streets during business hours, the risk level is manageable with personal security awareness. However, the beachfront promenade (Avenida Boa Viagem seafront path) has a documented pattern of robbery, particularly at dawn and after dark, and should not be used without security support. Movement between Boa Viagem and other areas of the city, including the Centro and Espinheiro, requires close escort regardless of time of day. Hotel security alone does not provide the movement coverage that a close protection team delivers.

Express kidnapping involves the short-term abduction of a target to force ATM withdrawals or handover of valuables, typically lasting between one and several hours. FCDO and OSAC both note this crime type as affecting foreign nationals in Brazilian cities including Recife. Perpetrators typically select targets based on observable wealth indicators or predictable routines identified through pre-incident surveillance. Close protection manages this risk by varying routes and departure times to disrupt surveillance, selecting low-profile vehicles, avoiding public display of valuables, and ensuring clients do not carry large amounts of cash or use ATMs in unsecured public locations. Officers maintain an awareness function during all external movements to identify potential surveillance or approach behaviour before it develops.
