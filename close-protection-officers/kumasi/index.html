The risk environment is moderate by West African standards, with the primary security concerns for visiting executives falling into two distinct categories: urban petty crime in Kumasi’s dense commercial areas, and road safety risk on the highway routes connecting the city to its mining hinterland. FCDO Ghana (2024) identifies Kejetia Market and Asafo Market – two of West Africa’s largest and most intensely trafficked commercial environments – as the primary zones for pickpocketing and bag-snatching targeting foreign visitors. The N6 highway to Accra and the roads south to Obuasi are documented high-risk routes in WHO road safety reporting, with commercial vehicle accidents and inadequate road surfaces creating hazards that require active management rather than routine vehicle provision. Most international principals arrive via Kotoka International Airport in Accra (ACC) and either connect to Kumasi by domestic flight or by road, and the choice and management of this first transfer is one of the most important security decisions of the Kumasi programme.

Close protection in Kumasi operates at a level appropriate to a moderate-risk West African city with a significant extractive sector presence. Evening movement requires heightened awareness, and vehicle security during any period of unoccupied vehicle parking must be managed actively. The standard hotel base in Ahodwo, where the Golden Tulip Kumasi City and Miklin Hotel provide the most appropriate accommodation for senior international visitors, is reasonably managed from a security perspective and close to the Roman Hill commercial district. What this covers

Operational detail for Kumasi

Regulatory Framework and Licensing

Private security in Ghana is governed by the Private Security Organizations Act (Act 803, 2009), administered by the Private Security Organizations Board (PSOB) under the Ministry of Interior. All security companies and individual officers providing close protection services in Ghana must hold valid PSOB licences. The licensing process involves company registration, individual officer vetting and training verification, and Ministry approval. Foreign security companies cannot operate in Ghana without PSOB registration or a formally structured partnership with a PSOB-licensed Ghanaian entity -- in practice this means that international clients deploying CPO teams to Kumasi must work through a locally licensed provider or a reputable international broker with established Ghanaian partnerships. The PSOB framework has improved in regulatory rigour since Act 803's implementation, but the quality of individual operators varies considerably, and due diligence on the specific company and officers is essential rather than simply verifying PSOB registration.

Street Crime and Market Zone Risk

FCDO Ghana (2024) identifies petty theft and street crime as the primary risks for foreign visitors in Kumasi. The highest-risk environments are the Kejetia Market area and the Asafo Market -- two of West Africa's largest and most densely populated market environments, handling hundreds of thousands of daily transactions in a chaotic, high-footfall geography. Pickpocketing, bag-snatching, and distraction-based theft are the dominant crime types in these zones. For business visitors, the Kejetia and Asafo areas are unlikely to be primary operating environments, but the transit routes between the Ahodwo hotel zone and the Roman Hill commercial district pass near these markets, and awareness of their locations informs routing decisions. Evening movement in less-assessed commercial streets carries a higher risk of opportunistic robbery than the daytime environment. Vehicle crime -- including break-ins to parked vehicles -- is documented at locations where foreign-registered or visibly high-value vehicles are left unattended. CPO protocols for Kumasi include maintaining occupied vehicles where possible and avoiding extended parking in unsupervised public areas.

Mining Sector Business Coverage

The Ashanti region produces a substantial share of Ghana's gold output, and Ghana ranked in the top ten globally for gold production (Ghana Chamber of Mines, 2023). The two largest individual operations within practical distance of Kumasi are AngloGold Ashanti's Obuasi Mine (65 kilometres south of Kumasi) and Gold Fields' Tarkwa Mine (approximately 180 kilometres west, typically accessed from Kumasi as the nearest significant city). Senior executive visits to either mine draw C-suite, investor relations, and engineering leadership from the operating companies and from the international financial sector. Kumasi serves as the accommodation base for most Obuasi visits, as hotel infrastructure at Obuasi itself is limited to what the mine-site compound provides. CPO support for mining sector executives must cover the road transfer between Kumasi and Obuasi or Tarkwa -- both routes involve extended highway and rural road transit -- in addition to the standard city-based programme. The Obuasi transfer in particular traverses road that is rated by the Ghana Chamber of Mines as requiring advance safety assessment.

Road Safety and Inter-City Transfer Risk

Road safety is a primary operational risk variable for Kumasi deployments. Ghana has among the highest road fatality rates in West Africa (WHO Global Road Safety Report). The N6 highway between Kumasi and Accra -- the primary route for principals arriving by road from Kotoka International Airport -- is specifically documented as a high-risk route, with commercial vehicle accidents, fuel tanker rollovers, and head-on collisions on inadequate road surfaces being the documented failure modes. The transfer between Accra and Kumasi by road takes approximately three hours under normal conditions and substantially longer during daylight hours when commercial traffic is heaviest. Most international principals fly into Accra (ACC) and either take a domestic flight to Kumasi Airport (KMS, 4km from centre) or transfer by road; the road option requires a dedicated CPO vehicle, a well-briefed driver with knowledge of the N6 risk points, and a planned rest stop at an assessed location. Night driving on the N6 is not recommended. Urban driving in Kumasi itself requires familiarity with the city's congestion patterns around the Kejetia and Asafo areas during peak hours.

Airport and Transit Security

Kumasi Airport (KMS) is a small domestic and regional airport located 4 kilometres from the city centre. Domestic connections from Accra (ACC) are operated by Africa World Airlines and Passion Air, with flight times of approximately 45-55 minutes. For principals arriving from outside Ghana, the standard routing is via Kotoka International Airport Accra (ACC), which handles all major international connections including direct services from London Heathrow (British Airways), Amsterdam, Frankfurt, and Dubai. Principals can then connect to KMS by domestic flight or by road. The domestic terminal at ACC and the KMS terminal are both compact and manageable from an arrivals security perspective. CPO meet-and-greet at KMS involves staging at the small arrivals area and a short vehicle transfer to the Ahodwo hotel zone. For arriving principals at ACC, the CPO team should be positioned in the international arrivals hall and manage the transition to vehicle at the ACC kerb, which can be disorganised during peak periods.

Emergency Response and Medical Support

Komfo Anokye Teaching Hospital (KATH, +233 3220 22301) is the primary public hospital in Kumasi and the main referral centre for the Ashanti region. It provides general acute care but has limitations in specialist capacity and pharmaceutical availability compared to international standards. For serious medical events -- significant trauma, cardiac emergencies, or conditions requiring intensive care -- the plan must assume medical evacuation to Accra, where Korle Bu Teaching Hospital and a number of private facilities including Trust Hospital and Nyaho Medical Centre provide substantially better resources. For conditions beyond what Accra can address, international evacuation to London, Paris, or Dubai is the contingency. Pre-arranged medical evacuation insurance covering Ghana is essential for any senior principal visiting Kumasi. Emergency contacts: 191 (police), 193 (ambulance). The British High Commission in Accra (+233 30 221 3250) handles consular matters for UK nationals across Ghana including the Ashanti region.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Under Ghana’s Private Security Organizations Act (Act 803, 2009), companies must hold a PSOB company licence from the Private Security Organizations Board under the Ministry of Interior. Individual officers must hold valid PSOB individual licences. Clients should request both the company licence number and individual officer PSOB cards before deployment. PSOB registration can be confirmed through the Ministry of Interior’s private security registry. Simply holding PSOB registration is a necessary but not sufficient indicator of quality – due diligence should also include reference checks and evidence of the company’s operational track record in Kumasi specifically.

The N6 highway south from Kumasi to Obuasi (65km) and the longer route west to Tarkwa (approximately 180km) both involve roads that carry heavy commercial vehicle traffic, have inadequate road surface quality in sections, and are documented in WHO road safety data as high-risk. Kumasi-to-Obuasi transfers require a CPO vehicle with an experienced local driver, pre-assessed route, daytime-only movement, and a communications plan with a check-in schedule. The Tarkwa transfer is longer and more demanding. Night driving on either route is not recommended under any circumstances. Principals visiting Obuasi may also have access to mine transport coordinated through AngloGold Ashanti’s site security team, which should be explored as a supplemental option.

Kejetia Market and Asafo Market are two of West Africa’s most densely populated commercial environments and represent the highest concentration of pickpocketing and bag-snatching risk in Kumasi (FCDO Ghana, 2024). CPO route planning identifies the market boundaries and plans vehicle transit routes to avoid the most congested approach roads during market peak hours (approximately 0800-1800). If a principal must be in the vicinity of the markets for a specific commercial purpose, foot escort in the market area requires close formation, visible deterrence through officer positioning, and minimal principal exposure time. Valuable items should not be worn or displayed in or around either market area.

The Ghana Peace Council has documented communal tensions in the Ashanti region and surrounding areas arising from chieftaincy succession disputes and land rights conflicts in mining communities. These tensions occasionally manifest as community protests or road blockages, particularly near mining operations where land acquisition and royalty distribution are contested. For executives visiting Obuasi Mine or other operations in these areas, the CPO team should obtain a current assessment of community relations from the mine’s security manager before the visit. Community protests targeting mine access roads are not violent against visiting executives in the documented record, but they can create unplanned delays and confusion that require a managed response. Route alternatives for each major mine transfer are a standard planning element.

Komfo Anokye Teaching Hospital (KATH) provides the primary acute care facility in Kumasi but has limited specialist capacity. For any serious medical emergency – significant trauma, cardiac event, or condition requiring intensive care – the plan should default to evacuation to Accra (approximately 3-4 hours by road or 45 minutes by domestic flight). In Accra, Trust Hospital, Nyaho Medical Centre, and Korle Bu Teaching Hospital provide substantially better resources. For life-threatening conditions beyond Accra’s capability, international evacuation to London, Paris, or Dubai is the appropriate pathway. Pre-arranged medical evacuation insurance with a provider covering Ghana is non-negotiable for senior principals. CPO officers should hold current first-aid certification and carry a basic trauma kit.
