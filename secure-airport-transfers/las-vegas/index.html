Las Vegas is among the world’s busiest convention and business event destinations, drawing millions of corporate visitors each year to events that span technology, broadcasting, automotive, and financial services. The city’s infrastructure is built for volume, not discretion: the Strip corridor processes tens of thousands of business arrivals during peak convention weeks, and the transport environment at Harry Reid International Airport reflects that scale. For executives and senior delegates, the arrival window at LAS during a major convention is one of the more exposed moments of any business trip.

The transfer from LAS to a Strip hotel, Henderson corporate office, or Summerlin residence is straightforward under normal conditions but requires specific knowledge and advance planning during CES, NAB, AWS re:Invent, and similar peak periods. Unvetted rideshare and taxi operators concentrate at the arrivals hall during these events; PILB-licensed, pre-booked vehicles with confirmed driver identification remove the principal exposure at the kerb. For off-Strip destinations in the Fremont and Downtown area, route planning that avoids the higher-crime blocks requires local knowledge that generic rideshare applications do not provide.

Situational awareness around the Strip’s outdoor loading areas and event access points remains a standing operational consideration following the Route 91 Harvest Festival incident of October 2017. What this covers

Operational detail for Las Vegas

Harry Reid International Airport Route and Logistics

Harry Reid International Airport (LAS) is located 8km south of the Las Vegas Strip. The primary transfer route runs north on Las Vegas Boulevard or via Interstate 15 and Koval Lane, depending on the destination property's porte-cochere access. Under normal conditions the transfer takes 10 to 20 minutes. During major convention periods -- CES (January), NAB (April), AWS re:Invent (November/December), and SEMA (November) -- hundreds of simultaneous arrivals generate significant congestion on all airport-to-Strip corridors, extending journey times to 30 to 45 minutes. Terminal 1 (T1) handles domestic flights; Terminal 3 (T3) handles international and many domestic arrivals. Vehicle positioning is confirmed against the specific terminal for each flight.

Vetted Driver Compliance and Licensing

Nevada requires all security-qualified transfer drivers to hold an individual Guard Card issued by the Nevada Private Investigators Licensing Board (PILB). The operating company must hold a Private Patrol Operator (PPO) licence, also issued by the PILB. All commercial transfer vehicles operating in Nevada additionally require Nevada Department of Motor Vehicles commercial vehicle licensing and current Certificate of Authority from the Nevada Transportation Authority for private hire operations. CloseProtectionHire engages only PILB-licensed operators with current PPO credentials. Driver Guard Cards and vehicle authorisation documentation are verified prior to every transfer.

Convention Peak and Situational Awareness Briefing

Las Vegas hosts some of the world's largest conventions. CES (Consumer Electronics Show) draws more than 135,000 attendees; AWS re:Invent brings over 55,000; NAB Show attracts broadcast and media industry visitors from 160 countries (CES figures: CTA, January 2024; AWS re:Invent: Amazon, December 2023). During these periods, legitimate vehicle availability at LAS is severely constrained and unvetted rideshare and taxi operators fill the gap, creating social engineering, overcharging, and tracking risk for business principals. Pre-booked, identified, PILB-licensed vehicles are essential. The 1 October 2017 mass-shooting at the Route 91 Harvest Festival has generated lasting situational awareness requirements around all external loading areas, open-air event venues, and unscreened public gathering points along the Strip.

Off-Strip and Fremont District Route Planning

Principals travelling to off-Strip destinations require specific route planning. Downtown Las Vegas (Fremont Street Experience area) has higher crime rates than the Strip corridor and requires route knowledge to avoid the highest-risk blocks after dark. The Summerlin executive and residential district (northwest, 20 to 30 minutes from LAS) and Henderson (southeast, 20 to 25 minutes) are common corporate residential destinations requiring freeway routing distinct from the Strip approach. Las Vegas Convention Center (LVCC) is at the north end of the Strip and is reached more efficiently via Paradise Road than via Las Vegas Boulevard during major show periods. All destination-specific routing is confirmed at the booking stage.

Vehicle and Driver Standards

Principal-level transfers in Las Vegas use executive saloon or SUV vehicles appropriate to the destination. For Strip hotel properties with porte-cocheres, standard executive vehicle configurations are used. For higher-risk off-Strip or late-night transfers, armoured or up-armoured vehicles are available. All vehicles are air-conditioned to Nevada desert climate standards (summer temperatures regularly exceed 40 degrees Celsius). PILB Guard Card holders operate alongside the driver for principal-level transfers where CPO presence is included. Drivers hold Nevada commercial vehicle licensing and current PPO authorisation. Vehicle condition and insurance are verified before each engagement.

Hotel and Compound Confirmation

The principal Strip hotel properties -- Wynn Las Vegas, The Venetian, Bellagio, MGM Grand, and Mandalay Bay -- each have specific porte-cochere and vehicle access arrangements. Several have multiple entrances, and the correct approach for a principal-level transfer (avoiding public taxi queues, high foot-traffic areas, and paparazzi positioning during high-profile events) requires advance knowledge of each property's current access configuration. For corporate residential properties in Summerlin or Henderson, gate codes and access protocols are confirmed with the receiving party before departure. For LVCC meeting access, the vehicle entry and registration drop-off points for each hall are verified prior to the transfer.
