What this covers

Operational detail for Tbilisi

Tbilisi International Airport Collection

Tbilisi International Airport (TBS) is located approximately 18 kilometres east of central Tbilisi. The transfer to the Old Town, Mtatsminda, and Vera hotel zones takes 25 to 40 minutes on the David Agmashenebeli highway under normal conditions. Tbilisi traffic is moderate by regional standards; the downtown approach on Vazha-Pshavela Avenue and Chavchavadze Avenue is subject to morning and evening peak congestion. Inside-terminal collection with the driver positioned before the principal exits customs is standard. Vehicle and driver identity are confirmed inside the terminal before departure.

Georgia Ministry of Internal Affairs Driver Licensing

Security drivers in Georgia are governed by the Law on Private Detective and Security Activity (2010), administered by the Ministry of Internal Affairs. Security companies must hold Ministry of Internal Affairs licences and individual drivers require registration. The Georgian regulatory framework has developed significantly following post-Rose Revolution institutional reforms and is more structured than comparable South Caucasus jurisdictions. All our Tbilisi transfer partners hold current Ministry of Internal Affairs licensing; driver documentation is provided before each engagement.

South Ossetia and Abkhazia Exclusion Routing

The administrative boundary lines (ABLs) with Russian-occupied South Ossetia and Abkhazia are hard exclusion limits for all principal movements in Georgia. The South Ossetia ABL runs approximately 100 kilometres north of Tbilisi on the S1/E60 highway through the Gori corridor. Transfer route planning for any itinerary element approaching the Gori or Military Highway direction maps ABL distances and confirms that the route does not approach the exclusion zone. The standard TBS-to-city transfer does not pass near these areas, but any itinerary extension toward Kazbegi or the north requires this confirmation. Source: FCDO Georgia travel advisory, April 2026.

Political Protest Avoidance Routing

Georgia's post-2024 political environment has produced significant demonstration activity on Rustaveli Avenue and around the parliament building. Transfers from TBS on days with confirmed or potential demonstration activity use alternate east-west routing: Freedom Square approach and the Agmashenebeli Avenue corridor rather than the Rustaveli Avenue direct route to the Old Town and Vera hotel zones. The operations controller monitors demonstration timing and provides route confirmation before the transfer vehicle departs from TBS.

Old Town and South Caucasus Regional Coverage

Tbilisi's Old Town (Abanotubani, Narikala, Metekhi) and the Vera and Mtatsminda districts have cobbled streets and access restrictions in pedestrianised zones. Transfer drivers covering Old Town destinations are specifically familiar with the vehicle access points for each hotel property and the alternate approaches that avoid pedestrian-only sections. Tbilisi serves as the logistics hub for the South Caucasus; our Georgia partners can extend transfer coverage to Batumi (approximately 5 hours west), Kazbegi (approximately 2.5 hours north), and the land crossings to Armenia and Azerbaijan.
