The Murtala Muhammed International Airport arrival is the single highest-risk moment of any business trip to Lagos. The FCDO and US State Department both carry Nigeria travel advisories that specifically reference the airport environment and the subsequent vehicle movement to accommodation as priority security concerns for arriving foreign visitors. No other moment in a Lagos trip concentrates as many risk factors simultaneously: visible luggage, unfamiliar surroundings, transit from secured terminal to open road, and the high-incident LOS-to-Victoria-Island corridor.

The LOS arrival risk environment

Security sector incident reporting and NSCDC data consistently identify the Murtala Muhammed arrivals zone and the subsequent road journey as elevated-risk periods. The kerb outside the terminal is a documented location for approaches to foreign visitors; unbooked taxis from this zone are linked to express kidnapping incidents in sector reporting. The Apapa-Oshodi route to Victoria Island is among the most incident-affected airport approach corridors in West Africa.

What the secure transfer covers in Lagos

Pre-arranged inside-terminal collection; NSCDC-licensed driver with verified vehicle registration; counter-kidnap protocols throughout the transfer; operations controller tracking and emergency contact chain; armoured vehicle option for elevated-risk principals; and the return departure transfer on the same terms. These elements together address the specific risk environment of the LOS transfer, not a generic security checklist.

What this covers

Operational detail for Lagos

Murtala Muhammed International Terminal Collection

Pre-arranged collection at Murtala Muhammed International Airport (LOS) is the first security decision on any Lagos trip. The international terminal arrivals zone is a documented contact point for individuals targeting foreign travellers. Pre-arranged NSCDC-licensed drivers meet inside the terminal, before the principal reaches the kerb. This eliminates the primary exposure window.

LOS-to-Victoria Island Route Planning

The Murtala Muhammed-to-Victoria Island corridor, typically via the Apapa-Oshodi Expressway, is the most frequently reported high-incident route for arriving foreign visitors in Lagos. Route planning takes current traffic and incident data into account, with contingency alternatives for when the primary route is blocked. Night arrivals use a modified route protocol.

NSCDC-Licensed Drivers and Vetted Vehicles

All Lagos transfer drivers hold NSCDC licensing. Vehicle selection is appropriate to the threat profile: standard vetted vehicles for most corporate executives, armoured-spec for principals in energy, financial services, and other high-targeting sectors. Vehicle registration is confirmed with the operations controller before each transfer.

Armoured Vehicle Options

Armoured vehicle hire in Lagos is available through NSCDC-licensed specialist providers for principals for whom the threat assessment warrants armoured transport. Armoured-spec vehicles are particularly appropriate for executives in energy sector companies, financial services, and roles with senior government-adjacent exposure in the Nigerian market.

Kidnap and Express Crime Counter-Protocols

Lagos transfer protocols include specific counter-protocols for vehicle-based express crime and kidnap risk: information security around arrival times and accommodation location, counter-surveillance during transit, driver communication discipline, and pre-established emergency extraction contacts. The operations controller is briefed on these protocols before every transfer.

Departure Transfer Protocols from LOS

The return journey to Murtala Muhammed for departure is provided on the same security basis as the arrival transfer. Departure transfers include earlier departure timing to account for Lagos traffic unpredictability, pre-confirmation of airline check-in requirements, and hotel security liaison on departure. The departure transfer window is a documented risk period in Lagos requiring the same protocol as arrival.
