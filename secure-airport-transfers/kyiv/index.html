What this covers

Operational detail for Kyiv

Airspace Closed: No Airport Transfers

Ukrainian airspace is closed to civil aviation as of 2022 and remains closed as of 2026. There are no commercial airport transfers to or from Kyiv. This page covers overland entry coordination and in-city secure ground transfers for principals making essential visits to Ukraine. The FCDO advises against all travel to Ukraine. Overland transfer services exist to support those with genuine essential requirements: diplomatic personnel, humanitarian organisations, media, reconstruction contractors, and legal professionals with non-delegable obligations.

Overland Entry Route from Poland

The standard entry route to Kyiv for international principals is by air to Warsaw or Rzeszow in Poland, followed by overland transfer by rail or road to the Ukrainian border crossing and onward to Kyiv. The Kyiv rail connection from the Medyka or Shehyni crossing points to Kyiv central station has demonstrated notable resilience under wartime conditions. Rail is the preferred option over road for the Kyiv leg. Border crossing wait times are variable and must be planned conservatively: two to four hours on departure from Poland, shorter on the Ukraine-to-Poland return during most periods.

Wartime-Experienced Ground Transfer Team

In-city ground transfers in Kyiv are managed by drivers with active in-country presence since 2022. Wartime ground transfer capability includes: air raid shelter location mapping along the principal's route; strike-pattern awareness informing departure timing from the hotel and between venues; checkpoint navigation with appropriate documentation; and vehicle selection appropriate to a wartime operating environment. This operational knowledge cannot be replicated without sustained Ukraine presence.

Air Raid Response During Transfer

Air raid alerts during an active in-city transfer require immediate shelter in the nearest confirmed basement or reinforced building. The driver does not continue to the destination during an active alert under any circumstances. Pre-mapped shelter points along every planned transfer route are confirmed before movement begins. No schedule element takes priority over shelter compliance during an active alert.

Power and Communications Resilience

Kyiv experiences planned power outages of eight to sixteen hours daily and unplanned outages following strikes. Ground transfer planning accounts for the possibility of reduced traffic light and road lighting function during outages. Communications backup via satellite device is maintained for transfers near infrastructure targets where mobile network disruption may occur following a nearby strike. Operations controller monitoring is maintained via available communications channels throughout every transfer.

Departure Contingency Planning

Every Kyiv ground transfer assignment includes a documented departure contingency: pre-planned overland route from Kyiv to the Polish border, including rail contingency for road infrastructure disruption after strikes, and border crossing timing assessment. Departure planning is built in from before arrival, not assessed when departure is needed. There is no air evacuation option from Ukraine.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Fly to Warsaw Chopin Airport, transfer to the rail station (approximately 20 minutes), and take the overnight train service to Kyiv, which departs from Warsaw Centralna and arrives at Kyiv Pasazhyrsky in approximately 12 to 20 hours. Alternatively, drive or transfer to Rzeszow for the Przemysl-Medyka-Lviv route, which adds ground time but uses the southern crossing. We coordinate the Warsaw staging leg and the Kyiv arrival collection.

On alert, the driver immediately identifies and moves to the nearest confirmed shelter on the pre-mapped shelter route. Movement does not continue to the destination during an active alert. The vehicle is left in a safe position if shelter cannot be reached in the vehicle. After the all-clear is confirmed, movement to the destination resumes. Pre-mapping shelter points along every route before departure is a standard element of all Kyiv transfer preparation.

Foreign nationals should carry their full passport and travel documentation at all checkpoints. Male foreign nationals should be prepared to clarify their nationality and status clearly, given Ukrainian mobilisation obligation awareness at checkpoints. Our drivers are briefed on current checkpoint protocols and manage checkpoint interactions throughout every Kyiv assignment.

Departure by rail to the Polish border is the standard plan. The rail route to Przemysl via Lviv is the most common route; the Chelm route via Kovel is an alternative. Road departure to the Polish border is the contingency if rail services are disrupted by infrastructure strikes. All Kyiv assignments include a departure plan and contingency before arrival.
