Zvartnots International Airport (EVN) sits 12km west of central Yerevan and handles a growing volume of European business connections and regional diplomatic traffic. The FCDO advises a high degree of caution in Armenia (2026), reflecting the regional security context, and the US State Department maintains a Level 2 designation (2026). Informal taxi operators are present in the external vehicle area at Zvartnots, and pre-arranged vetted collection inside the arrivals hall is the appropriate standard for corporate and diplomatic principals. Our team positions ahead of each flight, tracks arrival in real time, and coordinates the 12km transfer into central Yerevan using a vetted, locally licensed driver in an appropriate vehicle.

For principals operating in sensitive sectors or with elevated personal profiles, surveillance detection protocols are applied from the point of collection. This involves counter-surveillance vehicle positioning and route variation across the EVN to city centre corridor, and is offered as a standard component for higher-risk bookings. Medical contingency is referenced to Nairi Medical Centre in the city, with medevac routes to Tbilisi and Istanbul pre-identified for evacuation requirements. All transfers are planned against current FCDO and US State Department guidance, and route assessments are refreshed before each booking.

Our close protection officers in Yerevan provide in-country escort beyond the transfer, and our bodyguard hire service in Yerevan can be combined with the airport transfer as a full arrival-to-departure programme. The Yerevan city security profile provides a broader operating environment overview for principals planning their visit.

Low-Profile Vetted Vehicles

Business Travel and Diplomatic Support

Route Assessment and Timing

The 12km route between EVN and central Yerevan is assessed before each transfer against current FCDO Armenia travel advice (2026) and US State Department Level 2 designation (2026). Route and timing choices account for congestion and any reported incidents.

Medical Contingency Planning
