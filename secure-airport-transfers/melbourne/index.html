What this covers

Operational detail for Melbourne

Melbourne Airport and Terminal Layout

Melbourne Airport (MEL, also known as Tullamarine) sits 23 kilometres northwest of the Melbourne CBD. The airport operates four terminals: Terminal 1 handles all international arrivals and departures, Terminal 2 serves Qantas domestic operations, and Terminals 3 and 4 cover Jetstar, Virgin Australia, and other domestic carriers. There is no direct rail connection between the airport and the city centre; the SkyBus public coach service operates but is not appropriate for principals requiring a controlled, discreet transfer. Our drivers position in the authorised corporate pick-up area rather than the general taxi rank, reducing kerbside wait time and the principal's exposure in the terminal forecourt.

CityLink Routing to Melbourne CBD

The standard transfer route from Melbourne Airport to the CBD follows the Tullamarine Freeway (M2) and CityLink toll road, delivering principals to the Southbank hotel precinct, the Collins Street financial district, or St Kilda Road in 25 to 40 minutes under typical conditions. During peak commuter periods (7 to 9am and 4 to 7pm weekdays), Tullamarine Freeway traffic can extend this to 55 to 65 minutes. All CityLink passages are covered by E-Tag to avoid manual toll payment. The Western Ring Road (M80) provides an alternative corridor for principals travelling to the western suburbs or returning to the airport for departure. Routes to St Kilda and South Yarra are typically 35 to 50 minutes via CityLink and Domain Tunnel or the City Link Burnley Tunnel.

Victorian Licensing Under the Private Security Act 2004

Security personnel providing personal protection and escorted transfer services in Victoria must hold an individual security licence issued by Consumer Affairs Victoria (CAV) under the Private Security Act 2004 (Vic). Relevant licence categories include security officer, crowd controller, and bodyguard, with bodyguard being the appropriate category for principals requiring personal protection during transfer. Security companies providing these services must also hold an operator's licence from CAV. Licence renewals are managed by CAV on a multi-year cycle; current licence status can be verified on the CAV public register. Our Melbourne transfer network partners hold current CAV individual and operator licences, available for review before travel.

Private Aviation at Essendon Airport

Principals arriving by private or charter aircraft in Melbourne typically use Essendon Airport, located 13 kilometres north of the CBD, which serves as Melbourne's general aviation hub. Heritage Airlines and Execujet are the primary fixed base operators at Essendon. The transfer from Essendon to Melbourne CBD via the Tullamarine Freeway and CityLink takes approximately 20 to 30 minutes, shorter than from Tullamarine. Our operations centre coordinates with Essendon FBO ground staff to confirm arrival time, parking bay location, and vehicle staging area before the principal's flight lands. This pre-coordination allows for a smooth airside-to-vehicle transition without public terminal exposure. Essendon-based transfers are available on the same advance notice requirements as Tullamarine.

Major Events: Australian Open, Grand Prix, and AFL Grand Final

Melbourne hosts a number of events that generate significant HNWI and corporate principal traffic requiring transfer planning. The Australian Open tennis (January) and the Formula 1 Australian Grand Prix (March) both draw an international principal population, with hotels across Southbank, Collins Street, and St Kilda Road at capacity. The AFL Grand Final (September) is the largest domestic event, with elevated road activity across the CityLink corridor on Grand Final eve and morning. During these periods, transfer times from Tullamarine can increase by 30 to 45 minutes and hotel access routes may be diverted. Our operations team reviews VicRoads and Melbourne City Council event traffic management plans before transfers in event periods and adjusts route briefings accordingly.

Coordination with Melbourne Hotel and Corporate Security

Melbourne's central hotel corridor includes properties on Collins Street, Flinders Lane, and the Southbank precinct. Many five-star properties have designated corporate vehicle access points separate from the main lobby, allowing a controlled transfer handover away from the public forecourt. For principals with an in-house or contracted close protection team already in Melbourne, our transfer service provides a written handover note covering vehicle details, driver name, arrival time, and any observations from the Tullamarine run. Post-transfer continuity briefings are standard for clients on multi-day Melbourne programmes. Corporate clients managing a delegation across multiple flights can request a coordinated multi-vehicle transfer plan from the operations centre.
