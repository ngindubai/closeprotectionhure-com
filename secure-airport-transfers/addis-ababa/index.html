Operational detail for Addis Ababa

Bole International Airport Collection Protocol

Bole International Airport (ADD) is located approximately 6 kilometres south-east of Addis Ababa's Bole Road hotel and business corridor. Despite the relatively short transfer distance, FCDO Ethiopia travel advice (April 2026) and US Embassy Addis Ababa security reporting document that the airport arrival zone and the Bole Road corridor carry specific risks for foreign arrivals. Our collection team positions inside the arrivals hall before the principal exits baggage claim. Kerb exposure at Bole International is avoided entirely through inside-terminal collection and a pre-positioned vetted vehicle.

Checkpoint and Detention Risk Management

FCDO Ethiopia travel advice (April 2026) documents that Ethiopian security services conduct security operations and checkpoints across Addis Ababa, including at transport nodes. FCDO specifically notes that ethnic Tigrayans have been prevented from boarding flights at Bole International, and that arbitrary detention risk exists for foreign nationals. Transfer protocols for Addis Ababa include driver briefing on current checkpoint locations, appropriate document preparation for the principal, and an operations controller who monitors any checkpoint delay and holds British Embassy emergency contacts (telephone +251 11 617 0100).

Street Crime Risk: Bole Medhanealem and Bole Atlas Zones

FCDO Ethiopia travel advice (April 2026) identifies Bole Medhanealem and Bole Atlas as documented hotspots for mugging and street crime targeting foreign nationals, including knifepoint and choking attacks. Criminal groups in these zones use distraction techniques to identify and approach targets. The transfer route from Bole International to principal hotel addresses on Bole Road and in the Kazanchis area traverses these zones. Route selection and vehicle protocols for Addis Ababa transfers minimise exposure to pedestrian-accessible segments where the principal would be visible and approachable.

Regional Conflict Context

Ethiopia is engaged in armed conflict on multiple fronts as of April 2026, including resumed fighting in Tigray and ongoing OLF-Shene operations in Oromia. While Addis Ababa itself is not a direct conflict zone, FCDO advises that conflict-related disruption including security force surges, protest activity, and unpredictable road closures can affect movement within the capital. The operations controller monitors current security force activity and road-closure patterns affecting the Bole Road corridor and the transfer route from ADD to the principal's destination.

Ethiopia Private Security Regulatory Context

Ethiopia's private security sector is regulated by the Ministry of Justice and operates within a framework that has developed significantly since the 2010s but retains less formal individual-officer credential verification than regional peers. All transfer personnel are employed through operators with current business registration and whose drivers hold the appropriate category driving licence and commercial insurance. For Addis Ababa, operator credentials are provided to clients on request, and the pre-engagement briefing covers the current security environment at the time of travel.

Operations Controller and British Embassy Emergency Contacts

An operations controller tracks every Addis Ababa transfer from Bole International collection to destination. The controller holds British Embassy Addis Ababa emergency contacts (+251 11 617 0100) and US Embassy Addis Ababa duty officer contacts, monitors current security incidents on the transfer corridor, and maintains communication with the driver throughout. Detention of the principal or an unplanned stop triggers an immediate escalation protocol. For principals at elevated risk due to their professional profile, the operations controller can coordinate advance notification to the principal's embassy if the itinerary warrants it.
