What this covers

Operational detail for Colombo

Bandaranaike International Airport Collection

Bandaranaike International Airport (CMB) is located approximately 30 kilometres north of Colombo in Katunayake. The transfer to the Colombo 3 (Colpetty) and Colombo 7 (Cinnamon Gardens) business and hotel zones takes 45 to 75 minutes under normal conditions, depending on traffic on the E03 expressway and Galle Road approaches to the city. Arrivals at peak hours, particularly early morning, involve slower urban approaches from the expressway terminus. Inside-terminal collection with a pre-positioned vehicle is standard for all principal arrivals.

Post-Easter-Attack Hotel Arrival Protocol

The April 2019 Easter Sunday bombings targeted international hotels in Colombo by directing attackers at venues specifically associated with foreign guests. Hotel security at international properties has been significantly upgraded since 2019: vehicle searches at hotel entrances, visitor screening, and enhanced access control are now standard. Secure transfer drivers in Colombo are briefed on the specific vehicle entry procedures at each principal hotel, and transfers include a pre-confirmed arrival protocol that complies with the hotel's post-2019 security measures. Source: FCDO Sri Lanka travel advisory, April 2026.

Colombo City Route Management

Colombo's main hotel and business zones (Colombo 3, Colombo 7) are accessed via the Galle Road coastal approach from the south or the expressway terminus at Kadawatha and city approach from the north. Traffic in central Colombo is dense during morning and evening peaks. Secure transfer drivers are selected for current route knowledge covering the hotel zone approaches, the alternate routes during peak-hour congestion, and the specific access procedures for the Port City Colombo development zone. Colombo 10-16 peripheral areas are avoided as transit routes.

Port City Colombo Transfer Operations

Port City Colombo is an adjacent development zone with managed vehicle access and security screening at entry points. Transfers to meetings in Port City require the driver to know the specific entry point procedures, vehicle documentation requirements, and Port City Authority protocols. These differ from standard Colombo CBD hotel arrival procedures. Pre-confirmation of Port City access requirements is a standard element of the transfer brief for any itinerary that includes Port City Colombo as a destination.

Political Instability and Contingency Routing

Sri Lanka's 2022 political crisis, which involved protesters storming the presidential palace, demonstrated that movement in central Colombo can be disrupted by civil unrest with limited warning. Transfer contingency planning includes alternate routes that avoid the areas around the President's House on Janadhipathi Mawatha and the parliament building at Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte during periods of elevated political tension. The operations controller monitors political developments during the transfer period and advises the driver on route adjustment in real time.
