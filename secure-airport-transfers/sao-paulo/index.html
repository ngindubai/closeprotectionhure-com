Guarulhos International Airport is the gateway through which most international business visitors arrive in Sao Paulo, and the transfer from Guarulhos to the city’s financial districts is one of the most clearly documented risk windows in any Brazilian business trip. OSAC Sao Paulo crime reporting and security sector incident data consistently identify the GRU arrival and the Ayrton Senna Highway approach as elevated-risk periods for foreign executives. The express kidnapping threat – which in Sao Paulo can involve vehicle robbery during the transfer itself – makes the quality of the pre-arranged transfer the first material security decision of any Sao Paulo visit.

The Guarulhos transfer environment

GRU’s three passenger terminals handle Sao Paulo’s international traffic. The arrivals areas at T2 and T3 are managed environments, but the kerb zone outside and the first kilometres of the Ayrton Senna Highway are where most documented incidents affecting arriving foreign visitors occur. Inside-terminal collection by a Policia Federal-licensed driver, with vehicle confirmed before boarding and operations controller tracking throughout, is the correct response to this specific environment.

Congonhas and the domestic connection

Congonhas Airport (CGH) in south-central Sao Paulo is the domestic hub for routes connecting Sao Paulo with Rio de Janeiro, Brasilia, Belo Horizonte, and other major Brazilian cities. For principals arriving on domestic connections before an international departure or after an internal Brazilian leg, Congonhas pre-arranged collection follows the same driver and operations controller standard as Guarulhos.

What this covers

Operational detail for Sao Paulo

Guarulhos International Collection Protocol

Guarulhos International Airport (GRU), located in the municipality of Guarulhos approximately 25 km north-east of central Sao Paulo, operates three passenger terminals (T1, T2, T3). International arrivals primarily use T2 and T3. Pre-arranged inside-terminal collection by a Policia Federal-licensed driver is the correct protocol. The GRU arrivals area and the Ayrton Senna Highway approach are documented in OSAC Sao Paulo reporting as elevated-risk locations for vehicle robbery targeting airport arrivals.

Congonhas Airport Domestic Connections

Congonhas Airport (CGH) in the Congonhas district of south-central Sao Paulo handles domestic routes. For principals arriving on domestic connections from Rio, Brasilia, or other Brazilian cities, pre-arranged collection at Congonhas follows the same driver and operations controller standard as Guarulhos. The CGH-to-Faria Lima transfer is one of the shortest airport-to-financial-district transfers in Sao Paulo.

Policia Federal Licensed Drivers

All Sao Paulo transfer drivers operate under a Policia Federal-licensed private security company, authorised under Lei 7.102 of 1983. Vehicles are maintained executive-spec and appropriate to the principal's threat profile. For principals requiring armoured transport, armoured vehicle hire is available through specialist Policia Federal-licensed operators.

Ayrton Senna Highway Counter-Protocol Routing

The Ayrton Senna Highway (SP-070) from Guarulhos to the Marginal Tiete is the primary route for GRU transfers into central Sao Paulo and Faria Lima. This corridor is documented in OSAC and security sector reports as a location where vehicle robbery targeting identified airport arrivals occurs, particularly at night. Transfer routing takes current incident data into account, with contingency options and night-specific route modifications.

Express Kidnap Counter-Protocols

Express kidnapping (sequestro relampago) remains one of the most documented crime types for foreign executives in Sao Paulo. Transfer protocols specifically address this: inside-terminal-only collection, driver identity pre-confirmation before boarding, no display of destination in transit on visible devices, operations controller tracking throughout, and pre-established emergency contacts.

Viracopos International Transfers

Viracopos International Airport (VCP) in Campinas handles cargo and some low-cost international routes. Transfer from VCP to Sao Paulo's corporate zones is approximately 100 km and 60 to 90 minutes. For principals using Viracopos for charter or specific airlines, pre-arranged transfers follow the same NSCDC-licensed driver and operations controller standard, with the extended route accounted for in departure timing.
