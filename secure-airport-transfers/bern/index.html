Bern occupies a unique position among European capitals: compact, discreet, and at the centre of Swiss federal and diplomatic life. As the seat of the Federal Assembly, the Federal Council, and a dense concentration of international missions and NGO headquarters, it receives a steady flow of government ministers, senior diplomats, and corporate principals attending meetings that are often deliberately low-profile. The transfer from an arrival airport to the correct property in central Bern requires familiarity with a city that is navigated more by knowledge than by distance.

The most common transfer scenario is not from Bern Airport (BRN), which serves a limited range of routes, but from Zurich Airport (ZRH) 90 minutes to the northeast, or Geneva Airport (GVA) two hours to the southwest. Coordinating these longer transfers – managing flight timing, Swiss motorway peak hours, and the specific access requirements of the destination property – is the operational substance of a secure airport transfer to Bern. Drivers with verified experience on these routes and current knowledge of the Bundeshaus-area access arrangements are the standard for all principal-level bookings.

For the full picture of Bern’s security environment, logistics, and key venue access, see the Bern city guide. If in-city close protection is required alongside the airport transfer, our close protection officers in Bern are available to provide continuous cover throughout your visit.

What this covers

Operational detail for Bern

Airport Routes and Logistics: BRN, ZRH, and GVA

Bern Airport (BRN) is located 9km from the city centre and serves primarily domestic and leisure routes; the transfer takes 10 to 15 minutes under normal conditions. However, a significant proportion of principals and delegations arrive at Zurich Airport (ZRH), 120km northeast (90 minutes by road or 60 minutes by direct rail), or Geneva Airport (GVA), 165km southwest (approximately 2 hours by road). CloseProtectionHire plans and coordinates transfers from all three airports to Bern city. For ZRH and GVA arrivals, road transfer is the preferred option for principals with luggage or sensitive equipment; rail is an alternative for smaller parties. Transfer times from ZRH and GVA are discussed and confirmed at the booking stage, with appropriate schedule margin for Swiss motorway (A1 and A12) peak-hour conditions.

Vetted Driver Compliance and Licensing

Private security and private hire operators in Switzerland are regulated under the Concordat on Private Security (CIPES) at the intercantonal level, with Bern cantonal transport licensing applying to private hire vehicles operating within the canton. All drivers engaged by CloseProtectionHire hold current licensing under the CIPES framework and the applicable cantonal transport authority requirements. Swiss driving licence standards are among the most stringent in Europe; all drivers are verified for current licensing, vehicle insurance, and commercial operation authorisation before engagement. For diplomatic and government-adjacent transfers, drivers are additionally briefed on the access protocols relevant to Bern's protected zones.

Bundeshaus District Access and Restricted Zone Briefing

Bern's compact city centre includes the Bundeshaus (Federal Palace), the main diplomatic quarter, and several restricted vehicle access zones. Vehicle access to the Bundeshaus area and surrounding streets is controlled and may be subject to temporary restriction during parliamentary sessions (typically two weeks per quarter, with extraordinary sessions called at short notice). Principals attending meetings in or adjacent to the Bundeshaus, at federal ministries, or at embassies in the Kirchenfeldquartier require drivers with current knowledge of these access arrangements and alternative drop-off approaches. Pre-transfer route confirmation is standard for all government and diplomatic destinations in Bern.

Low-Profile Transfer Strategy for Diplomatic Principals

Bern's status as the Swiss federal capital and seat of the diplomatic corps means it hosts a disproportionate number of high-profile principals relative to its size. While Switzerland's overall security environment is low risk, discretion and a low-profile vehicle selection are the standard for government and diplomatic clients. Unmarked, executive saloon or SUV vehicles are used in preference to conspicuous transport. The city's compact geography means that hotel drop-off points at the Bellevue Palace, Schweizerhof, and Hotel Bristol are in close proximity to the Bundeshaus and require specific knowledge of the restricted parking and drop-off arrangements applicable to each property.

Vehicle and Driver Standards

All transfers in Bern use executive-specification saloon or SUV vehicles, maintained to current Swiss roadworthiness standards, with tinted windows and appropriate luggage capacity. Drivers hold full Swiss commercial transport licensing and CIPES-compliant private security authorisation where applicable. English, French, and German language capability is confirmed for all drivers engaged for international client transfers. For ZRH and GVA arrivals, the vehicle and driver are positioned to meet the principal at the international arrivals hall with appropriate identification, and sufficient time is built into the schedule to accommodate Swiss customs and baggage processing.

Hotel and Compound Confirmation

Bern's principal hotels are located in the compact central zone close to the Bundeshaus and main diplomatic district. The Bellevue Palace (+41 31 320 45 45) is the primary choice for government and diplomatic visitors, located directly adjacent to the Federal Palace. The Hotel Schweizerhof Bern and Hotel Bristol are the principal alternatives. All three properties are within a short walk of the Bundeshaus but have specific vehicle access and drop-off arrangements that differ from standard hotel arrivals. For embassies, government buildings, or private compounds, entry protocols and vehicle identification requirements are confirmed in advance with the receiving party.
