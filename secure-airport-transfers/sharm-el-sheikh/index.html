Sharm el-Sheikh is Egypt’s principal international resort on the Sinai Peninsula and one of the Middle East’s major conference and hospitality destinations. Its position at the southern tip of the Sinai, where the Gulf of Aqaba meets the Red Sea, has made it an internationally recognised venue for diplomatic summits and business events, as well as a major leisure destination. Sharm el-Sheikh International Airport (SSH) handles direct charter and scheduled services from Europe, with the airport approximately 10km from the main Naama Bay resort zone.

The security classification for Sharm el-Sheikh sits in a different category from most international resort destinations. The FCDO maintains a high terrorism threat level for Egypt, and the Sinai Peninsula carries a specific and documented extremist threat from Wilayat Sinai (Islamic State’s Sinai Province), which was responsible for the destruction of Metrojet Flight 9268 at SSH in October 2015. While the resort zone of south Sinai has a distinct risk profile from the north of the peninsula, where FCDO advises against all travel, the terrorism context is live and requires that transfers be arranged through MOI-registered operators with current security clearances, not arranged ad hoc at the airport on arrival.

Our Sharm el-Sheikh transfer service is delivered exclusively through Egyptian Ministry of Interior-registered operators with individually vetted drivers. Every transfer includes confirmation of the current airport security environment, routing via the established airport road to the resort zone, and a post-arrival check-in to confirm safe delivery. For corporate delegations attending conferences at the resort properties, we provide multi-leg arrival coordination across the transfer period, with a single operations contact managing all individual arrivals. Full details of the Sharm el-Sheikh security environment are available in our Sharm el-Sheikh city guide, and close protection for the duration of a stay can be arranged through our close protection officers in Sharm el-Sheikh service.

What this covers

Operational detail for Sharm el-Sheikh

Terrorism Risk and FCDO Advisory Context

The FCDO maintains a high terrorism threat warning for Egypt, with particular reference to the Sinai Peninsula, of which Sharm el-Sheikh is the principal international gateway. The 2015 bombing of Metrojet Flight 9268 shortly after departure from Sharm el-Sheikh International Airport (SSH), which killed all 224 people on board, demonstrated the severity of the threat in this environment and led to the suspension of UK commercial flights to SSH that year. Flights have partially resumed since, subject to enhanced airport security protocols, but the terrorism risk classification for the Sinai region remains in force. Pre-arranged transfers using Egyptian MOI-registered operators are an essential element of risk reduction for all arrivals.

Egyptian MOI-Registered Operator Requirement

Private security operators in Egypt are required to be registered with and approved by the Egyptian Ministry of Interior (MOI). In a terrorism-context destination such as Sharm el-Sheikh, MOI registration is not a bureaucratic technicality: it is the mechanism through which operators are vetted for associations with extremist networks, criminal history, and suitability for operating in a high-security environment. Our Sharm el-Sheikh transfer partners are MOI-registered, individually vetted, and operate within the Egyptian regulatory framework. We do not use unlicensed or unregistered operators in Egypt.

Airport Security Environment and Arrival Protocols

SSH airport operates under enhanced security protocols established following the 2015 incident, including additional screening procedures and heightened physical security presence. Arrivals are subject to standard Egyptian border control plus the airport-specific measures in force at the time of travel. Our drivers collect clients from the designated arrivals point and move directly to the secured vehicle, avoiding extended time in the open airport environs. For clients arriving on commercial services resuming from the UK, pre-arranged transfer with a named driver is standard practice and strongly advised.

Naama Bay and Resort Zone Transfer Routes

Sharm el-Sheikh International Airport is located approximately 10km from the Naama Bay resort zone, the primary destination for international visitors. The transfer passes through the airport road network and into the resort strip, a short journey by most standards but one that takes place in the context of an elevated terrorism threat environment. Route familiarity and knowledge of current security checkpoint positions along the airport road are held by our Egyptian operators as part of their standing operational briefing.

Sinai Peninsula Risk Zones

The FCDO advises against all travel to the north of Sinai governorate and against all but essential travel to areas outside the resort towns of the south Sinai. Sharm el-Sheikh sits in the south Sinai resort zone, which carries a different risk profile from the north of the peninsula, but remains within the broader Sinai security environment. Our transfer service operates within the resort zone and does not cover movement into north Sinai. Clients with business or operational requirements in other parts of Sinai should seek specialist advice before travel.

Corporate and Security Sector Visitor Support

Sharm el-Sheikh hosts international conferences and summit events, including several historic high-profile diplomatic meetings, as well as a substantial conference and corporate events industry associated with the resort hotels. Conference delegates, corporate event participants, and senior executives attending business events in Sharm el-Sheikh form a significant part of our transfer client base. For multi-delegate arrivals on different flights, we provide coordinated collection logistics with a single operations contact managing all arrivals on behalf of the organising team.
