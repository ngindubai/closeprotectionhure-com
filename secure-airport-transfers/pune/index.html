Pune has developed from a manufacturing and defence-sector city to one of India’s primary IT and financial services centres, with Hinjawadi now ranking among the largest IT campuses in South Asia. Pune Lohegaon International Airport serves this expanded business community with connections to major Indian hubs and a growing number of international routes. The airport is compact and the transfer distances are shorter than in Mumbai, but the Pune transfer window still carries the specific risks common to India’s business travel environment.

The Pune transfer context

PSARA 2005 compliance is the baseline check for any private security transport operator in Maharashtra. The specific risks at PNQ are concentrated in the uncontrolled contact zone outside arrivals and in the peak-hour congestion on the Koregaon Park and Hinjawadi corridors.

What secure transfers cover

A Pune secure airport transfer covers: inside-arrivals-hall collection at PNQ by a PSARA Maharashtra-licensed driver; operations controller tracking from PNQ to destination; real-time route management for Koregaon Park, Hinjawadi, or central Pune; and return transfers on the same terms.

Operational detail for Pune

PNQ Lohegaon Collection Protocol

Pune Lohegaon International Airport (PNQ) is approximately 10 kilometres from Koregaon Park and 15 kilometres from the central business district via the Nagar Road. Pre-arranged collection inside the arrivals hall covers the standard transfer window from baggage claim to vehicle. The airport access road and taxi queue area at PNQ are subject to unsolicited approach by unvetted operators; inside-hall collection eliminates this contact.

PSARA 2005 Maharashtra Compliance

Private security operators in Maharashtra must hold registration under the Private Security Agencies Regulation Act (PSARA) 2005 and be licensed by the Maharashtra State Controlling Authority. All drivers providing protective transport in Pune hold current PSARA Maharashtra registration with verified background checks. PSARA licence documentation is available on request.

Koregaon Park Corridor Management

Koregaon Park is Pune's primary expatriate residential and corporate hospitality district. The PNQ-to-Koregaon Park route via Nagar Road and the East Street junction is straightforward under off-peak conditions but subject to significant congestion during morning and evening peak hours. Transfer timing management accounts for the 20 to 40 minute range on this corridor.

Hinjawadi IT Campus Transfer Protocol

Hinjawadi Phase 1, 2, and 3 -- Pune's primary IT and technology campus district -- are 25 to 30 kilometres from PNQ via the Pune-Mumbai Expressway ring road. Transfer from PNQ to Hinjawadi is typically 40 to 60 minutes under off-peak conditions. Morning peak congestion on the Baner and Balewadi corridors feeding Hinjawadi Phase 2 and 3 can extend this significantly. The operations controller monitors Hinjawadi campus access conditions.

Kalyani Nagar and Kharadi Corridor

Kalyani Nagar and Kharadi are secondary business and residential districts for executive visitors. The PNQ-to-Kalyani Nagar route is among the shortest airport-to-destination corridors in Pune at approximately 8 kilometres. Kharadi, which houses a substantial IT Special Economic Zone, is 12 to 15 kilometres from PNQ via the Ring Road.

Operations Controller Throughout

An operations controller monitors every transfer from PNQ collection to accommodation or campus destination. Flight delay tracking adjusts collection timing automatically. Route changes are communicated in real time based on traffic and access conditions. Emergency contacts with local private security and medical response are maintained throughout every transfer.
