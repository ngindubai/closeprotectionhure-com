Buenos Aires presents a well-documented transfer risk landscape for arriving foreign nationals. The OSAC Buenos Aires Crime and Safety Report 2024 identifies the Autopista Ricchieri corridor between Ministro Pistarini International Airport (EZE) and the city centre as an active zone for express kidnapping, with curbside waiting at the international terminal named as a specific vulnerability.

Our secure airport transfer service addresses this through inside-terminal collection, planned route variation on the Ricchieri and General Paz, and continuous operations controller oversight from wheels-down to hotel arrival. All drivers hold the credencial habilitante required under Ley 12.297 and the CABA municipal transport regime. Transfers from Jorge Newbery Aeroparque (AEP) follow the same professional collection standard, with discreet vehicles and route awareness along Costanera Norte.

For broader security planning during your stay, see our Buenos Aires close protection overview and close protection officers in Buenos Aires. Piquetero blockade contingency and real-time route management are included in every transfer booking at no additional charge.

What this covers

Operational detail for Buenos Aires

Inside-Terminal Collection at Ezeiza (EZE)

Ministro Pistarini International Airport sits 35km south of Buenos Aires city centre on the Autopista Ricchieri. OSAC's Buenos Aires Crime and Safety Report 2024 identifies curbside waiting outside the international terminal as a documented vulnerability for arriving foreign nationals. Our operatives collect clients inside the arrivals hall, carrying a discreet name board, and escort them directly to a pre-positioned vehicle parked off the exposed curbside zone. This approach reduces the window during which a principal is standing unaccompanied with luggage in a publicly visible position.

Ezeiza Corridor Route Management

The Autopista Ricchieri and the General Paz ring road are the primary arteries linking EZE to central Buenos Aires. OSAC's 2024 report documents these corridors as active express kidnapping risk zones for foreign nationals arriving in Argentina. Our operations controller monitors the route in real time, applying planned variation points along the Ricchieri and alternative routing via Autopista Presidente Perón where the threat picture or traffic conditions warrant. Clients are briefed on the route and estimated journey time before departure.

Aeroparque (AEP) Transfer Operations

Jorge Newbery Aeroparque serves domestic and regional flights adjacent to the Palermo district, approximately 8km north of the city centre. Transfer risk at AEP is lower than at Ezeiza, but the airport's proximity to major arterials and the potential for opportunistic approaches near the terminal still justify professional collection. Our teams use discreet unmarked vehicles and vary approach routes along Avenida Costanera Norte to avoid predictable patterns.

Credencial Habilitante Compliant Drivers

All drivers operating within the Buenos Aires Metropolitan Area are required to hold a credencial habilitante under Ley 12.297 and the applicable CABA municipal transport regime. Our driver network holds the appropriate Buenos Aires transport licences, with regular compliance checks against the relevant provincial and municipal registers. Clients travelling on corporate duty-of-care programmes or insurance-backed security arrangements receive full driver documentation on request.

Piquetero Blockade Contingency Planning

Piquetero protest groups are a documented feature of Buenos Aires street politics. Blockades can affect the Ezeiza approach roads, the General Paz ring road, and Avenida 9 de Julio at relatively short notice. Our operations controller monitors Buenos Aires traffic management feeds and open-source protest intelligence to identify developing blockades before they affect the transfer route. Alternative routing plans are prepared in advance for all standard transfer corridors so diversions can be implemented without delay.

GPS Tracking and Operations Controller Support

Every transfer is tracked by a dedicated operations controller from vehicle despatch to client drop-off. The controller maintains two-way communication with the driver throughout the journey, monitors route progress against expected timings, and holds contingency contact details for the receiving venue. Incident logs are produced for all transfers, providing a documentary record suitable for corporate travel security programmes and post-incident review.
