Havana is a city that requires a specific understanding of its transport environment before any transfer is arranged. Cuba’s approach to foreign visitor transport is unlike any other destination on this network: the Ministry of Interior (MININT) controls all transport arrangements for foreign nationals, and the only legal path to a private hire vehicle is through state-sanctioned operators. This is not an inconvenience to be worked around; it is the legal and operational framework that provides the only reliable, insured, and accountable transfer service for business and diplomatic visitors.

The arrival hall at Terminal 2 and Terminal 3 at José Martí Airport concentrates jinetero activity targeting disembarking passengers. This is a known, documented, and persistent feature of the airport environment. Pre-arranged transfers through MININT-authorised operators – Cubacar, Transgaviota, or Rex – with a confirmed driver name, vehicle, and identification board remove this exposure entirely. The client arrives knowing exactly who to look for and exactly where to look; all other approaches are declined. The transfer to Vedado or Miramar takes 20 to 35 minutes in a maintained, air-conditioned vehicle from an authorised fleet, without the mechanical reliability concerns that affect the general Havana transport environment.

Luggage security during the airport-to-hotel transit is the secondary operational focus: devices and high-value items travel in the passenger compartment, and luggage loading is supervised by the transfer driver. Operational detail for Havana

Aeropuerto Internacional José Martí Route and Logistics

Aeropuerto Internacional José Martí (HAV) is located 20km southwest of the Vedado and Miramar business visitor districts. Terminal 2 handles most charter and specialist arrivals; Terminal 3 handles scheduled international airlines including regular services from Europe, Canada, and Latin America. The primary route follows the Autopista Nacional northeast toward the city, then transitions to Via Blanca or Avenida Linea/23 depending on whether the destination is Vedado (approximately 20 to 25km from the airport, 25 to 35 minutes) or Miramar (approximately 12km via the Malecon and 5th Avenue, 20 to 30 minutes). Journey times can be extended by vehicle mechanical issues -- the average age of Cuba's general transport fleet is high -- and by periodic checkpoint operations on the airport approach roads. Pre-arranged transfers use maintained vehicles from licensed operators that are not subject to the mechanical reliability issues of the general fleet.

MININT-Authorised Transport and State-Sanctioned Operators

Cuba's transport market for foreign visitors is distinctive: the Ministry of Interior (MININT) controls all transport operations involving foreign nationals, and the only legally permissible entities for arranging private hire transport for foreign visitors are state-sanctioned operators. These include Cubacar, Transgaviota, and Rex -- all state-owned or state-supervised entities with licensed fleets and drivers authorised for foreign visitor transport. There is no independent commercial security driver market operating outside this framework; any arrangement made through a non-state operator places the client and the operator in an irregular legal position under Cuban law and removes the protections that come with state-sanctioned transport (maintained vehicle, insured driver, identified booking). All CloseProtectionHire transfers in Havana are made exclusively through authorised state-sanctioned operators.

Airport Arrival Hall and Jinetero Activity Briefing

The arrival hall at José Martí Airport is a known concentration point for jineteros (touts) who approach disembarking foreign passengers offering transport, accommodation, and guide services. These individuals are not licensed transport operators and are not authorised to arrange transport for foreign visitors under Cuban law. Approaches are typically persistent and take advantage of the disorientation that accompanies arrival in a new country with an unfamiliar transport market. Clients arriving under a pre-arranged CloseProtectionHire transfer are met in the arrivals hall by an identified driver from an authorised operator holding a name board; this pre-arranged meeting point and the driver's identity are confirmed to the client before departure. The client should decline all approaches in the arrival hall before locating the identified pre-arranged driver.

Device and Luggage Security During Transfer

Havana's transfer environment for foreign visitors includes a specific petty crime risk that is concentrated in the tourism sector rather than in organised criminality. Luggage handling -- at the airport and during hotel check-in -- is the primary point of risk for bag-switching and item theft. Devices in outer pockets, laptop bags placed in the car boot rather than the passenger compartment, and unsecured valuables during the arrival process are the common loss points. Clients on CloseProtectionHire transfers are briefed to keep devices and high-value items in the passenger compartment during the transit. The driver is responsible for supervising luggage loading and unloading. The Vedado and Miramar hotel areas are significantly lower-risk environments than the Habana Vieja tourist core, where the concentration of tourism-focused street activity is highest.

Vehicle and Driver Standards

All Havana transfers are made in vehicles from state-sanctioned operator fleets (Cubacar, Transgaviota, Rex), which are maintained to a standard materially higher than the general Cuban transport fleet. Vehicles are air-conditioned and insured for foreign visitor transport. Drivers authorised by MININT for foreign visitor transport have passed the relevant background and licensing requirements under Cuban transport law. English and Spanish language capability is confirmed for all drivers engaged for international client transfers. Cubacar and Transgaviota maintain modern vehicles including Mercedes-Benz and Kia models for executive transfers; the fleet specification is confirmed at the booking stage.

Hotel and Compound Confirmation

Havana's principal hotels for business and diplomatic visitors are divided between Vedado (10km from the airport via Avenida Linea) and Miramar (12km via the Malecon and 5th Avenue). Hotel Nacional de Cuba (Vedado) and Melia Cohiba (Vedado, on the Malecon waterfront) are the primary choices for senior visitors. Paradisus Princesa del Mar is in Miramar. Each property has specific vehicle access arrangements that differ: the Nacional has a circular driveway approached from the O Street entrance; Melia Cohiba has a covered porte-cochere directly off the Malecon. For embassy, MINREX, or compound visits, vehicle access and arrival notification protocols are confirmed with the receiving party before departure. British interests in Cuba are handled by the Swiss Embassy Havana (+53 7 204 2611). Medical facilities for foreign visitors include CIREN International Hospital (+53 7 273 8102) and Clínica Central Cira García (+53 7 204 2811).
