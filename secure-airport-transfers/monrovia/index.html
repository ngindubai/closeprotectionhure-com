The extended corridor, documented vehicle-targeting risk on the highway, and the high-risk overall security environment in Liberia make pre-arranged secure transfer from ROB an operational requirement, not an optional service. Roberts International Airport Collection Protocol

Pre-arranged collection at Roberts International Airport (ROB) begins inside the arrivals hall before the principal exits to the vehicle bay. The 60km highway transfer from ROB to central Monrovia and the Mamba Point area is one of the longest airport-to-city transfers in West Africa. Vehicle-targeting incidents on the ROB highway have been recorded. Operations controller oversight is maintained continuously from the arrivals hall through the full 60km highway corridor to the principal's confirmed Monrovia destination.

LNP Licensed Drivers

All drivers providing secure transfers in Monrovia hold current authorisation under the Liberia National Police (LNP) regulatory framework. Vehicles are appropriate-specification, non-descript, and fully insured. Operations controller oversight is maintained from the ROB arrivals hall through the 60km highway transfer to Mamba Point, Sinkor, or confirmed business destinations. Organisational branding on vehicles is avoided.

ROB to Monrovia Transfer Route

Roberts International Airport to central Monrovia is approximately 60km via the highway, typically completing in 60 to 90 minutes depending on road condition and traffic. At 60km, this is among the longest airport-to-city transfers in West Africa. The highway passes through mixed urban and peri-urban areas; the condition of the road surface varies and can extend journey times after heavy rain. Route assessment is updated before each transfer.

Extended Transfer Corridor Vulnerability

The 60km ROB to Monrovia transfer is the primary operational planning consideration for all Liberia secure transfers. The extended corridor means that vehicle-targeting incidents -- which have been recorded on the ROB highway -- create a proportionally greater exposure than at airports with compact city transfers. Operations controller oversight covers the full 60km throughout; secondary routing options are pre-confirmed for situations where the primary highway is compromised. No transfer departs ROB without an updated highway condition assessment.

Night Transfer Enhanced Protocols

Night arrivals at ROB carry significantly elevated risk on the 60km highway corridor. FCDO Liberia travel advice (2026) advises against non-essential movement after dark across Monrovia and the approach roads. Night transfers require specific driver briefing, confirmed highway condition report, reduced speed protocols on the highway, and enhanced operations controller monitoring. Where the flight arrival time allows, daytime transfer scheduling is strongly preferred over night arrivals on the 60km corridor.

AFL Checkpoint Management

Armed Forces of Liberia (AFL) and LNP checkpoints are encountered on the ROB highway and some city routes. All LNP-licensed drivers are briefed on current checkpoint locations and the appropriate documentation and protocol for each. Operations controller oversight includes confirmed checkpoint status updates before departure from ROB. Checkpoint procedures are factored into the transfer time estimate; unplanned checkpoint delays are communicated to the operations controller in real time.
