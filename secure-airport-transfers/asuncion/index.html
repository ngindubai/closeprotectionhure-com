Operational detail for Asuncion

Silvio Pettirossi International Airport Collection Protocol

Silvio Pettirossi International Airport (ASU) is located in Luque municipality, approximately 15km north-east of the Asuncion city centre. The airport serves as the primary international gateway for Paraguay, with connections through regional South American hubs including Buenos Aires Ezeiza, Sao Paulo Guarulhos, and Lima. The arrivals zone at ASU is an active location for unregistered taxi operators and informal transport services targeting foreign business arrivals. Pre-arranged collection positions the driver inside the arrivals hall with name identification before the principal reaches the forecourt. The operations controller confirms flight tracking and communicates the driver's position before customs clearance. Passengers arriving on late-night or early-morning flights, which represent a significant share of regional connections, require particular attention to the collection protocol given the reduced official presence at off-peak hours.

Route Management from ASU to Asuncion City Centre

The primary route from Silvio Pettirossi International Airport to the Asuncion city centre follows the Avenida Madame Lynch south-west into the microcentro and the Villa Morra district. The 15km transfer takes approximately 25 to 40 minutes under normal conditions, extending to 60 minutes during peak hours on the main north-south arteries. The route passes through mixed residential and commercial areas before entering the more structured zones of Villa Morra and Carmelitas, which are the preferred accommodation and meeting zones for international business visitors. The southern approach to the microcentro via Avenida Espana passes through areas of elevated opportunistic crime at night; transfers into the city centre after 22:00 use modified routing avoiding those corridors. Source: FCDO Paraguay travel advice (2026).

Kidnapping and Vehicle Crime Risk

Paraguay has historically experienced elevated kidnapping activity, particularly in the eastern border zones proximate to the Triple Frontier and Ciudad del Este, where Brazilian and Paraguayan criminal organisations have conducted kidnap-for-ransom operations targeting business figures in agriculture and commerce. The FCDO Paraguay travel advice (2026) and the US State Department Paraguay Level 2 advisory (2026) both note the kidnapping risk and recommend heightened vigilance regarding personal security practices. In Asuncion itself, the kidnapping risk is lower than in eastern Paraguay, but express-kidnapping and vehicle-crime patterns affecting visitors in transit are documented. The transfer programme applies vehicle anti-surveillance discipline from ASU through the city approach roads, including counter-surveillance observation and avoidance of predictable routing patterns for high-profile principals.

Paraguay Ministry of Interior Security Licensing

Paraguay's private security sector is regulated by the Ministry of Interior (Ministerio del Interior), under Law No. 2860/06, which establishes the legal framework for commercial security companies operating in Paraguay. Companies must be registered with the Ministry of Interior and maintain current compliance with the National Police (Policia Nacional). The law covers guarding, transport security, and personal protection services. All drivers in the Asuncion transfer programme operate through Ministry of Interior-registered companies with current National Police compliance documentation. Verification of company registration under Law No. 2860/06 is confirmed before any driver is allocated to a principal. Source: Paraguay Law No. 2860/06 (Private Security Act).

Operations Controller Throughout the Transfer

An operations controller maintains active oversight from the point the principal's flight lands at ASU through to confirmed arrival at their Asuncion accommodation. The controller tracks the inbound flight, monitors the current security situation on the ASU-to-city corridor, selects routing based on time of day and any identified risk on the approach roads, and maintains check-in contact with the driver throughout. For high-profile principals, the controller coordinates a secondary vehicle check at a defined waypoint on the route. Late-night arrivals - which represent a significant proportion of ASU operations due to regional connection schedules - receive specific heightened protocols with more frequent driver check-ins.

Villa Morra, Carmelitas, Microcentro, and Sajonia Coverage

The Asuncion transfer programme covers the city's principal business and residential destinations: Villa Morra and Carmelitas (the primary business and safer residential district, preferred by international business visitors and home to the majority of quality hotels and corporate offices); the microcentro (the historic downtown, with elevated petty crime risk at night); Sajonia (northern commercial zone); Las Mercedes (residential and commercial, adjacent to Villa Morra); and embassy compounds in the diplomatic quarter. Villa Morra is consistently assessed as the safest urban district in Asuncion for international business visitors and is the recommended base for corporate stays.
