Operational detail for Thessaloniki

Makedonia Airport Route and Logistics

Makedonia Airport (IATA: SKG, ICAO: LGTS) is located 16 kilometres southeast of Thessaloniki city centre, accessed primarily via the Egnatia Odos motorway (A2) and the Thessaloniki Ring Road. The airport serves as the main commercial gateway to northern Greece and Macedonia, handling scheduled and charter traffic from across Europe. It operates a single passenger terminal. Under normal conditions the journey time from SKG to central Thessaloniki is 20 to 25 minutes, extending to 35 to 40 minutes during morning and evening peak periods, particularly on the Thessaloniki Ring Road approach to the waterfront district. Key city centre landmarks on the route include the Thessaloniki Exhibition Centre (TIF/Helexpo) on the western approach, which can generate significant additional traffic during major trade fairs including Thessaloniki International Fair (September, annual). Transfers to waterfront hotels such as Makedonia Palace or the Aristotelous Square area continue along the coastal road from the Ring Road junction. Passengers connecting onward to ferry services to the Aegean islands or Halkidiki peninsula should note that journey times to the port and to the Halkidiki highway junction are both within 30 to 40 minutes of the airport under normal conditions. Business travellers arriving for trade events at the TIF should account for additional journey time during the fair period.

Vehicle and Driver Standards

Drivers operating secure transfers from Makedonia Airport hold a valid Greek professional driver licence (Adeia Odigisis) and operate under commercial carrier insurance at the appropriate class for passenger transport. Vehicles used for corporate and executive transfers include Mercedes E-Class, S-Class, or V-Class variants in dark or neutral livery, all maintained to current KTEO (Greek vehicle roadworthiness inspection) standards. Enhanced background screening is conducted on all drivers, including criminal records checks via the Greek Ministry of Citizen Protection records system. Drivers are briefed on client confidentiality and do not share routing or client information with third parties. For larger groups, such as MICE delegations or incentive parties, minibus and Sprinter-class vehicles with a dedicated coordinator are available with advance notice. All vehicles carry vehicle first-aid kits and are equipped with live traffic navigation. For clients arriving on private aviation at Thessaloniki's general aviation apron, airside coordination with the handling agent is available to allow vehicle positioning at the ramp rather than the public terminal, eliminating exposure to the public arrivals hall entirely. Multilingual drivers with English and Greek proficiency are standard; German or French language drivers are available with advance request.

Arrival Hall Protocols and Risk Management

Makedonia Airport operates within the standard EU airport security environment. FCDO Greece travel advice (2024) identifies petty theft as the primary public safety risk for visitors, consistent with the general profile of European tourist-destination airports. The risk is concentrated in crowded public areas, including the arrivals hall during busy flight periods, rather than in the transfer vehicle or on route. Name-board collection at the designated arrivals meeting point ensures the principal is identified at the earliest safe point after baggage reclaim, without any need to enter the public forecourt or engage with unofficial transfer providers. The driver holds a clearly printed name board and presents identification on request. Principal contact is initiated inside the terminal rather than outside, reducing the time spent on the public forecourt where taxi touts operate. During peak summer season and major events, including Thessaloniki International Fair (September) and Thessaloniki International Film Festival (November), the arrivals area can be congested and collection time may extend. All principals are advised to share flight details so the driver can monitor delays and adjust meet time accordingly. The driver's name, vehicle registration, and a photograph are provided to the principal by secure message before departure.

Route Security Assessment

The Egnatia Odos motorway (A2) and Thessaloniki Ring Road transfer corridor presents a low-to-moderate security risk profile. There is no documented pattern of vehicle interdiction or crime specifically targeting travellers on this transfer route. The moderate risk designation for Thessaloniki as a whole reflects the occasional public disorder associated with political demonstrations in the city centre, particularly on Egnatia and Tsimiski streets, rather than any specific threat on the airport transfer route. Demonstration activity in Thessaloniki city centre is generally announced in advance and, where it affects the primary arrival route from the Ring Road, alternative approaches via the western bypass or the coastal Leoforos Megalou Alexandrou are available. The driver monitors current conditions via real-time navigation and will select an alternative route proactively if the primary approach is disrupted. No specific organised crime or kidnap risk targeting business travellers on the Thessaloniki airport corridor has been identified in publicly available intelligence sources. Counter-surveillance awareness during transfers is standard operating procedure. FCDO Greece (2024) maintains a national terrorism advisory for Greece but does not identify Thessaloniki specifically as an elevated-threat location.

Principal Hotel and Compound Drop-off

The principal Thessaloniki hotels served by this transfer service include Makedonia Palace Hotel (Meg. Alexandrou 2, the waterfront, approximately 20 kilometres from SKG), which has a formal porte-cochere with valet and luggage handling, and is the city's primary venue for high-profile corporate and diplomatic events. The Met Hotel (Proxenou Koromila 48, approximately 18 kilometres from SKG) and Electra Palace (Aristotelous Square 9, approximately 17 kilometres) are also within the standard downtown transfer catchment. Grand Hotel Palace (Tsimiski 12) is located on the main commercial street in the city centre at approximately 17 kilometres from the airport. All four properties have vehicle access for drop-off with luggage handling by hotel porter teams. For clients attending the Thessaloniki International Fair, the Helexpo venue has a dedicated vehicle drop-off zone on its western approach; the driver will confirm the relevant entrance based on the specific fair hall and event. Check-in briefing covering local emergency services, consular contacts, and on-site medical provision at the event venue is available on request. The driver remains until the principal confirms secure reception.

Emergency and Medical Protocols

The main public emergency hospital in Thessaloniki is Hippokration General Hospital (Konstantinoupoleos 49, Thessaloniki, telephone +30 2310 892 000), which operates a 24-hour accident and emergency department and is the primary trauma referral centre for northern Greece. For private medical care, Euromedica Panorama (+30 2310 348 000) offers English-speaking physician services and private consultation without waiting lists, making it the preferred option for non-life-threatening medical issues during a business visit. Interbalkan European Medical Centre (+30 2310 818 000) is a further private hospital option with English-speaking staff. The all-European emergency number is 112, which connects to police, fire, and ambulance services. The dedicated Greek ambulance number is 166. The British Embassy in Athens handles consular matters for the whole of Greece including Thessaloniki: telephone +30 210 727 2600; the Embassy operates an emergency out-of-hours line. The US Embassy in Athens covers the Thessaloniki consular district: +30 210 721 2951. In any medical emergency during a transfer, the driver will divert to the nearest accident and emergency department and notify the client's pre-briefed emergency contact.
