Ibadan is Nigeria’s largest city by area and a major centre for manufacturing, agri-business, higher education, and government relations in the southwest. Nigeria’s commercial and operational importance for British business remains significant across multiple sectors; Ibadan specifically is visited by executives in food processing, automotive supply, university partnership, and state government engagement. The city’s transfer environment is shaped by two distinct challenges: the Lagos-Ibadan Expressway and the after-dark risk within the city itself.

The 130km Lagos-Ibadan Expressway is the single most important logistics consideration for any Ibadan visit. Federal Road Safety Corps data identifies it as one of Nigeria’s highest-accident intercity roads, and the FCDO (2024) and US State Dept (2024) both specifically flag elevated road crime risk on Nigerian intercity routes after dark. The operational rule that CloseProtectionHire applies is straightforward: no transfer on this corridor departs after 14:00, and no passenger of any profile takes an unvetted vehicle at LOS airport for a destination in Ibadan. Self-drive and rideshare applications are not appropriate transport arrangements for this journey.

Within Ibadan, the city’s scale and road layout create specific exposure at junction bottlenecks, particularly at Ring Road and Dugbe Market, where stationary vehicles are the primary target for opportunistic crime after dark. All within-city movements after dark for principal-level visitors are planned with CPO accompaniment. For the full threat and logistics picture of Ibadan, see the Ibadan city guide. Operational detail for Ibadan

Lagos Airport and Lagos-Ibadan Expressway Logistics

Ibadan Airport (IBA) handles limited domestic routes with infrequent service. The primary gateway for international visitors to Ibadan is Murtala Muhammed International Airport Lagos (LOS), 130km south, connected via the Lagos-Ibadan Expressway. The road transfer takes 2 to 3 hours under normal conditions. During peak periods -- Friday afternoons, Monday mornings, public holidays, and following any expressway incident -- journey times extend to 4 to 5 hours. All Lagos-to-Ibadan transfers are planned as daytime-only movements departing no later than 14:00 to allow arrival in Ibadan before dark. Transfer timing is coordinated against the flight arrival time at LOS to determine whether a same-day departure to Ibadan is feasible, or whether an overnight stay in Lagos is the appropriate recommendation.

NSCDC Compliance and Driver Authorisation

Private security transport in Nigeria is regulated under the Nigeria Security and Civil Defence Corps (NSCDC) Act 2007 and the Private Guard Companies Act. All security-qualified drivers and personnel must hold current NSCDC compliance documentation. Commercial transport in Oyo State (in which Ibadan is located) is additionally subject to Oyo State licensing for private hire vehicles and Nigeria Road Transport Authority (NRTA) requirements for commercial vehicles. CloseProtectionHire engages only operators with current NSCDC documentation, Oyo State private hire licensing, and NRTA-compliant vehicle registration. Driver credentials and vehicle documentation are verified before every engagement. All transfer vehicles are inspected before departure.

Lagos-Ibadan Expressway Risk and Security Briefing

The Lagos-Ibadan Expressway is one of Nigeria's highest-risk road corridors. Federal Road Safety Corps of Nigeria data documents frequent fatal accidents, fuel tanker incidents, and sections of poorly maintained carriageway. Criminality on the expressway includes armed robbery targeting slow-moving and stationary vehicles at bottlenecks, and carjacking incidents at night. The FCDO Nigeria travel advice (2024) states that all road movement in Nigeria carries higher risk at night, and specifically cautions against night driving in Oyo State. US State Dept Nigeria advisory (Level 3, Reconsider Travel, 2024) notes carjacking and armed robbery as documented risks on major intercity routes. For these reasons, no Lagos-to-Ibadan expressway transfer is arranged to depart after 14:00, and no transfer movement within Ibadan is arranged after dark without CPO accompaniment and specific risk assessment.

Ibadan City Movement and After-Dark Risk

Within Ibadan, movement after dark carries elevated risk from armed robbery and carjacking at road junctions. Ibadan is Nigeria's largest city by land area, and its extensive road network creates multiple choke points at major junctions, including the Ring Road and Dugbe Market junction, where stationary vehicles are particularly exposed. Evening and night transfers within the city -- including returns to the Agodi GRA from meetings, university visits, or business appointments -- are planned with CPO accompaniment as standard. Jericho and Agodi GRA are the principal accommodation zones for business visitors; transfers to these areas from Ring Road locations require junction awareness. All within-city movements after dark are pre-planned rather than arranged ad hoc.

Vehicle and Driver Standards

All Lagos-to-Ibadan expressway transfers use modern, well-maintained vehicles with current NRTA registration, valid roadworthiness certificates, and commercial insurance. Vehicles are fuelled to full before departure from Lagos, as fuel availability at expressway stations can be inconsistent during fuel scarcity periods, which recur in Nigeria. Communication equipment for the transfer vehicle and the receiving party is confirmed before departure. Drivers hold NSCDC compliance documentation and Oyo State private hire licensing. English is an official language in Nigeria; all drivers communicate in English. For principal-level transfers, CPO accompaniment in a second vehicle is available and is recommended as standard for the expressway journey.

Hotel and Compound Confirmation

Ibadan's primary accommodation for business visitors is in the Agodi GRA district in the north-central area of the city. Jericho is the alternative premium residential and hotel zone. University College Hospital Ibadan (UCHI) in the Jericho area is the principal referral hospital for the city (+234 2 241 1768). University of Ibadan campus is in the northwest of the city. For all hotel, GRA compound, and university campus arrivals, vehicle access and gate protocols are confirmed with the receiving party before departure from Lagos. Entry details -- vehicle registration, driver name, number of passengers -- are provided to the receiving security team in advance. British Deputy High Commission Lagos can be reached at +234 1 262 2100 for consular assistance.
