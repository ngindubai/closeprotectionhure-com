Cali is Colombia’s third-largest city and the commercial capital of the Valle del Cauca, a region central to Colombia’s sugar, agribusiness, and industrial economy. Alfonso Bonilla Aragón International Airport (CLO) in neighbouring Palmira handles a significant volume of international business travel, with connections from Madrid, Miami, and major Colombian hubs serving the manufacturing, agriculture, and growing technology sectors based in the Cali metropolitan area.

The security picture for Cali is one of genuine improvement over the past two decades, but the city’s crime indicators remain elevated by international standards. OSAC’s annual Cali report documents vehicle crime, carjacking, and express kidnapping as primary concerns for international arrivals, with the airport transfer corridor specifically identified as a risk zone. The FCDO Colombia advisory, current as of mid-2026, advises heightened vigilance across the city and specific precautions for transport. Supervigilancia (Decree 356/1994) provides the regulatory framework for identifying compliant security operators, and our transfer service is delivered exclusively through registered and licensed partners.

For business visitors, the transfer from CLO to central Cali is a manageable journey that becomes significantly safer with the right pre-arrangement. Our service covers the full transfer from the arrivals hall to your hotel, compound, or business facility, with a vetted driver, GPS-monitored vehicle, and route selection based on current intelligence. For delegations visiting industrial facilities in Yumbo or supply chain sites in Palmira, we plan multi-stop itineraries with appropriate inter-site security protocols. Our Cali city guide covers the wider security environment, and close protection officers in Cali can be arranged for the full duration of your visit.

What this covers

Operational detail for Cali

Alfonso Bonilla Aragón Airport to City Centre

Alfonso Bonilla Aragón International Airport (CLO) is located in the municipality of Palmira, approximately 20km north of Cali city centre. The transfer route runs south through the Palmira corridor and into northern Cali, passing through areas with variable crime profiles. Journey time under normal conditions is 30 to 50 minutes, extending to 60 to 75 minutes in peak-hour traffic. The Palmira section of the route has been noted in OSAC reporting as an area where vehicle crime has been directed at arriving international passengers.

Gang Violence and Route Risk Assessment

Cali has a documented history of gang violence associated with criminal organisations operating in peripheral neighbourhoods and along certain transit corridors. The city's homicide rate, while reduced from peaks in earlier decades, remains among the higher rates in major Colombian cities. Our drivers receive daily route intelligence covering current gang activity zones, police checkpoint locations, and any incidents affecting the airport-to-city corridor. Route selection is an active operational decision for every Cali transfer, not a default navigation choice.

Supervigilancia Regulatory Compliance

Private security services in Colombia are regulated by Supervigilancia (Superintendencia de Vigilancia y Seguridad Privada) under Decree 356 of 1994. All our Cali transfer partners are registered with Supervigilancia and hold current operator licences. Driver vetting under this framework includes criminal background checks through the Colombian national system, employment verification, and competency assessment. We can provide Supervigilancia registration documentation on request for corporate duty-of-care purposes.

Carjacking and Vehicle Crime Protocols

Vehicle carjacking is a documented risk in Cali, particularly for arriving international passengers who are identifiable at the airport terminal. Our transfer protocol eliminates the key vulnerability windows: the driver meets clients inside the arrivals hall with a named board, luggage is loaded directly into the vehicle under driver supervision, and the vehicle moves promptly without stopping in the open kerb area where targeting assessments occur. Drivers are trained in anti-carjacking vehicle operation, including identification of following vehicles and use of alternate routes if surveillance is suspected.

FCDO Colombia Travel Advisory Context

The FCDO maintains a Colombia travel advisory that identifies areas of elevated risk within the country, including specific street-level advice for major cities. Cali is referenced in the advisory for general street crime and express kidnapping risk. Our pre-travel client briefing incorporates current FCDO advisory content specific to Cali, covering which districts carry the highest risk, behaviour at crime hotspots, and the standard precautions recommended for the Colombian urban environment.

Corporate and Industrial Sector Support

Cali is the commercial centre of Colombia's Valle del Cauca department, serving the sugar, paper, chemicals, and agribusiness sectors, as well as a growing technology and shared services sector. International business visitors, supply chain audit teams, and investment delegations form a significant part of the arriving passenger profile at CLO. Our transfer service supports multi-leg business itineraries across the Cali metropolitan area, including transfers to industrial zones in Yumbo and Palmira, with adjusted route planning for each destination.

Alfonso Bonilla Aragón Airport is located in the municipality of Palmira, in the fertile Cauca Valley plain north of Cali, partly for topographical reasons and partly for historical development patterns. Supervigilancia, the Superintendencia de Vigilancia y Seguridad Privada, is the Colombian government body responsible for licensing and supervising private security companies under Decree 356 of 1994. Registration requires criminal background clearance for personnel, minimum training standards, equipment compliance, and ongoing regulatory reporting. A Supervigilancia-registered operator has been through a formal compliance process and is subject to ongoing oversight. Using an unregistered operator in Colombia carries regulatory risk for the contracting organisation and means the driver has not been subjected to formal background screening.

Express kidnapping involves detaining a victim for a short period, typically two to twelve hours, while forcing withdrawals from ATM cards or bank accounts. It has been documented as a crime type directed specifically at international arrivals in Cali and other major Colombian cities, with airport taxis and informal transport being the most common initial point of contact. The crime depends on the victim not having a pre-arranged, vetted driver meeting them inside the terminal. Pre-arranged transfers eliminate this vulnerability point.
