Kumasi is Ghana’s second city and the commercial capital of the Ashanti Region, drawing business visitors primarily from the mining, logistics, and agricultural commodity sectors. The city is substantial in area – larger than its population might suggest, spread across hills and valleys with a sprawling commercial core – and its transfer environment is dominated not by the 4km airport corridor from KMS but by the 270km Accra-to-Kumasi road journey that most international visitors make after landing at Kotoka International Airport.

The N6 highway is the operational centrepiece of any transfer plan for Kumasi: it is the highest-traffic inter-city road in Ghana, and the WHO Global Road Safety Data (2023) places Ghana’s road fatality rate among the highest in West Africa. Commercial vehicles in poor mechanical condition, overloaded trucks, and unpredictable driving behaviour on a road with limited separation between oncoming lanes make this a route that requires a carefully selected, maintained vehicle and a driver with demonstrated experience on the corridor. Night-time movement is not an arrangement that responsible operators make: all CloseProtectionHire Accra-to-Kumasi transfers are planned as daylight movements with appropriate departure timing.

Within Kumasi itself, the Kejetia and Asafo market zones create severe congestion and the specific theft risk that comes with stationary vehicles in dense commercial areas. Hotel deliveries to the Ahodwo district bypass these zones entirely. For the full security and logistics picture of Kumasi and the Ashanti Region, see the Kumasi city guide. If in-city close protection or escort for mining sector facility visits is required, our close protection officers in Kumasi are available for the full duration of your operational schedule.

Operational detail for Kumasi

Kumasi Airport and Accra-Kumasi Transfer Route Logistics

Kumasi Airport (KMS) is located 4km from the city centre and handles domestic flights from Accra (approximately 45 minutes flight time) and some regional West African routes. However, the majority of international visitors arrive at Kotoka International Airport (ACC) in Accra, 270km south of Kumasi. The overland transfer from Accra to Kumasi via the N6 highway takes approximately 3 hours under normal conditions. This is a distinct, high-risk journey that requires specific planning. CloseProtectionHire provides transfer management for both KMS arrival and the Accra-to-Kumasi road journey. For KMS domestic arrivals, vehicle positioning and driver identification are confirmed before the flight. For ACC arrivals continuing to Kumasi by road, the transfer is planned as a daylight movement with pre-departure vehicle check and route intelligence review.

PSOB Licensing and Driver Compliance

Private security operators in Ghana are regulated under the Private Security Organizations Act (ACT 803, 2009), administered by the Private Security Organizations Board (PSOB). All security-qualified drivers and escort personnel must hold current PSOB registration. Commercial private hire vehicles require licensing from the Ghana Road Transport Authority (GPRTU) and vehicle registration with the Driver and Vehicle Licensing Authority (DVLA Ghana). CloseProtectionHire engages only PSOB-registered operators with current DVLA-registered vehicles and road transport authority licensing. Driver credentials, vehicle roadworthiness certificates, and commercial insurance are verified before every engagement.

N6 Highway Road Safety and Inter-City Transfer Risk Briefing

The Accra-to-Kumasi N6 highway is one of Ghana's most accident-prone road corridors. The WHO Global Road Safety Report documents Ghana's road fatality rate as among the highest in West Africa, and the N6 -- which carries the highest traffic volume of any inter-city route in Ghana -- accounts for a disproportionate share of serious accidents (WHO Global Road Safety Data, 2023). Risk factors include poorly maintained commercial vehicles, overloaded trucks, overtaking on blind corners and bends, and unpredictable driver behaviour by minibus (tro-tro) operators. Night-time movement on the N6 is strongly discouraged due to reduced visibility, limited roadside assistance, and the elevated accident frequency documented after dark. All Accra-to-Kumasi transfers are planned as daylight-only movements departing between 06:00 and 08:00 to reach Kumasi before afternoon peak congestion.

Kumasi City Congestion and Market Zone Awareness

Within Kumasi, the Kejetia Market area and central Asafo commercial zone create severe congestion and are associated with elevated opportunistic theft risk for stationary vehicles. Vehicles with visible luggage in the boot, or with windows open in stationary traffic, are the primary targets for bag and opportunistic theft in these areas. Route planning for all hotel deliveries to the Ahodwo district (southwest) actively avoids the Kejetia-Asafo corridor. For visitors continuing south to the Obuasi mining area (65km) or west toward Tarkwa (180km), onward transfer routing from Kumasi is planned as a separate leg with appropriate timing and vehicle preparation.

Vehicle and Driver Standards

All Kumasi and Accra-to-Kumasi transfers use well-maintained, relatively modern vehicles with current DVLA registration, roadworthiness certificates, and commercial insurance. Vehicles used for the N6 inter-city route are selected specifically for reliable performance over long distances on mixed road surfaces. Spare tyres, basic vehicle repair equipment, and adequate fuel are standard requirements for all inter-city movements. Drivers hold current Ghana Road Transport Authority licensing and PSOB registration where applicable. English is an official language in Ghana; all drivers engaged for international client transfers communicate in English. For longer mining sector transfers to Obuasi or Tarkwa, the vehicle specification and driver experience are matched to the specific route.

Hotel and Compound Confirmation

Kumasi's principal hotel properties for business and mining sector visitors are concentrated in the Ahodwo district in the city's southwest: Golden Tulip Kumasi City and Miklin Hotel are the primary choices. Both properties are in the southwest of the city, accessible from the Ahodwo Road without routing through the central market zones. Mining sector operational visits to Obuasi (65km south via the N6-A) and Tarkwa (approximately 180km west via secondary roads) require separate onward transfer planning from the Kumasi hotel. For compound or facility access in either location, entry protocols are confirmed with the receiving security team before departure. Medical contingency for the N6 corridor and Kumasi is Komfo Anokye Teaching Hospital (KATH, +233 3220 22301) in central Kumasi.
