What this covers

Operational detail for Manila

NAIA Terminal Knowledge and Kerbside Risk

Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA) operates four terminals across Pasay City. Terminal 1 handles international arrivals on non-Philippine carriers and is the highest-footfall terminal for foreign nationals. The US OSAC Manila Crime and Safety Report (2024) identifies the roads immediately outside NAIA Terminal 1 as elevated-risk zones for vehicle crime and opportunistic targeting of arriving foreign nationals with visible luggage. Our drivers position in the authorised pick-up zones rather than the exposed public kerbside, reducing dwell time in the high-exposure area outside the terminal doors. Pre-arrival coordination ensures the driver is in position before the principal clears customs.

Traffic and Route Planning on EDSA and Roxas Boulevard

The transfer from NAIA to Makati CBD or Bonifacio Global City (BGC) covers approximately 10 to 15 kilometres. The Metropolitan Manila Development Authority (MMDA) reports that this corridor can take between 30 minutes and 2.5 hours depending on time of day, EDSA coding restrictions, and weather conditions. Routes via Roxas Boulevard offer an alternative to EDSA for transfers to hotels on Manila Bay. Our operations team monitors MMDA traffic advisories and Waze data in real time and adjusts routing instructions to drivers before and during the transfer. All routes are pre-mapped with primary and secondary options. Principals with hard meeting commitments are advised of realistic journey time windows at booking.

Counter-Surveillance and Vehicle Profile

The OSAC Manila 2024 report notes that foreign nationals in visible high-value vehicles are disproportionately targeted near transport hubs. Transfer vehicles carry no corporate branding, security markings, or external accessories that distinguish them from local executive cars. Counter-surveillance checks are conducted during the transit from NAIA: the driver monitors for following vehicles and uses authorised route deviations if pattern-of-life surveillance is suspected. Principals carrying high-value items, travelling for sensitive business negotiations, or arriving for the first time in Manila receive a more detailed security intake at booking to assess whether a two-vehicle arrangement is appropriate.

Licensing Under Republic Act 5487

Security personnel providing transfer and personal protection services in the Philippines must hold a valid licence under Republic Act 5487 (Private Security Agency Law), administered by the Philippine National Police Supervisory Unit for Security and Investigation Agencies (PNP-SIU). All drivers and close protection personnel deployed in Manila transfers hold current PNP-SIU licences. Security agency operator licences are renewed annually under PNP-SIU requirements. Copies of operative licences are available on request before travel, consistent with due diligence requirements for corporate and HNWI clients.

BGC, Makati, and Manila Hotel Belt Coverage

The three principal destination zones for NAIA arrivals are Makati CBD (approximately 8 kilometres from NAIA, 30 to 60 minutes by road), Bonifacio Global City (BGC, approximately 10 kilometres, 30 to 75 minutes), and the Manila hotel belt along Roxas Boulevard and Ermita (approximately 7 kilometres, 25 to 50 minutes). Hotels including properties on Ayala Avenue, Arnaiz Avenue, and the BGC high street are standard delivery points. Transfers to Ortigas Center or Quezon City require extended journey time allowances, particularly during peak traffic periods. Principals requiring a discreet vehicle handover at the hotel security desk can arrange this as part of the transfer booking.

Coordination with In-Country Protection Teams

Manila is a common base for multinational corporate security programmes, with regional offices across Makati and BGC. Transfer services can be structured as a standalone airport collection or as the first element of a continuous protection itinerary, including handover to an in-country close protection team at the hotel or office. Our operations centre provides written transfer briefs to receiving security teams on request, covering vehicle details, driver name, estimated arrival time, and any security observations made during the transfer. This continuity reduces the gap-in-coverage risk at the transition point between airport and in-country protection.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Your driver will be assigned to the correct terminal based on your flight details provided at booking. Terminal 1 is used by most non-Philippine international carriers. Terminal 2 serves Philippine Airlines domestic and international routes. Terminal 3 handles Cebu Pacific, AirAsia, and several other carriers. Terminal 4 is domestic-only. The NAIA terminal assignment for your specific airline and flight number is confirmed in your booking documentation. Drivers are positioned in the authorised airside pick-up zones for each terminal, not at the exposed public kerbside, consistent with OSAC Manila 2024 guidance on kerbside exposure risk.

The MMDA (Metropolitan Manila Development Authority) regularly records average speeds of under 10 kilometres per hour on EDSA during peak periods (7 to 10am and 5 to 9pm on weekdays). A transfer that takes 30 minutes at midnight can take over 2 hours at 6pm. We recommend scheduling transfers with a realistic buffer: allow 90 minutes from NAIA to Makati or BGC during any weekday peak window, and at least 60 minutes at other times. Our operations team will provide an estimated journey time at booking based on your arrival time and destination. Principals with fixed onward commitments should be advised of this variability in advance.

Any security company providing transfer or personal protection services in the Philippines must hold a current operator’s licence issued under Republic Act 5487, administered by the PNP-SIU (Philippine National Police Supervisory Unit for Security and Investigation Agencies). Individual operatives must hold personal PNP-SIU licences, renewed annually. Corporate clients should request copies of both the company licence and the individual operative licence before confirming a booking. Using an unlicensed provider carries legal risk for the client under Philippine law and provides no recourse if an incident occurs. Our operatives hold current PNP-SIU credentials, available for review on request.

Manila transfer requests with specific operative requirements, including a female driver or escort, Tagalog or Cebuano language support, or medical-trained personnel, can be accommodated with a minimum of 72 hours advance notice. Manila’s licensed security sector is well developed and includes a range of qualified operatives. Requests should be noted at time of booking so that the correct operative is sourced and vetted before the transfer date. Last-minute requests for specialist requirements are subject to availability.

Our Philippines network covers Metro Manila as the primary service area. For principals travelling onward to Cebu, Davao, or other regional cities, domestic flight transfers from NAIA Terminal 2 or 3 (or the NAIA domestic terminals) can be arranged as a continuation of the original airport transfer. Separate transfer bookings for Cebu (Mactan-Cebu International Airport, CEB) or Davao (Francisco Bangoy International Airport, DVO) require advance notice and are subject to local network availability. Contact the operations centre at time of booking to discuss multi-city itineraries.
