Operational detail for Cape Town

Cape Town International Airport Terminal Handling

Cape Town International Airport (CPT) operates two primary terminal areas: the International Terminal, handling scheduled international arrivals and departures, and the Domestic Terminal, handling South African Airways, Kulula, FlySafair, and other domestic carriers. The two terminals are connected landside and share a common arrivals and departures road precinct. Pre-arrival confirmation of the correct terminal is built into the transfer booking process; however, CPT's more compact layout compared to Johannesburg OR Tambo reduces the risk of terminal navigation confusion for regular Africa-route travellers. The airport is operated by Airports Company South Africa (ACSA) and is located approximately 22km south-east of the Cape Town CBD via the N2 highway.

N2 Highway Route and Risk Assessment

The N2 highway is the primary road link between Cape Town International Airport and the Cape Town CBD. The N2 passes through or adjacent to several township communities including Gugulethu, Nyanga, and Mitchell's Plain, areas that the Western Cape Government Community Safety Department has consistently documented as experiencing elevated levels of violent crime, vehicle crime, and gang activity (Western Cape Community Safety Department Annual Report, 2023). The elevated crime environment on and adjacent to the N2 does not make normal airport transfers impossible, but it does make the N2 the primary operational risk variable. Transfer planning accounts for this through PSIRA-registered driver selection, route timing assessment, and contingency routing.

PSIRA-Registered Drivers

All Cape Town transfer drivers engaged for security roles are registered with the Private Security Industry Regulatory Authority (PSIRA), South Africa's statutory regulator for private security under the Private Security Industry Regulation Act 56 of 2001. PSIRA registration requires criminal background vetting, completion of approved training aligned to the PSETA (Private Security Sector Education and Training Authority) qualifications framework, and annual renewal. Grade C or Grade B PSIRA registration is the appropriate level for security driver functions, depending on the role scope. Drivers also hold the relevant Professional Driving Permit (PDP) issued by the Western Cape Department of Transport for commercial passenger carrying. Vehicles are unmarked executive class, with selection by group size and discretion profile.

Alternative Transfer Routes - False Bay and Atlantic Seaboard

The N2 is not the only route between CPT airport and key Cape Town destinations. For principals with accommodation on the Atlantic Seaboard (Sea Point, Clifton, Camps Bay, the V&A Waterfront) or in Hout Bay, the R300 and M5 freeway provide an alternative western corridor that avoids the N2's most crime-elevated sections. The R300 connects the airport to the N1 City area and from there the M5 reaches the Atlantic Seaboard corridor via De Waal Drive or the N2 to De Waal junction. Journey times on these alternative routes are comparable to the N2 direct route in normal conditions and offer a meaningfully different risk profile on the road environment. Klipfontein Road provides an additional alternative for intra-city routing.

FBO Services at CPT

Cape Town International Airport does not have a major standalone private aviation hub of the type found in London or Paris, but FBO-equivalent services are available through licensed handling agents at CPT. These services provide private aircraft handling, dedicated customs and immigration coordination for international arrivals, and a level of privacy appropriate for HNWI and corporate principals arriving on private or charter aircraft. Vehicle staging for FBO arrivals at CPT is coordinated with the handling agent before aircraft-on-blocks. Transfer vehicle access to the private handling area is confirmed as part of the pre-arrival coordination. Journey time from CPT to the V&A Waterfront or City Bowl via alternative routes is typically 25 to 40 minutes depending on route and time of day.

Operational Security - High-Risk Environment Planning

Cape Town's security environment is assessed as high relative to comparable gateway cities. Western Cape Government Community Safety Department statistics document significant levels of vehicle crime, robbery, and gang-related activity in areas adjacent to the main transfer routes (Annual Report, 2023). Transfer operations address this through selection of PSIRA-registered drivers with current local route knowledge, vehicle presentation that does not signal high-value or government occupancy, confirmed collection at the terminal kerbside rather than at a public waiting area, and operations controller oversight throughout the transfer. Journey time and route assessments are updated regularly against current SAPS (South African Police Service) crime mapping and community safety reporting.
