The Chittagong security environment

The FCDO rates Bangladesh as a destination requiring significant caution, noting an elevated terrorism threat from networks including Jamaat-ul-Mujahideen Bangladesh-linked groups and IS-affiliated activity within the country. Chittagong, as the country’s principal port city and a major industrial hub, has its own distinct threat profile, combining the national terrorism picture with port-area organised crime and the political strike disruption that characterises Bangladeshi civic life. The US State Department rates Bangladesh at Level 2 (exercise increased caution) as of its 2026 advisory. Hartal declarations, which can close city routes with hours of notice, are the most frequent operational disruption for visiting executives and require a standing contingency plan on every transfer booking.

Inside-terminal collection at CGP by a Ministry of Home Affairs-authorised driver; operations controller tracking from landing to drop-off; primary and contingency routing through the Agrabad corridor; hartal monitoring and departure time management; and night transfer protocols with heightened controller contact for late-arriving flights. The transfer programme covers the Agrabad CBD, CEPZ facilities, and Sadarghat port area destinations, with confirmed pre-notification protocols when dropping principals at industrial or port facilities.

Operational detail for Chittagong

Shah Amanat International Airport Collection Protocol

Shah Amanat International Airport (CGP) serves all scheduled international and domestic arrivals into Chittagong. The airport is located approximately 18km south of the Agrabad business district via the Shah Amanat Bridge corridor. Pre-arranged collection begins inside the arrivals hall: the driver carries name identification and meets the principal before they reach the kerb exit. The arrivals zone at CGP is an active location for unvetted taxi approaches and persistent touts targeting foreign business travellers. No unescorted kerb-side wait is acceptable under any circumstances. The operations controller confirms flight tracking and maintains communication with the collection vehicle from initial approach clearance through to drop-off at the principal's Chittagong accommodation.

Route Management on the Agrabad Corridor

The principal route from Shah Amanat International Airport to the Agrabad commercial district runs via the Shah Amanat Bridge and the CDA Avenue corridor. This 18km transfer typically takes 40 to 60 minutes under normal conditions, extending significantly during religious festival periods and hartal disruptions. Port-area congestion at the intersections of Station Road and Sheikh Mujib Road introduces predictable bottleneck points that should be avoided during peak freight movement hours. Secondary routing through Bahaddarhat and Muradpur exists for contingency use when the primary corridor is disrupted. Operations controllers monitor confirmed hartal declarations via Bangladesh Police public communications and adjust routing and departure timing accordingly. Source: FCDO Bangladesh travel advice (2026).

Hartal and Political Strike Disruption

Hartals, the political strike mechanism used by opposition parties and trade union organisations in Bangladesh, can close major roads throughout Chittagong with limited advance notice, sometimes within hours of declaration. The FCDO travel advisory for Bangladesh (2026) specifically notes the risk of civil unrest, including hartals, that can disrupt transport across the country. The Chittagong transfer programme includes advance monitoring of hartal declarations, contingency departure time management, and protocols for delaying non-essential movement during confirmed industrial action. During a confirmed hartal, all unnecessary vehicle movements are suspended; essential transfers use modified low-profile routing and maintain direct operations controller contact throughout.

Bangladesh Private Security Licensing

Commercial security companies in Bangladesh, including those providing secure ground transport, must hold authorisation from the Ministry of Home Affairs and operate within the oversight framework administered by Bangladesh Police. There is no single national private security act equivalent to some regional neighbours; authorisation is administered through Ministry directives and Bangladesh Police regulatory guidance. Licence documentation is confirmed before any driver is allocated to a principal. Unlicensed operators have no legal standing during security incidents and cannot operate within the framework of accountability that the transfer programme requires. Source: Ministry of Home Affairs Bangladesh security regulatory framework.

Operations Controller Throughout the Transfer

An operations controller maintains active oversight from the point the principal's flight lands at CGP through to confirmed arrival at their Chittagong accommodation. The controller tracks the inbound flight in real time, confirms CGP immigration and baggage queue conditions where available, coordinates the driver's terminal position, and maintains check-in contact throughout the journey. Any route deviation, vehicle issue, or external disruption is managed centrally through the controller. The principal is never left in an unmonitored vehicle or waiting unescorted in the CGP arrivals zone.

Agrabad, CEPZ, and Sadarghat Coverage

The Chittagong transfer programme covers the city's principal business and industrial zones: the Agrabad commercial district (the primary CBD and banking hub), the Chittagong Export Processing Zone (CEPZ) on the northern periphery, and the Sadarghat port area when business requirements take principals to the waterfront. The Sadarghat zone carries elevated opportunistic crime risk and requires heightened vehicle discipline. Drop-off at CEPZ facilities follows a confirmed pre-notification protocol with the receiving organisation's security team.
