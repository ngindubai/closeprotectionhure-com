Robert Gabriel Mugabe International Airport (HRE) sits approximately 12 kilometres from Harare’s central business district, with a transfer corridor that the FCDO Zimbabwe advisory (updated 2026) places in an area of documented carjacking risk, compounded by Zimbabwe’s chronic load-shedding programme that regularly removes street lighting from the airport road. Managing this specific combination of threats requires generator-equipped vehicles, inside-terminal collection, and route planning that accounts for both criminal and infrastructure variables.

The Harare arrival window

The arrivals hall at HRE is a single-channel facility where passengers exiting customs emerge directly into an area occupied by informal taxi operators. For business principals who do not have a pre-confirmed collection, this creates pressure to accept an unvetted vehicle, which is the primary point of exposure at this airport. The kerb area is uncontrolled and the absence of a dedicated, credentialled taxi zone makes distinguishing licensed from unlicensed operators difficult under normal arrival conditions.

The 12-kilometre road transfer to Borrowdale or Highlands passes through areas of Harare where carjacking has been recorded, with the airport road itself flagged in the FCDO Zimbabwe advisory. Night transfers carry an elevated risk because load-shedding removes street lighting across extended stretches of the corridor, reducing visibility for both driver and potential threat actors and making approaches to stationary vehicles harder to detect early.

Zimbabwe’s security environment also includes the possibility of police checkpoint delays, which can create static vehicle exposure on the transfer route. Drivers in our Harare network are briefed on checkpoint compliance procedures and on the distinction between official ZRP checkpoints and the less formal arrangements that can occur on certain routes.

What secure transfers cover in Harare

Our Harare programme provides a ZRP-licensed vetted driver, inside-terminal collection at the arrivals exit, real-time flight tracking, operations controller monitoring throughout, generator-equipped vehicle for infrastructure resilience, pre-planned primary and alternate routes to Borrowdale and Highlands, and coordination with the principal’s organisation in the event of a security incident.

Operational detail for Harare

Inside-Terminal Collection at Robert Gabriel Mugabe International Airport (HRE)

Robert Gabriel Mugabe International Airport lies approximately 12 kilometres south-east of Harare's central business district. The terminal building handles both international and domestic traffic through a single main arrivals hall. Our driver enters the arrivals hall before the principal clears customs, holds a discreet name board at the baggage collection exit, and accompanies the principal and luggage directly to the vehicle. No kerb waiting is required. The airport's informal taxi rank sits immediately outside the arrivals exit and is an uncontrolled environment where unlicensed operators approach passengers; inside-terminal collection removes this exposure point entirely. The vehicle is positioned at a controlled holding bay close to the terminal exit, reducing the time the principal spends in open external areas. For international arrivals, the driver monitors the flight in coordination with the operations controller and adjusts terminal entry time to avoid lengthy holds inside the building.

ZRP Licensing and Zimbabwe Driver Compliance

Private security operations in Zimbabwe are regulated under the Private Investigations and Security Guards Act (Chapter 27:10), with licensing administered through the Zimbabwe Republic Police (ZRP) Private Security and Licensing Authority. All drivers engaged for principal transfers in Harare hold current ZRP Private Security licences and valid commercial driving licences under the Road Traffic Act. Our Harare driver network undergoes background screening through ZRP records and criminal history checks before onboarding to the programme. Drivers are also vetted for familiarity with the specific security conditions on the HRE-to-CBD transfer corridor, including checkpoint compliance protocols and the documented carjacking profile on the airport road. Clients requiring written confirmation of driver licensing and compliance documentation for duty-of-care records can request a compliance summary before travel. ZRP licence renewal schedules are tracked centrally and no driver is assigned to a principal transfer with lapsed certification.

Operations Controller Throughout

A dedicated operations controller tracks every Harare transfer from the moment the principal's flight is wheels-down to confirmed safe arrival at the destination. The controller monitors the inbound flight via a live feed, communicates the updated arrival time to the driver, and stages terminal entry to match the principal's customs clearance window. During the 12-kilometre road transfer, encrypted mobile contact is maintained with the driver at regular intervals. Any missed check-in triggers a welfare call; if unresolved within five minutes, the emergency protocol activates: notification of the principal's organisation, and, where appropriate, liaison with ZRP (emergency number 995) or the British Embassy in Harare (+263 24 2338 800). Pre-trip information includes destination address coordinates, nearest hospital with emergency capability (Parirenyatwa Group of Hospitals and Avenues Clinic are the primary options for international patients), and direct contact for the operations controller.

Return Departure Transfer

The return transfer from Borrowdale, Highlands, or the central business district to HRE mirrors the arrival-transfer risk profile on the same 12-kilometre corridor. The operations controller schedules collection at least three hours before the international departure window to account for transit time, potential checkpoint delays, and terminal security processing. The driver confirms the vehicle and route with the controller one hour before collection. Luggage is staged under supervision at the departure point and loaded by the driver before the principal boards. Drop-off is at the international departures entrance and the driver remains until the principal has entered the terminal and confirmed check-in. For early-morning departures, the night-transfer heightened protocol applies, including enhanced awareness of the static-vehicle exposure risk that the airport road presents during low-traffic hours when vehicles can be approached with reduced visibility.

Carjacking and Power Infrastructure Protocol

The FCDO Zimbabwe advisory (updated 2026) identifies carjacking as an active risk in Harare, with the airport road among the documented risk corridors, particularly after dark. Zimbabwe's persistent load-shedding programme means that street lighting on the airport road is frequently absent during scheduled and unscheduled power cuts, which significantly increases night-transfer risk. Our Harare programme addresses both threats directly. All vehicles assigned to HRE transfers are generator-equipped or carry an independent power reserve sufficient to operate vehicle electronics and communications for the duration of the transfer without reliance on public power infrastructure. Drivers are briefed on carjacking countermeasures: maintaining rolling movement where possible, avoiding predictable stopping points, and managing following distance to reduce box-in exposure. Route selection avoids the documented high-frequency carjacking zones on the airport road at night. For particularly high-risk principals or night-time transfers, an escort vehicle option is available on request.
