Nanjing is the capital of Jiangsu Province and one of China’s most historically significant cities, serving as a major centre for defence industry, electronics manufacturing, petrochemical production, and academic research. Nanjing Lukou International Airport (NKG) handles regional connections from across Asia and international services via hub airports, bringing a regular flow of business travellers, academic delegations, and technology sector visitors into a city where the physical security environment is among the most stable in East Asia.

The transfer from Lukou Airport to central Nanjing is a straightforward 35km journey on the Airport Expressway, with journey times of 40 to 60 minutes under normal conditions. There is no meaningful violent crime risk for international travellers on this route, and Nanjing’s urban environment presents none of the vehicle crime or carjacking considerations that characterise transfers in high-risk cities. The relevant considerations for business visitors are instead those common to travel across China’s major business centres: counterintelligence awareness, digital security during transit, and the use of PSB-compliant operators who operate within the MPS Order 564 framework rather than informal or unregistered services.

Our Nanjing transfer service provides PSB-compliant drivers vetted through the Public Security Bureau, a pre-travel digital hygiene briefing tailored to PRC entry and the specific sectors most relevant to our Nanjing client base, and confirmed logistics from Lukou Airport to the destination hotel or facility. For multi-day visits involving university, government, or industrial facility appointments, we can coordinate a full in-city transfer programme from a single itinerary. Our Nanjing city guide provides broader security and business environment context, and in-city close protection during your stay can be arranged through our close protection officers in Nanjing service.

What this covers

Operational detail for Nanjing

Lukou Airport Route and Journey Times

Nanjing Lukou International Airport (NKG) is located approximately 35km south-east of the city centre, connected to the urban core via the Airport Expressway (S341 and G25 Changshen Expressway corridor). Under normal conditions, the transfer to central Nanjing districts including Gulou, Xuanwu, Jianye, and the major hotel zone around Xinjiekou takes 40 to 60 minutes. Journey times extend to 70 to 90 minutes during morning peak (07:30-09:00) and evening peak (17:30-19:30) on weekdays, and during major festivals and public holidays when city traffic volumes increase significantly.

PSB-Compliant Driver Vetting Under MPS Order 564

All drivers operating under our Nanjing transfer service are vetted and compliant under MPS (Ministry of Public Security) Order 564, the national framework for private security service licensing in China, administered locally by the Nanjing Public Security Bureau. PSB compliance requires criminal background clearance, approved training certification, and ongoing operator registration. Driver documentation is maintained on file and available for inspection by corporate clients. We do not use informal or unlicensed operators in China, where non-compliance carries regulatory risk for both the provider and, potentially, the client.

Business Intelligence and Counterintelligence Briefing

Nanjing is a major centre for defence industry, electronics, petrochemicals, and automotive manufacturing, and hosts significant research institutions including the Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics. Business travellers in sectors with sensitivity to Chinese intellectual property enforcement, M&A activity, or joint-venture negotiations should treat the airport transfer as part of a wider operational security framework. Our Nanjing transfer briefing covers device security during transit, avoiding discussion of commercially sensitive matters in the vehicle, and practical digital hygiene steps relevant to travel through PRC infrastructure.

Digital Surveillance Awareness

China operates an extensive digital surveillance infrastructure, and Nanjing Lukou Airport is subject to standard PRC border security screening including potential device inspection. Our pre-travel briefing for Nanjing clients covers what to expect at the border, PRC regulations regarding VPNs and foreign applications, and practical preparation steps for devices, accounts, and communications before departure from the UK or country of origin. This briefing is informational; compliance with all PRC laws and border requirements is the client's responsibility.

Vehicle Standards and Driver Language Capability

Vehicles used for Nanjing transfers are maintained to a defined standard covering mechanical condition and suitability for the client profile. For senior delegations, a higher-specification vehicle is available on request. English-language capability among local drivers varies; where English communication throughout the transfer is required, we confirm language competency as part of the booking process and assign drivers accordingly. For non-English instructions during a multi-day visit, a Chinese-English interpreter escort can be arranged alongside the transfer driver.

University and Institutional Visitor Support

Nanjing's academic and research institutions attract visiting academics, government delegations, and technology sector representatives from the UK and internationally. Our transfer service supports these visitors with confirmed arrival logistics, hotel confirmation at destination, and the option of a full arrival briefing covering local transport, currency, mobile connectivity, and day-to-day precautions during the stay. Multi-leg academic and institutional programmes across Nanjing and onward connections to Shanghai, Beijing, or Suzhou can be coordinated as a single itinerary.

MPS Order 564 (Regulations on the Administration of Security Services) is the national framework under which Chinese private security operators must be licensed. For international clients, the practical significance is that a compliant operator has been vetted through the PRC state system, reducing the risk of using drivers with concealed criminal histories or professional misconduct records. Corporate clients with supply chain due-diligence requirements can request copies of operator PSB registration as part of their procurement documentation.
