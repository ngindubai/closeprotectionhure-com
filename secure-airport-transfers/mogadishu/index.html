Aden Adde International Airport (MGQ) operates within the Halane compound, the UN-controlled security zone that constitutes the primary secure perimeter in Mogadishu. Al-Shabaab detonated a vehicle-borne IED at the MGQ VIP departure zone in 2021, and the FCDO Somalia advisory (updated 2026) advises against all travel to Somalia. The Mogadishu arrival window

The Halane compound perimeter is the operational boundary for all principal movements in Mogadishu. The arrivals hall at MGQ is within the compound, and collection takes place inside this secured zone. The principal does not wait unescorted at any point outside the compound perimeter. This is not a precautionary measure for high-profile principals: it is the baseline for all business travellers, given the pattern of Al-Shabaab targeting of vehicles and soft targets associated with international organisations in the city.

The transfer from MGQ to the principal’s accommodation involves a road segment that may pass through areas of Mogadishu beyond the Halane perimeter. Al-Shabaab has repeatedly targeted vehicles on routes used by international organisations, and documented attacks on international hotels, including the 2019 assault on the Jazeera Palace Hotel, establish that the threat follows principals to their accommodation, not just to the airport. Route planning for Mogadishu transfers accounts for current Al-Shabaab activity intelligence and varies timing to avoid predictable patterns.

For organisations whose principals have no prior Mogadishu experience, the city’s security baseline is substantially different from any other regional capital. The combination of an active Al-Shabaab presence, limited independent emergency response capacity, and an immature private security regulatory framework means that a controlled transfer programme is the minimum appropriate arrangement.

What secure transfers cover in Mogadishu

Our Mogadishu programme provides a NISA/SPF-vetted driver, B6-armoured vehicle, Halane compound-based collection, two-minute-interval operations controller monitoring during road segments, pre-planned and varied routes, medevac pre-arrangement to Nairobi, and full coordination with the principal’s organisation throughout the transfer.

What this covers

Operational detail for Mogadishu

Halane Compound Collection at Aden Adde International Airport (MGQ)

Aden Adde International Airport (IATA: MGQ) is located within the Halane compound, a UN-controlled security zone that constitutes the primary secure perimeter in Mogadishu. The principal's arrival and all in-terminal collection take place within this compound perimeter. Our driver enters the Halane compound through the authorised vehicle gate with pre-cleared documentation and waits at the arrivals collection point inside the secured zone. The principal does not wait at any point outside the compound perimeter without an armed close protection escort. Al-Shabaab conducted a vehicle-borne IED attack at the VIP departure zone of MGQ in 2021, demonstrating that even the immediate airport environment is within the threat's operational reach. All movements associated with the transfer, from compound entry to destination arrival, are conducted in armoured vehicles. Standard saloon vehicles are not used for Mogadishu transfers under any circumstances.

NISA/SPF Alignment and Somalia Driver Compliance

Somalia's formal private security regulatory framework is nascent and the operating environment is governed in practice by alignment with the National Intelligence and Security Agency (NISA) and the Somali Police Force (SPF). All drivers engaged for principal transfers in Mogadishu hold SPF-recognised transport authorisation and are vetted through both NISA security screening and the Federal Government of Somalia's vehicle registration framework. Driver vetting in Mogadishu is a more intensive process than in standard programme cities, given the infiltration risk that Al-Shabaab has historically demonstrated against transportation networks serving international organisations. Each driver assignment for a Mogadishu transfer is confirmed by the local programme manager with a positive NISA clearance reference. Drivers are also briefed on the specific protocols for Halane compound exit procedures, the route from MGQ to the principal's destination in the secured zone, and armoured vehicle operation under threat conditions.

Operations Controller Throughout

The operations controller function for Mogadishu transfers operates at the highest protocol level in the programme. The controller confirms, before the principal's departure from the origin city, that all Halane compound access documentation is in place, that the armoured vehicle has been inspected, and that the medevac pre-arrangement is confirmed. During the transfer, communication with the driver is maintained at two-minute intervals during any movement outside the Halane compound perimeter. Any missed check-in triggers immediate escalation: the close protection team lead is contacted simultaneously with the principal's organisation. Emergency contact references are confirmed before departure: AMISOM/ATMIS emergency coordination (+252 61 559 8000), the British Embassy in Nairobi (which covers Somalia for UK nationals, +254 20 284 4000), and the pre-arranged medevac provider. The controller also holds current intelligence on Al-Shabaab activity within Mogadishu and adjusts transfer timing if a recent incident has elevated the threat on the planned route.

Return Departure Transfer

Return transfers from the principal's Mogadishu destination to MGQ are subject to the same armoured vehicle requirement and Halane compound access protocol as the arrival transfer. The operations controller schedules departure at least four hours before the international flight window, accounting for the Mogadishu transfer time, compound exit procedures, and the heightened security processing at the MGQ departures zone. The close protection team briefs the principal on departure-day procedures, including the vehicle loading protocol at the accommodation, the compound exit documentation check, and the expected timeline to the terminal. No luggage or personal equipment is left unattended at any point during the departure transfer. For early-morning departures, which are common for flights routing through Nairobi, the night protocol applies to the departure transfer. The principal does not proceed to the airside departure zone until the operations controller has confirmed that the airside environment is clear.

Al-Shabaab Attack Pattern and Armoured Vehicle Protocol

Al-Shabaab has sustained a Mogadishu operational presence since 2006 and has conducted attacks targeting the airport environment, international hotels, government buildings, and vehicles carrying foreign nationals. The 2021 MGQ VIP zone car bomb, the 2019 attack on the Jazeera Palace Hotel (a primary international accommodation), and repeated targeting of vehicles on routes used by international organisations all establish the transfer route as an active threat zone. Our Mogadishu protocol mandates armoured vehicles with B6-rated protection as the minimum standard for all principal transfers. Vehicles are swept for tracking devices before each transfer. Route timing is varied to avoid predictable patterns, and departure from the Halane compound is not announced on unsecured channels. The principal's accommodation selection is reviewed against known Al-Shabaab targeting patterns: locations with a high-profile international presence, multiple UN or NGO organisations on site, or a documented attack history receive an enhanced threat assessment before the transfer programme is confirmed. Medevac pre-arrangement to Nairobi's Aga Khan University Hospital or Nairobi Hospital is confirmed as a standard programme element before the principal travels.
