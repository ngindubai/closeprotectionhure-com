Diori Hamani International Airport (NIM) serves Niamey, the capital of Niger, in a post-coup operating environment that the FCDO Niger advisory (updated 2026) rates as critical risk, advising against all but essential travel. The July 2023 military coup established new checkpoint and movement protocols on the city’s road network, including the 10-kilometre transfer corridor between NIM and central Niamey, requiring drivers with current knowledge of CNSP compliance procedures and documentation requirements.

The Niamey arrival window

The arrivals hall at NIM is a modest facility where the exit to the kerb places passengers directly in an uncontrolled transport area. In the post-coup environment, this is a particularly inappropriate moment for a business principal to engage with unvetted transport, as the distinction between licensed and unlicensed operators is not easily made from the kerb. Inside-terminal collection removes this exposure entirely.

The 10-kilometre transfer from NIM to Plateau or Yantala passes through CNSP checkpoint zones where vehicles carrying foreign nationals may be stopped for documentation checks. The unpredictability of checkpoint behaviour noted in the FCDO Niger advisory means that all vehicles must carry a complete documentation set and drivers must be briefed on the specific compliance protocol required. A driver who is unfamiliar with CNSP checkpoint procedures creates unnecessary delay and potential escalation at these stops.

Night arrivals add a further protocol layer. The airport handles international traffic in the evenings, and the reduced police visibility on the transfer corridor after 2100 local time, combined with the general post-coup security environment, means that every night transfer operates under the enhanced protocol by default.

What secure transfers cover in Niamey

Our Niamey programme provides a DSP-licensed vetted driver, inside-terminal collection at the arrivals meeting point, real-time flight tracking, five-minute-interval operations controller monitoring, CNSP checkpoint compliance documentation, pre-planned primary and alternate routes to Plateau and Yantala, and full coordination with the principal’s organisation throughout the transfer.

What this covers

Operational detail for Niamey

Inside-Terminal Collection at Diori Hamani International Airport (NIM)

Diori Hamani International Airport (IATA: NIM) lies approximately 10 kilometres east of central Niamey. The terminal is a modest facility that handles a mix of international and regional West African traffic. Our driver enters the arrivals hall before the principal clears customs and immigration, and waits at the designated meeting point inside the terminal. There is no kerb collection. The arrivals exit at NIM opens directly onto an informal transport area, and for a principal arriving in a post-coup political environment, engagement with unvetted transport at this point is a significant risk vector. The inside-terminal collection procedure means the principal is accompanied from the customs exit to the vehicle without exposure to the kerb zone. Luggage is carried by the driver and loaded under supervision. For international flights arriving during evening or night hours, the driver enters the terminal building with a heightened briefing on current conditions on the transfer corridor between the airport and central Niamey.

DSP Licensing and Niger Driver Compliance

Private security activity in Niger is regulated under the framework of the Direction de la Securite Publique (DSP). All drivers engaged for principal transfers in Niamey hold current DSP authorisation and valid commercial driving licences under Nigerien transport authority regulations. Following the July 2023 military coup, the security regulatory environment in Niger has been subject to revision; our local programme manager maintains current awareness of DSP licensing requirements and updates driver certification records on the schedule prescribed by the authority. Our Niamey driver network undergoes background screening through DSP records and police clearance documentation before any driver is assigned to a principal transfer. The post-coup operating environment requires drivers to be familiar with the current checkpoint framework operated by the military government, including documentation requirements and compliance protocols for stops by CNSP (Conseil National pour la Sauvegarde de la Patrie) forces.

Operations Controller Throughout

A dedicated operations controller monitors every Niamey transfer from wheels-down to confirmed safe arrival. The controller tracks the inbound flight in real time, communicates the arrival window to the driver, and stages terminal entry to align with the principal's customs clearance time. During the 10-kilometre road transfer, encrypted mobile contact is maintained at five-minute intervals, reflecting the critical risk rating of the Niamey operating environment. Any missed check-in triggers immediate escalation: notification of the principal's organisation, and contact with the relevant embassy (British Embassy Niamey has limited operational capacity since the 2023 coup; the FCDO advises checking current consular arrangements before travel). Pre-trip information includes destination coordinates for Plateau or Yantala zone accommodation, the nearest medical facility with emergency capability, and the direct contact for the operations controller. The controller remains on active monitoring through to confirmed safe arrival.

Return Departure Transfer

Return transfers from Niamey city to NIM are scheduled by the operations controller at least three hours before the international departure window. The 10-kilometre corridor between Plateau or Yantala and the airport requires the same checkpoint-compliance preparation as the arrival transfer. The driver confirms vehicle and route with the controller two hours before collection. For early-morning departures, which represent the majority of international flight windows at NIM, the night-transfer heightened protocol applies. Luggage is staged under supervision at the accommodation departure point and loaded before the principal boards. The principal is accompanied from the accommodation entrance to the international departures entrance at the terminal. The driver remains at the terminal until the principal has entered the building and confirmed check-in. Any checkpoint encounter on the departure transfer is handled using the same documentation set and compliance protocol briefed before the arrival transfer.

Post-Coup Checkpoint and Night Transfer Protocol

Niger's July 2023 military coup brought a new security apparatus to the country's roads, with CNSP-aligned checkpoints operating on main routes in and around Niamey, including the corridor between NIM and the city centre. The FCDO Niger advisory (updated 2026) advises against all travel to Niger outside of essential purposes and specifically notes the unpredictable nature of checkpoint behaviour under the post-coup framework. Our post-coup checkpoint protocol for Niamey requires that all vehicles carry the full documentation set: driver DSP licence, commercial vehicle licence, principal travel documentation if requested, and the transfer authorisation letter from the engaging organisation. Drivers are briefed on the specific compliance behaviour required at CNSP checkpoints, including vehicle stop procedure, window lowering protocol, and the correct posture for documentation presentation. Night transfers carry an additional protocol layer: the heightened approach described above is triggered for any transfer taking place between 2100 and 0600 local time, including reduced speed on approach to checkpoint locations, active communication with the operations controller before and after each checkpoint stop, and pre-arranged check-in points with the principal's accommodation.
