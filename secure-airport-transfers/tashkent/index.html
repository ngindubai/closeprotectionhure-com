What this covers

Operational detail for Tashkent

Islam Karimov International Airport Collection

Islam Karimov International Airport (TAS) is located approximately 6 kilometres south of central Tashkent, making it one of the more conveniently positioned capital airports in Central Asia. The transfer to the Mirzo-Ulugbek and Yakkasaray business hotel zones takes 15 to 30 minutes under normal conditions. Inside-terminal collection with the driver positioned before the principal exits the arrivals hall is standard. Driver identity and vehicle registration are confirmed inside the terminal before departure.

SNB Surveillance Awareness During Transfers

Uzbekistan's Security Service (SNB) maintains surveillance infrastructure at Islam Karimov International Airport and in the approaches to the business zone. This is not a physical security risk to the principal but is a relevant information security consideration: conversations in vehicles, device usage, and the principal's communications discipline during the transfer apply from the terminal departure. Transfer vehicles are selected and managed to minimise non-vetted party access. The transfer brief includes the specific communications discipline that the SNB surveillance environment requires. Source: FCDO Uzbekistan travel advisory.

Currency Declaration at Arrival

Uzbekistan requires declaration of foreign currency above USD 2,000 on entry. Principals carrying large amounts of USD cash (which is standard for business visits given Uzbekistan's banking environment) should have the completed declaration form before proceeding through customs. The transfer driver is available to assist with confirmation of declaration requirements and to advise on the specific customs procedure at TAS. Retaining all currency exchange receipts throughout the visit is required. Using unofficial currency exchange at or near the airport creates legal exposure.

Central Tashkent Business Zone Route Management

The primary business destinations for international visitors are in the Mirzo-Ulugbek district (international hotels, embassies, and corporate offices), the Yakkasaray district (business and residential zone), and the Yunusabad upmarket residential area. The Hotel Hyatt Regency Tashkent, Wyndham Tashkent, and Lotte City Hotel Tashkent are among the principal international hotel properties. Transfer routing from TAS uses the main city approach roads without the congestion that affects larger capitals; Tashkent's road infrastructure is generally manageable by comparison with most regional peers.

Photography Restrictions and Route Compliance

Uzbekistan strictly prohibits photography of government buildings, military installations, police, and border infrastructure. Transfer vehicles passing or stopping near government buildings do not create specific legal risk if the principal and any team members are briefed on the prohibition and do not photograph from the vehicle. The transfer driver is briefed on which approach routes pass government infrastructure and is prepared to advise the principal if the vehicle is near a restricted photography zone.
