Operational detail for Riga

Threat Assessment: Riga

Riga presents a low ambient threat environment. The FCDO advises normal precautions for Latvia (2026), and the US State Department assigns Level 1 (Exercise Normal Precautions) status as of 2026. Violent crime rates are moderate by European standards, with opportunistic theft concentrated in Old Riga's tourist areas and around the Central Market. Since Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine in February 2022, the Baltic states have operated under a heightened strategic security awareness. Latvia shares a border with Russia (and with Belarus, which the FCDO advises against all but essential travel). Approximately 25% of Latvia's population is Russian-speaking, and the post-2022 period has brought increased awareness of information operations, surveillance of senior officials and business figures, and a heightened physical security posture among government and corporate principals. For foreign executives and diplomats operating in Riga, the practical on-ground threat from crime or targeted violence remains low, but the geopolitical backdrop warrants more rigorous anti-surveillance protocols than a standard EU capital assignment. The financial services sector concentrated around the Latvian Central Bank and Old Town, and the technology and logistics clusters in the Skanste business district, are the primary operating environments for corporate protection deployments.

Latvia Close Protection Licensing and Vetting

Close protection officers operating in Latvia must hold a licence issued under the Latvian Security Guards Law (Apsardzes darbibas likums, 2004, as amended). The State Police of Latvia (Valsts policija) is the competent authority for licensing both security companies and individual security officers. Applicants must pass a criminal record check, a medical and psychological fitness assessment, and a professional competency examination. Security companies must be registered with the State Police and carry liability insurance. Latvia is an EU and Schengen member state, and EU CPOs may operate on short-term service provision engagements subject to notification requirements. For non-EU CPOs, a work permit and professional recognition procedure apply for sustained deployments. The post-2022 security environment has made Latvian authorities more attentive to the background and affiliations of security personnel operating in the country; all operators should ensure documentation is thorough and current. CloseProtectionHire.com operators undergo multi-point vetting covering criminal record disclosure, identity verification, employment history, and professional qualification verification. Officers carry current SIA-equivalent or nationally recognised close protection qualifications.

Airport and Arrival Security

Riga International Airport (IATA: RIX) is located approximately 10 kilometres southwest of the city centre in the Skulte area. It is the largest airport in the Baltic states, handling approximately 7 million passengers annually. The commercial arrivals hall is a single-level concourse. For principals requiring a controlled arrival, meet-and-greet can be arranged inside the arrivals hall or, for private aviation arrivals, through the Riga Airport VIP terminal. Business aviation at RIX is handled through the VIP terminal on the northwest apron, which provides separation from commercial arrivals. The primary transfer route from RIX to the city centre follows Maskavas iela and continues via Brivibas iela or the Vagonu iela route towards Old Riga and the Skanste business district. Journey time is approximately 15-25 minutes depending on traffic. The route passes through mixed residential and commercial areas with no particular security concern under normal conditions. Arrival timing should account for morning rush-hour congestion (07:30-09:00) on the airport road. For principals staying in Old Riga hotels, vehicle access to the historic centre is restricted; drop-off protocols should be confirmed in advance with accommodation providers.

Movement and Route Security

Route security in Riga requires awareness of two operating zones: Old Riga (Vecriga), a UNESCO-listed historic district with partial pedestrianisation and narrow streets, and the wider commercial city including the Skanste business district, the Central Station area, and the suburbs where residential and government facilities are concentrated. Vehicle movement in Old Riga is constrained; permit-only access applies to the core area, and CPO teams should plan for foot movement with vehicles positioned at permitted drop zones on Kalku iela, Torna iela, or Kr. Barona iela. Anti-surveillance drills are appropriate for principals operating in the financial sector or those connected to government and EU institutions, given the heightened intelligence environment in the Baltic states post-2022. Route surveys should document the location of the nearest police station and emergency medical facility relative to each planned venue. The Daugava River creates a geographic division; bridges at Akmens tilts (Stone Bridge), Vansu tilts, and Dienvidu tilts are the primary crossing points and can experience congestion during peak hours. Vetted security drivers with established knowledge of Riga's traffic patterns are a mandatory component of any close protection deployment.

Residential and Hotel Security

Principal accommodation options in Riga include the Grand Palace Hotel (Old Riga), the Pullman Riga Old Town, the Radisson Blu Latvija Hotel (a landmark tower at Elizabetes 55), and the Kempinski Hotel Riga. The Radisson Blu Latvija and the Grand Palace are the most commonly selected properties for senior corporate and diplomatic principals due to their security infrastructure and proximity to the city's key business and government districts. For principals staying in private residences or rented apartments, a residential security survey should be completed on arrival covering access control, entrance monitoring, staff vetting, and evacuation route identification. Riga's Old Town has numerous boutique hotels in historic properties; these can offer discretion but have limited vehicle access and may lack modern fire safety and emergency egress provisions, which should be assessed during an advance survey. The Skanste district, where a number of international companies and the Latvian National Library are located, offers more modern residential options with better vehicle access and less constrained movement.

Emergency Response and Medical Cover

The primary acute care facility for trauma and emergency medicine in Riga is Pauls Stradins Clinical University Hospital (Paula Stradina Kliniska universitates slimnica), located at Pilsonu 13, approximately 3 kilometres from Old Riga. It is Latvia's largest hospital and reference centre for trauma, cardiac, and neuro surgery. Riga East University Hospital (Rigas Austrumu kliniska universitates slimnica) is a secondary facility. For private medical care, American Clinic Riga and the ORTO Clinic provide English-language services. Medical evacuation for specialist intervention is typically routed to Stockholm (approximately 1 hour 20 minutes by direct flight from RIX) or Helsinki (approximately 55 minutes). Air Baltic and other carriers operate multiple daily services to both cities. For time-critical medevac, fixed-wing air ambulance from RIX to Stockholm is the standard protocol. Emergency contact numbers: European emergency number 112, Latvian Police 110, Ambulance 113. CPO teams maintain a pre-briefed medical protocol for each principal and are briefed on the location of the nearest appropriate facility for each venue and route on the deployment itinerary.
