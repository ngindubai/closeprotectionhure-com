Operational detail for Verona

Security Framework in Italy

Private security in Italy is regulated under Legislative Decree 153/2009, the reform of the historic Testo Unico delle Leggi di Pubblica Sicurezza (TULPS) framework. Companies must be licensed by the Prefettura of the relevant province; in Verona the licensing authority is the Prefettura di Verona. Individual close protection officers require a separate authorisation from the Questura di Verona before deployment. Armed close protection carries additional requirements: a specific Questura firearms authorisation is required, satisfying training and character criteria separate from the base CPO authorisation. In practice, almost all commercial close protection engagements in Verona are conducted on an unarmed basis. EU-licensed security officers from other member states may operate in Italy subject to local Questura registration under freedom of movement provisions. Clients commissioning bodyguard hire in Verona should request documentation confirming the company's Prefettura licence and the individual Questura authorisation for each deployed CPO. The TULPS-derived framework applies consistently across northern Italy; enforcement standards in Verona and the Veneto region are active and well-established.

Threat Environment

The FCDO Travel Advice for Italy, updated 2024, rates Italy as low overall risk. Verona presents no exceptional security hazard for visiting executives or HNWI principals beyond the petty crime patterns documented in the broader Italy advisory. Pickpocketing is the primary concern for tourist-area movement: the Arena di Verona, Piazza Bra, and Ponte Pietra are the highest-risk locations during the tourist season from April through September, when visitor density is highest and the conditions for distraction theft are most favourable. Verona Porta Nuova railway station, the main rail hub serving the city, is documented as a petty crime location for distraction theft, consistent with the pattern at all major Italian intercity rail stations. Vehicle crime at motorway service stations on the A4 corridor (the main Milan-Venice motorway passing south of Verona) is noted in ACI (Automobile Club d'Italia) guidance; this is relevant for principals making road transfers east to Venice or west to Milan. Italian FCDO advice recommends avoiding any gatherings or demonstrations; in Verona, occasional demonstrations in Piazza Bra or outside the Questura are possible but infrequent. There is no terrorism concern specific to Verona.

Key Operational Areas

The Citta Antica, Verona's historic walled city on the north bank of the Adige river, is the primary tourist zone and the location of the Arena di Verona, Piazza Bra, and the concentration of HNWI hotels and restaurants. It is well-policed but has the highest ambient petty crime density during summer and opera season. The Veronafiere exhibition complex, located to the south of the city beyond Porta Nuova station, is the primary venue for Vinitaly and other major trade events; it is a managed, ticketed environment with its own security infrastructure. Lake Garda, 15 to 30km to the west, takes in the resort towns of Peschiera del Garda, Bardolino, Lazise, and Sirmione, all of which are HNWI leisure destinations; Sirmione in particular, on a peninsula in the southern lake, is an exclusive villa and hotel destination with limited road access. The Valpolicella wine production zone lies to the northwest of Verona; estate visits to Amarone and Valpolicella producers require rural transfer planning. Key hotels include Due Torri Hotel (Piazza Sant'Anastasia 4, in the historic centre), Hotel Gabbia d'Oro (Corso Porta Borsari 4a), and Hotel Byblos Art Hotel Villa Amista at Corrubbio di Negarine in the Valpolicella zone.

Business and HNWI Protection

Verona generates CPO demand from a set of specific industries that distinguish it from other northern Italian cities. Vinitaly, held annually in April at the Veronafiere exhibition centre, is consistently ranked as the world's leading wine trade fair; it brings together senior buyers, investors, and producers from every major wine market globally. HNWI visitors attending Vinitaly or conducting pre- or post-fair estate visits in Valpolicella, Soave, or Bardolino require close protection adapted to the combination of trade fair environments and rural estate access. The automotive sector generates a distinct visitor flow: Verona sits at the junction of the A4 Milan-Venice and A22 Brenner motorways, making it an accessible stop for executives travelling between the supercar manufacturing estates of Emilia-Romagna (Ferrari at Maranello, Lamborghini at Sant'Agata Bolognese, Maserati in Modena) and Venice or Munich. Verona Quadrante Europa, Italy's largest inland freight terminal and a major logistics hub for northern Italian and European freight, generates logistics and supply chain executive visits with specific transfer and site access requirements. HNWI principals visiting Lake Garda properties, particularly the exclusive Sirmione peninsula, require transfer planning that accounts for the limited road access and seasonal traffic volumes on the Garda lakeside roads.

Airport and Transit Cover

Verona's airport is Valerio Catullo Airport (IATA: VRN), officially Verona Villafranca Airport, located 14km southwest of the city centre at Villafranca di Verona with a standard transfer time of 20 to 25 minutes via the A4 or A22 motorways. VRN handles a range of European routes and has general aviation handling facilities for private and business aviation arrivals. The airport's location adjacent to the A4-A22 motorway interchange makes it well-positioned for transfers either to the city centre or directly to Lake Garda resort destinations without passing through the city. Venice Marco Polo Airport (IATA: VCE), 110km east at approximately 90 minutes by road, is an alternative gateway for some international routes and is used by principals whose Verona visit is part of a Venice-Verona itinerary. Transfer from VRN to the city centre requires awareness of the A22 Brenner motorway junction and the Porta Nuova access to the city, which can be congested during peak morning and evening periods. For Lake Garda transfers direct from VRN, the A4 westbound to Peschiera del Garda exit provides a direct route without city centre transit.

Emergency Support

The main hospital in Verona is the Azienda Ospedaliera Universitaria Integrata (AOUI) Verona, located at Piazzale A. Stefani 1 and reachable at +39 045 812 1111. AOUI is the university teaching hospital complex and provides emergency, trauma, and specialist medical services for the Verona province. For principals based at Lake Garda, Casa di Cura Pederzoli (private, +39 045 663 9000, at Peschiera del Garda) is the nearest private medical facility of note. Emergency services in Italy operate on national numbers: 118 for medical emergencies and ambulance, 113 for Polizia di Stato, 112 as the pan-European emergency number, and 115 for the Vigili del Fuoco fire service. Consular assistance for British nationals is provided by the British Embassy in Rome (+39 06 4220 0001); there is no British consulate in Verona, and the Embassy handles all Veneto region matters. US nationals in Verona are served by the US Consulate General Milan (+39 02 290 351), which has consular jurisdiction over the Veneto region. Medical evacuation for principals requiring repatriation is coordinated through the travel insurance provider and the relevant embassy, with the CPO team providing initial scene management and liaison with AOUI emergency services.
