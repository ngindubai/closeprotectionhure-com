Operational detail for Vilnius

Threat Assessment: Vilnius

Vilnius presents a low ambient threat environment for routine corporate and diplomatic visits. The FCDO advises normal precautions for Lithuania (2026), and the US State Department assigns Level 1 (Exercise Normal Precautions) status as of 2026. Violent crime targeting foreign nationals is rare, and the city has a stable, EU-standard law enforcement environment. Lithuania's strategic geography significantly shapes the security context for professionals operating in the country. Lithuania borders Belarus to the east, a country to which the FCDO advises against all but essential travel, and Russia's Kaliningrad exclave to the west. The Suwalki Gap, the narrow land corridor between Kaliningrad and Belarus that connects Lithuania to Poland, is one of NATO's most discussed strategic vulnerabilities. Lithuania was the host of the 2023 NATO Vilnius Summit, which reinforced the country's position at the centre of Alliance eastern flank policy. For foreign executives in the fintech, logistics, and shared service centre sectors that dominate Vilnius's corporate economy, the practical on-ground risk is low. However, principals connected to defence, energy, or government sectors warrant anti-surveillance protocols commensurate with the geopolitical context. The Snipiskes business district, the historic Old Town, and the Pasilaiciai logistics corridor are the primary operating environments for close protection deployments.

Lithuania Close Protection Licensing and Vetting

Close protection officers operating in Lithuania must hold a licence issued under the Lithuanian Law on Private Security Activities (No. IX-1185, 2002, as amended). The Lithuanian Police Department (Policijos departamentas) is the competent authority for issuing licences to both security companies and individual security officers. Licence applicants must pass a criminal background check, a medical fitness assessment, and a professional competency examination administered by the Police Department. Security companies must be registered with the Police Department, hold liability insurance, and maintain a register of their licensed operatives. Lithuania is an EU and Schengen member state; EU CPOs may operate on short-term service provision engagements subject to advance notification. For non-EU CPOs, professional recognition procedures and work permit requirements apply to sustained deployments. The Lithuanian Police Department has increased scrutiny of security operator documentation and affiliations since 2022. All CloseProtectionHire.com operators undergo multi-point vetting covering criminal record disclosure, identity verification, employment history, and professional qualification verification. Officers carry current SIA-equivalent or nationally recognised close protection qualifications and hold appropriate professional indemnity insurance.

Airport and Arrival Security

Vilnius Airport (IATA: VNO) is located approximately 7 kilometres south of the city centre, making it one of Europe's most convenient capital airports for principal transfers. The airport handles approximately 4-5 million passengers annually. Commercial arrivals are processed through a single modern terminal. For principals requiring an enhanced arrival profile, meet-and-greet can be arranged in the arrivals hall or via the separate VIP lounge managed by the airport's ground handling team. Private aviation arrivals at Vilnius use the general aviation apron on the eastern side of the airport, and advance coordination with handlers is required for airside access. The primary transfer route from VNO to the city centre uses Rodunios kelias and Savanoriai prospektas, reaching the Snipiskes business district and the Old Town in approximately 15-20 minutes under normal traffic conditions. Alternative routes via Trakiai are available. Arrival timing should account for morning (08:00-09:00) and evening (17:00-19:00) rush-hour congestion on Savanoriai prospektas. For principals with onward travel to Kaunas or across the western border, route surveys should document the relevant transit routes and border crossing points.

Movement and Route Security

Route security in Vilnius operates across three main zones: the historic Old Town (Senamiestis), a UNESCO-listed district with significant pedestrianisation and restricted vehicle access; the Snipiskes modern business district across the Neris River, where the Europa Tower, the Vilnius Gates of Dawn Business Centre, and major hotel and corporate addresses are concentrated; and the outer suburbs, including the Pasilaiciai logistics park and the Grigiskes industrial zone. Vehicle movement in the Old Town is constrained by permit-only access and bollard-controlled entry points. CPO teams should plan for mixed vehicle and foot coverage in the historic centre, with vehicles positioned at Gedimino prospektas or the Rotuses square approach roads. Anti-surveillance protocols are a routine component of Vilnius deployments given the country's geographic and geopolitical context. Route surveys document police station locations, nearest emergency medical facilities, and vehicle access constraints for each planned venue. The Neris River divides the city; crossing points at the Green Bridge, Mindaugas Bridge, and Zverynas Bridge are the primary vehicle crossing options. Vetted security drivers familiar with Vilnius's one-way system and congestion patterns are a mandatory component of any deployment.

Residential and Hotel Security

Principal accommodation options in Vilnius include the Kempinski Hotel Cathedral Square (Old Town), the Radisson Blu Hotel Lietuva (Snipiskes), the Pacai Hotel (Old Town), and the Grand Hotel Kempinski Vilnius. The Radisson Blu Lietuva is positioned directly in the business district and offers the most practical access for principals with meetings in the corporate zone, with a vehicle drop-off lane on Konstitucijos prospektas and established protocols for VIP arrivals. The Kempinski Cathedral Square and the Pacai are preferred for principals requiring a prestigious Old Town address, but vehicle access to these properties requires advance coordination due to pedestrianisation. For principals staying in private residences, a residential security survey should be completed on arrival, covering access control, staff vetting, entrance monitoring, and evacuation route identification. The relatively compact geography of Vilnius means that most corporate, government, and diplomatic venues are within a 5-15 minute drive of the main hotel cluster, which simplifies daily itinerary planning and reduces exposure time during principal movements.

Emergency Response and Medical Cover

The primary acute care facility in Vilnius is Santaros Klinikos (Vilnius University Hospital Santaros Klinikos), located at Santariskiu 2, approximately 7 kilometres north of the city centre. It is Lithuania's largest teaching hospital and the national reference centre for trauma, cardiac surgery, and neurology. For private medical care, the Northway Medical Centre (Seskines 24) and the Northway Children's Clinic provide English-language services and a private GP function. Medical evacuation for specialist intervention not available in Lithuania is typically routed to Helsinki (approximately 1 hour 15 minutes direct flight from VNO) or Warsaw (approximately 1 hour direct). Both routes have multiple daily commercial services, and air ambulance charter from VNO is available through Lithuanian and regional operators. Emergency contact numbers: European emergency number 112, Lithuanian Police 112, Ambulance 112. CPO teams maintain a pre-briefed medical protocol for each principal covering blood type, allergies, existing conditions, and the preferred treatment facility. Teams are additionally briefed on the implications of the Belarus and Kaliningrad borders for medevac routing, as certain cross-border options are restricted under current sanctions regimes.
