New York State licensing

The NYS Division of Licensing Services regulates close protection work in New York State under the Security Guard Act of 1992. The licensing framework covers training requirements, criminal record checks, and annual registration renewal. The specific requirements for armed security guards are more stringent and involve separate authorisation under the NYS Penal Law. Operating company registration is separate from individual guard registration.

NYS Division of Licensing Services

Private security and close protection work in New York State is regulated by the New York State Division of Licensing Services (NYSDOS) under the Security Guard Act of 1992. Security guards and CPOs must hold a valid NYS security guard registration. Armed security guards require a separate armed security guard licence and compliance with the NYS Penal Law for carry authorisation. Operating companies are separately registered.

HNWI and Corporate Executive Protection

New York's concentration of HNWI and UHNWI individuals, financial sector executives, entertainment figures, and technology leaders produces a large and sophisticated CPO market. Targeted threats, including stalking, social media-driven incidents, and specific sector-related threats, are the primary engagement drivers for Manhattan-based CPO work. Ambient crime in Midtown and the Upper East Side is low relative to most international business cities.

Manhattan District and Venue Coverage

Operational familiarity with Manhattan's business and residential zones: Midtown (financial district, corporate headquarters), the Upper East Side (residential HNWI), Tribeca and SoHo (HNWI residential), and the specific security protocols at major hotels (The Mark, Four Seasons 57th, Mandarin Oriental), corporate campuses, and performing arts venues.

Specific Threat Assessment

New York CPO engagements are typically driven by a specific identified threat: a credible stalker, an on-record threat communication, a sector-related risk (financial services, political adjacency, public figure profile), or documented surveillance. The threat assessment shapes the CPO team configuration, profile, and the specific protocols applied.

JFK, Newark, and LaGuardia Transfer Integration

CPO cover for New York visits typically includes the airport transfer. JFK Terminal 4 (international arrivals) and Newark Terminal B/C cover most major international carriers. LaGuardia serves domestic routes. Inside-terminal collection by a state-licensed vetted driver is the standard for CPO-covered arrivals.

Multi-State Operations

New York CPO engagements frequently extend to New Jersey, Connecticut, and Washington DC. CPOs operating across state lines must comply with the licensing requirements of each state they work in. Operating companies with multi-state coverage handle the licensing compliance; clients confirm this capability before engagement.
