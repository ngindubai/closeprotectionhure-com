Operational detail for Turin

Security Framework in Italy

Private security in Italy is regulated under Legislative Decree 153/2009, the reform of the Testo Unico delle Leggi di Pubblica Sicurezza (TULPS) framework. Companies providing close protection services must hold a licence issued by the Prefettura of the province in which they operate. In Turin, the relevant authority is the Prefettura di Torino. Individual security personnel require a separate authorisation from the Questura di Torino before they may be deployed. Armed close protection requires additional Questura firearms authorisation and satisfies specific training and suitability requirements. In practice, virtually all commercial bodyguard hire engagements in Turin are conducted on an unarmed basis; armed cover is reserved for a small category of elevated-threat principals. EU-licensed security officers from other member states may operate in Italy subject to local Questura registration under freedom of movement provisions. Clients should request confirmation of the Prefettura company licence and the individual Questura authorisation for each CPO before engagement. Enforcement of the regulatory framework is consistent and active in Piedmont, and providers operating without valid licences expose clients to compliance risk.

Threat Environment

The FCDO Travel Advice for Italy, updated 2024, classifies Italy as low overall risk. Turin reflects this national picture, with no exceptional security hazard for visiting business travellers. Petty crime is the primary operational concern: pickpocketing is documented near Porta Nuova railway station, around Piazza San Carlo, and in the pedestrian shopping areas of Via Roma and Via Garibaldi. Neighbourhood variation within Turin is notable; areas such as Mirafiori Sud and Barriera di Milano carry higher background crime rates, though these are not business visitor zones and are rarely relevant to executive protection itineraries. Turin has a tradition of left-wing political activism and a history of industrial dispute days; protest activity related to labour relations in the automotive and aerospace sectors does occur and can affect city centre movement. ACI (Automobile Club d'Italia) guidance notes vehicle crime at service stations along the A4 motorway corridor connecting Turin to Milan, which is relevant for principals making that transfer by road. There is no terrorism concern specific to Turin; Italy's national advisory level applies.

Key Operational Areas

The primary business zones for CPO operations in Turin include Crocetta, a residential and professional district with lower ambient crime and a concentration of legal and financial offices; Piazza Castello, the historic city centre core, which is well-policed but sees elevated tourist density at peak periods; and Lingotto, the former Fiat factory complex in the south of the city now converted to a convention centre, exhibition spaces, and offices, including the Lingotto Fiere exhibition venue. The Lingotto Oval is a significant corporate event venue and conference space. Rivoli, a former royal residence to the west of Turin, is an HNWI residential area and an occasional destination for private client movements. The Piedmont wine country to the south, encompassing Barolo, Barbaresco, Monferrato, and the Langhe, is an important HNWI leisure and investment destination. Key hotels for principal coverage include Grand Hotel Sitea (Via Carlo Alberto 35), NH Collection Torino Piazza Carlina, and Hotel Le Meridien Lingotto (Via Nizza 262), which occupies the former Fiat rooftop test track and is the standard choice for automotive industry visitors.

Business and HNWI Protection

Turin generates a specific and consistent demand for executive close protection, driven by the concentration of automotive and aerospace industry headquarters in the city and its environs. Stellantis (formerly Fiat Chrysler Automobiles), Pininfarina, Bertone, and Italdesign all have significant Turin presences, creating a regular flow of senior automotive executives attending plant visits, design reviews, and joint venture meetings. Leonardo SpA and Avio Aero maintain aerospace and defence operations in the Turin area, with a security-sensitive visitor profile associated with defence contracting. The Artissima contemporary art fair, held annually in November at the Oval Lingotto, brings international collectors and gallery principals to the city with HNWI protection requirements. Piedmont wine estate visits for buyers and investors in Barolo, Barbaresco, and the broader Langhe zone require rural close protection adapted to single-road estate access and extended stays at isolated properties. Luxury goods sector activity is present, though less concentrated than in Milan.

Airport and Transit Cover

Turin's primary commercial airport is Torino Caselle Airport (IATA: TRN), located 16km north of the city centre with a standard transfer time of 25 to 30 minutes via the A55 Torino-Caselle motorway. TRN serves a range of European routes; private aviation handling facilities are available at the airport. For principals arriving on intercontinental connections, Milan Malpensa Airport (IATA: MXP) is an alternative gateway at 130km from Turin, with a transfer time of approximately 90 minutes by road. The A4 motorway corridor connecting Malpensa to Turin passes through areas with documented vehicle crime at motorway service stations, a factor in route planning for this transfer. CPO cover for airport transfers from TRN typically involves vehicle pre-positioning at the domestic arrivals area, airside coordination with the airline or handling agent where possible, and routing to the principal's Turin hotel or meeting venue. Lingotto transfers require specific awareness of the access arrangements for the Lingotto Fiere and hotel complex.

Emergency Support

The main public hospital in Turin is Ospedale Molinette, part of the Citta della Salute e della Scienza di Torino complex, reachable at +39 011 633 1633, which provides emergency, trauma, and specialist services. Private medical cover is available at Clinica Fornaca di Sessant (+39 011 569 7050) and other private clinics in the Crocetta and Santa Rita areas. Emergency services operate on Italian national numbers: 118 for medical emergencies and ambulance dispatch, 113 for Polizia di Stato, 112 as the pan-European emergency number, and 115 for fire services. For British nationals, consular assistance is provided by the British Embassy in Rome (+39 06 4220 0001); there is no dedicated British consulate in Turin. US nationals are served by the US Embassy Rome (+39 06 46741) and the US Consulate General Milan (+39 02 290 351), which has consular jurisdiction over Piedmont. Medical evacuation for principals requiring repatriation is arranged through the travel insurance provider; the CPO team manages initial scene care and coordinates with emergency services and consular contacts.
