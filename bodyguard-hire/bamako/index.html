Operational detail for Bamako

Ministry of Internal Security Licensing

Private security companies in Mali must hold licensing under the Ministry of Internal Security and Civil Protection (MISPC). Post-coup regulatory changes under the junta affect the commercial security sector; all our Bamako partners hold current MISPC-authorised operating status appropriate to the current governance environment. Armed security is standard for principals in Bamako's threat environment and requires specific Ministry authorisation.

JNIM Terrorism Awareness and Soft-Target Protocol

JNIM has demonstrated sustained attack capability in Bamako, including hotel sieges. Soft-target assessment is a mandatory component of every Bamako bodyguard detail: advance assessment of all hotels, restaurants, and meeting venues for access points, exits, and secondary escape routes; no accommodation in hotels with poor perimeter security or low international operator standards; and a standing brief on current JNIM activity from sources monitoring the Sahel. Source: FCDO Mali travel advice, 2025.

Anti-Kidnapping Protocol

Kidnapping targeting western nationals is documented in Mali. FCDO advises that western nationals face a significant kidnapping risk throughout the country, including Bamako. Anti-kidnapping protocol covers: no predictable movement patterns, no disclosure of the principal's identity to non-vetted parties, avoidance of areas and times with elevated kidnapping risk (particularly airport approaches and hotel vicinity after dark), and an immediate response protocol if any suspicious approach is made.

Military Rule and Junta Awareness

Mali's CNSP junta has expelled French and UN security presences and aligned with Russia's Africa Corps. The political environment is hostile to western government presence and sensitive regarding perceived political commentary. Bodyguard operations avoid any activity or communication that could be construed as political interference; Africa Corps presence and Malian military checkpoints are managed with strict compliance and minimal engagement.

Bamako-Senou Airport Transfer

Bamako-Senou International Airport (BKO) is approximately 15 kilometres from the city centre. Inside-terminal collection before the principal exits arrivals and immediate departure from the airport zone are the standard protocol. Night arrivals at BKO require a heightened transfer protocol. The airport approach road is an identified kidnapping risk point; no extended wait at arrivals is acceptable.
