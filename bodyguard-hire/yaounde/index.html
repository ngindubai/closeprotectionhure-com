Operational detail for Yaounde

Threat Assessment: Yaounde

Yaounde's executive threat environment operates on two distinct levels. Within the capital, petty crime, robbery, kidnapping risk in areas outside the Bastos diplomatic district, and the standard urban security environment of a developing-world capital city are the primary concerns. FCDO Cameroon advice (2024) advises a high degree of caution throughout Cameroon. US State Department designates Cameroon as Level 3: Reconsider Travel (2026). At the national level, Cameroon's regional conflicts add a macro-context that any executive visiting Yaounde must understand: the Far North region faces Boko Haram terrorism (FCDO Level 4, do not travel); the North-West and South-West regions face an Anglophone separatist conflict ongoing since 2017 with documented attacks on civilians and security forces (FCDO Level 4, do not travel). Any itinerary that extends beyond Yaounde toward these regions requires a separate and significantly more intensive security plan. Within the capital itself, the Bastos district and Centre Ville are workable for business operations with professional CP support.

Cameroon CPO Licensing and Vetting

Close protection operations in Cameroon require authorisation from the MINAT (Ministere de l'Administration Territoriale). Security companies must hold valid MINAT operating licences and individual CPOs must carry credentials from the relevant authority. The Cameroon private security sector includes both MINAT-compliant and non-compliant operators; verification of both company and individual credentials is essential before any deployment. Armed CPO operations require specific MINAT authorisation and may require additional coordination with relevant security authorities depending on the deployment location and risk level. Foreign CP operators work through MINAT-authorised Cameroonian partner companies. Regional travel outside Yaounde, particularly toward conflict-affected areas, requires additional authorisation and planning well beyond the standard capital-city CP package.

Route Security and Movement Planning

Yaounde CP route planning confines all principal movement to the Bastos district and Centre Ville corridors. Bastos is the embassy and diplomatic residential district in the north of Yaounde; Centre Ville is the main commercial and government area. Outer residential areas, suburbs to the south and east, and the approaches to areas outside the main city zone carry elevated robbery and petty crime risk. The Nsimalen airport transfer road (25 kilometres south) carries specific robbery risk at night; security drivers treat this transfer as a heightened-risk operation. Night movement in outer Yaounde areas is minimised. Any movement request toward regional conflict areas is treated as a separate high-risk operation requiring enhanced planning.

Residential and Hotel Security

International hotels in Yaounde are primarily located in or near the Bastos district and Centre Ville, including properties used by the diplomatic community. These hotels provide an appropriate accommodation base for visiting executives. CPO teams assess hotel access control, lobby security, parking, and room selection. Embassy proximity in Bastos is useful as an emergency coordination resource and CPO teams maintain current embassy contact information. For extended or HNWI engagements, residential security assessment in Bastos covers compound perimeter security, access control, and alarm systems.

Emergency Response and Medical Cover

Medical facilities in Yaounde include Yaounde Central Hospital for public acute care and Centre Medic Yaounde and private clinics in the Bastos area for private care. For complex trauma, medical evacuation to Europe (France, given Cameroon's ties) or to South Africa is the appropriate plan for the most serious cases; this is confirmed with the principal's travel health insurer before deployment. CPO teams carry first aid provisions and the operations controller holds current medical facility contacts. Emergency contacts include Cameroon emergency services and the principal's national embassy in Yaounde. Embassy contact details are verified as current before deployment.

Close Protection Team Configuration

For most executive visits to Yaounde, a two-CPO team with dedicated security driver and operations controller oversight is the recommended starting configuration. The 25-kilometre Nsimalen airport transfer, the proximity of higher-risk areas to the Bastos zone, and the kidnapping risk documented in Cameroon's broader security environment make a two-person team more appropriate than a single CPO for all but the lowest-profile visits. Armed CP is available under MINAT authorisation and is recommended for executives in the extractive sectors or those with a specific threat environment. Any itinerary element beyond Yaounde toward conflict-affected regions requires its own enhanced security plan, separate from the Yaounde package.
