Operational detail for Bordeaux

Licensing Framework

Private security in France is governed by Loi 83-629 (as amended), with licensing and oversight managed by the Conseil National des Activites Privees de Securite (CNAPS). Both operating companies and individual close protection officers must hold current CNAPS authorisations; the agency conducts character, background, and training checks before issuing credentials. Armed close protection requires specific additional authorisation and is rarely granted for routine commercial work in Bordeaux; the standard for engagements here is unarmed. Non-EU nationals wishing to operate as CPOs in France are subject to full French licensing requirements without the simplified EU recognition pathway. Clients should request the operator's CNAPS company authorisation and the individual CPO's CNAPS professional card before any deployment commences.

Threat Environment

The FCDO France Travel Advice (2024) notes that France operates at Vigipirate Urgence Attentat nationally, its highest terrorism alert level. While there is no specific elevated indicator for Bordeaux, the national posture means heightened security presence at Gare Saint-Jean, the airport, and major public venues. The day-to-day threat for visiting executives in Bordeaux is petty crime in the city centre and around Gare Saint-Jean. Bordeaux has also experienced periodic protest activity linked to national political issues, including Yellow Vest demonstrations and pension-reform protests; gatherings have concentrated at Place de la Victoire and Cours de l'Intendance and can disrupt principal movement in the city centre. The aerospace and defence sector, including Dassault Aviation, Thales, and MBDA operations in the Merignac and Bordeaux-Lac zones, raises the profile of some visiting executives and warrants proportionate discreet cover.

Key Operational Areas

CPO operations in Bordeaux span four environments. The Place de la Bourse and Miroir d'Eau waterfront is the principal tourist and corporate hospitality zone, hosting major hotels and dining venues. The Chartrons wine district, north of the centre, is the base for the wine-trade merchant houses and is a venue for Vinexpo and premium wine-buyer meetings requiring discreet cover. Gare Saint-Jean, the TGV rail terminus connecting to Paris Montparnasse (3 hours), is the primary arrival point for most visiting executives and carries moderate petty-crime exposure. The Merignac industrial and aerospace zone, adjacent to Bordeaux-Merignac Airport, hosts Dassault Aviation, Thales, and MBDA facilities requiring vehicle-staged access co-ordination.

Close Protection Services

Standard Bordeaux details cover principal movement between hotel, aerospace facility, and wine-trade venue; venue advance at Merignac aerospace campuses and Chartrons wine-merchant offices; discreet vehicle escort through the city and along the RN89 to Medoc estate visits; and crowd management during major wine trade events such as Vinexpo. The wine-trade itinerary, extending into the Medoc, Pauillac, and Saint-Emilion chateau circuit, requires rural close protection cover adapted to estate-access road conditions and extended stays without urban infrastructure. Defence-sector executives visiting Dassault and Thales facilities require advance co-ordination with facility security and vehicle-staging at access-controlled industrial gates. Officers operate in attire appropriate to each environment.

Airport and Transit Cover

Bordeaux-Merignac Airport (BOD) sits 12km west of the city centre with a standard road transfer of 20 to 25 minutes. BOD serves European and intercontinental routes; arrival cover includes confirmed inside-terminal collection, route-planned transfer, and operations-controller tracking to the principal's hotel or onward destination. Gare Saint-Jean TGV arrivals receive the same collection and tracking protocol adapted to the station environment. The airport's proximity to the Merignac aerospace zone allows combined airport-collection-and-site-visit itineraries with minimal additional transfer time.

Communications and Contingency

Every detail operates an operations-controller communications protocol with the French emergency baseline: 112 (pan-European all emergencies), 17 (Police), 15 (SAMU medical), and 18 (Pompiers fire). The principal hospital is CHU de Bordeaux, Hopital Pellegrin, reachable at +33 5 57 82 00 00. The British Embassy Paris (+33 1 44 51 31 00) handles consular assistance for British nationals in Aquitaine; the US Consulate Bordeaux (+33 5 56 48 63 80) provides US consular services locally. Medical evacuation co-ordination is managed through the principal's travel insurance provider, with the CPO team acting as the initial ground contact.
