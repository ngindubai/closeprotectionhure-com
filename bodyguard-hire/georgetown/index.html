Threat Assessment: Georgetown

Georgetown has experienced a measurable rise in violent crime since Guyana's offshore oil production began scaling rapidly in the early 2020s. The influx of foreign corporate workers and contractors, combined with rapid economic change and existing socio-economic inequality, has created a target-rich environment for armed robbery and opportunistic crime. The FCDO advises travellers to exercise a high degree of caution in Guyana, noting risks from petty crime and armed robbery particularly in Georgetown (FCDO Guyana travel advice, 2026). The US State Department rates Guyana at Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution) as of 2026. Georgetown's position as a transshipment point on regional drug-trafficking routes contributes to an armed criminal ecosystem that extends beyond petty theft. Business visitors arriving for oil-sector meetings, government negotiations, or financial services engagements are perceived as carrying significant wealth and require tailored security planning. Kidnap-for-ransom targeting of expatriate oil workers and their families has been reported, and the police service has limited capacity to respond rapidly in all areas of the city.

Guyana CPO Licensing and Vetting

Private security in Guyana operates under the Private Security Industry Regulatory Authority (PSIRA-Guyana), which licences both security companies and individual officers. A legitimate close protection operator in Georgetown should hold current PSIRA-Guyana company registration, and individual CPOs should carry personal licences verified through PSIRA-Guyana's register. Armed security is legally available in Guyana, with firearms authorisation requiring a separate permit process under the Guyana Firearms Act. When engaging a security provider for Georgetown, request documentary evidence of PSIRA-Guyana registration and verify currency of the licence. International operators should work with a registered local partner that has established relationships with the Guyana Police Force and, where relevant, the Guyana Defence Force intelligence community.

Route Security and Movement Planning

Georgetown's road infrastructure concentrates movement along a limited number of arteries, creating predictable patterns that can be exploited by those conducting surveillance. The main transfer route from Cheddi Jagan International Airport - approximately 42 kilometres south of the city on the East Bank Demerara highway - is a known risk corridor, particularly at night and in conditions of poor visibility. The route passes through communities with elevated crime profiles. Movement within Georgetown should prioritise the Kingston/Queenstown diplomatic district and Brickdam corridor, which host embassy compounds and international offices. Areas around Albouystown and Tiger Bay should be avoided. Route surveys prior to a principal's arrival are essential. All movement should use vetted vehicles with pre-planned abort routes and communication protocols.

Residential and Hotel Security

Georgetown's international hotel stock is limited. The Pegasus Hotel Georgetown and the Cara Lodge remain the primary choices for corporate visitors and offer the most reliable perimeter security. Both properties are located within accessible distance of the Kingston diplomatic district. Hotel advance work should include a sweep of the principal's room, confirmation of access control procedures, and identification of emergency exit routes. For extended stays or private residential arrangements, a security survey of the property is essential before occupation, covering perimeter integrity, lighting, communications, and access control. Static guarding at residential premises is a common mitigation for senior executives on extended Guyana postings.

Emergency Response and Medical Cover

Georgetown's medical infrastructure is limited relative to the demands a serious security incident can place on it. The Georgetown Public Hospital is the main public facility, but capacity and equipment standards can be inadequate for trauma cases. Pre-arranging access to private medical care through a reputable international medical assistance provider is strongly recommended for any principal-level visit. Emergency services in Guyana operate on 911. Medical evacuation options include air ambulance transfer to Barbados (approximately one hour) or Trinidad, where more comprehensive tertiary care is available. The close protection plan should identify a nominated medevac provider before the visit commences.

Close Protection Team Configuration

For a standard corporate visit to Georgetown by an oil-sector executive or similar high-value principal, a two-person team is the recommended minimum: a close protection officer and a vetted security driver with current local knowledge. For principal-level visits involving multiple engagements or movement to less secure areas, a three-person team with advance capability is preferable. Armed close protection is available and appropriate given the armed robbery risk in Georgetown. All personnel should hold current PSIRA-Guyana credentials. Teams should operate with pre-briefed emergency protocols covering vehicle interdiction, medical response, and police liaison procedures specific to Guyana.
