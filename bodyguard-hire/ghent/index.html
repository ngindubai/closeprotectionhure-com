Operational detail for Ghent

Licensing Framework

Private security in Belgium is governed by the Loi du 2 octobre 2017 reglementant la securite privee et particuliere, which replaced the 1990 framework and sets training, registration, and conduct standards for the sector. Operating companies must hold an agrement (company licence) from the SPF Interieur (Federal Public Service Interior), and individual security personnel must carry a badge issued by the Ministry of Interior confirming their personal authorisation. Both credentials are subject to ongoing oversight. Armed close protection in Belgium requires specific authorisation that is rarely granted for routine commercial work; the standard for Ghent engagements is unarmed. Clients should request the company SPF Interieur agrement and the individual personnel badge numbers before any deployment commences.

Threat Environment

The FCDO Belgium Travel Advice (2024) records petty crime, pickpocketing, and demonstrations as the principal risks for visitors in Belgian urban areas rather than violent or targeted offences. The Federale Politie (Federal Police) publishes data on petty crime concentration in Ghent, with the Graslei and Korenmarkt tourist waterfront and the Overpoort student area identified as locations of elevated opportunistic crime, particularly at night and during festival periods. Ghent hosts periodic demonstrations related to national and EU-level political issues; these typically concentrate at the Korenmarkt and Sint-Baafsplein areas. The industrial zone along the Ghent-Terneuzen Canal, home to ArcelorMittal steel operations and the port canal complex, requires distinct vehicle-access planning and industrial-site advance work for visiting executives.

Key Operational Areas

CPO operations in Ghent focus on four environments. The Graslei and Korenmarkt waterfront forms the tourist and corporate hospitality core, with principal hotels, restaurants, and cultural venues concentrated in the adjacent historic centre; the area warrants ambient awareness given documented petty-crime levels. Flanders Expo at Sint-Denijs-Westrem, southwest of the city, is the major trade and consumer exhibition venue and the destination for multi-day event cover requiring vehicle-staged access. The Canal zone along the Ghent-Terneuzen Canal hosts ArcelorMittal, Volvo Cars Belgium, and port operations, with industrial-site visits requiring advance co-ordination with facility security. Ghent-Sint-Pieters station is the primary intercity rail hub with connections to Brussels, Bruges, and Antwerp.

Close Protection Services

Standard Ghent details cover principal movement between hotel, industrial facility, and exhibition venue; venue advance at Flanders Expo halls and ArcelorMittal, Volvo Cars, UCB, and Ablynx/Sanofi facilities in the canal zone; discreet vehicle escort within the city and on the E17 and E40 motorway corridors to Brussels and Antwerp; and crowd management at Flanders Expo events. The pharmaceutical and biotech sector, including UCB and Ablynx/Sanofi Ghent operations, generates senior executive and investor visitor traffic that benefits from low-profile CPO cover during sensitive commercial or scientific visits. Officers operate in business-appropriate attire maintaining a low signature consistent with the industrial and corporate setting.

Airport and Transit Cover

Ghent has no major commercial airport. The primary international gateway is Brussels Airport (BRU), approximately 55km east by road or rail, with a standard transfer of 45 to 60 minutes. A secondary option is Antwerp Airport (ANR), approximately 40km northeast. Ghent-Sint-Pieters station provides regular direct intercity rail connections to Brussels Midi (30 minutes), which serves Eurostar and Thalys services, and to Brussels Airport via Brussels station. Transfer cover from Brussels Airport or Antwerp includes confirmed collection and operations-controller tracking to the principal's Ghent hotel or facility destination.

Communications and Contingency

Every detail operates an operations-controller communications protocol with the Belgian emergency baseline: 112 (pan-European all emergencies), 101 (Police Federale), and 100 (Ambulance and Fire). The principal hospital is UZ Gent (Ghent University Hospital), reachable at +32 9 332 21 11. The British Embassy Brussels (+32 2 287 62 11) handles consular assistance for British nationals in Belgium; the US Embassy Brussels (+32 2 811 4000) covers US nationals. Medical evacuation co-ordination is managed through the principal's travel insurance provider, with the CPO team acting as the initial ground contact.
