Threat Assessment: Hargeisa and Advisory Context

Hargeisa carries a medium ground-conditions rating, but travellers must understand the advisory context. FCDO advises against ALL travel to Somalia, a policy that includes Somaliland, reflecting the UK government's position on Somaliland's unrecognised status rather than solely ground-level conditions in Hargeisa (FCDO Somalia, 2026). The US State Department rates Somalia including Somaliland at Level 4. In practice, Hargeisa is substantially more stable than the rest of Somalia; large NGO, development, and commercial communities operate there. Al-Shabaab has not conducted major attacks in Hargeisa in recent years. Travelling to Hargeisa means travelling against FCDO and US State Department advisories, with significant insurance and duty-of-care implications that must be addressed before any visit.

Somaliland CPO Licensing and Vetting

Somaliland has developed its own private security regulations, more structured than those of Federal Somalia, governing the licensing and operation of security companies. CPOs deployed through this service are verified against Somaliland's own regulatory framework, hold current licences, and have verifiable experience in the Hargeisa operating environment. Familiarity with Somaliland's political landscape, the role of traditional clan structures, and the security context relative to Federal Somalia is a standard competency requirement.

Route Security and Movement Planning

Egal International Airport (HGA) is approximately 6 km east of the city centre. Airport transfers are planned with advance route intelligence and direct routing to secure accommodation in the Central Hargeisa area, including the 26 June district, Jigjiga Yar, and the international hotel zone. Street crime risk from opportunistic theft and robbery is elevated in markets and after dark. Movement planning identifies safe operating windows and avoids market areas during peak-crime periods.

Residential and Hotel Security

Secure accommodation options in Hargeisa concentrate around Central Hargeisa, the 26 June area, and the Jigjiga Yar district. CPOs provide pre-arrival hotel and residence security surveys, confirm emergency egress routes, and establish daily movement protocols. For NGO and development-sector clients with extended deployments, the team coordinates with compound management and advises on access control and domestic staff protocols.

Emergency Response and Medical Cover

Hargeisa Group Hospital and Edna Adan Maternity Hospital provide basic care. Serious trauma and medical emergencies require medevac to Djibouti City, Nairobi, or Dubai. Medevac planning is a non-negotiable element of any Hargeisa deployment given the distance to tertiary medical facilities. CPOs carry trauma kit, hold current first-aid certification, and are briefed on Somaliland Police (999) and Fire (555) contact protocols.

Duty-of-Care and Insurance Considerations

Organisations sending personnel to Hargeisa against FCDO and US State Department advisories carry a heightened duty-of-care obligation. Professional close protection does not substitute for confirmation that insurance coverage applies despite the advisory, a formal risk assessment presented to and signed off by the travelling individual, and notification of the nearest diplomatic mission. We provide the security element of this framework; legal and insurance review must be conducted separately.
