Operational detail for Skopje

Threat Assessment: Skopje

Low-to-medium risk rating. North Macedonia is a NATO member (2020) and EU accession candidate with improving governance. Organised crime is present but the direct personal security risk for corporate visitors is manageable with standard precautions. FCDO advises a high degree of caution (FCDO North Macedonia, 2026).

North Macedonia Close Protection Licensing and Vetting

All bodyguard and close protection personnel deployed in Skopje must hold licensing issued by the Ministry of Interior of North Macedonia under the Law on Private Security (2012). Vetting standards appropriate to the North Macedonian EU accession and organised crime environment include documented operational experience, professional references, and in-country knowledge. International clients should request compliance documentation from any operator before deployment commences. Operators without demonstrated North Macedonia market experience present unacceptable vetting gaps given the operating environment.

Airport and Arrival Security

Skopje Alexander the Great Airport (SKP) is the primary entry point. All arrival and departure transfers should use pre-arranged vetted security transport -- do not use unmarked or informal taxis. Surveillance detection during the arrival window is a standard element of the protective posture for principals who may be subject to monitoring. The close protection team should be in position before the principal's flight lands and maintain continuous coverage from the aircraft stand to the secure vehicle.

Movement and Route Security

Movement in and around Skopje requires route assessment and variable routing where the threat profile warrants it. Vetted security drivers familiar with local traffic patterns and the specific security geography of Skopje are an essential component of any close protection deployment. Routes to and from principal accommodation, meeting venues, and the airport should be pre-surveyed and alternatives identified for each journey.

Residential and Hotel Security

Principal accommodation in Skopje requires advance security survey. International hotel properties with established security management are preferred for short-stay principals. For longer residential assignments, a formal residential security survey covering perimeter, access control, communications, and medical contingency is required. Guard-force management and briefing of existing hotel or residential security staff is part of a professional close protection deployment.

Emergency Response and Medical Cover

General Hospital Skopje (8th September) is the principal medical facility for Skopje. Medical evacuation to Belgrade or Vienna is standard contingency for cases requiring specialist care beyond local capacity. Any close protection deployment must include pre-arranged medical evacuation cover, named medevac provider contact details, and briefed first-aid capability within the protection team. Emergency numbers: Police, Fire, Medical emergency -- confirm current local numbers as part of advance planning for any Skopje deployment.
