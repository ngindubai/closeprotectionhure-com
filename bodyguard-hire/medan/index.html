Operational detail for Medan

Licensing Framework

Private security in Indonesia is regulated by POLRI, the national police, alongside the Ministry of Manpower, the same framework covering Jakarta and Surabaya. Security firms need valid POLRI registration and individual officers need certification before taking on protective work. Armed private security is tightly restricted and requires specific police authorisation, so the large majority of Medan assignments run unarmed. Foreign operators need KITAS work permits and local registration, and in practice engagements here are staffed through POLRI-licensed Indonesian providers.

Threat Environment

The FCDO assesses that terrorists are likely to try to carry out attacks in Indonesia, a national picture that applies to Medan as much as anywhere else in the country, with risk assessed as heightened around religious holidays and elections. Medan's more distinctive factor is geographic: FCDO advice advises against all travel within 5km of the crater of Mount Sinabung in Karo Regency, an active volcano roughly 70km from the city that erupted in 2021 and showed renewed activity in May 2026. Day-to-day, motorbike-borne bag and phone snatching around crowded markets is the more routine concern for a working close protection detail.

Key Operational Areas

Medan's business core sits around Kesawan and the Merdeka Walk district, the old colonial commercial centre, and the newer Setia Budi corridor further south. Medan Polonia, built around the site of the former city-centre airport that closed in 2013, is the established upmarket hotel and residential district. Beyond the city, Medan is the standard base for plantation and mill site visits across North Sumatra's palm oil and agribusiness sector, and for road travel toward the Berastagi highlands and Lake Toba.

Close Protection Services

A Medan detail spends as much time managing crowd density in markets and transport hubs as it does anything more acute. Pasar Petisah and the bus and rail terminals are the city's clearest opportunistic-theft zones, and close, briefed positioning through these areas matters more than a static perimeter. For any leg that extends toward Berastagi or the Karo highlands, officers check current volcanic alert status before the movement and plan around the 5km Sinabung exclusion zone as a fixed boundary, not a suggestion.

Airport and Transit Cover

Kualanamu International Airport (KNO) sits roughly 39km east of central Medan, having replaced the old Polonia Airport when it closed to commercial traffic in July 2013. Officers meet principals airside where access allows and manage the transfer by either the Railink airport train, around 30 to 40 minutes and unaffected by road traffic, or a security-driven car, which typically runs 60 to 90 minutes depending on congestion.

Communications and Contingency

Indonesia's unified emergency number is 112, alongside 110 for police, 118 or 119 for ambulance, and 113 for fire. RSUP H. Adam Malik, +62 61 4533666, is Medan's national referral hospital and the standard reference point for serious medical incidents. Search and rescue support runs through BASARNAS on 115, relevant given the province's volcanic and flood-related hazard profile.
