Operational detail for The Hague

Security Framework in the Netherlands

Private security in the Netherlands is governed by the Wet particuliere beveiligingsorganisaties en recherchebureaus (WPBR, 1997). Companies must hold a police-granted operating permit, and all individual security personnel are subject to mandatory Wet Bibob background screening administered through the Dutch police. There is no individual CPO licence register equivalent to the UK's Security Industry Authority public register; the operating company's police permit is the primary compliance verification point. Armed close protection is tightly controlled under Dutch law and is uncommon for commercial engagements; the standard CPO posture in The Hague is unarmed plain-clothes. The Hague presents an additional regulatory dimension absent in most Dutch cities: diplomatic protection work involving principals connected to international courts or embassies requires coordination with the Dutch Diplomatic Security Service (Dienst Bewaking en Beveiliging, DBB), which provides state-provided protection to certain categories of international court personnel and diplomats. Commercial CPO providers operating alongside or adjacent to DBB-protected principals must align operational protocols with DBB coordination requirements. EU-licensed security officers can operate in the Netherlands subject to WPBR registration with the Dutch police.

Threat Environment

The FCDO Travel Advice for the Netherlands, updated 2024, rates the Netherlands at low overall risk. The Hague has a more complex threat environment than this national-level assessment suggests for a specific category of visitor. The AIVD (Algemene Inlichtingen- en Veiligheidsdienst, the Dutch domestic intelligence service) annual report 2023 specifically warns of foreign state actor intelligence operations targeting international organisations in The Hague, including the ICC, OPCW, and Europol. State actor surveillance of court principals, witnesses, and legal teams has been documented in the AIVD report; this is not a hypothetical threat for principals involved in high-profile ICC or ICJ proceedings. Demonstrations targeting international institutions have been documented at the ICC and Peace Palace vicinity, with examples in Dutch media from 2022 to 2024. Petty crime is present at The Hague Centraal Station and along the Scheveningen seafront during the summer tourist season. The NCTV terrorism threat level at Substantial applies nationally.

Key Operational Areas

The Hague's CPO operational geography is shaped by the concentration of international institutions and diplomatic missions in the city. Bezuidenhout is the primary international business and institutional district, hosting the ICC, Europol, Eurojust, and a concentration of international law firm offices; it is the zone most relevant to court-adjacent CPO operations. The Peace Palace area, home to the International Court of Justice and the Permanent Court of Arbitration, is within walking distance of Bezuidenhout and is well-policed by Dutch national and diplomatic security arrangements. Benoordenhout and Wassenaar (adjacent municipality) form the primary diplomatic residential zone, known as embassy row; principals residing in this area benefit from the existing security infrastructure but may require CPO cover for movement outside the immediate residential environment. Scheveningen, the coastal resort district 5km from the city centre, is the location of the Steigenberger Kurhaus Hotel and is a summer HNWI destination with elevated petty crime during the season. The Centrum and Spui commercial areas around The Hague Centraal Station form the retail and transit core, with standard petty crime exposure. Key hotels include Hotel Des Indes (Lange Voorhout 54, directly opposite the International Court), Steigenberger Kurhaus Hotel (Scheveningen), and Radisson Blu Palace Hotel.

Business and HNWI Protection

The Hague generates a distinctive and specialised CPO demand unlike any other city in the Netherlands. The concentration of international legal and intergovernmental institutions, including the ICJ, ICC, OPCW, Europol, Eurojust, the Permanent Court of Arbitration, and the Special Tribunal for Lebanon, creates a visitor base of diplomats, judges, prosecutors, defence counsel, witnesses, and NGO representatives who may have specific threat profiles arising from their involvement in international legal proceedings. This category of principal requires a CPO approach informed by the intersection of Dutch domestic law and the legal status of the international organisations involved. Shell NV's corporate headquarters at Carel van Bylandtlaan 30 generates a significant energy sector executive visitor cohort; Shell global management visiting The Hague for board and strategy functions represents a more conventional HNWI and C-suite CPO demand. Financial services, particularly ING Group's presence in the city, adds a banking and insurance executive visitor profile. The 150-plus embassies in The Hague create a diplomatic visitor base from their own country's senior officials attending bilateral meetings, generating short-duration CPO requirements for visiting dignitaries.

Airport and Transit Cover

The Hague is served primarily by Rotterdam The Hague Airport (IATA: RTM), located 10km south of the city centre with a standard transfer time of 15 to 20 minutes via the A4 motorway. RTM handles a range of European routes and is the preferred gateway for direct The Hague-focused visits given the shorter transfer time compared with Schiphol. Amsterdam Schiphol (IATA: AMS), 50km north of The Hague, is the main intercontinental gateway and handles the majority of international executive arrivals. The AMS to The Hague transfer by road is approximately 40 to 50 minutes via the A4 motorway; the Schiphol to The Hague Intercity Direct rail connection provides a competitive 25-minute journey from the airport to The Hague Centraal Station. CPO vehicle pre-positioning at Schiphol coordinates through the Schiphol Transfer Terminal or P1 facility, with timing adjusted to match international arrivals. For court-adjacent principals, The Hague Central Station vehicle staging and the ICC visitor access route via Maanweg are standard pre-advance elements. Private aviation arrivals at RTM have handling facilities with direct vehicle access.

Emergency Support

The primary hospital serving The Hague is Haaglanden Medical Centre (HMC), reachable at +31 70 210 0000, which provides emergency and trauma services across its sites. Bronovo Hospital (+31 70 312 4141) is located near the Peace Palace and is the nearest medical facility for the international institutions district. Emergency services in the Netherlands are accessed through 112, the pan-European emergency number for police, ambulance, and fire. The British Embassy is located in The Hague at Lange Voorhout 10 (+31 70 427 0427), making consular access direct and rapid for British nationals. The US Embassy is also in The Hague at Lange Voorhout 102 (+31 70 310 2209). This concentration of major embassies within the Bezuidenhout and Lange Voorhout diplomatic zone is operationally significant: consular response times for British and US principals requiring emergency assistance are substantially faster in The Hague than in any other Dutch city. Medical evacuation for principals requiring repatriation is coordinated through the travel insurance provider and the relevant embassy, both of which are within the city.
