Operational detail for Bangalore

PSARA Karnataka Licensing Framework

Private security agencies operating in Bangalore must hold a licence from the Government of Karnataka under the Private Security Agencies (Regulation) Act, 2005 (PSARA). Individual security personnel are trained and registered under the PSARA framework administered at state level. PSARA requires minimum training standards, background verification, and registration for all deployed guards and close protection officers. Before engaging any Bangalore security provider, request evidence of Karnataka PSARA licence and verify current validity. Officers must carry their registration documentation on duty.

Technology Sector Executive Protection

Bangalore's global technology sector generates specific close protection requirements distinct from those in most other Indian cities. Senior executives from major technology companies, visiting investors, and founders of high-profile Indian technology businesses receive targeted approaches from people seeking introductions, investment, or partnerships, and in some cases face threats linked to their public profile or wealth. CPO cover for technology sector principals in Bangalore is calibrated to the informal, campus-based corporate culture of the sector: discrete cover rather than a visible security posture, appropriate to a environment where conspicuous bodyguards are unusual.

FCDO India Terrorism Assessment and Local Risk Profile

FCDO India travel advice (April 2026) assesses that terrorists are very likely to carry out attacks in India. Bangalore's own attack history is limited compared to Mumbai or Delhi, but Karnataka and neighbouring states have experienced extremist activity. Active groups at national level include Daesh and AQIS. The 2013 Hyderabad bombings provide the closest regional precedent for a technology-hub targeting event. CPO operations in Bangalore incorporate terrorism awareness for hotel choices, vehicle routes, and meeting venues, with avoidance of known high-footfall target types including crowded markets and religious sites during elevated-threat periods.

Female Executive Protection

FCDO India travel advice (April 2026) specifically notes the sexual assault risk for female foreign nationals in India, with serious incidents documented across multiple states including those adjacent to Karnataka. Female principal protection in Bangalore incorporates female close protection officers where appropriate, specific protocols for hotel security liaison, vetted transport at all times of day, and situational awareness briefing appropriate to the specific environments the principal will visit. Solo movement after dark is not recommended for female principals, and social settings require additional situational assessment.

Whitefield, Electronic City and Tech Park Coverage

Bangalore CPO operations cover the principal business zones where technology sector activity is concentrated. Whitefield, the IT Corridor on Outer Ring Road, Electronic City, and Koramangala are the primary technology business districts. The ITC Gardenia, Taj West End, and Leela Palace are the principal HNWI hotel properties in Bangalore. CPO teams familiar with the specific layouts, traffic patterns, and venue security approaches at Whitefield tech park campuses and central Bangalore hotels are the appropriate partner for technology sector executive engagements. Pre-advance at principal hotel and meeting venues is standard.

Kempegowda International Airport Transfer

Kempegowda International Airport (BLR) is approximately 40 kilometres north of central Bangalore and 35 kilometres from Whitefield. This extended transfer distance creates a significant exposure window that is the highest-risk element of most Bangalore itineraries. CPO-covered transfers from BLR to principal hotels in central Bangalore and Whitefield are managed with operations controller tracking, pre-advance hotel liaison, and vetted vehicle selection appropriate to Bangalore's road conditions and the specific route taken.
