Operational detail for Tel Aviv

Israeli Private Security Licensing: Ministry of Public Security

Private security in Israel is regulated under the Private Security Services Law (Chok Shrutei Bitachon Pratiyim) 5765-2005, with oversight by the Ministry of Public Security through its Private Security Services Division. Both operating companies and individual close protection and security officers must hold licences from the Ministry of Public Security. Israel's private security market is among the most professionalised globally, reflecting the country's high security awareness and the integration of security training into national military service. Most Israeli CPOs have completed compulsory IDF service, with many holding additional specialist military or police experience.

Post-October 2023 Threat Environment

The Hamas attacks of 7 October 2023 and the subsequent Israeli military operations significantly elevated the threat environment for corporate and diplomatic principals operating in Israel. The FCDO Israel travel advice (2024) advises against travel to Gaza, within 4 kilometres of the Lebanon border, and to areas near the Israeli-Syrian and Israeli-Jordan borders, while noting a high threat of terrorism and rocket attacks across Israel. For corporate principals visiting Tel Aviv and the broader central Israel corridor, the pre-engagement threat assessment includes the current conflict status, alert levels, and any specific threat intelligence relevant to the principal's sector or nationality.

Tel Aviv Business District and Herzliya Coverage

Tel Aviv's principal corporate CPO operating zones are: the Azrieli Towers area and Kaplan Street corridor (the central Tel Aviv financial district), Sarona Market and Sarona Tech district (adjacent to the IDF headquarters, now a technology and financial hub), and Herzliya Pituah (the coastal business park north of Tel Aviv housing major technology companies, hedge funds, and private equity offices). Ra'anana is a secondary technology and corporate hub. CPO pre-advance at The Norman Tel Aviv, Setai Tel Aviv, Jaffa Hotel, and Intercontinental David Tel Aviv covers the principal hotel venues.

Israel's Nationally Elevated Security Culture

Israel's ambient security culture is materially different from most other high-income countries. Security personnel are present at the entrance to most shopping centres, restaurants, hotels, and public buildings; civilians with firearms carry permits are common in certain populations; and national preparedness for security incidents (sealed rooms, missile shelter awareness, emergency broadcast familiarity) is integrated into daily life. CPO operations in Israel can draw on this ambient security infrastructure while maintaining principal-specific protection protocols that go beyond the standard public security baseline.

Ben Gurion International Airport Transfer Security

Ben Gurion International Airport (TLV) at Lod, approximately 20 kilometres east of Tel Aviv, is one of the most security-intensive commercial airports globally. The Israel Airports Authority (IAA) and Shin Bet (Israel Security Agency) manage arrivals security with departure-side questioning, CCTV profiling, and checked baggage screening standards significantly exceeding most international equivalents. Transfer from Ben Gurion to central Tel Aviv via Highway 1 (Ayalon Highway) is 20 to 35 minutes under normal conditions. Pre-arranged collection by a vetted, licensed driver is standard protocol; kerbside approaches by unofficial drivers are managed within TLV's structured ground transport system.

Rocket Shelter and Emergency Protocol Awareness

Rocket and missile attacks targeting the Tel Aviv metropolitan area have been a recurring feature of Israel's security environment since 2006, with significant incidents in 2012, 2014, 2021, and 2023-2024 (ongoing). Israel's Home Front Command (Pikud HaOref) operates an alert system (Red Colour / Tzeva Adom) providing 60 to 90 seconds warning before rocket impact in the Tel Aviv area. CPO pre-engagement briefing for Tel Aviv includes: alert system identification, the location of the nearest sealed room or shelter in the principal's hotel and all scheduled meeting venues, the household and vehicle shelter procedure (vehicles should be stopped and occupants should move to any shelter or lie flat if no shelter is accessible), and the communication chain with the relevant embassy if an alert is issued.

The FCDO Israel travel advice (2024) notes a high threat of terrorism across Israel and advises against travel to areas near the Gaza and Lebanon borders. For Tel Aviv specifically, the assessment reflects the city’s distance from the principal conflict zones and its integrated national security infrastructure, while noting the ongoing rocket threat (addressed by Israel’s Iron Dome air defence system and the Home Front Command alert system). The US OSAC Israel Crime and Safety Report similarly assesses Tel Aviv as requiring heightened security awareness. Corporate principals should receive a pre-travel threat briefing specific to their sector, itinerary, and the current conflict status before confirming travel to Israel.

Israeli private security and close protection officer licences are issued by the Ministry of Public Security’s Private Security Services Division under the Private Security Services Law 5765-2005. Both operating company licences and individual officer licences can be confirmed through the Ministry of Public Security. Israel’s security culture creates a generally high standard of operating companies in the market; the licensing verification confirms the regulatory compliance baseline.

If a Red Colour (Tzeva Adom) missile alert is issued for the Tel Aviv area during an engagement, the procedure is: immediate movement to the nearest sealed room or reinforced shelter (tikun mamon), which is a standard feature of Israeli buildings; if in a vehicle, stop immediately, exit the vehicle, and move to any nearby building’s shelter or lie flat in a ditch or depression away from the vehicle if no shelter is accessible; wait 10 minutes after the alert ends before resuming movement (secondary fragments). The CPO is briefed on the shelter locations at every venue in the principal’s itinerary as part of the pre-advance. Israel’s Home Front Command app provides real-time alert notifications.

A Ministry of Public Security-licensed close protection officer in Tel Aviv for a corporate engagement typically ranges from USD 400 to USD 800 per day, depending on qualifications, former IDF or Shin Bet background, and the specific operational requirements. Post-October 2023, demand for experienced Tel Aviv CPOs has increased and pricing at the higher end of this range is more common for senior principal assignments. Multi-day engagements are structured on a retainer basis.
