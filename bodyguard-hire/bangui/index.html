Operational detail for Bangui

Threat Assessment: Bangui

Bangui carries an extreme risk rating. Sustained armed conflict has persisted since 2012, with multiple armed groups active nationally including the CPC, UPC, FPRC, MLCJ, and 3R (FCDO CAR, 2026). Africa Corps (formerly Wagner Group) has maintained a presence since 2018. FCDO advises against all but essential travel to Bangui and against all travel to most of the country. The US State Department rates CAR at Level 4 (Do Not Travel). Within the capital, UN MINUSCA maintains a peacekeeping presence; the PK0-PK5 corridor is the most controlled zone. The PK5 neighbourhood and all road approaches carry elevated armed-robbery risk. Close protection in Bangui is available for essential visits only, drawing on operators with demonstrated Central Africa conflict-environment experience.

CAR CPO Licensing and Conflict-Environment Vetting

Operators deployed in Bangui are assessed against the specific demands of a conflict-affected environment rather than standard commercial urban criteria. This includes verifiable Central Africa conflict-zone experience, coordination capability with UN MINUSCA and diplomatic security functions, and a thorough briefing on armed-group activity relative to current ground intelligence. Deployment planning is conducted in coordination with operators based in Yaounde or Ndjamena who maintain active CAR networks.

Movement Planning Within the PK0-PK5 Zone

Safe movement in Bangui is effectively restricted to the PK0-PK5 diplomatic corridor, encompassing the main diplomatic missions, MINUSCA facilities, and international organisation offices. Bangui M'Poko International Airport (BGF) is approximately 5 km from the city centre. All airport transfers are planned with security convoy protocols and direct routing through the controlled corridor. Movement outside PK0-PK5 requires a specific threat assessment and is only authorised for operationally essential purposes.

Coordination With MINUSCA and Diplomatic Security

UN MINUSCA is the primary coordination point for security incidents involving international personnel in Bangui. CPO teams are briefed on MINUSCA contact protocols (+236 75 50 00 00) and ICRC Bangui contacts (+236 75 03 05 70). Liaison with the relevant diplomatic mission's security function is established before each deployment as a standard pre-deployment step.

Emergency Response and Medical Cover

Medical facilities in Bangui are severely limited. Hopital de l'Amitie provides basic care; serious trauma and medical emergencies require medevac to Yaounde, South Africa, or France. Medevac planning is a non-negotiable deployment requirement. CPO teams carry extended trauma kits appropriate to a conflict environment and are briefed on Police (117) emergency contact procedures.

Essential-Visit Deployment Framework

Close protection in Bangui is planned as an end-to-end package covering pre-travel threat briefing, airport arrival and transfer, accommodation security in the PK0-PK5 zone, escorted movement to scheduled meetings, and departure planning. Deployments are assessed on a case-by-case basis relative to current armed-group activity and MINUSCA security reporting. Diplomatic, humanitarian, and essential corporate visits are the primary supported use cases.
