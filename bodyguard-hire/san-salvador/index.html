Threat Assessment: San Salvador

San Salvador's security environment has undergone a dramatic transformation since the Bukele government's Regimen de Excepcion, implemented from March 2022 following mass arrests of over 75,000 suspected gang members (Salvadoran government data, 2023). The national homicide rate fell from 106 per 100,000 in 2015 to approximately 2.4 per 100,000 in 2023 (Salvadoran National Police data), one of the fastest documented security improvements in Latin American history. FCDO now classifies El Salvador at a high degree of caution (improved from previous level), and the US State Department designates El Salvador as Level 2: Exercise Increased Caution (2026). Residual risks remain: some areas outside Zona Rosa and Escalon retain elevated crime risk, and the Regimen de Excepcion has introduced an active state surveillance and monitoring environment for all visitors that creates a distinct category of compliance risk. The 44-kilometre Oscar Arnulfo Romero International Airport transfer remains the most significant single movement risk.

El Salvador CPO Licensing and Vetting

Close protection operations in El Salvador are regulated under the Law on Regulation of Security Services, administered by the Ministry of Justice and Public Security. CPO companies must hold valid Ministry authorisation and individual officers must carry current Ministry credentials. The Regimen de Excepcion has given the Ministry expanded enforcement powers; CPOs in El Salvador must be thoroughly versed in the current legal provisions and how they apply to close protection operations, including the provisions for detention without warrant in certain circumstances and how to interact with law enforcement contacts. Foreign operators work through Ministry-authorised Salvadoran partner companies.

Route Security and Movement Planning

San Salvador CP route planning concentrates movement in the Zona Rosa/San Benito and Colonia Escalon corridors, which carry the lowest residual crime risk in the city. The Oscar Arnulfo Romero airport transfer (44 kilometres, Autopista Los Proceres) is the primary movement risk for arriving principals. Night movement outside the Zona Rosa and Escalon zone is avoided. CPO teams use low-profile, unmarked vehicles; El Salvador's surveillance environment means that vehicles with an obvious security profile can attract state attention. All route plans are approved by the operations controller before departure.

Residential and Hotel Security

International hotels in San Salvador are concentrated in the Zona Rosa and Escalon corridor. CPO teams assess hotel access control, parking security, lobby environment, and room selection. Properties in the Zona Rosa and San Benito areas offer the best combination of security posture and proximity to the main business and embassy district. For extended engagements or HNWI principals, residential security assessment in Escalon covers compound perimeter, access control, and alarm systems.

Emergency Response and Medical Cover

Private medical facilities in San Salvador include Hospital de Diagnostico and Hospital Profamilia for acute private care; Hospital Nacional Rosales is the main public emergency facility. CPO teams carry first aid provisions and the operations controller holds current medical facility contacts. For complex trauma, medical evacuation to the United States is the appropriate plan; this should be confirmed with the principal's travel health insurance provider before deployment. Emergency contacts include El Salvador emergency services (911) and the principal's national embassy.

Close Protection Team Configuration

For most corporate executive visits to San Salvador under current conditions, a single CPO with a vetted security driver and operations controller oversight is an appropriate starting configuration for movements within Zona Rosa and Escalon. The improved security environment since 2022 supports a lighter CP footprint for standard business visits than was appropriate pre-Regimen. For higher-profile executives, individuals with a specific threat environment, or visits that include movement outside the established zones, a two-CPO configuration is recommended. The surveillance environment also means that CPO visible security profile is kept to a minimum.
