What this covers

Operational detail for Naples

GPG Licensing: Prefettura di Napoli

Close protection officers in Naples must hold a Guardie Particolari Giurate (GPG) prefectural licence issued by the Prefettura di Napoli under Italy's Consolidated Law on Public Security (TULPS). Security companies operating in the Campania region must hold a Prefettura di Napoli operating licence. Armed CPO requires additional Ministry of Interior authorisation. All CPO personnel deployed for Naples and Campania engagements hold current GPG licences from the Naples prefecture, and documentation is provided to clients before the engagement.

Camorra Background and Due Diligence Context

The Camorra, Naples' organised crime structure, maintains a significant presence in Campania. Camorra activity primarily targets rival criminal factions and local businesses through extortion (the pizzo) and operates in areas including the construction, waste management, and retail sectors. For most international corporate visitors to Naples, Camorra activity is a due diligence consideration in commercial counterparty assessment rather than a direct physical threat. FCDO Italy travel advice notes the organised crime environment without advising against travel to Naples. CPO operations for Naples are focused on personal security rather than organised crime mitigation, which is more appropriately addressed through pre-engagement commercial due diligence.

Petty Crime and Scooter Theft Environment

Naples has elevated rates of bag snatching, scooter-borne theft, vehicle theft, and pickpocketing relative to northern Italian cities. The historic centre (Spaccanapoli), the area around Napoli Centrale station, and Via Toledo are higher-risk zones for personal property crime. Scooter-borne theft targeting pedestrians is a documented Naples-specific hazard. CPO operations incorporate discreet dress guidance, vehicle movement through the historic centre where practicable, and specific situational awareness protocols for the principal when pedestrian movement in high-risk zones is unavoidable.

Amalfi Coast and Campania Region Coverage

Naples CPO operations extend to the principal HNWI and high-profile destinations across the Campania region: the Amalfi Coast (Positano, Ravello, and the Villa Cimbrone for HNWI events), Capri (a significant HNWI yacht-based and villa-based social destination), Pompeii and the archaeological sites, and Caserta (Reggia di Caserta palace visits). Yacht security coordination for principals arriving at Naples or Positano by private vessel is available, with jetty-to-accommodation CPO cover and port security liaison. Road conditions on the Amalfi Drive (Strada Statale 163) require specific route management given the narrow cliff-road traffic and the driver skill required.

Terrorism Awareness for Italy

FCDO Italy travel advice rates Italy's terrorism threat as high. Naples is a lower-profile symbolic target than Rome or Milan, but standard terrorism awareness for principal movements at major transport hubs (Napoli Centrale, Naples International Airport) and crowded tourist sites applies across Italy. Italy's AISI (domestic intelligence) manages the threat picture effectively. CPO operations maintain terrorism awareness as a baseline element for all Naples itineraries, particularly for international events and high-footfall tourist sites on the principal's programme.

Naples International Airport and Port Transfer

Naples International Airport (NAP), also called Capodichino, is approximately 7 kilometres from central Naples and 5 kilometres from the port (Molo Beverello) for Capri and Amalfi ferry connections. The short transfer distances make Naples a relatively compact operational environment for CPO. CPO-covered transfers from Capodichino include inside-terminal collection, vetted vehicle, and route management through the airport approach to the principal's hotel address. For principals arriving by helicopter directly to the Amalfi Coast, landing coordination and ground team positioning are pre-arranged.
