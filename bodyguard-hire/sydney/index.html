NSW security licensing

The Security Licensing and Enforcement Directorate (SLED) administers NSW security licensing under the Security Industry Act 1997. The class 1E (Bodyguard) licence is specific to close personal protection work and is separate from general security guard licensing. SLED licence verification is the primary due diligence step for any Sydney CPO engagement. Interstate licence reciprocity is not automatic: a Victorian or Queensland close protection licence does not authorise commercial CPO work in NSW.

NSW Security Industry Act Licensing

Private security in New South Wales is regulated under the Security Industry Act 1997 (NSW) and the Security Industry Regulation 2016 (NSW). Individual close protection officers must hold a class 1E (Bodyguard) licence issued by Service NSW through the Security Licensing and Enforcement Directorate (SLED). The licence requires approved training in close personal protection and criminal record vetting.

HNWI Protection and Corporate Executive Cover

Sydney is Australia's largest HNWI concentration, with significant wealth in the Eastern Suburbs, North Shore, and Sydney Harbour waterfront. CPO demand in Sydney is driven primarily by HNWI and UHNWI principals, entertainment and sports figures, and visiting executives from higher-risk markets who maintain their home-market security standard in Australia.

Terrorism Awareness

The Australian Security Intelligence Organisation (ASIO) maintains a terrorism threat level for Australia; as of June 2026, the national threat level is PROBABLE. Sydney has experienced terrorism-related incidents including the 2014 Lindt Cafe siege (which resulted in two civilian deaths) and several disrupted plots. CPO briefings for Sydney include current ASIO threat status and mass gathering avoidance awareness.

Sydney CBD, Eastern Suburbs, and North Shore Coverage

Operational familiarity with Sydney's principal corporate and HNWI zones: the CBD (Martin Place, Barangaroo, Walsh Bay), the Eastern Suburbs (Double Bay, Bellevue Hill, Woollahra), and the North Shore (Mosman, Cremorne, Neutral Bay). Sydney Airport and Domestic Transfer Integration

Sydney Kingsford Smith Airport (SYD) handles both domestic and international arrivals in the same precinct. CPO transfer cover from SYD to CBD or Eastern Suburbs hotels follows the SLED-licensed driver and operations controller standard.

Interstate Operations to Melbourne and Brisbane

Sydney CPO engagements frequently extend to Melbourne (Vic), Brisbane (Qld), and Canberra (ACT). CPOs and operating companies must hold licences valid in each state they operate in; licence reciprocity between Australian states is not automatic. Operating companies with national coverage handle the multi-state compliance; confirm this before engagement.
