Threat Assessment: Bujumbura

Bujumbura carries a high risk rating. Political violence since the 2015 Nkurunziza third-term crisis resulted in thousands of deaths and large-scale displacement. Under President Ndayishimiye (in office since 2020), the ruling CNDD-FDD and its Imbonerakure youth wing have been documented for human rights abuses per the UN Human Rights Council. FCDO advises against all but essential travel to parts of Burundi including DRC and Rwanda border areas, and advises high caution for Bujumbura itself (FCDO Burundi, 2026). The US State Department rates Burundi at Level 3. Street crime including robbery concentrates in Kamenge, Bwiza/Buyenzi, and the lakefront after dark. The DRC border context, Lake Tanganyika, Kivu province instability, and M23 rebel group activity add a regional dimension to the security picture.

Burundi CPO Licensing and Vetting

Private security operators in Burundi are regulated by the Ministry of Interior. CPOs deployed through this service hold valid licences, have undergone background screening appropriate to Burundi's post-crisis environment, and have verifiable experience operating in Bujumbura's security conditions. Familiarity with the political dynamics of the CNDD-FDD government and the implications of these dynamics for freedom of movement and interaction with state security services is a standard competency for deployed operators.

Route Security and Movement Planning

Bujumbura International Airport (BJM) is approximately 11 km from the city centre, one of the longer airport transfer routes in the region. This transfer is a specific operational vulnerability that is addressed in pre-deployment planning with advance route reconnaissance, timing restrictions, and an alternative route protocol. The Rohero district and city-centre Avenue du Gouvernement corridor, and the Kiriri residential area, constitute the primary secure movement zones. The Kamenge, Bwiza, and Buyenzi areas and the lakefront after dark are treated as elevated-risk zones in all movement planning.

Residential and Hotel Security

Secure accommodation concentrates in the Rohero district, around Avenue du Gouvernement, and in the Kiriri residential area. CPOs provide pre-arrival hotel and residence security surveys, confirm emergency egress routes, and establish daily movement protocols. For mining-sector clients (nickel, cobalt, gold) and development-finance personnel on extended deployments, the team coordinates with compound management and advises on access-control procedures and domestic staff vetting.

Emergency Response and Medical Cover

Prince Louis Rwagasore Hospital is the primary public medical facility in Bujumbura; capacity is limited. Medevac to Nairobi, Kigali, or South Africa is the standard contingency for serious cases. CPOs carry trauma kit, hold current first-aid certification, and are briefed on Police (112) and Fire (113) contact protocols. Medevac coordination is confirmed with the client's travel insurance provider before deployment.

Close Protection Team Configuration

Bujumbura's high risk rating, the 11 km airport transfer vulnerability, and the elevated street crime profile typically require a two-person team: a CPO and a security-trained driver. For mining-sector executives, senior NGO leadership, or principals with a high public profile in Burundi's politically sensitive environment, a three-person team with a team leader is the recommended baseline. All team configurations include a formal pre-deployment briefing on the CNDD-FDD political environment and its implications for operational security.
