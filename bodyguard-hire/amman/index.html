Operational detail for Amman

Jordan Private Security Regulatory Framework

Private security in Jordan is regulated by the Ministry of Interior, which issues operating licences to qualifying security companies and oversees the credentialling of individual security personnel under the Private Security Law and its subsequent amendments. All private security companies operating in Jordan must hold a current Ministry of Interior licence. Individual guards must hold credentials issued under the Ministry's certification framework. International operators working in Jordan typically structure engagements through licensed Jordanian partner companies rather than deploying foreign officers directly; the Ministry of Interior framework requires Jordanian company registration for operational licences. Pre-engagement verification of the Jordanian partner's Ministry of Interior licence status is the essential compliance step.

Jordan Terrorism Risk and Regional Context

The FCDO Jordan travel advice (2024) requires heightened awareness for travellers rather than advising against travel to most of Jordan. The FCDO notes that terrorist attacks have occurred in Jordan and the threat from regional groups is present. Jordan shares land borders with Syria, Iraq, Israel and the Palestinian Territories, and Saudi Arabia; the Syrian and Iraqi borders carry elevated risk designations. The FCDO advises against all travel to within 3 kilometres of the Syrian border and to the Badia region near the Iraqi border. Amman, as the capital and primary business hub, sits at the centre of Jordan's more stable zone; however, the regional context creates a background threat that is periodically activated by events in neighbouring countries.

Crime in Amman: Lower Risk with Specific Patterns

The US OSAC Amman Crime and Safety Report (2024) assesses Amman as presenting a manageable environment for business travellers compared to regional peers. Petty theft, vehicle crime, and occasional protests in municipal areas (particularly around Al-Husseini Mosque in East Amman) are the documented crime patterns. Confidence crime targeting foreign nationals is present but at lower rates than Cairo, Istanbul, or Beirut. For HNWI principals, vehicle-based targeting at predictable locations and unsecured residences is a relevant consideration; Jordan's HNWI community maintains a strong security culture, and private security is normalised at the senior level. The overall crime profile supports a selective rather than comprehensive CPO approach for most corporate visits.

Amman as a Gulf HNWI Financial Hub

Amman has established itself as a significant financial services, real estate, and private equity hub for Gulf-based HNWI clients, particularly following the disruption in Beirut (since the 2019 financial crisis and 2020 port explosion). The Abdali development project and the Abdoun and Sweifieh districts house a concentration of family offices, private banks, and wealth management operations. Gulf HNWI principals visiting Amman for financial meetings, property transactions, or family-linked business routinely engage discreet close protection cover; this is a normalised practice rather than an indicator of specific threat. For visiting principals from Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Kuwait, Amman-based CPO cover for the duration of financial and business visits is standard.

Abdoun, Sweifieh, and Financial District Coverage

Amman CPO operations concentrate on the principal business and HNWI districts. Abdoun is the established premium residential and diplomatic address, home to numerous embassies, senior government residences, and five-star hotel accommodation including the Four Seasons Hotel Amman. Sweifieh is the principal commercial and retail hub for Amman's affluent western residential community. The Downtown financial and government district (near the 4th Circle and Jordan Gate Towers) is the primary location for government ministry meetings and senior financial sector engagements. Queen Alia International Airport (AMM) is located 35 kilometres south of the city centre; the airport transfer is a standard CPO coverage element for arriving HNWI and corporate principals.

Political Demonstration Risk and Local Assembly Awareness

Jordan has a tradition of managed political expression that periodically results in public demonstrations. The Friday prayer gatherings at Al-Husseini Mosque in East Amman have historically been associated with post-prayer political protest, particularly during periods of regional tension related to Palestinian affairs. The FCDO Jordan travel advice (2024) notes that demonstrations can occur and that participants should monitor local news. For corporate principals whose itineraries include visits to the Downtown or East Amman areas, awareness of Friday prayer timings and local political events is incorporated into route planning. Amman's western districts (Abdoun, Sweifieh) carry a substantially lower demonstration risk than the Downtown and East Amman zones.
