The CNAPS framework

CNAPS (Conseil National des Activites Privees de Securite) is the French state body that regulates all private security under Loi n°2011-267 of 14 March 2011. CNAPS issues authorisation cards to individual security professionals and operating authorisations to companies. The CNAPS public register allows verification of both individual and company authorisations. Any operator in France unable to provide a CNAPS authorisation number on request is not in compliance with French law.

Operational detail for Paris

CNAPS-Licensed Close Protection

Private security in France is regulated by the Conseil National des Activites Privees de Securite (CNAPS), established under Loi n°2011-267 of 14 March 2011. Individuals performing close protection work commercially must hold a CNAPS authorisation card (carte professionnelle) and operating companies must hold a CNAPS authorisation. CNAPS licences are verifiable through the CNAPS public register.

Terrorism Awareness

France's domestic intelligence service DGSI and the French government maintain a high terrorism alert posture. The FCDO travel advisory for France notes that terrorist attacks, including mass casualty attacks in public spaces, remain a threat. The 2015 Paris attacks, 2016 Nice lorry attack, and subsequent incidents across France form the operational context for Paris CPO threat briefings. Mass gathering avoidance and venue security assessment are standard elements.

HNWI Protection in the Golden Triangle

Paris's HNWI concentration in the 8th, 16th, and 17th arrondissements -- the hotel district between Avenue Montaigne, Avenue George V, and the Champs-Elysees -- produces specific CPO demand for visiting HNWI principals. High-value jewellery robberies and bag snatches targeting wealthy individuals are documented by Paris Prefecture crime statistics in the luxury retail areas of Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honore and Place Vendome.

Social Unrest and Protest Contingency

Paris has a history of significant social unrest disrupting central business operations: the 2018-2019 Gilets Jaunes (Yellow Vests) movement, the 2023 pension reform protests, and periodic trade union general strikes. CPO operational planning for Paris includes contingency routing for protest-affected areas, alternative accommodation access plans, and departure timing management during known demonstration dates.

CDG and Orly Airport Transfers

CPO cover for Paris engagements typically begins with the Charles de Gaulle (CDG) or Orly (ORY) arrival transfer. CDG T2 handles most international business travel; ORY serves domestic and some European routes. Inside-terminal collection by a CNAPS-licensed driver is standard for senior executive arrivals.

Discreet Profile Standard

Paris HNWI and corporate CPO engagements operate with a discreet profile appropriate to the venue context: business attire at luxury hotels (George V, Le Meurice, Hotel de Crillon) and corporate offices, and casual dress for leisure venues. French corporate culture values discretion; overt security presence is generally counterproductive in this market.
