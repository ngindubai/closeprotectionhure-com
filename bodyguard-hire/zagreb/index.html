Operational detail for Zagreb

Threat Assessment: Zagreb

Zagreb presents a low ambient threat environment for corporate and diplomatic visitors. The FCDO advises normal precautions for Croatia (2026), and the US State Department assigns Level 1 (Exercise Normal Precautions) status as of 2026. Croatia became a full Schengen member in January 2023 and adopted the euro, marking a significant step in its integration into EU's standard operating framework. Violent crime targeting foreign nationals is rare; opportunistic petty theft in tourist areas of the Lower Town and around the main train station is the most commonly reported issue. Zagreb has developed rapidly as a MICE (meetings, incentives, conferences, and exhibitions) destination, and a growing technology and shared services sector has established the city as one of the more commercially active Central European capitals. Pharmaceutical activity is concentrated around Pliva/TEVA's Zagreb facilities at Ulica Hrvoja Maceka 21. IT and technology services companies are clustered in the Heinzelova ulica district and the Zagreb Tech Park. For corporate executives visiting these sectors, and for high-profile attendees at major conferences held at the Zagreb Convention Centre or the Sheraton Zagreb, the practical on-ground threat is low, but close protection is appropriate for principals with elevated individual risk profiles. The Gornji Grad (Upper Town) political and cultural quarter and the Donji Grad (Lower Town) commercial district are the primary operating environments for most close protection deployments.

Croatia Close Protection Licensing and Vetting

Close protection officers operating in Croatia must hold a licence issued under the Croatian Private Security Act (Zakon o privatnoj zastiti, 2003, as amended). The Ministry of Interior of the Republic of Croatia (Ministarstvo unutarnjih poslova) is the competent authority for licensing both security companies and individual security officers. Applicants must pass a criminal background check, a medical fitness assessment, a psychological assessment, and a professional competency examination. Security companies must be registered with the Ministry, hold liability insurance, and maintain a register of licensed operatives. Croatia has been a NATO member since 2009 and an EU member since 2013; EU CPOs may operate on short-term service provision engagements subject to Ministry notification. Croatia became a Schengen member in January 2023, removing internal border checks with other Schengen states, which simplifies the movement of EU-licensed operators. For non-EU CPOs, professional recognition procedures and work permit requirements apply. CloseProtectionHire.com operators undergo multi-point vetting including criminal record disclosure, identity verification, employment history, and professional qualification verification. Officers carry current SIA-equivalent or nationally recognised close protection qualifications.

Airport and Arrival Security

Zagreb Airport Franjo Tudman (IATA: ZAG) is located approximately 17 kilometres southeast of Zagreb city centre in the Pleso area. The airport handles approximately 3-4 million passengers annually and is undergoing expansion following its concession to a private operator. The commercial arrivals hall is a modern single-terminal facility. For principals requiring an enhanced arrival profile, a meet-and-greet inside the arrivals hall or, for private aviation arrivals, through the business aviation FBO on the eastern apron, can be arranged. The primary transfer route from ZAG to Zagreb city centre follows the Zagreb bypass (A3/A4 motorway ring) and exits via Slavonska avenija or Dubrovacka avenija into the city centre, a journey of approximately 20-30 minutes under normal traffic conditions. Alternative routes via Velika Gorica are available for route variation. Arrival timing should account for peak-hour congestion on the bypass ring and the Slavonska avenija approach during 07:30-09:00 and 16:30-18:30. For principals with onward connections to the Dalmatian coast (Split, Dubrovnik), either ground transfer via the A1 motorway or domestic flight connections from ZAG are available; close protection teams can be extended to cover coastal visits.

Movement and Route Security

Route security in Zagreb operates across the city's two main historic zones and its modern commercial periphery. The Gornji Grad (Upper Town) contains the Croatian Parliament, St Mark's Church, the Presidential Palace, and many government and diplomatic offices; vehicle access to the core of the Upper Town is restricted, and the funicular railway is the quickest public link. Foot coverage is required for principal movements in the Upper Town, with vehicles positioned on Ilica or at the base of Gradec Hill. The Donji Grad (Lower Town) is the main commercial and hotel district, with more accessible vehicle movement. The Heinzelova ulica technology district and the Zagreb Tech Park are accessible by vehicle from the bypass ring. Anti-surveillance protocols are appropriate for principals in the pharmaceutical or financial sectors. The Pliva/TEVA campus at Ulica Hrvoja Maceka 21 has a controlled vehicle access gate; advance coordination with site security is recommended. The Sava River creates a geographic division; the A3 motorway bridge, the Domovinski bridge, and the Mosta slobode crossing are the primary vehicle crossing points. Vetted security drivers familiar with Zagreb's road network, bypass ring junctions, and Upper Town access restrictions are a mandatory component of any close protection deployment.

Residential and Hotel Security

Principal accommodation in Zagreb is concentrated in the Donji Grad and the Gornji Grad perimeter. Key properties for VVIP and senior corporate stays include the Sheraton Zagreb Hotel (Kneza Borne 2), the Esplanade Zagreb Hotel (Mihanoviceva 1), the Palace Hotel Zagreb (Strossmayerov trg 10), and the Westin Zagreb (Krsnjavljega 1). The Esplanade Zagreb, built in 1925 adjacent to the central train station, is the most historically prestigious and is the standard choice for senior diplomatic and corporate principals, with an established vehicle approach from Mihanoviceva and proximity to the Croatian National Theatre and the main hotel and business district. The Sheraton Zagreb offers modern conference and executive floor infrastructure and is close to the Zagreb Convention Centre. For principals staying in private apartments or villas, a residential security survey should be completed on arrival, covering access control, staff vetting, communications protocols, and primary and secondary evacuation routes. The Maksimir and Pantovak districts are popular residential areas for senior corporate figures; both are accessible from the city centre within 15-20 minutes.

Emergency Response and Medical Cover

The primary acute care facility in Zagreb is KBC Zagreb (Klinicki bolnicki centar Zagreb, Zagreb Clinical Hospital Centre), located at Kispaticeva 12 in the Maksimir district, approximately 5 kilometres from the city centre. It is Croatia's largest hospital and national reference centre for trauma, cardiac surgery, and neurology. KBC Rebro, also in Maksimir at Kispaticeva 12, is an affiliated facility handling internal medicine and transplant medicine. For private medical care, the Medikol Polyclinic, Magdalena Heart Hospital, and the Poliklinika Anadolu private network offer English-language services. Medical evacuation for specialist intervention beyond Zagreb's clinical capacity is typically routed to Vienna (approximately 400km by road or approximately 1 hour by direct flight from ZAG), which has one of Europe's most comprehensive tertiary medical centres. Air ambulance from ZAG to Vienna is the standard medevac protocol. Emergency contact numbers: European emergency number 112, Croatian Police 192, Ambulance 194, Fire 193. CPO teams maintain a pre-briefed medical protocol for each principal and are briefed on the location of the nearest appropriate facility for each venue and route on the deployment itinerary.
