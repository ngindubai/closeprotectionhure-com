That authorisation, issued under Legislative Decree 153/2009 and the TULPS, sits alongside a GPG (Guardia Particolare Giurata) qualification and, for the employing firm, an active Prefettura di Genova licence. Armed deployment is the exception rather than the rule; the large majority of corporate engagements in Genoa proceed unarmed, paired with counter-surveillance awareness and secure transport rather than a firearm. Clients engaging protection for the first time in the city should treat the credential check as a formality worth insisting on rather than skipping, since the licensing structure is exactly what distinguishes a properly registered provider from an unregistered one operating outside the Questura's oversight.

Genoa's officers spend more time managing terrain than managing threat. The Caruggi, a dense medieval lane network largely closed to vehicles, requires foot-escort planning and closer principal proximity than the open streets around Piazza de Ferrari, Genoa's principal business district.
