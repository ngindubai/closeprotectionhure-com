Hannover Messe defines most of the protection work here. Deutsche Messe AG's flagship show is widely regarded within industrial and manufacturing circles as the world's largest industrial trade fair, drawing more than 4,000 exhibiting companies from over 70 countries in a typical edition, and an officer assigned to fair week plans first around exceptional hotel and transfer scarcity, then coordinates with venue security across one of the largest exhibition grounds anywhere.

The rest of the year runs quieter. Continental AG's Continental-Plaza headquarters, opened in December 2023, is a modern, low-crime corporate campus that calls for a different posture than fair week's terminal-scale movement. The one location worth genuine attention is the Hauptbahnhof: Bundespolizei-sourced reporting placed it among Germany's higher-incident stations in early 2025 figures, though a joint police and city security initiative running through September 2025 delivered a documented, more than 20 percent reduction in violent crime there. That is a real problem and a real fix, worth knowing rather than a reason for concern.

Officers here also plan for the Wolfsburg corridor. Volkswagen Group's global headquarters sits roughly 90km away, about half an hour by direct ICE train, and covering that leg as a continuous extension of a Hannover Messe visit is routine rather than exceptional. All officers hold current IHK Sachkundeprufung certification under the Bewachungsverordnung, and unarmed deployment with Lower Saxony Police liaison is the operational standard.
