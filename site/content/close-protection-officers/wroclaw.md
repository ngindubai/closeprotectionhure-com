Wroclaw counted 234 modern business-service centres as of May 2025, according to a KPMG Poland report on the country's shared-services sector, with Google, HP, Nokia, IBM, UBS and Volvo IT all named among the operations based here. That's a serious concentration of corporate footprint for a city of its size, and it's why officer work in Wroclaw looks less like classic threat mitigation and more like discreet, logistically smooth executive travel into a campus environment.

That footprint moves, though. Away from the campuses, the Rynek market square and its tram stops carry the highest tourist footfall in the city, and travel guidance consistently flags pickpocketing and scam risk there, including a documented fake-petition approach used against visitors. Poland's role as NATO's principal logistics hub for Ukraine support has added a genuine hybrid-threat dimension nationally, tracked by the ABW security agency, though it sits well above the everyday corporate visitor experience.
