Chisinau is the capital of Moldova, a small Eastern European state whose security environment has been defined by the Transnistria frozen conflict since the early 1990s and by its proximity to the war in Ukraine since 2022. The FCDO advises a high degree of caution for Moldova (2026) and advises against all travel to Transnistria. The US State Department rates Moldova Level 2 (2026). Chisinau itself is medium risk -- the city functions normally and receives regular diplomatic and commercial visits -- but the geopolitical pressures on a country bordering both a frozen conflict zone and an active war require that security planning extend beyond standard awareness measures.

CPO deployments in Chisinau serve corporate visitors, EU advisory and development teams, diplomatic missions, and investors engaged in Romania-Moldova trade and EU accession-related activity. Deployments are built around Chisinau International Airport (KIV) protocols and structured around Moldovan Police-registered local operators working alongside internationally trained close protection officers.
