Addis Ababa is Ethiopia's capital and the seat of the African Union, making it one of Africa's most significant diplomatic cities and a regular destination for government delegations, UN agency staff, and senior executives of development finance institutions. The city's Bole, Kazanchis, and Kirkos districts host the majority of international hotels, UN offices, and corporate premises. FCDO advises against all but essential travel to several Ethiopian regions affected by conflict; Addis Ababa itself carries a lower threat rating, though the broader national instability requires that CPO advance planning accounts for the regional context as well as the city-level risk environment. Full details on the security situation are available on the [Addis Ababa city page](/cities/addis-ababa/).

Our close protection officers in Addis Ababa are deployed through Ministry of Trade and Industry-licensed local partners, as required under Ethiopian commercial security regulations. Trained professionals operating within this framework, with proper advance work and counter-surveillance protocols applied, can reduce risk for corporate visitors and diplomatic staff conducting essential business in the city. For principals whose Addis Ababa programme includes government or AU campus visits requiring secure vehicle movements, our [security drivers in Addis Ababa](/security-drivers/addis-ababa/) provide integrated convoy support coordinated with the CPO team throughout the assignment.
