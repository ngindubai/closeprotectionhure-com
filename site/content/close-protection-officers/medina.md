    description: "The private security framework in Medina follows the national Saudi Arabian structure: the General Directorate of Public Security and the National Security Council framework govern all licensed security activity, and Council of Ministers Resolution No. 58 (2020 revision) sets out the licensing requirements for commercial security companies. All security companies must hold Ministry of Interior licences and individual officers must hold personal authorisations. Commercial close protection in Medina, as elsewhere in Saudi Arabia, is generally conducted unarmed; armed security remains state-controlled. Foreign firms must operate through a Saudi-licensed entity with appropriate sponsorship arrangements for foreign personnel. One constraint that is specific to Medina and does not arise elsewhere in Saudi Arabia is the Haram boundary restriction: non-Muslim security personnel are not permitted within the Haram area, the restricted zone surrounding Al-Masjid an-Nabawi (the Prophet's Mosque). This restriction is enforced at checkpoints and is applied consistently by Saudi authorities. Any protection brief that requires a CPO to accompany the principal into the Haram must be staffed with Muslim CPOs only; non-Muslim CPOs must remain at the Haram boundary and hand off protection to Muslim colleagues or to trusted mosque security personnel for the duration of the principal's visit to the holy site. This access restriction must be addressed explicitly in the operational plan before any Medina assignment begins."
