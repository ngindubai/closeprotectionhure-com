## The PSIRA licensing framework

South Africa's Private Security Industry Regulatory Authority (PSIRA) is the statutory body that governs all commercial private security work under the Private Security Industry Regulation Act 2001. PSIRA registration is mandatory for both individual CPOs and operating companies. Grades run from E (entry security guard) through A (close protection and senior security management). The distinction matters operationally: Grade E registration does not qualify an individual for close protection work.

The public PSIRA registry allows clients to verify any registration number. This is the standard verification step: ask for the PSIRA number of every CPO and the operating company's licence, then confirm both are current and the grade is appropriate for close protection work.

## What operational CPO cover looks like in Johannesburg

A Johannesburg CPO detail typically involves pre-advance work at accommodation, primary meeting venues, and restaurants before the principal arrives; route selection and timing for all ground movements; and operations controller oversight maintaining the timeline and location throughout. The threat vectors driving these protocols are carjacking, armed robbery, and the lower-frequency but documented risk of targeted kidnapping affecting senior business visitors to the Gauteng region.
