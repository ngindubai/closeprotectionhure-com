A Palma assignment can look completely different depending on the client. One brief is a superyacht owner arriving through Real Club Nautico de Palma or STP Shipyard Palma, where the job is a discreet, well-timed quayside-to-residence handover. The next might be a family group staying at a Son Vida villa, where officers coordinate with the estate's own private security rather than operating independently in the gated hillside enclave.

Licensing here runs through Spain's Ley de Seguridad Privada 5/2014, with firms registered through the SES and individual officers carrying a TIP issued by the Direccion General de la Policia. Armed authorisation stays rare for commercial work, so unarmed deployment is the norm. FCDO Spain's 2024 guidance is specific about the practical risks: organised distraction theft around the cruise terminal and tourist core, drink spiking including GHB in nightlife venues, and documented falls linked to balcony misuse, particularly relevant for younger principals or family groups on a Mallorca visit. Recurring anti-tourism demonstrations since 2024 add a schedule-affecting factor without amounting to a serious threat to visitors who plan around them.
