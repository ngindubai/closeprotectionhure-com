The demand for close protection officers in Bordeaux does not look like the demand in Paris or on the Cote d'Azur. There is no significant celebrity or political-profile driver here. Instead, two distinct client groups shape almost all of the CPO work: wine-trade principals targeted for acquisitive crime during auctions and primeur events, and aerospace and defence executives visiting the Dassault Aviation, Thales, MBDA, and Safran facilities around Merignac. A good Bordeaux CPO needs to be genuinely comfortable moving between both worlds, sometimes within the same week.

Licensing here is non-negotiable and worth checking carefully. CNAPS authorisation under Loi 83-629 is the only lawful basis for close protection work in France, and the Nouvelle-Aquitaine regional delegation is the relevant authority for anyone operating in Bordeaux and the wider Gironde. Armed protection exists as a category but is rarely granted for commercial deployments, so unarmed cover with trained physical intervention capability is the realistic baseline for almost every engagement.
