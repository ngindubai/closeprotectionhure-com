A close protection officer's badge in Ghent means something specific: it means the SPF Interieur, Belgium's Federal Public Service Interior, has vetted that individual under the Loi du 2 octobre 2017. That's the law, and it's the first thing worth checking before an officer ever gets near a principal. This page covers what our Ghent-based CPOs actually do day to day: how they're licensed, what threats they plan around, and how their work changes depending on whether the assignment is a hotel-zone visit, an industrial site tour, or a festival-week movement. The bulk of Ghent bookings fall into two categories that don't look alike at all. There's the industrial visit, an executive heading to ArcelorMittal's steel plant or Volvo Cars Belgium's Ghent factory, where the officer's real value is coordinating facility access protocols before the car ever reaches the gate. And there's hotel-zone and historic-centre coverage, where the job is more about knowing which streets to avoid at which hours, Overpoort after dark, the Graslei waterfront during Gentse Feesten, than about anything resembling a bodyguard film. Both draw on the same Belgian licensing baseline, but the actual work differs enough that briefings are built separately for each.
