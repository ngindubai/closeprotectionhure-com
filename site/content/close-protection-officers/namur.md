Namur is the one Walloon assignment built almost entirely around institutional access rather than street-level threat. The city has been Wallonia's official capital since 1986, confirmed by the Parliament of Wallonia in 2010, and hosts both the Parliament of Wallonia and the Government of Wallonia at the Elysette. Officers covering any delegation here coordinate access through the host institution well in advance, since sitting weeks bring extra security presence and occasional temporary restrictions around the government quarter that a standard corporate visit simply does not encounter.

The street-level picture is genuinely calmer than Namur's Walloon neighbours. A 2023 local security-perception survey found residents most concerned about road-user behaviour and public-space nuisance rather than serious crime, consistent with Namur's identity as a smaller administrative and university city rather than a former industrial basin like Charleroi. Belgium's national OCAM/CUTA threat level of 3 out of 4 through early 2026 still applies here as everywhere in the country, and officers build standard transport-hub vigilance into any plan regardless.

Every officer holds the specific CPO qualification under the Act of 2 October 2017, and unarmed deployment is the operating norm. Namur has no airport of its own, so officers meet the principal at Brussels or Charleroi's BSCA and travel the roughly hour-long rail connection together. The Citadel of Namur, an 80-hectare fortress with some of Europe's most extensive underground galleries, occasionally hosts events that call for a dedicated advance survey given its layout.
