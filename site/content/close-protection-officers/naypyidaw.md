Naypyidaw functions as Myanmar's purpose-built administrative capital, housing the country's government ministries, military headquarters, and the diplomatic compound areas used for official engagements with foreign governments and international organisations. Since the February 2021 military coup, the State Administration Council (SAC) exercises full control over all security and movement functions in the city, making Naypyidaw one of the most operationally constrained environments for CPO operations anywhere in the world. The FCDO Myanmar advisory (2025) rates Myanmar as very high risk and advises against all but essential travel. The [Naypyidaw city briefing](/cities/naypyidaw/) provides the full security and operating context.

CPO operations in Naypyidaw are structured entirely around SAC-approved movement parameters, with all principal movements pre-coordinated with the assigned SAC security liaison. Independent route advances and off-plan movements are not operationally possible in this environment; our teams provide close protection within the approved framework, supplementing rather than replacing the official SAC security presence.
