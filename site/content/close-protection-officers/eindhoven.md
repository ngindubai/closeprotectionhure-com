A close protection officer in Eindhoven does two jobs at once that most CPO postings keep separate. There is the standard duty-of-care work: route planning, hotel assessment, medical contingency, all the things any low-crime European city needs. Then there is the sector-specific layer, because Eindhoven hosts ASML, the only company on earth making the EUV lithography machines modern chip manufacturing depends on, and that fact alone puts a documented espionage target on the map that most cities this safe simply don't have.

That distinction shapes everything else. AIVD's 2023 annual report names the Dutch semiconductor and technology sector as a foreign intelligence target, and NXP Semiconductors and the Philips Innovation Campus sit in the same bracket as ASML. A CPO briefing here folds counter-surveillance awareness into what would otherwise be a routine campus-visit detail: varied routes, discreet device handling, an officer who knows the difference between ordinary curiosity and something worth flagging. None of that requires an armed posture. Dutch law makes armed commercial protection rare in practice, and WPBR-registered, Politie-cleared officers operating unarmed are the norm and, for most engagements, the only lawful option anyway.
