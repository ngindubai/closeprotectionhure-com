Justis, the Ministry of Justice and Security's screening authority, issues the company approval that every close protection firm operating in Eindhoven must hold, and the individual Politie clearance that every officer on an Eindhoven detail must carry before deployment, under the Wet particuliere beveiligingsorganisaties en recherchebureaus (WPBR) 1997. That licensing structure is the same across the Netherlands, but what it is applied to in Eindhoven is distinctive: the AIVD's 2023 annual report names the Dutch semiconductor and high-technology sector, concentrated here around ASML, NXP Semiconductors, and the wider Philips-descended ecosystem, as a target of foreign state-actor intelligence activity. That single finding shapes a meaningful part of the briefing content for Eindhoven details, adding counter-surveillance and device-hygiene awareness to a baseline that FCDO Netherlands (2024) otherwise rates as low overall risk, with petty theft at Eindhoven Centraal and Eindhoven Airport as the more conventional day-to-day concern.

Officer deployment in Eindhoven follows the principal's itinerary rather than a fixed city pattern: centre-based visits cluster around Eindhoven Centraal and Stationsplein, ASML-focused trips are based on the Veldhoven side of the city, and Strijp-S has become a meeting venue in its own right as the former Philips site has redeveloped. Stratumseind, the city's main bar street, is excluded from accommodation planning given its documented weekend disorder, though it remains relevant to route awareness on evening transfers. Airport cover splits between Eindhoven Airport for European connections, a 15 to 20 minute transfer from the centre, and Amsterdam Schiphol for intercontinental arrivals, where road transfer in a single vehicle is generally preferred over changing to rail partway for principals under an elevated threat assessment.
