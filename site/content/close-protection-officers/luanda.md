Luanda is sub-Saharan Africa's highest-cost city for international business visitors and one where professional close protection is standard practice rather than an optional addition for oil sector executives, senior corporate visitors, and NGO personnel. The combination of documented armed robbery, carjacking, the airport arrival risk window, and the distance from international-standard medical care produces a CPO requirement that experienced operators in the market treat as the baseline for professional duty of care.
