Two very different kinds of visit define most close protection work in Nuremberg. One is Spielwarenmesse, by its own organisers' account the world's biggest toy trade fair, which brings around 2,800 exhibitors from roughly 60 countries and 67,000 trade visitors through NürnbergMesse each year, alongside BrauBeviale and Consumenta. Officers here spend most of their time on hall-to-hall movement and exhibitor coordination across a venue that can span hundreds of thousands of square metres.

The other is quieter and completely different in rhythm. The Palace of Justice remains a working Bavarian courthouse, and its former Courtroom 600, now the Memorium Nuremberg Trials memorial, sits within the same complex as the city-run Documentation Centre Nazi Party Rally Grounds. Institutional and diplomatic visits here are scheduled and protocol-heavy, not crowd-managed, and an officer briefed for a trade fair does not automatically transfer well to this setting.

Licensing follows the national German pattern: the Bewachungsverordnung, Section 34a of the Gewerbeordnung, and a mandatory IHK Sachkundeprufung since January 2021. Armed authorisation stays rare under the Waffengesetz, so officers work unarmed with Bavarian Police liaison. Bavaria posts among the lowest crime rates of any German state, though the Hauptbahnhof and Konigstrasse corridor see more petty-crime activity than the city average, particularly during the Christkindlesmarkt from late November to late December.
