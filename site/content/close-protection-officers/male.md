  - title: "Regulatory Framework and Licensing"
    description: "Private security in the Maldives is governed by the Ministry of Home Affairs under Private Security Services Regulation No. 2013/R-92 and subsequent updates. All security companies operating commercially must hold a Ministry of Home Affairs operating licence; individual security personnel must hold personal authorisations. Commercial close protection in the Maldives is conducted unarmed: armed protection is restricted to government-contracted entities under the Maldives National Defence Force (MNDF) and Maldives Police Service (MPS); private commercial close protection companies do not have access to weapons authorisation. Foreign close protection officers require Maldivian work authorisation from the Department of Immigration before commencing any paid security activity; the typical processing time is three to four weeks from a complete application. Foreign licences are not directly recognised under the Maldivian framework. International firms regularly operate through Maldivian-licensed partner firms to navigate these requirements. The luxury resort sector, which is economically dominant in the Maldives, typically manages resort-island security through in-house teams under their own licensing arrangements; CPOs accompanying principals to outer-atoll resorts should expect to coordinate with the resort's own security management and confirm access arrangements for protection personnel in advance of the principal's arrival."

  - title: "Threat Environment in Male"
    description: "The FCDO Maldives travel advisory (2024) notes a terrorism threat in the Maldives. Local extremist networks have been documented, and Maldivian nationals have travelled to conflict zones as foreign terrorist fighters; the UN Monitoring Group's 2024 assessment (document S/2024) included the Maldives in its broader foreign terrorist fighter analysis. These networks have not, to date, been associated with attacks on foreign business visitors or resort areas, but their documented presence is a component of the threat environment that should be included in written risk assessments. Gang-related knife crime in Male's residential and commercial districts is the more immediate day-to-day concern: the Maldives Police Service crime statistics (2023) document gang-related violence as a persistent feature of Male city. Male is one of the most densely populated cities in the world by land area, creating a dense urban environment that complicates vehicle access and close-range protection. Political instability following the 2023 elections has generated periodic protests. Seaplane and speedboat transfers between Male and outer-atoll resorts carry an intrinsic weather-related safety risk: the Civil Aviation Authority of Maldives (CAM) grounds seaplane operations in poor visibility, and the Maldivian weather can change rapidly, particularly outside the main tourist dry season."
