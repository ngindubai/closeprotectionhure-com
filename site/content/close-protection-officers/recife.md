Suape Port, located 40 kilometres south of the city, is one of Brazil's most significant deep-water ports and draws substantial international commercial activity to the greater Recife area. The city also hosts the Recife/Guararapes International Airport, which serves as the primary hub for Northeast Brazil.

Despite its commercial importance, Recife carries a high risk designation based on consistently elevated violent crime statistics. The risk is not uniform across the city: Boa Viagem's hotel corridor is significantly more controlled than the Centro or the northern peripheral neighbourhoods, but no part of the city is without meaningful risk for an unprepared international visitor.
