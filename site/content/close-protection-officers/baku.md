Baku is Azerbaijan's capital and the operational headquarters of the country's significant oil and gas sector, with BP, SOCAR, and a wide range of international energy services companies maintaining major presences in the city. The Nizami commercial district, Narimanov residential and business area, and the historic Old City (Icheri Sheher) handle the majority of corporate and diplomatic activity. Azerbaijan's Ministry of Internal Affairs (MIA) regulates private security; our Baku operations are delivered through fully MIA-compliant partner companies. Baku carries a medium risk rating, and for the majority of assignments a low-visibility, discreet CPO profile is the proportionate and appropriate approach. Further background on the local security environment is available on the [Baku city page](/cities/baku/).

Energy sector principals visiting BP's Baku offices or conducting meetings with SOCAR represent a significant portion of our Baku client base. Counter-surveillance awareness near state oil company facilities and government buildings is a standard component of all assignments, given the concentration of sensitive state infrastructure in the city. Trained professionals operating within the MIA licensing framework can reduce risk for corporate executives, energy sector visitors, and diplomatic staff conducting essential business in Azerbaijan's capital. For principals requiring integrated secure ground transport throughout their Baku programme, our [security drivers in Baku](/security-drivers/baku/) work in direct coordination with the CPO team, covering all movements from Heydar Aliyev International Airport through to departure.
