Lome is Togo's capital and commercial centre, positioned on the Gulf of Guinea coast at the border with Ghana. Its port handles significant transshipment volumes, and the city attracts executives across logistics, infrastructure finance, commodities, and development sectors. The political environment under the Gnassingbe government has produced recurring demonstrations, and the FCDO advises a high degree of caution for Togo (2026), with the US State Department rating the country Level 2 (2026). While the immediate security picture in Lome is manageable for prepared travellers, the regional threat environment elevated by Sahel jihadist activity in the north of the country means security planning cannot be confined to street-level situational awareness.

CPO deployments in Lome are typically configured around airport arrival protocols at Lome-Tokoin International Airport (LFW), secure transfers to business premises, and coverage during government or port authority meetings. Operators work with locally authorised security personnel to satisfy Togolese regulatory requirements and to draw on the local route knowledge that is essential in a city where road conditions and demonstration activity can change rapidly.
