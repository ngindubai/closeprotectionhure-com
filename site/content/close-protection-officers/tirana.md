Tirana is the capital and largest city of Albania, a country in the Western Balkans that is actively pursuing EU membership. Albania has made significant governance and rule-of-law improvements over the past decade, but organised crime networks retain a meaningful presence in parts of the economy, and the FCDO advises a high degree of caution (2026). The US State Department rates Albania Level 2 (2026). For senior executives, diplomatic personnel, and high-net-worth individuals visiting Tirana for investment, due diligence, or official engagements, structured close protection provides a professional baseline that accounts for this background risk.

CPO deployments in Tirana are typically centred on airport arrivals at Tirana International Airport Mother Teresa (TIA), secure transfers to business premises or hotels in the Blloku district, and coverage during commercial negotiations, government meetings, or private engagements. Albanian-licensed operators are integrated into all deployment teams under Law No. 75/2014, providing both regulatory compliance and the local knowledge that effective route planning requires.
