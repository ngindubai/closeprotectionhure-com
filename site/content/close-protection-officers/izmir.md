    description: "Private security in Izmir is governed by Turkish Law 5188, Ozel Guvenlik Hizmetlerine Dair Kanun (2004), which regulates private security services nationally. Licensing is administered by the Turkish National Police (Emniyet Mudurlugu) at both national and provincial level; the Izmir Police Directorate oversees local compliance. All security companies must hold a national operating licence and all individual security officers must hold personal certificates issued following Ministry of Interior-approved training. Armed close protection requires specific authorisation from the provincial police directorate and is significantly more restricted for commercial clients than in some neighbouring jurisdictions. Foreign security firms must partner with a Turkish-licensed entity; they cannot operate directly without Turkish registration. Foreign individual security officers may not hold firearms in Turkey without special state approval, which is not routinely granted for commercial assignments. Work permits for foreign security personnel are processed through the Ministry of Labour and require the Turkish partner firm to demonstrate the necessity of foreign expertise. The full permit process typically takes four to six weeks; short-notice deployments therefore depend heavily on the capability of the Turkish partner firm rather than on foreign CPO deployment."
