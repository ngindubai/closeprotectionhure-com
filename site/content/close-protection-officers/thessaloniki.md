    description: "Private security in Thessaloniki is governed by Greek Law 2518/1997 on private security companies, as subsequently amended, with oversight administered by the Greek Police (ELAS, Elliniki Astynomia). All private security companies operating in Greece must hold a licence issued by the Greek Police, and individual security officers must hold personal authorisations. Armed close protection in a commercial context requires specific approval from the Ministry of Public Order and Citizen Protection; this level of authorisation is uncommon for standard corporate clients and the approval process is protracted. EU-licensed security officers may operate in Greece under freedom of movement provisions, though recognition of individual foreign qualifications varies in practice. Foreign firms without a Greek-licensed presence typically work through established Greek security partners. Due diligence on any Greek security firm should include verification of their ELAS licence number and confirmation that individual officers hold personal authorisations.
