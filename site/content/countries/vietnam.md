Vietnam's two cities on this network sit at the low end of this site's risk scale, a genuinely different profile from most of the destinations covered here. What drives the security plan in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City is less classic close-protection threat and more a combination of licensing structure, motorbike-based street crime, and traffic risk that is easy to underestimate from outside the country.

## An unarmed, provincially licensed sector

Private security here is unarmed by default and tightly regulated. Private Security Decree 96/2016/ND-CP puts every company under Ministry of Public Security oversight, with licensing issued at the provincial level. That has a practical consequence for anyone planning a two-city Vietnam itinerary: a company's MPS licence for Hanoi work is not proof of its standing for Ho Chi Minh City, and foreign firms cannot deliver services independently in either city without a Vietnamese-registered, currently licensed local entity behind the engagement.

## Motorbike-based street crime, not violent crime

Neither city carries a significant violent-crime profile for foreign visitors. The recurring theme instead is motorbike bag and phone snatching, concentrated in the Old Quarter and Hoan Kiem in Hanoi, and District 1's Ben Thanh area in Ho Chi Minh City, both places where tourists and business visitors carry visible valuables. Victims who resist a snatch attempt can be dragged, so the standard advice, don't carry valuables visibly and don't resist, is a genuine risk-reduction measure rather than boilerplate caution.

## Traffic as a first-order risk

Both cities have among the highest motorbike densities anywhere, and road traffic incidents involving foreigners happen regularly enough that Vietnam's own Road Traffic Safety Committee tracks it as a standing concern. Emergency response times vary by district and can be materially longer than a visitor might expect from a low-crime destination. Vetted drivers who understand local traffic patterns are, in practical terms, the single highest-value security measure available in either city.
