Iraq's three cities on this network span two genuinely different regulatory jurisdictions inside one country, and that split matters more here than in almost anywhere else this site covers. Federal Iraq, Baghdad and Basra, runs private security through the Ministry of Interior's Private Security Companies Department under Law No. 2 of 2017. The Kurdistan Region of Iraq, home to Erbil, licenses separately through its own Ministry of Interior. Neither credential transfers to the other's territory.

## Two licensing systems, not one

A security company's PSCD registration establishes its standing to operate in Baghdad and Basra. It says nothing about Erbil, where the Kurdistan Regional Government issues its own permits through a wholly separate register. Clients planning a multi-city Iraq itinerary, Baghdad to Erbil, say, need licensing confirmed for each leg rather than assumed from the first city. This is not a bureaucratic technicality: engaging an unlicensed operator in either jurisdiction carries real legal exposure on top of the physical security risk.

## Armed protection as the operational default

Unlike most destinations on this network, armed close protection in Iraq is the norm rather than an escalation. In federal territory, armed teams work under PSCD licensing and Ministry of Interior weapons oversight. In the KRI, armoured vehicles and armed protection are standard for higher-risk principals, licensed through the KRG rather than Baghdad. Both routes require currently valid credentials; neither substitutes for the other.

## Where the threat picture actually differs

Kidnap-for-ransom targeting of Western business executives is the consistent thread across all three cities, sharpest in Basra given its concentration of oil and gas sector personnel. Baghdad adds Iran-aligned militia activity and residual IED capability to that picture. Erbil is materially calmer day to day, but Iran-aligned rocket and drone attacks have struck Erbil International Airport and the US Consulate without warning, and ISIL retains insurgent capability near the region's administrative border. The FCDO advises against all travel to most of federal Iraq outside the Kurdistan Region, a distinction that itself reflects the genuinely different risk environments involved.
