Havana occupies a unique position in the Caribbean executive travel market: a city of significant historical and commercial interest that operates under a security and transport framework unlike any other destination in the region. For business visitors, journalists, and investment scouts, the key transport challenge in Havana is not primarily physical security but operational reliability in an environment where standard transport alternatives are limited and unpredictable.

Ride-share applications do not operate in Cuba. Informal taxi arrangements are unreliable and, for sensitive commercial visits, operationally unsuitable. Pre-arranged professional ground transport from a driver who knows the city, understands the access protocols of key business destinations, and operates with appropriate discretion is the practical standard for any executive visit.
