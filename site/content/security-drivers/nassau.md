Nassau is the capital of the Bahamas and one of the Caribbean's most active private aviation and superyacht destinations, drawing high-net-worth principals to resort properties including Atlantis Paradise Island, Albany, and the private Lyford Cay Club. The FCDO Bahamas travel advice (2024) records violent crime including armed robbery concentrated in certain residential areas of New Providence, creating a moderate risk environment that warrants professional ground transport planning for HNWI and corporate principals.

Lynden Pindling International Airport (NAS), 16km from central Nassau, is the primary international arrival point; the airport also has private aviation FBO facilities frequently used by charter and business jet arrivals. All transfers to resort and hotel destinations are managed with inside-terminal or FBO collection and operations-controller GPS tracking throughout. Drivers hold current BSIRA credentials under the Bahamas Private Security Licensing Act Cap. 199.
