N'Djamena is the capital of Chad, a landlocked Sahelian country whose oil sector (ExxonMobil, CNPC, TotalEnergies) draws corporate visitors and whose large humanitarian sector supports a significant NGO and UN agency presence. Chad operates under a transitional military council following the death of President Idriss Deby in 2021. The US State Department rates Chad at Level 3 (Reconsider Travel) as of 2026, and FCDO advises against all travel to the country's border regions with Sudan, Libya, Nigeria, and the CAR. In N'Djamena itself, the security environment concentrates on robbery and opportunistic crime risk outside the central diplomatic and commercial districts.
