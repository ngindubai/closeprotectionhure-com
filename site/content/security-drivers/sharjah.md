The single most useful thing a security driver working Sharjah can offer isn't a fast car, it's a realistic journey-time estimate across the Al Ittihad Road border into Dubai. That corridor, particularly around Al Nahda, backs up predictably during the morning and evening commute, a pattern Gulf News has tracked through 2026, and Emirates Road (E611) is the usual fallback when a reliable arrival time matters more than the shortest route.

Licensing runs differently here than in Dubai. Drivers operating legally in Sharjah hold Private Security Business Department (PSBD) certification through Abu Dhabi Police, under the federal Ministry of Interior, rather than Dubai's SIRA licence. Since most Sharjah assignments also touch Dubai, in a single day, drivers commonly need familiarity with both regulatory environments rather than one or the other.

Sharjah International Airport (SHJ) is worth considering as an arrival point in its own right. As Air Arabia's home base, it handles close to 20 million passengers a year across more than 90 destinations, and sits around 20 kilometres from Dubai's northern edge, often a less congested alternative to DXB for a Sharjah-focused visit. Where the itinerary includes the Sharjah Airport International Free Zone (SAIF Zone), drivers who know the approach roads and the industrial-area layout make the difference between a smooth site visit and a late one.
