Riyadh's ground transport security picture is shaped by a combination of factors that do not apply in most other major business cities: the active regional conflict creating missile and drone threat to Saudi infrastructure including the main airport, the specific legal and cultural environment for foreign visitors, and the scale and unfamiliarity of Riyadh's road network to first-time visitors. A pre-arranged MoI-authorised security driver addresses all three.

## The ground transport context in Riyadh

Violent crime against foreign business visitors in Riyadh's central districts is low by international comparison. The Saudi security services are effective and well-resourced. The threats that shape security driving planning in Riyadh are: the Houthi missile and drone threat to Saudi infrastructure (which has targeted RUH specifically), the legal environment risk for foreign nationals who may be unfamiliar with Saudi regulations, and the road safety dimension of Riyadh's motorway network.

The Diplomatic Quarter has specific access protocols that experienced local drivers handle as standard; improvised transport arrangements at the Diplomatic Quarter compound are not straightforward. KAFD similarly has specific access protocols that pre-arrangement resolves.

## MoI authorisation framework

Commercial security operations in Saudi Arabia require Ministry of Interior authorisation. Foreign security companies cannot operate directly; they work through MoI-authorised Saudi partners. The verification step for clients is to ask for the MoI authorisation number of the Saudi operating company.
