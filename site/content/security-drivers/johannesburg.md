Johannesburg's road network is where most security incidents affecting foreign visitors occur. The carjacking pattern in Gauteng is well-documented in South African Police Service quarterly statistics: the N3 and N1 corridors from OR Tambo, the stop-light environments in Johannesburg's inner suburbs, and the parking structures around major commercial and hotel destinations all feature in the incident data. A vetted security driver addresses these specific patterns, not a generic urban risk.

## The ground transport threat in Johannesburg

Carjacking in South Africa is not opportunistic; it is organised. Vehicles are often followed from OR Tambo arrivals, from ATMs, or from known departure points. Routes and timing that signal predictability create vulnerability. The operational mitigations, pre-arranged collection with known vehicle identification at a specific terminal point, route variation, stop-light protocols, and operations controller oversight, address the documented pattern rather than a generic threat.

Armoured vehicles are available and appropriate for specific profiles. For most corporate visitors in Sandton, Rosebank, and the established corporate belt, PSIRA-trained standard vehicles with proper route planning meet the risk picture. The armoured option is a calibrated response to a specific threat assessment, not a default requirement.

## PSIRA compliance

All drivers engaged for commercial security purposes in South Africa must hold current PSIRA registration. The verification step is to ask for the PSIRA registration number of each driver and confirm currency. A standard taxi or ride-share driver is not PSIRA-registered for security work, regardless of how well they know the roads.
