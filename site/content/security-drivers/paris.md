Paris's security driver market is shaped by three specific risk vectors that distinguish it from most other Western European capitals: the organised motorcycle gang threat targeting luxury vehicles in traffic, the HNWI jewellery and luxury goods theft in the central arrondissements, and the periodic significant social unrest that disrupts normal vehicle movement across the city. None of these vectors is primarily addressed by close protection officers; all three are directly addressed by a properly qualified, counter-aware security driver operating to an operations controller standard.

## The Paris vehicle threat environment

French police statistics and security sector reporting document the specific pattern of luxury vehicle targeting in Paris traffic. The incidents concentrate in the 8th and 16th arrondissements, the Champs-Elysees corridor, and around the major luxury hotels and jewellery retail addresses. Security driver training addresses these specific incidents with counter-protocols that do not require a full CPO presence to implement effectively.

## VTC licensing and CNAPS authorisation

The combination of VTC professional transport licence and CNAPS carte professionnelle provides the complete regulatory basis for commercial security driving in France. Both licences are verifiable, and any operator unable to confirm both for a proposed driver is not in full compliance with the French legal framework for security driving.
