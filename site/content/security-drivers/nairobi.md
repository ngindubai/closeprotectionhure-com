Nairobi's ground transport security picture is shaped primarily by vehicle crime on specific corridors at specific times. The Mombasa Road from JKIA, the Thika Road, and movement after dark in less central areas are the focus of incident reporting. A PSRA-registered security driver with local route knowledge and carjacking avoidance training addresses these patterns directly; a standard taxi or ride-share driver does not.

## The ground transport threat in Nairobi

Carjacking and armed robbery targeting vehicles carrying foreign visitors are documented by both the FCDO and the Kenya Police Service. The pattern concentrates on the JKIA arrival corridor and on movements in peripheral Nairobi after dark. The central diplomatic and corporate belt, Westlands, Kilimani, Upper Hill, and the Karen and Runda residential areas, are lower-risk but not risk-free, and the journeys between them and meeting venues outside that belt create vulnerability windows.

For NGO and UN sector visitors, Nairobi's journey management requirements are specific and established: journey management plans, check-in intervals, and response protocols are part of the standard operating environment, not exceptional measures. Our drivers are briefed on these standards and can work within them.

## PSRA compliance

Kenya's Private Security Regulation Act 2016 requires security personnel to hold PSRA registration. The verification step is to ask for the PSRA registration number of the operating company and of the specific driver assigned. Confirm the registration is current.
