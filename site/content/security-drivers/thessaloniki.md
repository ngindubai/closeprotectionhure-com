Thessaloniki is northern Greece's largest city and the country's second commercial centre, hosting the annual Thessaloniki International Fair in September and a year-round programme of business, academic, and cultural events. The city presents a low-moderate risk profile: the FCDO Greece travel advice (2024) records occasional civil unrest and protest activity around the central districts and university area. Security driver services focus on professional route-planned ground transport, with particular attention to protest intelligence during periods of known civil unrest and the substantially increased vehicle and crowd pressure during TIF season.

Thessaloniki Makedonia Airport (SKG), 13km southeast of the city, is the primary arrival point for international corporate principals. All transfers are managed with inside-terminal collection and operations-controller GPS tracking. Drivers hold current Hellenic Police credentials under Greek Law 2518/1997.
