Few German cities pair an aerospace giant with one of Europe's biggest port complexes quite the way Bremen does. Airbus Bremen alone covers around 450,000 square metres and employs well over 4,000 people, and a good number of the visits a security driver handles here start or end at that gate off the A27. Bremerhaven, roughly 65 kilometres to the north and reached by the same motorway, extends the working day considerably when a principal's itinerary includes the port as well as the city.

That combination shapes the licensing and training picture too. Drivers register through the Handelskammer Bremen and the Bremen Ordnungsamt under Sec 34a Gewerbeordnung, hold a Sachkundeprufung certificate for anything beyond routine work, and operate unarmed, in keeping with standard German private security practice. Route familiarity matters more than it might in a smaller, single-centre city: knowing when to route around Groepelingen or the Bahnhofsvorstadt near the Hauptbahnhof, and when Schwachhausen or Horn-Lehe make the better collection point, is built up over repeated runs rather than a single briefing.

Principals moving on from Bremen typically continue north to Hamburg or south towards Frankfurt, and it helps to have drivers who already know those legs.
