## PSIRA and PDP compliance

South Africa's PSIRA registration is national in scope; the same framework applies in Cape Town as in Johannesburg. In addition, commercial passenger transport requires a Professional Driving Permit (PDP) from the relevant provincial transport authority. In the Western Cape, PDPs are issued by the Department of Transport and Public Works. Both the PSIRA registration and the PDP are verifiable, and operating companies should provide both on request.

## The N2 corridor: the Cape Town transfer's key risk point

The N2 between Cape Town International Airport and the CBD is the most specific and consistently documented vehicle crime risk for arriving business visitors to Cape Town. The areas adjacent to the N2 through Langa, Gugulethu, and Mitchells Plain carry elevated gang-related violence statistics that place the transfer window in a materially different risk category from the destination areas in the city bowl. Security driver N2 route monitoring, alternative routing through the R300, and operations controller tracking throughout are the correct operational responses.
