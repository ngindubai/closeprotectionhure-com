Basra's oil-and-gas economy brings a consistent flow of international executives, project managers, and technical specialists to one of the world's most challenging security environments. Ground transport in Basra is not a logistical convenience: it is a primary security risk that demands professional management at every stage of a movement.

The FCDO advises against all but essential travel to Basra Governorate. Armed ambush, kidnap-for-ransom, and militia checkpoint activity are live threats on the road corridors that connect Basra International Airport, accommodation compounds, and oil-field operational sites. Standard taxi or car-hire arrangements are wholly inadequate for these conditions.

Our Basra security drivers are PSCD-licensed under Law No. 2/2017, have completed hostile-environment vehicle operations training, and operate in B6-level armoured SUVs as the default platform for all client movements. Route intelligence is updated daily, convoy protocols are defined before each movement, and all operations maintain contact with a 24-hour operations centre.

For executives arriving from outside Iraq, the detail begins at Basra International Airport (BSR) and continues through every movement until departure. Compound-to-compound transfers, oil-field site visits, and port-district movements are all managed under the same strict protocol.
