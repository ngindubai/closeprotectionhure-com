Santiago's ground transport environment is shaped by several specific, FCDO-documented threat patterns that are distinct from the ambient crime picture. The city is assessed as lower risk than most South American capitals, and Chile's overall stability is genuine. However, the airport taxi scam, tyre puncture scam, vehicle targeting and civil unrest calendar are concrete operational factors that professional security driving directly addresses.

## The SCL airport problem

The most immediate risk for corporate arrivals in Santiago is at Arturo Merino Benitez Airport (SCL). FCDO documents individual losses of up to GBP 10,000 from a well-organised taxi scam: criminals in the arrivals hall wear official-looking lanyards and approach passengers before they reach the official pre-paid booth inside the terminal. Fares are then inflated by adding digits to the metre. The mitigation is structural, not behavioural: a pre-briefed officer positioned in the arrivals hall before the principal clears customs, with confirmed named transport to the vehicle. Our SCL collection protocol provides this as standard.

## Vehicle targeting awareness

FCDO specifically identifies hire cars as targeted at viewpoints and petrol stations across the Santiago metropolitan area, with armed carjacking documented on affluent highway corridors. Ground transport vehicles used for professional security driving in Santiago are not in a hire-car profile. Stops at exposed viewpoints are avoided, and highway routing is managed to reduce the specific corridors identified by FCDO documentation.

## Civil unrest planning

Santiago's protest calendar is predictable and is built into every ground movement plan. The 11 September coup anniversary, 29 March Day of Young Combatant, 1 May Workers Day and Friday afternoons are consistently active. Alternative routing is prepared in advance for itineraries falling near these dates, rather than improvised at the time.
