Ankara presents a security driver operating environment shaped by two distinct factors: the concentration of Turkish government, military, and diplomatic infrastructure that makes it the hub for a specific category of high-profile engagement; and the elevated terrorism threat that has historically made Turkey's capital a target for both PKK and DHKP-C activity. Security drivers in Ankara require ÖSAS certification, knowledge of government district access procedures, and an operational briefing that addresses the current FCDO advisory alongside the practical logistics of the capital city.

## Ankara versus Istanbul for Turkey engagements

Ankara and Istanbul have fundamentally different security driver operating environments. Istanbul is Turkey's commercial capital, with a larger corporate and HNWI client base, two major airports, and a more developed luxury hospitality infrastructure. Ankara is the administrative capital: its security driver demand comes primarily from diplomatic missions, government contractors, and organisations engaged with Turkish state institutions. Security drivers with experience of both cities, and specifically of the Ankara government district access protocols, are the appropriate choice for principals whose Turkey programme includes government-quarter engagements.

## Coordinating for diplomatic missions

Ankara hosts all foreign embassies to Turkey. Security drivers covering embassy district movements (Cankaya, Kavaklidere) should maintain knowledge of which embassies have specific access restrictions, outer gate procedures, and any current temporary security measures. The UK Embassy, the US Embassy (now at the new compound), and several other diplomatic missions have implemented vehicle access controls that require pre-notification. Drivers should confirm access procedures directly with the embassy's security section before the first movement.
