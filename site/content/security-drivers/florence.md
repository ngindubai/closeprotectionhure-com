Florence draws a concentrated mix of principals: art-market buyers, fashion executives attending Pitti Uomo and Pitti Immagine, and high-net-worth visitors to Tuscany's wine estates and private collections. The FCDO classifies Italy as a broadly low-risk destination, while advising awareness of pickpocketing in tourist-heavy city centres. In Florence, the Uffizi queue, Ponte Vecchio, and the Mercato Centrale represent the highest pedestrian-crime density. Demonstration activity around Piazza della Repubblica can generate short-notice route changes, and ACI data from 2024 records vehicle crime at A1 motorway service stations on the approach to the city.

Security driver operations in Florence require navigation of the Zona a Traffico Limitato (ZTL) network that restricts most private vehicles from the historic core. Drivers hold current ZTL access permits for applicable vehicle categories and know the restricted-hour enforcement schedule. Route planning around the Pitti Uomo season (January and June) accounts for elevated pedestrian density near the Fortezza da Basso and Piazza Santa Croce. All journeys are tracked from an operations controller who maintains the ability to reroute around incidents or demonstrations in real time.
