Brasilia International Airport (BSB) is the first thing any security driver here plans around: about 11 kilometres out, 15 to 25 minutes into the Plano Piloto depending on traffic, and the standard model is direct terminal collection rather than a public rank. Compare that to Sao Paulo's sprawl, where the airport-to-city leg alone can eat an hour, and Brasilia's compact, planned geometry starts to look like an advantage rather than a footnote.

That geometry is Lucio Costa's doing. His original plan runs along two long axes, the Monumental Axis carrying the government sector and the Residential Axis running through Asa Sul and Asa Norte, and drivers use that predictable grid to keep routing simple. Government-sector and Setor de Embaixadas access, and the routing implications of the November 2024 Praca dos Tres Poderes incident, get built into the briefing for any assignment touching those precincts. Outside the Plano Piloto, satellite cities including Ceilandia, Santa Maria, Sao Sebastiao, and Paranoa carry a different after-dark profile, per the OSAC Brasilia crime and safety report, and drivers route around them where possible.

Vehicle specification here is lighter than Sao Paulo's armoured-B4-as-standard model. Executive saloons suit most Brasilia assignments, and drivers hold Policia Federal registration under Portaria 3233/2012, with Distrito Federal state oversight, the same national framework applied across Brazil.
