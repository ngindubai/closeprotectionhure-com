Sao Paulo's ground transport security picture is dominated by one well-documented threat: armed robbery of executive vehicles during traffic stops and at traffic lights. This is recorded in both FCDO and US State Department advisories and in multiple years of local incident reporting. A DPF-authorised security driver with stop-light protocols, route variation, and operations controller oversight addresses this threat directly.

## The ground transport threat in Sao Paulo

The FCDO travel advisory for Brazil notes the risk of armed robbery in major cities including Sao Paulo. Vehicle crime, particularly at traffic stops, has been documented across the GRU corridor, peripheral Sao Paulo districts, and movements after dark outside the core corporate belt. The Faria Lima and Jardins corporate districts have a better ambient profile than peripheral Sao Paulo, but the journeys to and from them via GRU and via late-evening returns from client events are where incident risk concentrates.

Armoured vehicles represent a calibrated response to the documented armed robbery threat. For principals in sectors with elevated profiles, they are the operationally appropriate choice rather than an exceptional measure.

## DPF compliance

Commercial security operations in Brazil are regulated by the Departamento de Policia Federal (DPF) under Lei No. 7.102/1983. Both operating companies and individual personnel require current DPF authorisation. The verification step is to ask for the DPF authorisation number of the operating company and the specific drivers proposed.
