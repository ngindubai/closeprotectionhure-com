Kualanamu International Airport is the starting point for almost every Medan security-driver assignment. It replaced the old Polonia Airport, which closed to commercial traffic in July 2013, and now sits roughly 39km east of the city centre. The Railink train covers that distance in 30 to 40 minutes regardless of road conditions, while a car transfer typically runs 60 to 90 minutes depending on traffic, so drivers plan the option that matches the principal's schedule rather than defaulting to one or the other.

City routes centre on Kesawan, Merdeka Walk, and the newer Setia Budi corridor, alongside the Medan Polonia district built around the old airport site. Beyond the city, a sizeable share of the work here is provincial: the roughly 170km, three-to-four hour drive to Parapat for Lake Toba access, or plantation and mill visits tied to North Sumatra's palm oil and agribusiness sector. Drivers heading toward Berastagi also work around a fixed boundary, the 5km exclusion zone FCDO advice sets around Mount Sinabung's crater in Karo Regency, an active volcano that erupted in 2021 and showed renewed activity in May 2026.

Licensing runs through POLRI registration and, for drivers taking on protective duties, individual certification, the same national framework applied across Indonesia. Seasonal haze, typically June to September, is a practical planning factor too, affecting both road visibility and Kualanamu's flight schedules.
