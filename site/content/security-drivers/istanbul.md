## The ground transport context in Istanbul

The FCDO terrorism threat rating for Turkey is high. Previous Istanbul attacks have targeted crowded public spaces and transport hubs. For ground movement planning, this translates to route awareness around known high-target-density areas at high-risk times, rather than route-avoidance that makes the itinerary unworkable. Istanbul is a functioning major business city; security driving here is about calibrated awareness, not paralysis.

The Bosphorus geography is the single most distinctive operational planning element in Istanbul. Principals who cross regularly, particularly those moving between IST on the European side and meetings or accommodation on the Asian side, create predictable patterns at the crossing points. Route and timing variation is the standard mitigation.

The intelligence environment in Istanbul affects executives in defence, energy, and technology. Counter-surveillance awareness in ground movement, OGG-certified drivers who know what out-of-pattern following looks like, and clear contact protocols if surveillance is suspected add operational value for these client profiles.

## OGG compliance

Turkey's Law 5188 requires OGG certification for commercial security driving. Operating companies must also hold Ministry of Interior registration. The verification step is to ask for the OGG certificate number of the specific driver assigned and the operating company's Ministry registration number.
