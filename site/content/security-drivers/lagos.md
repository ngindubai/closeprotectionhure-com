Lagos is Nigeria's commercial capital and the city where the gap between organised security driving and improvised transport creates the most material difference to the risk profile of a business trip. The LOS arrival and the subsequent drive to the accommodation is the single highest-risk window for most foreign visitors. ## The ground transport threat in Lagos

Vehicle-based robbery and express kidnapping targeting foreign executives are active threats in Lagos. The FCDO travel advisory for Nigeria specifically notes the risk to travellers, including in Lagos. Murtala Muhammed International's arrival zone is a documented contact point for individuals approaching foreign visitors; unbooked taxis from the airport precinct are linked to multiple documented incidents.

Armoured vehicles are available and appropriate for specific profiles. For executives in financial services and the energy sector, or those with a publicly visible high-net-worth profile, armoured-spec transport is a calibrated response to the documented threat level rather than an exceptional measure.

## NSCDC compliance

Commercial security operations in Nigeria require NSCDC licensing distinct from a standard commercial driving licence. The verification step for clients is to ask for the NSCDC licence number of the operating company and to confirm the specific drivers are employed under that licence.
