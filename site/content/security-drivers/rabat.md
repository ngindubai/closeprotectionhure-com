Two airports, one city: that's the first thing a security driver plans around in Rabat. Casablanca Mohammed V International Airport (CMN) is Morocco's main gateway, and getting from there to Rabat takes anywhere from 70 minutes to two hours by road or ONCF rail, depending on traffic. Rabat-Sale Airport (RBA) sits inside the city itself, cutting that leg out entirely for the 43 destinations it serves directly, Paris, Madrid, and Jeddah among them. Which one a driver builds a route around depends entirely on how the principal is flying in.

Once inside Rabat, the geography splits again. The medina and the Kasbah of the Oudayas are narrow, mostly pedestrian, and simply not built for vehicles, so pickups happen at the edge and the walk gets planned into the schedule. Souissi, the diplomatic quarter, is a different kind of constraint: heavy static security around the embassies there means drivers avoid unplanned stops and know the checkpoint protocol before they arrive, not after.

Licensing runs through Law 27-06, the same national framework covering close protection officers. Foreign firms don't deploy independently here; every assignment goes through an accredited Moroccan partner.
