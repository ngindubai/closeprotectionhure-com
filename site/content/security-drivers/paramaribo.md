Paramaribo is a medium-risk environment by regional standards, but the 45km airport transfer on the Johan Adolf Pengel Highway is one of the longest exposed transfer corridors for any capital city in the Americas. The FCDO Suriname advisory (2026) notes robbery risk, particularly after dark, and the US State Department rates Suriname at Level 2 (2026). The combination of a long predictable highway transfer and limited after-dark police presence makes pre-arranged, Ministry of Justice-authorised security driver cover the appropriate baseline for corporate visits rather than relying on kerbside transport at Johan Pengel.

Suriname's security environment is shaped by its extractive industries: gold mining and offshore oil exploration bring a steady flow of international business visitors, and this population carries elevated robbery risk documented in both FCDO and State Department reporting. The city centre around Waterkant and the diplomatic district is the lower-risk operating zone for corporate principals, but the highway transfer to and from Johan Pengel crosses areas with limited oversight, particularly at night. Security companies in Paramaribo operate under Ministry of Justice and Police oversight, and standards vary between providers: the growth of the sector to serve the mining industry has introduced operators with differing levels of training and compliance. Confirming Ministry authorisation for both the company and the assigned driver is the essential due-diligence step before any deployment.
