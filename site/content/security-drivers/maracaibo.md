Maracaibo is Venezuela's second city and the centre of the country's oil industry, in Zulia state on the western shore of Lake Maracaibo. The FCDO advises against all but essential travel to Venezuela (2024), citing very high violent crime, kidnapping, and armed robbery rates. Ground transport in Maracaibo is among the most complex and risk-intensive in South America, requiring armoured vehicles, trained security drivers with anti-ambush experience, and real-time route intelligence as the operational baseline for any principal movement.

La Chinita Airport (MAR), 25km northwest of the city, is the primary international entry and exit point. Airport transfers are planned as the highest-exposure event of any Maracaibo deployment, with route intelligence updated on the day of travel and convoy support applied where the risk assessment warrants it. Night movement is avoided as a standing operational principle. All deployments include medical evacuation pre-planning, as local trauma care infrastructure is limited.
