Bogota's ground transport security picture is dominated by paseo millonario, the local variant of express kidnapping carried out through unbooked taxis. This is not a theoretical threat; it is a documented, active pattern recorded in both FCDO and US State Department reporting as well as in local crime statistics. A SuperVigilancia-licensed security driver with the appropriate arrival protocols removes this threat entirely and provides informed management of secondary risks throughout the itinerary.

## The ground transport threat in Bogota

The FCDO travel advisory for Colombia notes the risk of kidnapping and robbery, with taxi-based express kidnapping specifically documented. The US State Department Colombia advisory similarly flags the transportation crime risk. The documented pattern for foreign business visitors concentrates on the airport arrival window and on any use of unbooked transport during the visit.

Within northern Bogota's Chico, Usaquen, Zona G, and Zona T districts, the risk profile for vetted-vehicle movements is materially more manageable. Movement into central and southern Bogota after dark is where secondary risks concentrate.

## SuperVigilancia compliance

Commercial security driving in Colombia requires SuperVigilancia authorisation under Decree 356/1994. Operating companies and individual drivers must hold current authorisation. The verification step is to ask for the SuperVigilancia licence number of the operating company and the specific driver assigned.
