Getting a principal to Canberra is often the first real planning decision. Canberra Airport (CBR) runs domestic services plus a handful of direct international routes to Bali, Fiji, and, from December 2026, Qatar Airways, but the large majority of international visitors still land at Sydney Airport and finish the trip by road. That overland leg covers roughly 286 kilometres and runs 2 hours 45 minutes in light traffic, stretching to 4 hours during Sydney's peak periods, generally via the Hume Highway through Goulburn. Building that transfer into the itinerary from the start, rather than treating Canberra as a standalone arrival point, is the practical starting position for most jobs here.

Once inside the city, the driving geography is unusually formal. Canberra was designed around Lake Burley Griffin and a deliberate set of boulevards and axes from Walter Burley Griffin's 1911 winning competition entry, and the Parliamentary Triangle, home to Parliament House, the High Court, and Government House, along with the Yarralumla embassy precinct nearby, both run on advance-clearance access rather than open entry. Drivers plan routes and staging points around confirmed clearances rather than assuming they can pull up wherever a schedule suggests.

Licensing runs through the ACT's own Security Industry Act 2003, administered by Access Canberra, distinct from the NSW framework across the border that governs Sydney. A driver's NSW credentials don't carry over automatically; ACT accreditation, or interstate mutual recognition where it applies, needs confirming before any Canberra assignment. Seasonal planning matters too. December to February brings Canberra's bushfire smoke risk, at its worst during the 2019-2020 Black Summer season when the city recorded the world's worst air quality on some days, and vehicle cabin filtration is worth checking as part of standard prep during that window.
