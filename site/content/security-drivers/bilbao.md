Bilbao is the commercial hub of the Basque Country, headquarters to Iberdrola, a major Spanish energy group, and home to BBVA's Basque operations and the internationally recognised Guggenheim Museum. The FCDO's Spain travel advice (2024) identifies petty crime in the Casco Viejo and on high-tourist days around the Guggenheim as the primary concerns, while noting that demonstration activity can affect the Gran Via. ETA disbanded formally in 2018, confirmed by the Basque Government in an official statement, and presents no current security threat. The city's valley geography means that main arterial routes funnel through a limited number of river crossings, making advance route planning for time-sensitive movements particularly important.

Security driver operations in Bilbao require familiarity with the Ertzaintza's operational role as the primary police force in the Basque Autonomous Community, working alongside Policia Nacional and Guardia Civil under a tri-service structure not found elsewhere in Spain. Drivers hold current TIP authorisation under Ley 5/2014 and are familiar with Ertzaintza protocols and emergency coordination procedures. The hilly terrain and narrow streets of the Casco Viejo require local navigational knowledge; the A-8 and A-68 motorway corridors provide the primary routes to San Sebastian, France, Madrid, and the Rioja wine estates.
