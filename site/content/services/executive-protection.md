Executive protection goes beyond putting an operator next to a principal. It is a coordinated security plan that begins before departure and continues until the principal is safely back home.

## The difference between presence and protection

Having someone who looks the part standing nearby is not executive protection. Actual protection requires threat intelligence, advance route planning, secure transport logistics, and a team that knows the local environment. The operator standing next to you is the visible layer of a much larger system.

## Who needs executive protection

The most common clients are C-suite executives travelling to cities where their company or industry carries elevated risk. Oil and gas executives in West Africa, financial services leaders in Latin America, and technology executives in regions with intellectual property theft concerns.

High-net-worth individuals, family offices, and their representatives also use executive protection when travelling to cities where their wealth profile makes them a potential target for kidnapping or extortion.

## How a detail operates

A standard executive protection detail in a high-risk city consists of a team leader (responsible for overall security planning), a close protection officer (nearest to the principal at all times), and a security driver. In elevated-threat environments, the team expands to include advance operatives and counter-surveillance.

Before arrival, the advance operative surveys every location on the principal's itinerary. Meeting venues, restaurants, hotels, airports. They identify entry and exit points, emergency routes, and the nearest medical facilities.

During the assignment, the team maintains continuous communication. The close protection officer manages immediate proximity. The driver maintains vehicle readiness. The team leader coordinates with our operations centre, which monitors local threat feeds and intelligence sources.
