Executive protection in Baghdad is a programme, not a single officer. It is the discipline a company applies when it owes a duty of care to an executive facing militia activity, kidnapping, and IED risk: advance work on venues and routes, a written threat assessment, an operations controller, and an armed secure convoy integrated with the corporate itinerary. This page is for security managers designing protection around a visit, where the need is layered, documented risk management rather than ad-hoc cover.

Our Baghdad teams work with Ministry of Interior-licensed operators under the Private Security Companies Law No. 19 of 2015, drawing on operatives with direct Green Zone and wider Baghdad operational experience. Every Baghdad engagement begins with a detailed written threat assessment covering the current FCDO advisory, the principal's specific itinerary, the access protocols at each planned venue, and the crisis and extraction plan that will govern the detail. Baghdad International Airport arrivals and departures are managed as high-exposure events with full armoured-vehicle protocols. The operations room tracks all movements in real time throughout the principal's stay.
