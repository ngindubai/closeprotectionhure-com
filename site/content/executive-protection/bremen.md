Airbus operates its second-largest German production site in Bremen, and the Bremen and Bremerhaven ports together form one of Europe's largest seaport complexes, giving the city a distinctly industrial and maritime character that shapes most executive protection demand here. FCDO Germany travel advice (2026) places general crime broadly in line with the UK, and the two areas requiring the most deliberate route planning are Bahnhofsvorstadt, with its longstanding street-level drug scene around Bremen Hauptbahnhof, and Gröpelingen, a port-adjacent district with documented elevated property crime.

CPOs qualified through the Handelskammer Bremen, the city-state's chamber of commerce equivalent to a regional IHK, operate unarmed as standard under the Bewachungsverordnung. Access to the Airbus site or port terminals always requires advance coordination, since corporate visitors cannot enter operational zones unescorted, and executive accommodation concentrates in Schwachhausen and Horn-Lehe, both low-crime eastern districts.
