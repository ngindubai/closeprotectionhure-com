Kumasi is Ghana's 2nd largest city and the Ashanti Region's commercial and cultural centre. FCDO Ghana 2026 rates the city at Level 2 (exercise normal precautions) and the overall risk profile is moderate. Road traffic safety (NRSA Ghana 2024: high road fatality rate) is the primary daily operational risk, followed by petty crime in the Kejetia Market area. GPS-registered executive protection with road safety advance planning, Kejetia Market protocols, and election-period monitoring provides the appropriate baseline for corporate and HNWI principals visiting Kumasi.

## Operating in Kumasi

KNUST and Asokwa districts form the primary EP base for visiting principals. Adum CBD is the primary business area with moderate risk. Kejetia Market requires specific advance work for any itinerary visit. Manhyia area movements need advance coordination for ceremonial periods. Road safety protocols apply to all movements including the N6 Accra-Kumasi highway.

## Licensing and operator selection

All executive protection operators in Kumasi must hold GPS registration under the Private Security Organisation Act 1963 (Act 199). For transport cover, see our [Kumasi city security briefing](/cities/kumasi/) and [security drivers in Kumasi](/security-drivers/kumasi/).
