Executive protection in Mumbai is a programme, not a single officer. It is the discipline a company applies to protect a travelling executive against a documented terrorism history and dense-city exposure: advance work on venues and routes, a written threat assessment, an operations controller, and secure transport integrated with the corporate itinerary. This page is for security managers designing protection around a C-suite visit, where the need is layered, documented risk management rather than ad-hoc cover.

## The EP requirement in Mumbai

For most senior executive visitors to Mumbai, the EP baseline is a Maharashtra PSARA-licensed close protection officer with a security driver and BOM collection protocol. The terrorism risk drives hotel security integration and the venue advance work component; the road safety dimension drives the specific driver selection standard. For female executives, specific EP options are discussed at the planning stage.

For executives in sectors with elevated threat profiles in India, technology IP, defence, extractives, or financial services with sensitive India exposure, a sector-specific threat dimension is added to the pre-travel assessment.

## PSARA licensing for EP in Mumbai

Maharashtra PSARA licensing is the regulatory requirement for EP in Mumbai. For multi-city India itineraries, each state requires separate PSARA licensing; coordinating this is part of what a professional India EP engagement provides. The verification step is the Maharashtra PSARA licence number of the operating company.
