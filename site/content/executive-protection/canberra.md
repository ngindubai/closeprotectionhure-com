Executive protection in Canberra runs on a different client base to Australia's larger cities. This is the federal capital, purpose-built after the 1911 Griffin design competition rather than grown up around trade or industry, and its principal work reflects that: government contracts, public-sector executives, and diplomatic-adjacent business dominate over the finance-sector and retail-crowd settings that define Sydney or Melbourne assignments. The Australian Public Service based in the ACT numbered 70,221 in 2025, around 35 per cent of the entire federal workforce according to the 2025 State of the Service figures, and Canberra also hosts over 100 foreign embassies and high commissions, more than any other Australian city, per the National Capital Authority.

That combination changes what "threat assessment" means here. Australia's national terrorism threat level is PROBABLE under ASIO, a nationwide baseline that applies to Canberra as much as anywhere else, but the city carries none of Sydney's recent mass-casualty history. What actually drives operational complexity is density and access control: Parliament House, the High Court, Government House, and the Yarralumla embassy precinct sit inside a compact, formally planned geography, and each runs its own clearance procedures rather than allowing casual visits. Advance work here is about confirming the right clearance for the right venue, not managing crowd risk.

Licensing compliance is a real planning item rather than a formality. Canberra's Class 1B bodyguard licence, issued under the ACT Security Industry Act 2003 and administered by Access Canberra, is a separate regime from the NSW licence covering Sydney, and the two do not transfer automatically. Firms running principals between Canberra and Sydney assignments need to check ACT-specific compliance directly. Seasonal considerations round out the picture: bushfire smoke contingency planning for December to February, and awareness of magpie swooping season, roughly July to November, for outdoor movement across Canberra's tree-lined government precincts.
