Moscow represents the most operationally complex EP environment within Europe and the former Soviet Union for Western principals. The FCDO advice against all travel to Russia means that visits to Moscow carry a formal risk threshold that requires board or legal counsel authorisation before EP planning begins. EP in this context is not primarily about street-level protection but about managing the legal, political, and counter-intelligence dimensions of the visit alongside the physical security requirements.

The FSB's documented monitoring of foreign business meetings in Moscow means that counter-intelligence protocols are integrated into EP planning from day one. Device security, secure communication, and hotel selection are addressed in pre-travel briefings alongside the more conventional route advance and transfer protocols. For principals visiting [government ministries or corporate partners in Moscow](/cities/moscow/), advance coordination with the host organisation's own security team is essential, but the EP plan must operate independently of that coordination and must not assume it will be complete or candid.

Armed close protection through a Rosgvardia-licensed CHOP is a legal option for principals with documented threat profiles. Engagement of any local security subcontractor requires sanctions compliance screening under UK and EU law. For principals who require [bodyguard hire in Moscow](/bodyguard-hire/moscow/) on an extended basis, the EP provider must maintain continuous legal review of the operating environment and must establish clear protocols for early departure if the political situation deteriorates. Security drivers operating in Moscow must be vetted under the same Rosgvardia framework and screened under the same sanctions compliance process.
