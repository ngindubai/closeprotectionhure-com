Spielwarenmesse alone brings around 2,800 exhibitors from roughly 60 countries and some 67,000 visitors through NürnbergMesse each year, and it is one of three major fairs, alongside BrauBeviale and Consumenta, that shape what executive protection in Nuremberg actually involves. For most principals visiting the city, the job is less about the wider streets and more about credentialed access: hospitality suites, delegation transfers, and opening events coordinated with the exhibition centre's own security team well before arrival.

Bavaria is among the lowest-crime German states, and Nuremberg carries no targeted threat to corporate visitors beyond the nationwide terrorism assessment that covers the whole country, with enhanced security typical around large public events such as fair openings. Two sites here need particular care regardless of the calm baseline. The Palace of Justice is still a working Bavarian courthouse, and institutional visitors need access arranged through their host rather than turning up. Former Courtroom 600, now the Memorium Nuremberg Trials memorial, and the nearby Documentation Centre Nazi Party Rally Grounds draw a steady stream of diplomatic and institutional delegations, and both need advance coordination given their sensitivity and scale.

Officers here have held a mandatory IHK Sachkundeprufung since January 2021 on top of Sec 34a Gewerbeordnung registration, though that exam sets a training floor rather than a CP specialism, so additional protection-specific training matters for higher-profile work.
