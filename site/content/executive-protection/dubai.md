Executive protection in Dubai is a programme, not a single officer. Even in a low street-crime environment, it is the discipline a company applies to protect a travelling executive's privacy, schedule, and legal and cultural compliance: advance work on venues, a written threat assessment, an operations controller, and discreet secure transport integrated with the corporate itinerary. This page is for security managers designing protection around a C-suite visit, where the need is layered, documented risk management rather than ad-hoc cover.

## The EP requirement in Dubai

For the majority of corporate visitors to Dubai, formal EP is not required. The security environment for routine business travel in DIFC, Downtown, and the corporate hotel districts is genuinely low-risk. EP in Dubai serves a specific slice of the visitor and resident population: HNWI clients for whom the concentration of wealth creates a targeting profile, executives in sectors with regional intelligence interest, principals with documented threats, and family-office clients with UAE residency.

The 2022 Abu Dhabi infrastructure attack added a regional tension dimension to all UAE security planning. It has not changed the ambient crime picture, but it has changed the thoroughness of shelter-in-place planning and the depth of the regional threat section in pre-travel briefs.

## SIRA licensing for EP

All commercial EP in Dubai is regulated by SIRA. Operating companies and individual CPOs must hold current SIRA licences. The verification step for clients is to ask for the SIRA licence numbers of the operating company and the specific officers proposed.
