## Licensing and due diligence in Osaka

All close protection officers deployed in Osaka must be licensed under Security Business Act (Act No.117 of 1972), Osaka Prefectural Police approval. All close protection in Japan is unarmed; operational effectiveness relies on advance planning and structured movement protocols. The Osaka Prefectural Police maintains the register; confirming individual CPO licensing before engagement is standard practice, not an optional step.
