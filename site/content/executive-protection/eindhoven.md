Eindhoven is the Netherlands' high-technology capital, home to ASML (the world's largest manufacturer of photolithography systems, headquartered in adjacent Veldhoven), NXP Semiconductors, and the Philips Innovation Campus. The AIVD annual report (2023) explicitly identifies the Dutch semiconductor and high-tech sector as a target of foreign state actor intelligence operations, making Eindhoven unique among low-threat European cities in carrying a documented counter-surveillance consideration for senior technology executives. The baseline security environment is low-risk: the FCDO Netherlands travel advice (2024) records petty theft in city centres as the primary concern for general business travellers.

Justis-registered close protection officers operating in Eindhoven hold individual Ministry of Justice screening clearance under the WPBR 1997. They operate in business-appropriate attire consistent with the high-tech professional environment, and counter-surveillance briefings are incorporated as standard for ASML, NXP, and Philips Innovation Campus deployments. Eindhoven Airport (EIN) transfers, 7km from the city, and the 75-minute Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) corridor are both managed with inside-terminal collection and operations-controller tracking.
