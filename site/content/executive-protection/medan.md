Executive visits to Medan tend to look different from those to Jakarta or Surabaya, and that difference starts with the economy. North Sumatra is one of Indonesia's largest palm oil producing regions, and Medan is where the companies operating those plantations, including Asian Agri, are based. A Medan trip therefore more often includes a plantation or mill site visit in the surrounding regencies than a single office meeting, and that provincial leg needs its own route and timing plan rather than an extension of city-centre coverage.

The threat picture layers Indonesia's national terrorism assessment, which the FCDO applies to Medan as it does across the country, with a genuinely local factor: FCDO advice advises against all travel within 5km of the crater of Mount Sinabung, an active volcano in Karo Regency roughly 70km from the city that erupted in 2021 and showed renewed activity in May 2026. Neither is an acute concern for a typical office-based visit, but both matter the moment an itinerary reaches toward Berastagi or the wider Karo highlands. Officers here hold POLRI registration under Indonesia's standard national licensing framework, and protection runs unarmed as the norm.

Kualanamu International Airport, roughly 39km from the city centre and the replacement for Polonia Airport after its 2013 closure to commercial traffic, is the standard arrival point, with the Railink train the more predictable onward option at 30 to 40 minutes. Executives who want to pair a Medan visit with Lake Toba, the world's largest volcanic lake and one of Sumatra's defining destinations, should treat the roughly three-to-four hour drive to Parapat as its own day rather than a quick add-on.
