Cairo presents a high-risk EP environment driven by Egypt's FCDO HIGH terrorism advisory, documented protest and anti-foreign sentiment risk, and the operational complexity of a city where major government functions have been relocating to the New Administrative Capital some 45 km to the east. For foreign executives visiting EGPC, the Central Bank of Egypt, or government ministries, the combination of politically sensitive meeting subjects and the structural vulnerability of the airport transfer requires EP planning that begins well before travel.

The airport transfer from Cairo International Airport to hotels in Zamalek, Maadi, or central Cairo is the phase most consistently identified as the highest-risk window in any Cairo deployment. Terminal 3 arrivals halls have limited vehicle control immediately outside, and the approach roads are subject to congestion and unpredictable traffic management. EP vehicles with vetted, MoI-cleared drivers are pre-positioned inside the airport before principal arrival, and transfer routes are surveyed 24 hours in advance with alternatives identified. Further detail on Cairo's security environment is available on the [Cairo city security page](/cities/cairo/).

Zamalek island, as the primary diplomatic and expatriate residential zone, has a relatively mature security environment by Cairo standards, but its island geography creates chokepoints at the two main bridges. EP teams account for these chokepoints in departure and arrival timing and maintain alternative crossing protocols for periods when one bridge is restricted.
