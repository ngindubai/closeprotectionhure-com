Antwerp's executive protection work splits between two very different worlds: the Diamond District, which handles a very large share of the world's rough and polished diamond trade behind its own jointly administered security regime, and the Port of Antwerp-Bruges, home to Europe's largest integrated chemical cluster and, less happily, identified by Belgian federal authorities as the continent's leading entry point for cocaine trafficking. The risk profile sits at low-to-medium, and FCDO Belgium travel advice (2026) is clear that organised crime violence linked to the port trade overwhelmingly affects those involved in it rather than corporate visitors.

SPF Interieur-licenced CPOs operate unarmed as standard, and Diamond District access always runs through the district's own security office alongside Antwerp Local Police rather than open entry. Belgium's OCAM/CUTA has held the national threat level at 3 out of 4 through early 2026, with reinforced patrols confirmed at Antwerp's Jewish quarter, a factor built into any crisis briefing for the city.
