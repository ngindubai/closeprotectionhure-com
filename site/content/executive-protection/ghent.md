Ghent is East Flanders' industrial and university capital, home to ArcelorMittal steel operations, Volvo Cars Belgium, and a significant pharmaceutical cluster including UCB and Ablynx/Sanofi at the Ghent campus. The city is also internationally known for the Gentse Feesten, a ten-day July festival that attracts up to one million visitors (City of Ghent data), making crowd management planning a distinctive requirement for any principal movement during that period. The security environment is low-risk: the FCDO Belgium travel advice (2024) records petty theft as the primary concern for business travellers.

SPF Interieur-authorised close protection officers hold Ministry of Interior personal badges under the Loi du 2 octobre 2017 and operate in business-appropriate attire suited to the industrial, pharmaceutical, and convention environments that characterise most Ghent executive itineraries. Brussels Airport (BRU) transfers, 55km southwest, and Ghent-Sint-Pieters rail arrivals are managed with inside-terminal and inside-station collection and operations-controller tracking. Gentse Feesten deployments in July require dedicated advance planning and early engagement to manage vehicle access restrictions and crowd density.
