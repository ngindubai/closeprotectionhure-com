Maracaibo is Venezuela's second city and the historic centre of the country's oil industry, attracting the international energy sector professionals who represent the primary category of international visitor to whom security planning is relevant. The FCDO advises against all but essential travel to Venezuela (2024), citing very high rates of violent crime and kidnapping. Executive protection in Maracaibo operates at the high-threat end of the close protection spectrum: armed CPOs, armoured vehicle convoy, minimal-exposure principal movement, and pre-arranged medical evacuation are the operational baseline, not optional enhancements.

All engagement planning begins before the principal's departure from their home country, not on arrival. Site surveys, accommodation vetting, route intelligence, armed provider selection, and medical evacuation confirmation are completed in the pre-deployment phase. La Chinita Airport (MAR), 25km northwest of the city, is treated as the highest-exposure point of any Maracaibo itinerary and managed accordingly. Provider selection operates through established long-term local partners with verified track records in Zulia state.
