Tallinn, Estonia's compact capital, is served by Lennart Meri Tallinn Airport (TLL), just 4 km east of the city centre. The UK FCDO advises normal precautions for Estonia as of 2026, and the US State Department rates the country at Level 1. Estonia's position as an EU and NATO member state, combined with its role as a global reference point for digital governance and cybersecurity, makes Tallinn a destination for an unusually concentrated mix of government, technology, defence, and financial services visitors. NATO's Cooperative Cyber Defence Centre of Excellence (CCDCOE) is based in the city, giving the information security dimension of any visit practical relevance.

The transfer from TLL to the Old Town or city centre is one of the shortest airport-to-centre runs in any European capital, normally around 10 minutes via the Tartu maantee corridor. Despite this, informal taxi operators have been documented overcharging arriving passengers outside the terminal, and the pedestrianised zones within Tallinn's UNESCO-listed Old Town require driver familiarity with current access restrictions. Our operatives meet clients named-board in arrivals, assist with luggage, and deliver them to the nearest permitted vehicle position within the Old Town. Medical contingency planning for Tallinn benefits from the city's proximity to Helsinki: medevac by air takes approximately 30 minutes, one of the fastest evacuation options from any European capital. The North Estonia Medical Centre (PERH) is the primary local facility for emergency care. Clients in technology, government, or defence sectors should discuss information security protocols, including device handling and communication practices, at the enquiry stage.
