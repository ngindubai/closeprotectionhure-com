Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA) serves Manila across four terminals in Pasay City, with Terminal 1 handling the majority of foreign national arrivals. The US OSAC Manila Crime and Safety Report (2024) identifies the roads immediately outside NAIA and the terminal kerbside as elevated-risk areas for vehicle crime targeting arriving passengers. Combined with EDSA congestion that can extend transfer times to over two hours during peak periods, the NAIA-to-city corridor requires careful planning rather than an improvised taxi queue.

Our secure transfer service deploys PNP-SIU licensed drivers (Republic Act 5487) in unmarked executive vehicles, positioned in authorised pick-up zones before your flight lands. Route planning covers primary and secondary options to Makati, Bonifacio Global City, and the Manila hotel belt, with live MMDA traffic monitoring throughout the transfer.
