Secure airport transfers in Ulaanbaatar begin at New Ulaanbaatar International Airport (NBIA), opened in July 2021 approximately 52km south-west of the city centre, a distance that represents the most significant logistical challenge in the Ulaanbaatar transfer programme. The closure of the former Buyant-Ukhaa Airport and the opening of NBIA fundamentally changed the arrival experience for Ulaanbaatar: what was previously a 15km transfer from the old site has become a 52km highway run exposed to some of the most extreme winter weather conditions of any capital-city transfer route in the world.

## The Ulaanbaatar security environment

The FCDO Mongolia travel advice (2026) and the US State Department Mongolia Level 1 advisory (exercise normal precautions, 2026) identify petty crime in the city centre, weather-related travel disruption, and periodic political demonstrations as the principal concerns for foreign visitors. Ulaanbaatar's winter temperatures reach -30 degrees Celsius and below, creating vehicle and exposure risks on the NBIA corridor that require specific mitigation planning. Demonstrations linked to corruption scandals and resource-revenue disputes have historically concentrated around Chinggis Square and the Government Palace, and have on occasion turned violent. The mining and resource extraction industries, which drive the majority of international business travel to Ulaanbaatar, also bring specific operational and regulatory contexts that security planning must account for.

## What the Ulaanbaatar transfer service covers

Inside-terminal collection at NBIA by a National Police Agency-registered driver; operations controller tracking active from landing through to confirmed accommodation arrival; winter-protocol vehicles from November to March with cold-start verification, dual heating checks, and emergency equipment; route management on the 52km NBIA corridor with defined waypoint check-ins; and coverage of Sukhbaatar District, Khan-Uul, and the Chinggis Square civic zone.
