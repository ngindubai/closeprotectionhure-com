Twelve kilometres west of central Bordeaux, Bordeaux-Merignac Airport's two terminals handle everything from domestic hops to long-haul Schengen arrivals, and knowing which one a principal is landing at is the first thing a driver confirms before positioning for collection. The A630 ring motorway and Boulevard Wilson corridor into the city takes 20 to 25 minutes in normal conditions, a figure that holds up well against FCDO France (2024) guidance for the region, which does not flag this route as carrying any particular vehicle crime risk. Gare Saint-Jean, reached from Paris in 2h04 by TGV, receives the same name-board collection standard for principals travelling by rail rather than air, and the two arrival points are treated as equally important in our route planning rather than the station being an afterthought.

The bigger planning variable is congestion rather than crime: trade fair weeks at the Parc des Expositions, which sits close to the airport corridor, and demonstration activity affecting the tram network in central Bordeaux can both add time to a transfer, so alternative routing is worked out ahead of the booking rather than on the day. Drivers hold a CNAPS carte professionnelle for the security side of the role and a VTC licence from DREAL Nouvelle-Aquitaine for the vehicle-hire side, and both credentials can be confirmed before a booking is accepted. For clients whose day continues on to a Médoc or Saint-Emilion chateau, a mid-size SUV generally handles the narrower estate approach lanes better than a full-size platform, and the driver checks the specific property's access arrangements in advance rather than relying on a standard satellite-navigation route.
