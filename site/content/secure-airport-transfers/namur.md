Namur's transfer logistics start somewhere else entirely, since the city has no airport of its own. Brussels Airport and Brussels South Charleroi Airport (BSCA) are the two entry points, with a direct IC rail connection to Brussels Central running roughly an hour over the 55km route, and drivers meet the principal at whichever airport the flight lands at before continuing.

What makes Namur's transfer planning distinctive is its institutional calendar. The city has been Wallonia's official capital since 1986, hosting the Parliament of Wallonia and the Government of Wallonia at the Elysette, and sitting weeks can bring temporary access restrictions and extra security presence around the government quarter that drivers need to confirm in advance rather than assume away. The rest of the time, Namur is a genuinely calm destination: a 2023 local perception survey found residents worried more about road-user behaviour and public nuisance than serious crime, a notably different picture from Charleroi or Liege.

Drivers hold valid Belgian licences, and where the transfer includes a protective dimension, the firm carries an FPS Interior agrement under the Act of 2 October 2017. For visits that include the Citadel of Namur, an 80-hectare fortress complex with an unusual multi-level layout, drivers confirm site access points ahead of time rather than treating it like a standard venue.
