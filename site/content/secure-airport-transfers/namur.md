Namur transfers start somewhere else entirely, since the city has no airport of its own. Brussels Airport or Brussels South Charleroi Airport is the arrival point, and drivers continue by direct IC rail from Brussels Central, roughly 63 to 67 minutes over about 55km, or manage the whole leg by road as a single briefed movement.

Two destinations shape most drop-off planning once the principal reaches Namur. The government quarter around the Parliament of Wallonia and the Elysette needs advance liaison, particularly during sitting weeks when temporary access restrictions and a heavier security presence can apply. The Citadel of Namur, an 80-hectare fortress with around 7km of underground galleries, is more straightforward for a small party but benefits from planning ahead for anything larger.

Drivers hold Belgian professional licences and, for assignments with a security dimension, FPS Interior badging under the Act of 2 October 2017. Belgium's national terrorism threat level has held at 3 of 4 through early 2026 under OCAM/CUTA, the same national backdrop that applies across every Belgian city on the network.
