Ghent has no major airport of its own, so most visiting principals arrive via Brussels Airport, around 55 kilometres southeast with a 45 to 60 minute transfer via the E40, or via Eurostar into Brussels-Midi station for UK travellers. Brussels South Charleroi and Ostend-Bruges serve as secondary options depending on flight availability and route. Pickpocketing is documented around the Graslei and Korenlei waterfront in central Ghent, a risk that rises sharply during Gentse Feesten each July, when the City of Ghent has recorded visitor numbers of up to 1 million across the festival period.

The E40 run from Brussels Airport into Ghent is generally straightforward outside peak commuter hours, but Gentse Feesten brings substantial road closures around the historic centre, and drivers confirm an alternative drop-off point with hotels well in advance during the festival.
