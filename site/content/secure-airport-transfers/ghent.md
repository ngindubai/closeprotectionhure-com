Ghent doesn't have its own airport, so every secure transfer into the city starts somewhere else, usually Brussels Airport, 55km east, occasionally Antwerp or a rail connection via Eurostar. That single fact shapes the whole service: the transfer isn't a five-minute taxi ride from a local terminal, it's a planned 45-to-60-minute road journey (or a 30-minute rail leg from Brussels Midi) that needs the same attention as any longer executive transfer. Getting the pickup point, the route, and the arrival protocol right matters more here than it would in a city with its own airport on the doorstep. For the broader Ghent security picture behind these transfer plans, see our [Ghent city page](/cities/ghent/).

Brussels Airport's arrivals hall is large, and large arrivals halls are where unlicensed touts tend to work. Our name-board protocol handles that directly: the driver waits inside the terminal, doesn't approach first, and lets the client verify identification before any luggage changes hands. The E40 corridor back to Ghent carries a low security risk under FCDO Belgium guidance, but friction is still possible: Brussels ring road congestion at peak hours is real, and during Gentse Feesten each July, historic centre access restrictions can force a different drop-off entirely. We check the route on the day rather than assuming yesterday's conditions still apply. Clients arriving via Eurostar and Brussels-Midi get the same treatment at Ghent-Sint-Pieters station.
