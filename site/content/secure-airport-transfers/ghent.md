Ghent does not have its own commercial airport, which means the first decision in any secure transfer is not about Ghent at all, it is about which arrival point the principal is actually using. Brussels Airport (BRU) sits roughly 55 kilometres away and handles the great majority of international arrivals; Ostend-Bruges Airport, about 80 kilometres out, covers seasonal and charter routes; and Eurostar passengers arriving via Brussels-Midi bypass an airport altogether. [Ghent](/cities/ghent/) itself carries a low risk rating, but that rating says nothing about the arrival environment at a major international airport or a busy Brussels rail terminal, and our transfer planning treats the two as distinct problems rather than assuming one covers the other.

Whichever point the itinerary uses, the same discipline applies: a driver licensed under the Loi du 2 octobre 2017 with a confirmed SPF Interieur badge, a vehicle checked before dispatch, and positioning at the arrivals hall or platform close enough that the principal's time in an open public space is kept short. The E40 road corridor from BRU into Ghent is straightforward by regional standards, though a route assessment still runs for every transfer, covering current road works and, where the destination sits in the historic centre, the access effects of the Gentse Feesten in July. Transfers ending at the ArcelorMittal or Volvo Cars Gent canal zone sites carry an extra step, since both facilities control their own site access independently of the general city route.
