Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport is one of China's three primary international hubs and the principal gateway for the Pearl River Delta's manufacturing, trading, and financial business community. The 28-kilometre CAN-to-Tianhe corridor is a well-managed urban motorway route, but Guangzhou's position as a global trade hub -- particularly during the semi-annual Canton Fair -- creates specific transfer-period risks that a standard taxi or ride-share service does not address.

## The Guangzhou transfer context

Business intelligence exposure and PSB legal compliance are the primary considerations for executive airport transfers in Guangzhou, as in all Chinese cities. The Ministry of Public Security Order No. 564 (2010) governs all commercial security operations. Arrangements that do not comply with this framework create legal exposure for the principal and the supporting operator.

## What secure transfers cover

A Guangzhou secure airport transfer covers: inside-hall collection at CAN by a PSB-compliant vetted driver; operations controller tracking from CAN to accommodation; real-time route management on the airport expressway corridor; Canton Fair period scheduling adjustments; and return transfers on the same terms.
