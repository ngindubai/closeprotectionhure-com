NürnbergMesse dominates transfer planning here more than the airport route itself does. Spielwarenmesse alone brings around 2,800 exhibitors from roughly 60 countries and 67,000 trade visitors a year, and drivers collecting principals at NUE, roughly 5km north of the centre, build extra time into any run toward the venue during fair weeks, when both the airport and the surrounding roads run heavier than usual.

The Palace of Justice complex is a different kind of destination entirely. It's still a working Bavarian courthouse, home to the Memorium Nuremberg Trials in the former Courtroom 600, and drivers confirm access arrangements ahead of any visit rather than treating it like a typical venue drop-off. Old town transfers shift in character too during the Christkindlesmarkt, from late November to late December, when pedestrian density climbs sharply and drop-off points need adjusting accordingly.

Drivers hold German professional licences and, where the job carries a protective element, Sachkundeprufung certification under the Bewachungsverordnung. FCDO guidance for Germany sets a national terrorism assessment with no Nuremberg-specific element, so the standard name-board collection and identity check at NUE remains the main practical safeguard.
