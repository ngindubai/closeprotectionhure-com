Nuremberg Airport sits close enough to the centre, about 5km, that the transfer itself is rarely the challenge. The real planning variable is timing: NürnbergMesse's Spielwarenmesse show alone draws roughly 67,000 trade visitors a year, and fair weeks bring the kind of hotel and transfer scarcity that turns an ordinary airport run into a genuine logistics problem if it isn't booked well in advance.

Drivers track the inbound flight and are already positioned at the terminal before the principal clears customs, meeting them with a clearly printed name board rather than approaching in the open arrivals hall, a sensible precaution given general travel guidance's consistent flagging of the Hauptbahnhof and Hauptmarkt-Konigstrasse corridor for opportunistic theft. Vehicles are maintained executive saloons or MPVs, and where the transfer includes a protective dimension, the driver holds current IHK Sachkundeprufung certification under the Bewachungsverordnung.

For executives whose itinerary extends to Herzogenaurach, home to Adidas and Puma headquarters, the same driver covers that short corridor as a routine extension rather than a separate engagement. Institutional visits to the Palace of Justice or the Documentation Centre Nazi Party Rally Grounds need transfers coordinated with the host's own access arrangements rather than assumed general entry.
