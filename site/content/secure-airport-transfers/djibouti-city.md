Djibouti-Ambouli International Airport (JIB) is 5km from the Plateau du Serpent district and the European Quarter of Djibouti City. The FCDO Djibouti travel advice (2026) assesses the primary risks as petty crime and pickpocketing at the kerb and in less managed areas rather than violent crime. Pre-arranged nationally licensed collection inside the arrivals hall addresses these exposures and provides managed transport from landing to hotel or office in Djibouti City's principal business districts.

## The JIB arrival environment

Djibouti-Ambouli International serves international flights from Addis Ababa, Dubai, Istanbul, Paris, Nairobi, and regional Horn of Africa routes. The airport's traffic mix includes military and diplomatic personnel connected to the US Camp Lemonnier, French Forces Djibouti, and Chinese PLA Navy Support Base, as well as port logistics professionals and NGO staff using Djibouti as a staging point for Horn of Africa and Yemen operations. Houthi attacks on Red Sea shipping since late 2023 have increased Djibouti's strategic profile and professional traffic through JIB.
