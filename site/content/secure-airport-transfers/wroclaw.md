Most Wroclaw transfers head straight for the business-park corridor rather than the old town. The city hosted 234 modern business-service centres as of a May 2025 KPMG Poland report, with named operations from Google, HP, Nokia, IBM, UBS and Volvo IT, and drivers familiar with the Bielany Wroclawskie corridor can move a principal between several employer sites in a single booking rather than treating each stop as a separate transfer.

Wroclaw Copernicus Airport sits about 10km southwest of the centre, a straightforward 20 to 30 minute drive, and drivers are positioned in the terminal ahead of the principal clearing customs. For visitors whose trip does include the Rynek, general travel guidance consistently flags the market square and its tram network for pickpocketing and a documented fake-petition distraction scam, so the collection protocol relies on a clearly printed name board rather than an open approach, and drivers confirm the nearest accessible drop-off point given the area's pedestrianised sections.

Drivers hold valid Polish licences, and where the transfer includes a protective dimension, the operator holds a Ministry of Internal Affairs and Administration concession under the Act of 22 August 1997.
