Most transfers out of WRO head somewhere with a formal reception desk. Wroclaw counted 234 modern business-service centres as of May 2025, per KPMG Poland, with Google, HP, Nokia, IBM, UBS and Volvo IT among the named operations, and drivers collecting principals at the airport, roughly 10km southwest of the centre, run straight to whichever campus the day's meeting is at.

That corporate footprint shifts, though, and drivers account for it. UBS's Wroclaw headcount reportedly fell from around 2,200 to between 1,400 and 1,600 by the third quarter of 2024 amid sector-wide restructuring, so confirming the current office location before a transfer is standard practice rather than an extra step.

For visitors with time in the old town, the Rynek market square carries the heaviest tourist footfall in the city, and travel guidance consistently flags pickpocketing and a documented fake-petition scam there, so drivers stage drop-offs at the square's edge rather than driving into the crowd. Drivers hold Polish professional licences and, for assignments with a security dimension, a Ministry concession under the Act of 22 August 1997.
