Getting the airport leg right in Eindhoven starts with a question most cities don't ask: which airport are you actually landing at? Eindhoven Airport itself is a short hop from the city but handles mostly European routes, so a large proportion of the executives and specialists we transfer are coming through Amsterdam Schiphol instead, roughly 110km away with a 75-minute direct train link, or a road transfer if that suits the itinerary better. Planning around the right gateway from the outset avoids the scramble of rearranging a pickup after the flight details are already confirmed.

Whichever airport is involved, the same protocol applies at arrival: a Politie-cleared driver positioned inside the terminal with a name board, no unsolicited approach, and immediate luggage handling once contact is confirmed. FCDO guidance on the Netherlands is specific about petty crime at transport hubs, and Schiphol's scale in particular means the meeting point has to be agreed well before landing rather than sorted out on the day. The route itself, whether the short EIN run or the longer A2 corridor from Schiphol, carries low baseline risk, though the latter is long enough that protest-related disruption, which FCDO guidance flags as a live consideration for Dutch transport routes, is worth a contingency plan rather than an afterthought.

A significant share of our Eindhoven transfers go straight to the ASML campus in Veldhoven, NXP Semiconductors, or the Philips Innovation Campus, and for those journeys the destination gets the same advance attention as the pickup.
