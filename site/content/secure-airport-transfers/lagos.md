The Murtala Muhammed International Airport arrival is the single highest-risk moment of any business trip to Lagos. The FCDO and US State Department both carry Nigeria travel advisories that specifically reference the airport environment and the subsequent vehicle movement to accommodation as priority security concerns for arriving foreign visitors. No other moment in a Lagos trip concentrates as many risk factors simultaneously: visible luggage, unfamiliar surroundings, transit from secured terminal to open road, and the high-incident LOS-to-Victoria-Island corridor.

## The LOS arrival risk environment

Security sector incident reporting and NSCDC data consistently identify the Murtala Muhammed arrivals zone and the subsequent road journey as elevated-risk periods. The kerb outside the terminal is a documented location for approaches to foreign visitors; unbooked taxis from this zone are linked to express kidnapping incidents in sector reporting. The Apapa-Oshodi route to Victoria Island is among the most incident-affected airport approach corridors in West Africa.

## What the secure transfer covers in Lagos

Pre-arranged inside-terminal collection; NSCDC-licensed driver with verified vehicle registration; counter-kidnap protocols throughout the transfer; operations controller tracking and emergency contact chain; armoured vehicle option for elevated-risk principals; and the return departure transfer on the same terms. These elements together address the specific risk environment of the LOS transfer, not a generic security checklist.
