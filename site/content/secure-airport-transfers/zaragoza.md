Zaragoza sits on the Madrid-Barcelona high-speed rail corridor, and a significant proportion of corporate visitors arrive via Zaragoza-Delicias station rather than through Zaragoza Airport itself, which handles a comparatively modest volume of scheduled flights. FCDO Spain travel advice (2024) documents pickpocketing at major Spanish rail stations and in dense historic centre districts as a recurring pattern, and this is noted locally around Delicias station and the Casco Historico, including the narrow lanes of El Tubo. A pre-arranged transfer with name-board collection applies equally at the airport terminal or the station concourse, meeting the principal before they reach the wider public area.

The route into central Zaragoza is short and generally free of congestion outside festival periods, but Fiestas del Pilar in October brings substantial road closures and increased pedestrian density around the historic centre, and drivers plan routing and timing around this well in advance.
