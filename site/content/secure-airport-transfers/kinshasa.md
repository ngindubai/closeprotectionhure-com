N'djili International Airport is approximately 25 kilometres from Gombe, Kinshasa's principal business district. The airport approach road is a documented high-risk corridor for arriving passengers. The standard for professional arrivals into Kinshasa is inside-terminal collection by a Ministry of Interior-licensed driver before the principal exits into the arrivals hall, with immediate departure from the airport zone.

## The N'djili arrival risk

The combination of the N'djili approach road crime profile, visible luggage, and the extended wait that informal transport arrangements require creates the arrival window vulnerability. Ministry of Interior-licensed operators with inside-terminal access eliminate the kerb exposure. Night arrivals at N'djili require a heightened transfer protocol with earlier accommodation notification and modified routing.

## What secure transfers cover in Kinshasa

A Kinshasa secure airport transfer covers: inside-terminal collection at FIH; operations controller tracking throughout; vehicle management for the N'djili corridor; transfer to Gombe, Ngaliema, or Lingwala accommodation; night-arrival heightened protocol; and the return departure transfer on the same basis.
