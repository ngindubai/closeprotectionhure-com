Cruise-ship schedules shape a lot of transfer planning at GDN, roughly 12km northwest of the centre. When arrivals coincide with a cruise call, the old town around Dlugi Targ and the Motlawa riverfront fills up fast, and drivers time the run accordingly rather than treating every arrival the same way.

Two destinations stand out beyond the standard hotel drop-off. The European Solidarity Centre, next to the historic shipyard gates, sees a steady flow of institutional and commemorative visits, particularly around August anniversary dates, when drivers confirm access and security arrangements in advance given the heavier presence those dates bring. Amberif and Ambermart, the city's major amber trade fairs, bring a different kind of traffic entirely, standard buyer-and-exhibitor transfer work rather than anything historical.

In the old town itself, general travel guidance flags a reported bar-touting scam network and cruise-season pickpocketing risk, and drivers stage collection points with that in mind. Nowy Port, given its proximity to working port and shipyard operations, is routed around after dark as a standard precaution rather than a response to a specific incident. Drivers hold Polish professional licences and, for assignments with a security dimension, a Ministry concession under the Act of 22 August 1997.
