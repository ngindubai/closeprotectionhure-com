Jose Maria Cordova International Airport is located in Rionegro, approximately 45 kilometres from central Medellin and El Poblado. The Las Palmas or Llano Grande road corridor takes 45 to 75 minutes. This extended mountain road is the defining operational feature of Medellin airport transfer management: pre-booked Supervigilancia-licensed drivers with inside-terminal collection and operations controller tracking provide the professional standard for the corridor.

## The Rionegro corridor

The winding mountain road between Rionegro and Medellin is significantly different from the urban approach roads of Lima or Dakar. Its length and the mountain terrain make breakdown management and contingency routing operationally relevant. Supervigilancia-licensed drivers from the Medellin corporate security market know the road, its traffic patterns, and the appropriate vehicle standard for the altitude and gradient.

Olaya Herrera Airport (EOH) in western Medellin handles domestic traffic and some charter flights. Its city-centre location makes the transfer shorter but traverses different zone considerations; inside-terminal collection and vetted driver use are the same standard.

## What secure transfers cover in Medellin

A Medellin secure airport transfer covers: inside-terminal collection at MDE (Cordova) or EOH (Olaya Herrera); operations controller tracking throughout; corridor management for the Rionegro road; transfer to El Poblado or Laureles accommodation; and the return departure transfer with adequate time for the 45-kilometre journey.
