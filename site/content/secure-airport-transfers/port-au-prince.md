The Toussaint Louverture International Airport transfer in Port-au-Prince is one of the highest-risk airport transfers in the world in the current security environment. Gang activity in the lower city and on the airport approach corridors has made the PAP transfer a planning centrepiece for any Port-au-Prince engagement. Inside-terminal collection with armed escort, real-time gang-route intelligence, and flexible route selection are baseline requirements -- not optional security enhancements.

## The PAP arrival environment

Toussaint Louverture International Airport is located in the lower city, in the zone most directly affected by gang activity. The route to Petionville -- where the Hotel Montana, Villa Creole, and international organisation offices are located -- passes through areas where gang control has been documented and where kidnapping targeting arriving international visitors has occurred. This makes the PAP transfer fundamentally different from most airport security planning.

## What a Port-au-Prince secure transfer covers

Inside-terminal collection by a vetted driver with armed escort; real-time gang-route intelligence from the operations controller; flexible routing against current conditions; and the return departure transfer on the same intelligence-managed basis.
