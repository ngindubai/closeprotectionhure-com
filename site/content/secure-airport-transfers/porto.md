Francisco Sa Carneiro Airport (OPO) serves Porto, one of Europe's fastest-growing business and tourism destinations, with the city's Boavista financial district, historic Ribeira waterfront, and Foz Atlantic seafront all hosting significant corporate travel. PSP Crime Statistics 2024 record a generally low crime environment in Porto, with the notable exception of tourist pickpocket activity in Ribeira, Aliados, and the transport connections used by unescorted arrivals from the airport. Pre-arranged inside-terminal collection, a vetted driver licenced under Portuguese security law and familiar with Porto's topography, and operations controller oversight from touchdown to accommodation address the specific characteristics of this city.

## The Porto airport transfer environment

The VCI (Via de Cintura Interna) motorway is the standard corridor between OPO and central Porto, with the final approach to destination properties presenting the most operationally complex element of any Porto transfer. Porto's granite hillside topography produces steep, narrow cobbled streets and tram crossings in its historic centre that require genuine local knowledge to navigate correctly. GPS routing without local knowledge frequently directs vehicles into pedestrian zones or restricted streets, creating delays and navigational exposure. All Porto transfer drivers are assessed for city-specific topographic familiarity before engagement.

## Licensing and verification in Porto

All drivers providing secure transfers in Porto hold a current licence under Lei n. 34/2013, the Portuguese private security framework, operating under PSP supervision. Licence status is verified before any driver is engaged. Porto's steep street network and tram corridor crossings are assessed as part of each driver's operational evaluation.
