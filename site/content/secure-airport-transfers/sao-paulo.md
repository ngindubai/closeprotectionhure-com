Guarulhos International Airport is the gateway through which most international business visitors arrive in Sao Paulo, and the transfer from Guarulhos to the city's financial districts is one of the most clearly documented risk windows in any Brazilian business trip. OSAC Sao Paulo crime reporting and security sector incident data consistently identify the GRU arrival and the Ayrton Senna Highway approach as elevated-risk periods for foreign executives. The express kidnapping threat -- which in Sao Paulo can involve vehicle robbery during the transfer itself -- makes the quality of the pre-arranged transfer the first material security decision of any Sao Paulo visit.

## The Guarulhos transfer environment

GRU's three passenger terminals handle Sao Paulo's international traffic. The arrivals areas at T2 and T3 are managed environments, but the kerb zone outside and the first kilometres of the Ayrton Senna Highway are where most documented incidents affecting arriving foreign visitors occur. Inside-terminal collection by a Policia Federal-licensed driver, with vehicle confirmed before boarding and operations controller tracking throughout, is the correct response to this specific environment.

## Congonhas and the domestic connection

Congonhas Airport (CGH) in south-central Sao Paulo is the domestic hub for routes connecting Sao Paulo with Rio de Janeiro, Brasilia, Belo Horizonte, and other major Brazilian cities. For principals arriving on domestic connections before an international departure or after an internal Brazilian leg, Congonhas pre-arranged collection follows the same driver and operations controller standard as Guarulhos.
