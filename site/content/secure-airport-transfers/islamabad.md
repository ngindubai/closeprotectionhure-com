Islamabad International Airport (ISB) is a modern facility opened in 2018, approximately 30 kilometres from the capital's Blue Area and Diplomatic Enclave, creating a substantial transfer corridor that passes through areas rated high risk by the FCDO Pakistan advisory (June 2026). The long approach road and the city transit route both represent exposure points in an environment where Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan has conducted attacks against transportation infrastructure and hotels.

## The Islamabad arrival window

The arrivals hall at ISB is well-organised by regional standards, but the kerb zone outside the terminal is an uncontrolled environment where unlicensed taxi operators and informal touts approach newly arrived passengers. For principals arriving from international destinations, this moment of post-flight disorientation represents an elevated risk period, particularly given Pakistan's documented kidnapping and targeted robbery threat against business travellers and foreign nationals.

The 30-kilometre corridor from ISB to the Blue Area, F-6, or the Diplomatic Enclave passes through areas where vehicle interdiction has been recorded. The FCDO Pakistan advisory recommends against travel in private vehicles without appropriate security arrangements outside the main secured zones. Checkpoint activity on the Islamabad Highway can create static vehicle exposure, which requires both route pre-planning and driver training in checkpoint compliance protocols.

Night arrivals compound these risks. Reduced visibility on the airport approach road, limited checkpoint staffing in some corridors, and the targeting of late-arriving international flights by criminal networks all increase the transfer risk profile after 2200 local time.
