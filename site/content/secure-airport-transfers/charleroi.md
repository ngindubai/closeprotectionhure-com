Brussels South Charleroi Airport is the entry point for most transfers here, and its scale sets the tone: a record 10.5 million passengers in 2024 made it Belgium's second-busiest airport and Ryanair's continental base, sitting just 7km north of the city centre. Drivers build extra time into transfers during peak Ryanair scheduling windows, when terminal and access-road congestion runs highest.

Gosselies is the other main destination worth planning for. Sonaca's aerospace-structures work and the newer A6K digital-innovation hub, funded through the European Recovery and Resilience Plan and set to scale toward 10,000 learners a year from autumn 2026, both run formal access protocols that drivers confirm before any drop-off. In the city centre itself, postal code 6000 accounts for a significant share of vehicle-related theft across the wider police zone, per Federal Police-sourced reporting, so drivers keep standard vigilance around vehicle security as a routine rather than exceptional precaution.

Drivers hold Belgian professional licences and, for assignments that include a security dimension, FPS Interior badging under the Act of 2 October 2017.
