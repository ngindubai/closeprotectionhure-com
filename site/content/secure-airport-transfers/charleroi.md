For most visitors, Brussels South Charleroi Airport is not a stopover on the way to Charleroi, it is the reason for the trip. BSCA, Ryanair's continental base and Belgium's second-busiest airport at a record 10.5 million passengers in 2024, sits roughly 7km from the city centre and closer still to Gosselies, home to Sonaca's aerospace operations and the A6K digital-innovation hub. That makes the airport-to-business-district run one of the shorter, more predictable legs in the whole network.

Drivers track the inbound flight and are positioned in the terminal area before the principal clears customs, accounting for the routine congestion BSCA's passenger volume brings at peak times. Federal Police-sourced reporting found local vehicle theft and burglary trends broadly stable rather than worsening in recent years, so standard property vigilance covers the realistic risk, backed by a name-board collection protocol rather than an open terminal approach. Drivers hold valid Belgian licences, and where the transfer includes a protective dimension, the firm carries an FPS Interior agrement under the Act of 2 October 2017.

For principals whose visit spans both cities, the same driver covers the roughly 46km onward run to Brussels, timed around peak-hour congestion on the E42/A54 corridor.
