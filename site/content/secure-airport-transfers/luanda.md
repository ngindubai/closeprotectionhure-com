The Luanda airport transfer is one of the highest-risk transit moments in any Angola visit. 4 de Fevereiro International Airport has documented criminal approaches targeting arriving expatriates, and the road from the airport to Talatona, Miramar, or Alvalade accommodation crosses zones with documented vehicle crime. The pre-arranged, inside-terminal collection model eliminates the kerb exposure and the unescorted wait that creates the vulnerability window.

## The 4 de Fevereiro arrival risk

FCDO's Angola travel advisory identifies the airport approach corridor as a specific risk zone for arriving passengers. The combination of visible luggage, unfamiliarity with the environment, and the extended kerb-wait common when using unofficial drivers at the arrival zone creates the conditions for approaches. Ministry of Interior-licensed operators with inside-terminal collection access eliminate this through structured arrival management.

## What secure transfers cover in Luanda

A Luanda secure airport transfer covers: inside-terminal collection by a Ministry of Interior-licensed driver; operations controller tracking throughout; carjacking counter-protocol routing to Talatona, Miramar, or Alvalade; return departure transfer on the same terms; and night-arrival heightened protocol for late-flight arrivals.
