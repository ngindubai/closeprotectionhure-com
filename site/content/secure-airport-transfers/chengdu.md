Chengdu is western China's primary commercial hub and the gateway to Sichuan Province's aerospace, manufacturing, and technology sectors. The city operates two international airports, Shuangliu International (CTU) and Tianfu International (TFU), which creates a specific transfer-planning complexity absent in single-airport cities. The security environment for business visitors is assessed as low to moderate by Chinese urban standards, but the legal framework governing security operations in China applies fully in Chengdu and must be reflected in transfer arrangements.

## The Chengdu dual-airport transfer environment

The primary operational challenge in Chengdu transfers is route clarity: CTU and TFU are 50 kilometres apart, and a missed confirmation of the arrival airport creates a direct logistics failure. Beyond the airport identification requirement, the Ministry of Public Security Order No. 564 (2010) framework shapes what protective transport arrangements are legally permissible.

## What secure transfers cover

A Chengdu secure airport transfer covers: pre-confirmed airport identification (CTU or TFU); inside-hall collection by a PSB-compliant vetted driver; operations controller tracking from collection to accommodation; real-time route management on either expressway corridor; and return transfers on the same terms.
