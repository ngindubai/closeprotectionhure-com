Barranquilla is Colombia's primary Caribbean port city and the capital of Atletico department, with a substantial industrial, logistics, and manufacturing base that generates significant business travel. Ernesto Cortissoz International Airport serves the city with connections to Colombian hubs and some international routes. The Circunvalar motorway linking BAQ to the city centre is a documented carjacking risk corridor, and the airport transfer window represents a specific and concentrated risk for business arrivals.

## The BAQ transfer risk environment

The primary transfer risk at BAQ is on the Circunvalar motorway and in the uncontrolled contact zone at the airport kerb. SVSP registration under Decreto 356/1994 is the baseline check for any private security transport operator in Colombia.

## What secure transfers cover

A Barranquilla secure airport transfer covers: inside-hall collection at BAQ by a SVSP-registered driver; operations controller tracking from BAQ to accommodation; Circunvalar carjacking counter-protocols; Carnival season route adjustments; and return transfers from accommodation to BAQ on the same terms.
