DPD Crime Report 2024 records elevated property crime citywide in Denver, and DEN's Pena Boulevard approach creates one of the longest vehicle exposure corridors of any major US airport. The additional operational dimension unique to Denver is altitude: at 1,609 metres above sea level, arriving principals from sea-level cities may experience altitude-related symptoms in their first 24 hours, and mountain transfers to Aspen or Vail extend this consideration significantly.

## The Denver airport transfer environment

The I-70 corridor between DEN and Downtown Denver is the primary transfer route, with Pena Boulevard serving as the extended airport-side approach. DPD Crime Report 2024 informs route planning, with property crime patterns incorporated into the route selection and approach roads to destination properties. Friday afternoon I-70 westbound traffic is a material planning factor: ski resort traffic creates congestion extending well beyond peak hour during winter months, and I-70 through Glenwood Canyon can be closed by rockfall or avalanche events, requiring a contingency routing through US-24 and CO-82 for Aspen-bound principals.

## Licensing and verification in Denver

All drivers providing secure transfers in Denver hold a current licence under Colorado DORA CRS 12-58.5-101, administered by the Colorado Department of Regulatory Agencies. For mountain transfers, driver experience on I-70 mountain grades and winter driving conditions is assessed as part of the operational evaluation. Licence status is verified before any driver is engaged.
