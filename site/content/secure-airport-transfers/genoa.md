Cristoforo Colombo Airport sits on a jetty built out into the Ligurian Sea, six kilometres west of central Genoa, a runway configuration that shapes almost every transfer route into the city. The standard approach follows the A26 motorway spur, with a journey time of 15 to 20 minutes under normal conditions, though the single terminal building and its compact arrivals hall become considerably busier during the Salone Nautico Internazionale boat show at Fiera di Genova and during peak cruise-season weeks at the Stazione Marittima. FCDO Italy (2024) consistently identifies petty crime and unlicensed operator approaches at Italian transport hubs as the primary arrival-phase risk, and Genoa's airport is no exception during its busiest periods. A pre-arranged transfer with name-board collection inside the terminal resolves this exposure without requiring the principal to navigate the public forecourt, where most unsolicited approaches take place.

The route itself carries a low risk profile consistent with FCDO Italy (2024) assessments for northern Italy, but Genoa's geography adds practical complexity that a flatter Italian city does not: the historic centre's ZTL restrictions, the narrow Caruggi lanes that rule out larger vehicles near certain hotel entrances, and the seasonal congestion around Porto Antico when Fiera di Genova hosts a major event. Drivers hold the NCC licence issued by the Comune di Genova alongside the security authorisation required under Legislative Decree 153/2009 and the TULPS, and vehicles are matched to the drop-off point rather than assigned uniformly. Genoa's maritime and shipping sector generates a steady flow of executive arrivals connected to Fincantieri, MSC, and Grimaldi Lines, and drivers handling these transfers are briefed on communication discretion given the commercially sensitive nature of many of these visits.
