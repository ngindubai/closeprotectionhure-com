Genoa is built along a narrow coastal strip beneath steep hillside terrain, with a historic old port, a working cruise terminal at Stazione Marittima, and a dense old town of narrow Caruggi lanes that were never designed for vehicle traffic. Cristoforo Colombo Airport (GOA) sits on reclaimed coastal land west of the centre and serves both leisure arrivals and corporate visitors to the city's maritime and logistics sector. FCDO Italy travel advice (2024) documents pickpocketing and distraction theft at Italian transport hubs generally, and specifically within Genoa's old town and around Stazione Marittima when cruise ships are in port. A pre-arranged transfer with name-board collection inside the single terminal removes the principal from the public forecourt on arrival.

The 6-kilometre coastal run from GOA into the centre is direct in normal conditions, though the final approach to some old town hotels requires a short walking handover from the nearest point a vehicle can legally reach.
