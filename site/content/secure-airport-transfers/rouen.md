There is no such thing as a direct flight into Rouen, so every transfer genuinely starts in Paris. Direct SNCF rail from Paris Saint-Lazare, at around 1 hour 15 minutes, is usually the better choice over the 1.5-to-2-hour road alternative, and drivers meet the principal at Charles de Gaulle or Orly before either leg begins.

French Ministry of Interior data reported in April 2024 placed Rouen fourth of 368 cities over 22,500 residents for recorded crime per capita, ahead of Lyon and Paris, though the Ministry's own methodology describes the figure as informational rather than comprehensive. Standard transit-area vigilance around Gare de Rouen-Rive-Droite is the practical response, and the collection protocol keeps the principal away from open platform areas via a clearly printed name board. Drivers hold valid French licences, and where the transfer includes a protective dimension, the operator carries CNAPS authorisation under Loi n. 83-629, mandatory for any private security activity in France.

Visits touching the Port of Rouen or the Seine valley petrochemical corridor need advance coordination: both run independent site accreditation, and the 2019 Lubrizol Seveso-site fire remains the standing reference point for industrial risk in the area. Vieux Rouen's pedestrianised medieval core means the vehicle drops the principal at the nearest accessible point rather than driving in directly.
