There is no shortcut into Rouen. With no major airport of its own, every secure transfer starts at Paris Charles de Gaulle or the Gare Saint-Lazare, and drivers plan the whole journey as one briefed movement rather than splitting it into separate legs: roughly one and a half to two hours by road from CDG, or about one hour fifteen minutes by direct SNCF train from Saint-Lazare.

Rouen's own risk profile shapes the rest of the plan. French Ministry of Interior data reported in April 2024 ranked the city 4th of 368 French cities over 22,500 residents for recorded crime per capita, ahead of Lyon and Paris in the same dataset, and drivers respond with confirmed collection points and disciplined route planning rather than treating the city as routine. Where a transfer extends to the Port of Rouen or the Seine valley's petrochemical corridor, home to Europe's leading grain-export port and still shaped by the memory of the 2019 Lubrizol fire, gate access needs confirming well before the vehicle arrives.

Drivers hold CNAPS authorisation under Loi n. 83-629, the same national framework covering all private security work in France, and check the current demonstration calendar given Rouen's documented recent history with the gilets jaunes movement and the 2023 pension-reform protests.
