Almost no one flies directly into Cordoba. The transfer that matters starts at Madrid-Barajas or Seville airport, since Cordoba's own airport carries negligible scheduled traffic, and continues by AVE high-speed rail into the city, a comfortable run of around one hour forty minutes from Madrid or 45 minutes to an hour from Seville. Drivers track the inbound international flight and meet the principal at that arrival airport rather than waiting at the Cordoba end.

What sets a Cordoba transfer apart from most European equivalents is heat. AEMET, Spain's state meteorological agency, recorded the country's all-time temperature record here, 47.4C in August 2021, and ordinary summer days above 37C are unremarkable. Verified air conditioning and minimal time spent waiting outdoors between the AVE platform and the car are built into the plan as standard, not offered as an upgrade. Drivers hold valid Spanish professional licences, and where the transfer includes a protective dimension, the operator carries the Escolta Privado qualification under Ley 5/2014.

For visitors continuing into the Mezquita-Catedral area or the Juderia, both pedestrianised, the vehicle drops the principal at the nearest accessible point rather than attempting direct access, and journey times through the old town stretch considerably during the Patios Festival and Feria de Cordoba each May.
