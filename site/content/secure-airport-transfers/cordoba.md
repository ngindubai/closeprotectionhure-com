There is no direct flight into Cordoba worth planning around. Cordoba Airport (ODB) sees next to no scheduled traffic, so a secure transfer here genuinely starts at Madrid-Barajas or Seville, continuing by AVE high-speed rail or road, with the Seville leg the shorter of the two at around one and a half to two hours.

Once the principal is in the city, the geometry changes. The Mezquita-Catedral and the Juderia's lanes are narrow and largely pedestrianised, so drivers stage drop-off points at the edge of the historic core rather than attempting to push a vehicle into streets that were never built for one. Add AEMET's national temperature record, 47.4C set at La Rambla in August 2021, and a working air conditioning check before every job stops being optional. May brings its own complication: the Patios Festival and the Feria de Cordoba both push old-town crowds and road congestion up sharply, and journey times get padded across the whole month rather than just on event dates.

Drivers hold accreditation under Ley 5/2014 de Seguridad Privada, adding the Escolta Privado qualification for assignments that carry a protective element beyond straightforward chauffeuring.
