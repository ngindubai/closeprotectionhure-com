Cordoba is a rail-first city for secure transfer purposes. With Cordoba Airport (ODB) carrying negligible scheduled traffic, the practical route for almost any arriving principal is a flight into Madrid or Seville followed by AVE high-speed rail into Santa Justa station, and the collection protocol is built around that platform arrival rather than a conventional terminal kerb.

The two operational variables that actually shape a Cordoba transfer are heat and old-town access. Vehicle air conditioning is verified working before every journey given Andalusia's genuinely extreme summer temperatures, and hotel drop-off points inside the Juderia's pedestrianised lanes are confirmed in advance rather than left to be resolved at the kerb.
