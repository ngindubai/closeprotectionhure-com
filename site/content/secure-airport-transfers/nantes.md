Nantes Atlantique Airport carries the ICAO code LFRS and sits around 10 kilometres southwest of the city, on the far side of the ring road from the Ile de Nantes business district, which keeps the standard transfer to a straightforward 20 to 25 minutes on the peripherique and D85. The airport's single terminal handles domestic, Schengen, and a limited set of longer-haul connections, and volume rises noticeably during trade-fair weeks at Parc des Expositions de la Beaujoire, when drivers build in extra time and monitor the arrivals board for delayed inbound flights rather than working to a fixed schedule. Principals travelling for aerospace or agri-food industry meetings make up a steady share of NTE arrivals, and drivers are briefed on the discreet, no-conversation approach that this kind of commercially sensitive travel generally calls for.

FCDO France (2024) flags petty crime and unsolicited approaches from unlicensed operators as a recurring issue at French transport hubs, and NTE's compact arrivals hall is no exception during busy periods. A pre-arranged transfer with name-board collection inside the terminal removes this exposure: the principal is met at a fixed point, luggage handling begins immediately, and there is no need to engage with anyone in the public forecourt. Drivers hold a CNAPS carte professionnelle for the security element of the role and a VTC licence from the DREAL Pays de la Loire or the Prefecture de Loire-Atlantique for the hire vehicle itself, and both credentials can be confirmed before travel. The vehicle itself is a dark-liveried executive saloon or SUV, kept to the same roadworthiness and insurance standard applied across the wider CNAPS-regulated fleet used for corporate transfers in the region.

A meaningful share of NTE arrivals are bound onward to Saint-Nazaire for the aerospace and naval sectors, and this leg is planned as part of the original booking rather than added later, since Chantiers de l'Atlantique requires vehicle and driver pre-registration with its own security office at least 48 hours ahead of any site visit.
