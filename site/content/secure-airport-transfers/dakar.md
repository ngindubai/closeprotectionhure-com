Blaise Diagne International Airport is 65 kilometres east of Dakar's Plateau district, making the airport transfer the most logistically significant security segment of any Dakar assignment. The 45 to 90-minute road section to the city, with its specific night protocols for evening arrivals, requires professional management that informal transport cannot provide.

## The Blaise Diagne corridor

The extended road section from Blaise Diagne to Dakar's principal business and residential zones (Plateau, Almadies, Les Mamelles embassy district) is an operationally significant segment by any international comparison. Evening arrivals involve a long night road section. Pre-booked Ministry-licensed drivers with inside-terminal collection access and an operations controller tracking the transfer throughout provide the appropriate management standard.

## What secure transfers cover in Dakar

A Dakar secure airport transfer covers: inside-terminal collection at Blaise Diagne before the principal exits customs; operations controller tracking throughout the 65-kilometre transfer; pre-confirmed transfer time that accounts for traffic conditions; night-arrival protocol for evening flights; and the return departure transfer on the same basis with adequate lead time for the 65-kilometre distance.
