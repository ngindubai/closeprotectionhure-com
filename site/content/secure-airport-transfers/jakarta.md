Jakarta's Soekarno-Hatta International Airport is one of Southeast Asia's busiest international gateways, and the transfer from CGK to the city's central business districts presents two distinct challenges: a genuinely high terrorism threat identified in FCDO Indonesia travel advice (2024), and some of the worst road congestion in the region. The Tol Jakarta-Tangerang corridor connects the airport to central Jakarta, but peak-hour journeys routinely take 1.5 to 3 hours.

## Transfer operations at CGK and HLP

All Jakarta transfers use inside-terminal collection by POLRI-licensed drivers (Perkapolri No. 24/2007), with operations controller route management through the Jakarta Integrated Traffic Management Centre feed. The CPO pre-brief covers the current BNPT terrorism threat status and any venue or corridor-specific advisories relevant to the transfer route and destination. Protest and demonstration activity affecting Jalan Thamrin, Jalan Sudirman, and the Gambir district is monitored continuously, with pre-planned JORR diversions activated where needed. Halim Perdanakusuma Airport (HLP) is covered for private aviation arrivals with the same protocol.
