Jorge Chavez International Airport is approximately 12 kilometres from Miraflores through the Callao district, one of Lima's highest-crime corridors. The airport transfer is the moment in any Lima visit when the combination of visible luggage, the Callao approach road, and the unescorted kerb wait creates the highest concentrated vehicle crime and express kidnapping risk. Pre-arranged SUCAMEC-licensed collection inside the terminal eliminates the primary risk factors.

## The Callao corridor

The approach road from Jorge Chavez to Miraflores traverses Callao, a district with documented armed robbery and vehicle crime statistics among Lima's highest. Late-night arrivals via this corridor require a specific protocol: transfer timing, route selection, and window and door management throughout the transit. The inside-terminal collection model means the principal never uses the unescorted taxi zone at Jorge Chavez.

## What secure transfers cover in Lima

A Lima secure airport transfer covers: inside-terminal collection at LIM before the principal exits baggage claim; operations controller tracking throughout; Callao counter-protocol routing to Miraflores or San Isidro accommodation; late-night and early-morning arrival protocols; and the return departure transfer with route planning for the specific departure time.
