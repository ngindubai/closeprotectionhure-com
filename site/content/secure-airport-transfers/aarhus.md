There's no shortcut for reaching Aarhus by air. AAR itself carries little more than a domestic Copenhagen route and a handful of international connections, so most transfers genuinely start at Billund or Copenhagen Airport, continuing by road or the direct Copenhagen-Aarhus rail link, roughly three hours as the fastest overland option.

Once the principal is in the city, the destination usually says a lot about the transfer. The Port of Aarhus handles more than half of Denmark's container traffic, and runs to Maersk or Vestas facilities need advance coordination with site security for gate access and vehicle staging. University and Agro Food Park visits are a gentler proposition, closer to a standard campus drop-off than an industrial gate. Drivers route hotel placement and travel away from the Gellerup area of Brabrand, on Denmark's disadvantaged-area list since 2014, as routine planning rather than a response to any specific incident.

Drivers hold authorisation under the Danish Guard Act, with Rigspolitiet approval required for the operating company and, for assignments with a security element, personal police approval and an ID card for the individual driver. Routine armed protection simply isn't permitted for standard commercial work in Denmark.
