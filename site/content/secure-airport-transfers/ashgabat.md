Secure airport transfers in Ashgabat begin at Ashgabat International Airport (ASB), approximately 10km south of the white-marble Neutral Zone government district, in a country where the choice of transfer operator is itself a compliance decision. Turkmenistan operates one of the most closely monitored environments for foreign visitors of any country in the world, and the Ministry of National Security (MNB) maintains active oversight of all foreign visitor activity. Arriving with an unapproved, ad-hoc driver is not simply an operational risk in Ashgabat; it is a regulatory exposure that can affect the principal's standing with the Turkmen authorities for the remainder of their visit.

## The Ashgabat security environment

The FCDO Turkmenistan travel advice (2026) advises travellers to exercise increased caution and notes that movements and communications may be monitored, photography restrictions are extensively enforced, and visa and registration requirements carry legal consequences for non-compliance. The US State Department rates Turkmenistan at Level 2 (exercise increased caution) in its 2026 advisory. Violent crime targeting foreign visitors is not the primary risk in Ashgabat; the risk profile is dominated by the surveillance and regulatory environment, in which inadvertent violations of Turkmen law - whether through photography near restricted areas, use of unapproved service providers, or registration failures - can result in detention, confiscation of materials, or curtailment of the visit.
