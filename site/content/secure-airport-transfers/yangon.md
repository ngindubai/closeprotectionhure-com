Yangon International Airport is approximately 19 kilometres from the city centre. Under Myanmar's post-coup operating environment, the airport transfer includes checkpoint management, documentation readiness, and operations controller oversight that is substantively different from a standard low-risk city transfer.

## The Yangon transfer in the post-coup context

Military and police checkpoints operate across Yangon and on airport approach roads. Transfer management covers: documentation readiness for checkpoint compliance, route planning that accounts for current checkpoint locations, and an operations controller who maintains a live check-in cadence with the driver throughout the transfer. The evacuation route from any accommodation back to RGN is also a standing element of every Yangon transfer plan.

## What secure transfers cover in Yangon

A Yangon secure airport transfer covers: inside-terminal collection at RGN; operations controller tracking and check-in cadence; checkpoint-aware routing to Bahan, Hlaing, or Kamayut accommodation; documentation management; and the return departure transfer with the same operational controls and adequate lead time for the airport's check-in and security process.
