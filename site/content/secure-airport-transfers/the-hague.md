The Hague is the seat of the Dutch government and the Netherlands' diplomatic and international legal capital, home to the ICC, ICJ, Europol, Interpol liaison offices, and dozens of embassy compounds. It draws a distinctive mix of senior diplomats, legal practitioners, government officials, and international organisation staff who frequently have both confidential itineraries and elevated personal security considerations. The Hague has no commercial airport of its own; most visitors arrive at Rotterdam The Hague Airport (RTM) for European connections or at Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) for intercontinental journeys, with many opting for the NS Intercity rail connection from Schiphol to Den Haag Centraal. FCDO Netherlands travel advice (2024) rates the city as low risk for general visitors, while AIVD 2023 reporting identifies targeted activity relevant to ICC and ICJ personnel. Pre-arranged transfers with name-board collection manage the airport-to-city leg cleanly for all visitor types.

For institutional clients, pre-arrival co-ordination with the relevant security directorate at the ICC, ICJ, or Europol is a standard part of the transfer briefing process. Drivers familiar with the Lange Voorhout diplomatic district, the ICC vehicle access procedures at Oude Waalsdorperweg, and the Peace Palace approach at Carnegieplein make a material difference to the efficiency and discretion of institutional arrivals.
