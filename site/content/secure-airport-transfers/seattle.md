The arrival experience at Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA) places corporate principals in a high-footfall, multi-concourse terminal environment where the kerbside is the first uncontrolled contact point with the wider city. SPD Crime Report 2024 identifies the Third Avenue corridor and areas of Downtown Seattle as locations of elevated disorder, and the kerbside zone at SEA as a documented contact point for unlicensed transport operators. Pre-arranged inside-terminal collection, a vetted and licensed driver, and operations controller oversight from touchdown to accommodation remove that exposure.

## The Seattle airport transfer environment

The I-5 corridor between SEA and Downtown Seattle is the primary transfer route and the most congestion-affected arterial in the region during peak periods. Route planning for secure transfers incorporates current SPD incident data and pre-surveys the SR-99 alternative before each engagement. Principals staying in South Lake Union, Capitol Hill, or Bellevue have specific route profiles that differ from a standard Downtown transfer, and planning is adjusted accordingly.

## Licensing and verification in Seattle

All drivers providing secure transfers in Seattle hold a current licence under Washington DOL RCW 18.170, administered by the Washington State Department of Licensing. This covers both private security functions and commercial transportation with security roles. Licence status is verified against the public register before any driver is engaged.
