Ahmed Sekou Toure International Airport (CKY) serves Guinea's capital Conakry, situated approximately 15 kilometres north of the Kaloum peninsula via a single approach road that the FCDO Guinea advisory (updated 2026) identifies as a documented armed robbery risk area. The combination of a constrained road network, a modest terminal environment, and a challenging urban security context makes controlled, pre-planned airport transfers an important baseline for all business principals arriving in the city.

## The Conakry arrival window

The arrivals hall at CKY is a compact facility by international standards. The single-exit arrivals channel means that passengers emerging from customs are immediately in a space where unlicensed taxi operators and informal intermediaries approach directly. For a principal arriving after a long international flight, this environment creates disorientation and pressure that increases the likelihood of accepting an unvetted vehicle.

The 15-kilometre road transfer from CKY to Kaloum or Dixinn passes through areas of Conakry with limited street lighting and inconsistent police coverage, particularly during night hours. The FCDO Guinea advisory notes that crime, including armed robbery targeting vehicles, occurs across the city and that the approach road to the airport is a specific concern. Incidents have been recorded on this corridor both during night arrivals and, less frequently, during daytime transfers.

The terminal itself has limited holding capacity, and if a driver is not pre-positioned inside the arrivals hall, a principal waiting for a vehicle faces an extended dwell period in the congested kerb zone, which the FCDO Guinea guidance specifically advises against for business travellers.
