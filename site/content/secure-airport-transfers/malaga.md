Most AGP arrivals fall into one of two categories, and the categories need different planning. Some passengers are heading straight into Malaga city, an 8km, 15-to-20-minute run under normal conditions. Others are continuing west along the A-7 to Marbella, Puerto Banus, or another point on the Costa del Sol, a longer transfer with its own destination profile: villa estates and resort hotels rather than city-centre addresses. Treating both as the same booking is how gate codes get forgotten and estate access gets delayed. We plan them separately from the outset.

AGP is genuinely one of Spain's busiest airports, and FCDO Spain (2024) flags the pattern that comes with that volume: pickpocketing and bag-snatching concentrated in crowded transport hubs, plus the occasional unlicensed operator working the arrivals hall. Our drivers wait inside the terminal with a printed name board and don't approach the principal first; the principal shouldn't accept an offer of transport from anyone who wasn't pre-confirmed at booking. It's a simple protocol, and it removes almost the entire risk window in one step. For the road leg itself, particularly the A-7 corridor toward Marbella, FCDO guidance notes vehicle crime clustered at motorway service stations, which is a large part of why a vetted driver beats a self-drive hire car on this route.
