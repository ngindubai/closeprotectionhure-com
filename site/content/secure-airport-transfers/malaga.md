Malaga-Costa del Sol Airport (AGP) is one of Spain's busiest international gateways, and that volume shapes the transfer experience as much as the airport's 8-kilometre distance from the city centre does. FCDO Spain travel advice (2024) records petty theft and bag-snatching as the principal risk at Spanish airports, a risk that scales with AGP's crowded arrivals concourse during peak season. A pre-arranged transfer with inside-terminal name-board collection avoids the principal having to search for transport in that environment, moving straight from contact to a pre-staged vehicle.

For a large share of clients, the Malaga transfer is only the first leg: the AP-7 motorway carries onward traffic 60 kilometres west to Marbella and the wider Costa del Sol, and this leg is planned as part of the same booking rather than a separate arrangement. FCDO guidance notes vehicle crime at Spanish motorway service stations generally, which drivers factor into route planning on the longer AP-7 run by avoiding unscheduled stops. Marbella hotels and private villas frequently have gated or controlled access, so gate codes and porter arrangements are confirmed with the principal before the vehicle leaves AGP.
