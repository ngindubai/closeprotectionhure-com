AGP, the IATA code for Malaga-Costa del Sol Airport, marks the start of the great majority of secure transfers into both Malaga city and the wider Costa del Sol, since the airport sits 8km southwest of the city centre and serves as the principal gateway for Marbella, Puerto Banus, and destinations further west along the coast. Terminal 2 handles international arrivals and Terminal 3 handles low-cost carriers, and drivers confirm the correct terminal against the booking before positioning the vehicle. Under Spain's Ley de Seguridad Privada 5/2014, any protective element built into a transfer requires the driver to hold a Tarjeta de Identidad Profesional and the operating company to hold Secretaria de Estado de Seguridad registration, and the vehicle-hire function itself operates under Andalusia's VTC licensing regime. For context on the wider security environment a transfer moves through, see the [Malaga city security guide](/cities/malaga/).

FCDO Spain travel advice (2024) documents vehicle crime and unsolicited approaches from unlicensed operators at Spanish airports as the principal arrival-phase risk, a pattern distinct from the pickpocketing and bag-snatching recorded in Malaga's historic centre. Name-board collection from inside the terminal, rather than a forecourt meeting point, removes most of that exposure, and the driver's booking confirmation can be checked by the principal before any luggage is handled. The onward route to the city centre via the A-7 and MA-20 carries no documented crime pattern of its own, but transfers continuing along the AP-7 or the A-7 coastal road toward Marbella face the motorway service-station risk that FCDO guidance specifically flags, which is managed by avoiding unnecessary stops on longer westbound legs. For principals whose Malaga visit also requires personal protection beyond the transfer itself, [close protection officers in Malaga](/close-protection-officers/malaga/) can be engaged to provide continuous cover from landing through to departure.

Drop-off destinations shape the transfer as much as the airport leg does. City-centre hotels such as Hotel Vincci Seleccion Posada del Patio require little more than arrival-time coordination with reception, while Malaga TechPark meetings in Puerto de la Torre need building and car park access confirmed in advance given the concentration of technology-sector tenants on the site. Costa del Sol destinations, including the Gran Melia Don Pepe in Marbella and villa or estate properties further along the coast, require the full AP-7 transfer plus, for gated properties, an access code or estate security liaison arranged before departure from the airport.
