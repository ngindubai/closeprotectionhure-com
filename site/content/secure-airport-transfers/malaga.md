AGP Malaga-Costa del Sol Airport is the arrival point for almost every visit to this region, but "a Malaga airport transfer" actually covers two quite different journeys. One runs a few minutes into central Malaga or the Pedregalejo suburb. The other runs a much longer stretch of the A-7 motorway out to Marbella, Puerto Banus, or elsewhere on the Costa del Sol, a corridor where FCDO reporting documents vehicle crime at motorway service stations and specifically flags foreign-plate and hire vehicles as disproportionately targeted. We plan for whichever journey a client actually needs rather than treating both as the same short hop.

The collection itself is the part most transfers get wrong. Documented vehicle crime concentrates around AGP's hire car collection areas, which is exactly where an unplanned taxi pickup or a rental counter queue leaves a principal standing exposed with luggage. Our driver, or the accompanying close protection officer on higher-profile assignments, positions inside the terminal ahead of baggage claim, so the handover happens before the open collection area rather than in it. Every driver operates through an SES-registered company under Ley de Seguridad Privada 5/2014, and any accompanying officer holds the Escolta Privado qualification and TIP credential required for close protection work in Spain; standard deployment throughout is unarmed, consistent with Malaga's low FCDO risk rating and with how uncommon armed authorisation is for commercial work here.
