Mandalay presents one of the most demanding airport transfer environments encountered by international travellers anywhere in the world. Since the military coup of February 2021, Myanmar has experienced sustained armed conflict between the SAC military and a range of resistance forces, producing conditions that the FCDO classifies as requiring avoidance of all but essential travel. For the small number of international professionals, journalists, humanitarian workers, and business operators who must travel to Mandalay, the transfer from Mandalay International Airport (MDL) to the city is a critical security event requiring specialist planning, not a logistical convenience.

Mandalay International Airport sits approximately 35km south of the city, and the route to the urban centre passes through areas where checkpoint positions, curfew enforcement, and road closures change with the security situation. The standard risk profile for this transfer includes military checkpoints requiring documentation and compliance, the possibility of route closure due to active operations or civil unrest, and communications blackouts that may prevent real-time coordination. Commercial transfer services and informal taxis cannot provide the contingency planning, daily intelligence briefing, and crisis escalation capability that this environment demands.
