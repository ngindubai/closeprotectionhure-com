King Shaka International Airport serves Durban, the port and manufacturing capital of KwaZulu-Natal, and the gateway to a substantial industrial and tourism economy on the South African east coast. The 35-kilometre N2 corridor from King Shaka to Durban CBD is a high-functioning dual carriageway that is simultaneously one of KwaZulu-Natal's most documented carjacking routes. The arrival at King Shaka is among the highest-risk moments in a Durban business trip.

## The King Shaka transfer risk environment

SAPS KZN crime statistics identify the N2 north of eThekwini as a priority carjacking zone. The combination of a predictable single-road corridor, arriving foreign visitors unfamiliar with the route, and identifiable hire vehicles makes airport transfer a specific risk category in Durban rather than a general ambient risk.

## What secure transfers cover

A Durban secure airport transfer covers: inside-hall collection at King Shaka by a PSIRA-registered driver; operations controller tracking from DUR to accommodation; N2 carjacking counter-protocol routing; load shedding continuity planning; and return transfers from accommodation to DUR on the same terms.
