The airport-to-centre run is the first thing to plan around in Katowice. KTW, at Pyrzowice, sits roughly 30 to 35km out, around 30 to 40 minutes by road with no closer alternative, and drivers build that distance into scheduling from the start rather than treating it as a quick hop the way many European regional airports allow.

Most transfers head toward one of two very different destinations. The Katowice Special Economic Zone, which has drawn more than PLN 50 billion in investment since 1996 and now hosts a fast-growing financial services and IT sector, calls for standard corporate campus-access coordination. The Katowice International Congress Centre and adjacent Spodek Arena, which hosted COP24 in December 2018 with roughly 20,000 attendees from 190 countries, still runs conferences at a scale that needs venue-security liaison and crowd-aware routing rather than a standard hotel drop-off.

Drivers hold Polish professional licences and, for assignments with a security dimension, a Ministry concession under the Act of 22 August 1997. The main railway station and bus depot area is routed around after dark, a standard precaution flagged consistently in general travel guidance for major Polish transit hubs.
