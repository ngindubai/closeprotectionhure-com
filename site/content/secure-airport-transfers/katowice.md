Katowice is the one city on this network where the airport transfer itself is the main planning item. Katowice Airport, known locally as Pyrzowice, sits roughly 30 to 35km from the centre, a good 30 to 40 minutes by road and notably farther than most comparable European business destinations. There is no closer alternative, so drivers build the distance into the schedule from the start rather than treating it as an unusual delay.

Once past that stretch, Katowice is a straightforward city to move through. General travel guidance flags the main railway station and bus depot area for petty crime after dark, but airport-based collection with a clearly printed name board keeps the principal out of that exposure altogether. Drivers hold valid Polish licences, and where the transfer includes a protective dimension, the operator carries a Ministry of Internal Affairs and Administration concession under the Act of 22 August 1997.

For conference visitors, the Katowice International Congress Centre, which hosted COP24 in December 2018 with roughly 20,000 attendees from 190 countries, needs arrival timing coordinated around event schedules given the volume of traffic such gatherings bring. The Katowice Special Economic Zone, named Europe's best in 2024, spans multiple sites across more than 100,000 associated jobs, and drivers confirm access arrangements ahead of any KSSE-linked visit.
