Glasgow Airport (GLA) serves Scotland's largest city and a major financial, energy, and events hub, with Glasgow Prestwick Airport (PIK) handling the private aviation traffic that GLA does not serve. Police Scotland Crime Statistics 2024 record alcohol-related disorder in the East End and weekend city centre areas, and the UK national terrorism threat level of Substantial (Home Office 2026) is the active planning baseline for all secure transfer operations in Glasgow. Pre-arranged inside-terminal collection, an SIA-licensed driver, and operations controller oversight from touchdown to accommodation provide the appropriate operational standard for this environment.

## The Glasgow airport transfer environment

The M8 motorway is the primary corridor between GLA and Glasgow City Centre, a 13km route that operates efficiently outside peak hours but congests significantly during morning and evening commuter periods. Route planning incorporates Police Scotland's current operational picture and the UK Substantial terrorism threat baseline, with at least one alternative route pre-surveyed before each transfer. Prestwick transfers for private aviation principals use the A77 and M77 corridors and are coordinated around FBO arrival times rather than scheduled flight data.

## SIA licensing and verification in Glasgow

All drivers conducting security-related transport in Glasgow hold a current SIA licence under the Private Security Industry Act 2001. SIA licence status is publicly verifiable at sia.homeoffice.gov.uk and is confirmed before any driver is engaged. This is the standard UK licensing requirement for close protection drivers and distinguishes vetted secure transfer provision from standard taxi and rideshare services.
