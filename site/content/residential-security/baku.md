Baku is Azerbaijan's capital and the operational hub for one of the world's most significant hydrocarbon industries, with BP, SOCAR and a large community of international contractors maintaining a substantial expatriate residential presence across the city. The FCDO assesses Azerbaijan at medium risk, noting concerns around awareness near government facilities, the potential for civil unrest and a crime environment that, while lower than many comparable energy-sector capitals, warrants standard residential security precautions. Energy sector assignees in Narimanov, Nizami and the purpose-built expat developments of northern Baku benefit from a more developed private security market than many Central Asian or Caucasus cities, but the quality of providers varies and formal procurement against Ministry of Internal Affairs licensing standards remains the appropriate approach. The [Baku city security briefing](/cities/baku/) provides current FCDO context, district-level risk information and emergency contact details.

Residential security in Baku for energy sector households is built around a thorough property survey, MIA-licensed guard deployment, domestic staff background checks through the Azerbaijani state identity register and documented emergency protocols. CCTV and electronic access control are well-suited to the apartment complexes and compound developments typical of the expat residential market and are integrated into the security plan during the initial survey phase. The government facility proximity assessment is a specific element for Baku given the concentration of state security infrastructure in the city: some residential areas carry an elevated awareness requirement due to proximity to facilities that attract surveillance interest.
