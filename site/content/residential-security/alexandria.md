Residential security in Alexandria splits along a fairly clear line: newer, business-district housing in Smouha and San Stefano, and older Corniche-facing buildings along the waterfront itself. The newer districts generally have conventional gated or concierge-level access already built in, and a survey there is mostly about confirming standards rather than uncovering gaps. Corniche properties are more variable, older buildings with a wide range of existing security infrastructure, sitting on a stretch of waterfront that never really empties of foot traffic, which changes what a meaningful security upgrade actually looks like from one building to the next.

Alexandria's expatriate and corporate relocation pattern is smaller and more project-driven than Cairo's, usually tied to the city's energy, shipping, or petrochemical business rather than a permanent family posting. That shapes vetting timelines for domestic staff, which more often run against a fixed assignment length than an open-ended one, though the underlying Ministry of Interior framework is identical to the rest of Egypt. Smouha, San Stefano, Kafr Abdou, and Roushdy remain the districts most families settle in, with reasonable access to Alexandria International Hospital and established international schooling.

Two shootings, in May 2023 and October 2024, targeted individuals with Israeli association in Alexandria, a factor that matters for household security planning mainly where a family's own profile carries that connection. For the great majority of expatriate households, the more routine work is building-level access control and staff vetting rather than a heightened threat posture. Alexandria has no operating British Consulate, so any household emergency plan needs to route consular contingencies through the Embassy in Cairo, roughly 220km away, rather than assume local support.
