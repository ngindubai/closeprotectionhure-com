Amman is Jordan's capital and the primary base for diplomatic missions, international organisations and corporate operations serving the Levant and Gulf regions. The residential community in Abdoun, Sweifieh, Khalda and Um Uthaina is substantial and well-established, with good access to international schools, private hospitals and the diplomatic infrastructure concentrated in West Amman. The FCDO assesses Jordan at medium risk, acknowledging that the country maintains a stable security environment relative to its neighbours while noting that proximity to the Syrian and Iraqi conflicts creates a regional threat layer that cannot be ignored in security planning. For expatriate households, that regional context is the primary driver of emergency and evacuation planning alongside the standard residential crime measures applicable at medium-risk postings. The [Amman city security briefing](/cities/amman/) provides current FCDO context, district-level risk detail and emergency contact information.

Residential security in Amman is proportionate to the medium-risk environment: a thorough property survey, Public Security Directorate-licensed guard deployment, domestic staff vetting and documented emergency protocols form the standard package. Evacuation route planning receives greater emphasis in Amman than in comparable medium-risk cities in more stable regions, because the geographic proximity of active conflict zones means that a regional escalation scenario requiring rapid departure from Jordan is a planning consideration rather than a remote contingency. All emergency protocols are coordinated with British Embassy Amman emergency procedures and Queen Alia International Airport access routes. For clients requiring personal protection alongside residential cover, details of [close protection officers in Amman](/close-protection-officers/amman/) cover mobile escort services and secure vehicle arrangements within the city.
