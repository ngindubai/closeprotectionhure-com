Citadelpark and Leopoldspark, the parkland district immediately south of Ghent's historic centre, is where a significant share of the city's relocating executive population settles, drawn by its distance from the Graslei tourist crowds and the Overpoort student nightlife corridor that the Federale Politie associates with elevated petty crime. Detached housing in Drongen, a western suburb with direct access to the E40 motorway for Brussels and Antwerp, offers a second option for families wanting more garden space than the city's terraced housing stock typically provides. Both districts sit outside the crime concentration the FCDO Belgium Travel Advice (2024) associates with Ghent's historic waterfront, giving residential security planning a genuinely lower starting risk than for a property directly on the Graslei or Korenmarkt. Executives posted to Ghent for assignments with ArcelorMittal, Volvo Cars Belgium, or the UCB and Ablynx/Sanofi pharmaceutical campuses along the Ghent-Terneuzen Canal typically take corporate-let apartments in one of these two districts rather than in the tourist core, and our survey work reflects that pattern.

Household access control in Ghent follows the building type rather than a single citywide standard. Terraced Citadelpark townhouses share party walls with neighbours and typically rely on a front door and a rear garden gate onto a service lane, both of which need independent locking rather than a single point of control. Drongen's detached homes present a larger perimeter, with driveway gates and garden boundaries that benefit from lighting and automation. Domestic staff working in either setting, whether full-time housekeepers or part-time gardeners, are vetted to the same standard: a criminal record extract, identity and right-to-work verification, and structured reference checks, processed under the EU GDPR with oversight from Belgium's Gegevensbeschermingsautoriteit.
