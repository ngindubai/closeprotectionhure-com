Residential security in Kingston is a response to a genuinely elevated threat environment. Jamaica's murder rate of approximately 44 homicides per 100,000 population in 2023 (Jamaica Constabulary Force statistics) is among the highest in the Western Hemisphere, driven by gang activity in defined communities of West and Downtown Kingston. The UK FCDO Jamaica travel advice (2026) advises against all travel to a specific list of Kingston communities. Expatriates and international staff based in Kingston require a structured residential programme that addresses compound security, staff vetting, and emergency response from first arrival.

## The residential environment in Kingston

The risk to residential security in Kingston is real but concentrated. International staff who select properties within the New Kingston or Liguanea/Barbican zones, operate with PSRA-licensed guarding, and follow a structured staff vetting process face a materially different risk exposure from the city's aggregate statistics. Property selection is the first decision in the residential security programme: a property in a recommended zone with a solid perimeter, generator-backed access control, and CCTV coverage starts the programme from a sound foundation. Sources: FCDO Jamaica travel advice (2026); US State Dept Jamaica travel advisory Level 3 (2026).

## What residential security covers in Kingston

A Kingston residential programme covers PSRA-licensed static compound guarding, vehicle access control, CCTV and lighting maintenance, periodic security surveys, domestic staff vetting, and a documented emergency response plan including medevac routing.
