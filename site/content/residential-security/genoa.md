Genoa is a city defined by its slope: the port sits at sea level, the caruggi wind through the old town in a dense grid barely wide enough for two people to pass, and the villa districts climb the hills above in a series of terraces. A residential security assessment here has to work with that geography rather than against it, because a generic flat-plot survey does not translate to a hillside property with a single steep access road or a caruggi apartment reached through a shared street door. FCDO guidance on Italy continues to describe an overall low-risk picture, with pickpocketing near the port and historic centre as the most consistently documented issue rather than residential burglary.

Households connected to Genoa's maritime and shipbuilding sector, centred on Fincantieri and the cruise operations at Stazione Marittima, sometimes see visitor and staff activity rise around ship arrival schedules, which is worth building into an access-control plan even though it is a scheduling consideration rather than a security risk in itself. Vetting for domestic staff follows the same Italian framework used across the country: Casellario Giudiziale checks, identity verification, and structured references, with the Questura di Genova overseeing local licensing compliance for the operatives involved.
