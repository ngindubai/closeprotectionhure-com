Almaty is Kazakhstan's largest city and principal financial centre, home to a well-established expatriate community drawn predominantly from the energy, banking and international organisation sectors. The city's residential security environment is assessed at medium risk, reflecting a crime profile that includes vehicle theft and residential burglary alongside a political risk layer that the January 2022 unrest made difficult to ignore. The disturbances, which originated in the oil-producing western regions before spreading to Almaty, saw significant violence in central areas within hours and demonstrated the importance of pre-planned emergency responses that do not assume a stable city environment. Expatriate households in the Medeu and Bostandyk districts, which account for the majority of corporate residential demand, are well served by the private security market but benefit from formal security planning rather than ad-hoc arrangements. The [Almaty city security briefing](/cities/almaty/) provides district-level detail, current FCDO context and emergency contact information.

Residential security for Almaty is calibrated to the medium-risk profile: a thorough property survey, MIA-licensed guard deployment, domestic staff background checks through Kazakhstan's state identity register and a clear emergency plan form the core package. CCTV and electronic access control are well-suited to the apartment complexes and gated residential developments that characterise upper Almaty, and integration with building management security systems is addressed during the initial survey. Energy sector employers frequently require formal security documentation for duty-of-care compliance, and our assessment reports are structured to meet those requirements.
