Johannesburg residential security is not a niche concern. South Africa has the largest private residential security industry in Africa, shaped by a crime environment that makes professional residential protection a normal part of life for expatriates and HNWI families in Gauteng. The question for most expats is not whether to have residential security but whether the arrangements they have are actually adequate.

## The residential threat in Johannesburg

Home invasion and armed robbery at residential properties are consistently among the most reported serious crime types in Gauteng in SAPS quarterly statistics. The Houghton, Sandhurst, Hyde Park, Bryanston, and Morningside suburbs that house most senior expat and HNWI families are not immune; they are targeted specifically because of the wealth profile of their residents. Carjacking at the property entrance and exit point is also documented in these suburbs.

Domestic staff access is the most consistently overlooked security variable. Household staff who know the family's routine, have keys or access codes, and move freely in the property without background checks represent a material information and access risk that external guards and CCTV do not address.

## PSIRA compliance for residential security

All commercial residential security operators in South Africa must hold current PSIRA registration. For manned guarding, the relevant PSIRA grades cover the specific security function. Domestic staff vetting is not a PSIRA function, but the vetting agencies used should be accredited and their SAPS criminal record check process verifiable.
