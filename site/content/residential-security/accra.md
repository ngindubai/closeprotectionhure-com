Accra is West Africa's most stable major capital city and is assessed at medium risk by comparison with its regional peers, though that lower baseline does not eliminate the need for residential security planning. OSAC Ghana Crime and Safety reporting consistently identifies residential burglary as the primary property crime affecting expatriates in Accra, including in premium districts such as Airport Residential Area, Cantonments and Labone. The risk of insider facilitation, where household staff either participate in or provide information to burglary networks, is specifically noted. Ghana's Private Security Organisations Act 2020 provides a clearer regulatory framework than much of the region, and enforcement has improved since the legislation came into force.

Our Accra residential security service is calibrated to the city's medium-risk profile: thorough rather than excessive, with an emphasis on perimeter integrity, domestic staff vetting and integration with the neighbourhood security associations that operate in most expat districts. Property surveys cover CCTV placement, gate management, lighting gaps and alarm continuity during power interruptions before any guard deployment is designed. All partner companies hold Private Security Organisations Act 2020 licences, and documentation is provided to clients on request. For larger compounds in East Legon or Trasacco Valley, patrols are structured around the specific layout of each site.
