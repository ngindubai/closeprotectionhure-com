## The residential environment in Guadalajara

Guadalajara is Mexico's second-largest city and a major corporate and technology hub, with a substantial expatriate community concentrated in Providencia and Zapopan's gated developments. The FCDO maintains a high-risk designation for Jalisco, noting the CJNG's presence as the dominant driver of elevated violent crime and kidnapping risk in the metropolitan area. While most CJNG activity is directed at rival criminal organisations and state security forces, the cartel's presence shapes the threat environment for senior corporate and high-net-worth residential principals.

Kidnapping for ransom targeting executives is documented. Carjacking at residential gates, particularly during predictable arrival and departure patterns, is a specific residential risk. The city's affluent northern and western suburbs, particularly Zapopan, have experienced targeted criminal surveillance of corporate households.

Mexico's private security sector is well-developed by regional standards, with a clear federal licensing framework through SVSP and an established market of professional residential security providers. The quality gap between licensed and unlicensed operators is significant, and verification of SVSP registration is a straightforward first step.

## What residential security covers in Guadalajara

A residential security programme in Guadalajara covers property surveys, armed guard deployment for senior principals, CCTV and alarm system integration with a licensed armed response company, domestic staff vetting through CURP and RENIS channels, pattern-of-life review, and anti-surveillance awareness training for principals and family members.

For senior executives, programmes include residential armed guard presence, route variation protocols, and coordination with the principal's corporate security team where one exists. Medevac is managed through the city's own advanced hospital network with an international policy for evacuation if required.
