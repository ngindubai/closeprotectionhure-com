San Jose's residential security environment is shaped by Costa Rica's genuine stability, which distinguishes it clearly from most Central American alternatives. Escazu, the primary residential zone for multinational executives and senior corporate households, is assessed as one of the safest residential districts in Central America. A professional residential security assessment in this context is proportionate, targeted and typically focused on the gaps that even safe environments carry: vehicle break-in risk, domestic staff access management and physical property security.

## Escazu as a residential base

Escazu hosts the majority of San Jose's expatriate corporate households and is the operating area for most residential security assignments. The district's international hotels, multinational offices and established residential developments provide a solid environmental baseline. A professional assessment builds on that baseline by identifying the specific variables it does not address: keyholder management, domestic staff vetting, parking and vehicle security, alarm monitoring, and CCTV coverage gaps.

## Vehicle security as a primary concern

Vehicle break-ins are the most consistently reported property crime for expatriate households in San Jose. The risk applies particularly to hire cars, and for residential households the relevant interventions are garage and parking security, vehicle selection, key management and the household's protocol for parking during regular journeys. These measures are identified and documented through a property assessment rather than left to individual judgement.

## Proportionate staffing

Most Escazu residential assessments do not recommend manned guarding as an outcome. The typical programme is a written property assessment, targeted physical security upgrades, domestic staff vetting and a vehicle security protocol. Where a principal's profile or specific incidents warrant manned guarding, Ministry of Security-licensed residential officers are available. The key is matching the programme to the actual threat picture rather than defaulting to either no security or disproportionate guarding.
