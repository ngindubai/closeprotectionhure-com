Extended technology-sector assignments change what residential security has to cover. A three-day hotel stay is one problem; a family relocated to Eindhoven for eight months while a parent works on an ASML programme is another, and it needs a different kind of attention entirely, one that starts with the building and works outward.

The city's own risk profile helps here: Eindhoven is not a high-crime environment, and the FCDO's general guidance on the Netherlands reflects that. What it does carry, uniquely among low-risk European postings, is the espionage dimension flagged in AIVD's 2023 annual report, which names the Dutch semiconductor sector as a target for foreign state intelligence activity. That context matters for where a family lives, not just where an executive works, because a residence with weak access control or unmonitored network infrastructure is simply an easier route to the same target. We survey accommodation in Strijp-S and Woensel-Noord in preference to the city centre for exactly this reason, and we vet domestic staff through identity, reference, and VOG checks rather than assuming a relocation agency has already done the work.
