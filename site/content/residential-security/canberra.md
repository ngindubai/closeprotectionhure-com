Yarralumla and Forrest form the practical centre of residential security work in Canberra. These two suburbs hold the city's largest concentration of embassy residences and senior-government housing, alongside neighbouring Barton and Deakin, and all four offer larger blocks and established gardens rather than the dense apartment stock typical of central Sydney or Melbourne. That geography, combined with generally low crime, makes conventional gating, perimeter lighting, and driveway access control straightforward to implement.

Domestic staff vetting is where Canberra's regulatory separation actually matters day to day. It runs under the ACT Security Industry Act 2003, administered by Access Canberra rather than the NSW framework used across the border in Sydney, and diplomatic households frequently add their own mission-specific background checks on top. Getting this sequence right, ACT baseline vetting first, mission-specific requirements second, avoids delays for households moving into embassy-adjacent properties.

Bushfire smoke is the other planning factor worth building in from the start. The 2019-2020 Black Summer season gave Canberra the worst recorded air quality of any city in the world on its worst days, according to ACT Government reporting, and that history now shapes residential security packages here more directly than in Australia's coastal cities: sealed-room planning and indoor air-quality monitoring sit alongside standard CCTV and alarm coverage rather than as an afterthought, particularly for households with children or older residents.
