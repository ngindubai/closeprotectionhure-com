Malaga's residential security picture splits along a fairly clear line: the Costa del Sol and Marbella villa corridor to the west, where properties are often seasonal and sit empty for parts of the year, and the city itself, where central apartments and the family homes linked to Malaga TechPark are occupied year-round. Those are different problems. A villa that stands vacant for months needs a maintained-presence protocol and remote-monitored alarms so it does not read as an easy target, while an occupied TechPark-area home is more concerned with vetted staff and estate-level access control working together.

FCDO guidance continues to describe Spain as a low overall risk, with pickpocketing and opportunistic theft in tourist zones as the most consistently documented issue rather than residential burglary as a general pattern. Where burglary risk does concentrate is in the seasonal vacancy window for coastal holiday properties, which is a different, more targeted problem to solve than city-centre residential security. Any security provider working in the Malaga area should hold SES registration under the Ley de Seguridad Privada 5/2014, and individual operatives should carry a valid TIP issued by the Direccion General de Policia.
