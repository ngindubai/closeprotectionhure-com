The Costa del Sol corridor running west from Malaga through Marbella and Puerto Banus holds one of Europe's densest concentrations of high-net-worth residential and seasonal property, and it is the starting point for most of the estate security work we do in this region. A gated Puerto Banus villa and a Pedregalejo apartment near the [Malaga TechPark](/cities/malaga/) present different risk profiles entirely, so our surveys are built around the property type in front of us rather than a single regional template. Owners with year-round staff, seasonal occupancy patterns, or direct A-7 motorway frontage each need a slightly different starting point, and we say so plainly in the written survey rather than smoothing it into generic language.

Malaga itself sits in the low-risk band under FCDO assessment, but that rating masks real variation at street level. Puerto de Malaga and Pedregalejo are quieter, better-managed zones; the Calle Larios and Alcazaba approach corridor carries the city's highest tourist density and the professional pickpocketing that comes with it, and the Paseo del Parque seafront has a documented pattern of scooter-assisted bag-snatching. None of that changes the overall city rating, but it does change where we recommend heavier perimeter investment versus lighter-touch monitoring. Domestic staff vetting, access control tailored to gated estates versus city buildings, and emergency protocols that account for real driving distances to Hospital Regional Universitario de Malaga round out a plan that treats the coast as several distinct micro-markets rather than one.
