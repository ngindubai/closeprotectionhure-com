Along the coast west of Malaga, the security conversation is usually about property, not people passing through. Marbella and Puerto Banus hold one of the densest concentrations of high-value villas and gated estates anywhere in Europe, and the requirements there are structurally different from anything in Malaga's historic centre. A gated Costa del Sol villa needs perimeter integrity, staff vetting, and a plan for the weeks the owner isn't there. A city-centre apartment needs shared-access control and an awareness of the pickpocketing pattern FCDO Spain (2024) documents around Calle Larios and the Alcazaba approach. We build separate survey methodologies for each, rather than force one template onto both.

Marbella's household-staffing market is genuinely international, and that shapes how vetting has to work. Housekeepers, gardeners, and estate managers often arrive with employment histories spanning several countries, so identity checks and criminal record verification (the Spanish Certificado de Antecedentes Penales, or its home-country equivalent for foreign staff) need to reach further than a single-jurisdiction check would. Every stage of that process, along with any CCTV footage the property generates, sits under Spain's LOPDGDD data protection law, which the AEPD enforces domestically alongside EU GDPR. Getting this wrong doesn't just create a privacy risk. It creates a compliance one.
