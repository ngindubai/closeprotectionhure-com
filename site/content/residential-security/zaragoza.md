Most residential security enquiries we handle for Zaragoza do not come from tourists or short-stay visitors. They come from companies relocating an executive, engineer, or plant manager for a project tied to PLAZA (Plataforma Logistica de Zaragoza) or the Stellantis Figueruelas manufacturing plant, often for a period measured in months. That changes the brief: instead of a one-off event or a hotel stay, the question is whether the accommodation, the commute, and the household itself hold up under sustained use. This page sets out how we approach that, from the property survey through to staff vetting and emergency planning, for the [Zaragoza](/cities/zaragoza/) market specifically.

The starting point is always the neighbourhood. Actur, north of the city centre, tends to suit relocating families well: it is modern, well connected, and carries a lower documented crime profile than the historic centre. Casco Historico and El Tubo are a different proposition entirely. They are the areas around the Pilar basilica and the city's bar and restaurant quarter, and Spanish police have documented organised pickpocketing activity there for years. A household whose daily pattern runs through those streets, whether for the school run, for shopping, or simply for evenings out, needs that risk factored into planning rather than discovered after the fact. Zaragoza Delicias, Spain's third busiest rail station, carries a similar counter-theft consideration for anyone using it regularly.

None of this is separate from the wider security picture for a relocating executive.
