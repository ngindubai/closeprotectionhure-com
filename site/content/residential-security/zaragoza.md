Two industrial sites do most of the work in shaping Zaragoza's residential security picture: the Stellantis automotive plant at Figueruelas and the PLAZA logistics park, one of the largest inland logistics platforms in Europe and home to operations for firms including Inditex/Zara and Amazon. Together they drive a significant share of the residential demand in the surrounding suburbs, and households connected to that sector often run on shift-pattern schedules, which means the practical security question is less about crime statistics and more about making sure a home is properly monitored and lit for arrivals and departures that fall outside standard daylight hours.

FCDO guidance continues to rate Spain overall as low risk, and Zaragoza does not stand out from comparable Spanish cities on residential burglary. That said, no residential security arrangement removes risk entirely, and a proportionate plan for a Zaragoza household should combine standard perimeter measures, appropriately vetted domestic staff under the Ley de Seguridad Privada 5/2014 framework, and remote-monitored alarm coverage suited to irregular occupancy patterns rather than relying on a single layer of protection.
