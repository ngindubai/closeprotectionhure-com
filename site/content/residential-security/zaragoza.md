Executives posted to Zaragoza for months at a time need something different from a hotel security brief. A residence, once staff and family are involved, carries risks a short stay never does: who has keys, who has access to the building, and what the surrounding streets look like after dark. The Stellantis Figueruelas plant and the PLAZA logistics platform draw a steady flow of this kind of long-assignment executive to the city, and our residential security service is built around that specific pattern rather than a generic tourist-risk template. Full context on the city's broader risk environment sits on our [Zaragoza city page](/cities/zaragoza/).

Zone selection does most of the work before any hardware goes in. Actur and the district around the Ciudad de la Justicia both offer modern, controlled-entry buildings and calmer streets than the Casco Historico, and we steer extended-stay clients toward these areas wherever the assignment allows it. Where a villa or standalone property is required instead, typically for a client whose work sits closer to the Figueruelas corridor, the perimeter plan has to account for lower street lighting and thinner foot traffic after dark. Domestic staff vetting follows the same discipline throughout: a criminal record certificate, verified identity, and structured references, regardless of whether staff arrive through an agency or a personal referral.
