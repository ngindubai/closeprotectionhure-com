The Antwerp World Diamond Centre runs its own access-control regime for the Diamond District, jointly with the Antwerp Local Police diamond department, and a household connected to that trade needs its own residential security planned alongside that layer rather than as something separate. Most households, though, are placed a short distance away in Zurenborg or Berchem, both well outside the district's tightly controlled perimeter.

Zurenborg's Belle Epoque streets and Berchem's more conventional southern layout both carry lower crime density than the city centre and support standard gate and perimeter planning. Antwerpen-Centraal's surrounds, including De Keyserlei and Pelikaanstraat, see a concentration of pickpocketing linked to high footfall, and Borgerhout carries pockets of higher crime than the Antwerp average; neither pattern reaches Zurenborg or Berchem.

The Port of Antwerp-Bruges' documented role as Europe's leading entry point for cocaine trafficking, and the organised crime violence connected to it, is a genuine part of the city's security picture, but Belgian federal authorities are clear that it overwhelmingly affects those involved in the trade rather than residential households. OCAM/CUTA's national threat level of 3 out of 4, maintained through early 2026, with reinforced patrols confirmed at locations including the Jewish quarter, is a further background factor for any household's emergency planning rather than a reason to change how a Zurenborg or Berchem property's own security is assessed.
