Bremen is a city-state built around its port and, more recently, its aerospace sector, and its residential security work reflects a straightforward split: quiet eastern districts for accommodation, and a couple of specific areas to be aware of rather than live in. Schwachhausen is the district most executive families land in, an affluent quarter with good infrastructure and a police presence that reads as consistent rather than reactive. Horn-Lehe, further out near the university, offers a similarly settled but more modest alternative.

Gröpelingen, on the port-adjacent western side of the city, carries documented elevated property crime, and Bahnhofsvorstadt near the Hauptbahnhof has had a persistent street-level drug scene for years. Both matter for staff movement planning even though neither is recommended for accommodation. Villas in Schwachhausen tend to sit on larger plots, which means garden and rear-boundary security gets as much attention in a survey as the front door.
