Airbus's Bremen production site covers roughly 450,000 square metres and employs over 4,000 people. Together with the Bremen and Bremerhaven port complex, one of Europe's largest, it explains why most executive households relocating to Bremen arrive for a maritime or aerospace sector role in the first place, though none of that scale changes what a residential property survey actually looks for: Schwachhausen and Horn-Lehe remain the two districts where the large majority of these households are placed.

Both are low-crime, well-connected districts that support conventional gate and perimeter specification. Gröpelingen, a western district close to the port, records a documented higher concentration of property crime than the Bremen average, and the Bahnhofsvorstadt around Bremen Hauptbahnhof has a longstanding street-level drug scene; neither reaches Schwachhausen or Horn-Lehe, and there is no routine reason for a household or its staff to enter Gröpelingen.

Staff vetting, covering identity verification and employment history review, runs under Section 34a of the Gewerbeordnung and the Bewachungsverordnung, with Bremen's city-state status meaning operators register through the Handelskammer Bremen rather than a regional IHK. Households connected to Airbus or the port sector generally need that vetting arranged against a fixed assignment start date.
