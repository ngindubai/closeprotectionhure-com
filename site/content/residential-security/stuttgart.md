What does a household relocating for a Mercedes-Benz or Porsche assignment actually need from residential security in Stuttgart? Mostly, it needs staff vetted against a fixed start date and a degree of discretion around home offices and document storage, reflecting the BfV's repeated flagging of industrial espionage as a risk for Germany's automotive sector executives, rather than anything unusual in the physical property itself.

The property side of the picture is fairly settled. Killesberg's park-adjacent streets and Degerloch's quiet hillside setting both carry low ambient crime and a steady police presence, supporting conventional gate and perimeter specification. Stuttgart Hauptbahnhof, still disrupted by the long-running Stuttgart 21 construction project, is the city's one consistent concentration point for pickpocketing, and Bad Cannstatt sees a comparable rise in petty theft and disorder during the Cannstatter Volksfest and spring Frühlingsfest.

None of that extends into the western and southern residential districts where most executive households are placed. Staff vetting, covering identity verification and employment history review, runs under Section 34a of the Gewerbeordnung and the Bewachungsverordnung, with operators registered through the Stuttgart Ordnungsamt and IHK Region Stuttgart qualification.
