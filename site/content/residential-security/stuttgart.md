Stuttgart's residential security demand tracks its two flagship automotive employers closely: Mercedes-Benz at Untertruckheim and Porsche at Zuffenhausen between them account for a large share of the executive and expatriate families choosing Killesberg or Degerloch as a base. Killesberg's park-side setting and Degerloch's hillside quiet both carry a genuinely low crime baseline, well below the city average.

There are two situations worth flagging without overstating them. The Hauptbahnhof concourse has seen documented pickpocketing linked to the ongoing Stuttgart 21 construction disruption, and Bad Cannstatt, home to the Cannstatter Wasen fairground, sees a real rise in theft and disorder during the Cannstatter Volksfest and Fruhlingsfest festival periods. Neither reflects a residential-district risk; they are date- and location-specific and matter mainly for planning household movement around those events.

One local detail sets Stuttgart apart from a typical residential brief: Germany's BfV domestic intelligence service has publicly flagged industrial espionage risk tied to the automotive sector, and for families connected to Mercedes-Benz or Porsche, that sometimes extends a household security review into home-office device security rather than stopping at the front door.
