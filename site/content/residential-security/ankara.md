Ankara is Turkey's capital and the seat of government, housing a large diplomatic community and a significant number of corporate executives alongside Turkish national institutions. The residential areas of Cankaya, Kavaklidere and Cinnah accommodate the majority of expatriate and diplomatic households, occupying elevated ground in the south of the city with proximity to the Grand National Assembly, ministry buildings and a concentration of foreign embassies. The FCDO's medium-risk assessment for Turkey reflects a genuine terrorism threat history: Ankara has experienced significant attacks, including the 2016 car bomb incidents, and the city's character as a government capital means that symbolic-target risk remains a planning consideration even during quieter periods. For expatriate residents, property security planning in Ankara therefore addresses both the standard residential crime profile and the specific implications of operating in close proximity to high-profile government and diplomatic infrastructure. The [Ankara city security briefing](/cities/ankara/) provides current FCDO context and district-level detail.

A residential security programme for Ankara begins with a property survey that specifically maps proximity to government facilities and diplomatic sites before making hardening recommendations. Standard perimeter and CCTV measures address the day-to-day crime risk. Window blast-film and reinforced entry-door specifications are assessed where proximity to high-profile targets justifies additional protection. Domestic staff vetting is conducted through licensed verification processes under Turkish Law No. 5188's operational framework. Emergency and evacuation planning is coordinated with British Embassy Ankara warden procedures.
