Cuba's socialist governance structure means that conventional private security provision does not exist in the form available elsewhere; the absence of independent operators is a structural given, not a gap to be filled with informal alternatives. Petty crime targeting foreign nationals (jinetero approaches, distraction theft in Habana Vieja and Centro Habana) is a secondary but documented concern.
