Algiers is Algeria's capital and commercial centre, housing a community of energy sector contractors, diplomatic staff and corporate executives concentrated primarily in the Hydra, El Biar, Telemly and Bouzareah residential districts on the hills above the Bay of Algiers. The UK Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office classifies Algeria at a medium-high threat level, noting risks from terrorism with regional roots in the Sahel, civil unrest and a security environment shaped by the country's history of political violence during the 1990s. For expatriate residents, that historical context creates a security culture that is more sophisticated than the current crime statistics alone would suggest: Algerian authorities, building managers and private security providers all operate with a heightened baseline of awareness that residential security planning should match rather than underestimate. The [Algiers city security briefing](/cities/algiers/) provides current FCDO context and district-level detail for incoming residents.

A residential security programme for Algiers is built around property hardening, domestic staff vetting and clear emergency protocols rather than large guard deployments. Crime rates in the expatriate districts are comparatively low by regional standards, but the potential for rapid escalation in a deteriorating security environment means that written emergency procedures, reliable communication with the British Embassy and an understanding of evacuation routes are non-negotiable components. All guard providers hold Ministry of Interior authorisation, and documentation is supplied to clients as standard. For clients requiring mobile personal protection for movement outside the residence, details of [executive protection in Algiers](/executive-protection/algiers/) cover secure vehicle arrangements and close protection officer deployment across the city.
