This page is for a narrow readership: the small number of people who maintain a genuinely necessary residential presence in Saint Petersburg despite the FCDO's advisory against all travel to Russia. It is not written to encourage anyone toward relocation, and if you are weighing a move to the city for any reason other than an obligation you cannot defer, the advisory itself is the first document to read, not this one.

For households that do maintain a presence, the regulatory picture mirrors Moscow: Federal Law No. 2487-1 governs private security, with Rosgvardiya licensing required for both companies and individual guards. The practical difference in Saint Petersburg is consular support. The Consulate General closed in 2018 and has not reopened, so any household security plan has to route emergency contact through the Embassy in Moscow rather than a local office, and that gap should be written into the plan rather than discovered when it matters.

Physical measures, staff vetting, and emergency departure planning follow the same three-pillar approach used in Moscow: harden the property, manage what household staff can observe of a principal's movements and communications, and rehearse a departure route that accounts for Pulkovo's lack of direct Western flights and the fact that Western bank cards do not function anywhere in Russia. Budgeting for cash roubles, for contractors and monitoring services alike, is a practical planning point rather than a footnote.
