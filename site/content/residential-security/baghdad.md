Baghdad residential security services are intended exclusively for individuals with an essential deployment requirement in Iraq. The Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office advises against all but essential travel to Iraq, citing the persistent threat from terrorism, armed militia activity, civil unrest and criminality. Those with an essential residential presence in Baghdad, including embassy and consulate staff, international organisation personnel, NGO workers and energy sector contractors, face a specific and acute security requirement that is qualitatively different from residential security provision in any other city covered on this site. This is high-specification compound security, not standard residential guarding. The [Baghdad city security briefing](/cities/baghdad/) provides current FCDO advisory detail, district-level context and emergency contact information for those who must operate in the city.

Residential security in Baghdad is structured around compound integrity rather than individual property measures. The compound perimeter, vehicle access control and blast-resistant construction are the primary layers of protection, supported by a mandatory safe room, armed response with a defined quick-reaction-force arrangement and documented counter-IED protocols for vehicle movements. All providers hold Ministry of Interior authorisation under Private Security Companies Law No. 19 of 2015, and operational plans are reviewed against current intelligence on route security and district-level threat. Emergency and extraction planning is coordinated with British Embassy Baghdad emergency procedures and includes pre-planned routes to Baghdad International Airport and alternative extraction points. For clients requiring close protection alongside compound security, details of [executive protection in Baghdad](/executive-protection/baghdad/) cover personal escort, vehicle movement and protective detail arrangements for essential journeys within the city.
