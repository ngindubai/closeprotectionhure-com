Continental AG's headquarters opened in its current Continental-Plaza form in December 2023, and it anchors a good part of Hannover's residential security work: a modern, low-crime corporate campus in the southern business district that draws relocating executive families almost by default.

List and Waldhausen are the established alternative, residential districts long favoured by expatriate and corporate accommodation with a consistently low crime profile. Wolfsburg, home to Volkswagen Group's headquarters, sits around 90km away and roughly 30 minutes off by ICE train, close enough that some relocating families treat it as within commuting range rather than a separate city.

Nordstadt's nightlife strip is worth flagging for accommodation purposes: elevated reported-offence volume after dark, tied to late-night venues rather than business or family life. Hannover Hauptbahnhof was named a higher-incident station in Bundespolizei data, 1,606 offences from January to May 2025, though a year-long joint police and city initiative cut violent crime there by more than 20%, a genuine and documented turnaround.
