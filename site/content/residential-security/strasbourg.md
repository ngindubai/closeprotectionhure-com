Five people died and eleven were wounded when a gunman attacked Strasbourg's Christmas market on 11 December 2018, and that history, together with the city's role as host to the European Parliament and Council of Europe, is why Strasbourg carries a low-moderate rating rather than the low rating typical of comparable French regional cities. France's Vigipirate Urgence Attentat level has applied nationally since, with visible security around major public venues.

None of that changes the basic residential picture, though. Neustadt, with its wide imperial-era boulevards, and the canal-side Petite France quarter remain settled, well-patrolled districts suited to executive accommodation, while Neuhof and Hautepierre, peripheral districts with documented elevated crime and drug dealing near the Hautepierre shopping centre, sit well outside the scope of any standard placement.

Households tied to a European Parliament posting or a Council of Europe contract, or to the pharmaceutical sector linked to the European Pharmacopoeia, tend to relocate on a defined session or contract date, and staff vetting, covering identity verification and employment history review, is structured to keep pace with that without cutting any individual check short.
