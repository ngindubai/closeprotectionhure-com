Dubai residential security is shaped by the city's unusual combination of low ambient crime and a growing regional threat dimension. For most expat households in established community developments, the relevant security work is a periodic assessment and targeted upgrades rather than ongoing manned guarding. For HNWI villas in Emirates Hills, Palm Jumeirah, and comparable locations, the calculus is different.

## The residential context in Dubai

Dubai's violent crime rate at residential properties is very low by international comparison. The threats that drive residential security planning for expat and HNWI households are: the post-2022 regional tension dimension (shelter-in-place awareness), domestic staff access management, and for HNWI clients, the targeting risk that wealth concentration generates.

The 2022 Abu Dhabi attack changed the question most expat households ask about residential security. Previously the question was whether residential security was needed at all; now the question is whether the household has a shelter-in-place plan, and the answer is often no.

## SIRA compliance for residential security

Commercial residential guarding in Dubai requires SIRA licensing. For households using existing community security, this is typically already in place through the development's contracted provider. For standalone villa security, verifying SIRA licensing of the guarding company is the appropriate check.
