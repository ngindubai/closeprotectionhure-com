Mumbai residential security is shaped by the city's position as one of the safer major Indian cities for expatriate residents, combined with a building security quality that varies enormously across the residential stock. The most material residential security gap for most Mumbai expat households is domestic staff vetting, not the need for manned guarding.

## The residential context in Mumbai

Violent crime targeting residential properties in Mumbai's expat and HNWI residential areas is low by the standards of most cities we cover. The threats that are relevant are: building access control that does not adequately screen visitors; domestic staff with property access who have not been vetted; and for female residents, specific safety considerations around late-night building entry and transport that are documented in NCRB data for India broadly and Mumbai specifically.

The luxury residential tower sector in Mumbai has generally good building security, driven partly by post-2008 investment in the security culture. The mid-market apartment stock varies significantly.

## PSARA compliance for residential security

Maharashtra PSARA licensing is required for commercial residential security in Mumbai. Building security providers should hold this licensing; the verification step is to ask for the Maharashtra PSARA licence number of the current provider.
