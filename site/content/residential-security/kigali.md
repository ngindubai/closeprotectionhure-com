Kigali's residential security programme reflects Rwanda's status as one of East Africa's most stable and well-governed cities. The residential programme focuses on domestic staff vetting, property survey improvements, digital privacy considerations for politically exposed principals, and emergency protocol establishment rather than the high-intensity compound security model required in higher-risk cities.

## The residential environment in Kigali

Kigali consistently records among the lowest street crime rates in East Africa. The Rwanda National Police maintains a visible and professional presence, and Rwanda's governance framework is reflected in the comparatively orderly functioning of the private security sector. The residential security considerations that remain relevant are domestic staff vetting (which is necessary regardless of external crime levels), property-specific vulnerabilities in a rapidly developing city with varied property stock, and the specific surveillance and political environment considerations that affect diplomatically or politically exposed principals.

## What residential security covers in Kigali

A Kigali residential programme covers a written property security survey, Rwanda National Police-compliant guard deployment where required, domestic staff vetting through the national identity system, and a communications and digital security brief for principals with relevant political exposure. Emergency protocols covering King Faisal Hospital access and Nairobi medevac coordination are standard.
