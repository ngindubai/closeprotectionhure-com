Residential security in Guatemala City requires structured compound protection, systematic staff vetting, and explicit anti-kidnapping protocols for any international executive or family based in the city. Guatemala City has a high crime rate with kidnapping, extortion, and violent crime affecting multiple zones. The recommended residential zones in Zone 10, Zone 14, and Zone 15 (Vista Hermosa) provide the most appropriate security environment, but structured residential security programming is the required baseline rather than an optional enhancement. Source: FCDO Guatemala travel advice (2026); US State Dept Guatemala Level 3 travel advisory (2026).

## The residential environment in Guatemala City

Guatemala City's kidnapping risk distinguishes it from many high-crime cities in the region: the risk is not limited to gang-on-gang violence but extends to targeted kidnapping of business executives and their families. Property selection in the established expatriate zones (Zones 10, 14, and 15) reduces exposure materially, but does not eliminate it. Anti-surveillance routines, predictability avoidance, and systematic domestic staff vetting address the specific vectors through which kidnapping networks gather intelligence on potential targets.

## What residential security covers in Guatemala City
