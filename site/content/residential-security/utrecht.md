    answer: "Yes. Private security in the Netherlands, including residential guarding, is regulated under the WPBR (Wet particuliere beveiligingsorganisaties en recherchebureaus, 1997, as amended). Any firm providing residential security services must hold WPBR registration through Justis, the Dutch screening authority, and individual operatives need Politie clearance and relevant certification before they can work. Armed protection requires specific police authorisation and is rarely granted for commercial residential work, so unarmed guarding is the standard model in Utrecht. EU-licensed security professionals may operate in the Netherlands subject to WPBR registration, while non-EU operators need either Dutch licensing in their own right or a working arrangement with a WPBR-registered Dutch firm. Ask any provider for their WPBR registration details before engaging them."
