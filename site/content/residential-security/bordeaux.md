Few cities pair heavy industry with heritage quite like Bordeaux. The aerospace and defence cluster around Merignac, anchored by Dassault Aviation, Thales, MBDA, and Safran, sits a short drive from a UNESCO World Heritage-listed historic centre, which itself sits within reach of the Medoc, Saint-Emilion, and Pomerol wine estate circuit. Each of those three settings calls for a different residential security approach: aerospace-linked suburban homes need conventional perimeter measures, historic centre apartments need discreet solutions that respect protected facades, and wine estates need a plan built around seasonal visitor surges during en primeur tastings and harvest.

French Interior Ministry data confirm that Bordeaux has been one of the more consistently affected cities during both the 2018-19 Yellow Vest movement and the 2023 pension-reform protests, with activity concentrated around Place de la Victoire, Place de la Bourse, and cours Victor Hugo. That pattern is a movement-planning consideration for city-centre households rather than a residential burglary risk, and it has little practical bearing on estate properties out in wine country. Any provider engaged for household or estate security in the region should be registered with CNAPS through its Nouvelle-Aquitaine regional delegation.
