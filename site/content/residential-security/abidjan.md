Abidjan is Ivory Coast's commercial capital and the primary base for multinational companies, diplomatic missions and high-net-worth individuals operating across Francophone West Africa. The Cocody plateau districts, Les Deux Plateaux and the Riviera corridor concentrate the majority of expat residential demand, while Ebrié Lagoon compounds offer waterfront living with perimeter challenges specific to that setting. FCDO travel guidance for Ivory Coast highlights the risk of crime against expatriates, including residential burglary and opportunistic vehicle crime in upmarket districts, and recommends that residents maintain strong security measures. Residential security planning in Abidjan therefore begins with an honest assessment of what licensed infrastructure exists locally, because the armed response and monitoring market here is notably less developed than in Lagos or Johannesburg.

Our Abidjan residential security service covers the full lifecycle from initial property survey through domestic staff vetting, physical hardening and ongoing guard deployment. All partner companies hold Ministry of Interior authorisation, a non-negotiable requirement in a market where unlicensed operators are common. Domestic staff vetting is integrated from the outset: household employees represent the most persistent insider-threat vector in high-risk residential environments, and formal background checks through licensed channels reduce that exposure materially. For clients in Ebrié Lagoon compounds or gated Cocody communities, perimeter management protocols are tailored to the physical layout of each site rather than applied generically.
