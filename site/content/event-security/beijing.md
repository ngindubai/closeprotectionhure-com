Beijing hosts a significant volume of international trade fairs, diplomatic conferences, and corporate summits, primarily at the National Convention Center and China National Convention Center near the Olympic Green. The operating environment is characterised by low physical crime at venues but a high level of regulatory oversight: large gatherings require Public Security Bureau approval, event content must comply with Chinese regulations, and foreign organisations must work through locally approved security operators rather than deploying international teams independently.

The FCDO and US State Dept maintain Level 2 advisories for China, citing general geopolitical caution rather than specific event-level threats. Access control at Beijing's major venues is stringent, with credentialling and screening processes aligned to PSB expectations. Executive protection details are available for senior delegates requiring secure transport across Beijing's road network between accommodation and event venues.
