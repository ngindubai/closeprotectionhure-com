Addis Ababa is Africa's diplomatic capital, hosting the headquarters of the African Union and serving as a regular venue for AU summits, IGAD meetings and World Health Assembly for Africa sessions at the UN Conference Centre, Sheraton Addis and Hilton Addis. Ethiopia's Ministry of Trade and Industry licences private security companies operating in the country, and we engage only registered, licensed local partners for all operational roles. The FCDO rates Ethiopia as a high-risk destination due to regional conflict and instability, and this assessment directly shapes the planning framework we apply to every event brief in the city. For the broader operating environment, our [Addis Ababa city security overview](/cities/addis-ababa/) provides relevant context on transport, threat categories and emergency contacts.

Close protection for diplomatic delegates and senior officials attending AU summits requires experience with multi-layer security environments where national government security services, AU security staff and private close protection teams must operate in coordination rather than in competition. Our team manages pre-event liaison, delegates advance work, and day-of communications to ensure that private security contributions mesh with official protective frameworks. Venue hardening at properties such as the Sheraton Addis and the UN Conference Centre is a critical planning step given the high-risk rating, and secure transport protocols for all delegate movements are treated as load-bearing rather than advisory. Our [event security service overview](/services/event-security/) describes the full planning methodology for diplomatic and summit-level events in high-risk environments.
