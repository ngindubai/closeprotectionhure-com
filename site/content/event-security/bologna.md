Bologna's event calendar runs on trade fairs and pharma-sector conferences more than tourism, and that shapes what event security actually looks like here. BolognaFiere alone carries EIMA, Cersaie, and a historic Motor Show through the year, each with its own hall layout, exhibitor credentials, and vehicle-staging needs. Add a pharmaceutical and biotech cluster built around firms like IMA Group and Dompé, and the result is a city where discreet executive cover and large-scale trade-fair crowd management are both regular requirements, often in the same week.

Licensing runs through the Prefettura di Bologna for operating companies and the Questura di Bologna for individual officers, under the national TULPS and Legislative Decree 153/2009 framework. Most bookings proceed unarmed, which is standard for a city where the recorded risk is pickpocketing around Piazza Maggiore and the porticoes rather than anything more serious. Airport transfers from Guglielmo Marconi (BLQ), roughly 15 to 20 minutes from the centre, and rail arrivals at Bologna Centrale are the two points where the highest-value planning gets applied, since a missed or mishandled transfer is the moment most exposure actually occurs.
