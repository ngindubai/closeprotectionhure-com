Bologna's role as one of Italy's principal trade fair cities is built on the scale of BolognaFiere, which each year hosts Cosmoprof, one of the largest beauty industry trade fairs anywhere in the world, alongside Cersaie, SANA and the Bologna Motor Show. Security planning for these events must account for a large, multi-hall exhibition site with several separate pavilion entrances, an international buyer and press delegation, and a city centre that periodically hosts its own demonstrations linked to the University of Bologna's long academic tradition. For the city's general risk profile, see our [Bologna city page](/cities/bologna/).

Cosmoprof and Cersaie each bring several thousand exhibitors and international trade buyers to BolognaFiere across their runs, and credential management has to work at pace across multiple hall entrances while keeping trade, press and VIP access properly separated. Our fair operation assigns a team lead to each hall in use, reporting to a single operations coordinator, with vehicle assets available for transfers between the exhibition site, city-centre hotels and any satellite hospitality events. Where individual principals need close protection during these fairs, our [Bologna close protection officers](/bodyguard-hire/bologna/) operate in the low-key civilian style appropriate to business-formal trade and hospitality settings.

Delegate arrivals during major fair weeks are planned around both Bologna Guglielmo Marconi Airport and Bologna Centrale railway station, with the Marconi Express monorail giving a fast, congestion-free link between the two that we favour when road traffic around the fairground is at its heaviest. Vehicles are pre-positioned at the arrivals kerb so that principals are not left queuing alongside the general passenger flow, and drivers are briefed each week on any notified demonstration activity near the university quarter that might affect the fastest route into the city centre.
