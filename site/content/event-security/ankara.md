Ankara is Turkey's capital and the seat of the Grand National Assembly, making it a recurring host for NATO-related diplomatic meetings, intergovernmental conferences and government-adjacent business events at venues including Congresium Ankara and Bilkent Otel. Turkey's private security sector is governed by Law No. 5188 (Ozel Guvenlik Hizmetlerine Dair Kanun), and all operators and individual personnel must be licensed under this framework. We engage only Law 5188-licensed local partners for every role in Ankara deployments. The FCDO advises heightened vigilance throughout Turkey, with specific reference to PKK-related terrorism and the risk of symbolic attacks in the capital. Counter-terrorism emergency planning is a non-negotiable element of every event brief we accept in Ankara. For broader context on the operating environment, see our [Ankara city security overview](/cities/ankara/).

Diplomatic events in a national capital require coordination with national police authorities from the earliest planning stages. Pre-event liaison with Turkey's Emniyet Teskilati ensures that private security deployments are aligned with police presence and that emergency communication channels are established and tested before the event begins. Venue hardening at Congresium Ankara and Bilkent Otel follows a standard methodology covering perimeter integrity, access point assessment and emergency egress options. Secure transport from Esenboga International Airport for all international delegates is a baseline recommendation, and close protection for senior diplomatic and corporate attendees is standard at this risk level. Our [event security service overview](/services/event-security/) sets out the full planning framework we apply to diplomatic and summit events in capital cities.
