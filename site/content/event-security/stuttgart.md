Stuttgart's event security work is shaped by three things that do not usually sit together in one city: a trade fair venue built almost on top of the airport, two of the world's best-known automotive manufacturers headquartered locally, and a folk festival calendar rivalling Oktoberfest in scale. Messe Stuttgart's fairs, AMB and Blechexpo among them, benefit from a roughly 12km, direct route from Stuttgart Airport, while Mercedes-Benz at Untertürkheim and Porsche at Zuffenhausen generate corporate visits that call for a much quieter, more discreet security posture entirely.

Licensing runs through Sec 34a Gewerbeordnung and the Bewachungsverordnung, with registration via the Stuttgart Ordnungsamt and IHK Region Stuttgart. Germany's BfV has specifically flagged industrial espionage as a live risk around automotive sector visits, a detail we brief for any executive carrying sensitive technical material into a Mercedes-Benz or Porsche site visit. Stuttgart 21, the long-running rail infrastructure project, continues to disrupt pedestrian and vehicle routes around Hauptbahnhof, Schlossplatz and Konigstrasse, and our planning routes around that rather than assuming it has finished.
