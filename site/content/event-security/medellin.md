  - title: "SVSP Licensing and Regulatory Framework"
    description: "Private security in Colombia is regulated at the national level by the Superintendencia de Vigilancia y Seguridad Privada (SVSP), an agency of the Ministry of Defence. All private security firms operating in Colombia, including those providing event security and close protection, must hold a valid SVSP operating licence. Individual security personnel must be registered with the SVSP and carry their identification credentials on duty. Colombia's national regulatory framework is more developed than many Latin American comparators: the SVSP conducts inspections, applies sanctions for unlicensed activity and maintains a public register of authorised firms. International clients should verify SVSP registration before contracting any provider. The Colombian National Police (Policia Nacional) maintains a liaison function for large events and can provide uniformed augmentation where the security plan requires it, subject to advance notification and coordination. For events at Plaza Mayor, the venue has its own licensed security contract that forms the outer layer of the access control structure."
