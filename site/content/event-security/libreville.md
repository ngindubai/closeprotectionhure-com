Libreville is the commercial and administrative capital of Gabon and the principal hub for the country's hydrocarbons sector, drawing oil executives from TotalEnergies, Perenco and Assala Energy alongside CEMAC and African Union institutional delegates throughout the year. The political landscape shifted significantly with the August 2023 coup that brought the CTRI transitional committee to power, but Libreville has maintained functioning commercial activity and the Quartier Louis and La Sabliere hotel district continues to host the majority of international conference activity. The FCDO Gabon travel advice (2026) rates the city at medium risk, with opportunistic robbery and the transitional political environment as the primary considerations for event planners.

## The Libreville event security environment

The CTRI's presence since the coup has changed the security calculus for high-profile events in Libreville. Events involving foreign government ministers, oil-sector executives or senior multilateral officials now require advance coordination with CTRI-affiliated presidential security services, in addition to the standard Interior Ministry licensing process for private security operators. Demonstrations can occur at short notice in central Libreville, particularly around the administrative quarter near the presidency and government buildings, and organisers should build contingency plans for delegate movement that avoid those areas if political announcements are expected. Crime risk in the city is rated at medium, with robbery the primary daily concern rather than organised targeting of international delegates. Source: FCDO Gabon travel advice (2026).

## Planning events in Libreville

The Radisson Blu Libreville and the Intercontinental Libreville in the La Sabliere and Quartier Louis districts are the standard venues for oil-sector conferences, CEMAC regional meetings and diplomatic receptions. Interior Ministry-licensed security operators are required for all event security functions. Delegate transfer planning from Leon M'ba International Airport (12km north) is a standard planning element, and the medevac protocol to Douala or Paris should be confirmed in writing before the event begins.
