Event security in Bordeaux has to serve two very different worlds that happen to occupy the same city. On one side is the wine trade calendar: Vinexpo, the Bordeaux Wine Festival on the Garonne quaysides, private auctions, and the chateau circuit through the Medoc and Saint-Emilion during en primeur week. On the other is a defence and aerospace cluster around Merignac that runs restricted, credential-heavy conferences with security requirements closer to a government contractor's event than a trade fair. A booking might need both in the same week. Our teams plan for whichever profile applies rather than defaulting to a single event-security template.

Crowd management in Bordeaux also has to account for the city's protest history in a way that many other event cities don't. Yellow Vest demonstrations in 2018 and 2019, and pension reform marches in 2023, concentrated around Place de la Victoire, Place de la Bourse, and the cours Victor Hugo corridor, the same detail set out on our [Bordeaux city security guide](/cities/bordeaux/). An event scheduled near any of those locations needs an alternative-route plan from the outset, not a reactive scramble if a march materialises nearby. Getting delegates and VIP guests into and out of an event safely is often the part clients think about last, and it shouldn't be. Whether guests are arriving via Bordeaux-Merignac Airport for a defence-sector conference or via Gare Saint-Jean on the TGV from Paris for a wine trade event, transfer planning is built into the event security proposal from day one rather than bolted on afterwards.
