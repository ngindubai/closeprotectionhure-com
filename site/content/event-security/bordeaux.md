Vinexpo, the Bordeaux Wine Festival, and the en primeur tastings each spring turn Bordeaux into one of the wine trade's busiest calendars, pulling in buyers, collectors, journalists, and HNWI clients for a short, intense window rather than a steady year-round flow. Event security here has to work at the pace of that calendar: accreditation and access control for trade-only sessions, protection of wine stock worth considerably more than the room it sits in, and close coordination with venue management on egress for VIP buyer visits.

A second, quite different demand comes from Merignac, where Dassault Aviation, Thales, MBDA, and Safran run aerospace and defence facilities next to Bordeaux-Merignac Airport. Conferences and supplier days connected to that sector carry attendee lists of a different character, facility access that has to be coordinated with the host company's own site security, and France's national Vigipirate Urgence Attentat posture as a standing planning factor. Bordeaux's demonstration history adds a third layer that applies to almost any large or outdoor gathering: protest activity connected to national issues, documented through the 2018-19 Yellow Vest period and the 2023 pension-reform marches, has concentrated at Place de la Victoire, Place de la Bourse, and the cours Victor Hugo corridor, and the current FCDO advice is simply to avoid demonstrations rather than treat them as background noise.

CNAPS-authorised teams handle accreditation, crowd management, and close protection for keynote speakers and VIP delegates across both event types, unarmed as standard under Loi 83-629, since armed authorisation is rarely granted for commercial event work.
