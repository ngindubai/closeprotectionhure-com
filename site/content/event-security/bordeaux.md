Bordeaux's event calendar sits at the intersection of two distinct industries: the wine trade, which brings international buyers to the city and out to chateaux across the Medoc and Saint-Emilion appellations each en primeur season, and aerospace and defence, anchored by Dassault, Thales, MBDA and Safran around Merignac. The biennial Bordeaux Fete le Vin adds a third dimension entirely, an open-air festival on the Garonne quays that generates crowd density on a scale no conventional venue in the city needs to manage. Planning event security across this range means moving between discreet wine-trade hospitality coverage, confidentiality-focused aerospace conference security, and municipal-scale crowd coordination during the festival. For the city's general risk profile, see our [Bordeaux city page](/cities/bordeaux/).

En primeur season draws international trade buyers, sommeliers and winery group principals to a concentrated run of city tastings and chateau visits, and our coverage for these delegations typically runs to a single CPO in civilian dress, moving discreetly between venues rather than presenting a visible security profile at what are fundamentally hospitality events. Aerospace sector conferences linked to the Merignac cluster carry a different emphasis, given the defence-adjacent content often under discussion, and principals attending these events can be supported by our [Bordeaux close protection officers](/bodyguard-hire/bordeaux/), briefed specifically on confidentiality requirements alongside standard protective duties.

Bordeaux-Merignac Airport handles the great majority of delegate arrivals, whether for wine trade or aerospace sector events, with a typical 20-25 minute transfer to the city centre and onward journeys of 45-75 minutes to specific chateaux depending on the appellation. During the Fete le Vin, any client event we support is planned in direct coordination with the municipal crowd management structure already covering the quays, rather than run as a standalone operation across a festival site that draws exceptionally high visitor numbers along an extended stretch of open waterfront.
