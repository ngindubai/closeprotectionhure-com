Zaragoza's event calendar runs on two very different rhythms. Feria de Zaragoza delivers scheduled, credentialed trade exhibitions tied to the city's automotive and logistics economy; Fiestas del Pilar delivers ten days of uncontrolled civic crowd density every October. Planning event security here means holding both models in the same operational framework rather than defaulting to one. Background on the wider city risk picture, including the FCDO-documented petty crime patterns that inform our venue and route planning, sits on the [Zaragoza city page](/cities/zaragoza/).

Trade fair events at Feria de Zaragoza and corporate gatherings at the PLAZA logistics platform share a common thread: both sit in a business rather than a civic environment, and both require advance liaison with a venue or facility operator who has their own access rules. Fiestas del Pilar could not be more different. Vehicle access collapses across large parts of the centre, and a transfer that takes fifteen minutes in June can take three times as long in October. We do not treat festival-week events as a variation on a normal booking; they get a separate planning track from the outset, with route options confirmed days rather than hours ahead. Getting delegates to the venue safely starts before they land. Most arrive through Zaragoza Delicias, one of Spain's busiest AVE interchanges, or through Zaragoza Airport, and both routes benefit from a pre-arranged collection rather than an ad hoc taxi from the concourse.
