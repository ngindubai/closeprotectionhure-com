Feria de Zaragoza sits on the eastern edge of the city, close to the airport approach road, and functions as Aragon's principal venue for trade exhibitions connected to the region's agricultural, logistics, and automotive supply-chain industries. Alongside it, the Palacio de Congresos de Zaragoza, built on the Ebro riverside for Expo Zaragoza 2008, hosts corporate summits and keynote conferences in a more contained single-level layout. Security planning across the two venues differs accordingly: Feria de Zaragoza's larger, multi-hall exhibition footprint calls for a perimeter-first approach with several managed entry points, while the Palacio de Congresos' riverside setting concentrates access control around a smaller number of gates but adds embankment sightlines to the planning picture. For the wider Zaragoza risk environment behind both venues, see the [Zaragoza city page](/cities/zaragoza/).

The PLAZA logistics platform and the Stellantis Figueruelas automotive plant, roughly 40km from the city, generate a distinct category of Zaragoza event: investor briefings, supply-chain summits, and plant-visit itineraries that combine a city-based venue with an industrial site. These carry a commercial-sensitivity profile close to a product launch, with restricted attendee lists and accreditation kept separate from general venue access.
