Zaragoza's Feria de Zaragoza exhibition complex anchors a calendar that ranges from consumer-facing trade fairs to automotive and logistics sector conferences tied to the Stellantis manufacturing plant at Figueruelas and the PLAZA logistics park, one of the largest of its kind in Europe. Alongside this business calendar sits the Fiestas del Pilar each October, a festival drawing over one million visitors across roughly ten days that generates crowd density on a different scale entirely to any single exhibition event. Security planning across this range means switching between conventional trade fair access control, controlled delegate-list verification for industrial sector conferences, and coordination with municipal crowd management during the festival. For the city's general risk profile, see our [Zaragoza city page](/cities/zaragoza/).

Automotive and logistics conferences linked to Figueruelas and PLAZA typically involve a pre-vetted delegate list rather than open ticketing, and where a facility visit forms part of the itinerary, our advance team confirms access protocols directly with the host company's own security function ahead of the delegation's arrival. Principals attending these events, or requiring dedicated coverage during a Feria de Zaragoza trade fair, can be supported by our [Zaragoza close protection officers](/bodyguard-hire/zaragoza/), who work in civilian dress appropriate to formal business and manufacturing-sector settings.

Many international delegates reach Zaragoza via Madrid-Barajas and the high-speed AVE rail link rather than flying direct, given the more limited scheduled international service at Zaragoza Airport, and we provide platform-level meet-and-greet at Zaragoza-Delicias station for arrivals by this route alongside standard airport collection. During the Fiestas del Pilar, any client event we support defers to the established regional police and municipal crowd management plan already in place for the festival, rather than attempting a standalone perimeter across streets carrying some of the highest visitor density seen anywhere in Spain that week.
