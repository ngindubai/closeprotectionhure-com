  - title: "PPSRA Licensing and Regulatory Framework"
    description: "Private security operations in Pakistan's Punjab province, which covers Islamabad's broader regulatory environment, fall under the Punjab Private Security Regulatory Authority (PPSRA). Firms providing close protection or event security services must hold a valid PPSRA licence, and individual operatives must carry authorised identification cards at all times. The Islamabad Capital Territory (ICT) administration imposes additional permit requirements for armed personnel deployed at private events. Any event-security provider operating in Islamabad without PPSRA compliance exposes the event organiser to legal liability and the risk of forced operational withdrawal at short notice. Reputable providers will supply licence documentation on request and will coordinate directly with ICT administration on permits for armed detail. International clients should request written confirmation of licensing status before signing any contract. The Pakistan Security Industry Association offers a secondary reference point for vetting firms operating across multiple provinces."

  - title: "Terrorism and Soft-Target Risk"
    description: "Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan (TTP) and affiliated groups have demonstrated both the intent and capability to target soft venues in Pakistan's urban centres. The FCDO Pakistan travel advisory (2025) rates Pakistan at a high-threat terrorism level and notes that hotels, crowded markets and public gatherings are among the categories most frequently identified as soft targets in threat assessments. Islamabad's diplomatic concentration means that incidents here attract disproportionate international media coverage, making high-profile venues operationally attractive to groups seeking to demonstrate reach. Security protocols for international events must include advance venue surveys, sweeps for improvised explosive devices prior to delegate arrival, perimeter management throughout the event duration and a tested emergency evacuation plan. All delegates should receive a pre-event security briefing covering route variations, assembly points and communications procedures. Event planners must also factor in the Pakistani government's security checkpoint infrastructure: delegates will encounter checkpoints on approach routes and should be briefed accordingly to avoid delays or misunderstandings."

  - title: "Venue Access Control"
    description: "Robust access control at Islamabad venues requires a layered approach beginning at the outer perimeter and tightening progressively toward the principal meeting room or stage area. International-standard hotels such as the Serena and Marriott operate their own vehicle checkpoints and bag-screening infrastructure, but these are baseline hotel measures and are not calibrated to the threat profile of a specific event. A dedicated event-security team should establish a credentialing process separate from the hotel's general guest access system, using photo identification verified against a pre-registered delegate list. VIP and keynote speakers should have a segregated entry route that does not intersect with general delegate flow. Catering, technical and AV staff should be background-checked and issued with distinct event passes that are visually distinguishable from delegate credentials. Any contractor not on the pre-approved list should be refused access regardless of claimed affiliation. Communications between the access control team and the event security command should be maintained on a dedicated encrypted channel throughout the event."

  - title: "ISB Airport Delegate Transfers"
    description: "Islamabad International Airport (ISB), opened in 2018, handles all international arrivals. The airport's landside zone, including the arrivals hall and the road approaches, represents the highest vulnerability point for arriving delegates: identity can be inferred from flight manifests, and the public nature of the arrivals corridor limits pre-screening options. An inside-terminal collection protocol, in which a licensed CPO meets the delegate airside where access permits or at the controlled domestic exit, reduces exposure. Vehicle selection for the transfer leg should favour low-profile, locally registered estate cars over conspicuous executive vehicles. The route between ISB and the city centre passes through several zones where roadside incidents have historically occurred; the chosen route should be varied on each transfer leg. All transfer vehicles should be equipped with a communications device linked to the event-security command, and a contingency route to the nearest international standard medical facility should be confirmed in advance of each transfer operation."
