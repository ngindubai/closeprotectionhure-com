Hong Kong remains one of Asia's most active corporate event destinations, with the HKCEC and AsiaWorld-Expo hosting major international conferences, trade fairs, and financial summits. Since 2020, the operating environment for event planners has changed substantially: large gatherings require HKPF liaison, and event content with political or advocacy dimensions carries legal risk under the National Security Law and the Safeguarding National Security Ordinance 2024. Professional event security teams must be familiar with both frameworks to operate effectively in this environment.

HKPF-licensed security personnel manage access control, credentialling, and VIP protection at all significant events. Protest activity is essentially nil in the post-2020 environment, which simplifies public-order planning, but the legal operating environment demands careful pre-event content review and close coordination with local counsel. The FCDO advises normal travel precautions while noting the NSL's wide scope as a consideration for international visitors and event organisers.
