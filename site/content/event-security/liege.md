Liege Airport is the fifth-largest cargo airport in Europe by volume, operating around the clock with no noise curfew, and that logistics-first identity shapes how event security here differs from a conventional trade-show city. Site visits and briefings tied to cargo terminal or e-commerce logistics operations require advance access coordination, and our role is to manage delegate movement and close protection inside the host site's existing framework rather than build an independent perimeter around an active freight zone.

The Palais des Congres carries the city's more conventional conference and trade-event calendar, with hall-level credential planning and registration routes agreed with venue management ahead of any event opening. Because LGG itself handles very limited passenger traffic, corporate delegates typically arrive through Brussels Airport or Brussels South Charleroi Airport, with a vetted security driver covering the E40 or E25 transfer into the city.

Belgian private security runs under the 2017 law on private and particular security, with firms holding an SPF Interieur agrement and officers carrying a Ministry of Interior badge. Sainte Marguerite is flagged in local safety guidance as an elevated-crime district and is excluded from all event routing, and Belgium's OCAM/CUTA threat level has held at 3 out of 4 through 2026, a posture that shapes vigilance at any high-footfall Liege event.
