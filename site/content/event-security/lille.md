A single major exhibition at Lille Grand Palais can move up to 20,000 delegates through the venue, and that scale is why event security here starts with hall-level crowd planning rather than a single point of entry. Registration desks, exhibitor loading bays and VIP arrival routes are agreed with venue management well ahead of doors opening, and credential lanes are kept separate for trade visitors, press and general admission wherever a show format mixes them.

A different, quieter pattern runs through Euralille, the business district built directly between Lille Flandres and Lille Europe stations, home to around 150 companies including proximity to Auchan's Croix headquarters and Decathlon's base in Villeneuve-d'Ascq. Corporate functions and product launches here favour a discreet, function-room presence over an exhibition-scale perimeter. Delegates arriving from abroad most often come by Eurostar or TGV, and local station-area pickpocketing, a pattern FCDO France notes at transport hubs nationally, is why we collect arrivals at platform level rather than waiting in the main concourse.

CNAPS licenses both firms and individual officers under the Code de la securite interieure, and Lille-Sud and Moulins carry an official priority security zone designation that keeps them out of any route plan regardless of the event. France's Vigipirate Urgence Attentat posture applies nationally, with FCDO France advising heightened vigilance at transport hubs, a standard that shapes perimeter thinking at both Grand Palais and the Eurostar termini.
