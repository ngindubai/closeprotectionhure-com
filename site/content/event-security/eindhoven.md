Dutch Design Week, held every October, is the reason most large-scale event security bookings come into Eindhoven, and it is also the reason a single-venue approach to event security here does not work. The event spreads across the city with Strijp-S as its primary hub and significant additional venues at Evoluon and Klokgebouw, so a security plan that treats it as one site with one perimeter misses the movement between locations, which is where accreditation gaps and briefing failures tend to surface. Planning for DDW starts from the venue map, not from a generic event-security checklist.

Away from the design calendar, Eindhoven's event security work is shaped by the same fact that defines the whole city commercially: it is a semiconductor and technology hub built around ASML, NXP Semiconductors and the Philips Innovation Campus. Corporate showcases, product launches and investor days connected to that cluster carry a threat profile that goes beyond general public interest, given AIVD's 2023 documentation of foreign state actors targeting Dutch high-technology firms. That does not mean every event needs a heavy security footprint; Eindhoven remains a low-risk city by any general measure. It means the specific dimension of protecting sensitive material and controlling who observes a demonstration area gets built into the plan from the outset rather than added after the fact. Delegate transfers, whether through Eindhoven Airport or a coordinated run from Amsterdam Schiphol, are scheduled as part of the same operational picture, and where a speaker's profile warrants it we layer in [close protection officers](/close-protection-officers/eindhoven/) or wider [bodyguard hire in Eindhoven](/bodyguard-hire/eindhoven/) alongside the general venue team.
