Eindhoven's Strijp-S campus, anchored by the Evoluon exhibition building and Klokgebouw, hosts two very different kinds of event: Dutch Design Week each October, Europe's largest design event and a largely open, walk-up public festival, and a steady calendar of technology sector conferences linked to ASML, NXP and Philips that run to a closed, invitation-only model. Security planning has to switch between these two modes rather than applying a single template, particularly given the Dutch General Intelligence and Security Service AIVD's documented reporting on foreign state interest in Dutch semiconductor firms, which shapes the confidentiality standard we apply to technology sector events specifically. For the city's general risk profile, see our [Eindhoven city page](/cities/eindhoven/).

Dutch Design Week's distributed footprint across Strijp-S and satellite venues means access control is agreed building by building with each venue operator rather than through a single festival-wide perimeter, and credential checks are concentrated at entrances rather than a single gate given the high proportion of general public attendance. Technology sector conferences, by contrast, involve a pre-vetted delegate list and a considerably higher emphasis on controlled access; principals attending these events can be supported by our [Eindhoven close protection officers](/bodyguard-hire/eindhoven/), briefed specifically on the confidentiality expectations that distinguish semiconductor-sector events from a public design festival.

Eindhoven Airport handles the majority of direct arrivals at a 15-20 minute transfer to the city centre, while delegates travelling from further afield frequently connect via Amsterdam Schiphol, roughly 90 minutes away, with platform-level meet-and-greet provided at Eindhoven Centraal for arrivals by rail. During Dutch Design Week, both the airport and the station see a marked increase in passenger volumes, and vehicles are pre-positioned at the arrivals point so that principals are not left waiting alongside the general festival crowd converging on the city that week.
