Eindhoven's event calendar runs from a design festival that spills across the whole city to tightly controlled corporate briefings inside some of the world's most strategically significant technology campuses, and those two ends of the spectrum need almost opposite security approaches. Dutch Design Week, held every October with Strijp-S as its anchor site, asks for coverage spread thin across many venues at once. An ASML or Philips Innovation Campus investor event asks for the reverse: a small, tightly controlled footprint with credential checking that leaves no room for error.

What ties the two together is the same regulatory floor. Every operative deployed at an Eindhoven event, whether staffing a DDW registration desk or supporting a Philips campus briefing, works under WPBR registration via Justis and individual Politie clearance, the same framework covered on our [Eindhoven city security guide](/cities/eindhoven/). What changes is the threat picture layered on top: for ASML and NXP-adjacent events, that means the counter-espionage awareness documented in AIVD's 2023 annual report; for large public gatherings, it means demonstration-related transport disruption, which FCDO guidance flags as a live risk for routes into and out of Eindhoven Airport.
