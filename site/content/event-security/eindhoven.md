Eindhoven's event calendar is shaped by two forces: Dutch Design Week's public-facing crowds at Strijp-S and the Philips Innovation Campus, and the far more discreet world of semiconductor-sector investor days tied to ASML in neighbouring Veldhoven. The two could not be more different in profile. Design Week needs conventional crowd management across converted industrial buildings, keeping general admission separate from trade and press lanes; ASML's investor events need almost the opposite, a quiet, tightly verified guest list rather than a large public footprint.

That distinction matters because of a specific, named risk: the Dutch intelligence service AIVD's 2023 annual report identified state-actor corporate-espionage targeting of the country's semiconductor and high-tech sector, naming ASML and NXP Semiconductors directly. It is not a physical-safety concern in the way a demonstration or a crowd surge would be, but it does change how we handle delegate lists, credentials, and event materials at any conference in this sector. Licensing for private security in the Netherlands runs through WPBR and Justis approval, with individual officers screened by the police; armed protection is not available for commercial event work here, so cover is unarmed by default and relies on planning rather than a visible show of force.

Most long-haul delegates route through Amsterdam Schiphol and take the roughly 75-minute direct train into Eindhoven Centraal, rather than flying into Eindhoven Airport itself.
