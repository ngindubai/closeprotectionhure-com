Guatemala City is the capital of Guatemala and the largest city in Central America, hosting the region's principal commodity sector events, regional trade forums, and diplomatic gatherings. Zone 10 (Zona Viva) and Zone 14 provide the primary event venue and hotel cluster for international visitors, concentrated around the Westin Camino Real, Hilton Guatemala City, and the Centro de Convenciones America. Guatemala City's event security environment reflects one of the region's highest rates of kidnapping and violent crime targeting perceived high-value individuals, requiring structured security planning for any organised international event.

## The Guatemala City event security environment

The FCDO advises against all but essential travel to several specific municipalities in Guatemala, while noting that Zone 10 and other established business districts in Guatemala City are more manageable for international visitors. The US State Department classifies Guatemala at Level 3 (Reconsider Travel). Kidnapping targeting business executives is a documented and active risk. Extortion targeting organisations is widespread. Event security planning for Guatemala City must address the gap between the managed hotel venue environment and the broader city risk level. Source: FCDO Guatemala travel advice (2026); US State Dept Guatemala Level 3 advisory (2026).

## Planning events in Guatemala City

Venue selection for international events in Guatemala City is effectively constrained to Zone 10 and Zone 14. Gobernacion-licensed operators with current UESSP registration are engaged for all event security functions. Delegate transport is planned from first contact, with inside-terminal collection at La Aurora Airport and operations controller tracking for all movements. Kidnapping awareness is an integrated element of delegate briefings for all international events.
