Luxembourg City event security operates under the Law of 12 November 2002 on private security, overseen by the Grand Ducal Police (Police Grand-Ducale), covering principal venues including the European Convention Centre Luxembourg (ECCL) at Kirchberg Plateau, LuxExpo The Box, and Chambre de Commerce Luxembourg. Core service components span credentialled access control, delegate close protection, and transfer management from Luxembourg Airport Findel (LUX). The Kirchberg Plateau's concentration of EU institutions, including the European Court of Justice, Court of Auditors, and European Investment Bank, means many events carry a diplomatic or institutional profile requiring coordination with EU institutional security services alongside standard licensed private security.

## Luxembourg regulatory framework

All event security personnel at Luxembourg City events hold valid professional authorisation under the Law of 12 November 2002 on private security. The Grand Ducal Police (Police Grand-Ducale) provides licensing oversight and coordinates public security at ECCL and Kirchberg Plateau institutional venues. Luxembourg consistently records one of Europe's lowest crime rates, and the FCDO rates the country at normal precautions (2026), with the US State Department at Level 1 (2026).
