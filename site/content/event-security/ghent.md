ICC Ghent and the neighbouring Flanders Expo halls at Sint-Denijs-Westrem give the city a conference and trade-event campus capable of hosting several thousand delegates at once, and it sits alongside a very different kind of event entirely: the Gentse Feesten, the open-air July festival that City of Ghent data credit with up to one million visitors across the historic centre over ten days. Few cities combine a purpose-built convention campus with a festival of that scale within the same municipal boundary, and event security planning in Ghent has to move between the two registers, sometimes within the same week. A product launch or investor briefing at Flanders Expo calls for controlled accreditation and discreet VIP handling; a festival deployment calls for open-street crowd management across a historic centre with narrow medieval lanes that were never designed for modern visitor volumes. Both fall under the same Belgian licensing regime, the Loi du 2 octobre 2017, administered through SPF Interieur agrements and individual Ministry of Interior badges.

Ghent's canal-zone industrial and pharmaceutical employers, ArcelorMittal, Volvo Cars Belgium, and the UCB and Ablynx/Sanofi campuses, generate a steady flow of investor briefings and product events that sit alongside Ghent University's academic conference calendar. Delegates for both typically arrive through Brussels Airport, 55km southeast via the E40, or by direct rail from Brussels Midi to Ghent-Sint-Pieters in 30 minutes.
