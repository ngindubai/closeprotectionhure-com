Ghent runs on two very different event rhythms. There's Gentse Feesten, the ten-day July festival that, per City of Ghent data, brings up to one million visitors into a historic centre built for a fraction of that number. And there's the steadier calendar of Flanders Expo trade shows, industrial exhibitions, and private corporate functions tied to the city's steel, automotive, and pharma sectors. Good event security in Ghent has to work for both, and they don't respond to the same playbook. A crowd-management plan built for a controlled exhibition hall does very little for a VIP trying to reach a canal-side reception during festival week. Background on the wider city threat picture is available on our [Ghent city page](/cities/ghent/).

Flanders Expo, southwest of the centre at Sint-Denijs-Westrem, is the more conventional problem: purpose-built halls, staged vehicle access, credential checks at defined entry points. Private events in the historic centre, guild houses along Graslei, functions in Patershol, bring the opposite challenge, narrow entrances, listed-building constraints, and limited vehicle access for arriving guests. Both need a venue advance before the first delegate arrives, but the advances look nothing alike. Where an event calls for personal protection for a specific principal rather than general venue coverage, our [close protection officers in Ghent](/close-protection-officers/ghent/) page covers that layer in more detail, and for principals needing secure ground transport between BRU, the venue, and their hotel, see [secure airport transfers in Ghent](/secure-airport-transfers/ghent/).

Gentse Feesten is the outlier that deserves its own planning cycle rather than a standard event brief. Federal Police data document elevated pickpocketing at Graslei and Korenlei throughout the festival, and vehicle access through the historic centre is constrained for most of the ten days. Any client hosting or attending an event during this window should engage well ahead of July; the brief needs daily updates rather than a single static document, since crowd conditions shift noticeably from one day to the next. Belgian student and environmental demonstrations, documented in Federal Police incident data for 2022 and 2023, cluster around the Korenmarkt and university district and are a separate, year-round consideration for any event planned near either location.
