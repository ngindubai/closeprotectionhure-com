Ghent runs two very different kinds of event through its calendar: a steady programme of port, steel and pharmaceutical sector conferences at ICC Gent, housed within the historic Het Pand complex, and the Gentse Feesten each July, a citywide festival that City of Ghent data suggests can draw up to one million visitors over its run, concentrated around the Graslei, Korenlei and Korenmarkt. Security planning has to shift between a conventional, confidentiality-focused business-conference model for ICC Gent's industrial sector events and coordination with an established municipal crowd management operation during the festival. Port, steel and pharmaceutical sector conferences at ICC Gent typically involve a pre-vetted delegate list, and where a conference includes a site visit to an industrial or port facility, our advance team confirms access protocols directly with the host company's own security function before the delegation travels. Principals attending these events can be supported by our [Ghent close protection officers](/bodyguard-hire/ghent/), who work in civilian dress appropriate to the business-formal environment of most industrial-sector conferences in the city.

With no major airport of its own, Ghent's delegate arrivals route through Brussels Airport, roughly 50-60 minutes away by road or a comparable direct rail journey to Gent-Sint-Pieters station, where we provide platform-level meet-and-greet for arrivals by train. During the Gentse Feesten, Belgian Federal Police reporting documents elevated pickpocketing activity around the festival's core waterfront and market areas, and any client event coordination we provide during this period defers to the established municipal and federal crowd management plan rather than an independent security perimeter across streets already carrying exceptional visitor volumes.
