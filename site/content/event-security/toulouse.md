Toulouse holds a unique position in the European event calendar: it is simultaneously a world-class aerospace industrial hub and a university city with an active public political culture. This combination means that event security planning here must address both the controlled, credential-heavy environment of aerospace sector gatherings and the somewhat less predictable conditions created by periodic civil unrest and the Vigipirate Urgence Attentat national terrorism posture. Both dimensions are built into every security plan we deliver for Toulouse events. For the general risk environment and city context, see our [Toulouse city page](/cities/toulouse/).

The aerospace and space sectors dominate the Toulouse corporate event calendar. Airbus delivery events, investor days, and supplier conferences take place throughout the year, many of them on or adjacent to the Airbus campus at Blagnac. These events carry specific security requirements that do not apply to general commercial conferences: embargo management for pre-delivery ceremonies, clearance verification for certain facility areas, and close coordination with Airbus's corporate security function. Our teams are experienced in operating within these constraints and include the client's corporate security protocols in the 72-hour event brief as a standard element. For executives and senior delegates requiring individual close protection during Toulouse engagements, our [Toulouse close protection officers](/close-protection-officers/toulouse/) include operatives with aerospace and defence sector experience who understand the specific discretion requirements of that environment.

The MEETT convention complex at Aussonne, adjacent to Toulouse-Blagnac Airport, has established itself as the venue for large-format events that previously could not have been hosted in Toulouse. Its airport adjacency reduces delegate transfer time and simplifies the vehicle logistics that are otherwise the most complex element of multi-day conference security. Our operations team pre-positions vehicles at the MEETT dedicated drop zone and coordinates directly with the airport's business aviation handling agent for principal-level arrivals. For events in the city centre at Pierre Baudis, the Allee Jean Jaures frontage requires a perimeter plan calibrated to the Vigipirate Urgence Attentat posture: bag screening at all entry points, coordinated radio communications with venue staff, and an immediate-response protocol aligned with the Police Nationale station closest to the venue.
