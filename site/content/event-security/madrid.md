Madrid is Spain's primary city for major trade fairs, international conferences, and political-adjacent corporate events. IFEMA Madrid at Campo de las Naciones hosts a full calendar of large-scale international events; Palacio Municipal de Congresos and hotel conference venues in Salamanca and Recoletos serve the corporate and government sector calendar. Private security in Spain operates under Ley 5/2014 de Seguridad Privada, with the Cuerpo Nacional de Policia (CNP) responsible for registering and regulating all security companies and personnel.

## The security context

Spain's Centro Nacional de Inteligencia (CNI) has maintained the national terrorism threat at Nivel 4 (Alto) continuously since November 2015, the second-highest level on Spain's five-point scale. The 2017 Barcelona vehicle attack, which killed 14 people, confirmed active jihadist operational capability in Spain and triggered a significant review of vehicle-exclusion and hostile-vehicle mitigation standards at major venues across the country. Both the jihadist strand and residual domestic political violence considerations (despite ETA's dissolution in 2018) are factored into Spain's ongoing threat assessment. This makes counter-terrorism planning a professional standard for significant Madrid events rather than an exceptional precaution.

Madrid also has a politically active protest culture. Demonstrations near IFEMA, the Palacio de Cibeles, and hotel venues in the Salamanca and Recoletos areas are not uncommon for events covering financial regulation, housing, energy, or government policy. These are manageable risks with correct planning and access route management, but they require assessment specific to each event's theme rather than a generic security overlay.

## Planning for Madrid events

IFEMA Madrid has a professional in-house security team with long experience of major international trade fairs. The venue has established CNP liaison protocols and its outer perimeter is well-configured for vehicle exclusion. Palacio Municipal de Congresos and hotel conference venues in central Madrid have more variable own-security provision, and independent venue assessment is advisable for high-profile functions at these locations.

For events with identified high-risk or high-profile principals, Escolta Privado-qualified close protection officers provide the required CPO-grade coverage under Ley 5/2014. All companies we work with hold CNP habilitacion empresarial; personnel hold current VS or Escolta Privado licences as appropriate to their role. CNP liaison is managed as a standard part of pre-event planning, ensuring private security and police response are clearly deconflicted and communication channels are established before the event opens.
