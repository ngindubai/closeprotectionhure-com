Verona is one of Italy's most distinctive event cities, defined by two overlapping calendar events that together cover much of the spring, summer, and autumn: Vinitaly, the world-class wine trade fair held at Veronafiere each April, and the Arena di Verona opera season, which runs from June through September and attracts international corporate hospitality events of the highest level. Security planning in Verona must account for the very different crowd and venue characteristics of these two environments -- the high-throughput, multi-pavilion exhibition centre at Veronafiere and the ancient open-air amphitheatre in the historic centre -- as well as the steady stream of wine-sector and luxury industry events that continue throughout the year. For the city's general risk profile, see our [Verona city page](/cities/verona/).

Vinitaly demands a specific operational approach that differs from most trade show security. The international profile of the attendance -- 4,000 exhibitors and 93,000 trade visitors from 140 countries -- means that credential management must operate at high speed across multiple pavilion entry points simultaneously, while still maintaining the separation between trade, press, and VIP access that the event requires. Our Vinitaly operation assigns a team lead to each major pavilion who reports to a central operations coordinator, with vehicle assets available throughout the day for VIP hospitality transfers between the main site and satellite events at Veronese wine estates and hotel venues in the city. Where close protection is required for individual principals attending Vinitaly or the Arena hospitality season, our [Verona close protection officers](/close-protection-officers/verona/) operate in the low-profile civilian style appropriate to the luxury and cultural settings in which these events take place.

Delegate arrivals during Vinitaly require specific advance coordination. Valerio Catullo Airport handles a substantial uplift in passenger volumes during Vinitaly week as charter services augment the scheduled programme; we coordinate with the airport's handling agents in advance and pre-position vehicles at the arrivals exit to avoid the extended queuing that can affect general passengers. Intercontinental delegates arriving via Venice Marco Polo are met at the arrivals hall and escorted directly to vehicles for the A4 transfer to Verona, with a briefing provided en route on any specific conditions affecting the city or the event in the current period. Verona Porta Nuova rail arrivals are met at platform level; the station is noted in the FCDO Italy 2024 advisory as a distraction-theft location and our platform escort service eliminates the period of highest exposure between train exit and vehicle boarding.
