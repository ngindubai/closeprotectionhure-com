Panama City is the financial and logistics hub of Central America, hosting regional headquarters for major multinationals, international banking summits, and a high volume of cross-border trade events. The city's USD economy and Canal Zone infrastructure attract a concentrated corporate calendar. Security planning for events here focuses on organised crime exposure, district differentiation, and the transport corridors between hotels and venues.

## Threat Context for Event Planners

Panama's medium risk classification reflects a clear geographic divide. Punta Pacifica, Punta Paitilla, Miraflores, and San Francisco carry a substantially lower day-to-day crime burden than Calidonia or the Casco Viejo area after dark. Organised crime networks with links to Clan del Golfo operate across the country; while high-profile attacks on corporate venues are uncommon, armed robbery and carjacking on principal transport routes represent a credible exposure that the security plan must address.

The 2022-2023 protest cycle demonstrated how quickly arterial routes in Panama City can be blocked. Any event involving international guests who need guaranteed airport transfers requires contingency transport planning as a standard deliverable.

## Structuring Event Security in Panama City

The security plan for a Panama City corporate event begins with venue selection and district assessment. Licensed operators under the Directorate of Private Security Services provide the personnel framework. For events in Casco Viejo, which has genuine character for evening functions, the access management and principal extraction plan requires additional detail given the street-level dynamics after dark.

Armed provision through licensed Panamanian operators is available and is most commonly requested by financial sector clients and executives with individual threat assessments. The scoping conversation determines whether armed or unarmed provision is appropriate for the specific event profile.

Panama City's role as a regional financial hub means many events involve principals arriving from multiple countries through Tocumen International Airport. Coordinated pre-arrival and post-event transfer planning reduces exposure at the most predictable points of vulnerability.
