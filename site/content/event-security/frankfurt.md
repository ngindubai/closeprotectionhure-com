Frankfurt event security operates under Germany's Trade Regulation Act (Gewerbeordnung) Section 34a, with Hessian oversight from the Hessisches Landeskriminalamt (HLKA), covering principal venues including Messe Frankfurt, Kap Europa Congress Center Frankfurt, and Frankfurt Congress Center. Core service components span credentialled access control, delegate close protection, and transfer management from Frankfurt Airport (FRA) Fraport. Frankfurt's role as the European Central Bank seat and Germany's primary financial hub means many corporate events carry a financial sector or regulatory profile requiring coordination with Bankenviertel security protocols alongside standard Messe venue procedures.

## Germany regulatory framework

All event security personnel at Frankfurt events hold current Section 34a certification under the German Trade Regulation Act (Gewerbeordnung). The Hessian State Criminal Police Office (Hessisches Landeskriminalamt, HLKA) provides oversight at the state level. The Federal Office for the Protection of the Constitution (BfV) assesses the abstract terrorism threat for Germany as high (2026), informing event security planning for large international trade fairs and financial sector gatherings at Messe Frankfurt.
