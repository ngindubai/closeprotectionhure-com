Munich event security operates under Germany's Trade Regulation Act (Gewerbeordnung) Section 34a, with Bavarian oversight from the Bayerisches Landeskriminalamt (BLKA), covering principal venues including the ICM International Congress Centre Munich at Messe Munchen, MOC Event Center Munich, and Gasteig Munich. Core service components span credentialled access control, delegate close protection, and transfer management from Munich Airport (MUC) Franz Josef Strauss. Munich's calendar of major international trade fairs, including Automatica, BAU, and Expo Real, alongside the annual Oktoberfest festival, creates a security planning environment that combines congress logistics with concentrated crowd management requirements.

## Germany regulatory framework

All event security personnel at Munich events hold current Section 34a certification under the German Trade Regulation Act (Gewerbeordnung). The Bavarian State Criminal Police Office (Bayerisches Landeskriminalamt, BLKA) provides oversight at the state level. The Federal Office for the Protection of the Constitution (BfV) assesses the abstract terrorism threat for Germany as high (2026), informing venue security planning for large international gatherings.
