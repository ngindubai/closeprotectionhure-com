Almaty is Kazakhstan's largest city and its primary commercial and conference centre, hosting SCO-related meetings, international business events and diplomatic gatherings at venues including Almaty Arena, the Almaty International Conference Hall and Rahat Palace Hotel. Kazakhstan's Ministry of Internal Affairs (MIA) licences all private security operators, and we engage only MIA-compliant partners for every role in our Almaty deployments. The January 2022 civil unrest, which saw significant incidents in Almaty specifically, is a concrete reference point for crowd management and emergency planning: every event security brief we develop for the city incorporates enhanced protocols that reflect that experience. Our [Almaty city security overview](/cities/almaty/) provides broader context on the security environment and transport infrastructure.

At medium risk level, the planning priorities for Almaty events are structured crowd management, detailed emergency evacuation procedures and close protection for senior delegates. The Almaty Police Directorate is a standard liaison partner for large-scale or diplomatically sensitive events, and pre-event coordination with local authorities is built into all significant briefs. Secure transport from the international airport to conference venues is a baseline recommendation for all international delegates, given the distance from the airport and the importance of maintaining controlled movement from arrival. Our [event security service overview](/services/event-security/) sets out how we integrate close protection, venue security and transport management into a coordinated plan for conference and summit events.
