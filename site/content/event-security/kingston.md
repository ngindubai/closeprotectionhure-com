Kingston is Jamaica's capital and principal business city, host to the Caribbean's financial services, energy, and regional trade event calendar. The Jamaica Pegasus, Knutsford Court, and Jamaica Conference Centre in Kingston Harbour are the primary event venues, all concentrated in the New Kingston and downtown areas. Kingston's corporate event environment is shaped by a significant ambient crime risk: the city has one of the Caribbean's highest murder rates and documented robbery targeting of international visitors, making structured event security planning a professional requirement, not an optional add-on.

## The Kingston event security environment

The FCDO advises against all but essential travel to certain parts of Kingston, particularly the inner-city communities in the west and south, while noting that New Kingston -- the primary hotel and conference district -- is a more manageable environment. The US State Department classifies Jamaica at Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution), specifically noting violent crime. Event security in Kingston must account for the transition zones between the manageable New Kingston conference environment and the higher-risk surrounding areas, and must address transport security for delegates arriving and departing. Source: FCDO Jamaica travel advice (2026); US State Dept Jamaica Level 2 advisory (2026).

## Planning events in Kingston

Venue selection carries significant security implications in Kingston. The New Kingston hotel cluster and the Jamaica Conference Centre are the appropriate venue zones for international corporate events. Outdoor events, community-facing events, and off-plan venues require detailed pre-event assessment. PSRA-licensed operators are engaged for all event security functions, and delegate transport is planned from first contact, not added after arrival. All operators hold current PSRA company and individual licensing.
