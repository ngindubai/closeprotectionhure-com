Hamburg event security operates under Germany's Trade Regulation Act (Gewerbeordnung) Section 34a, with Hamburg-state oversight from the Landeskriminalamt Hamburg (LKA Hamburg), covering principal venues including the CCH Congress Center Hamburg, Hamburg Messe, and Elbphilharmonie. Core service components span credentialled access control, delegate close protection, and transfer management from Hamburg Airport (HAM) Helmut Schmidt. Hamburg's position as Germany's largest port city and a major media and shipping sector hub means many events carry a mixed delegate profile, and the G20 Hamburg Summit (2017) established public-order planning frameworks that continue to inform security coordination with Hamburg Police for large international gatherings.

## Germany regulatory framework

All event security personnel at Hamburg events hold current Section 34a certification under the German Trade Regulation Act (Gewerbeordnung). The Hamburg Criminal Police Office (Landeskriminalamt Hamburg, LKA Hamburg) provides oversight at the state level. The Federal Office for the Protection of the Constitution (BfV) assesses the abstract terrorism threat for Germany as high (2026), which informs venue and transfer security planning for international congresses and public-facing events at Hamburg Messe and the waterfront.
