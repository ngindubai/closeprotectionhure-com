Yaounde is the political capital of Cameroon and the base for the country's diplomatic community, government affairs activity, and international development finance operations. The Hilton Yaounde and Hotel Mont Febe in the Bastos diplomatic district, and the Palais des Congres, are the primary venues for international events. Cameroon's oil sector, its position in the Central African region, and the large AfDB and World Bank country operation all generate a regular international conference calendar in Yaounde. The event security environment reflects Cameroon's high-risk rating, shaped by robbery and kidnapping risk in the city and the broader Anglophone crisis security context.

## The Yaounde event security environment

The FCDO advises against all but essential travel to Cameroon's North-West and South-West regions due to the Anglophone crisis, while rating Yaounde itself as requiring normal precautions with specific crime awareness. The US State Department classifies Cameroon at Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution). Yaounde's Bastos district and hilltop hotel zone are the most manageable environments for international events, but robbery and kidnapping targeting high-profile visitors remain documented risks for individuals transiting between venues and vehicles. Source: FCDO Cameroon travel advice (2026); US State Dept Cameroon Level 2 advisory (2026).

## Planning events in Yaounde

Venue selection for international events in Yaounde centres on the Bastos diplomatic district and the Palais des Congres. MINAT-licensed operators are engaged for all security functions, with pre-engagement credential verification essential given the variability of operator standards in the Yaounde market. Delegate transport planning from Nsimalen Airport (25km) is a required planning element for all international events.
