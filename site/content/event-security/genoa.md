Every September, the Salone Nautico Internazionale turns the waterfront halls of Fiera di Genova into one of the largest boat shows in the world, mooring superyachts alongside the exhibition pavilions and drawing brokers, buyers, and marine industry executives from across Europe. Security planning for an event on this scale has to treat the open water frontage as seriously as the conventional exhibition-hall entrances, since a displayed vessel can be boarded from the quay as readily as a stand can be approached from the aisle. Genoa's other principal event spaces, Porto Antico's Palazzo Ducale for civic and corporate receptions and the Magazzini del Cotone congress centre for smaller conferences, sit within the same waterfront district and share its dependence on the narrow streets of the adjoining Caruggi for pedestrian access, which shapes how a crowd management plan positions its checkpoints.

Maritime and shipping conferences connected to Fincantieri, MSC Mediterranean Shipping Company, and Grimaldi Lines bring a different delegate profile again: senior executives whose commercial discussions warrant separating delegate access from press and trade visitors, and whose itineraries sometimes extend into the working port under Capitaneria di Porto clearance. Delegate arrival is managed through Genoa Cristoforo Colombo Airport, 6km west of the centre, and through Brignole station for rail arrivals from Milan and Turin, with dedicated drivers and GPS tracking assigned to each delegation. Port and transport sector industrial action is a recurring feature of Genoa's operating environment, and FCDO Italy (2024) advises avoiding demonstrations generally; our pre-event intelligence brief checks Questura di Genova public order notifications for the event period so that any planned strike action is factored into arrival timing and route planning well before delegates land.
