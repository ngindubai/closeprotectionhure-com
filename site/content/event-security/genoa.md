Genoa's Fiera di Genova sits directly on the waterfront and each year hosts the Salone Nautico Internazionale, one of the Mediterranean's largest boat shows, drawing yacht manufacturers, brokers and HNWI buyers alongside a broader maritime and shipping industry delegation linked to the port and to Fincantieri's shipbuilding operations. Planning event security here differs from a conventional exhibition hall assignment because much of the show takes place on open-air pontoons alongside high-value vessels moored for the duration, which changes both the perimeter plan and the daytime and overnight protection requirements. For the city's general risk profile, see our [Genoa city page](/cities/genoa/).

The boat show's marina display area brings together yacht owners, manufacturers and brokers for whom discretion matters as much as physical security, and our approach favours a single CPO working in civilian dress across the pavilion and pontoon circuit rather than a more visible team. Where Fincantieri-linked shipbuilding conferences run alongside the boat show, our access control and credential management follows a more conventional business-event model. Principals requiring dedicated coverage during their stay can be supported by our [Genoa close protection officers](/bodyguard-hire/genoa/), briefed specifically on the city's hillside terrain and the narrow Caruggi lanes that shape vehicle routing into and out of the historic centre.

Delegate transfers from Genoa Cristoforo Colombo Airport typically take 15-20 minutes, though Genoa's steep topography means drivers work from a small set of viable arterial routes rather than a conventional grid, and we brief on this for every transfer regardless of how familiar the driver is with the city. During the Salone Nautico, occasional VIP arrivals by sea via Stazione Marittima are met dockside, giving principals arriving this way the same level of coordinated collection as those coming by road or air.
