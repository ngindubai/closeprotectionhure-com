Genoa's event calendar is built around one venue more than any other: [Fiera di Genova](/cities/genoa/), the host of Seatrade Cruise Med and a run of maritime industry trade fairs that draw exhibitors and delegates from MSC Mediterranean Shipping Company, Fincantieri, Grimaldi Lines and the wider shipping sector into a single site. Event security here starts from the fact that these are trade and industry gatherings, not general public events: badge control, exhibitor stand access, and delegate list verification matter more than crowd barriers at a public festival, though the fundamentals of perimeter security and emergency evacuation planning still apply in full.

The second recurring problem is Stazione Marittima. Cruise embarkation and disembarkation days push very high passenger volumes through the port area, and unlicensed transfer operators have been documented approaching arriving passengers there unsolicited. Any event that touches the terminal, whether hosted nearby or drawing delegates who arrive by ship, needs a collection protocol that steers people toward vetted, identifiable transport rather than leaving them to work it out on the quayside. Genoa's steep hillside topography and the narrow Caruggi lane quarter also mean that a delegate transfer from GOA Cristoforo Colombo Airport, or a short hop between hotel and venue, takes longer than it looks on a map, and route timing has to reflect that rather than an optimistic best case.
