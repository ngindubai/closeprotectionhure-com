Security for an event like this is not the same job as securing a general business conference. The attendee list runs to shipowners, port authority officials, and executives connected to MSC, Fincantieri, and Grimaldi Lines, and several of those principals travel with a discretion requirement that a standard credentialing desk doesn't meet. Our venue teams build separate access lanes for exhibitor, press, and VIP credentials from the outset, so a bottleneck at one checkpoint doesn't cascade through the whole event. The city's geography adds a layer that flatter event cities simply don't have. Genoa rises steeply from the port, and any outdoor component of an event, whether a reception near Porto Antico or a delegate walk through the historic centre, has to be planned around gradient, narrow Caruggi lanes, and a transport network that leans on funiculars and lifts as much as roads. It sounds like a logistics footnote until the day a barrier line is placed on a slope nobody checked for wheelchair access. Delegate movement rounds out the plan. Most international arrivals route through Genoa Cristoforo Colombo Airport, roughly 15 to 20 minutes from the centre via the A26, while rail connections from Milan and Turin bring others through Brignole station.
