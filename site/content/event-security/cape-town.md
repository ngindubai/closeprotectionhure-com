Cape Town hosts a growing volume of international conferences and corporate events, with the Cape Town International Convention Centre (CTICC) as the primary venue. The city presents a high-risk environment by regional standards: South Africa's overall crime rates are among the highest in the world, and while the CTICC precinct and city bowl are better controlled, transport routes to outlying hotels and Cape Town International Airport require careful security planning.

Private security operators must hold PSIRA registration under the Private Security Industry Regulation Act (Act 56 of 2001), and major events require coordination with SAPS. Our teams manage both requirements, providing a legally compliant security presence from pre-event planning through to post-event dispersal. Delegate transport, including airport transfers and inter-venue movement, is planned with current SAPS intelligence on protest and crime activity in the city.
