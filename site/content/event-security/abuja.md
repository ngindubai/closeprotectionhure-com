Abuja serves as Nigeria's federal capital and a recurring host city for ECOWAS, African Union and government-adjacent conferences, most commonly at the Transcorp Hilton Abuja, which is the FCT's primary international conference property. The FCDO assesses Nigeria as a high-risk destination, citing terrorism, kidnapping and civil unrest as active threats that apply across the country, including in the FCT. Private security companies operating in Nigeria must be licensed by the Nigeria Security and Civil Defence Corps (NSCDC) under the NSCDC Act 2003, and we engage only registered, licensed local partners for all armed response and guarding roles. For delegates and event organisers, our [Abuja city security overview](/cities/abuja/) provides a fuller picture of the FCT operating environment.

Event security in Abuja requires a threat-aware approach from the earliest planning stages. Secure transport between the international airport and conference hotels is a non-negotiable baseline, given kidnap risk on approach routes. Access control at conference properties must balance openness for large diplomatic delegations with thorough credential verification. Close protection officers working in the FCT must be familiar with the local security architecture and maintain active liaison with Nigerian authorities.
