Georgetown is the capital of Guyana and, since the discovery and development of the Stabroek offshore oil block, has become one of the fastest-growing business event destinations in the Caribbean and South American region. ExxonMobil, Hess, SBM Offshore, and the international finance institutions now bring major conferences and investment forums to the Marriott and Pegasus hotel venues. Georgetown's event security environment reflects this rapid transformation: significant wealth and international executive visibility in a city that carries elevated robbery, violent crime, and kidnapping risk.

## The Georgetown event security environment

The FCDO advises against all but essential travel to several areas of Georgetown due to violent crime, while noting that the main hotel districts in Kingston and Prashad Nagar are the most manageable areas for international visitors. The US State Department classifies Guyana at Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution). Georgetown's main event security risk categories are robbery targeting high-value visitors, opportunistic crime at hotel approaches and venue exteriors, and the kidnapping risk that has increased in the context of Guyana's oil wealth concentration. Source: FCDO Guyana travel advice (2026); US State Dept Guyana Level 2 advisory (2026).

## Planning events in Georgetown

Venue selection in Georgetown is effectively constrained to the Marriott and Pegasus hotel venues and the Kingston Seawall area for international corporate events. Vetted security driver transport for all delegate movements is the standard baseline, given the 41km airport transfer and Georgetown's ambient crime level. Operator licensing must be verified before engagement, as the rapid expansion of Georgetown's event market has attracted operators with variable compliance standards.
