Rennes has built a genuine congress trade around its telecoms, technology, and agri-food sectors, and the Couvent des Jacobins gives the city a convention venue capable of hosting large multi-day events. Event security here means two things running side by side: standard venue and delegate protection, and a live awareness of Rennes' demonstration culture, which is not a rare occurrence but a recurring feature of the city's civic life. Our teams plan for both from the outset rather than treating protest risk as an afterthought. Background on the wider city context sits on our [Rennes city page](/cities/rennes/).

The demonstration point deserves its own emphasis. Place de la Republique and Place du Parlement de Bretagne are both regular assembly points and, not coincidentally, close to the hotels and venues used for corporate hospitality in Rennes. The 2023 pension reform protests are the clearest recent example of how quickly this can escalate, and any event scheduled during a period of national social policy debate should assume some probability of disruption rather than treat it as unlikely. Contingency routing, built from the demonstration calendar rather than guesswork, is standard on every booking.
