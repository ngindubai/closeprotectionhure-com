Abu Dhabi is a major destination for energy sector conferences, sovereign wealth and financial summits, and international defence exhibitions. The emirate's event security industry operates under Federal Law No. 37 of 2006 on Private Security Services, with the Ministry of Interior (MoI) responsible for licensing security companies and approving personnel qualifications. Abu Dhabi Police provides strong local coordination and has established liaison protocols for major events at ADNEC and Yas Island.

## The security context

The UK FCDO rates the UAE overall as having a low crime risk, and Abu Dhabi's ambient security environment is among the most stable of any major conference city in the region. The primary security consideration for corporate event planners is not crime but the elevated regional threat context. In January 2022, Houthi drone and missile strikes targeted Abu Dhabi, with impacts reported near the ADNEC area and Mussafah industrial zone. The UAE government responded with a significant upgrade to air defence and venue protective measures. Event emergency planning for Abu Dhabi must reflect these post-2022 standards rather than earlier baselines.

The energy and defence character of Abu Dhabi's event calendar creates a specific protection requirement. ADIPEC (Abu Dhabi International Petroleum Exhibition and Conference) and IDEX (International Defence Exhibition) draw CEO-level principals, senior government ministers, and sovereign wealth fund representatives in high concentration. The profile of attendees at these events means close protection for identified principals is a standard professional practice rather than an exceptional measure. Abu Dhabi Police maintains a strong visible presence at major international gatherings and has well-developed coordination protocols for security companies.

## Planning for Abu Dhabi events

Abu Dhabi National Exhibition Centre (ADNEC) is well-equipped for large-scale international security requirements and has hosted events with significant principal protection requirements on multiple occasions. ADNEC's in-house security function provides a strong baseline; our assessment confirms whether existing provision is calibrated for the specific event's attendee profile, and identifies where supplementary principal protection or access control management is appropriate.

Hotel venues at Emirates Palace, Jumeirah at Etihad Towers, and Yas Island conference facilities provide strong access control infrastructure. For high-profile private functions at these venues, principal-specific credentialling, secure vehicle drop-off management, and a written emergency response plan that coordinates with Abu Dhabi Police are standard operational additions. All operators we deploy hold valid MoI licences under Federal Law No. 37 of 2006, and manage advance ADP liaison as part of the pre-event planning process.
