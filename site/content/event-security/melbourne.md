Melbourne hosts some of the Southern Hemisphere's most prominent sporting and cultural events, drawing large international audiences to venues including the MCG, Rod Laver Arena and the Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre. Victoria's Security Industry Act 1997 sets a clear licensing framework for all private security operatives, with Consumer Affairs Victoria as the licensing authority. Every crowd controller and security guard we deploy in Melbourne holds a current Consumer Affairs Victoria licence, and our event security plans are developed in coordination with Victoria Police event planning teams. For a fuller picture of Melbourne's security environment, see our [Melbourne city security overview](/cities/melbourne/).

Counter-terrorism emergency planning is a standard component of our Melbourne event briefs. ASIO's Probable threat rating (2024) means that detailed response protocols, venue hardening assessments and hostile vehicle mitigation considerations are built into all large-event security plans from the outset, not treated as optional extras. The Formula 1 Australian Grand Prix, Australian Open and Melbourne International Film Festival each present distinct crowd management and access control challenges that require tailored rather than generic security plans. Our approach integrates venue-specific risk assessments with the broader guidance from our [event security service overview](/services/event-security/), ensuring that staffing, zoning and emergency communication are appropriate to the scale and profile of each event.
