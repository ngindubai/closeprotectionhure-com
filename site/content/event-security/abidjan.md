Abidjan is West Africa's principal commercial city and a recurring host for ECOWAS summits and large international conferences at Hotel Ivoire and Sofitel Abidjan. The FCDO advises heightened awareness of terrorism risk in Ivory Coast, linked to instability in the Sahel region to the north, and this assessment shapes every event security plan we develop for the city. Private security operators in Abidjan are licensed by the Ministere de l'Interieur, and we engage only verified, licensed local partners for armed response and close protection deployments. For delegates planning to attend conferences in Abidjan, our [Abidjan city security overview](/cities/abidjan/) sets out the broader threat environment and transport considerations.

Effective event security in a high-risk city requires planning to begin well before the event itself. Venue hardening assessments, route reconnaissance, delegate arrival coordination and pre-event briefings for the event management team are all part of a standard Abidjan brief. Access control and credentialing at conference properties must account for large media contingents and government delegations with differing security requirements. Our [event security service overview](/services/event-security/) describes how we structure security operations for international conferences and diplomatic gatherings, and how close protection integrates with fixed venue security to reduce risk for high-profile attendees.
