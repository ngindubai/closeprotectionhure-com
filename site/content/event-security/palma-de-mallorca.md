Palma de Mallorca's event work does not look like a conventional conference city's, and it should not be planned as one. The bookings that come our way here are private villa functions, superyacht arrival events at Real Club Nautico de Palma or STP Shipyard Palma, and corporate or HNWI gatherings up at Son Vida, all of which call for a different kind of discretion than a credentialed trade fair does.

Spain's Ley de Seguridad Privada 5/2014 sets the licensing framework, with firms registered through the SES and officers carrying a TIP from the national police. Most guests arrive through Palma de Mallorca Airport, with a further share coming via the cruise terminal, and FCDO guidance on Spain (2024) is specific that organised distraction-theft teams work the busy tourist concentrations around that terminal and the cathedral-Born core, a detail that shapes our transport routing away from peak congestion. See the [Palma de Mallorca city page](/cities/palma-de-mallorca/) for the fuller risk picture, including guidance on evening drink-spiking incidents.

Recurring anti-tourism demonstrations since 2024, run under the banner Menys Turisme, Mes Vida, have brought temporary road closures that we check against any event date in advance. None of this changes the island's fundamentally low overall risk profile; it simply means logistics, not threat level, drive most of the planning conversation.
