San Salvador is the capital of El Salvador and has undergone a significant security transformation since the Bukele government's gang suppression programme beginning in 2022. The dramatic reduction in gang activity has repositioned San Salvador from a high-risk to a medium-risk environment for international business visitors, with the Escalon, Zona Rosa, and Santa Elena districts providing a manageable event hosting environment. International investor activity -- driven in part by El Salvador's Bitcoin legal tender status and its active pursuit of foreign direct investment -- has increased the demand for structured event security in San Salvador.

## The San Salvador event security environment

The FCDO currently advises normal travel precautions for most of El Salvador while acknowledging the significant security improvements under the Bukele administration. The US State Department classifies El Salvador at Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution). Residual crime risk remains, particularly outside the main business districts, and the underlying structural factors that generated El Salvador's previous gang problem have not been fully resolved. Event security planning remains necessary, but at a substantially lower level of intervention than was required before 2022. Source: FCDO El Salvador travel advice (2026); US State Dept El Salvador Level 2 advisory (2026).

## Planning events in San Salvador

Venue selection for international corporate events in San Salvador centres on the Hilton Princess, Sheraton Presidente, and CIFCO in the western business districts. PNC-registered operators are engaged for event security functions. The 44km airport transfer from Romero International Airport requires pre-arranged vetted transport. For events with international investor profiles, security planning is integrated with communications security for commercially sensitive proceedings.
