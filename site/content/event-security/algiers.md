Algiers is a working venue for government-adjacent trade events and international business meetings, most notably the Foire Internationale d'Alger at the Palais des Expositions, which draws exhibitors and delegations from across North Africa and the wider Mediterranean region. Algeria's Ministry of Interior licences private security companies operating in the country, and we engage only registered, licensed local operators for all guarding and close protection roles. The FCDO advises a high degree of vigilance in Algeria, citing residual terrorism risk, and this advisory shapes the threat-aware planning framework we apply to every Algiers event brief. For a fuller picture of the operating environment, see our [Algiers city security overview](/cities/algiers/).

Corporate events in Algiers require careful attention to venue selection and pre-event hardening, since the city's international hotel infrastructure is more limited than comparable North African capitals, and not all conference properties offer equivalent perimeter security standards. Close protection for senior executives attending trade events is recommended at medium-high risk level, and coordination with the DGSN is standard practice for diplomatically sensitive events. Our [event security service overview](/services/event-security/) sets out the planning methodology we apply to conferences and trade events in elevated-risk cities, covering venue assessment, access control, close protection integration and emergency response planning.
