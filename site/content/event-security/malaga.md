Malaga runs two distinct event calendars, and treating them as one leads to under-planning either side. PalacioFeria hosts the corporate congress and trade-event traffic that increasingly follows the city's tech-sector growth, while Feria de Malaga each August turns the fairground and historic centre into one of Spain's largest urban festivals for over a week. Both need advance planning, but the planning itself looks nothing alike: a congress needs credential lanes and delegate transport; Feria needs crowd-density management and realistic movement-time briefings for anyone attending during peak hours. For background on the city's broader risk profile, see our [Malaga city page](/cities/malaga/).

West along the coast, the picture shifts again. Marbella and Puerto Banus host private HNWI functions where discretion, not crowd control, is the operating priority: villa events integrated with the property's existing perimeter, marina receptions where guests arrive by both boat and car, and static guarding at higher-value functions. Officers working these events dress for the setting rather than the office, and close protection for individual hosts or keynote speakers runs alongside the general event security detail rather than as an afterthought. Getting delegates and guests to any of these venues safely starts at Malaga-Costa del Sol Airport (AGP), 8km southwest of the city, where confirmed inside-terminal collection avoids the crowded arrivals concourse that FCDO-documented petty theft tends to concentrate around.
