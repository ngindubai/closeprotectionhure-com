Malaga's event calendar runs from formal congress and trade fair business at the Palacio de Ferias y Congresos de Malaga through to the technology sector conferences hosted at Malaga TechPark, and on to the very different demands of Feria de Malaga each August, when the city's historic centre and fairground districts absorb some of the highest sustained crowd density in Andalusia. Planning event security across this range means adapting between conventional congress access control, smaller high-value delegate-list verification for tech sector events, and municipal-scale crowd coordination during the festival period. For the city's general risk profile, see our [Malaga city page](/cities/malaga/).

FYCMA's congress and trade fair programme calls for credential lane separation between trade, press and VIP visitors from the earliest queue point, while Malaga TechPark's product launch and partnership events, tied to companies such as Google, Oracle and Vodafone, involve smaller but more senior delegate lists where access-list verification matters more than volume management. Principals attending either type of event, or private functions along the Costa del Sol, can be supported by our [Malaga close protection officers](/bodyguard-hire/malaga/), who work in the low-profile civilian style appropriate to Spanish business and luxury hospitality settings.

Arrivals through Malaga-Costa del Sol Airport are typically a 15-20 minute transfer to the city centre, extending to 40-60 minutes for events further along the coast towards Marbella, and we coordinate directly with the airport's handling agents ahead of major congresses to pre-position vehicles at the arrivals kerb. During Feria de Malaga, our planning for any client event defers to the established municipal and Policia Nacional crowd management measures already in place across the city, rather than attempting to run an independent perimeter through what is one of the region's largest annual festivals.
