FYCMA, the Palacio de Ferias y Congresos de Malaga, fills its exhibition halls with technology conferences, trade fairs, and corporate hospitality throughout the year, and it sits alongside a second, very different event circuit forty minutes west on the Costa del Sol: the marina and yacht-industry showcases centred on Puerto Banus. Event security in Malaga has to move between these two environments, plus a third, the red-carpet premieres of the Festival de Malaga each spring, without treating any of them as a generic event template. A TechPark investor day calls for accreditation discipline and non-disclosure awareness among staff; a marina showcase calls for a lower-visibility presence suited to a resort setting; a film premiere calls for public-facing crowd and media management. All event security providers operating in Malaga must hold current registration with the Secretaria de Estado de Seguridad under the Ley de Seguridad Privada 5/2014, and any officer providing close protection to a VIP delegate must separately hold a Tarjeta de Identidad Profesional.

Delegate logistics for a Malaga event usually start at Malaga-Costa del Sol Airport (AGP), 8km southwest of the city centre with a standard transfer of 15 to 20 minutes into FYCMA or the principal city-centre hotels. Events that also draw delegates from along the coast extend transfer planning to the AP-7 and A-7 corridors toward Marbella and Puerto Banus, where journey times run 45 to 60 minutes. FCDO Spain travel advice (2024) records pickpocketing and bag-snatching as the principal documented risks in Malaga's tourist areas, information that feeds directly into the crowd management plan for any event held during the historic centre's peak footfall periods, including the citywide congestion produced by the August Feria de Malaga. For a broader view of these risks across the city, see the [Malaga city security guide](/cities/malaga/).

Clients whose event includes a named principal, a keynote speaker, a festival guest, or a marina brokerage figure, rather than only general delegate attendance, typically request close protection alongside the venue and crowd security described here. See [close protection officers in Malaga](/close-protection-officers/malaga/) for the credential and deployment detail behind that cover, and [secure airport transfers in Malaga](/secure-airport-transfers/malaga/) for how delegate arrivals are staged from AGP into the event itself. Every Malaga event booking includes a written security brief issued at least 72 hours before the first arrival, covering venue-specific emergency routing, the current FCDO advisory position, and the nearest appropriate hospital, Hospital Regional Universitario de Malaga for city-centre events or Hospital Costa del Sol in Marbella for events further along the coast.
