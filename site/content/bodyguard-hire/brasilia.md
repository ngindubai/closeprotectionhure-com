Brasilia is Brazil's federal capital and the centre of government and diplomatic activity in South America's largest country. FCDO Brazil 2026 rates the city at Level 2 (exercise caution) and it records lower violent crime than Rio de Janeiro and Sao Paulo, but the carjacking risk at semaforos, peripheral city crime, and the Rodoviaria district's elevated street crime mean that professional DPF-registered close protection is recommended for senior executive and HNWI principals visiting the capital.

## Operating in Brasilia

The Plano Piloto core - the Ministerial Esplanade, Embassy Quarter, Asa Norte and Asa Sul - is where most corporate and diplomatic meetings take place. Lago Sul and Lago Norte provide the upscale residential base for visiting HNWI principals. Movement between these areas and the city periphery requires carjacking-aware driving and pre-surveyed routes.

## Licensing and operator selection

All close protection operators in Brasilia must hold DPF registration under Federal Law 7.102/1983. For transport cover and full security planning, see our [Brasilia city security briefing](/cities/brasilia/) and [security drivers in Brasilia](/security-drivers/brasilia/).
