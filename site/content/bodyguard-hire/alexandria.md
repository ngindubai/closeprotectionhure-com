Alexandria's threat profile sits apart from Cairo's in one important respect: two documented shootings, in May 2023 and October 2024, targeted individuals with Israeli association in the city itself, not in the Sinai zones that dominate Egypt's national terrorism picture. FCDO travel advice records both incidents. For any itinerary with an Israeli connection, that means a specific threat assessment rather than a generic Egypt briefing, whatever else the trip involves.

Most Alexandria business visits combine a commercial purpose, often tied to the city's refining and petrochemical industry or its port trade, with a shorter cultural stop at the Bibliotheca Alexandrina, Qaitbay Citadel, or Pompey's Pillar. The two environments carry different rules: port and industrial-site access operates under strict photography restrictions and active security checks, while the Corniche and historic core are open, pedestrian-friendly, and comparatively relaxed. A close protection detail working Alexandria needs to shift posture between the two within a single day, which is a different operational rhythm from a Cairo-only trip.

Licensing runs through Egypt's Ministry of Interior with no Alexandria-specific variation, and armed protection stays restricted to government-authorised work, so commercial details operate unarmed with police liaison as standard. Getting to Alexandria usually means flying into Cairo first: Borg El Arab (HBE) has limited scheduled international service, so the desert road, roughly 220km and 2.5 to 3 hours from Cairo, or the rail link, is the practical route for most principals, and a CPO collecting from Cairo airport keeps the whole overland leg under direct protective control.
