Nassau is the capital of the Bahamas and one of the Caribbean's primary HNWI leisure and private banking destinations, combining resort infrastructure at Atlantis Paradise Island and Albany with a busy commercial centre around Bay Street. The FCDO Bahamas travel advice (2024) records violent crime including armed robbery and gang activity concentrated in residential areas south of Downtown Nassau, creating a moderate risk backdrop against which close protection for high-profile visitors is a sensible precaution rather than an emergency response.

CPO cover in Nassau is calibrated to the HNWI resort environment: discreet, appropriately dressed officers managing principal movement between the Atlantis complex, Paradise Island waterfront, Albany, Lyford Cay, and Downtown venues. Lynden Pindling International Airport (NAS), 16km from central Nassau, is the primary arrival and departure point; transfers between the airport and resort accommodation are the highest-exposure moments of most itineraries. The Bahamas Private Security Licensing Act Cap. 199 and BSIRA licensing framework apply to all operators and individual officers; armed cover requires RBPF authorisation.
