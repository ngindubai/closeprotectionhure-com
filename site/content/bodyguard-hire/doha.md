Doha is a low-risk, high-infrastructure city with a mature executive protection market developed, in part, around its 2022 FIFA World Cup preparations. The Ministry of Interior (MoI) licensing framework under Ministerial Decree No. 11 of 2006 governs all private security activity in Qatar. For most international principals, CPO cover in Doha is driven by the principal's own threat profile, corporate governance requirements, or the scale and sensitivity of events attended rather than the city's inherent risk level.

## The Qatari licensing framework

All commercial security operators in Qatar must hold MoI licensing under Ministerial Decree No. 11 of 2006. Foreign operators must work through a Qatari-registered partner. Individual personnel require MoI approval. The post-2022 World Cup investment in event security has raised the baseline capability of Doha's licensed operator market.

## Operational considerations in Doha

Qatar's legal framework, including restrictions on alcohol and public behaviour codes, requires specific CPO briefing before deployment. The West Bay business district, Pearl-Qatar, and Lusail City are the primary operational environments for HNWI and corporate principals.
