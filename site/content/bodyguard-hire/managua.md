Managua is Nicaragua's capital and the centre of a country under the political control of the Ortega administration, which has systematically dismantled civil society, imprisoned political opponents and clergy, and built a state surveillance infrastructure that affects the operating environment for all international visitors. Conventional crime risk in Managua is lower than in neighbouring Honduras or Guatemala, but the political environment creates a distinct set of risks - surveillance, arbitrary detention, restricted movement - that require a specifically calibrated security approach.

## The security environment

The FCDO advises exercising a high degree of caution in Nicaragua as of 2026, citing political repression, arbitrary detention risk, and restricted civil liberties. The US State Department rates Nicaragua at Level 3 (Reconsider Travel). The IACHR/CIDH has documented systematic human rights violations since the April 2018 crackdown and continuing imprisonment of political, religious, and civil society figures. For business visitors, the risk of state surveillance is the primary operational consideration; for visitors with any civil society or political connections, the risk of arbitrary detention is a real planning variable. Source: FCDO Nicaragua travel advice (2026); US State Dept Nicaragua advisory (2026); IACHR/CIDH Nicaragua reports (2024).

## What authorised CP covers in Managua

A National Police-authorised Managua CPO programme covers: vetted vehicle transfer from Sandino International Airport; low-profile protection for business meetings in the Carretera Masaya corridor; hotel security arrangements; communications security awareness; and emergency response coordination with the British Embassy and private medical providers. Security posture is calibrated to the political risk profile of the specific visit.
