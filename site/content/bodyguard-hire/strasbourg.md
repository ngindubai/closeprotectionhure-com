Strasbourg is the one city in this network where a client will ask, directly, why the risk rating sits above most of its French peers. The honest answer: a documented terrorist attack on the Christmas Market in 2018, and a genuine institutional target profile that comes with hosting the European Parliament and Council of Europe. That combination shapes bodyguard hire here more than it does in most regional French cities.

CNAPS licenses every firm and officer working in the Bas-Rhin department under the Code de la securite interieure, and armed protection stays a separate, rarely used authorisation. What's different in Strasbourg is that a close protection detail also needs to coordinate directly with institutional security during Parliament session weeks, when the city carries an additional security perimeter around its European quarter. Route planning routes around Neuhof and Hautepierre as a matter of course, both peripheral districts with documented elevated crime, in favour of Petite France and the Neustadt for daytime movement and accommodation.

The Christkindelsmarik Christmas Market brings roughly two million visitors a year through the Grande Ile across 300-plus chalets, a dense-crowd environment that calls for a different set of protocols than institutional access management. For most visitors during that period, a vetted driver and standard counter-theft awareness cover the practical risk; close protection is reserved for senior principals whose profile, or whose timing, intersects with Strasbourg's documented history.
