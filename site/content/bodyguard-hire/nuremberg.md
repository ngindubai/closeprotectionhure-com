Spielwarenmesse, by its own organisers' account the world's biggest toy trade fair, brings around 2,800 exhibitors from roughly 60 countries and 67,000 trade visitors through NürnbergMesse every year. That single event, alongside BrauBeviale and Consumenta on the same calendar, gives Nuremberg a trade-fair footprint most cities its size simply don't have, and it's the main driver behind corporate bodyguard hire here: hall-to-hall movement, exhibitor meetings, and coordination with a venue security operation running across hundreds of thousands of square metres.

There's a second, quieter stream of visitors too. The Palace of Justice is still a working Bavarian courthouse, and its former Courtroom 600, now the Memorium Nuremberg Trials memorial, sits in the same complex, close to the city-run Documentation Centre Nazi Party Rally Grounds. Institutional and diplomatic visits here run on a completely different rhythm from a trade fair, scheduled and protocol-heavy rather than crowd-managed, and a detail built for NürnbergMesse doesn't automatically transfer across.

Licensing follows the national German pattern: Section 34a of the Gewerbeordnung, the Bewachungsverordnung, and a mandatory IHK Sachkundeprufung for every operative since January 2021. Armed authorisation stays rare under the Waffengesetz, so unarmed protection with Bavarian Police liaison is standard practice. Bavaria posts among the lowest crime rates of any German state, though the Hauptbahnhof and Konigstrasse corridor see more petty-crime activity than the city average, particularly during the Christkindlesmarkt from late November to late December.
