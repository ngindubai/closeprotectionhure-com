The city operates under a HIGH terrorism threat assessment by the CNI, with the 2004 Madrid train bombings (191 killed) and 2017 Barcelona attack remaining relevant context for any security planning. Bodyguard hire in Madrid is regulated by Ley de Seguridad Privada (Ley 5/2014), with the DGP-issued Escolta privado habilitación the mandatory qualification for commercial CPO work.

## The Spanish licensing framework

Ley 5/2014 establishes both the company licensing and individual accreditation requirements for private security in Spain. The DGP (Dirección General de la Policía) issues the Escolta privado habilitación after candidates complete a minimum 150-hour approved training programme. Armed operations require an additional firearms qualification. The DGP register is the authoritative source for verifying whether an individual or company is licensed to operate in Spain.

The CNI HIGH terrorism threat and Madrid's history as a target for mass-casualty attacks define the terrorism awareness element of CPO briefings.
