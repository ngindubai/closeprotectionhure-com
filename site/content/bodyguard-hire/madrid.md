## The Ley 5/2014 Escolta Privado licensing framework

Ley 5/2014 de Seguridad Privada established Escolta Privado as a distinct, regulated professional category in Spain, requiring individual CNP habilitación for all commercial close protection officers. Both the individual CPO and the operating company must hold current CNP-issued licences; neither can operate lawfully without the other. The publicly verifiable habilitación system means clients can confirm the regulatory status of both the company and the deployed CPO before the engagement commences.
