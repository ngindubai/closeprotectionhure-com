Belize City is the commercial and financial hub of Belize, hosting the country's main port, the majority of its banking and commercial operations, and the primary international point of entry. It is also one of the region's more challenging security environments: with a murder rate of approximately 38 per 100,000 in 2022 per Belize Police Department data, specific gang territorial control on the south side of the city, and FCDO and US State Department specific warnings for that area, Belize City requires active security management for business visitors rather than ordinary tourist precautions.

## The security environment

The FCDO advises exercising a high degree of caution in Belize as of 2026, with specific warnings for the south side of Belize City. The US State Department's Level 3 designation for the south side - a step above its Level 2 national rating - reflects the concentrated gang violence in that area. Drug trafficking through Belize as a transit corridor funds the armed criminal networks operating in the city. The Fort George district on the north-east peninsula, which hosts most international hotels and embassy compounds, has a materially better risk profile but remains within a high-risk overall environment. Source: FCDO Belize travel advice (2026); US State Dept Belize advisory (2026); Belize Police Dept crime statistics (2022).

## What licensed CP covers in Belize City

A Ministry of National Security-licensed Belize City CPO programme covers: vetted vehicle collection from Philip S.W. Goldson International Airport; protection for business meetings and port/commercial visits in and around the Fort George district; hotel pre-advance; and emergency response coordination with the British High Commission and Karl Heusner Memorial Hospital. Visits involving south-side port or industrial facilities require enhanced armed team configuration.
