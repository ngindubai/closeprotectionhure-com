## The Johannesburg threat environment

Johannesburg has one of the highest violent crime rates of any major business destination globally. The South African Police Service publishes detailed quarterly statistics; the most recent figures show that Gauteng province continues to record elevated rates of murder, aggravated robbery, and carjacking compared with most international comparators. The FCDO and US State Department both maintain advisories that reflect this picture without advising against travel to Johannesburg.

The specific risks that drive close protection demand in Johannesburg are: armed robbery (including in residential and parking-deck contexts), carjacking (particularly at red lights and on the journey to and from OR Tambo), kidnapping for ransom (less common than in Lagos but documented), and home invasion of the residences and gated communities where senior executives stay. None of this prevents successful corporate operations in Johannesburg. It is precisely why a mature private security industry exists.

## The PSIRA framework

The Private Security Industry Regulatory Authority (PSIRA) is the South African statutory body that licenses all commercial private security personnel under the Private Security Industry Regulation Act 2001. PSIRA grades range from E (entry) to A (close protection and senior security management). Companies must hold a separate operating licence, and armed personnel must hold an additional Firearms Control Act 2000 competency certificate.

For clients, the verification step is straightforward. Ask for the PSIRA registration number of each officer proposed for your detail, and the operating company's licence number. Confirm that the registrations are current. The PSIRA registry is publicly verifiable.
