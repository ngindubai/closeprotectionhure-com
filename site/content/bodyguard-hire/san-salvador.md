San Salvador has undergone one of the most dramatic documented security transformations of any Latin American capital in the past decade. El Salvador's murder rate has fallen from among the highest in the world to a level comparable with many Western European capitals following President Bukele's Regimen de Excepcion from March 2022. For international business visitors, this means that San Salvador now presents a medium-high rather than extreme risk environment - a material improvement - but one that still requires active security management rather than the passive precautions adequate for low-risk destinations.

## The security environment

The FCDO advises exercising a high degree of caution in El Salvador as of 2026, and the US State Department rates El Salvador at Level 2 (Exercise Increased Caution). The Regimen de Excepcion has created a more complex security environment in which gang violence has diminished substantially but government surveillance of civil society, journalists, and foreign visitors perceived as critical of the administration has become a new operational consideration. Some districts retain residual risk profiles. The Zona Rosa, San Benito, and Colonia Escalon areas provide a stable operational environment for most business visit programmes. Source: FCDO El Salvador travel advice (2026); PNC statistics (2023); US State Dept El Salvador advisory (2026).

## What licensed CP covers in San Salvador

A Ministry of Justice-licensed San Salvador CPO programme covers: vetted vehicle collection from Romero International Airport; protection during Zona Rosa and Escalon business meetings; hotel pre-advance; and emergency response coordination with the British Embassy and private medical facilities. Counter-surveillance awareness is built into the operational brief for all visits given the monitoring environment.
