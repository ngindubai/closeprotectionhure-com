Panama City's position as Central America's primary financial centre, combined with its role as the gateway to the Panama Canal and the Colon Free Zone, brings a steady flow of senior executives, legal professionals and international business principals through the city. The medium risk rating reflects a manageable but real threat picture: organised crime networks including Clan del Golfo affiliates operate in border areas, petty crime including bag snatching is documented in Casco Viejo and Calidonia, and armed robbery and carjacking are active concerns across parts of the city.

Panama's Directorate of Private Security Services (DGSP), operating under the Ministry of Government, licenses all security operators and provides a more developed regulatory framework than most Central American neighbours. Armed close protection is available through locally licensed operators, and foreign companies work through Panamanian-licensed entities. This structure is standard for all our Panama City engagements and is coordinated in advance of deployment.

The Darien province border represents a categorically different risk environment. FCDO advises against all travel to Darien, and our teams do not deploy to that area under standard close protection parameters without a prior specialist threat review. For principals requiring any movements in the direction of the Colombian border, early engagement allows appropriate planning.

Protest activity is a recurring operational consideration. The 2022 and 2023 civil unrest, which saw the Pan-American Highway blocked for extended periods, demonstrated that ground transport disruption can affect even otherwise straightforward itineraries. Our teams carry current knowledge of protest patterns and maintain alternative routing for all principal movements.
