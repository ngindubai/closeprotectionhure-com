Guatemala City is the political, economic, and commercial capital of Central America's most populous country, and hosts the regional headquarters of numerous multinational organisations. It is also one of the region's most demanding security environments for business visitors: kidnapping targeting executives and foreign nationals is an active risk, gang networks with cartel connections operate throughout the metropolitan area, and corruption in security institutions means that official protection cannot substitute for private close protection arrangements.

## The security environment

The FCDO advises exercising a high degree of caution throughout Guatemala as of 2026, with zone-specific warnings for areas of Guatemala City. The US State Department rates Guatemala at Level 3 (Reconsider Travel). MS-13 and Barrio 18 gang structures maintain territorial presence in residential zones surrounding the commercial districts. Kidnapping - both planned abduction for ransom and express kidnapping - is documented and actively targeting the business community. Cartel-linked narcotics trafficking networks operating through Guatemala add a layer of organised criminal activity that compounds the gang-level threat. Rule-of-law weakness, evidenced by high impunity rates, means that official response alone is not a reliable security mechanism. Source: FCDO Guatemala travel advice (2026); US State Dept Guatemala advisory (2026).

## What DIGESSP-registered CP covers in Guatemala City

A DIGESSP-registered Guatemala City CPO programme covers: inside-terminal collection from La Aurora International Airport; vetted vehicle movement to the Zona 10 operational base; protection for business meetings and embassy visits; hotel pre-advance; and emergency response coordination with the British Embassy and private medical facilities. Armed capability is standard for principal-level visits.
