Two names explain most of what bodyguard hire in Stuttgart is actually about: Mercedes-Benz, headquartered in Untertürkheim, and Porsche, in Zuffenhausen. The BfV has flagged Germany's automotive sector as an active target for industrial espionage more than once, and that assessment shapes protective work here more than street crime does.

Section 34a of the Gewerbeordnung and the Bewachungsverordnung govern the sector, with officers needing a Sachkundeprüfung certificate through IHK Region Stuttgart before taking on higher-risk protective work. A private Waffenschein is hard to obtain, so armed cover is rare in practice; unarmed protection paired with device and meeting security briefings is the standard for automotive sector visits. Stuttgart Hauptbahnhof remains a long-running Stuttgart 21 construction site, and it's the city's most consistent location for opportunistic theft, alongside the busy Konigstrasse shopping precinct.

Messe Stuttgart sits right beside the airport, about 12km south of the centre, which sounds convenient until a major exhibition like AMB or Blechexpo fills both road and rail approaches at once. The Cannstatter Volksfest, one of Germany's largest folk festivals after Munich's Oktoberfest, brings a similar seasonal congestion problem to Bad Cannstatt. Neither requires a large security footprint, just a driver who plans around it rather than into it.
