Roughly 450,000 square metres and a workforce of over 4,000: that's the scale of Airbus's second-largest German production site, sitting in Bremen alongside a port complex, shared with Bremerhaven, that ranks among Europe's largest. Between aerospace and maritime logistics, those two operations generate most of the demand for bodyguard hire in the city.

Section 34a of the Gewerbeordnung and the Bewachungsverordnung apply here as everywhere in Germany, but Bremen's status as a city-state means operators register through the Handelskammer Bremen rather than a regional IHK, a small but genuine procedural difference worth knowing before booking a firm. Armed protection stays rare, gated behind a Waffenschein that's hard to obtain commercially, so unarmed cover paired with strict site-access coordination is the norm for both the Airbus plant and the port terminals.

Street-level risk concentrates in two places: Bahnhofsvorstadt, around the Hauptbahnhof, with a longstanding drug scene and consistent opportunistic theft, and Gröpelingen, a western district with elevated property crime that corporate visitors have no real reason to enter. Neither changes the overall picture much. Bremen is a low-risk city, and most engagements come down to a vetted driver covering the airport transfer and, where the assignment stretches north to Bremerhaven, the roughly 65km run up the A27 to the overseas port.
