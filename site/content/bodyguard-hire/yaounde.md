Yaounde is the political capital of Cameroon and the seat of government, hosting the presidency, national assembly, most ministry headquarters, and the majority of embassy compounds. It is the operational base for international visits related to Cameroon's government, extractive industries, and the significant international NGO and humanitarian sector responding to the country's multiple conflict crises. Yaounde sits within a national security context rated by both the FCDO and US State Department as requiring significant caution, with three regional conflicts - the Far North (Boko Haram), the Northwest and Southwest (Anglophone crisis) - creating pressures that reach the capital even though Yaounde itself is not in an active conflict zone.

## The security environment

The FCDO advises a high degree of caution for Yaounde and against all travel to the Far North, Northwest, and Southwest regions of Cameroon as of 2026. The US State Department rates Cameroon at Level 3 with Level 4 for the three conflict regions. In Yaounde, petty crime, theft, and robbery - particularly after dark - are the primary direct risks for international visitors. The ongoing Anglophone conflict, now in its eighth year with over 900,000 displaced per UN OCHA, generates political tensions in the capital and requires awareness in security planning even for visits focused on the Francophone south. Source: FCDO Cameroon travel advice (2026); US State Dept Cameroon advisory (2026).

## What MINAT-authorised CP covers in Yaounde

A MINAT-authorised Yaounde CPO programme covers: vetted vehicle collection from Nsimalen International Airport; protection for business meetings, government ministry visits, and embassy engagements in the Bastos and Centre Ville areas; hotel pre-advance; and emergency response coordination with the British High Commission and private medical facilities. Any programme elements outside Yaounde or near conflict regions require a completely separate security assessment.
