Tegucigalpa is Honduras's capital and the centre of its government, financial, and commercial activity. It sits in a basin surrounded by hills that give the city its distinctive topography - and which also define the security geography, with gang-controlled colonias occupying many of the hillside areas surrounding the commercial and diplomatic districts. The FCDO advises exercising a high degree of caution throughout Honduras, and the US State Department's Level 3 rating reflects a sustained elevated threat environment driven by MS-13 and Barrio 18 gang networks, narcotics trafficking, and targeted kidnapping of business figures.

## The security environment

Kidnapping targeting executives and perceived high-value individuals is a documented risk in Tegucigalpa, with both planned abduction and express kidnapping recorded. Corruption within security institutions complicates the reliability of official response. Business visitors should conduct all movement in vetted vehicles with pre-assessed routes, avoid night-time movement outside the diplomatic corridor, and operate from the Colonia Palmira base. The Toncontin airport transfer, while short in distance, passes through districts requiring specific route assessment. Source: FCDO Honduras travel advice (2026); IUDPAS/UNAH crime statistics (2023); US State Dept Honduras advisory (2026).

## What authorised CP covers in Tegucigalpa

A National Police-authorised Tegucigalpa CPO programme covers: inside-terminal collection from Toncontin International Airport; vetted vehicle movement to and from the Colonia Palmira district; protection for business meetings and government visits; hotel pre-advance; and emergency response coordination with the British Embassy and private medical providers.
