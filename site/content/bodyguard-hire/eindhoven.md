Eindhoven is the capital of the Brainport technology region and home to ASML, the world's largest semiconductor lithography manufacturer, as well as NXP Semiconductors and the Philips Innovation Campus. The AIVD (Dutch intelligence service, 2023 annual report) explicitly identifies state-actor targeting of the Dutch semiconductor sector, making Eindhoven one of the few low-overall-crime European cities where corporate espionage risk is a distinct and documented element of the CPO briefing for senior technology executives. Dutch private security operates under the WPBR 1997, with Justis company approvals and Politie individual screenings as the regulatory baseline; the FCDO Netherlands Travel Advice (2024) confirms the Netherlands as a low overall risk destination.

CPO operations concentrate on three zones: the ASML campus in adjacent Veldhoven, which requires advance co-ordination with facility security and vehicle-staging at controlled access points; the Philips Innovation Campus at Strijp-S for technology and design executive visits; and Eindhoven Centraal and the surrounding city centre for hotel, hospitality, and station-transfer coverage. Eindhoven Airport (EIN), 7km southwest, handles European connections, while Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) serves as the long-haul gateway with a 75-minute direct train connection.
