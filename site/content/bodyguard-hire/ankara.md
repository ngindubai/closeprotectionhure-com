Ankara is Turkey's capital and the location of all foreign embassies, government ministries, and the Turkish Grand National Assembly. It operates under a HIGH terrorism threat assessment, with a specific history of mass-casualty attacks in the city, most notably the October 2015 bombings (103 killed). Bodyguard hire in Ankara is governed by Law 5188 (2004), with the Ministry of Interior OSAS certification the mandatory qualification for all commercial close protection personnel.

## The Turkish licensing framework

Law 5188 (2004) establishes the licensing framework for all private security activity in Turkey. The OSAS examination is the standardised Ministry of Interior test for individual security personnel. Companies must hold Ministry of Interior company licences. Armed close protection requires additional firearms authorisation and is available to principals with a verified elevated threat profile. The HIGH terrorism threat in Ankara makes armed cover a more common consideration here than in lower-threat European capitals.

## Operational context in Ankara

The Cankaya diplomatic quarter, the Kavaklidere commercial district, and government ministry areas are the primary operational environments for CPO work in Ankara. Protest and public gathering avoidance protocols, government quarter access management, and current MiT threat awareness are standard elements of CPO briefings for Ankara.
