More than 4,000 exhibiting companies from over 70 countries attend a typical edition of Hannover Messe, an event widely described within manufacturing circles as the world's largest industrial trade fair. Continental AG chose Hannover for its new global headquarters campus, Continental-Plaza, opened in December 2023, and Wolfsburg, home to Volkswagen Group's headquarters, sits just 30 minutes away by direct ICE train. Put those together and Hannover carries an industrial and automotive business footprint well beyond what its size alone would suggest.

The one area worth genuine attention is the Hauptbahnhof. Bundespolizei data reported in German media recorded 1,606 offences there between January and May 2025, placing it among Germany's higher-incident stations, though a joint policing and city security initiative running October 2024 to September 2025 cut violent crime there by more than 20 percent over that same window. That's real, measurable progress, but it doesn't mean the vigilance drops entirely for anyone transiting on foot. Nordstadt, near the university, sees a similarly elevated pattern, mostly nightlife disorder rather than anything aimed at business visitors, and it's easily avoided through sensible hotel placement.

Licensing runs on the standard German model: Section 34a of the Gewerbeordnung, the Bewachungsverordnung, and mandatory IHK certification since January 2021.
