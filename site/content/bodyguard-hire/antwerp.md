The 2003 Antwerp diamond heist is still the reference point for anyone asking why the Diamond District runs its own security office alongside the Antwerp Local Police diamond department. Access to trading buildings is tightly restricted, and bodyguard hire in Antwerp for diamond sector clients is largely about navigating that dual structure rather than adding a visible presence on top of it.

The Loi du 2 octobre 2017 governs licensing nationally, with the SPF Interieur issuing firm agrements and individual badges, and the Diamond District adding its own access layer on top. Armed protection stays behind a high ministerial bar, so unarmed cover is standard. What makes Antwerp genuinely different from a typical low-risk European city is the Port of Antwerp-Bruges, repeatedly named by Belgian federal authorities as Europe's leading entry point for cocaine trafficking. FCDO Belgium (2026) is clear that the resulting organised crime violence overwhelmingly affects those involved in the trade, not corporate visitors, but it's a real factor a competent team plans around rather than ignores.

Petrochemical site visits, to Europe's largest integrated chemical cluster at the port, require the same formal accreditation and defined routes as the diamond trade's own access controls. Day to day, the more practical concern is pickpocketing around Antwerpen-Centraal and De Keyserlei, manageable with standard awareness and a vetted driver for most corporate trips.
