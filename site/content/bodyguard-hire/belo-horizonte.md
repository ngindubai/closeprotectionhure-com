Belo Horizonte is Minas Gerais state's commercial and mining-sector hub, drawing corporate principals across extractive industries, manufacturing, and regional business. FCDO Brazil 2026 advises heightened caution and SESP-MG Crime Report 2024 records elevated violent crime across the metropolitan area, making professional close protection an operational necessity rather than a discretionary addition for high-value principals.

## Operating in Belo Horizonte

Savassi and Funcionarios are the recommended base districts for corporate operations, carrying a lower crime profile relative to the wider city. Movement to Hipercentro or the Praca Sete commercial area requires specific advance planning with vehicle positioning and a brief-visit protocol. Periphery municipalities including Contagem and Venda Nova are avoided unless the operational requirement demands otherwise, and carry specific pre-planning requirements when they cannot be avoided.

## Licensing and operator selection

All close protection operators in Belo Horizonte must hold DPF registration under Federal Law 7.102/1983, verified through the national DPF register.
