BMW's plant and Porsche's Leipzig manufacturing site sit at the centre of most bodyguard hire bookings in the city, and both run the kind of formal industrial access protocol that makes a vetted driver and a device security briefing more useful than a visible protection detail. That's the BfV's own framing: Germany's automotive sector as an ongoing espionage target, not a violent crime concern.

Section 34a of the Gewerbeordnung and the Bewachungsverordnung govern licensing here, with IHK zu Leipzig issuing the Sachkundeprüfung qualification officers need for higher-risk work. What's distinctive about Leipzig isn't the automotive plants, though, it's Connewitz. The district has a genuine history of confrontation between demonstrators and police, most visibly after the 2023 Lina E. verdict, and a properly briefed team checks local police advisories before planning any route nearby rather than treating it as a permanent no-go zone.

Leipzig Hauptbahnhof is one of Europe's largest stations by floor area, and its Promenaden shopping level draws the highest footfall in the city, with the pickpocketing risk that comes with it. Leipziger Messe, tracing back to a fair first recorded in 1165, adds seasonal congestion between the station, LEJ airport, and the fairground during major exhibitions like the Leipzig Book Fair, worth building extra time into any transfer during those weeks.
