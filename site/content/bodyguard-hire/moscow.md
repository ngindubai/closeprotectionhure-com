Moscow presents a fundamentally different close protection environment from Western European capitals. ## The Russian legal framework for close protection

Federal Law 2487-1 (1992, as amended) and Rosgvardia's licensing regime are the legal basis for all private security activity in Russia. CHOPs (chastnye okhrannye predpriyatiya) must hold current Rosgvardia licences, and individual personnel must be separately licensed. FSB coordination applies to certain activities. Western-standard CPO frameworks do not apply in this jurisdiction; operational planning must be built around what CHOP operators can legally deliver under current Russian law.

## Planning context for essential-travel principals

The current operating environment in Moscow requires specific attention to communications security, financial transaction compliance with sanctions regimes, and the significant limitation that Western consulates have in providing assistance to foreign nationals detained under Russian law.
