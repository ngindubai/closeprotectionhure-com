Osaka is Japan's principal commercial centre outside Tokyo, with corporate activity concentrated in the Umeda/Kita CBD, Nakanoshima government and banking district, and the Namba/Minami commercial area. The ambient security environment is among the calmest of any major global business city: NPA Crime Statistics 2024/2025 confirm very low violent crime, with residual risk limited to petty theft in crowded tourist areas such as Dotonbori at peak times. The primary planning considerations are discretion and natural-disaster preparedness, as Osaka lies in a seismically active region, and the extended transfer from Kansai International Airport.

For principals requiring close protection in Osaka, all operators must hold Osaka Prefectural Police-approved licensing under the Security Business Act (Act No. 117 of 1972).
