Manila is the Philippines' commercial capital and financial centre, and the base for corporate travel across one of Southeast Asia's largest economies. The security environment in Metro Manila is genuinely elevated — kidnapping rates are among the region's highest, armed robbery is a daily reality in many districts, and the gap between safe and unsafe areas is sharper than in most cities in our network.

**Armed close protection is legally available and appropriate for many engagements in Manila.** The decision whether to use armed or unarmed CP depends on the principal's profile, movement pattern, and the districts they will operate in. For most senior executives with predictable schedules, a PNP-licensed armed security driver combined with an unarmed CPO is the standard detail configuration.

## The security landscape

Kidnap-for-ransom is the defining threat for corporate and HNWI visitors to Manila. Organised crime groups have historically targeted business executives, their family members, and Chinese community figures. The threat is concentrated but real — executives who are visibly wealthy, travel with predictable routines, and use commercial transport are at the highest risk. Professional close protection with anti-kidnapping protocols substantially reduces this exposure.

Street crime — armed robbery, bag snatching, and carjacking — is a daily reality across much of Metro Manila outside the secured business zones. The airport (NAIA) approach roads are among the highest-risk single locations in the city for arriving executives.

The terrorism threat is primarily located in Mindanao, not Metro Manila, but IS-linked groups have demonstrated reach into the capital in the past.

## Business districts and movement

**Bonifacio Global City (BGC)** is the safest operating environment in Metro Manila — a planned, controlled-access development with active security, lower ambient crime, and modern infrastructure. Most multinational headquarters and international hotels for corporate visitors are here or in **Makati CBD**. Movement between these two districts using pre-arranged secure transport is low-risk. Movement outside them, particularly into older Manila districts at night, requires close protection.
