Surabaya is Indonesia's 2nd largest city and the capital of East Java, with approximately 10 million people in the metro area. It is a major port and industrial centre serving the manufacturing, food processing, and petrochemical sectors. FCDO Indonesia 2026 rates Java at Level 1 (exercise normal precautions), making Surabaya one of the more accessible cities in the region for corporate operations. The primary operational risk is road traffic safety: NTMC Indonesia 2024 records a high road fatality rate, and Polri BUJP-permitted drivers trained in defensive driving provide meaningful risk reduction on every journey.

## Security landscape in Surabaya

The Ciputra World and Galaxy Mall corridor, along with the Wiyung and Pakuwon residential districts, represent the lowest-risk operating areas for HNWI and senior executive principals. Surabaya CBD and Gubeng carry moderate risk with standard precautions. Market areas including Pasar Turi and Wonokromo carry elevated petty crime exposure and should be approached with advance planning.

Terrorism awareness applies at the national level in Indonesia; however, Surabaya's security situation has improved since 2018 and the city is regularly visited by international business delegations without incident.

## Regulatory environment

All security operators in Surabaya must hold a BUJP permit from the Indonesian National Police (Polri) under Regulation No. 24/2007. Individual guards require Polri-certified training. The BUJP register is verified before any engagement.
