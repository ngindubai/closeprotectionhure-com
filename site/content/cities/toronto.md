Toronto is Canada's largest city and financial capital, home to the country's major banks, a significant technology and AI sector, and one of North America's most diverse business environments. The Greater Toronto Area hosts major financial conferences, government summits, and a large HNWI population. Within Canada's stable governance framework, the relevant security considerations for corporate visitors are more intelligence and counter-interference focused than physical threat driven.

## Foreign state interference: the primary elevated risk

CSIS has publicly and consistently identified foreign state interference as an active concern in Toronto. The city's large and diverse diaspora communities from China, India, and Iran, combined with its concentration of financial, technology, and defence-adjacent industry, make it one of the highest-priority target environments in Canada for foreign intelligence operations. The 2024 NSICOP report confirmed the scale of these operations and their targeting of both political figures and commercial entities.

For corporate executives in technology, financial services, and sensitive commercial sectors, the practical implication is counter-intelligence awareness: attention to approach vectors, discretion in commercial discussions, and device security. This is a planning precaution rather than a physical threat for most visiting executives, but it is a well-documented and active risk rather than a theoretical one.

## Vehicle theft and HNWI targeting

Vehicle theft has risen sharply in the Greater Toronto Area in recent years, with Canada ranking among the countries with the highest auto-theft rates globally. High-value vehicles are disproportionately targeted, and organised criminal networks export stolen vehicles from Canadian ports. Residential break-ins targeting HNWI households in Rosedale, Forest Hill, and The Bridle Path occur at documented frequency. For visiting HNWI principals, attention to vehicle security and residential security measures is more relevant than the very low physical assault risk.

## Events security

Toronto hosts several events that generate significant HNWI and high-profile principal security demand. The Toronto International Film Festival in September concentrates entertainment industry executives and public figures in a city-centre setting. Major financial conferences bring senior executives to the downtown hotel corridor. G7-adjacent government meetings generate diplomatic security requirements. For all of these, advance venue assessment and transport planning for the specific event environment are the primary security tasks.
