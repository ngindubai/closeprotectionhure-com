Chittagong (officially Chattogram) is Bangladesh's second-largest city and the country's principal seaport, handling approximately 90% of Bangladesh's import-export trade. It is the operational hub for the ready-made garment industry's export infrastructure, Bangladesh's petroleum refining sector, and a significant shipbuilding and ship-breaking industry. International business travel to Chittagong is driven primarily by the garment sector -- international buyers, compliance auditors, and brand due diligence teams visiting manufacturing operations -- alongside shipping, energy, and infrastructure sector visitors.

## The security environment

The FCDO assesses the terrorism threat in Bangladesh as high, noting that attacks targeting foreigners and crowded places have occurred. The July 2016 Holey Artisan Bakery attack in Dhaka, which killed 22 people including multiple foreign nationals, established that Bangladesh-based extremist groups have the capability and intent to conduct mass-casualty attacks targeting foreign visitors. Chittagong, as a major commercial city, carries an elevated profile as a potential target for international attention. The FCDO also notes that political hartals and blockades can disrupt movement across the country, including the routes to Chittagong port and the industrial zones. Source: FCDO Bangladesh travel advice (2026). US State Department Bangladesh advisory Level 2 (2026).

## Business context

The Chittagong Export Processing Zone and the main industrial areas outside the port attract compliance teams, buyers, and operational visitors from global brands and shipping companies. The Agrabad commercial district has the most developed infrastructure for business visitors, including international-standard hotels and corporate facilities. The port area itself -- Sadarghat and the Chittagong Port Authority zone -- requires specific access management protocols for visiting port and logistics professionals.
