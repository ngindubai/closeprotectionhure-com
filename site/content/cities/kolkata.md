Kolkata presents a moderate risk profile for visiting executives. West Bengal Police Crime Statistics 2024 identify Howrah Station, the Maidan area, and New Market as the primary zones of elevated petty theft and pickpocketing. The city's documented history of hartals (general strikes) and political demonstrations, per WB Police incident logs 2024, introduces unpredictable road closures and movement disruption that make itinerary flexibility and vetted transport non-negotiable for corporate principals. The FCDO advises normal precautions for India (2026) and advises awareness of political demonstrations in West Bengal; the US State Department rates India at Level 2.

The corporate footprint is anchored in Salt Lake City (Sector V) and Bidhannagar, which house the city's IT and corporate hub, and in Alipore, which provides the principal HNWI residential and diplomatic zone. Biswa Bangla Convention Centre and ITC Sonar serve as the primary events venues. Principals with itineraries extending to North Kolkata's older districts after dark or to the Howrah Station area require close protection cover. Traffic congestion on the EM Bypass, Howrah Bridge approach, and Park Street is a consistent operational variable, and vetted drivers familiar with Kolkata's route network are essential for predictable transfers from Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport (CCU).
