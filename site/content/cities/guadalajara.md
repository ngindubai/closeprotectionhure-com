Guadalajara is the capital of Jalisco state and Mexico's second-largest city. With a population of approximately 5 million in the metropolitan area, it is Mexico's technology capital, hosting the Latin American and Mexican operations of most major international technology companies. It is also the home territory of CJNG, currently assessed as one of Mexico's most powerful criminal organisations.

## The technology sector context

Guadalajara's technology cluster is centred in Zapopan municipality, north of the city centre. The Puerta de Hierro and Via Vallarta corridors host the campuses of IBM, Intel, Oracle, HP, and dozens of software development companies. The annual Talent Land conference and other technology events draw international visitors specifically to this area.

For technology sector business travel, Guadalajara is a genuine destination with specific security requirements. The corporate zone (Providencia, Andares, Puerta de Hierro) has reasonable private security infrastructure. The challenge is that this zone sits within a metropolitan area where CJNG maintains significant operational presence.

## The CJNG context

Jalisco New Generation Cartel emerged from Jalisco in the 2010s and has grown to be one of Mexico's most powerful criminal organisations. It has demonstrated capacity for sophisticated violence including the use of drones, armoured vehicles, and direct confrontation with military forces. CJNG's primary activities are drug trafficking, territorial control, and conflict with rival organisations.

For business visitors, the CJNG presence means that cartel-related violence (shootings, roadblocks, confrontations with security forces) can occur in the wider metropolitan area. Business visitors are not CJNG targets. The risk is ambient, not directed.

## Operational baseline

A pre-booked vetted driver from Miguel Hidalgo y Costilla airport is mandatory. All in-city movement by pre-booked vehicle. Accommodation in Providencia or the Zapopan commercial zone. No street-hailed taxis.
