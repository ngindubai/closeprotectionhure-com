Frankfurt is Germany's financial capital and the seat of the European Central Bank, making it one of Western Europe's most important destinations for high-level executive travel. The operational security environment is manageable for most corporate visits. The Bahnhofsviertel (main station district) presents the most concentrated risk, but this is largely avoidable through appropriate accommodation and routing choices. The more operationally relevant considerations for financial sector executives are information and device security: the BfV (2025) confirms Frankfurt as a primary corporate espionage target, and senior banking and financial delegations should treat meeting security, device hygiene, and counter-surveillance as baseline requirements rather than optional upgrades.
