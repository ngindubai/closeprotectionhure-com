Izmir is Turkey's 3rd largest city and the principal commercial centre of the Aegean coast, with a major port and industrial base spanning manufacturing, chemicals, and logistics. FCDO Turkey 2026 rates Izmir at Level 2 (exercise heightened caution), with terrorism awareness at the national level applicable across Turkey. For most corporate operations concentrated in Alsancak, Konak, and the Karsiyaka residential zone, the day-to-day risk profile is moderate, with political demonstration activity and the national terrorism context as the key planning factors.

## Security landscape in Izmir

Alsancak and Konak form the primary CBD and hotel district for corporate operations in Izmir. Karsiyaka and Bornova offer a lower-risk residential alternative with good access to the Bornova-Kemalpasa industrial corridor. The coastal resort district of Cesme, approximately 80km to the west, is a popular HNWI leisure destination with a lower crime profile.

The Kemeralti Bazaar historic market area requires specific planning for principal visits due to crowd density and opportunistic crime. Demonstration activity in Alsancak and Konak can restrict movement; advance monitoring of the protest calendar is standard practice for Izmir itineraries.

## Regulatory environment

All private security operators in Izmir must hold a TNP permit under Law No. 5188 on Private Security Services. Individual guards require TNP-issued licence cards. The TNP register is verified before any engagement.
