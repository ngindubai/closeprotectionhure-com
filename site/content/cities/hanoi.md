Hanoi presents a moderate risk profile for visiting executives. The Ministry of Public Security Crime Statistics 2024 identify motorbike bag-snatching in the Old Quarter and Hoan Kiem district as the principal concern for corporate principals, particularly after dark. The Vietnam Road Traffic Safety Committee Report 2024 identifies road traffic conditions, driven by Hanoi's exceptional motorbike density, as a consistent operational risk for foreign nationals; unvetted transport is a frequent contributory factor in incidents involving foreign executives. The FCDO advises normal precautions for Vietnam (2026), and the US State Department rates the country at Level 1.

The corporate footprint is anchored in Ba Dinh district (embassies, government ministries, international organisations) and Tay Ho (West Lake), which provides the preferred HNWI residential and hotel zone. The National Convention Centre in Ba Dinh and the Lotte Center Hanoi serve as the primary events and business meeting venues. Principals operating in and around the Old Quarter should plan movements to avoid late-evening exposure, and all airport transfers from Noi Bai International Airport (HAN, 35km from the city centre) require vetted drivers familiar with the Nhat Tan Bridge and Vo Nguyen Giap expressway corridor.
