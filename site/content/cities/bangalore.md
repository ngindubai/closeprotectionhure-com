Bangalore is the centre of India's technology economy. It draws more international corporate visitors than any other Indian city outside Mumbai, with a steady flow of investor delegations, partnership visits, and technology sector business. The risk profile is lower than Delhi or Mumbai. That does not mean it is absent.

## The risk level in context

Bangalore's overall risk rating is low to moderate. Crime in the IT corridors (Whitefield, Electronic City, Koramangala, Indiranagar) is lower than in Delhi's equivalent business districts. Kidnapping is not a material concern for corporate travellers here. The Karnataka police reliability score is better than Delhi's.

What Bangalore shares with every Indian city is the national terrorism threat. The FCDO is direct: terrorists are very likely to carry out attacks in India. That assessment covers Bangalore, even though the city itself has limited attack history. The 2013 Hyderabad bombings are the closest regional precedent and a reminder that the threat geography extends beyond Mumbai and Delhi.

## Cauvery protests

This is the specific local disruption risk for Bangalore that most security briefings miss. The Cauvery river water dispute between Karnataka and Tamil Nadu has triggered protests, road closures, and periodic violence in the city on multiple occasions. The pattern repeats whenever the Supreme Court-managed allocation cycle is contested.

For corporate visitors, the effect is transport disruption. Major roads can close with limited warning. The Bengaluru-Mysuru highway, IT corridor access routes, and roads serving Electronic City have all been affected in past cycles. A local security driver who monitors this in real time is the practical mitigation.

## Operating in Bangalore

Namma Metro is safe for daytime travel in the areas it serves. The network is expanding. For most IT corridor movement, app-based taxis (Uber, Ola) are the standard. Traffic in Bangalore is among the worst in India by density. Build transit time generously into every Bangalore itinerary.

Female travellers should note that the FCDO's documented warnings about sexual assault risk for female foreign nationals in India apply to Bangalore as they do across the country. The IT districts are better managed than central Delhi, but the national threat environment does not stop at a district boundary.
