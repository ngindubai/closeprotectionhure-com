Belo Horizonte is Minas Gerais state's commercial and mining-sector hub, drawing corporate principals across extractive industries, manufacturing, and regional business. FCDO Brazil 2026 advises heightened caution and SESP-MG Crime Report 2024 records elevated violent crime across the metropolitan area, making professional close protection an operational requirement for senior executives and HNWI principals. Savassi and Funcionarios are the recommended operating base, with specific protocols required for any movement into Hipercentro or the city periphery.

## Security landscape in Belo Horizonte

Savassi and Funcionarios carry a lower crime profile relative to the wider city and are the recommended base districts for corporate operations. Movement to Hipercentro or the Praca Sete commercial area requires specific advance planning with vehicle positioning and a brief-visit protocol. Periphery municipalities including Contagem and Venda Nova are avoided unless the operational requirement demands otherwise.

Carjacking at semaforos (traffic light stops) is a consistent risk across Belo Horizonte's transition zones. Counter-carjacking disciplines -- vehicle gap maintenance, central locking, and driver response protocols -- are standard for all principal movements.

## Regulatory environment

All close protection operators in Belo Horizonte must hold DPF registration under Federal Law 7.102/1983, administered through SENASP and the Secretaria de Seguranca Publica de Minas Gerais.
