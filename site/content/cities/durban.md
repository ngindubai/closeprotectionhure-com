Durban (eThekwini Municipality) is South Africa's 3rd largest city by population and the busiest container port in Africa. SAPS Annual Crime Report 2023/24 identifies eThekwini as among the highest-crime metro areas in South Africa, with carjacking, armed robbery, and vehicle crime all at elevated levels. FCDO South Africa 2026 advises against travel to specific township areas and notes high risk of violent crime in Durban CBD. Corporate and HNWI principals visiting Durban are best served by basing themselves in Umhlanga or La Lucia Ridge, operating exclusively with PSIRA-registered transport, and employing close protection cover for movements into the CBD.

## Security landscape in Durban

Umhlanga is the primary HNWI and corporate district, offering a substantially lower crime profile than Durban CBD with direct N2 access to King Shaka International Airport. La Lucia Ridge provides the primary corporate office corridor. The Durban CBD should be treated as an elevated-risk zone for movement purposes, with vetted transport only and no on-foot movement after dark.

The N2 corridor between Umhlanga and the CBD is the most commonly used route for corporate movement and requires carjacking-aware driving, particularly at slower-moving traffic sections and robot (traffic light) intersections.

## Regulatory environment

All close protection operators in Durban must hold current PSIRA registration under PSIRA 2001. Armed operators additionally require Firearms Control Act 60/2000 competency certificates. The PSIRA register is publicly accessible and verified before any operator engagement.
