Kyiv is the capital of Ukraine, a country of 44 million people engaged in a full-scale war with Russia since February 2022. This page exists for those with essential requirements that make Kyiv travel unavoidable: humanitarian organisations, media, diplomatic personnel, reconstruction professionals, and family circumstances.

FCDO advises against all travel to Ukraine.

## Why this page exists

War correspondents, NGO workers, reconstruction contractors, diaspora with family obligations, and commercial interests that cannot be suspended all require professional risk assessment. The information here is not a recommendation to travel. It is support for those who have decided that travel is necessary and need to minimise risk in conditions that have no peacetime equivalent.

## The strike picture

Russia has conducted drone and missile strikes on Kyiv continuously since 2022. Attacks use a range of systems: Iskander ballistic missiles, Kh-101 cruise missiles, and Shahed-136 kamikaze drone swarms. Ukraine's air defence capability has improved and intercepts significant proportions of incoming attacks. Some strikes reach targets in Kyiv. Energy infrastructure has been the primary systematic target. Residential and commercial areas have also been struck.

For visitors, this means the air raid alert system is the critical daily interface. Sheltering immediately on alert, knowing the nearest designated shelter, and selecting accommodation with bunker access are not optional precautions.

## The power situation

Ukraine's energy grid has been severely degraded by sustained strikes. Power outages in Kyiv are planned (rolling blackouts) and unplanned (emergency after strikes). Planned outages typically run 8-16 hours per day. Essential operations require generator backup. Pre-booking generator-equipped accommodation is a basic logistics requirement.
