Kumasi is the capital of the Ashanti Region and Ghana's second-largest city, serving as the commercial and cultural hub of inland West Africa with major mining, agricultural, and regional trade operations. FCDO Ghana 2026 rates most of Ghana, including Kumasi, at Level 1 (normal precautions). The primary operational risks for corporate principals are petty crime in the dense market areas around Kejetia and Kumasi Central Market, vehicle crime on peripheral highway routes, and road safety hazards on national highways north of the city.

## Security landscape in Kumasi

Nhyiaeso and Asokwa are the primary HNWI residential and accommodation zones, offering lower crime relative to the CBD. The Adum and Roman Hill business district is where most corporate meetings and trade offices are concentrated, with moderate daytime exposure and standard precautions required for evening movements. Kejetia market and Kumasi Central Market are the highest-density areas and require specific awareness protocols for any principal visit.

Intercity movements between Kumasi and Accra on the N6 highway are best conducted during daylight hours with GPS-registered drivers. Northern highway routes towards Tamale carry elevated accident risk after dark and should not be used for night driving without specific security planning.

## Regulatory environment

All private security operators in Kumasi must hold GPS registration under the Private Security Organisation Act 1963 (Ghana). The GPS register is verified before any operator engagement.
