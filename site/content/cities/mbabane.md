Mbabane is the administrative capital of the Kingdom of Eswatini (formerly Swaziland), a small landlocked country of approximately 1.2 million people in southern Africa, bordered by South Africa and Mozambique. Eswatini is an absolute monarchy under King Mswati III, the last absolute monarchy on the African continent. The economy is based on sugar and related agro-processing, soft drink concentrate manufacturing (using preferential trade arrangements with the EU and US), citrus and pineapple exports, timber, and some mining. International visitors come to Mbabane primarily for government affairs and regulatory engagement, corporate governance of agro-processing operations, and investment discussions.

The security environment in Mbabane is low risk by regional standards. The FCDO advises normal precautions for Eswatini. Petty theft and road safety are the relevant concerns for business visitors. The governance environment -- an absolute monarchy with restrictions on political parties and civil society -- requires awareness for corporate visitors in politically visible sectors, and political demonstrations have at times been subject to security force response. Medical facilities are limited; South Africa provides the practical medevac destination. Source: FCDO Eswatini travel advice (2026); US State Dept Eswatini Level 1 travel advisory (2026).

Most international visitors to Mbabane route through [Johannesburg](/cities/johannesburg/) in South Africa, approximately 3 hours by road via the Oshoek-Ngwenya border crossing. For regional corporate context, [Cape Town](/cities/cape-town/) provides an alternative South African base for combined South Africa and Eswatini engagements.
