Victoria is the capital of the Republic of Seychelles, an archipelago state of 115 islands in the western Indian Ocean, approximately 1,500km east of mainland Africa. It is one of the smallest capital cities in the world by population, serving a nation of approximately 100,000 people. Despite its size, Victoria is an internationally active financial and regulatory centre: the Seychelles International Business Authority (SIBA) framework supports a significant offshore company registration and investment structure sector, and the city attracts international legal, financial, and private equity visitors.

The security environment in Victoria is low risk. The FCDO advises normal precautions for Seychelles, and the US State Department classifies Seychelles at Level 1 (Exercise Normal Precautions). Petty theft in market areas and drug-related background crime are the relevant concerns for international visitors; violent crime targeting business visitors is rare. For HNWI and private family visitors, profile management and personal privacy are the more relevant security considerations. Source: FCDO Seychelles travel advice (2026); US State Dept Seychelles Level 1 travel advisory (2026).

For regional staging and corporate operations that use Seychelles-based structures, [Nairobi](/cities/nairobi/) in Kenya is the primary East African hub, and [Dubai](/cities/dubai/) provides the main GCC connection point with direct flights to SEZ. Seychelles International Airport (SEZ) is approximately 10km south of Victoria.
