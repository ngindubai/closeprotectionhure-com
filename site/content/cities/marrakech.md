Marrakech is Morocco's principal HNWI destination and a major corporate events hub, home to international luxury hotels, private riads, and a significant conference and incentive travel market. FCDO Morocco 2026 rates Morocco at Level 2 (exercise heightened caution), with specific terrorism awareness applicable at tourist sites and crowded public spaces including the Djemaa el-Fna. For most corporate principals operating from Gueliz or Hivernage, the day-to-day risk profile is moderate, with petty crime in the Medina and motorcycle-borne theft as the primary operational considerations.

## Security landscape in Marrakech

Gueliz is the modern business district and primary base for corporate operations, offering international hotels, offices, and a lower crime profile than the Medina. Hivernage, adjacent to Gueliz, is the primary luxury hotel zone for HNWI principals. The Palmeraie villa district provides the preferred private accommodation option for extended-stay principals.

The Medina and Djemaa el-Fna require specific planning for principal visits: narrow derbs (lanes) limit vehicle access, motorcycle-borne theft is a consistent hazard at souk entrances, and the general crowd density increases opportunistic crime exposure. DGSN-authorised close protection provides effective risk reduction for Medina visits.

## Regulatory environment

All private security operators in Marrakech must hold DGSN authorisation under Law 17-89 on Private Security Activities. The DGSN register is verified before any operator engagement.
