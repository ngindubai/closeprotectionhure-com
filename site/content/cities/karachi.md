Karachi is Pakistan's commercial capital, its largest city, and its primary port. It is also one of the most security-intensive operating environments in our network. For the NGO workers, energy sector executives, and corporate travellers who must operate there, professional armed security is not a discretionary upgrade — it is a baseline operational requirement.

**Armed close protection is the standard for corporate engagements in Karachi.** The city's combination of terrorism, kidnap-for-ransom, armed street crime, and periodic political and ethnic violence means unarmed CP provides inadequate deterrence for most principals. A minimum detail is an armed CPO and an armed security driver.

## The security landscape

Karachi's security environment has improved from its worst period (2011–2014), when it was experiencing dozens of politically and ethnically motivated killings per month alongside a sustained terrorism campaign. The Pakistan Rangers and police conducted sustained security operations that reduced the most acute violence. The city is more stable than that peak, but remains genuinely high-risk by any objective measure.

Terrorism from TTP-linked and sectarian groups remains an active threat. Kidnapping targeting business executives and their families has continued despite improved policing. Armed street robbery is a daily reality across much of the city. For visiting foreign nationals, the combined threat requires active, professional security from arrival to departure.

## Movement and accommodation

Route security is the most critical operational variable in Karachi. The combination of predictable movement patterns and the kidnapping threat means route variation is not optional — it is a core part of security planning. Your driver should survey routes before each significant movement. Departure times should vary. Hotel selection should be discussed with your security provider before booking, not after arrival.

For extended assignments, safe-house accommodation may be more appropriate than hotels with a visible international profile. Discuss this with us when briefing your requirements.
