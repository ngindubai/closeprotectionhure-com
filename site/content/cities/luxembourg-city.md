Luxembourg City presents one of the most operationally straightforward security environments of any European capital. Ambient crime is minimal, the infrastructure is excellent, and the Grand Duchy Police maintains an effective presence across the city. The operational security discipline for corporate visitors centres almost entirely on information and meeting security: the concentration of EU institutions, international banks, fund houses, and legal arbitration creates an environment in which corporate and financial espionage is a documented risk (Surete de l'Etat annual assessment 2025). Senior executives attending ECJ hearings, EIB board sessions, or private banking conferences in Luxembourg should treat device hygiene, meeting room security, and counter-surveillance as standard operating practice.

For principals requiring physical close protection, operators must hold a licence under the Loi du 12 novembre 2002.
