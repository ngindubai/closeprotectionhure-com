Monrovia is the capital of Liberia, a West African country of approximately 5 million people with a history shaped by two devastating civil wars (1989 to 1996 and 1999 to 2003) and an Ebola epidemic (2014 to 2016). Post-conflict reconstruction has been underway for over two decades, supported by a large international community, but state institutions including the police, judiciary, and health system remain fragile. International business in Liberia is driven by iron ore (ArcelorMittal), the world's largest natural rubber plantation (Firestone, near Harbel), gold and diamond mining, timber, and one of the world's largest shipping flag registries by tonnage. The Mamba Point diplomatic district houses most international organisations and visiting corporate personnel.

The security environment in Monrovia is high risk. The FCDO advises a high degree of caution throughout Liberia. Armed robbery and carjacking targeting international visitors are documented. The 60km Roberts International Airport transfer is the most significant logistical security challenge. Medical evacuation capability is a prerequisite for any Liberia engagement. Source: FCDO Liberia travel advice (2026); US State Dept Liberia Level 2 travel advisory (2026).

For regional staging and medical evacuation, [Accra](/cities/accra/) in Ghana and [Abidjan](/cities/abidjan/) in Cote d'Ivoire are the primary regional hubs with direct air connections. Roberts International Airport (ROB) is approximately 60km southeast of Monrovia.
