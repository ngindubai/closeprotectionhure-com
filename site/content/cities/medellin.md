Medellin is Colombia's second city and the capital of Antioquia department. With a population approaching 4 million in the metropolitan area, it is Colombia's industrial heartland and, increasingly, its technology capital. The transformation from the world's most dangerous city in the early 1990s to a recognised innovation hub is real, but the underlying organised crime infrastructure that generated that history has not disappeared.

## The current security picture

Medellin's violent crime rates have declined dramatically since the 1990s. The homicide rate, once above 300 per 100,000 population, has fallen to figures comparable to other large Latin American cities. The dismantling of the Medellin Cartel did not eliminate organised crime from the city; it fragmented it. Successor structures including the Oficina de Envigado, Clan del Golfo affiliates, and multiple smaller criminal networks maintain territorial control in peripheral comunas.

For business visitors operating in El Poblado and the established commercial zones, the direct exposure to organised crime violence is low. The more immediate risks are opportunistic crime, express kidnapping, and the scopolamine pattern documented across Colombian cities.

## El Poblado as the operating zone

El Poblado is a practical base for corporate Medellin visits. The district has the highest concentration of international hotels, restaurants, and business venues. Private security presence in the zone is substantial. The main business hotels have their own security infrastructure appropriate to the environment.

Movement outside El Poblado should be by pre-booked vetted vehicle. The Parque Lleras nightlife area within El Poblado carries its own elevated risk, particularly at night, from scopolamine and robbery targeting visitors in social settings.

## Airport logistics

Jose Maria Cordova International Airport is located in Rionegro, approximately 40km east of Medellin's city centre. The journey takes 60-90 minutes depending on traffic and weather on the mountain road. This distance matters for security planning: airport runs are not short, and informal or unlicensed taxis from the airport to El Poblado are a documented risk point. Pre-book the transfer before arrival.
