Cebu is the Philippines' second-largest metropolitan area and a major port, tourism, and technology hub in the Visayas. FCDO Philippines 2026 rates the Visayas at Level 2 (exercise heightened caution). The Cebu Business Park and IT Park districts carry substantially lower crime rates than the wider city and are the recommended operating base for corporate principals. The primary operational considerations are opportunistic street crime in the Colon Street area, Marcelo Fernan Bridge congestion affecting airport transfer timing, and typhoon season planning between June and November.

## Security landscape in Cebu

Cebu Business Park, Ayala Center, and IT Park in Lahug are the preferred zones for corporate operations, offering lower crime exposure relative to the historic Colon Street commercial district. Mactan Island provides a lower-crime airport-adjacent option for principals on short visits. South Cebu City port areas and the Carbon Market carry elevated crime after dark and should be avoided on foot.

Mactan-Cebu International Airport (CEB) is connected to the city via the Marcelo Fernan Bridge, which is subject to significant peak-hour congestion. All principal transfers include congestion-aware route planning with a pre-surveyed alternative via the Mactan-Mandaue Bridge.

## Regulatory environment

All close protection operators in Cebu must hold PNP-SAGS accreditation under Republic Act 11458 (2019). Individual guards require PNP registration and current training certification. The PNP register is verified before any operator engagement.
