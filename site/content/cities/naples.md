Naples is southern Italy's largest city and commercial centre, a hub for manufacturing, logistics, and the cultural and luxury tourism sector. It sits at the gateway to the Amalfi Coast, Capri, Ischia, and the broader Campania region, making it a common destination for both corporate visits and high-net-worth leisure. Within Italy's functional security framework, Naples is distinguished from other Italian cities by its organised crime environment and its notably challenging operating conditions.

## The security landscape

Naples operates within Italy's functional legal and security framework. Carabinieri and Polizia are present and operational, emergency services function, and the city is not lawless. The elevated petty crime rates relative to northern Italy are real, concentrated in the historic centre, around the central station, and in the form of scooter-borne snatch theft, but they are manageable with awareness and appropriate precautions.

The organised crime dimension is the genuine differentiator from other Italian cities. The Camorra is a structured criminal organisation with real presence in commercial and construction sectors. For business visitors, this is primarily a due diligence consideration rather than a physical security risk: direct targeting of international visitors is not the typical pattern, but commercial and reputational exposure through partners and contractors is a real consideration that warrants more rigour than in Milan or Rome.

## The operating environment

Naples presents practical operating challenges that distinguish it from northern Italian cities. The road environment is notoriously difficult: driver behaviour is unpredictable by northern European standards, scooter traffic is dense, and the historic centre's narrow streets require local knowledge. Scooter-borne snatch theft and road incidents are both common. A vetted security driver with genuine local knowledge is consequently a meaningful asset rather than a convenience, both for the protective driving dimension and for the simple navigation of a congested, complex city.

## The luxury leisure dimension

Much of the HNWI security demand in Naples is connected to the surrounding region rather than the city itself. The Amalfi Coast, Capri, and Ischia are premium leisure destinations, and Naples is the typical gateway. For high-value clients, the combination of a recognisable profile and an unfamiliar, congested environment, often involving coastal road travel and yacht or boat operations, is what makes professional security support worthwhile. The package typically combines close protection, a vetted driver familiar with the coastal roads, and venue coordination.
