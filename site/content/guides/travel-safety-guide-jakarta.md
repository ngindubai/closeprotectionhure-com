Jakarta is Indonesia's capital and the commercial, financial, and governmental centre of Southeast Asia's largest economy. With a metropolitan population exceeding 30 million, it is one of the world's largest urban areas. It is an important business destination for companies with Indonesian operations or regional Southeast Asian strategies.

## Before You Travel

**Traffic planning is security planning.** Jakarta's traffic is not an inconvenience — it is an operational variable that affects every schedule and contingency plan. Build 2-3x normal journey time buffers for all peak-hour movements. Late evening and early morning journeys are substantially faster. If your flight arrives during peak hours, be prepared for an extended airport transfer.

**FCDO advisory check.** FCDO rates Indonesia as Level 2 overall with a high terrorism warning. The advisory covers the full country; the Papua and Maluku regions carry separate specific advisories.

**Visa.** Indonesia offers visa-on-arrival for many nationalities for 30 days, extendable once. Confirm for your nationality. eVisa is available through the official imigrasi.go.id portal.

**Vaccinations.** Hepatitis A and typhoid are recommended. Dengue fever exists in Indonesia. Confirm with your GP.

## On the Ground

**Transport.** Pre-booked Gojek or Grab (app-based transport) is the appropriate option for corporate movement. Hotel-provided transport for airport transfers. Avoid ojek (motorcycle taxis) for corporate travel. The MRT covers the main Sudirman-SCBD corridor and is functional for day use.

**Stay zones.** SCBD, Sudirman, Kuningan, and Menteng are appropriate. International hotel brands in these areas have good security infrastructure.

**Flooding.** Jakarta is subject to flooding, particularly January-March (wet season). Flooding can cut off roads and disrupt movement significantly. Monitor conditions and have alternative routes identified.

**Language.** Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia) is the national language. English is spoken in corporate settings and international hotels. A translator is useful for meetings outside standard corporate venues.
