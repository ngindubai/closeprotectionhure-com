Colombia's private security industry is one of Latin America's most developed and professional, shaped by decades of operating in a challenging security environment. SuperVigilancia provides regulatory oversight that has produced a relatively well-regulated market compared to many regional peers.

## The Regulatory Framework

**SuperVigilancia licensing.** All private security companies operating in Colombia must be licensed by the Superintendencia de Vigilancia y Seguridad Privada. The licensing process is detailed and includes financial, operational, and training requirements.

**Company categories.** The Colombian framework distinguishes between different service categories: vigilance services (fixed location), mobile protection, personal protection (close protection), transport of valuables, and others. Each category requires specific licensing.

**Armed operations.** Armed private security requires SuperVigilancia approval and specific training credentials. Armed close protection is legally available and commonly used for senior corporate executives.

**Continuing compliance.** Licensed companies are subject to SuperVigilancia inspection and audit. Industry standards have improved significantly over the past decade.

## Bogota Operations

Bogota has a well-developed professional security market. The financial district and northern Bogota commercial areas are accessible for corporate travel with appropriate security measures. Major licensed operators provide close protection and secure transport to international standards.

Armed close protection is standard for senior executives. Armoured vehicles are commonly used and available in the Bogota market.

## Regional Operations

For operations outside Bogota (Medellín, Cali, Cartagena, and regional cities) verify SuperVigilancia licensing and assess current threat conditions specific to the area. The ELN and BACRIM presence varies by region and requires current assessment.
