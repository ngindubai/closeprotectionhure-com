Technical & Content SEO Audit

CloseProtectionHire.com — SEO Audit & Remediation Brief

Grounded in a full read of the live Hugo repository (branch master content tree), cross-referenced against the 29 June 2026 SEO & Search intelligence brief. Every finding below is evidenced from the actual codebase, not generic best practice.

2 July 2026 2,396 content pages audited Site type: YMYL programmatic SEO Execution model: Sonnet (Opus steps flagged)

§Executive summary

The site is in far better shape than the June brief's "audit the thin pages" instinct would suggest: the content is genuinely researched, sourced (FCDO, OSAC, ACLED, national licensing bodies), and written in a credible institutional voice. The problem is not that the writing is thin. The problem is that the single most valuable content on ~1,638 pages never reaches the rendered HTML, and that the programmatic architecture creates a large internal-duplication surface that is precisely what the June 2026 spam update targets.

There is one finding here that dwarfs all others in return-on-effort, and it is a two-file template fix, not a content rewrite. Fixing it converts ~1,638 pages from "thin, ~250 rendered words" to "substantial, ~1,100 sourced words with structured data" in a single deploy — turning the biggest spam-update liability into the portfolio's strongest E-E-A-T asset. Start there.

The one-paragraph diagnosis

Six service silos (bodyguard-hire, executive-protection, close-protection-officers, security-drivers, residential-security, secure-airport-transfers) have no dedicated Hugo layout, so they render through layouts/_default/single.html. That fallback renders the H1, a ~250-word intro, the FAQs and the quote form — but it never iterates .Params.components. Every one of these 1,638 pages carries six sourced intelligence components (~900 words of the page's best material) in front matter that is completely absent from the HTML Google sees. The event-security template already renders this exact array (line 85). The fix is to port that block into the fallback and add a schema block. One template edit repairs 1,638 pages.

1Rich components content is silently dropped on 1,638 pages

The fallback template ignores the array that holds the page's best content

Critical Sonnet OK Effort: S (1 template)
Evidence. layouts/_default/single.html renders .Content, .Params.faqs and the quote form — and nothing else. grep confirms 278/278, 268/268 … of every silo's markdown files carry a components: array (1,638 files total), yet no non-event template references .Params.components. A sample bodyguard-hire/abidjan.md has a ~200-word body plus six components (~900 words of FCDO/OSAC/ACLED-sourced regulatory and threat intelligence) that render as zero bytes of HTML.
Why it matters
These pages currently present to Google as ~250-word templated location pages with near-identical structure — the textbook "scaled content abuse" profile the June 2026 spam update (completed 26 June) enforces against. The content that would make them substantive and defensible exists; it is simply not wired to the template.
The fix
Port the {{ with .Params.components }} card-grid block from layouts/event-security/single.html (lines 84–110) into layouts/_default/single.html, above the FAQ partial. The safe printf pattern using backticks (printf `%.1fs`) is already in that source block — reuse it verbatim to avoid the escaped-quote build break documented in CLAUDE.md.
Expected impact
1,638 pages move from ~250 to ~1,100 rendered words of sourced content in one deploy. This is the highest-ROI action available and the correct first response to the spam-update exposure the brief rates MEDIUM for this repo.
Trade-off. Essentially none. The content already passed the QA gate as front matter; rendering it adds no new claims. The only risk is a template typo breaking the build, which a single local hugo --gc --minify before commit fully mitigates. Do not reformat the components' text — render as-is.

2Intent cannibalisation: three near-duplicate silos compete for one query per city

bodyguard-hire vs executive-protection vs close-protection-officers

Critical Opus for strategy Effort: L
Evidence. For every city, three pages exist targeting what searchers treat as one intent. Lagos example: Bodyguard Hire in Lagos, Executive Protection Services in Lagos, Close Protection Officers in Lagos — same city, same buyer, heavily overlapping keyword set, ~230–400 body words each. That is ~810 internally competing pages (270 cities × 3 silos).
Why it matters
Google consolidates near-duplicate intent to one URL and can discount the cluster; internal competition splits link equity and dilutes the entity signal. This is the exact pattern the unconfirmed 19 June spam-tactic movement and the 26 June update act on. The other three silos (security-drivers, residential-security, secure-airport-transfers) are genuinely distinct intents and are not part of this problem.
Options (pick one per the trade-offs)
  • A — Consolidate + canonical. Elect one money page per city (recommend close-protection-officers as the on-the-ground service term, or bodyguard-hire as the highest-volume head term) and set canonical on the other two to it. Keeps URLs live, concedes the duplicates cleanly.
  • B — Differentiate intent. Rewrite the three so each owns a distinct sub-intent: bodyguard-hire = short-term/single-principal hire, executive-protection = corporate/programme-level, close-protection-officers = the operator/vetting/credentials angle. Preserves three ranking pages if the differentiation is real.
  • C — Prune + redirect. 301 two of three into the survivor. Strongest anti-spam signal, smallest footprint, permanent URL loss.
Trade-off. A is reversible and safe but leaves crawl budget on duplicates. B keeps the most pages but only works if the rewrites are genuinely distinct — done lazily it re-creates the problem and is worse than doing nothing. C is the cleanest ranking signal but discards ~540 URLs and any equity they hold. This decision is judgement-heavy and touches ~810 pages — see the Opus note in the build plan. Do not let an execution model pick the option or improvise the differentiation; it must be handed a decided model.

3Zero structured data on the six service silos

The fallback template defines no schema block

Critical Sonnet OK Effort: S
Evidence. baseof.html exposes {{ block "schema" . }}, but only cities, services, event-security, blog, countries and index define it. The six silos fall through _default/single.html, which has no define "schema" — so 1,638 pages emit no Service, LocalBusiness, or Organization JSON-LD at all (only the FAQ microdata from the partial).
Why it matters
The June brief is explicit: entity authority and standard structured data are what Google says does matter for AI Overview citation. These pages give Google nothing to bind to an entity. Fixing it is a natural companion to Finding 1 — same file.
The fix
Add a {{ define "schema" }} block to _default/single.html emitting a Service node (with serviceType from the section, provider Organization, areaServed City from .Params.city), mirroring the event-security schema. Use jsonify on all interpolated strings to prevent quote-injection build breaks.
Trade-off. None material. Do not add aggregateRating or fabricated review counts — that is a structured-data spam violation on a YMYL site and would invite a manual action. Emit only claims the page can substantiate.

4FAQ schema is microdata, not JSON-LD

faq-accordion.html uses itemscope/itemprop on collapsed elements

HighSonnet OKEffort: S
Evidence. The partial marks up FAQPage/Question/Answer as microdata, with the answer itemprop="text" living inside a .collapse (hidden by default). No page emits FAQ JSON-LD.
Why it matters
Google parses JSON-LD most reliably and it is decoupled from the DOM's visibility state. Microdata on hidden accordion bodies is the fragile case. JSON-LD FAQ also feeds AI Overview / AI Mode answer extraction, which the brief identifies as the 12-month strategic surface.
The fix
Add a JSON-LD FAQPage emitter (ranging .Params.faqs with jsonify) in the schema block, and keep the accordion for humans. Optionally strip the now-redundant microdata attributes to avoid double-declaration.
Trade-off. Google deprecated FAQ rich results for non-government/health sites in 2023, so expect no star-style SERP snippet from this. The value is entity clarity and AI-answer eligibility, not a rich result. Low effort, so still worth doing — just calibrate expectations. Ensure the JSON-LD answer text matches the on-page answer exactly.

5No og:image or Twitter Card on any page

Social & AI preview cards render bare

HighSonnet OKEffort: S
Evidence. baseof.html emits og:image only {{ with .Params.og_image }}, and 0 content files set og_image. There are no twitter:card tags anywhere.
Why it matters
Every share on LinkedIn/X/WhatsApp and many AI/social preview surfaces show a blank card, depressing click-through on exactly the referral-driven channel this market runs on. Cheap, sitewide win.
The fix
In baseof.html: fall back og:image to a branded default (e.g. Close-Protection-2560.webp) or the page's hero; add twitter:card=summary_large_image, twitter:title, twitter:description, twitter:image; add og:site_name and og:locale=en_GB.
Trade-off. None. Purely additive head tags. Use an absolute URL for the image (absURL) or the card breaks.

6Organization entity is one line — weak for a YMYL brand

Provider Organization has a name and nothing else

HighSonnet OKEffort: S
Evidence. Across every schema block the provider is {"@type":"Organization","name":"CloseProtectionHire.com"} — no logo, url, sameAs, contactPoint, or areaServed. The homepage Organization node is equally minimal.
Why it matters
On a YMYL site, the Organization is the E-E-A-T anchor that every Service node's provider should reference. A thin entity gives Google little to trust and nothing to build a Knowledge Panel from — directly counter to the brief's "entity authority is what matters for AI Overviews" point.
The fix
Define one canonical Organization (ideally @id-referenced) in a shared partial with logo, url, contactPoint (the FormSubmit email / phone if available), areaServed, and real sameAs profiles. Reference it by @id from every Service node instead of re-declaring a bare name.
Trade-off. sameAs must point to real, live profiles — inventing them is an authenticity violation and the brief explicitly flags inauthentic signals as black-hat. If no social profiles exist yet, ship the logo/url/contactPoint now and add sameAs when profiles are real.

7Duplicate country pages

uk.md + united-kingdom.md, usa.md + united-states.md

HighSonnet OKEffort: S
Evidence. content/countries/ contains both uk.md and united-kingdom.md, and both usa.md and united-states.md — two pairs of pages targeting the same country entity.
Why it matters
Two URLs per country compete for the same query and split equity — country hubs are high in the internal hierarchy, so the dilution is disproportionate.
The fix
Choose the canonical slug per pair (recommend united-kingdom and united-states for full-word clarity, or the higher-traffic slug if data exists), 301 the other via static/_redirects, and repoint internal links. Verify no other silo pages rely on the retired slug.
Trade-off. Minor — pick the survivor by existing impressions if you have Search Console data; otherwise the full-word slug is the safe default. Confirm the redirect syntax matches the existing _redirects format before shipping.

8Add a direct-answer block for AI Overview capture

The brief's core GEO thesis, applied to the templates

MediumSonnet OK (template) / Opus (copy model)Effort: M
Evidence. The 29 June brief: "Pages that answer the query directly and completely in the first 200 words … will be the ones cited in AI Overviews." Current silo/city pages open with a scene-setting paragraph, not a crisp 40–55 word direct answer to "who provides / how do I hire X in {city}".
The fix
Add an optional answer front-matter field and render it as a styled lead block directly under the H1 (before the hero narrative). Where answer is absent, fall back to the page description. Seed the field programmatically from existing data for the top ~40 priority cities first.
Why it matters
This is the single design change that aligns the whole network with the formalised GEO guidance the brief calls the 12-month strategic signal, at near-zero ranking risk.
Trade-off. The template change is mechanical (Sonnet). Writing genuinely distinct, non-boilerplate answer copy across hundreds of pages is where quality risk lives — a repeated formula reads as spun content. Recommend authoring the answer pattern and the first tranche with Opus, then templating the rollout. Do not auto-generate identical answers with only the city name swapped.

9Thin internal link graph (~2 links per page)

Pages sit at the minimum link floor

MediumSonnet OKEffort: M
Evidence. Sampled silo bodies carry ~2 in-body links — the CLAUDE.md floor, not a network. With the components unrendered (Finding 1), even the contextual linking those sections could carry is lost.
The fix
Add a template-driven "Related security services in {city}" block (links the sibling silos for the same city) and a "Nearby / same-country cities" block, driven by front-matter city/country. This is Engine 4 (internal link graph) and needs no per-page authoring. Sequence it after Finding 2 so you are not hard-wiring links into pages you may canonicalise or prune.
Trade-off. If built before the cannibalisation decision, you may create links into pages that later get canonicalised/redirected, creating cleanup. Order matters — do Finding 2 first.

10Blog Article schema missing image and real author entity

Article node has no image; author description is hardcoded

MediumSonnet OKEffort: S
Evidence. blog/single.html emits Article with no image and no mainEntityOfPage; the author's description is a single hardcoded string ("Senior security consultant with 15+ years…") applied regardless of whether the byline is James Calloway or Marcus Webb.
The fix
Add image (the hero), mainEntityOfPage, and drive the author Person from a small author-data map so each persona gets its correct bio and (real) profile sameAs. The two personas are already defined in AGENTS.md.
Trade-off. None, provided author bios are accurate to the persona system already in use. Do not attach a real named individual's credentials to a persona byline in a way that misrepresents authorship.

11Breadcrumb is visual only — no BreadcrumbList schema

Visible breadcrumb, invisible to Google's structured parser

LowSonnet OKEffort: S
Evidence. _default/single.html (and others) render a .de-breadcrumb list but emit no BreadcrumbList JSON-LD.

Add a BreadcrumbList emitter (Home → Section → Page) in a shared partial called from the schema blocks. Improves SERP breadcrumb display and helps Google model the silo hierarchy.

Trade-off. None. Roll it into the same schema-partial work as Findings 3 and 6.

12Sitemap priority/changefreq undifferentiated

Every URL is priority 0.5, changefreq weekly

LowSonnet OKEffort: S
Evidence. hugo.toml [sitemap] sets a flat priority 0.5 / weekly across all 2,396 URLs.

Low leverage (Google largely ignores sitemap priority now), but a per-section override — homepage and city hubs higher, deep combinatorial pages lower — is a tidy, honest signal and costs nothing once you are editing config. Optional.

Trade-off. Real-world ranking impact is close to nil; do it for hygiene, not results. Deprioritise against Findings 1–7.

13Operational notes: GSC index lag & the llms.txt reality

No code change — measurement & expectation-setting

Watch
  • GSC page-indexing report lag. The brief confirms the report has not updated since 11 June. After you deploy Finding 1, do not validate re-indexing via the bulk report — use the URL Inspection tool on a sample of repaired pages instead.
  • llms.txt. robots.txt references llms.txt in a comment. The brief is explicit that Google does not read llms.txt for AI ranking. Keep it only if it serves non-Google bots; do not invest build time in it. The AI-crawler allow-list in robots.txt is already correct and worth keeping.
  • Measurement. Sitewide organic CTR benchmark is 1–2% (Ahrefs, per brief). Use position tracking (Ahrefs/Semrush), not the Semrush Sensor, to detect movement on these niche queries — the Sensor samples US head terms and will not see this network.

Where Opus is genuinely required

Almost everything here is a well-specified template edit that Sonnet executes cleanly. Two things are not mechanical and should run on Opus:

  • Step 4 — the cannibalisation decision (Finding 2). Choosing consolidate vs differentiate vs prune, and if differentiating, defining a genuinely distinct intent model for three silos across 810 pages, is judgement under ambiguity with real downside. Hand Sonnet a decided spec; do not let it choose.
  • Step 6 — the direct-answer copy pattern (Finding 8). The template is Sonnet's; the answer-writing model that stays non-boilerplate across hundreds of cities needs Opus to set the pattern and write the first tranche. Templated identical answers would re-introduce the thin-content problem.

Everything else in the plan is safe on Sonnet, provided each batch ends with a local hugo --gc --minify build check and the QA gate before commit.
