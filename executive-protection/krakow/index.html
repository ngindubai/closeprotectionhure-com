Executive protection in Krakow is a programme, not a single officer. It is the discipline a company applies to protect a travelling executive against tourist-area petty crime and periodic far-right demonstration activity: advance work on venues and routes, a written risk assessment, an operations controller, and licensed secure transport integrated with the corporate itinerary. This page is for security managers designing protection around a C-suite visit, where the need is layered, documented risk management rather than ad-hoc cover.

For short-term, single-principal cover tied to one visit, see bodyguard hire in Krakow. For the operators who staff the programme and how they are vetted, see close protection officers in Krakow.

Operational detail for Krakow

Threat and Risk Assessment: Krakow

Krakow carries a low overall risk rating for visiting executives, consistent with Poland's stable political environment and strong rule of law. The principal threat categories are petty crime in the Old Town tourist areas, drink-spiking incidents in the Kazimierz nightlife district, vehicle crime at tourist-adjacent car parks, and occasional demonstrations that may disrupt city-centre movement without prior notice. FCDO Poland travel advice (2024) specifically notes pickpocketing and bag-snatching in Rynek Glowny (the Main Market Square) and surrounding Stare Miasto streets as the most consistent risk for visiting professionals. Drink-spiking has been reported in bar and nightlife venues on Szeroka Street and in Kazimierz; executives attending corporate hospitality in these areas should maintain situational awareness and avoid accepting drinks from unverified sources. FCDO Poland 2024 further notes that demonstrations, including far-right gatherings, may occur without prior notice and can affect city-centre movement. A pre-deployment threat assessment covers current demonstration calendars and any elevated crime advisories before each assignment.

Licensing and Vetting in Poland

Private security in Poland is governed by the Act on Physical Protection of Persons and Property (Ustawa o ochronie osob i mienia, 1997, as amended 2022). Licensing is administered by the Komendant Wojewodzki Policji Malopolskiej (Regional Police Commander for Malopolska), the competent authority for Krakow and the surrounding region. Close protection officers must hold a valid Physical Protection Licence (Licencja pracownika ochrony fizycznej) issued by the Regional Police Commander. Companies providing security services must be registered in the national security operator register maintained by the Komendant Glowny Policji. Our vetting process confirms licence status against the national register, training qualification records and any adverse disciplinary findings. The 2022 amendment introduced enhanced competency requirements for officers working close protection assignments; all deployed operators meet the updated standard.

Principal Movement and Route Planning in Krakow

Krakow's executive movement zones divide into four primary areas: the Old Town (Stare Miasto, centred on Rynek Glowny -- corporate hospitality, historic centre meetings, hotel district); Kazimierz (creative and professional quarter, corporate entertaining); Nowa Huta (industrial eastern district, manufacturing sector visits); and the Malopolska Business Park and Zabierzow technology campus (approximately 10km west, the primary zone for technology and BPO sector engagements with Motorola Solutions, IBM, Capgemini and HSBC's Polish operations). Route pre-surveys are completed in advance of each principal movement, assessing tram infrastructure constraints in the Old Town pedestrian zone, planned demonstration activity, and road conditions on the DK7 and A4 routes to Zabierzow. The Old Town ring road (Planty Park) divides the vehicle-accessible perimeter from the pedestrian Stare Miasto core; vehicle staging and rapid extraction points are identified for all meetings within the inner pedestrian zone.

Airport Arrival and Departure Security: John Paul II International (KRK)

John Paul II International Airport (IATA: KRK) is located 11km west of the Old Town, with a transfer time of 15-25 minutes depending on traffic. The airport serves as the primary gateway for Krakow, handling a broad range of European and long-haul connections. CPOs meet principals at the arrivals hall, coordinate vehicle positioning at the kerbside pick-up zone and conduct a vehicle sweep before departure. The transfer route from KRK to the Old Town uses the DK7 and the ring road; alternative routing via the A4 motorway is pre-surveyed as a contingency. Departure movements include advance positioning at the departures drop-off to minimise public terminal dwell time. For private aviation, Krakow Airport handles general aviation through the dedicated FBO facility on the south apron, with direct airside access available through advance coordination with the airport operator.

Residential and Venue Security in Krakow

Executive-tier accommodation in Krakow is concentrated in the Old Town and near the Wawel area. Preferred properties include Hotel Stary (Szczepanska Street, Old Town), Sheraton Grand Krakow (Powisle, Vistula riverfront) and the Andel's by Vienna House (Pawia Street, close to the Galeria Krakowska transport hub). CPOs conduct a venue security survey on arrival, identifying emergency exits, nearest medical facilities and vehicle staging for rapid departure. Meeting venues at the Krakow Business Park (Zabierzow) and Malopolska Business Park are pre-surveyed with a focus on parking security and perimeter access control. The Kazimierz district, while lower-risk for daytime corporate entertainment, warrants enhanced situational awareness during evening hospitality events.

Medical and Emergency Protocols: Krakow

Primary medical facility: Szpital Uniwersytecki (University Hospital Krakow), Kopernika 36, telephone +48 12 424 70 00 -- the main trauma and specialist referral centre for Malopolska. Secondary facility: LUX MED Krakow (private clinic network, multiple city-centre locations). For conditions requiring specialist care not available in Krakow, medical evacuation routing to Vienna (approximately 3 hours by road or 1.5 hours by air from KRK) is pre-planned. CPOs hold current first-aid certification; a first-aid kit is carried on all principal movements. Emergency numbers: 112 (EU unified emergency), 997 (police), 999 (ambulance). The British Consulate in Krakow is available at +48 12 421 70 30 for consular support.
