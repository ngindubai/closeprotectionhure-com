Executive protection in Cape Town is a programme, not a single officer. It is the discipline a company applies when it owes a duty of care to an executive facing carjacking, follow-home robbery, and kidnapping: advance work on venues and routes, a written threat assessment, an operations controller, and Grade A PSIRA-licensed armed secure transport integrated with the corporate itinerary. This page is for security managers designing protection around a C-suite visit, where the need is layered, documented risk management.

PSIRA Grade A registration is the legal and quality baseline. The South African private security industry is large - one of the largest per capita in the world - and highly variable in quality. Regulatory compliance is a necessary but not sufficient filter; specific operational experience of Cape Town’s geography, crime patterns, and the specific risks of the Atlantic Seaboard, CBD, and V and A Waterfront environments is what distinguishes effective EP from compliance-box-ticking in this market.

What this covers

Operational detail for Cape Town

PSIRA Grade A Registration and Regulatory Compliance

All security service providers in South Africa, including close protection officers, must be registered with the Private Security Industry Regulatory Authority (PSIRA) under the Private Security Industry Regulation Act (No. 56 of 2001). Close protection specifically requires Grade A registration, the highest PSIRA category, which involves additional training requirements above the standard security officer grades. Clients should verify Grade A PSIRA registration for both the operating company and the individual CPOs before any engagement. The PSIRA register is publicly searchable.

CBD Crime Concentration and Route Management

Cape Town's Central Business District has documented above-average concentrations of street crime, vehicle crime, and opportunistic robbery compared to the broader metropolitan area. South African Police Service (SAPS) crime statistics for the Cape Town Central police precinct confirm this. Corporate EP in the CBD addresses the specific transit risks during principal movements between hotels, offices, and Cape Town International Convention Centre events, with vehicle selection, route pre-planning, and close positioning calibrated to the ambient crime level in each specific zone.

Atlantic Seaboard HNWI Cover

The Atlantic Seaboard - Camps Bay, Clifton, Bantry Bay, and Sea Point - concentrates Cape Town's residential HNWI population and is a primary location for visiting high-net-worth principals. Despite its aspirational profile, the Atlantic Seaboard is not crime-free: vehicle crime, residential burglary, and targeted theft of luxury items are documented risks. EP for principals resident or visiting in this zone covers residential security assessment, vehicle and route security, close protection during beach and outdoor social activities, and awareness of the specific crime patterns in the beachfront and main road corridors.

V and A Waterfront and Corporate Entertainment Security

The V and A Waterfront is Cape Town's primary corporate entertainment and hotel district, concentrating high-end retail, hospitality, and conference facilities around the historic Alfred and Victoria basins. It is also one of Cape Town's highest-footfall tourist destinations, with the crowd density and transit volumes that accompany that. EP at the Waterfront addresses the specific access and egress challenges of a closed waterfront environment, the vehicle and pedestrian access norms of the precinct security infrastructure, and close protection during the corporate entertainment engagements that routinely bring senior executives to this area.

Vehicle and Transit Security

Vehicle security is a primary EP consideration in Cape Town. SAPS statistics document significant levels of hijacking and vehicle-related crime in greater Cape Town, with specific chokepoints on approach routes from Cape Town International Airport (CPT) and in the approaches to the CBD via the N2. Airport transfers are a high-risk window for predictable principal movement and are planned accordingly: vehicle pre-positioning, route pre-assessment, alternative routes, and counter-surveillance covering the transfer from arrivals. Vehicle selection, driver training, and route variation are standard EP components for all Cape Town engagements.

Advance Work and Venue Security Assessment

Given Cape Town's elevated ambient crime level, advance work is a more material EP component than in lower-risk cities. Venue security assessment covers the specific access, egress, and ambient security infrastructure of every location on the principal's itinerary - from CBD office buildings and Waterfront hotels to private residences and outdoor event venues on the Cape Peninsula. Advance work outputs include primary and alternative vehicle drop-off points, emergency contact protocols, and a real-time liaison arrangement with SAPS for the duration of the principal's visit where the engagement scope warrants it.

Frequently Asked Questions

PSIRA Grade A is the highest registration category under the Private Security Industry Regulation Act (No. 56 of 2001). It covers close protection officers and requires training and assessment beyond the standard security officer categories (Grades B through E). Grade A registration is the legal requirement for providing close protection services in South Africa. Clients engaging EP in Cape Town should verify Grade A specifically - not general PSIRA registration - for both the company and individual CPOs. The PSIRA register is publicly searchable and verification is straightforward. Grade A registration is the baseline, not the ceiling; operational experience and track record are the additional quality filters.

Cape Town’s risk profile is among the more serious of any significant international business city that corporate visitors routinely travel to. SAPS crime statistics document high rates of robbery, vehicle hijacking, and residential burglary across the metropolitan area, with specific concentrations in and around the CBD. The Atlantic Seaboard and V and A Waterfront are better managed than many other areas but are not crime-free. For senior executives, HNWI clients, and principals with any elevated profile, professional EP is strongly advisable. Visitors who would not normally require EP in London or Singapore should reconsider that baseline for Cape Town.

The airport transfer from Cape Town International Airport (CPT) to the CBD or Atlantic Seaboard is the highest-risk predictable transit window for most principals. The N2 corridor approaching the city has documented hijacking risk, and the predictability of airport transfers - known arrival time, known destination - makes them a specific planning priority. Beyond the airport transfer, the approaches to the CBD through De Waal Drive and the Foreshore, and movement on the Atlantic Seaboard main road (Victoria Road) at night, are specific risk windows. All are addressed through vehicle pre-positioning, route pre-assessment, and counter-surveillance.

Atlantic Seaboard residential EP for visiting HNWI clients is structured around the specific geography of the area: a narrow coastal strip between the mountain and the ocean, with limited approach and egress routes and a concentration of high-value residential properties that present documented residential burglary and theft risks. EP for principals in Camps Bay or Clifton covers perimeter assessment of the accommodation, domestic staff awareness briefing, vehicle and route security for all movements, and close protection during outdoor and social activities. The social environment of the Atlantic Seaboard - beach clubs, restaurants on Camps Bay Drive, private events - requires a CPO calibration that is both operationally alert and socially appropriate.
