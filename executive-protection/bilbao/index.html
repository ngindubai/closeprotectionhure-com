What this covers

Operational detail for Bilbao

Licensing and CPO Standards

Executive protection in Spain is regulated by Ley 5/2014 de Seguridad Privada. CPOs must hold a valid Tarjeta de Identificacion Profesional (TIP) issued by the Direccion General de la Policia (DGP). Security firms must be registered with the Servicio de Empresas de Seguridad (SES). EU professionals with equivalent qualifications may obtain a TIP; non-EU nationals must engage through a Spanish-licensed firm. Bilbao operates within the Basque Autonomous Community, where the Ertzaintza (Basque Autonomous Police) operates alongside Policia Nacional and Guardia Civil. Coordination protocols with the Ertzaintza differ from those in other Spanish regions and providers should confirm their familiarity with local procedures. Deployments are unarmed as standard. Clients should request sight of TIP credentials and SES registration before engagement.

Threat Assessment

Bilbao carries a low overall risk rating. The FCDO Spain 2024 advisory identifies petty crime as the primary concern, with pickpocketing in the Casco Viejo (Siete Calles) and around the Guggenheim Museum on busy tourist days. Demonstrations occur in the Gran Via and around the Arriaga Theatre area. Bilbao's valley and hillside geography, with its complex urban logistics, presents operational considerations for CPO teams managing ground movement in a city where standard routing assumptions do not apply. ETA disbanded in 2018 per the Basque Government official statement and presents no current threat; this context is noted for client awareness only.

Principal Movement Security

Principal movement planning in Bilbao focuses on the Ensanche business district (where Iberdrola and BBVA maintain significant offices), the Guggenheim Museum and Abandoibarra waterfront for HNWI and cultural visits, and the Casco Viejo for leisure movements. The city's hillside geography and the Nervion river valley create a constrained road network with limited bridge crossings, which CPO teams factor into contingency routing. Gran Via de Don Diego Lopez de Haro is the main commercial artery and the approach to most hotel accommodation and corporate offices. BEC Bilbao at Barakaldo requires cross-river transfer planning.

Corporate and Event Security

The BEC (Bilbao Exhibition Centre) in Barakaldo is the primary large-scale event venue for the region, hosting energy, industrial and technology conferences that draw senior executives from Iberdrola and BBVA. The Palacio Euskalduna congress centre on the Abandoibarra waterfront serves as Bilbao's principal city-centre conference facility. Guggenheim Museum events attract HNWI and cultural-sector principals. Aste Nagusia (the city's main summer festival in August) brings very large crowd concentrations that substantially increase opportunistic crime exposure and complicate principal movement in the Casco Viejo and along the waterfront.

Secure Transit

Bilbao Airport (BIO) is approximately 9km north of the city centre, typically 15-20 minutes by secure vehicle. CPOs conduct airside-to-vehicle transfers with real-time operations-controller tracking. The airport sits on the northern Txorierri valley approach to the city, providing a direct route via the BI-30 bypass that avoids the most congested central districts. For principals attending BEC Bilbao in Barakaldo, the transfer route from BIO can proceed directly without entering the city centre, reducing transit time and public-space exposure. San Sebastian (Donostia-San Sebastian, EAS) is approximately 100km east for regional connectivity.

Crisis and Medical Response

The primary emergency facilities in Bilbao are Hospital Universitario de Basurto (the main Bilbao city hospital) and Hospital Universitario Cruces in Barakaldo (the major regional trauma centre), located near BEC Bilbao. Emergency numbers in Spain: unified emergency 112, Policia Nacional 091, Guardia Civil 062, Ertzaintza (Basque police) 088. British nationals should contact the British Embassy Madrid on +34 91 714 6300; US nationals the US Embassy Madrid on +34 91 587 2200. CPO teams carry first-aid kits, hold pre-briefed routes to both hospitals and include Ertzaintza contact protocols in all pre-deployment briefings.
