Executive protection in San Salvador is a programme, not a single officer. It is the discipline a company applies to protect a travelling executive against a residual gang environment and state surveillance and monitoring: advance work on venues and routes, a written risk assessment, an operations controller, and Ministerio de Justicia registered secure transport integrated with the corporate itinerary. This page is for security managers designing protection around a C-suite visit, where the need is layered, documented risk management.

Executive Risk Profile: San Salvador

San Salvador's executive risk profile has changed substantially since the Bukele government's Regimen de Excepcion, implemented from March 2022. The homicide rate fell from 106 per 100,000 in 2015 to approximately 2.4 per 100,000 in 2023 (Salvadoran National Police data), and gang-visible activity in the city has been dramatically reduced following mass arrests of over 75,000 suspected gang members. FCDO now classifies El Salvador at a high degree of caution (improved from previous level), and the US State Department designates it Level 2: Exercise Increased Caution (2026). Residual risks remain: some areas outside Zona Rosa and Escalon retain elevated crime risk, and the Regimen de Excepcion has introduced an active surveillance and monitoring environment for all visitors that requires specific EP planning awareness.

Pre-Advance and Venue Assessment

Pre-advance for San Salvador EP covers the Oscar Arnulfo Romero International Airport transfer route (44 kilometres), the principal's accommodation in Zona Rosa or Escalon, and all meeting venues. The current environment requires that pre-advance work also assess the surveillance exposure of planned venues and meetings: under the Regimen de Excepcion, associations with individuals or organisations that have prior gang or opposition associations can draw law enforcement attention to the principal. All meeting venues are cleared for access, egress, and current legal status of the host organisation before the principal's arrival.

Protective Intelligence in El Salvador

Protective intelligence for San Salvador deployments must account for two distinct threat streams: the residual criminal environment in areas outside the Zona Rosa and Escalon corridor, and the political surveillance environment under the Regimen de Excepcion. Intelligence draws on FCDO El Salvador advice (2024), US State Department reporting, Salvadoran National Police public data, and operator network intelligence. The surveillance dimension means that intelligence monitoring of the principal's planned associations and meetings is included as a standard element, not an optional add-on, for San Salvador deployments.

Motorcade and Movement Security

San Salvador EP movements use discreet, low-profile vehicles without corporate insignia. El Salvador's surveillance environment means that a visibly armoured vehicle formation can attract law enforcement attention that creates its own complications. The standard formation is a principal vehicle and a discreet follow car, both soft-skin and anonymous. All movements are confined to the Zona Rosa, Escalon, and major commercial arterials. Counter-surveillance protocols apply but are adapted to the current environment: in El Salvador, the risk of appearing to evade state surveillance is itself a risk that EP planners account for.

El Salvador EP Operator Licensing

Executive protection operations in El Salvador are regulated under the Law on Regulation of Security Services, administered by the Ministry of Justice and Public Security. EP company licences and individual officer credentials from the Ministry should be verified before deployment. The Regimen de Excepcion has given the Ministry of Justice and Public Security expanded enforcement powers; EP operators in El Salvador must be well-versed in the current legal provisions and how they affect close protection operations. Armed EP operations require specific Ministry authorisation. Foreign operators work through Ministry-authorised Salvadoran partner companies.

Crisis Response and Evacuation Planning

San Salvador EP crisis plans cover: medical emergency (Hospital de Diagnostico for private acute care; Hospital Nacional Rosales for public emergency), armed attack response protocol, and extraction routes from the principal's accommodation and each confirmed meeting venue. The Romero airport at 44 kilometres is the primary evacuation gateway; extraction planning confirms current route conditions and airport operational status. The Regimen de Excepcion environment adds a specific layer: crisis plans include a protocol for the scenario in which law enforcement action affects the principal or the EP team, with pre-agreed legal representation contact established before deployment.
