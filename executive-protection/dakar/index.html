Executive protection in Dakar is a programme, not a single officer. It is the discipline a company applies to protect a travelling executive against terrorism awareness and periodic civil unrest: advance work on venues and routes, a written threat assessment, an operations controller, and secure transport integrated with the corporate itinerary. What this covers

Operational detail for Dakar

Senegal Ministry of Interior Licensing

Private security in Senegal is regulated by the Ministry of Interior. Executive protection companies must hold valid ministry registration. The Senegalese regulatory framework is functioning and more developed than several West African neighbours. Foreign EP personnel operate in an unarmed capacity alongside licensed local operators. All our Dakar EP partners hold current Ministry of Interior registration and carry corporate references from international clients in the development finance, diplomatic, energy, and private equity sectors. Verify operator standing before engagement.

Principal Risk Profiling for West Africa Operations

Dakar's EP programme serves a distinct principal category: senior executives of development finance institutions (World Bank, AfDB, IFC), private equity and infrastructure investors with West Africa portfolios, energy sector principals (hydrocarbon exploration, renewable energy development), and senior diplomatic and government affairs visitors. Each category carries a different risk profile. Development finance principals may attract protest or demonstration attention linked to austerity or project impacts; private equity principals face surveillance and commercial intelligence risk; diplomatic principals carry a government-linked profile. EP planning begins with a specific principal risk assessment that addresses the Dakar context rather than a generic African capital template.

Sahel Terrorism Awareness in EP Operations

Senegal maintains a significantly better security profile than its Sahel neighbours, but the proximity of JNIM and Islamic State Sahel Province operations in Mali, Burkina Faso, and Niger is a standing EP context. The terrorism risk in Dakar specifically is assessed as lower than border areas, but FCDO maintains an elevated terrorism advisory for Senegal. EP operations apply Sahel terrorism awareness to venue selection: government area approaches, hotels with Western executive concentration, public gatherings. Advance CPO work evaluates venue threat profiles against the established Sahel soft-target templates before confirming any meeting or dining location. Source: FCDO Senegal travel advisory, 2026.

Political Unrest and Demonstration Management

Dakar experienced significant political unrest in 2023-2024 linked to the Sonko-Faye political process. The post-election environment has stabilised, but Dakar retains a protest-active political culture. EP operations include standing political monitoring: advance assessment of demonstration risk on planned route days, pre-confirmed alternate routing away from the Plateau government zone during tension periods, and a shelter-in-place protocol at vetted accommodation if demonstrations develop. For principals whose sector or profile might be associated with contested policy -- IMF austerity, extractive industry, government relations -- the political monitoring is extended to include sector-specific protest indicators. Source: FCDO Senegal travel advisory, 2026.

Les Almadies, Plateau, and 65km Airport Operations

EP operations in Dakar are structured around the principal's accommodation zone (typically the Radisson Blu or Terrou-Bi in the Plateau, or Les Almadies coastal residential) and the meeting location corridor. The 65km Blaise Diagne Airport transfer is the most extended single exposure element in any Dakar EP assignment: the 60-90 minute motorway corridor requires pre-confirmed vehicle, inside-terminal collection, and a monitored route that has no shorter alternative. The EP advance team confirms the transfer route and vehicle staging before every arrival or departure. Movements within the Plateau business zone and the Les Almadies residential strip are the operational core.
