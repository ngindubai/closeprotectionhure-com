Executive protection in Basra is a programme, not a single officer. It is the discipline a company applies when it owes a duty of care to an executive facing armed militia activity and targeted energy-sector attacks: advance work on venues and routes, a written threat assessment, an operations controller, and PSCD-licensed armed secure transport integrated with the corporate itinerary. This page is for security managers designing protection around a C-suite visit, where the need is layered, documented risk management.

What this covers

Operational detail for Basra

Threat and Risk Assessment: Basra

Basra carries a high risk rating. The US State Dept maintains a Level 4 Do Not Travel advisory for Iraq (2024), and FCDO advises against all but essential travel to Basra Governorate (FCDO Iraq travel advice, 2024). Executives who must operate in Basra do so in connection with the major oil and gas concessions -- BP's Rumaila field, ExxonMobil's West Qurna 1, TotalEnergies' Halfaya, and Lukoil's West Qurna 2 -- and port logistics operations at Umm Qasr. The principal threat categories are armed militia activity, kidnapping for ransom, mortar and rocket attacks on hotel areas and energy infrastructure, and protest violence. Iran-aligned militia groups (PMF/Hashd al-Shaabi) have been implicated in targeted attacks on foreign nationals and energy infrastructure in Basra Governorate (US State Dept 2024). The 2018 Basra protests saw oil company offices directly targeted and multiple fatalities among demonstrators and security personnel; residual tension around energy sector presence remains a baseline risk. FCDO specifically flags kidnapping risk for foreigners in Iraq as a distinct and serious threat category. Every deployment to Basra requires a current threat assessment reviewed no more than 72 hours before travel, anti-kidnap planning, and a communications protocol agreed with the client organisation before departure.

Licensing and Vetting in Iraq

Private security in Iraq is governed by the Private Security Companies Licensing Bureau (PSCL) under the Ministry of Interior. The legal framework derives from Order 3 of 2005 as amended by subsequent Iraqi legislation. All security companies operating in Iraq must hold a valid PSCL licence; individual operators require MOI authorisation for their specific deployment role. Armed teams require a weapons carriage permit for each specific assignment -- permits are site-specific and assignment-specific, not general. Weapons type and quantity permitted are specified in the permit. Our vetting process confirms PSCL licence status, MOI authorisation records, and weapons permit validity for each deployed operator. Given the complexity of the Iraqi licensing environment and the risks of operating with unlicensed or under-authorised teams, all Basra deployments are subject to enhanced due diligence including direct confirmation from the PSCL of licence currency before mobilisation.

Principal Movement and Route Planning in Basra

Movement in Basra is treated as inherently high-risk and all routes are treated as potentially surveilled or compromised. The four primary movement zones are: the Sheraton/Al Basrah Hotel district (the principal zone for visiting executives and the starting point for all movements); the Shatt al-Arab waterfront area (city centre, limited movement for executives without direct operational need); Rumaila oil field (90km northwest, the primary BP operational area, route via the M14/Highway 6 corridor); and Al Faw peninsula and Umm Qasr Port (port and logistics sector, southern Basra Governorate). All routes are pre-surveyed before each movement, assessing current militia checkpoints, road closure information from the relevant energy company security teams, and crowd activity. Movement timings are varied to avoid predictable patterns. Low-profile vehicles appropriate to the local environment are used -- conspicuous armoured SUVs that mark occupants as high-value targets are avoided where the threat profile permits softer options. All movements are tracked in real time from the operations base.

Airport Arrival and Departure Security: Basra International (BSR)

Basra International Airport (IATA: BSR) is located 20km from the city centre. The airport serves domestic Iraqi routes and limited international connections; most international principals arrive via Baghdad (BGW) and transfer to Basra by fixed-wing or rotary charter, or overland with a full escort. CPO teams position at the arrival point -- terminal landside or airside on the general aviation apron, depending on the arrival type -- and conduct a full vehicle sweep before departure. The 20km transfer from BSR to the city or hotel zone is treated as a high-risk movement; route pre-survey includes current militia checkpoint locations and traffic conditions. Helicopter transfer options from BSR are coordinated for principals whose threat profile warrants aviation over road movement. Departure is pre-coordinated with airport security and, where applicable, the relevant oil company ground security team to minimise dwell time in the terminal.

Residential and Venue Security in Basra

The primary executive accommodation in Basra is the Sheraton Basra (Corniche, Shatt al-Arab waterfront area) and the Al Basrah Hotel, both of which have established relationships with energy sector security teams. CPOs conduct a venue security survey on arrival, confirming emergency egress routes, the location of hardened safe areas, vehicle access for rapid extraction, and the hotel's own security resources including CCTV coverage and access control. Meeting venues at Rumaila field operations bases and at the Umm Qasr port logistics zone are coordinated in advance with the relevant field security team, which retains primacy for on-site security within the concession perimeter. Residential security assessments are conducted for long-stay executives accommodated outside the primary hotel zone. Medical routing from each residential venue to the nearest treatment facility is established before the detail commences.

Medical and Emergency Protocols: Basra

Primary medical facility within Basra city: Basra Teaching Hospital. Medical capability in Basra is limited relative to the risk environment; for any serious casualty, medical evacuation is the primary response. Pre-planned evacuation routing covers three options: Erbil (EBL, northern Iraq, approximately 450km, suitable for fixed-wing evacuation where weather and route permit); Kuwait City (KWI, approximately 90km, the primary evacuation point for southern Basra incidents by road or helicopter); and Amman, Jordan (AMM, suitable for severe cases requiring specialist care beyond regional capability). Medical evacuation insurance with a provider covering Iraq is mandatory for all principals. CPOs hold current Battlefield First Aid (BFA) or Trauma First Aid (TFA) certification and carry comprehensive trauma kits including tourniquets, haemostatic agents and chest seals on all movements. Emergency numbers: 104 (police), 115 (ambulance). The British Embassy in Baghdad is the primary consular contact: +964 790 192 6280.
