Operational detail for Miami

Florida PSA Chapter 493 EP Licensing

Executive protection CPOs in Florida operate under Florida Statutes Chapter 493 (Security Officers), administered by the Florida Division of Licensing within the Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services. Class D Security Officer licences cover unarmed security personnel; Class G (Statewide Firearm) licences are required for armed CPOs. EP companies operating in Florida must hold Class B (Private Investigative Agency) or Class BB licences and Class D Security Agency licences as applicable to their services. CPOs performing personal protection roles may also require specific Class C (Private Investigator) licensing for aspects of protective intelligence work. Clients should confirm the operating company's agency licence class and the individual CPO's Class D (and Class G if armed) status through the Florida Division of Licensing public registry.

HNWI and Luxury Sector Threat Context

Miami's concentration of visible wealth creates a specific EP threat environment centred on HNWI targeting rather than general population crime. FCDO USA travel advice (2024) and FBI Miami Field Office crime data document targeted robbery of high-net-worth individuals as an organised activity in South Florida. Luxury watch and jewellery theft from HNWI individuals is a documented and rising concern in the Miami area. Miami-Dade County's carjacking statistics have risen in recent years. For EP purposes, the Miami threat picture is defined by organised criminal activity targeting predictable HNWI behaviour patterns: visible luxury display, known hotel and venue attendance, and predictable transfer timing for seasonal resident and event-period visitor principals.

Art Basel and Major Event EP Planning

Art Basel Miami Beach (December) is South Florida's highest-profile HNWI event, concentrating international collectors, gallery principals, studio owners, and high-value asset movement. EP planning for Art Basel covers: principal protection during gallery walk-throughs and VIP preview events at the Miami Beach Convention Center, secure transport co-ordination for art acquisition movements, personal protection at private collector dinners in South Beach and Brickell, and awareness of the specific robbery and fraud targeting that follows Art Basel's public concentration of HNWI individuals. The Miami Formula 1 Grand Prix (May, Hard Rock Stadium) and major entertainment events create secondary demand peaks with similar HNWI protection requirements.

Advance Work and Miami Venue Assessment

Miami EP advance work covers the principal's hotel, event venues, private residences for extended-stay principals, and yacht and marina access locations. The Four Seasons Hotel Miami and Mandarin Oriental Miami in Brickell, the Fontainebleau Miami Beach and Faena Hotel Miami Beach on Miami Beach, and the COMO Metropolitan Miami Beach are the primary venues for senior corporate and HNWI arrivals. Advance work at Miami Beach venues requires specific attention to the pedestrianised areas and vehicle-restriction zones on Ocean Drive and Collins Avenue during Art Basel and other major event periods. For principals with Coconut Grove or Coral Gables residential addresses, advance work covers route planning on the Palmetto Expressway and US-1 corridor.

Maritime EP Integration

Miami's significant HNWI maritime activity creates EP requirements that integrate land-based close protection with yacht and marina security logistics. Principals arriving by private yacht to Coconut Grove Marina, Island Gardens on Watson Island, or the Ritz-Carlton Coconut Grove marina facilities require CPO vessel-to-vehicle transition management. Vessel-board security, crew vetting, and marina approach awareness are relevant components for HNWI principals with South Florida yacht operations. EP teams accompanying principals on day trips to the Bahamas via private or charter aviation from Opa-locka Executive Airport or Fort Lauderdale Executive Airport manage the multi-modal transition logistics in both departure and return movements.
