What this covers

Operational detail for Medan

Licensing and CPO Standards

Executive protection in Medan operates under POLRI, Indonesia's national police, alongside Ministry of Manpower oversight, the same national framework used in Jakarta and Surabaya. Firms need valid POLRI registration and officers need individual certification. Armed protection requires specific police authorisation and is uncommon in commercial work, so the large majority of Medan details run unarmed.

Threat Assessment

The FCDO assesses that terrorists are likely to try to carry out attacks in Indonesia, and that national picture covers Medan as much as anywhere else in the country, with risk assessed as heightened around religious holidays and elections. The city's more distinctive factor is geographic rather than political: FCDO advice advises against all travel within 5km of the crater of Mount Sinabung in Karo Regency, roughly 70km away, a volcano that erupted in 2021 and showed renewed activity in May 2026. Neither threat is acute for most visits, but both feed directly into itinerary planning for principals travelling beyond the city centre.

Principal Movement Security

Medan's corporate visitor base looks different from Jakarta's or Surabaya's. North Sumatra is one of Indonesia's largest palm oil producing regions, and companies including Asian Agri run substantial operations from the city, so a Medan itinerary more often involves a plantation or mill visit in the surrounding regencies than a single office meeting. Those site visits are planned as distinct provincial ground movements, with their own route, timing, and, where relevant, volcanic-alert considerations.

Corporate and Event Security

The annual PALMEX trade event reflects the palm oil sector's weight in Medan's corporate calendar, and industry gatherings of this kind bring their own venue and crowd-management requirements. Outside such events, corporate security in Medan is a quieter proposition, centred on office visits in the Kesawan and Setia Budi business corridors and hotel-based meetings in the Medan Polonia district.

Secure Transit

Kualanamu International Airport (KNO), which replaced Polonia Airport after its closure to commercial traffic in July 2013, sits roughly 39km from central Medan. Principals typically arrive here and transfer by Railink train, around 30 to 40 minutes, or by security-driven car, 60 to 90 minutes depending on traffic. Executives combining a Medan visit with Lake Toba travel should plan the roughly 170km, three-to-four hour road leg to Parapat as a separate day's movement rather than a short add-on.

Crisis and Medical Response

Indonesia's emergency number is 112. RSUP H. Adam Malik, +62 61 4533666, is Medan's national referral hospital and the standard pre-planned medical destination for serious incidents. BASARNAS search and rescue on 115 is a relevant contingency resource given the province's volcanic and flood-related hazard profile, particularly for any provincial leg of an itinerary.
