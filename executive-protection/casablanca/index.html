Executive protection in Casablanca is a programme, not a single officer. It is the discipline a company applies to protect a travelling executive against terrorism awareness and opportunistic theft: advance work on venues and routes, a written threat assessment, an operations controller, and Law 27-06 licensed secure transport integrated with the corporate itinerary. This page is for security managers designing protection around a C-suite visit, where the need is layered, documented risk management rather than ad-hoc cover.

Licensed operators holding current Ministry of Interior authorisation under Law 27-06 deliver all Casablanca assignments. The unarmed standard is appropriate for the city’s risk level, with the option for Gendarmerie coordination on assignments requiring armed support for higher threat profiles. Mohammed V International Airport transfers are managed with pre-positioned vehicles on the Route Cotiere approach to Ain Diab and the CFC complex.

Female principals receive a specific security plan that addresses Morocco’s documented pattern of unwanted attention: route selection, close escort in public areas, and a specific venue review for the whole itinerary. What this covers

Operational detail for Casablanca

Threat Assessment

Casablanca carries a low overall risk rating for most corporate visitors. The primary concerns are a genuine background terrorism threat and a strict legal framework that catches unprepared visitors. Morocco's counter-terrorism services regularly disrupt Daesh-inspired cells. In December 2018, two Scandinavian tourists were murdered near Marrakech by attackers with documented Daesh links, demonstrating that the threat extends to locations associated with foreign visitors. The FCDO rates terrorism in Morocco as likely. High-footfall hotels, transport hubs, and tourist areas carry the most relevant exposure. Source: FCDO Morocco travel advice, April 2026.

Ministry of Interior Licensed Operators

Morocco's private security sector is regulated by the Ministry of Interior under Law 27-06. Security companies must hold current government licensing and individual operators require formal accreditation and background verification. Standard private security in Morocco operates on an unarmed basis. Our Casablanca partners hold current Ministry of Interior authorisation, and documentation is provided to clients as part of the pre-deployment package.

Mohammed V International Airport Transfer

Mohammed V International Airport (CMN) is located approximately 30 kilometres south of Casablanca. Pre-arranged vetted transport is provided from CMN to the principal hotel and meeting venues in the Maarif, Gauthier, and Ain Diab commercial districts. Casablanca Finance City (CFC), the primary international business district, is the central hub for senior-level corporate engagements and pre-advance is completed there before the principal's arrival.

Legal Compliance Briefing

Morocco's legal environment carries specific risks for foreign principals who are not properly briefed. Drug possession carries long prison sentences; alcohol is only available at licensed establishments; LGBT activity and sex outside marriage are criminal offences; evangelical or proselytising materials are illegal. Photography of military or government infrastructure should be avoided. These laws apply to all visitors without exception and form part of the mandatory pre-deployment briefing for every Casablanca assignment.

Medina and Crowded Area Protocol

Motorcycle drive-by theft of jewellery, handbags, and visible valuables is documented in Casablanca's medina areas and busy commercial streets. Thieves operate in pairs from motorcycles, the attacks are fast and often involve physical force, and recovery of stolen items is negligible. Discreet dress, low-visibility accessories, and close escort in medina environments are standard elements of the Casablanca executive protection brief. Female principals face elevated attention in Morocco and receive a specific security plan.

CFC and Business District Coverage

Casablanca Finance City and the broader business district around Maarif and Gauthier provide the primary operating environment for senior corporate visits. Major international hotels in Ain Diab have established security infrastructure. Pre-advance at hotel properties and CFC meeting venues covers room allocation review, perimeter assessment, and liaison with venue security management. Counter-surveillance protocols are applied for any HNWI or high-profile principal visiting Casablanca's banking and investment community.
