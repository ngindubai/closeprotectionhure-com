What this covers

Operational detail for Kyiv

FCDO Advises Against All Travel

The FCDO advises against all travel to Ukraine. This page supports those with essential requirements that make a Kyiv visit unavoidable: humanitarian organisations, media, diplomatic personnel, reconstruction contractors, legal and financial professionals with non-delegable obligations, and family circumstances. Russia conducts regular drone and missile strikes on Kyiv, targeting energy infrastructure, commercial buildings, and residential areas. Executive protection in Kyiv operates within a threat environment that has no peacetime equivalent. Source: FCDO Ukraine travel advice, 2026.

Wartime Operator Capability

Our Kyiv partners have operated continuously in Ukraine since 2022. Operator capability includes air raid response, shelter location mapping across the principal's planned operating area, strike pattern awareness, and wartime route planning. The standard close protection skill set is a baseline; Kyiv-specific operational knowledge of the conflict environment is the differentiating capability. Assignments are subject to current conditions and require a direct assessment conversation before deployment.

Overland Entry and Transit Planning

Ukrainian airspace is closed to civil aviation. Standard entry is by rail or road from a neighbouring hub, most commonly Warsaw or Rzeszow in Poland. The Kyiv rail connection from the Polish border has demonstrated notable resilience despite infrastructure targeting. Border crossing times are variable. A Kyiv assignment requires route and timing assessment for both arrival and departure, with contingency planning for rail disruption after infrastructure strikes. There is no rapid departure option from Ukraine once inside.

Strike Response and Shelter Protocol

Air raid alerts provide minutes of notice for incoming drone and missile attacks. Shelter in the nearest designated basement or reinforced structure immediately on alert. No business consideration justifies remaining in an exposed position during an alert. Pre-advance on the principal's route in Kyiv maps designated shelters at each waypoint before movement begins. Hotels recommended for Kyiv assignments have bunker-standard basement capacity. Strikes have reached central Kyiv commercial and hotel areas.

Critical Infrastructure and Power Planning

Ukraine's power grid has been systematically targeted, and planned outages of eight to sixteen hours daily are standard in Kyiv alongside unplanned outages following strikes. Essential operations require generator-powered accommodation. Mobile networks remain functional under most conditions. Satellite communication devices provide backup for periods of mobile disruption near strike zones. All Kyiv assignment logistics include power contingency planning from the outset.

Pechersk and Diplomatic Quarter Operations

Pechersk is Kyiv's government and diplomatic quarter and has the highest concentration of security infrastructure in the city. The principal hotel zone for senior visitors is the established international hotel cluster in this district. Pre-advance at destination hotels confirms bunker location, generator capacity, and security management procedures. Movement in Podil and the commercial areas of central Kyiv is planned with shelter mapping and current strike-pattern awareness applied throughout.
