Executive protection in Port Harcourt is a programme, not a single officer. It is the discipline a company applies when it owes a duty of care to an executive facing kidnapping, militancy, and waterway piracy: advance work on venues and routes, a written threat assessment, an operations controller, and NSCDC-registered armed secure transport integrated with the corporate itinerary. This page is for security managers designing protection around a C-suite visit, where the need is layered, documented risk management.

The Port Harcourt EP environment

The threat environment in Port Harcourt warrants a structured EP programme for senior principals, with armed capability and armoured vehicles as the recommended configuration. Source: FCDO Nigeria travel advice (2026).

An EP programme in Port Harcourt covers the full principal movement cycle: advance work at all venues, close escort during movements, accommodation security liaison, daily route variation, a documented emergency action plan, and medevac provider confirmation before arrival.

Operational detail for Port Harcourt

Regulatory Framework for EP in Nigeria

Executive protection operations in Port Harcourt are delivered through operators licensed by the relevant Nigeria security regulatory authority. All EP providers must hold current licensing and provide documentation before deployment. Source: Nigeria security regulatory framework.

Principal Movement Programme

The EP programme for Port Harcourt covers the full principal movement cycle: airport arrivals, hotel accommodation, meetings at government offices or corporate premises, site visits where applicable, and airport departure. Advance work at each venue is completed before the principal's arrival. All movement routes are pre-assessed.

Threat and Risk Assessment

A pre-deployment risk assessment is completed for every Port Harcourt EP engagement, covering the current threat environment, the principal's specific profile and activities, and any heightened risk factors such as deal-related targeting, political calendar, or geographic exposures. Source: FCDO Nigeria travel advice (2026).

Team Configuration and Vehicle

EP team configuration in Port Harcourt is based on the principal's threat assessment. Armed CPO with an armoured or reinforced vehicle is the recommended configuration given the documented threat environment. Driver selection is a core part of the programme, with local knowledge providing route flexibility.

Medical and Emergency Action Plan

Every Port Harcourt EP programme includes a documented emergency action plan covering medical emergency, vehicle compromise, political demonstration response, and shelter-in-place procedures. The plan names the receiving medical facility, evacuation routing, and the operations controller's emergency activation protocol.
