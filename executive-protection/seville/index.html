What this covers

Operational detail for Seville

Licensing and CPO Standards

Executive protection in Spain is governed by Ley 5/2014 de Seguridad Privada. CPOs must hold a valid Tarjeta de Identificacion Profesional (TIP) issued by the Direccion General de la Policia (DGP). Security firms operating in Seville must be registered with the Servicio de Empresas de Seguridad (SES). EU nationals with equivalent professional qualifications can obtain a TIP; non-EU professionals must engage through a Spanish-licensed firm. All assignments are unarmed as standard. Clients should request sight of a provider's TIP cards and SES firm registration before confirming any engagement.

Threat Assessment

Seville carries a low overall risk rating. The FCDO Spain 2024 advisory identifies pickpocketing and bag-snatching as the primary concerns, concentrated in Barrio Santa Cruz, around the cathedral, along Avenida de la Constitucion and on buses and trams. Scooter bag-snatching is a noted pattern. Demonstrations occur occasionally in the city centre. Festival periods, particularly Semana Santa (Holy Week) and Feria de Abril, bring very large crowd concentrations that substantially increase opportunistic crime exposure and complicate principal movement for executives and HNWI visitors.

Principal Movement Security

Movement planning in Seville centres on the historic core around the Alcazar, cathedral and Barrio Santa Cruz for HNWI leisure visits, and the Isla de la Cartuja business district and San Pablo area for corporate appointments. CPOs operating in the historic centre manage pedestrian-priority zones where vehicle access is restricted, requiring foot-mobile close cover on approaches to key venues. During Semana Santa and Feria, advance route planning is critical as major road closures and crowd barriers affect all standard approaches to the city centre and hotel districts.

Corporate and Event Security

Seville's principal conference and exhibition venue is FIBES, the Palacio de Exposiciones y Congresos de Sevilla, located on the Isla de la Cartuja. Energy sector visitors (Endesa and Iberdrola maintain southern Spain offices in the city) and aerospace principals visiting the Airbus Tablada and San Pablo facilities require CPO teams that can coordinate with corporate campus security directorates. Luxury hotels including Hotel Alfonso XIII and Hotel Colon serve as primary principal accommodation and briefing venues, with CPO teams conducting advance liaison with hotel security managers.

Secure Transit

San Pablo Airport (SVQ) is approximately 10km north-east of the city centre, typically 15-20 minutes by secure vehicle. CPOs conduct airside-to-vehicle transfers with an operations controller tracking principal movement throughout. For executives connecting from wider European hubs, Malaga Costa del Sol (AGP, ~200km) and Madrid Barajas (MAD, ~530km) are secondary access points with armoured vehicle transfers on request. During Semana Santa and Feria periods, airport transfer routes require extended advance planning due to road closures affecting the N-IV and SE-30 approaches.

Crisis and Medical Response

The primary trauma facility in Seville is Hospital Universitario Virgen del Rocio, the main university hospital serving the city. Emergency numbers in Spain: unified emergency 112, Policia Nacional 091, Guardia Civil 062, ambulance 061. British nationals should contact the British Consulate Madrid on +34 91 714 6300; US nationals the US Embassy Madrid on +34 91 587 2200. CPO teams carry first-aid kits, maintain pre-briefed hospital routes and follow consular notification protocols as a standard pre-deployment briefing item.
