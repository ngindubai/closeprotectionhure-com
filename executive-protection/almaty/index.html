Executive protection in Almaty is a programme, not a single officer. It is the discipline a company applies to protect a travelling executive against organised crime exposure and a shifting political context: advance work on venues and routes, a written threat assessment, an operations controller, and secure transport integrated with the corporate itinerary. This page is for security managers designing protection around a C-suite visit, where the need is layered, documented risk management rather than ad-hoc cover.

Our executive protection teams in Almaty work with Ministry of Internal Affairs-licensed operators who have direct experience supporting energy sector, banking and conference-event clients across the city. Almaty International Airport transfers are planned with consideration for the north-south city geography and the traffic patterns that affect the main hotel corridors during peak hours. Operational detail for Almaty

Threat Assessment

Almaty presents a medium risk environment for foreign business visitors. The January 2022 civil unrest, which followed fuel-price protests and resulted in a significant security response, illustrated the speed with which the public-order environment can shift in Kazakhstan. Day-to-day crime risk in the Medeu, Bostandyk and Almaty-1 business districts is comparatively low, but professional route discipline and situational awareness are appropriate baselines, particularly for principals in the energy sector who carry a higher profile in Kazakhstan's operating environment.

Licensed Local Operatives

Private security companies in Kazakhstan are regulated by the Ministry of Internal Affairs (MIA). Our Almaty partners hold current MIA licensing and employ operatives with experience supporting energy sector and corporate clients across the city's financial and commercial districts. Russian and Kazakh language capability is standard, with English-speaking supervisors available for international principals.

Secure Ground Transport

Almaty International Airport transfers are conducted with pre-positioned vehicles and pre-cleared routes covering the principal journey to Medeu, Bostandyk and the main business hotel corridor. Almaty's mountainous southern perimeter and the concentrated commercial activity in the Almaty-1 district create specific route-planning considerations that our teams account for in advance.

Energy and Finance Sector Coverage

Almaty is Kazakhstan's financial hub and a key base for major energy companies including those with BP and SOCAR upstream interests, as well as international banks and commodity trading firms. Our operatives are experienced in managing principals attending meetings at financial institutions, trading company offices and the energy ministry. Counter-surveillance disciplines are applied as standard for energy-sector engagements.

Residential and Hotel Security

Advance assessments at preferred hotels and serviced apartments in Medeu and Bostandyk. Room allocation review, perimeter assessment and liaison with property security management. After-dark movement guidance covering the Almaty-1 and Bostandyk dining and entertainment districts is issued on arrival.

OSCE and Multilateral Event Coverage

Almaty hosts OSCE and other multilateral events periodically, generating concentrated principal-movement activity requiring coordinated advance work. Our teams are experienced in managing close protection during major conference events, including venue advance, delegate escort and secure transportation logistics.

In 2026, the day-to-day environment in Almaty is stable, but the episode is factored into our contingency planning and our operatives maintain awareness of political and social indicators that might signal a recurrence of public-order disturbance.

Private security companies in Kazakhstan are regulated by the Ministry of Internal Affairs (MIA). Confirm that any provider you engage holds a current MIA licence before commencing operations in Almaty.

Medeu and Bostandyk in the southern districts host the main international hotels and a significant portion of the financial and commercial office stock. Almaty-1 is the city centre district covering key government offices and retail banking headquarters. OSCE offices and some international organisation offices are located in the northern reaches of the city.

For many standard corporate visits to Almaty’s central business districts, a vetted security driver with current MIA licensing provides a proportionate baseline. For higher-profile principals, those in the energy sector, or those attending sensitive government meetings, a dedicated close protection operative is the appropriate step up. A pre-travel risk assessment establishes the right level of cover.

Almaty International Airport is located in the north of the city, and the main business districts are in the south and south-east, creating a substantial transfer distance.
