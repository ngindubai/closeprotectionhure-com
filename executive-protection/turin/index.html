What this covers

Operational detail for Turin

Licensing and CPO Standards

Executive protection in Italy operates under Legislative Decree 153/2009 and the TULPS (Testo Unico delle Leggi di Pubblica Sicurezza). CPOs working in Turin require authorisation from the Questura di Torino or Prefettura di Torino. EU-licensed officers benefit from reciprocal recognition; non-EU nationals must hold Italian licensing. All deployments are unarmed as standard. Clients engaging any provider should request written evidence of TULPS authorisation and Prefettura registration before confirming the assignment.

Threat Assessment

Turin operates at a low overall risk level. The FCDO Italy 2024 advisory identifies pickpocketing as the primary concern, particularly around Porta Nuova station and Piazza San Carlo. Industrial and political protests are a noted feature of the city, with the FCDO 2024 guidance flagging organised demonstrations that can affect movement in the centre. Vehicle crime is reported on the A4 motorway corridor (ACI data). For automotive, aerospace and defence sector visitors attending corporate campuses, route awareness and executive vehicle discipline are the key protective measures.

Principal Movement Security

Movement planning in Turin focuses on the corporate corridor between Porta Nuova station, Piazza Castello and the Lingotto district. Stellantis, Leonardo and Intesa Sanpaolo all maintain significant operations in the city, requiring discreet CPO presence calibrated to corporate campus access protocols. The Piedmont wine estates (Barolo, Barbaresco) are a frequent HNWI leisure destination requiring rural route planning and secure vehicle cover. Turin's grid street layout aids advance route planning but the A4 approach from Milan requires vehicle-crime-aware protocols.

Corporate and Event Security

Turin's primary conference venues include OGR Torino (a major cultural and conference facility in the former railway workshops), the Lingotto convention centre - the converted Fiat factory with rooftop test track - and major corporate campuses including Pininfarina's design centre at Cambiano and Maserati facilities. Automotive industry visits frequently involve sensitive R&D site access requiring CPO teams to observe strict photography and device protocols. Banking sector visits to Intesa Sanpaolo's headquarter complex require advance liaison with in-house security.

Secure Transit

Torino Caselle Airport (TRN) is approximately 16km north of the city centre, around 25-35 minutes by secure vehicle. Operations controllers track all airside-to-vehicle transfers in real time. For principals arriving on wider European connections, Milan Malpensa (MXP, ~90km) and Milan Linate (LIN, ~140km) provide additional access points with armoured vehicle transfers available on request. All A4 motorway transfers include vehicle-crime-aware routing protocols, particularly for itineraries involving luggage or high-value cargo.

Crisis and Medical Response

The primary emergency facilities in Turin are CTO-Maria Adelaide Hospital and Ospedale Molinette (part of the Citta della Salute complex). Emergency numbers: unified emergency 112, Polizia di Stato 113, ambulance (Emergenza Sanitaria) 118, fire brigade 115. British nationals should contact the British Embassy Rome on +39 06 4220 0001; US nationals the US Embassy Rome on +39 06 46741. All CPO teams carry first-aid kits, hold pre-briefed hospital routes and are trained in consular notification procedures.
