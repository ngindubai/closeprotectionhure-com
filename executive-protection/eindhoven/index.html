Operational detail for Eindhoven

Licensing and CPO Standards

Executive protection in the Netherlands is governed by the Wet Particuliere Beveiligingsorganisaties en Recherchebureaus (WPBR 1997). All close protection officers (CPOs) must be individually screened and registered with Justis (the Ministry of Justice and Security's screening and licensing body). Operating companies must hold a separate company licence. Standard commercial deployments in the Netherlands are unarmed; armed protection requires specific police authorisation and is not routinely granted for commercial executive protection. The Politie (National Police) provides regulatory oversight. Non-EU personnel require Dutch licensing through the same Justis process. Clients engaging protection in Eindhoven should request sight of company and individual Justis registration documents before deployment commences.

Eindhoven presents a low threat profile for most corporate visitors, but holds a specific and documented espionage-related consideration for executives in the semiconductor and high-technology sector. The AIVD (Algemene Inlichtingen- en Veiligheidsdienst) annual report (2023) explicitly identifies the Dutch semiconductor and high-tech sector as a target of foreign state actor intelligence operations. ASML (the world's largest maker of photolithography systems, headquartered in Veldhoven 5km southwest of Eindhoven), NXP Semiconductors, and the Philips Innovation Campus are among the globally strategic facilities in the immediate Eindhoven area. For senior executives at or visiting these organisations, counter-surveillance awareness and personal device hygiene are advisable in addition to physical protection. Secure Transit

All executive protection deployments in Eindhoven include secure vehicle transit. Eindhoven Airport (EIN) is 7km southwest; arrival and departure are the highest-exposure moments of most itineraries. Amsterdam Schiphol Airport (AMS) is approximately 75 minutes north by road and is frequently used by senior executives on international itineraries; transfer management covers the full AMS-to-Eindhoven corridor including operations-controller tracking. The A2 motorway connects Eindhoven to Amsterdam (1h15), Maastricht (50 minutes), and Brussels (1h30), making road transfer planning a standard element for multi-city Benelux itineraries. Vehicle selection and route planning account for motorway traffic between EIN and AMS.

Crisis and Medical Response

Contingency planning covers the Dutch emergency services baseline: 112 for all emergencies (police, fire, medical), 0900 8844 for police non-emergency. Medical routing to Catharina Ziekenhuis Eindhoven (+31 40 239 9111), the city's principal hospital, is pre-planned for all engagements. The British Embassy The Hague (+31 70 427 0427) and the US Embassy The Hague (+31 70 310 2209) are held in the crisis plan as consular contacts for non-Dutch principals.
