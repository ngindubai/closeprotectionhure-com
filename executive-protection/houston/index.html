Executive protection in Houston is a programme, not a single officer. It is the discipline a company applies to protect a travelling executive against energy-sector-specific targeting and localised violent crime: advance work on venues and routes, a written threat assessment, an operations controller, and DPS-licensed armed secure transport integrated with the corporate itinerary. This page is for security managers designing protection around a C-suite visit, where the need is layered, documented risk management rather than ad-hoc cover.

Licensing and due diligence in Houston

All close protection officers deployed in Houston must be licensed under Texas Private Security Act (Texas Occupations Code Ch 1702), Texas DPS. Unarmed close protection covers most corporate Galleria/Uptown and Energy Corridor engagements; armed options are assessed case by case. The Texas Department of Public Safety maintains the register; confirming individual CPO licensing before engagement is standard practice, not an optional step.

Houston EP context: sector and threat

Houston’s position in energy, petrochemicals, healthcare, and international oilfield services executives creates a specific EP operating context: principals in these sectors face both the ambient threat picture identified in the HPD Crime Report 2025 and sector-specific risks, including competitive intelligence activity and, for some industries, targeted crime against high-value corporate visitors. The pre-travel assessment addresses both layers.

Operational detail for Houston

Pre-Travel Threat Assessment for Houston

Every executive protection engagement in Houston begins with a written pre-travel threat assessment covering the current advisory (FCDO normal precautions 2026; US State Dept Level 1), the threat picture derived from the HPD Crime Report 2025, sector-specific risks relevant to the principal's industry context in Houston's energy, petrochemicals, healthcare, and international oilfield services executives sector, and venue-by-venue security considerations across the planned itinerary.

Licensed Close Protection Officers Under Texas Occupations Code Ch 1702, Texas DPS

Close protection officers deployed in Houston are licensed under Texas Private Security Act (Texas Occupations Code Ch 1702), Texas DPS. The Texas Department of Public Safety maintains the licensing register; principals should request confirmation of each CPO's current licence before engagement. Vetting covers criminal records check, employment history, and relevant security training credentials appropriate to the Houston operating environment.

Secure Vehicle and Vetted Security Driver

EP operations in Houston integrate a vetted security driver and appropriate vehicle as a core component of the protection detail, not an optional add-on. The vehicle is selected for the Houston road environment: I-610 loop, I-10 Katy Freeway, and the Hardy Toll Road IAH approach. Route planning is completed during the advance phase; route variation is applied across all principal movements between Galleria/Uptown, River Oaks, Energy Corridor, Texas Medical Center.

Venue Advance Work

Venue advance for Houston EP engagements covers access and egress points at hotels, corporate offices, and meeting venues across Galleria/Uptown commercial corridor and River Oaks residential district. The advance officer confirms security management contact, available safe areas, nearest medical facility (nearest: Houston Methodist 713-790-3311), and emergency service response protocol (911) before the principal arrives.

Operations Controller

A dedicated operations controller manages each EP deployment in Houston remotely, tracking vehicle position, maintaining timed-check communication with the protection team, and holding contingency protocols for medical emergency, route compromise, and principal extraction. The controller is briefed on the full itinerary and holds direct contact for Houston emergency services: 911.

HNWI and Residential EP Options

For HNWI principals with a residential presence in Houston, EP options extend beyond point-to-point corporate cover to include residential security integration, domestic staff vetting, and recurring threat-assessment reviews for properties in River Oaks estates and the Memorial Villages gated communities. Residential and mobile EP are coordinated under a unified security plan rather than treated as separate contracts.
