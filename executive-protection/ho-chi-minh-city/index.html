What this covers

Operational detail for Ho Chi Minh City

Threat Assessment

Ho Chi Minh City carries a low risk rating for executive protection purposes. Vietnam has a stable political environment under one-party rule; terrorism directed at foreign nationals is extremely low risk. The FCDO does not advise against travel to Vietnam. The primary executive protection considerations are: motorbike bag snatching and petty theft (common, visible impact on principals), the specific traffic management requirement for pedestrian crossings in high-density motorbike traffic, the Vietnamese Ministry of Public Security licensing constraint for operators, and the commercial risk environment around investment and joint-venture fraud. Source: FCDO Vietnam travel advice, 2025.

Ministry of Public Security Licensing

Executive protection in Vietnam operates under Decree 52/2008/ND-CP, administered by the Ministry of Public Security. Security companies must hold MPS licences; individual EPO personnel require MPS registration. Foreign EP companies cannot operate directly. Our Ho Chi Minh City partners hold current MPS licensing; documentation is provided before each deployment. The MPS framework limits options for foreign EP providers and makes local partner quality the critical selection factor.

Motorbike Theft and Pedestrian Protocol

Motorbike bag snatching from pedestrians is the most consistently reported personal security incident for foreign nationals in Ho Chi Minh City, with incidents concentrated in the District 1 tourist and commercial zone. EPO close escort protocol addresses this: no visible bags, jewellery, or phones on streets near traffic; bags carried on the building side; close physical proximity maintained during all pedestrian sections; and the principal is not left to manage road crossings in heavy motorbike traffic independently. EPO escorts all pedestrian movement in the commercial zone.

Tan Son Nhat Airport Transfer and Protocol

EP teams meet the principal inside Tan Son Nhat International Airport (SGN) arrivals. The transfer to District 1 via the Pham Van Dong corridor takes 20 to 35 minutes under normal conditions; peak-hour traffic extends this to 45 to 60 minutes. The ep transfer is straightforward relative to higher-risk cities: no specific zone avoidance is required, and the SGN to District 1 route is well-maintained and lit.

HNWI and Corporate Entertainment Venue Management

Ho Chi Minh City's HNWI and senior executive entertainment infrastructure is concentrated in District 1 (Dong Khoi, Nguyen Hue), the Bui Vien and Pham Ngu Lao entertainment area, and the new Saigon riverside development. EP operations at restaurant and event venues apply pre-arrival advance assessment of the venue's security posture, vehicle access management, and the identification of discrete exit routes from crowded venues. The opera house, Reunification Palace, and rooftop bars on Nguyen Hue are standard HNWI itinerary elements.

Multi-City Vietnam EP Coverage

HNWI principals visiting Vietnam frequently include Hanoi, Da Nang, Hoi An, and Phu Quoc. Our Vietnam partner network provides EP coverage across the country. The Hanoi operating environment differs from Saigon in its more formal political atmosphere (proximity to government ministries and embassies); Hoi An and Phu Quoc have distinct resort-environment EP profiles. Multi-city itineraries are coordinated from the Ho Chi Minh City operations controller.
