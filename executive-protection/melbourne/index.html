What this covers

Operational detail for Melbourne

Victoria Private Security Act 2004 and VSIRA EP Licensing

Executive protection in Victoria is governed by the Private Security Act 2004 (Vic) and administered by the Victorian Security Industry Regulatory Authority (VSIRA). Individual CPOs must hold a VSIRA Bodyguard licence class. Licence applications require a national police records check through the Australian Criminal Intelligence Commission (ACIC), identity verification, completion of a Certificate III in Security Operations from a Registered Training Organisation (RTO), and a VSIRA fitness to hold a licence assessment. For higher-risk EP functions, Certificate IV in Security and Risk Management is the professional development standard beyond the minimum licence requirement. Security companies must hold a VSIRA Proprietary Security Organisation licence. Clients should confirm both the company's VSIRA organisation licence and the individual CPO's Bodyguard class licence before deployment.

Australian PROBABLE Terrorism Threat and Victoria History

Australia's national terrorism threat level is PROBABLE -- an attack is likely -- as assessed by ASIO and the National Terrorism Threat Advisory System (NTTАС). Victoria has a specific and documented terrorism history that directly informs Melbourne EP planning: the 2017 Bourke Street Mall vehicle attack (6 killed, 30 injured) was a deliberate vehicle ramming in the Melbourne CBD; Victoria's ASIO threat history includes multiple foiled plots by domestic Islamist networks in the 2010s and 2020s. For EP, the PROBABLE national threat combined with Victoria's track record means terrorism awareness is integrated into every Melbourne engagement as a standard planning input, not applied only during elevated-threat periods. Crowded pedestrian areas including Bourke Street, Swanston Street, and the Flinders Street Station precinct are assessed as elevated-risk category locations.

Melbourne Major Events and EP Planning

Melbourne's calendar of major international events creates the primary EP demand peaks in the city. The Australian Open (Melbourne Park, January) draws major corporate hospitality principals; the Melbourne Formula 1 Grand Prix (Albert Park, March) concentrates international automotive and corporate principals; AFL Finals (MCG, September) create the highest domestic crowd-events security challenge. EP advance work for Australian Open covers Melbourne Park venue access, principal hotel management in the CBD and Southbank, and the specific traffic disruption on Batman Avenue and Olympic Boulevard during the two-week tournament. Grand Prix EP covers Albert Park access credentials, corporate hospitality suite security, and the Inner Domain road closure logistics. All major event EP includes terrorism-aware venue assessment given the PROBABLE national threat level.

Advance Work and Melbourne Venue Assessment

Melbourne EP advance work covers the principal's hotel, corporate and legal district meeting venues, and Melbourne Airport transfer logistics. The Crown Towers Melbourne and Grand Hyatt Melbourne on Southbank and Collins Street are the primary luxury hotel venues for senior corporate arrivals. Crown Towers advance work is particularly relevant: the property is adjacent to Crown Casino, which creates specific crowd management and vehicle approach logistics that differ from standard CBD hotel operations. The Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre (MCEC) at South Wharf is a primary conference venue requiring specific advance for major international conferences. Victoria Police has well-developed major event security protocols and EPT (Executive Protection Team) liaison relationships that are relevant for very high-profile principal visits.

HNWI Residential EP and Toorak Coverage

Melbourne's HNWI residential community is concentrated primarily in Toorak, South Yarra, Brighton, and Armadale -- the Port Phillip Bay-facing suburbs that form Melbourne's traditional wealth corridor. EP for extended-stay HNWI principals in these areas covers residential security assessment, route planning from Toorak and South Yarra to the CBD, school run security for principals with children in Melbourne private schools (Scotch College, Melbourne Grammar, Geelong Grammar), and domestic staff vetting. The Toorak-CBD commute route on Punt Road and St Kilda Road is subject to predictable congestion; security driver route planning addresses this specifically. For principals with long-stay Melbourne programmes, the EP team structure integrates with residential security provision.
