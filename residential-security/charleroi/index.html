Charleroi carries a reputation as one of Belgium’s higher-crime cities, a legacy of its coal and steel past and a regional unemployment rate, 7.8% in Wallonia in 2025 against a national figure of 6.2% per Statbel, that still runs above the country average. It’s a reputation worth taking seriously in relocation planning, but not one that rules the whole city out.

Gosselies is the area doing the most to change that picture. Sonaca’s aerospace-structures campus and the A6K digital-innovation hub, backed by EU Recovery and Resilience Plan funding and scaling toward 10,000 learners a year from autumn 2026, represent genuine redevelopment, and while Gosselies itself is primarily a business and campus district, the areas around it carry a materially calmer profile than the city centre and are the natural draw for relocating Sonaca or A6K-linked families.

Charleroi city centre, postal code 6000, is a different story. Federal Police-sourced reporting found a significant share of vehicle-related theft recorded across the wider police zone concentrated there, and it’s generally not the first choice for family residential placement as a result, even though the same reporting found overall crime levels broadly stable rather than worsening between 2022 and 2023.
