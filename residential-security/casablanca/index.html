What this covers

Operational detail for Casablanca

Property Security Survey

Structured assessment of residential properties in Maarif, Gauthier, Ain Diab, and Anfa, covering perimeter integrity, access control, CCTV placement, and alarm specification appropriate to the residential property types used by international banking and corporate expats in Casablanca. The assessment includes review of proximity to government facilities and any factors that would elevate the property's risk profile above the city baseline.

Ministry of Interior Licensed Guard Deployment

Supply of residential guards from companies authorised under Morocco's Ministry of Interior Law 27-06. All security companies must hold current government licensing and individual operators require formal accreditation. Standard private security in Morocco operates on an unarmed basis. Shift management procedures and supervisor contact details are documented for every deployment. Gendarmerie coordination is used for higher-risk assignments requiring armed support.

Domestic Staff Background Checks

Verification of household employees through Morocco's national identity system (CNIE), covering identity confirmation, address verification, and employment reference contact. Morocco's identity documentation is generally reliable and verification is practical. Employment history checks are completed through direct employer reference contact. Staff without verifiable previous employment history or documented inconsistencies are flagged for client review before engagement.

Legal Compliance Briefing for Household Staff

Morocco's legal environment includes strict laws affecting household conduct: alcohol is only available at licensed premises, and laws on drugs, personal behaviour, and photography of government infrastructure carry real legal consequences for foreign nationals. Household staff briefings cover the legal framework as it applies to the residence and its management, ensuring no household activities create legal exposure for the principal.

Terrorism Awareness for Residential Planning

Morocco's counter-terrorism services regularly disrupt Daesh-inspired cells, and the FCDO rates the terrorism threat as likely. Residential security planning includes awareness protocols for high-threat periods, safe room or shelter designation within the property, and emergency contact procedures for the nearest police station and British Embassy Rabat.

Emergency Protocol Development

Written emergency procedures covering security incident response, medical emergency, and evacuation planning, coordinated with Mohammed V International Airport emergency access and British Embassy Rabat (+212-537-633-333) consular emergency procedures. Casablanca's private medical sector includes Clinique Badr and Clinique du Parc as the primary international-standard facilities.
