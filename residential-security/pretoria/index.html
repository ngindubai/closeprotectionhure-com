Waterkloof and Brooklyn hold most of Pretoria’s diplomatic community and, with it, the city’s most developed residential-security infrastructure: neighbourhood watch structures, estate-level access control in newer developments, and a private-patrol presence that reflects the concentration of embassies and senior corporate households in these suburbs. That infrastructure is well established, but it exists because of the underlying risk, not despite it. Gauteng province, which contains Pretoria, records a disproportionate share of South Africa’s carjackings and residential crime, and security planning here starts from that provincial baseline.

A property survey in these suburbs covers the fundamentals common across South Africa, perimeter fencing standard, electrified fencing certification, monitored gate access, CCTV on approach routes, with one addition that matters more here than in most other markets: backup power sized specifically for security systems. Load shedding, the country’s scheduled outage programme, disables mains-powered alarms and electric fencing for the duration of each outage window, and the City of Tshwane runs its own version of the national Eskom schedule. A household relying on standard backup power rather than a system built for security equipment specifically is exposed during exactly the hours crime rates tend to rise.

Access Control and Perimeter

Standard Pretoria residential security includes palisade or wall perimeters, electrified fencing compliant with national safety standards, monitored gate access, and CCTV coverage of approach routes. Given Gauteng's carjacking pattern, driveway and gate design that minimises the time a vehicle sits stationary and exposed while gates open is a standard recommendation rather than an optional upgrade.

Domestic Staff Vetting

Vetting of domestic staff, drivers, and gardeners follows the same PSIRA-linked verification principles used for any private security placement in South Africa, alongside standard criminal-record and reference checks. Diplomatic and corporate households relocating to Pretoria typically build vetting into the onboarding timeline before staff are given unsupervised property access.

Emergency Response Protocols

Emergency numbers: 10111 for police, 10177 for ambulance, and 112 free from any mobile phone. An active armed-response subscription, standard across Gauteng, is the baseline expectation for any Pretoria residential security plan.

Technology and Monitoring

CCTV, alarm systems, and armed-response integration are standard across Waterkloof and Brooklyn.
