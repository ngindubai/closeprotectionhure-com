Nassau, the capital of the Bahamas, functions simultaneously as a financial centre, a luxury real estate market, and a Caribbean gateway for HNWI clients with private aviation and yacht operations. The city’s residential security environment reflects this dual character: gated communities in the west of New Providence maintain standards comparable to secure residential estates in Florida or the Cayman Islands, while parts of central and eastern Nassau carry crime rates that the US State Dept Bahamas Advisory (2024) rates as requiring increased caution. For the broader security context, see the Nassau city security profile.

The HNWI residential market in Nassau is concentrated in a handful of gated communities – Lyford Cay, Old Fort Bay, and Albany – that have maintained controlled-access security management for decades. For assignees in these communities, residential security surveys focus on the gaps between community perimeter security and property-level controls: safe-room specification, CCTV blind spots, dock-access vulnerability for waterfront properties, and backup power provision. Outside these communities, property-level security investment requirements are substantially higher.

Domestic staff vetting is a priority for Nassau HNWI households. The city’s residential community is small and well-connected, and household staff with knowledge of asset locations, travel patterns, or security codes represent a meaningful exposure. Full vetting for all household staff, with enhanced checks for those handling valuables or financial information, is the standard we apply. Hurricane season preparedness – covering both structural hardening and post-storm security protocols during power outages – is integrated into every residential security assessment for Nassau, given the documented Category 5 impact experience from Dorian in 2019.

All security personnel we deploy in Nassau hold current BSIRA licences under the Private Security Guard Industry Act. Property Security Survey

Residential surveys in Nassau cover the three principal zones used by international residents: gated community estates in western New Providence (Lyford Cay, Old Fort Bay, Cable Beach corridor), the Albany private community on the southwest coast, and premium waterfront properties on Paradise Island. Surveys cover perimeter wall and fence integrity, access control systems, CCTV coverage, lighting standards, safe-room specification, backup power provision, and vessel-access points for properties with dock or beach frontage. Reports identify priority remediation items graded by risk, with attention to hurricane-hardening gaps that carry a security as well as a weather dimension.

Regulatory Framework in the Bahamas

Private security companies in the Bahamas operate under the Private Security Guard Industry Act (Chapter 263, Statute Law of the Bahamas) and must be licensed by the Bahamas Security Industry Regulatory Authority (BSIRA). Individual guards must hold a current BSIRA guard licence. Security managers require a separate BSIRA management licence. There is no prohibition on hiring foreign security professionals for personal protection roles, but any individual performing guard-for-hire functions on Bahamian territory must hold a valid BSIRA licence. We verify BSIRA licence status for all personnel deployed in Nassau.

Domestic Staff Vetting

Domestic staff in Nassau present a moderate-integrity risk, particularly for HNWI households where staff have access to high-value assets, travel schedules, and personal information. Vetting covers national identity verification against the Bahamas Department of Immigration records, employment history references with previous household or hospitality employers, criminal record check with the Royal Bahamas Police Force, and a structured in-person interview. For staff handling valuables, financial assets, or access to children, an enhanced check including financial conduct review and social-media screening is appropriate. Seasonal household staff hired for winter residency periods should be reveted annually.

Neighbourhood and Zone Assessment: Nassau

Lyford Cay and Old Fort Bay on the western tip of New Providence offer the most controlled residential environments in Nassau: both are gated with 24-hour guard presence and restricted vehicle access, and incident rates within their perimeters are low. Albany on the southwest coast provides a newer high-security residential and resort community with integrated security management. Paradise Island, accessed via the bridges from Nassau, has elevated visitor footfall from the Atlantis resort complex and benefits from hotel-district security infrastructure but less controlled street access than Lyford Cay. The eastern and southern suburbs of Nassau proper (Fox Hill, Bain Town, Englerston) carry significantly higher crime rates per the US State Dept Bahamas Advisory (2024) and are not suitable for residential assignment for HNWI clients.

Emergency Protocols and Consular Contact

Primary emergency contacts: police 919, ambulance 911. Medical facilities: Doctors Hospital Nassau (+1 242 302 4600) and Princess Margaret Hospital (+1 242 322 2861). For conditions requiring specialist intervention, medical evacuation to Miami (less than one hour by air) is the standard routing; Lynden Pindling International Airport (NAS) supports medevac operations. The British High Commission in Nassau can be reached at +1 242 325 7471. Hurricane emergency planning must include shelter-in-place criteria for Category 1-2 events and pre-agreed evacuation departure triggers for Category 3 and above, with confirmed departure slots at NAS before storm landfall.

Hurricane Season Residential Security

The Atlantic hurricane season runs from June to November, with peak risk in August to October. Nassau experienced significant impact from Hurricane Dorian (Category 5, 2019), which caused infrastructure disruption across New Providence. Residential hurricane preparedness for Nassau assignees includes: structural assessment of the property's hurricane rating and shutter provision, backup generator testing and fuel storage, 72-hour water and supply reserves, vehicle fuelling protocols ahead of storm warnings, and a confirmed departure-trigger date (typically 72 hours before projected landfall for a Category 3 or above). Security implications of post-storm periods include opportunistic property crime during power outages and infrastructure disruption affecting emergency response times.
