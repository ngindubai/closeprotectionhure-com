Property Security Survey

Property assessments for Kazimierz upmarket residential, Debniki and Salwator residential south of Wawel, Nowe Miasto central apartment stock, and Ruczaj suburban developments near Malopolska Business Park. Surveys cover entry-door and window standards (Polish PN-EN standards), intercom and video-entry, perimeter lighting, parking security, communal-building access control (Krakow's older tenement stock frequently has weak communal-access controls), and alarm-monitoring provision. Ruczaj assessments include transit-route review for personnel commuting to the technology and BPO parks.

Regulatory Framework in Poland

Private security in Poland is governed by the Act on Physical Protection of Persons and Property (1997, amended 2022). Operators are licensed by the Komendant Wojewodzki Policji Malopolskiej (Malopolska Regional Police Commander). Security operatives must hold a licencja pracownika ochrony (security worker licence). The Act specifies both physical and technical security provision; residential services are typically structured as a combination of technical alarm monitoring and human-response capacity. Verify operator licence status with the Malopolska police licensing register before engagement.

Domestic Staff Vetting

Poland's low overall crime rate reduces but does not eliminate the domestic-staff vetting requirement for corporate assignees. Vetting covers PESEL number verification (national identification), National Criminal Register (KRK) extract from the Ministry of Justice, employment reference verification with direct contact, and a structured interview. EU and EEA nationals working in Poland require standard identity verification; non-EU staff require Karta Pobytu verification. For tech-sector assignees with sensitive data access at home, enhanced vetting including social-media review is advisable.

Neighbourhood and Zone Assessment: Krakow

Kazimierz, the historic Jewish quarter now popular with professionals and expats, has good residential stock but nightlife activity creates petty-crime exposure particularly at weekends (pickpocketing documented in Rynek Glowny and Kazimierz nightlife areas). Debniki and Salwator, south of Wawel Hill, offer quieter residential character with lower footfall and better parking security. Ruczaj is the standard choice for technology and BPO sector assignees given proximity to Malopolska Business Park; its newer development stock typically meets a higher baseline physical security standard. Vehicle crime in surface car parks is documented in Krakow; secure underground parking is a material residential security factor.

Emergency Protocols and Consular Contact

Emergency services: 112 (pan-European, police and ambulance). Dedicated emergency lines: 997 (police), 999 (ambulance). Primary medical facility: Szpital Uniwersytecki (University Hospital Krakow), +48 12 424 70 00. British Consulate Krakow: +48 12 421 70 30 (note: for emergency assistance outside office hours, contact British Embassy Warsaw: +48 22 311 00 00). Emergency protocols should cover the university area demonstration scenario, which generates periodic road closures in central Krakow.

Digital and Communications Security

Krakow's large technology and BPO sector concentration means assignees frequently handle sensitive corporate data in residential settings. Appropriate measures include: secure home Wi-Fi configuration (WPA3 where available, separate guest network for visitors), encrypted communications for all business matters, physical screen-privacy measures for home-office use in shared or communal-facing settings, and device-management protocols for company-issued equipment. Awareness of cybersecurity risk in the tech sector is generally higher among Krakow's corporate community than in comparable cities, but residential network security often lags workplace standards.
