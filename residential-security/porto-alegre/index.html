Domestic Staff Vetting

Background checks on household staff who have property access. Brazil's domestic staff vetting process includes: CPF/RG identity verification, antecedentes criminais (criminal record) search through the SSP-RS online portal, reference checks with previous Porto Alegre employers, and employment contract review under CLT domestic worker regulations. Staff with unsupervised property access and knowledge of the household's routine and assets are the primary insider-risk variable in Porto Alegre.

Express Kidnapping and Robbery Awareness

SESP-RS 2024 records robbery and express crime targeting residents in Porto Alegre's higher-income districts. Express kidnapping -- short-duration abductions targeting ATM and digital payment accounts -- is documented in Porto Alegre's commercial and residential neighbourhoods. Residential security planning addresses this through: ATM protocol briefing, predictable routine variation, awareness of approach indicators, and the emergency contact chain for private crisis management resources.

Flood Recovery and Infrastructure Security Considerations

The May 2024 Rio Grande do Sul floods caused significant infrastructure disruption across Porto Alegre. For residential properties in affected lower-elevation areas, the assessment covers: infrastructure reliability (power, water, access routes), the condition of perimeter security elements that may have been affected by flood damage, and any changes to the immediate neighbourhood security environment during the recovery period. Properties in Moinhos de Vento on the city's higher elevation were largely unaffected.

Condominium and Gated Building Security Review

Porto Alegre's premium residential market is dominated by high-rise condominium buildings with shared security infrastructure. The review covers: portaria (concierge/security desk) staffing and protocols, CCTV in common areas and underground car parks, visitor access management, and the condominium's contracted security company Policia Federal licence status. Buildings with 24-hour portaria security and vehicle access control provide the strongest baseline for high-income residential security in Porto Alegre.
