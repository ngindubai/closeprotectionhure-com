Izmir’s international business community, concentrated in the Alsancak waterfront district, Karsiyaka northern shore, and Bornova university area, drives demand for residential security services oriented to the specific risk profile of Turkey’s third city. The FCDO Turkey travel advice (2024) advises enhanced precautions throughout Turkey, citing terrorism risk; the 2020 earthquake (magnitude 6.6) adds a seismic dimension that is unique to Izmir among Turkey’s major business cities. Residential security surveys in Izmir accordingly include earthquake preparedness planning alongside the standard access control and threat assessment elements.

Political demonstrations occur periodically in central Konak and Alsancak, affecting access routes to residential areas. Domestic staff vetting through Turkish channels is recommended for all long-term employees. CCTV installations comply with Turkish data protection law (KVKK). What this covers

Operational detail for Izmir

Property Security Survey

A survey of your Izmir residence, whether in the Alsancak, Karsiyaka, Bornova, or Buca districts, covering perimeter vulnerabilities, access points, CCTV coverage gaps, and structural access security. A distinctive element of Izmir property surveys is earthquake preparedness: the October 2020 Izmir earthquake (magnitude 6.6) caused significant building damage and casualties. Surveys identify structural risk factors and include earthquake-specific emergency response planning for each property.

Neighbourhood Threat Assessment

Izmir presents a moderate risk profile for residential security. The FCDO Turkey travel advice (2024) advises enhanced precautions throughout Turkey, citing terrorism risk and noting the January 2021 Izmir courthouse attack. Political demonstrations occur periodically in central Konak and Alsancak. Petty crime and vehicle theft are at levels consistent with a major Turkish city. Residential areas in Karsiyaka and Bornova have lower direct exposure to central district unrest but require awareness of citywide protest patterns and access route impacts.

Access Control and Perimeter

Access control for Izmir residences covers apartment building entry systems and intercom management in urban areas, vehicle parking security in the central districts, and perimeter management for detached or compound properties in suburban areas. During periods of political demonstration in Konak and Alsancak, alternative access routes to residential properties in and around those districts are documented and communicated to the household.

Domestic Staff Vetting

Background verification of household employees through Turkish channels, covering identity verification (Turkish ID or residency permit), employment history, and where applicable, criminal record checks. Izmir's international business community and expat residential population typically employs domestic staff with regular property access; vetting is recommended for all long-term household employees and can be applied to existing staff.

Emergency Response Protocols

Emergency baseline for Izmir residences: 155 for police, 112 for all emergencies and ambulance, 110 for fire. Earthquake-specific protocols are documented for each property: structural safe points, emergency exit routes, and post-earthquake assembly points. The British Consulate Izmir (+90 232 463 5151) and US Consulate Izmir (+90 232 455 1593) are held in the household emergency plan. AFAD (Disaster and Emergency Management Authority) is the primary earthquake response authority in Turkey.

Technology and Monitoring

CCTV installation, alarm integration, and remote monitoring through Turkish National Police-licensed technology partners. Turkish personal data protection law (KVKK) applies to CCTV covering shared or public spaces. Earthquake-resistant mounting of technology equipment is specified where appropriate. Remote monitoring provides continuity during owner absences and alerts to unusual access or perimeter activity.
