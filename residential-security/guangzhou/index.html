Operational detail for Guangzhou

Property Security Survey

Structured assessment of residential properties in Tianhe's commercial and residential hub, Zhujiang New Town's high-rise developments, Haizhu's riverside compounds, and the Pearl River New Town cluster used by senior trade and manufacturing assignees. The survey covers perimeter access, CCTV blind spots, shared lobby intercom control, and alarm system adequacy for properties in Guangzhou's competitive premium residential market.

Ministry of Public Security Regulatory Context

Private security in China is regulated by the Ministry of Public Security (MPS) under the Security Services Management Regulations (MPS Order No. 564, 2010). Foreign security companies cannot operate directly in China without MPS-approved structures. Residential security assessments for expat assignees in Guangzhou are delivered through vetted MPS-compliant operators. Guangzhou Municipal PSB 2024 oversees security licensing in the city.

Domestic Staff Vetting

Identity verification and employment history checks for household employees using China's resident registration (hukou) system. Guangzhou's international trade environment means many household staff have previous employment with international businesses, making reference verification with former employers particularly straightforward. Criminal record checks through available channels are included as standard.

Digital and Communications Security

Guangzhou residences operate within China's monitored telecommunications infrastructure. Principals in trade, manufacturing, or commercially sensitive sectors should avoid conducting sensitive discussions using standard Chinese network infrastructure in the home. VPN guidance within legal parameters, device security for work equipment, and network segregation for home office setups are included in the digital security component.

Canton Fair and Trade Cycle Security Planning

Guangzhou's role as host of the China Import and Export Fair (Canton Fair, Pazhou Complex) means assignees in trade and procurement sectors receive heightened attention during twice-annual fair cycles. Residential security during fair periods includes specific protocols for managing visitor access, increasing awareness of social engineering targeting trade principals, and protecting commercially sensitive samples and contract documentation at the residence.

Emergency Protocols and Consular Contact

Written emergency procedures co-ordinated with the British Consulate-General Guangzhou at +86 20 8314 3000, the nearest international medical facility (Clifford Hospital, Guangzhou; Guangzhou International Medical Center), and Baiyun International Airport emergency routing. Exit ban risk assessment is included for principals involved in significant commercial activity in the Pearl River Delta.
