Operational detail for Tel Aviv

Israeli RAAS Residential Security Regulation

Residential security companies in Israel must hold RAAS (Regulatory Authority for Private Security Services) company licences under the Ministry of Public Security. Individual residential security personnel hold RAAS personal licences at the appropriate level. Armed residential security officers hold RAAS firearms permits authorised through Israel Police. The Israeli residential security market has a developed manned guarding sector with ex-IDF and Israeli Police experience throughout; RAAS licensing combined with relevant service background is the quality indicator for the Israeli market. Residential buildings in Tel Aviv, particularly apartment tower blocks, often have their own building security management (sha'ar shomer - gate guard infrastructure); individual household residential security supplements this building baseline.

Conflict Environment Residential Security Context

FCDO advises against all travel to Israel, and any residential presence in Tel Aviv as at June 2026 requires a specific, current risk assessment. The conflict environment creates residential security requirements with no equivalent in standard commercial markets: Hamas rocket and missile fire from Gaza has targeted the Tel Aviv area since October 2023; Houthi ballistic missiles and drones have struck Israeli territory; and the Hezbollah ceasefire on the northern border remains fragile. Residential security in Tel Aviv integrates these threats into household planning: bomb shelter (miklat) integrity assessment is a primary residential security component, and every Tel Aviv residential security brief begins with the miklat location, structural condition, and capacity for the specific residential property.

Miklat (Bomb Shelter) Assessment and Rocket Alert Planning

Every Israeli residential building is legally required to have a miklat (bomb shelter) or a reinforced safe room (mamad) under the Israeli Home Front Command (PIKUD HAOREF) standards. Residential security assessment in Tel Aviv verifies: the miklat or mamad location within the residential building or property, the structural condition and door seal integrity of the shelter, the capacity of the shelter relative to the number of household occupants, the stocking of the shelter with emergency supplies (water, first aid, communications), and the rehearsed procedure for reaching the shelter within the 90-second Red Alert (Tzeva Adom) window for the Tel Aviv area. Principals maintaining Tel Aviv residences should rehearse the Tzeva Adom shelter procedure on arrival and ensure all household staff are briefed on the protocol.

Tel Aviv HNWI Residential Districts

Tel Aviv's principal HNWI residential zones are: North Tel Aviv (the HaYarkon Park corridor, Ramat Aviv, and Neve Avivim neighbourhoods) for large apartment properties and villas in the city's most established HNWI residential area, the Old North (the Lev HaIr heritage district adjacent to the seafront) for high-specification apartment renovations, and the coastal seafront corridor along HaYarkon Street for premium apartments with direct sea access. Herzliya Pituach, approximately 15 km north of Tel Aviv, is Israel's most exclusive residential address for international business and diplomatic principals, with large villas, private marina access, and proximity to the Israeli defence technology sector. Residential security assessment in Herzliya Pituach covers villa perimeter security specific to the large-property format, rather than the apartment building gate-guard model applicable to central Tel Aviv.

Armed Residential Security and Household Emergency Planning

Armed residential security is standard for HNWI households in Tel Aviv with specific threat indicators or elevated conflict-environment risk. RAAS-licensed armed residential guards with Israeli Police firearms authorisations are deployed at properties where the threat assessment warrants it. Household emergency planning in Tel Aviv extends beyond conventional residential security: evacuation planning from Tel Aviv in the event of a significant escalation, communications protocols for contacting the relevant embassy or consulate emergency service, safe house or hotel alternatives with confirmed miklat infrastructure, and international evacuation routing through Ben Gurion International Airport are all components of a comprehensive Tel Aviv residential security and emergency plan.
