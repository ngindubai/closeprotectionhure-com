Residential Risk Assessment: Chisinau

Medium risk rating. Chisinau city operations are manageable, but Moldova's eastern Transnistria region (controlled by a Russian-backed separatist entity with approximately 1,500 Russian troops) is off-limits (FCDO against all travel). Russian hybrid threat activity -- energy manipulation, disinformation, election interference -- affects the national operating environment. FCDO advises a high degree of caution (FCDO Moldova, 2026).

Property Selection and Security Survey

Property selection for expatriate and executive residential accommodation in Chisinau should be guided by a security survey conducted before tenancy begins. The survey assesses: perimeter integrity (walls, fencing, gate strength and control), access control (door, window, lock quality), natural surveillance (sightlines from the property), lighting adequacy, neighbourhood security environment, and proximity to emergency services. In Moldovan Transnistria and Russian influence environment, properties that appear attractive but are located in poorly policed or remote areas should be rejected regardless of quality or price.

Guard Force Selection and Management

Residential guard forces in Chisinau must meet the vetting standards required by the Moldovan Ministry of Internal Affairs under the Law No. 283/2006 on Private Security. This includes identity verification, criminal record check where accessible in Moldova, professional references, and demonstrated familiarity with the residential protection role. Guard-force briefings should cover: access control procedures, emergency response protocols, communications with the principal and the security manager, prohibited actions (including the use of personal mobile phones during duty hours), and escalation thresholds.

Physical Security Enhancements

Where a residential security survey identifies gaps in the baseline physical security of a property in Chisinau, enhancements are assessed and prioritised by cost-effectiveness and risk reduction. Common enhancements in the Moldovan Transnistria and Russian influence environment include: upgraded door and window locks, perimeter lighting, security camera coverage of access points, panic room or safe room provision where the threat profile warrants it, and communications redundancy (including satellite communications for remote or isolated properties).

Emergency Response Planning

All residential security arrangements in Chisinau must include a documented emergency response plan covering: fire (evacuation routes, assembly point, fire warden designation), medical emergency (nearest facility Spitalul Clinic Republican, medevac pre-arrangement to Bucharest), security intrusion (lockdown procedure, alarm response, police contact protocol), and political or civil disturbance (shelter-in-place or evacuation trigger criteria). The plan must be briefed to all occupants and the guard force before occupancy begins.

Ongoing Security Management

Residential security is not a one-time survey activity -- it requires ongoing management in Chisinau's evolving security environment. A named security manager should conduct periodic reviews of the guard-force performance, physical security status, and threat picture for the specific neighbourhood. Any change in occupancy, principal profile, or local security conditions should trigger a review. Maintain contact with the Moldovan Ministry of Internal Affairs's published security advisories and the FCDO and US State Dept travel updates for Moldova as part of the ongoing management cycle.
