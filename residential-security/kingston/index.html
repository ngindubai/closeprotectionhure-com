PSRA Licensing for Residential Security in Jamaica

All residential security companies operating in Jamaica must hold current licensing from the Private Security Regulation Authority (PSRA) under the Private Security Regulation Authority Act (2009). Individual security officers require individual PSRA registration. Verify PSRA licensing documentation before engaging any Kingston operator. Companies operating without current PSRA registration have no legal standing during security incidents.

Expatriate Residential Zones in Kingston

The recommended residential zones for expatriates and international staff in Kingston are New Kingston and the Liguanea/Barbican area to the south-east. Both zones are operationally separated from the gang-controlled communities named in FCDO warnings against travel, including Tivoli Gardens, Trench Town, Arnett Gardens, Jones Town, Denham Town, and Waterhouse. Property selection within these zones materially affects the threat exposure of the residential security programme.

Property Security Survey and Hardening

A residential security survey for Kingston covers perimeter integrity including wall height and anti-climb measures, vehicle gate control with generator backup to maintain access control during power cuts, CCTV coverage with remote monitoring capability, motion-activated lighting across all approaches, and safe room specification for properties where the threat assessment warrants it. Surveys are refreshed after significant local incidents.

Domestic Staff Vetting in Kingston

Staff vetting in Kingston uses identity document verification through the Registrar General's Department, Jamaica Constabulary Force police clearance, and employment reference checks from previous Kingston households or employers. A probationary period with restricted access to security-sensitive areas and valuables is applied before full access is granted. Periodic re-vetting is recommended for long-term staff.

Emergency Response and Medevac Planning

The residential security programme for Kingston includes a documented emergency response plan covering the security team's first-response role, contact protocols with Jamaica Constabulary Force (119) and ambulance services (110), and medical evacuation routing. The University Hospital of the West Indies provides initial stabilisation; for serious trauma or complex conditions, medical evacuation to the United States or United Kingdom is the appropriate response.
