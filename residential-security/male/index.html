Male, the capital of the Maldives, is both a densely populated urban centre and the operational hub for one of the world’s most geographically dispersed luxury resort industries. Corporate assignees in the Maldives – including hospitality sector management, offshore infrastructure project staff, and financial services personnel – face a residential security environment unlike any other: many live not in the capital itself but on private resort islands accessible only by seaplane or speedboat, creating water-transport dependencies that are a defining variable in security planning. For the broader security context, see the Male city security profile.

The FCDO Maldives travel advice (2024) notes a generally low crime rate but highlights specific risks: terrorist attacks targeting foreign nationals (including the 2020 assassination attempt on former President Nasheed), gang violence in parts of Male city, and the implications of the 2023 presidential transition for political stability. These risks do not fundamentally alter the security profile for most corporate residents, but they do underline that the Maldives is not simply a benign beach destination; it is an Indian Ocean state with its own political dynamics and security tensions.

Residential security planning for Male assignments starts with the accommodation zone question: urban Male, Hulhumale, or resort island. Each environment has different security infrastructure and different dependencies. Hulhumale is generally recommended for longer-term corporate residence, offering newer builds, lower population density, and direct road access to Velana International Airport. Resort island accommodation offers isolation but requires detailed transfer-disruption protocols because medical evacuation or emergency departure depends entirely on weather-dependent water transport. All security providers we deploy in the Maldives hold Ministry of Home Affairs licences under Regulation No. 2013/R-92. For executive protection covering movement including seaplane and speedboat transfers, see our executive protection in Male page.

Operational detail for Male

Property Security Survey

Residential surveys for Male and wider Maldives assignments cover two distinct environments: urban residential properties in Male city (concentrated in Maafannu, Henveiru, and the Hulhumale development across the lagoon bridge) and resort-island staff accommodation used by corporate assignees on extended deployment at luxury properties. Surveys for Male city properties cover perimeter integrity, access control, CCTV coverage, lighting standards, lock specification, and flood-risk mitigation given the island's mean elevation of 1.5 metres above sea level. Resort accommodation surveys assess physical security of staff quarters, separation from guest areas, water-transport access control, and emergency evacuation route to Male or Velana International Airport (MLE).

Regulatory Framework in the Maldives

Private security companies in the Maldives operate under the Private Security Service Regulation (Regulation No. 2013/R-92), administered by the Ministry of Home Affairs. Security companies must hold a ministry licence; individual guards must be registered and carry identification. Resort islands maintain their own security management under agreements with the Tourism Ministry, and close protection personnel accompanying corporate principals to resort islands must co-ordinate access with resort security management in advance. Foreign close protection officers may accompany principals as personal staff; independent security operations by foreign companies on Maldivian territory require Ministry of Home Affairs authorisation.

Domestic Staff Vetting

Household staff in Male present a moderate-integrity risk, shaped by the high proportion of migrant labour from Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, and India that characterises the Maldivian domestic employment market. Vetting covers national identity verification against the Maldives Immigration Department records and country-of-origin documentation, employment history references from previous employers in the Maldives or country of origin, criminal record enquiry with the Maldives Police Service, and a structured in-person interview. For staff with access to principal residences during periods of absence -- common in resort-based assignments where the principal travels frequently between islands -- a verified key and access-code management protocol is established from the outset.

Neighbourhood and Zone Assessment: Male

Male city is among the most densely populated capital cities in the world at approximately 170,000 people on a 5.8 square kilometre island. The Maafannu district in the northwest and the Henveiru district in the east are the primary residential zones for senior assignees; both are well-policed with low street crime by regional standards. Hulhumale, connected to Male by the Sinamalé Bridge, offers newer residential developments with lower density and proximity to Velana International Airport, and is the preferred zone for longer-term corporate residents. The FCDO Maldives travel advice (2024) notes a low crime rate but highlights terrorist risk (particularly targeting tourists and foreign nationals), referencing the 2020 attack on former President Nasheed and periodic gang violence in parts of Male city.

Emergency Protocols and Consular Contact

Primary emergency contacts: police 119, ambulance 102. Medical facilities: ADK Hospital Male (+960 331 3553) and Indira Gandhi Memorial Hospital (IGMH, +960 332 4851). For conditions requiring specialist intervention, medical evacuation to Colombo, Sri Lanka (approximately 90 minutes by air) or Mumbai is the standard routing; Velana International Airport (MLE) supports medevac operations. The nearest British High Commission is in Colombo, Sri Lanka (+94 11 539 0639). Emergency protocol should include an inter-island transfer component: many corporate assignees reside on resort islands and must reach Velana International Airport via seaplane or speedboat, both of which operate in weather-dependent conditions.

Inter-Island Transfer and Water-Transport Security

A defining feature of residential security in the Maldives is the dependence on water transport for most inter-island movement. Seaplane operations to resort islands are weather-dependent and operate daylight hours only; speedboat transfers are available after dark but subject to wave conditions. For corporate assignees on extended resort-island deployments, security planning must include: pre-agreed transfer protocols with confirmed departure windows, communication procedures for periods of transfer disruption, contingency accommodation in Male for delays caused by weather or operational issues, and a medical evacuation routing plan that accounts for transfer-dependent access to Velana International Airport.
