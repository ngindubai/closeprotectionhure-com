Tbilisi residential security serves a medium-risk city with specific considerations for historic building access control in the Old Town, political protest proximity in the central districts, and the Georgian Ministry of Internal Affairs licensing framework. The overall security specification is lower than high-risk East African or South Asian cities but requires specific attention to Tbilisi’s distinct residential property stock and political context.

What this covers

Operational detail for Tbilisi

Property Security Survey

Structured assessment of villa, apartment, and historic building properties in the Mtatsminda, Vera, Vake, and Saburtalo residential districts, covering access control, CCTV placement, alarm specification, and the specific Tbilisi consideration of historic building access control: much of the desirable residential stock in Tbilisi's Old Town and Mtatsminda areas is in converted 19th and early 20th century buildings with original entry systems and multiple informal access points that require specific assessment and remediation.

Georgia Ministry of Internal Affairs Guard Licensing

Residential guards in Georgia must be deployed through Ministry of Internal Affairs-licensed security companies under the Law on Private Detective and Security Activity (2010). Individual guards require Ministry of Internal Affairs registration. Our Tbilisi residential security partners hold current Ministry of Internal Affairs licensing; documentation is provided before guard deployment. Standard deployments are unarmed; armed guard provision requires specific Ministry of Internal Affairs authorisation.

Domestic Staff Vetting

Domestic staff vetting in Georgia uses the Georgian national ID (ID card or biometric passport), Police clearance certificate from the Ministry of Internal Affairs of Georgia, and direct employment reference contact. Employment references are contacted in Georgian (requiring local-language vetting capacity). The domestic staff market in Tbilisi is relatively small compared with larger post-Soviet capitals; many household workers have worked for multiple expatriate households, and reference networks within the expatriate community are a useful supplementary vetting resource.

Historic Building and Old Town Property Access

Tbilisi's most sought-after residential properties are in historic buildings in the Abanotubani, Narikala, and Mtatsminda areas. These properties often have: multiple informal entry points created by decades of subdivision and renovation; shared courtyards with multiple household access; staircases shared between residential and commercial uses; and original door and window hardware that does not meet contemporary access control standards. Residential security assessments for Old Town properties specifically address each access point, recommend specific upgrades to door, lock, and window hardware, and identify the priority remediation sequence.

Political Protest Awareness for Residential Areas

Georgia's post-2024 election political environment includes significant protest activity in central Tbilisi. The protest geography (Rustaveli Avenue, Orbeliani Square, and the parliament surroundings) is close to some of the city's premium residential districts. Residential security programmes for properties in the Vera and Vake districts include an advisory on the protest proximity risk: routes from the property to the principal hotel zone are assessed for protest exposure, and the property's immediate security posture is reviewed for the scenario of protest activity moving into the adjacent streets.

Emergency Protocol

Residential emergency protocols for Tbilisi cover: direct transfer routes to the Tbilisi Medical Centre and Aversi Palitra clinic as primary private facilities, and the MediClub Georgia network; and a documented property emergency egress plan. For complex specialist cases, medical evacuation to Istanbul, Turkey (approximately 2.5 hours by air from TBS) or to Vienna (4 hours) provides access to tier-1 European medical facilities. Georgia's healthcare infrastructure has improved significantly since 2004 but remains limited for complex specialist treatment.
