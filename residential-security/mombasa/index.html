Mombasa residential security serves a high-risk coastal environment with specific considerations for beach and marine perimeter management not present in inland city residential programmes. PSRA-licensed operators under Kenya’s Security Industry Act 2018 are the regulatory foundation; DCI-verified domestic staff vetting and specific night guard coastal patrol protocols complete the programme.

What this covers

Operational detail for Mombasa

Property Security Survey

Structured assessment of coastal villa, compound, and apartment properties in Nyali, Bamburi, Shanzu, and the Diani south coast corridor, covering perimeter security, access control, CCTV placement, alarm specification, guard post positioning, and the specific coastal property vulnerability of marine and beach access points. Mombasa's coastal properties often have direct beach or waterfront access that presents a specific perimeter challenge distinct from the landside compound security standard.

Kenya PSRA Guard Licensing

Residential guards in Kenya must be deployed through PSRA-licensed companies under the Security Industry Act 2018. Individual guards require PSRA registration. Armed guards require additional PSRA firearm authorisation. Our Mombasa residential security partners hold current PSRA licences; documentation is provided before guard deployment. Kenya's PSRA framework is among East Africa's most developed regulatory systems.

Domestic Staff Vetting

Domestic staff vetting in Kenya uses the Huduma Number national identity system, Kenya Certificate of Good Conduct from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI), and direct employment reference contact. For coastal properties, staff from the tourism and hospitality sector with multiple short-term employment histories require extended reference tracing. Household staff hired locally on the Mombasa and Diani coast are subject to the same vetting standard as those hired through Nairobi agencies.

Coastal Beach and Marine Perimeter

Residential properties on the Nyali, Bamburi, Shanzu, and Diani coast with direct beach access present a distinct perimeter security challenge: ocean-facing sections cannot be fully walled; beach access points are a vulnerability. Coastal residential security assessments specifically address: beach-facing section perimeter design (natural barrier supplementation, lighting, CCTV coverage of the beach approach); marine access point protocol for vessel arrivals; and the tide and low-water vulnerability window when beach access is most direct.

Night Security for Coastal Properties

Coastal property night security in Mombasa requires specific guard protocols for the beach perimeter sections. Night patrol coverage of the ocean-facing sections, motion-activated lighting on the beach approach, and clear guard communication protocols between the landside and beachside guard posts are standard elements of the coastal residential security programme. Night movement policy for the household outside the compound perimeter is an advisory element given elevated crime risk after dark on the Mombasa coast.

Emergency Protocol

Residential emergency protocols for Mombasa cover: direct transfer routes to Aga Khan Hospital Mombasa (on Vanga Road, Coast Province) as the primary private facility; the Coast General Hospital as the public facility; and a property emergency egress plan. For complex cases, medical evacuation to Nairobi (Kenyatta National Hospital or Aga Khan Hospital Nairobi, approximately one hour by domestic flight from MBA) is the standard option. Coastal property emergency protocols include a specific marine evacuation option for beachside properties.
