What this covers

Operational detail for Freetown

Private Security Industry Act Licensing

Private security in Sierra Leone is regulated under the Private Security Industry Act. Residential security companies must hold current licensing under this Act, and individual guards require documented registration. Verify operator licensing and individual guard registration before engaging any Freetown residential security provider. The FCDO advises a high degree of caution throughout Sierra Leone. Given the high-risk environment, a residential security provider with current Sierra Leone licensing and documented experience in the post-conflict Freetown operating environment is required. Source: Private Security Industry Act, Sierra Leone; FCDO Sierra Leone travel advice (2026).

Recommended Residential Zones in Freetown

The Aberdeen peninsula, the main international hotel and NGO office area on the western peninsula of Freetown, is the primary residential zone for mining sector expatriates and senior NGO leadership. Aberdeen's geography on a narrow peninsula provides natural access management advantages. The Hill Station district, the highest-elevation residential area used by diplomatic personnel and senior international staff, offers lower ambient crime exposure than central Freetown. The Spur Road corridor is an additional established expatriate residential area. All three zones are materially more manageable than central Freetown, the Waterside commercial area, or outer residential districts.

High-Risk Compound Security Hardening

A residential security survey for Freetown in a high-risk environment covers perimeter wall height and integrity, dedicated compound security guard posts with multiple licensed guards, vehicle access gate control with generator backup, comprehensive CCTV coverage of the perimeter and all entry points, safe room or hardened shelter specification, and emergency communications independent of the standard power and telephone infrastructure. Guard post positioning and overlap of sight lines is assessed for each property. Generator backup for all security-critical systems is not optional in Freetown given the unreliable grid power supply. Night-time guard staffing is a specific focus given the elevated robbery risk after dark.

Domestic Staff Vetting

Domestic staff vetting in Freetown uses identity document verification, Sierra Leone Police Force clearance, and employment reference checks from prior international mining, NGO, or diplomatic households. Sierra Leone's post-conflict labour market means that many domestic staff candidates have extended prior experience with international organisations. Reference verification from prior international employers is the most informative vetting step. Probationary access restrictions apply until vetting is complete and a consistent track record established. Staff are briefed on compound security protocols including visitor management, delivery handling, and suspicious activity reporting.

Post-Election Unrest and Emergency Protocols

Sierra Leone's post-election violence in 2022 to 2023 demonstrated that political unrest can escalate rapidly in Freetown. Residential security programmes include current political monitoring against FCDO and US State Department reporting, confirmed British High Commission emergency contact procedures, documented shelter-in-place and evacuation plans, and emergency supplies for shelter-in-place scenarios. The Lungi airport estuary crossing is the primary evacuation bottleneck: evacuation plans confirm helicopter access arrangements from Freetown waterfront to Lungi International Airport (FNA) for emergency departures.

Medical Response and West Africa Medevac

Choithram Hospital in Freetown provides the best available private care for non-complex conditions. For serious trauma, complex surgery, or conditions requiring advanced facilities, medical evacuation from Lungi International Airport (FNA) to Accra, Ghana (approximately 1 hour by air) or Abidjan, Cote d'Ivoire (approximately 1 hour) is the standard pathway. A medevac provider with West Africa experience must be on retainer before any residential programme commences. The medevac activation protocol is briefed to all residential programme personnel.
