1. Competitive Landscape Overview

Key Finding: None of the five target competitors operate a city-level SEO play for bodyguard/EP hire. They all target enterprise buyers through service-category pages and thought leadership content. The "hire a bodyguard in [city]" search space is essentially uncontested at scale.

The security/EP industry has two distinct tiers online:

TierPlayersOnline ModelSEO Approach
Enterprise Control Risks, Pinkerton, GardaWorld/Crisis24, Global Guardian Corporate consultancy. Inbound via reputation, referrals, brand search. Contact forms lead to sales teams. Service-category pages + thought leadership blog + whitepapers. No city-level landing pages. SEO is incidental, not primary acquisition.
Local/Boutique Hundreds of small firms per country (1-50 operators) Local websites, Google Business Profile, word of mouth. Limited SEO presence. 1-5 pages. "Bodyguard hire [their city]" with minimal content. No cross-city coverage.

Our play sits between these tiers: city-level SEO at global scale, targeting the individual/SME buyer who searches for protection in a specific location. Neither the enterprise firms nor the local boutiques compete here.

  • All competitors use no-price model. Every firm hides pricing behind "contact us." We also use a quote-based model (pricing is entirely country and service-level dependent) but differentiate through lower-friction enquiry forms and faster response.

10. Strategic Opportunities

1. City-Level SEO Monopoly

PRIMARY OPPORTUNITY

Zero enterprise competitors have city-level pages. With 3,000-5,000 pages targeting "[service] in [city]" queries, we can own this space before anyone else enters. The moat is the volume and quality of city-specific content that would take competitors years to replicate.

2. "Bodyguard" Keyword Ownership

HIGH VALUE

Enterprise competitors actively avoid the word "bodyguard." Pinkerton says EP "should never be mistaken for body guarding." This means "bodyguard hire", "hire a bodyguard", "personal bodyguard" keywords are unclaimed by high-authority domains. Higher search volume than "executive protection."

3. Lower-Friction Enquiry Differentiator

MEDIUM VALUE

Pricing is entirely country and service-level dependent, so we use a quote-based model like competitors. The differentiator is speed and simplicity: our 6-field confidential enquiry form (vs 10+ fields from competitors) captures the time-sensitive buyer. Faster response to enquiries closes the deal before the buyer contacts an enterprise firm.

4. Individual/SME Buyer Gap

HIGH VALUE

Competitors target CSOs, security directors, and enterprise procurement. Nobody targets: HNWI PAs booking for their principal, media producers booking for journalists in conflict zones, corporate travel managers at mid-size firms, individual business travellers to high-risk cities. Our form and funnel serve these buyers.

5. AS Solution Domain Vacuum

MEDIUM VALUE

AS Solution was one of the few EP-specialist online presences. Their domain expiry means broken backlinks, orphaned content references, and abandoned keyword positions in the EP space. We can capture some of this displaced traffic.

6. Free Risk Intelligence vs Gated Products

HIGH VALUE

Control Risks charges for RiskMap. Global Guardian gates some reports. We publish city risk ratings, threat summaries, and safety tips for free on every city page. This is better for SEO (indexable content) and better for trust (proving expertise without a paywall).

7. Low-Friction Enquiry Path

MEDIUM VALUE

Competitors require 10+ form fields, job titles, company names. A corporate security director may accept this. A PA trying to book a bodyguard for her boss's trip to Bogota next week will not. Our 6-field confidential enquiry (2 optional) captures the time-sensitive buyer.

8. Regulatory Content Authority

HIGH VALUE

No competitor publishes city/country-level security regulations (licence requirements, firearms laws, foreign operator rules). Our "Local Regulations" section on every city page fills a genuine information gap, builds E-E-A-T, and targets long-tail regulatory search queries.
