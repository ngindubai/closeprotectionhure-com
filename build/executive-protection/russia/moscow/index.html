Security landscape

Moscow security overview

Russia is the only country on our platform where both the FCDO and the US State Department issue their highest-level travel warnings simultaneously. Against All Travel. Level 4: Do Not Travel. These are not precautionary statements. They reflect an assessment that the risks to foreign nationals in Russia are severe, layered, and partially outside anyone's ability to control (FCDO travel advisory, Against All Travel, April 2026; US State Dept Level 4, Do Not Travel).

The terrorism picture in Moscow changed permanently on 22 March 2024. Gunmen stormed the Crocus City Concert Hall and killed 145 people. IS claimed the attack. It exposed gaps in a security apparatus that had focused almost entirely on political threats and the Ukraine conflict. Six months later, coordinated assaults on a synagogue and churches in Dagestan killed more than 20 people. At the same time, Ukrainian drone strikes now reach Moscow on a regular basis, hitting residential areas and causing disruptions at Sheremetyevo and Domodedovo airports.

For corporate travellers, the threat is not limited to terrorism. The Russian state itself presents risk. December 2024 legislation expanded criminal liability for foreign nationals engaged in activities deemed "against Russian interests." The definition is broad. Social media activity, business dealings that attract official attention, even casual conversations can trigger interest from security services. The FCDO warns that its ability to assist detained British nationals in Russia is extremely limited (OSAC crime rating: High).

Street crime is a secondary concern, but it exists. Drink spiking in bars leads to robbery. In Moscow winters, victims left unconscious outside face hypothermia. Fake police demand documents and steal from tourists. Railway stations are hotspots. Racial attacks against African and Asian visitors are documented.

Regulations

Security regulations in Russia

Russia's private security sector operates under Federal Law No. 2487-1 (1992, amended), with Rosgvardia (the National Guard) as the regulatory authority. Licensed CHOPs (private security companies) can deploy armed personnel carrying firearms registered with the regulator. This makes Russia one of the few markets where private armed EP is a legal, structured option.

The barriers for foreign operators are now insurmountable for Western companies. Sanctions imposed since 2022 restrict financial transactions, corporate registration, and personnel movement. Russia's December 2024 legislation criminalises a broad range of activities by foreigners. Russian citizenship is required for most private security roles, with mandatory FSB and MVD background checks. The domestic security industry is large and has expanded significantly through private military company activity during the Ukraine conflict.

Any executive protection operation in Moscow must use Russian-licensed personnel. There is no legal mechanism for a Western security company to deploy its own team. Our model reflects this: we vet, coordinate, and support from outside Russia while Russian-licensed operators provide the physical protection on the ground (Federal Law No. 2487-1, 1992 as amended; FCDO travel advisory, April 2026).

Traveller guidance

Security tips for visitors to Moscow

Scrub your digital footprint before travelling. Russian authorities have prosecuted foreign nationals over social media posts about the Ukraine conflict. Review your accounts, delete anything that could be interpreted as supporting Ukraine, and consider using a clean device for your trip. The December 2024 law defining activities "against Russian interests" is intentionally broad.

Assume surveillance. State monitoring of communications, hotel rooms, and electronic devices is standard practice in Russia for foreign business visitors. Use encrypted communications where possible, but understand that VPN usage itself may attract attention. Do not discuss anything sensitive in hotel rooms or over Russian mobile networks.

Drink spiking is not a minor concern. Victims in Moscow bars are drugged, robbed, and left outside. In temperatures that drop below minus 20 Celsius, this is life-threatening. Never leave a drink unattended. Avoid accepting drinks from strangers. If a member of your group feels suddenly unwell, get them to a warm, secure location immediately and call 103.

Fake police scams target tourists in central Moscow, particularly around Red Square and Arbat. If someone in uniform demands to inspect your wallet, insist on going to a police station. Carry a photocopy of your passport, visa, and migration card separately from the originals. Random legitimate passport checks by police do occur and can result in up to 48 hours of detention.

Build departure flexibility into every Moscow trip. There are no direct flights to the UK or EU. Routes go through Turkey, UAE, or Central Asia. Drone attacks cause unpredictable flight disruptions at both Sheremetyevo and Domodedovo. Have a backup departure plan that does not depend on a single airport.
