Security landscape

Manila security overview for corporate travellers

Manila holds a high overall risk rating of 4/5. Crime, terrorism, and kidnapping each score 4/5 (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026; OSAC Critical). The US State Department rates the Philippines at Level 2. The FCDO advises against all travel to western and central Mindanao and the Sulu archipelago, which is directly relevant for corporate principals with mining, energy, or agricultural operations in the south.

The terrorism threat is active. Abu Sayyaf Group, IS-Philippines, and BIFF maintain operational capability. In December 2023, an explosion during Catholic mass in Marawi killed four people. Historical targets include shopping malls and public transport. Manila's response is visible: metal detectors and bag checks at every building entrance. For corporate visitors, this means soft-target venues (malls used for meetings, hotel lobbies, conference centres) carry residual risk that an EP team must factor into advance planning.

Kidnapping is the threat that changes the calculus for executive protection. ASG has historically targeted foreigners including British nationals. Criminal kidnapping for ransom operates in parallel within metropolitan Manila. A corporate visitor whose profile, itinerary, or sector affiliation creates exposure needs active protection, not just awareness. The objective is to eliminate the isolation window that kidnappers require.

Street-level crime fills the remaining picture. Armed robbery, phone snatching, taxi-related crime. Police reliability sits at 2/5, with high corruption and a mixed PNP reputation. Corporate travellers cannot rely on police response as a primary safety mechanism. Private security is the norm here, and for good reason.

Regulations

Security regulations in the Philippines

Private security in the Philippines is regulated by the Supervisory Office for Security and Investigation Agencies (SOSIA) under the Philippine National Police. The governing legislation is RA 5487 (Private Security Agency Law), significantly amended by RA 11917 in 2022. Companies must hold PNP-SOSIA licences with financial qualification and organisational structure approval. The industry employs over 600,000 licensed security guards.

The 60-40 ownership rule is constitutionally mandated. Filipino nationals must hold at least 60% of any security company operating in the country. Foreign firms cannot hold a controlling interest. International operators must partner with a Filipino-owned entity to provide security services. This affects how EP programmes are structured for multinational clients. Our operations in Manila are delivered through locally licensed partner agencies that meet both PNP-SOSIA requirements and our internal vetting standards.

Armed security is standard practice. PNP-FEO (Firearms and Explosives Office) manages weapons licensing for security agencies. All EP personnel require PNP-SOSIA individual licensing, NBI clearance, drug testing, neuro-psychiatric examination, and annual in-service training (RA 5487; RA 11917, 2022; PNP-SOSIA).

Traveller guidance

Security guidance for corporate visitors to Manila

Makati and Bonifacio Global City (BGC) are the primary zones for corporate activity. Both have strong private security infrastructure, modern hotel stock, and established business environments. Ortigas Center serves as a secondary hub. Alabang in the south offers gated residential compounds for longer stays. Keep your schedule within these areas where possible, and brief your EP team on any movements outside them (OSAC).

Conference venues, hotel meeting rooms, and restaurant locations should be confirmed with your EP team in advance. Advance work matters in Manila. A venue that looks appropriate on a website may present access control issues, poor evacuation routes, or proximity to areas where the threat environment is different. Your EP team assesses the location before you arrive.

Typhoon season (June to November) requires contingency planning. Flight cancellations, road flooding, power failures, and transport network collapse are all possible on short notice. Your EP team monitors forecasts and maintains alternate extraction plans. If a major storm is approaching, schedule flexibility is essential.

Do not share your itinerary on social media or with anyone outside your trusted circle. The kidnapping threat in the Philippines is real, and publicly broadcasting when and where a corporate principal will be present creates unnecessary exposure. Your EP team manages information security around your movements as part of the programme.
