Security landscape

Dubai security overview

Dubai carries an overall risk rating of 2/5 (Low). Crime sits at 1/5, the lowest in our 15-city network. Street crime against visitors is essentially nonexistent. Fraud and scam activity represent most reported incidents, not violence. Surveillance is pervasive, policing is visible, and penalties are severe enough to suppress opportunistic crime entirely (OSAC crime rating: Low).

Terrorism risk is 2/5. The threat is regional, not domestic. Houthi maritime operations in surrounding waters and broader Middle East instability prompted the FCDO to advise against all but essential travel to the UAE as of April 2026. The US State Department holds a Level 2 advisory. No attacks have occurred within Dubai in recent years, and counter-terrorism capability is strong and well-funded.

Kidnapping and civil unrest each score 1/5. Protests are effectively prohibited. Public criticism of the UAE on social media is a criminal offence. This is a predictable operating environment in terms of civil disorder.

For executives, the practical risks are road accidents on Sheikh Zayed Road (transport risk: 2/5), the UAE's strict legal framework around drugs, alcohol, and public conduct, and regional instability that could affect flight operations or trigger corporate travel policy restrictions. The police reliability score is 5/5, the highest in our network. Response is fast, corruption is minimal, and tourist police are available (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026).

Regulations

Security regulations in the UAE

SIRA (Security Industry Regulatory Agency) governs private security in Dubai. Abu Dhabi operates under ADSSSA. Each emirate maintains its own licensing standards, so coverage in one does not automatically extend to another. EP operatives working in Dubai must hold current SIRA certification, which includes background checks, medical fitness, and training through SIRA-accredited centres.

Firearms are prohibited for private security across the UAE. No exceptions for EP assignments. No weapons in vehicles. Even replica weapons are illegal. Armed operations are limited to government-authorised activities only. All executive protection in Dubai is unarmed, relying on awareness, planning, and coordination rather than armed response (SIRA regulatory framework).

Foreign EP providers cannot operate directly. A local entity or free zone licence is required, along with SIRA registration. UAE national hiring preference applies across the industry. Our Dubai EP operatives work through locally registered, SIRA-licensed companies that satisfy both regulatory obligations and our internal vetting framework.

Traveller guidance

Security tips for executives visiting Dubai

Dubai's main business districts are extremely safe. DIFC, Downtown, Business Bay, and Media City all have strong security infrastructure. Marina, JBR, and Palm Jumeirah are equally secure for evening schedules. There are effectively no areas that present a criminal threat to visiting executives (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026).

Road safety is the leading physical risk. Sheikh Zayed Road traffic is fast and aggressive. Tailgating at high speed is routine. Lane discipline is poor by European standards. For executives on tight schedules moving between venues, a professional driver with local road knowledge is the single most effective risk reduction measure. Do not rent a car unless you are familiar with the driving culture.

Legal awareness is essential for corporate visitors. Prescription medications that are legal in Europe or the US may be controlled substances in the UAE. Check before you travel. Business disputes can trigger travel bans. Social media posts made from Dubai that criticise the UAE or its government are prosecutable. Brief your executive before arrival, not after an incident.

During Ramadan, public eating, drinking, and smoking during daylight hours is prohibited. Dress conservatively at government offices. Alcohol is available in licensed hotels and restaurants but public intoxication is a criminal offence. These rules are enforced consistently and apply to visitors without exception.
