Security landscape

Jakarta security overview

Jakarta's overall risk rating sits at 3/5, a number that smooths over a sharp spike in one category: terrorism at 4/5. The FCDO rates further terrorist attacks in Indonesia as "likely" and warns that Western interests are specifically at risk (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026). The US State Department holds Indonesia at Level 2 (exercise increased caution). OSAC classifies the threat as HIGH.

The terrorism threat is not abstract. Jemaah Islamiyah remnants and IS-affiliated cells have carried out recent attacks. A suicide bomber hit a police post in Bandung in 2022. A suicide attack struck Makassar cathedral in 2021. Jakarta itself has been a target in previous years. The FCDO identifies hotels, bars, shopping malls carrying international brands, places of worship, police posts, foreign embassies, and transport hubs as target categories. Risk heightens around Christmas, New Year, Ramadan, and Independence Day. For corporate travellers staying at international chain hotels, this places them inside the targeting profile rather than adjacent to it.

Crime scores 3/5. Pickpocketing, bag snatching, credit card fraud, and drink spiking are documented. Scams target foreigners. Methanol poisoning from counterfeit alcohol is a real killer across Indonesia. The FCDO warns specifically about this risk. Civil unrest scores 3/5. Elections, events in the Middle East, and religious tensions trigger protests that can turn violent without warning. Student demonstrations have blocked arterial roads in the past.

Police reliability rates at 2/5. Corruption is documented. Officers may seek informal payments. Drug enforcement is ferocious: trafficking carries the death penalty, and police regularly raid venues popular with foreigners. British nationals have received extended sentences for drug offences in Indonesia. This is an operational consideration for any EP team managing evening schedules (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026).

Regulations

Security regulations in Indonesia

Chief of Police Regulation No. 24/2007 and the Government Regulation on Industrial Security set the framework for private security in Indonesia. Companies need a business licence from the provincial government, police approval for operations, and Ministry of Manpower oversight. The regulatory environment is layered but functional.

Firearms are prohibited for civilians. Full stop. Only military, police, and authorised officials carry weapons in Indonesia. Armed private security does not exist in any form. When armed protection is operationally necessary, Polri provides it. Licensed security firms arrange police escort through established liaison channels. This model applies across corporate EP, resource sector protection, and high-profile movements.

Foreign operators must establish an Indonesian legal entity (PT PMA) to operate. The negative investment list may restrict foreign participation in the security sector, and a local partner is typically required. Personnel must be Indonesian citizens. Background checks run through police. Physical fitness requirements apply. Polri sets minimum training standards, but multinationals with operations in Indonesia routinely set higher internal standards for their security teams (Chief of Police Regulation No. 24/2007; Ministry of Manpower).

Traveller guidance

Security tips for corporate visitors to Jakarta

Base yourself in the districts where security infrastructure and international presence concentrate. Sudirman CBD houses the major business towers and international hotel chains. Menteng is the diplomatic quarter with embassy proximity. Kuningan is the embassy district proper. Kemang serves the expatriate social scene. These areas offer visible security, functioning surveillance, and fast access to police and diplomatic support (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026).

Hotels with international brand affiliation sit on the documented terrorism target list. This is not a reason to avoid them. They also have the most developed security infrastructure: screening, CCTV, access control, and trained security staff. The calculus is about awareness, not avoidance. Your EP team assesses each property and adjusts protocols accordingly. Request rooms above the third floor but below the seventh. Avoid ground-floor restaurants facing the street during heightened threat periods.

Do not underestimate Indonesia's drug enforcement. Police conduct random raids on bars and nightclubs popular with foreigners, including urine testing on the spot. Trafficking carries the death penalty. Possession carries years in prison. If your evening schedule includes hospitality, your EP team factors this into venue selection. Methanol poisoning from counterfeit alcohol is a separate but documented risk. Only consume sealed, branded spirits from reputable establishments (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026).

Jakarta's traffic is among the worst in the world. A meeting across the city can take two hours by road. Movement planning is a core EP function here, not an afterthought. Your team coordinates departure times, route selection, and contingency routes around both congestion and protest activity. During elections or following external political events, demonstrations can block arterial roads without notice.
