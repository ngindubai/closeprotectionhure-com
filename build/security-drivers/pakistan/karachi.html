Security landscape

Karachi transport security overview

Karachi holds an overall risk rating of 5/5, with transport risk at 4/5 and crime, terrorism, and kidnapping each at 5/5 (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026; US State Dept Level 3; OSAC Critical). For anyone moving through Karachi by road, these numbers describe a city where every journey carries compounded risk from multiple simultaneous threat categories.

The airport approach is the most immediate concern. In October 2024, a suicide attack near Jinnah International Airport killed four people, including two Chinese nationals. The airport perimeter and approach roads remain on the target list for TTP, BLA, and ISKP. Arriving at or departing from Jinnah International without a pre-positioned armoured vehicle and a planned route with variance is an unmanaged exposure window at a documented attack site (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026).

Carjacking and vehicle-based kidnapping operate throughout the city. Criminals target vehicles at intersections, in slower traffic, and at predictable stopping points. Express kidnapping follows the same pattern seen across South Asia and Latin America: forced entry into the vehicle, ATM withdrawals at gunpoint, escalation if the victim resists. Outside DHA and Clifton, where military checkpoints and private security create a higher baseline, road travel through areas like Saddar, Korangi, or anywhere near Lyari introduces gang warfare as an additional variable.

Rail travel is not viable. The FCDO explicitly advises avoiding all railways in Pakistan. In March 2025, BLA hijacked the Jaffar Express train and killed 64 passengers. Derailments, attacks on railway infrastructure, and ambushes at rural stations make rail the single most dangerous transport option in the country. Road transport, with appropriate security, is the only practical alternative for inter-city movement (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026).

Public transport in all forms should be avoided. The FCDO advises against Metro Bus, local buses, and e-taxis. Online taxi apps do not guarantee driver vetting, vehicle standards, or route security. Erratic driving standards across the city compound the baseline risk. DHA checkpoints create congestion that can be managed by a driver who knows the system. For everyone else, those queues are just another exposure window.

Regulations

Security regulations in Pakistan

Private security regulation in Pakistan is provincial. Sindh province governs Karachi. Punjab operates under the Punjab Private Security Companies Ordinance (2002). KP and Balochistan maintain separate frameworks. There is no single national licence. A company registered in Sindh cannot automatically operate in Punjab. Inter-provincial travel may require No Objection Certificates. Armed drivers must hold valid arms licences issued by district authorities, and those licences may require additional clearances for cross-provincial operations (Provincial Home Departments).

Firearms licensing is more permissive than most countries. Armed security drivers are standard for corporate and VIP transport. All drivers require background verification through police and arms training certification. Foreign operators must register locally and work through a licensed Pakistani partner. Government authorities routinely assign police escorts for foreign clients travelling outside major commercial zones, and for inter-provincial convoy operations the escort may include Rangers personnel (Provincial Home Departments; Pakistan Security Guard Ordinance).

Traveller guidance

Transport safety guidance for Karachi visitors

Avoid all public transport. The FCDO is unambiguous: avoid all public transport in Pakistan, including Metro Bus and local bus services. Railways are not an option. The BLA Jaffar Express hijacking in March 2025 killed 64 people. Derailments and infrastructure attacks make rail the most dangerous transport mode in the country. E-taxis and online taxi apps do not guarantee driver vetting or vehicle security. All ground transport should go through your security provider (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026).

Keep ground movements within DHA and Clifton wherever your schedule allows. Defence Housing Authority checkpoints, while they create congestion, also create a controlled perimeter that reduces carjacking and kidnapping risk. Clifton operates with private security and police checkpoints. Bahria Town is gated. Outside these zones, road conditions deteriorate and threat density increases. If your business requires travel to Saddar, industrial areas, or the port, use an armoured vehicle with an armed driver as a non-negotiable baseline.

Jinnah International Airport is a documented target. The October 2024 suicide attack killed four people on the approach road. Do not arrange airport transport on arrival. Do not use taxi apps or airport taxi services. Your vehicle should be pre-positioned before you land, with a planned route and variance already established. The airport perimeter is where you are most exposed. Minimise time there.

If mobile data or internet is cut (common during political unrest, elections, or security operations), your driver should be operating on an offline protocol with pre-established routes and rally points. Do not assume connectivity for navigation or communication. Satellite phones or VHF radio backup should be part of any serious transport operation in Karachi.
