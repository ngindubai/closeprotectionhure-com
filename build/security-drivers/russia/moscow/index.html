Security landscape

Moscow security overview

Ground transport in Moscow carries risks that do not exist in any other city on our platform. Drone attacks from the Ukraine conflict hit Moscow itself. Residential areas, infrastructure near airports, and districts on the city's outskirts have all been struck. Sheremetyevo and Domodedovo airports experience frequent flight delays, diversions, and cancellations as airspace restrictions are imposed at short notice. Getting into and out of Moscow by air is unpredictable (FCDO travel advisory, Against All Travel, April 2026).

On the roads, Moscow is a different problem. Traffic police conduct random vehicle stops and document checks. Foreign nationals in vehicles are targets for spot inspections that can result in delays, requests for bribes, or short-term detention. Road conditions outside Moscow's main highways deteriorate sharply. Driving at night on peripheral roads is not recommended. The FCDO advises against it explicitly (US State Dept Level 4, Do Not Travel).

Then there are the criminal risks specific to transport. Unregistered taxis have been linked to robberies. Airport arrivals are vulnerable to passport theft. Fake police stop vehicles and demand payment. For a foreign visitor arriving at a Moscow airport, the journey from the terminal to your hotel is the first exposure point, and without a pre-arranged, vetted driver, it is an unnecessary risk.

The broader context matters too. March 2024: 145 people killed at Crocus City Concert Hall by IS gunmen. Regular drone strikes on Moscow. December 2024 legislation criminalising foreign activities. The FCDO's Against All Travel advisory. This is the environment your driver operates in (OSAC crime rating: High).

Regulations

Security regulations in Russia

Russia's private security industry falls under Federal Law No. 2487-1 (1992, amended), regulated by Rosgvardia. Security transport operators require appropriate licensing. Traffic police (DPS, part of the MVD) conduct roadside checks and can stop any vehicle for document inspection. Foreign nationals should expect this, particularly when travelling in vehicles with non-standard plates or near government-restricted zones.

For Western companies, operating security transport in Russia is not possible under current sanctions. Russian citizenship requirements apply to licensed security roles. FSB and MVD background checks are mandatory. The December 2024 legislation expanding criminal liability for foreigners adds another layer: even routine business activities can attract scrutiny if they are deemed "against Russian interests."

Traveller guidance

Security tips for visitors to Moscow

Never use unregistered taxis in Moscow. Unlicensed operators at airports and railway stations have been linked to robberies targeting foreign nationals. Pre-arranged transport through a vetted provider eliminates this risk entirely. If you must use a taxi service, only use app-based services where the driver and vehicle are registered and tracked.

Traffic police (DPS) conduct random stops. They can ask for your passport, visa, and vehicle documents. Cooperate, but know your rights. If asked to pay an on-the-spot fine, request a formal citation. Fake police are also a documented problem: individuals in uniform who demand document inspections and steal cash. A vetted driver familiar with DPS procedures knows how to handle legitimate stops and when something is not right.

Moscow's metro is generally safe and operates airport-style security screening at stations. However, it was a target of the 2010 bombings by North Caucasus insurgents. For security-conscious travel, a private vehicle with a known driver remains the preferred option.

Do not drive at night outside Moscow's main highways. Road conditions deteriorate sharply. Lighting is poor. The FCDO advises against it. Within central Moscow, roads are maintained, but congestion is severe during peak hours. Build buffer time into every schedule. The drive from Sheremetyevo to central Moscow can take anywhere from 45 minutes to over two hours depending on traffic and weather.

Keep your departure plans flexible. Drone attacks cause unpredictable airport disruptions. Know the current status of both Sheremetyevo and Domodedovo before heading to either. Have overland departure options mapped if air travel becomes impossible at short notice.
