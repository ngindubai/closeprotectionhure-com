Security landscape

Lagos transport security overview

Transport is the single biggest vulnerability for visitors to Lagos. The FCDO specifically warns about carjacking on urban and rural roads, unauthorized criminal checkpoints, and armed robbery on expressways after dark (FCDO Nigeria advisory, April 2026). OSAC rates the overall crime environment as critical.

Carjacking is the defining road risk. Criminals target vehicles at traffic lights, intersections, and on the bridges connecting Lagos Island to the mainland. The Lekki-Epe Expressway is an established hotspot for armed robbery, particularly at night. Criminals also set up unauthorized checkpoints on highways, using them to stop and rob vehicle occupants.

Airport transfers are a specific concern. Murtala Muhammed International has documented issues with follow-from-airport crime, where criminals identify arriving passengers by their luggage or appearance and track their vehicles to hotels or residences. OSAC also notes petty theft in the baggage area and taxi scams targeting new arrivals.

Public transport is not a realistic option. Danfo minibuses and BRT services are targets for robbery. The Lagos traffic environment is aggressive and unfamiliar to visitors. A security driver who knows the roads, the threat patterns, and the alternative routes is the minimum standard for corporate travel in Lagos.

Regulations

Security regulations in Nigeria

Private security is regulated by the NSCDC (Nigeria Security and Civil Defence Corps). Licensed security companies can provide armed or unarmed driver services. Armoured vehicles are legal with specific permits, used widely by banks, oil companies, and HNWIs. Foreign security firms must partner with Nigerian-registered companies to operate legally (NSCDC regulations, April 2026).

Your security driver will be a locally licensed Nigerian professional operating under a registered firm. Foreign nationals cannot carry firearms in Nigeria, so if you want an armed driver or an armed CPO in the vehicle, that must be a locally licensed operator.

Traveller guidance

Transport security tips for Lagos

Never hail a taxi at the airport or on the street. Pre-arrange all transfers through your hotel, security provider, or a registered rideshare app. The Murtala Muhammed International arrival area is monitored by criminals looking for disoriented travellers with expensive luggage.

Keep vehicle doors locked and windows up at all times. Smash-and-grab crimes at traffic lights are common. Do not display phones or valuables through the window. If your vehicle is blocked, do not exit. Your driver will know how to respond.

Avoid all road travel after dark when possible. The Lekki-Epe Expressway, Third Mainland Bridge, and highway approaches to mainland Lagos carry significantly higher risk at night. If night travel is unavoidable, an armoured vehicle is strongly recommended. Danfo minibuses and public BRT should be avoided entirely.
