Security landscape

Dubai security overview

Dubai records an overall risk rating of 2/5 (Low) with the lowest crime score in our network at 1/5. Street crime against visitors is essentially nonexistent. The security environment is defined by heavy surveillance, visible policing, and severe penalties that make opportunistic crime extremely rare. Fraud and scam activity account for most reported incidents (OSAC crime rating: Low).

Terrorism risk sits at 2/5. The threat is regional. Houthi maritime operations in surrounding waters and broader Middle East instability have pushed the FCDO to advise against all but essential travel to the UAE as of April 2026. The US State Department holds a Level 2 advisory. Within Dubai itself, counter-terrorism capability is strong and well-funded, and no recent attacks have occurred domestically.

Kidnapping and civil unrest both score 1/5. Protests are effectively prohibited. Social media criticism of the UAE can lead to criminal charges. This is not a city where demonstrations block roads or disrupt transport schedules.

For security drivers specifically, the critical data point is transport risk: 2/5. Road accidents are the primary physical threat to visitors in Dubai. Sheikh Zayed Road carries fast, aggressive traffic. Tailgating is standard. Lane discipline is poor. This is exactly why a vetted driver with defensive training and local road knowledge makes a practical difference, even in a city where crime is negligible (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026).

Regulations

Security regulations in the UAE

Private security services in Dubai, including secure transport, fall under SIRA (Security Industry Regulatory Agency). Abu Dhabi has its own authority, ADSSSA. Each emirate sets its own licensing standards. Drivers providing security-related transport services must comply with SIRA's registration requirements, which include background screening, fitness checks, and documented training.

Firearms are prohibited entirely. No civilian carry. No private security carry. This extends to vehicles: no weapons of any kind are permitted in private security transport. Armoured vehicles are available through specialised, authorised providers but are uncommon for standard executive transport assignments in Dubai.

Foreign operators must establish a local entity or partnership and complete SIRA registration. UAE nationals receive hiring preference. Our Dubai drivers work through locally registered SIRA-licensed companies that meet both regulatory obligations and our internal vetting framework (SIRA regulatory framework).

Traveller guidance

Security tips for visitors to Dubai

All major business and leisure districts (Downtown, DIFC, Marina, JBR, Palm Jumeirah) are very safe for visitors. Security infrastructure is visible and effective. Deira and older commercial districts feel different after dark but crime remains rare everywhere. There are effectively no areas of Dubai we would classify as unsafe (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026).

Road safety is the number one risk for visitors. Sheikh Zayed Road traffic is fast and aggressive. Tailgating at 120 km/h is routine. Lane changes happen without indicators. Roundabouts in older districts follow unwritten local rules. If you are unfamiliar with Dubai's driving culture, do not rent a car. A professional driver is the single most effective risk reduction measure available in this city.

Public transport is safe and well-maintained. The Metro covers main corridors. Uber and Careem operate throughout Dubai and provide traceable, air-conditioned transport. Taxis are metered and reliable. DXB Airport has excellent security and is well-connected by road and Metro.

Legal awareness matters. Zero tolerance on drugs. Alcohol only in licensed venues. Social media criticism of the UAE is a criminal offence. Dress modestly at government offices and cultural sites. During Ramadan, do not eat, drink, or smoke in public during daylight hours. These laws apply to visitors without exception, and enforcement is real.
