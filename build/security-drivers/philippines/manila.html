Transport landscape

Manila transport security overview

Ground transport in Manila carries a risk profile that most visitors underestimate. The overall transport risk scores 3/5, but that figure masks specific dangers that sit much higher (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026; OSAC Critical). Armed hold-ups on jeepneys and buses are documented and have resulted in fatalities. These are not isolated incidents. They are a pattern that makes public road transport one of the highest-risk daily activities in Metro Manila.

NAIA airport is the first exposure point. Unlicensed taxi operators work the arrivals area, and the FCDO specifically warns against using them. Taxi-related robbery is a documented threat for foreign arrivals. The transition from terminal to vehicle is where many visitors are most exposed, arriving without local knowledge, carrying luggage, visibly foreign, and uncertain which vehicles are legitimate.

Metro Manila's road network centres on EDSA, a multi-lane highway that functions as the city's main artery and its worst bottleneck. Traffic is gridlocked during peak hours and unpredictable at all other times. Poor road conditions, non-enforcement of traffic laws, and aggressive driving behaviour from all categories of road user make every journey a variable. Random security checkpoints add delay and require drivers who know the protocols and can manage the interaction professionally.

Typhoon season (June to November) rewrites the transport equation. Major storms cause flooding that submerges roads, collapses infrastructure, and shuts down the transport network. Power failures disable traffic signals. Routes that worked yesterday may be impassable today. A security driver with contingency route knowledge and real-time weather intelligence is the difference between reaching your destination and being stranded in a flooded district with no extraction plan.

Regulations

Security regulations in the Philippines

Private security operations in the Philippines fall under PNP-SOSIA (Supervisory Office for Security and Investigation Agencies), a division of the Philippine National Police. The governing legislation is RA 5487, amended by RA 11917 (2022). All security personnel, including security drivers attached to protection details, require PNP-SOSIA individual licensing, NBI clearance, drug testing, and neuro-psychiatric examination.

Armed drivers operate under the same PNP-FEO (Firearms and Explosives Office) framework as other armed security personnel. Licensed agencies can arm personnel through proper authorisation channels. Armed security is standard practice in the Philippines and does not draw unusual attention.

The constitutional 60-40 ownership rule applies. Foreign firms must partner with Filipino-majority owned entities to provide security services. All transport security is delivered through locally licensed partner agencies that meet both PNP-SOSIA requirements and our internal vetting standards (RA 5487; RA 11917, 2022; PNP-SOSIA).

Traveller guidance

Transport safety tips for visitors to Manila

Do not use jeepneys or public buses. This is not general travel caution. Armed hold-ups on these vehicles are documented by the FCDO and have resulted in fatalities (FCDO travel advisory, April 2026). The MRT and LRT are overcrowded and present pickpocketing risk, but the threat level is lower than road-based public transport. For any ground movement, pre-arranged secure vehicles are the minimum standard.

At NAIA airport, ignore anyone approaching you in the arrivals area offering taxi services. Unlicensed taxis at Manila's airports are a documented robbery vector. Use only pre-arranged transfers from your security provider or hotel. If absolutely necessary, use only the official metered taxi queue inside the terminal, not drivers soliciting outside.

EDSA is unavoidable but manageable with the right timing and route intelligence. Peak congestion runs roughly 7:00 to 9:30 and 17:00 to 20:00, though "off-peak" in Manila is relative. The Skyway system provides toll-based alternatives for some routes. Allow significantly more time than the distance suggests. A meeting 8 kilometres away can take over an hour to reach during heavy traffic.

During typhoon season, check weather forecasts before any journey. Roads flood rapidly in Manila, and drainage infrastructure is inadequate for major storms. If a typhoon warning is active, postpone non-essential travel. If you must move, your security driver will have contingency routes and real-time information on which roads are passable. Do not attempt to drive through floodwater.
