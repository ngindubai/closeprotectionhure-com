Vetting is the product

Anyone can set up a website and list security firms. The value is in knowing which ones are actually qualified, licensed, insured, and capable. That is what we do.

Why this exists

A corporate traveller lands in Lagos and needs close protection. They search online and find dozens of security companies. Some have professional websites. Some are just a WhatsApp number on a Facebook page. A few are competent operations with trained personnel. Others are three guys with a car and a firearm they may or may not be licensed to carry.

The traveller has no way to tell the difference. Not from a website. Not from a quick phone call. And getting it wrong is not an inconvenience. It is a genuine safety risk.

We exist to solve that specific problem. We vet security operators in high-risk cities to a documented standard, then match those operators to clients who need them. Every operator in our network has been through our three-tier verification process before they are put in front of a client.

The three tiers

We don't call everyone "vetted" and leave it there. Our vetting has three distinct levels, and we tell clients which tier an operator holds.

Bronze: listed

Identity and compliance

The minimum bar. Every operator passes this before they enter our system.

  • Registered business entity (verified against national registry)
  • Valid security licence for each country claimed (licence number checked against issuing authority)
  • Professional liability insurance with current certificate
  • Employer's liability insurance where staff are employed
  • Director/principal identity verified with government ID
  • Company address verified (not just a PO box)

Bronze operators are in our database but are not matched to clients. They must complete Silver to work with us.

Silver: verified

Background and competence

The default. Every operator matched to a client holds Silver or above.

  • Criminal background check on all directors and principals (DBS in the UK, FBI/state check in the US, equivalent elsewhere)
  • No convictions for violent crime, fraud, theft, or sexual offences
  • Minimum 3 professional references, at least 1 from a corporate client, all contacted and verified
  • Close protection or EP training from a recognised institution (certificates verified against issuer)
  • Current first aid certification
  • For armed services: firearms training certification and valid licence
  • For security drivers: advanced/defensive driving qualification
  • Video interview with our vetting team (scored against standard rubric)

Background checks are refreshed every 2 years. Insurance and licensing are tracked for expiry.

Gold: approved

Operational excellence

The highest tier. Proven through on-ground assessment and client track record.

  • Everything in Silver
  • Operational assessment completed in their operating environment (advance planning, route selection, comms, emergency response observed
  • Minimum 3 completed client engagements through our platform, reviewed
  • Client satisfaction above minimum threshold
  • No incidents or red flags recorded
  • Featured operator status on relevant city pages

Gold status is reviewed annually. Any incident triggers an immediate review regardless of schedule.

What vetting does not cover

We are honest about the limits. Vetting reduces risk. It does not eliminate it. Here is what we check, and what remains outside our control.

We verify:

  • Operator identity and legal standing
  • Licensing validity at the time of check
  • Insurance coverage at the time of check
  • Criminal history (to the extent background checks reveal it in each jurisdiction)
  • Training and qualifications (verified against issuing institution)
  • Professional references (contacted, not just listed)
  • Operational capability (Gold tier only, assessed on-ground)

We cannot guarantee:

  • Future behaviour of any individual
  • Conditions on the ground during your specific trip
  • That background check systems in every jurisdiction are complete or current
  • Operator performance in scenarios they have not previously encountered
  • Outcomes in any security deployment

Any provider who guarantees safety is either misleading you or not thinking carefully about what they are promising. Security reduces the probability of harm. It does not make it zero.

How we're different from a directory

Directories list. We vet, then match. The difference matters when you're in a city where the wrong operator is not just unhelpful but actively dangerous.

A directory

Lists anyone who signs up. No verification beyond maybe an email address. Buyer does all the due diligence. If the operator is unlicensed, uninsured, or incompetent, that is the buyer's problem.

What we do

Verify identity, licensing, insurance, and criminal history before an operator enters our system. Interview them. Check their references. For Gold operators, assess them on the ground. Match them to your specific requirements. Remain accountable for the introduction.
