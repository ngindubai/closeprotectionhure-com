Toulouse holds a unique position in the European event calendar: it is simultaneously a world-class aerospace industrial hub and a university city with an active public political culture. This combination means that event security planning here must address both the controlled, credential-heavy environment of aerospace sector gatherings and the somewhat less predictable conditions created by periodic civil unrest and the Vigipirate Urgence Attentat national terrorism posture. Both dimensions are built into every security plan we deliver for Toulouse events. For the general risk environment and city context, see our Toulouse city page.

The aerospace and space sectors dominate the Toulouse corporate event calendar. Airbus delivery events, investor days, and supplier conferences take place throughout the year, many of them on or adjacent to the Airbus campus at Blagnac. These events carry specific security requirements that do not apply to general commercial conferences: embargo management for pre-delivery ceremonies, clearance verification for certain facility areas, and close coordination with Airbus’s corporate security function. Our teams are experienced in operating within these constraints and include the client’s corporate security protocols in the 72-hour event brief as a standard element. For executives and senior delegates requiring individual close protection during Toulouse engagements, our Toulouse close protection officers include operatives with aerospace and defence sector experience who understand the specific discretion requirements of that environment.

The MEETT convention complex at Aussonne, adjacent to Toulouse-Blagnac Airport, has established itself as the venue for large-format events that previously could not have been hosted in Toulouse. Its airport adjacency reduces delegate transfer time and simplifies the vehicle logistics that are otherwise the most complex element of multi-day conference security. Our operations team pre-positions vehicles at the MEETT dedicated drop zone and coordinates directly with the airport’s business aviation handling agent for principal-level arrivals. For events in the city centre at Pierre Baudis, the Allee Jean Jaures frontage requires a perimeter plan calibrated to the Vigipirate Urgence Attentat posture: bag screening at all entry points, coordinated radio communications with venue staff, and an immediate-response protocol aligned with the Police Nationale station closest to the venue.

Planning

What our event security covers

Venue Security at Pierre Baudis and MEETT

Centre de Congres Pierre Baudis, Toulouse's principal conference centre on Allee Jean Jaures, has a modern layout designed for large international delegations. Our teams manage all credentialled access points, coordinate with the venue's own security staff on CCTV coverage and fire evacuation procedures, and brief on the Vigipirate Urgence Attentat security posture that applies nationally in France. The MEETT (Toulouse Exhibition and Convention Centre at Aussonne) opened in 2021 adjacent to Toulouse-Blagnac Airport and serves as the venue for large trade shows and sector conferences. Its proximity to the airport provides direct transfer convenience for intercontinental delegates; our vehicle assets are pre-positioned at the MEETT drop zone to reduce airside-to-event transition time. Cite de l'Espace, the space science venue used for corporate events and industry receptions, requires advance liaison with site management on access conditions.

Aerospace, Defence and Space Sector Event Security

Toulouse is Europe's primary aerospace production city and the global headquarters of Airbus. Its event calendar is dominated by aerospace delivery events, investor days, defence contractor meetings, and space industry gatherings involving CNES and Thales Alenia Space. Security planning for these events has features not found in general conference security. Airbus delivery events at the Airbus Delivery Centre bring VIP airline customers, government ministers, and media in controlled numbers; our teams manage credentialled access in coordination with Airbus's corporate security function. Defence and space events involving MBDA, ATR, or CNES facilities may require additional security clearance coordination: we advise clients on this requirement at the planning stage and include clearance verification in the 72-hour brief. Embargo management for pre-delivery ceremonies and commercial announcements requires media accreditation to be separated from client and government guest lanes from arrival.

Delegate Movement and Airport Transfers

Toulouse-Blagnac Airport (TLS) is approximately 20 minutes from the city centre and adjacent to the MEETT exhibition complex and the Airbus campus. This geographic concentration means that for many aerospace events, delegates move between arrivals, venue, and Airbus facilities within a very small geographic area, reducing transfer risk. We pre-position vehicles at the dedicated business aviation terminal at Blagnac for principal-level arrivals and coordinate with the airport's VIP handling agent in advance. For delegates arriving by rail (Toulouse-Matabiau is the city's TGV station, connected to Paris Montparnasse and Lyon), platform-level escorts are provided. The French national rail network has been affected by strike action in past years (pension reform periods in 2023); our operations brief includes a contingency plan for each delegate transfer if rail services are disrupted.

Close Protection for Keynote Speakers and VIP Delegates

Toulouse events regularly host Airbus chief executives, EU space agency officials, defence ministry representatives, and senior figures from international aviation authorities. Close protection assignments are structured around the principal's specific exposure: for public-facing keynotes at large conferences, a two-CPO team provides advance and close cover; for industrial site visits or private briefings, a single experienced CPO with advance reconnaissance is appropriate. All CPOs carry contact cards for CHU de Toulouse (Purpan campus, the main regional trauma and emergency centre, +33 5 61 77 22 33), Police Nationale emergency line (17), and the British Embassy Paris (+33 1 44 51 31 00). Shift handovers are documented and briefed to the principal's personal assistant where requested. The Vigipirate Urgence Attentat posture (France's highest national terrorism alert level) is briefed to all principals as part of the pre-arrival pack.

Crowd Management and Perimeter Security

Toulouse's city centre has been a focal point for French civil unrest, particularly during periods of national industrial action (pension reform protests in 2023 drew significant numbers to Place du Capitole and the surrounding streets). Our pre-event intelligence brief includes current Prefet de la Haute-Garonne public order notifications and any announced demonstrations in the event period. For events at Pierre Baudis, our perimeter plan covers the Allee Jean Jaures approach and coordinates with venue management on the building's access control integration. For events at MEETT or the Airbus facilities, the perimeter is significantly easier to manage due to controlled industrial or purpose-built campus environments. Vigipirate measures at all venues require bag checks at entry and coordination with any police presence assigned under the national security framework.

Security Briefings and Pre-Event Intelligence

Security briefings are delivered 72 hours before first delegate arrival. The brief covers FCDO France travel advisory status (current: advise normal precautions; FCDO 2024 highlights pickpocketing at Place du Capitole and metro stations, civil unrest risk, and the national Vigipirate Urgence Attentat terrorism alert level), current SGDSN terrorism threat assessment, Prefet de la Haute-Garonne public order notifications, and any planned demonstrations. For aerospace and defence events, the brief also covers any specific client-directed clearance protocols. Primary medical facility: CHU de Toulouse Purpan (+33 5 61 77 22 33). Emergency contacts for all team members: 112 (all services), Police 17, SAMU 15, Pompiers 18, British Embassy Paris +33 1 44 51 31 00, US Embassy Paris +33 1 43 12 22 22. Intelligence updates continue daily throughout the event.

France requires security firms to hold authorisation under Loi 83-629 (modified by subsequent legislation) and be registered with the CNAPS (Conseil National des Activites Privees de Securite). Individual security operatives must hold a professional card issued by CNAPS. EU nationals with equivalent qualifications may register with CNAPS; non-EU nationals must work through a CNAPS-licensed French firm. The CNAPS regional delegation covering Toulouse is the Occitanie delegation.
