What our event security covers

Palexpo and WTO Conference Centre Security

Geneva's principal conference venues include Palexpo (the large-scale exhibition and convention centre adjacent to Geneva International Airport), the World Trade Organisation (WTO) Ministerial Conference facilities, the United Nations European headquarters (Palais des Nations), and the International Conference Centre Geneva (CICG). Each venue presents distinct security infrastructure and access control requirements. Palexpo hosts major international trade exhibitions and conventions accessible to pre-registered delegates. The Palais des Nations applies UN security protocols for events at the UN complex. Pre-event security assessment evaluates the applicable venue protocols and identifies supplementary measures for private corporate and HNWI event elements.

Cantonal Licensing and FEDPOL Framework

Private security in Geneva is regulated at cantonal level under the Geneva cantonal law on private security companies (LPSP). Security companies operating in Geneva must hold a Geneva cantonal licence, and individual security personnel must be registered with the Cantonal Police (gendarmerie cantonale). At federal level, FEDPOL (Federal Police) oversees the national security sector including protection of international organisations on Swiss territory. For events involving foreign heads of state or senior UN officials, the Federal Department of Foreign Affairs (DFAE) and FEDPOL protocol divisions are engaged in coordinating private security with state-provided protection.

Espionage and Intelligence Environment Awareness

Geneva is one of Europe's most active intelligence environments. The concentration of UN agencies, the WHO, ICRC, WTO, UNHCR, and hundreds of NGOs creates a target environment for intelligence operations from multiple states around every major multilateral process. FCDO notes this environment in its Switzerland travel advice. Pre-event counter-intelligence briefing for events at which sensitive negotiations or commercially significant information will be discussed covers: device hygiene in Geneva meeting rooms, awareness of social engineering approaches from intelligence contacts active in the Geneva international organisation environment, and communications security for sensitive discussions.

HNWI and Private Banking Event Close Protection

Geneva's private banking and wealth management sector generates a significant calendar of HNWI client events: family office investment conferences, private bank client days, and ultra-high-net-worth charitable and cultural events. Close protection for HNWI principals at Geneva events is calibrated to the discreet professional standard appropriate to the Swiss financial environment, where visible security is often less appropriate than in more elevated-risk markets. CPO deployment for Geneva HNWI events prioritises discretion, vehicle-based cover, and pre-advance at hotel and event venues.

Geneva International Airport (GVA) Delegate Transfers

Geneva International Airport (GVA) is directly adjacent to Palexpo, making it one of the most convenient airport-to-venue transfer combinations in Europe. For other central Geneva venues including the Palais des Nations, the Four Seasons Hotel des Bergues, and the Beau-Rivage Hotel, the GVA-to-city transfer takes 15 to 25 minutes under normal conditions. Pre-arranged vetted transport covers GVA collection through to event venue. For UN-side events, vehicle access protocols are coordinated with the relevant UN security office.

Access Control for Geneva Events

Multi-tier access management applies to Geneva conferences proportionate to the event's profile. For events at UN premises, access is managed under UN security protocols, with supplementary private security covering non-UN areas of the event. For private corporate and HNWI events at Geneva hotels, the access control plan is designed to be professional and discreet, consistent with the expectations of the Swiss financial and international organisation environment. Device security protocols can be applied for events at which sensitive commercial or diplomatic discussions occur.
