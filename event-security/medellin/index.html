Medellin Event Landscape

Medellin has undergone a substantial urban transformation since the early 1990s and is now one of Latin America's most active cities for international conferences and investment forums. The city hosted the UN-Habitat World Urban Forum in 2014, and that profile has continued with a growing technology and innovation conference scene, Antioquia-Caldas-Cundinamarca (ACI) investment zone forums, the annual Colombiamoda fashion and textile trade event, and a range of fintech and startup gatherings that attract delegations from Europe, North America and the wider Andean region. Plaza Mayor Convention and Exhibition Centre is the primary large-scale venue, capable of hosting several thousand delegates. International hotel groups including the InterContinental and the Dann Carlton provide conference-scale accommodation with established security infrastructure. The delegate profile across these events is typically corporate and professional, but the city's evolving security environment means that risk assessments must be event-specific rather than based on general impressions of urban improvement.

SVSP Licensing and Regulatory Framework

Private security in Colombia is regulated at the national level by the Superintendencia de Vigilancia y Seguridad Privada (SVSP), an agency of the Ministry of Defence. All private security firms operating in Colombia, including those providing event security and close protection, must hold a valid SVSP operating licence. Individual security personnel must be registered with the SVSP and carry their identification credentials on duty. Colombia's national regulatory framework is more developed than many Latin American comparators: the SVSP conducts inspections, applies sanctions for unlicensed activity and maintains a public register of authorised firms. International clients should verify SVSP registration before contracting any provider. The Colombian National Police (Policia Nacional) maintains a liaison function for large events and can provide uniformed augmentation where the security plan requires it, subject to advance notification and coordination. For events at Plaza Mayor, the venue has its own licensed security contract that forms the outer layer of the access control structure.

FARC Dissident and Gang Security Risk

The FCDO Colombia travel advisory highlights that while Medellin has undergone significant security improvements, FARC dissident groups (principally the AGC and ex-FARC factions) remain active in Antioquia department, and urban gang networks continue to operate in communas on the city's periphery. For international delegates attending events in the city centre and established conference hotel zones, the direct risk from these groups is low. However, extortion threats targeting businesses, and the risk of being caught in confrontations between rival gangs or between gangs and security forces, remain relevant. Delegates should be briefed to avoid peripheral neighbourhoods without a security escort and to exercise caution in tourist areas that have recorded targeted robberies, including cable car routes and the Poblado district after dark. Event security teams should conduct an advance assessment of the specific venues on the programme, including route reconnaissance between the hotel and the event venue, and should maintain intelligence monitoring of any significant security incidents in the city during the event period.

Venue Access Control

Plaza Mayor Convention and Exhibition Centre has a structured security perimeter with vehicle exclusion zones, bag and equipment screening at public entrances, and a credentialling function for conference access. Event security teams should supplement the venue's baseline with a dedicated delegate credentialling process verified against a pre-registered attendee list, separate from the general public access routes. VIP and keynote speakers should use a back-of-house entry route that does not intersect with general delegate and exhibitor flow. Media and technical contractor access should be managed on a separate verification track with event-specific passes. Venue staff should receive a security briefing before each event day, covering the escalation procedure in the event of a threat or suspicious item. Communications between the access control team and the event security command must run on a dedicated channel independent of the venue's general radio network. For hotel-based conference events, the same layered credentialling approach applies from the hotel vehicle entrance.

MDE Airport Delegate Transfer Management

Jose Maria Cordova International Airport (MDE) is located approximately 35 kilometres east of central Medellin in Rionegro municipality. The road connection between the airport and the city passes through semi-rural terrain and the Oriente region, where gang and ex-FARC activity has been recorded on approach roads. Transfer vehicles should be pre-positioned inside the terminal arrivals zone to minimise kerb-side exposure. The transfer route to central Medellin or the Poblado hotel zone typically takes 40 to 60 minutes depending on traffic. Route variation is advisable for multi-day events with repeat delegate transfers. All vehicles should maintain a live communications link to the event-security command. Delegates should be briefed not to use unverified taxi services at the airport: Medellin has a documented risk of express kidnapping through illegal taxi operations. Enrique Olaya Herrera Airport (EOH) near the city centre handles some domestic connections and is used for some charter operations; delegate collection from EOH follows the same inside-terminal protocol.

Urban Security Transformation and Practical Context

Medellin's transition from one of the world's most dangerous cities in the early 1990s to a city that successfully hosted the World Urban Forum is a frequently cited example of urban regeneration. However, international delegates should not interpret this transformation as meaning the city presents low risk. The FCDO Colombia advisory maintains an overall elevated risk rating for the country and notes that urban gang activity, petty crime and the ongoing influence of illegal armed groups remain features of daily life across Colombian cities. For international event delegates, the practical risk profile is manageable with appropriate security measures. The greatest vulnerabilities are the airport transfer, visible wealth display in public areas, unsupported movement in non-hotel zones after dark and the use of unauthorised ground transport. A well-structured event security plan covering these specific risk vectors allows professionally managed events to proceed with confidence across Medellin's established conference venues.
