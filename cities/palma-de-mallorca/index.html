Threat Intelligence

Threat Profile

Petty Theft and Distraction Crime

The FCDO Spain travel advice (2024) records organised distraction-theft teams operating around airports, car rental desks and busy tourist concentrations. In Palma this is most relevant around the cruise terminal on arrival days, the cathedral and Born tourist core, and the Mesa y Lopez shopping district, where high footfall creates opportunities for bag-snatching and pickpocketing.

Drink Spiking and Nightlife Risk

The FCDO Spain travel advice (2024) warns of drink spiking, including GHB, in bars and clubs, and notes that measures poured in Spanish venues are often stronger than UK equivalents. This is a genuine consideration for principals and family members attending evening functions or nightlife venues during a Palma visit.

Anti-Tourism Demonstrations

Mallorca has seen recurring, well-documented protests under the 'Menys Turisme, Mes Vida' banner since 2024, driven by housing costs and overtourism pressure, with further demonstrations reported in Palma in 2025 and 2026. The FCDO (2024) advises avoiding all demonstrations, which are generally peaceful but can cause temporary road closures and disruption to city-centre movement.

Balearic Alcohol and Balcony Regulation

The Balearic regional government restricts happy hours, all-you-can-drink offers, alcohol vending machines and late-night off-licence sales in named resort zones, and the FCDO (2024) separately documents fatal and serious falls linked to balcony misuse ('balconing'), particularly among British nationals. Both are relevant to any duty-of-care briefing for younger principals or family groups.

Compliance

Security Regulations

Key regulatory requirements for operating security services in Palma de Mallorca.

Firearms Policy

Armed private security is tightly restricted under Spanish law. The large majority of close protection deployments in Palma are unarmed; armed work requires specific Ministry of Interior authorisation and is uncommon for commercial clients.

Licensing

Spain's Ley de Seguridad Privada 5/2014 governs the sector. Security companies register with the Secretaria de Estado de Seguridad (SES), and individual close protection officers hold a Tarjeta de Identidad Profesional (TIP) issued by the Direccion General de la Policia (DGP).

Foreign Operators

EU-licensed officers may operate under EU freedom of movement provisions, subject to recognition requirements. Non-EU firms typically partner with an SES-registered Spanish provider for any deployment on the island.

Local Intel

Zone Intelligence

Lower-Risk Areas

  • Portixol: Former fishing village turned seafront district with a lit promenade and a regular police presence, suitable for extended stays away from the tourist core.
  • Son Vida: Gated hillside residential enclave with private security and controlled access, the established address for HNWI and executive residents.

Elevated-Risk Areas

  • Playa de Palma and s'Arenal: High-density nightlife strip spanning the Palma and Llucmajor municipal boundary; elevated theft and disorder risk after dark.
  • Cathedral and Born tourist core, and the cruise terminal on arrival days: Dense crowds create sustained opportunities for pickpocketing and distraction theft.
