Hannover’s international profile rests on one event more than any other: Hannover Messe, organised by Deutsche Messe AG and widely described within industrial and manufacturing circles as the world’s largest industrial trade fair, drawing more than 4,000 exhibiting companies from over 70 countries in a typical year. Continental AG’s decision to open its new global headquarters campus, Continental-Plaza, in the city in December 2023 reinforced Hannover’s standing as a genuine corporate destination rather than simply an exhibition venue that empties out between shows. CeBIT, which ran here from 1986 until its discontinuation in 2018 after 33 years, is a reminder that Hannover’s fair-city identity predates the current exhibition calendar by decades.

The security environment is what corporate travellers would expect of a mid-sized German city: low ambient crime, effective policing, and the same national-level terrorism awareness that FCDO guidance applies across Germany rather than to Hannover specifically. The one area worth genuine attention is the Hauptbahnhof, which Bundespolizei-sourced reporting placed among Germany’s higher-incident stations in 2025 figures, a finding that a joint police and city security initiative running through September 2025 was credited with meaningfully improving, cutting violent crime there by more than a fifth. That combination, a documented issue and a documented response, is a useful pattern to be aware of rather than a reason for alarm.

Wolfsburg, roughly 90km away and reachable by direct ICE train in about 30 minutes, adds a further practical dimension for executives whose Hannover visit connects to Volkswagen Group business. Fair-week hotel and transfer scarcity is the most consistent operational headache for anyone visiting during Hannover Messe itself, and it is worth planning around well ahead of arrival rather than assuming last-minute availability.

Threat Intelligence

Threat Profile

Industrial Trade Fair Logistics

Hannover Messe, organised by Deutsche Messe AG, is widely described within the industrial and manufacturing sector as the world's largest industrial trade fair, with more than 4,000 exhibiting companies from over 70 countries in a typical edition. Fair week brings the same pattern seen at other major German exhibition cities: hotel occupancy at or near capacity, transfer and taxi scarcity, and access congestion around the Hannover exhibition grounds, one of the largest in the world.

Transport Hub Petty Crime

Bundespolizei data reported by German media identified Hannover Hauptbahnhof among the country's higher-incident stations, with 1,606 recorded offences logged January to May 2025. A joint police and city security cooperation, running October 2024 to September 2025, was credited by Polizeidirektion Hannover with cutting violent crime at the station by more than 20 percent over that period. This is a genuine, named improvement, but it confirms the station remains a location warranting standard vigilance for visitors transiting on foot rather than by vetted transport.

Terrorism Awareness (National)

FCDO Germany travel advice states that terrorist attacks are very likely to be attempted nationwide, citing both Islamist-extremist and far-right threat actors, with enhanced security at transport hubs and major public events. This is a Germany-wide assessment rather than intelligence specific to Hannover, and it applies most directly to large gatherings such as Hannover Messe's opening events.

Nightlife District Activity

Hannover's Nordstadt district, close to the university and a concentration of bars and late-night venues, records a meaningfully higher volume of reported offences than the city average, according to secondary reporting on regional police data, though this is predominantly nightlife-adjacent disorder rather than crime targeting business travellers. Corporate accommodation away from the immediate Nordstadt nightlife strip is the straightforward mitigation.

Security Regulations

Key regulatory requirements for operating security services in Hannover.

Firearms Policy

Firearms for private security in Germany fall under the Waffengesetz. Armed private close protection is not routinely available; the Waffenschein is granted only in narrow circumstances. Unarmed protection with Lower Saxony Police liaison is the standard model for corporate deployments in Hannover.

Licensing

Private security in Germany is governed by the Bewachungsverordnung (BewachV) and Section 34a of the Gewerbeordnung. All close protection operatives must hold current IHK Sachkundeprufung certification, mandatory nationwide since January 2021.

Foreign Operators

EU nationals may work in Germany under free movement provisions subject to Bewachungsverordnung compliance and IHK certification. Non-EU operators require German work authorisation and full regulatory compliance before providing commercial security services.
