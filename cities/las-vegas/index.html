Las Vegas occupies a paradoxical position in the executive protection landscape. It is one of the world’s most intensively secured entertainment environments, with major resorts deploying hundreds of in-house security personnel and Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department (LVMPD) maintaining one of the largest dedicated resort-corridor presences in North American law enforcement. Yet the same characteristics that make Las Vegas a global destination for major corporate conventions, HNWI entertainment, and high-stakes events also concentrate risk in specific ways: large crowds, significant cash flows, high-profile gatherings, and a transient visitor population that creates sustained opportunity for opportunistic and targeted crime.

The corporate travel market for Las Vegas is substantial. The Las Vegas Convention Centre (LVCC) and the Venetian Expo collectively host some of the world’s largest technology and media conventions, including CES (Consumer Electronics Show, approximately 130,000 attendees annually), the NAB Show, and major financial sector gatherings. AWS re:Invent, held each December, regularly attracts over 60,000 technology executives. During these periods, the concentration of C-suite executives, technology leaders, and institutional investors creates a specific demand for executive protection that goes beyond the resort’s internal security capability.

The 1 October 2017 mass shooting at the Route 91 Harvest festival was the deadliest such incident in modern US history and permanently altered the security posture for large outdoor events in Las Vegas and across the United States. LVMPD, the Clark County Sheriff’s Office, and major venue operators subsequently invested significantly in security technology, crowd monitoring, and response protocols. Corporate event planners and principal security teams visiting Las Vegas should familiarise themselves with the venue-specific security measures at each location on their itinerary and ensure that extraction contingencies are documented before any large-venue attendance.

Climate is an underappreciated security factor in Las Vegas. Summer temperatures regularly exceed 40 degrees Celsius, and heat-related medical emergencies are documented throughout the year. Principals and their teams arriving from the UK and northern Europe are not always acclimatised to desert heat conditions. Secure, climate-controlled transport is not merely a comfort provision during June to September; it is a basic risk-reduction measure. All vehicle arrangements should confirm air conditioning capability, and principals should not be left waiting outdoors for transport.

Threat Intelligence

Threat Profile

Large-Venue and Mass-Gathering Risk

The 1 October 2017 Route 91 Harvest festival mass-shooting, which resulted in 60 fatalities and over 400 injuries, permanently altered the security posture for large outdoor events in Las Vegas. LVMPD and Clark County event security requirements were substantially revised following the incident. Corporate event planners and principals attending large conventions, concerts, or sporting events at Las Vegas venues should incorporate venue security assessments and crowd-management contingency planning into their pre-event preparation.

Street Crime and Robbery

Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department (LVMPD) crime data shows higher rates of robbery and larceny than the US national average in and around the Strip corridor. Off-Strip areas, particularly around Fremont Street and the immediate surroundings of Downtown Las Vegas, have elevated incident rates. Opportunistic crime increases during major convention periods (CES, NAB, AWS re:Invent) when visitor volumes are at their highest.

Drug-Related Crime

Clark County has significant drug-related crime concentrated in the Downtown and Fremont Street areas. Fentanyl-adulterated narcotics have driven fatality rates. For corporate executives, the risk is primarily one of being present in areas where drug-related incidents are more likely. Hotels and venues on the mid-Strip are generally separated from the highest-concentration areas.

Extreme Heat

Summer temperatures in Las Vegas regularly exceed 40 degrees Celsius. Heat-related medical emergencies are documented across the convention calendar, particularly during CES (January is mild, but other major summer events are not). Secure transport with climate control is a practical necessity during summer months. Principals and their teams should maintain hydration protocols and avoid prolonged outdoor exposure between June and September.

Compliance

Security Regulations

Key regulatory requirements for operating security services in Las Vegas.

Firearms Policy

Nevada permits concealed carry with a valid permit. Private security officers providing armed executive protection in Nevada must hold a Pilb (Private Investigators Licensing Board) licence with a firearm endorsement under NRS 648. UK nationals cannot carry firearms in the US without complying with US federal and Nevada state law; this is a significant legal barrier for visiting UK close protection officers wishing to operate armed.

Licensing

Private security in Nevada is regulated under the Nevada Security Guard Act (NRS 648). The Nevada Private Investigators Licensing Board (Pilb) licenses security guard companies and individual officers. All commercially engaged security personnel must be Pilb-licensed. Corporate security managers not providing commercial services operate under different rules.

Foreign Operators

UK close protection officers travelling with a principal may do so in a personal capacity for non-commercial protection. Providing commercial security services in Nevada requires Pilb licensing. UK nationals wishing to deploy armed must comply with federal Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF) requirements and Nevada Pilb firearm endorsement requirements, which are generally not accessible on short visits.

Local Intel

Zone Intelligence

Lower-Risk Areas

  • Mid-Strip resort corridor (MGM Grand to Palazzo): Dense hotel security, high LVMPD presence. Lowest street crime risk on the Strip for principals moving between major properties.
  • Summerlin and Henderson: Master-planned residential communities with low violent crime rates. Common executive and HNWI accommodation base during extended stays.
  • Las Vegas Convention Centre (LVCC) and adjacent convention district: Well-secured during major events with coordinated private and LVMPD security presence.

Elevated-Risk Areas

  • Fremont Street and Downtown Las Vegas: Higher concentrations of street crime, drug activity, and disorder. Not recommended for unaccompanied executive movement, particularly at night.
  • Off-Strip residential areas east and south of the airport: Limited police visibility and higher robbery rates. Avoid as transit routes for principal vehicles.
