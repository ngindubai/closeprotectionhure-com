Kyiv is the capital of Ukraine, a country of 44 million people engaged in a full-scale war with Russia since February 2022. This page exists for those with essential requirements that make Kyiv travel unavoidable: humanitarian organisations, media, diplomatic personnel, reconstruction professionals, and family circumstances.

FCDO advises against all travel to Ukraine.

Why this page exists

War correspondents, NGO workers, reconstruction contractors, diaspora with family obligations, and commercial interests that cannot be suspended all require professional risk assessment. The information here is not a recommendation to travel. It is support for those who have decided that travel is necessary and need to minimise risk in conditions that have no peacetime equivalent.

The strike picture

Russia has conducted drone and missile strikes on Kyiv continuously since 2022. Attacks use a range of systems: Iskander ballistic missiles, Kh-101 cruise missiles, and Shahed-136 kamikaze drone swarms. Ukraine’s air defence capability has improved and intercepts significant proportions of incoming attacks. Some strikes reach targets in Kyiv. Energy infrastructure has been the primary systematic target. Residential and commercial areas have also been struck.

For visitors, this means the air raid alert system is the critical daily interface. Sheltering immediately on alert, knowing the nearest designated shelter, and selecting accommodation with bunker access are not optional precautions.

The power situation

Ukraine’s energy grid has been severely degraded by sustained strikes. Power outages in Kyiv are planned (rolling blackouts) and unplanned (emergency after strikes). Planned outages typically run 8-16 hours per day. Essential operations require generator backup. Pre-booking generator-equipped accommodation is a basic logistics requirement.

Our approach

We do not normalise Kyiv as a standard business destination. We assist those with genuine essential requirements by providing the most current available picture, practical protocols, and, where deployment is feasible, operators with active Ukraine experience.

Threat Intelligence

Threat Profile

Active Military Conflict

Ukraine is in a state of full-scale war with Russia. Russian drone and missile attacks on Kyiv occur regularly, targeting infrastructure (power stations, water treatment, energy grid). FCDO advises against all travel.

Missile and Drone Strikes

Kyiv has been struck by ballistic missiles, cruise missiles, and Shahed drone swarms since February 2022. Attacks are unpredictable in timing and have struck civilian areas, hotels, and commercial buildings.

Critical Infrastructure Disruption

Ukraine's power grid has been systematically targeted. Power outages of 8-16 hours or more are common. Water supply and heating infrastructure have also been struck. Generator capacity is a planning requirement.

Mobilisation Risk

Male Ukrainian nationals are subject to military mobilisation. Foreign males may be approached at checkpoints. Understanding the legal position for foreign nationals at Ukrainian checkpoints is essential advance planning.

Emergency Response Limitations

Emergency services operate in wartime conditions. Medical resources are prioritised for military casualties. Trauma care is available but general medical capacity is reduced. Evacuation from Ukraine if seriously injured is genuinely complex.

Security Regulations

Key regulatory requirements for operating security services in Kyiv.

Firearms Policy

Ukraine's wartime conditions have significantly altered the civilian firearms environment. Armed security is available. The legal framework governing private security operations is functioning but adapted to wartime conditions.

Licensing

Ukrainian licensing framework for private security exists but the wartime environment means operational decisions take precedence over administrative norms. Operators with pre-war Kyiv experience and current operating presence are the only viable option.

Foreign Operators

Foreign security operators work through established Ukrainian firms. Wartime conditions require current ground knowledge that external operators cannot replicate without sustained in-country presence.

Local Intel

Zone Intelligence

Lower-Risk Areas

  • Pechersk: Government and diplomatic quarter with highest security infrastructure concentration
  • Podil: Commercial and residential area. Relatively lower strike frequency in 2024.
  • Established international hotel zone: Properties with bunkers and strike response protocols

Elevated-Risk Areas

  • Areas within 1km of energy infrastructure: Transformer stations and power facilities have been targeted repeatedly
  • Eastern and northeastern Kyiv outskirts: Closer to conflict front and historically higher strike frequency
  • All of Ukraine outside Kyiv: FCDO advises against all travel. Do not travel beyond Kyiv without specialist assessment.

Important Warnings

  • FCDO advises against all travel to Ukraine. This is not precautionary language. Russia is conducting regular missile and drone strikes on Kyiv.
  • Know your nearest shelter before moving anywhere in Kyiv. Air raid alerts require immediate shelter in a designated building. Do not ignore sirens.

Ukrainian airspace is closed to civil aviation. The standard route is to fly to a neighbouring hub, most commonly Warsaw or Rzeszow in Poland, and then travel overland by rail or road into Ukraine. The Kyiv rail link is the most reliable option and operates with notable resilience despite the conflict. Border crossing times are variable and the overland leg requires its own route and timing assessment. The return journey must be planned with the same rigour as arrival, including contingency for rail disruption after strikes on infrastructure.
