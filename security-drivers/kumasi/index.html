Kumasi sits at the centre of Ghana’s gold-mining industry, with the surrounding Ashanti Region hosting some of West Africa’s most productive extraction operations. Executives visiting from London, Toronto, or Johannesburg require professional ground transport that is calibrated to the specific demands of both urban Kumasi and the rural mine-site corridors that extend south towards Obuasi.

The road environment in and around Kumasi presents moderate risks. Armed robbery on roads outside the city centre, variable road conditions on mine-site access routes, and the unpredictability introduced by illegal mining activity in the region create a context where local knowledge and security-driving training make a material difference to journey safety.

Our Kumasi security drivers hold PNDCL 320 licences and are experienced in both the urban commercial district and the mine-site corridor. They maintain daily route intelligence, coordinate with mine-site security teams for access clearance, and use 4WD vehicles suited to the varied road conditions of the region.

For executives arriving via Accra Kotoka International Airport, we provide end-to-end cover for the Accra-Kumasi highway journey as well as all in-city movements.

What this covers

Operational detail for Kumasi

Licensing and Legal Framework

Security drivers in Ghana must hold licences under the Private Security Organisation Act 1993 (PNDCL 320), administered by the Ministry of Interior. Drivers providing protective transport must complete approved training. Our Kumasi partners are fully PNDCL 320-licensed and operate within the statutory framework.

Threat Environment

Kumasi presents a moderate threat environment for vehicle movements. Armed robbery on roads in and around the city, petty crime in market areas, and road security risks on the Kumasi-Obuasi mining corridor are active concerns. Galamsey (illegal small-scale mining) activity in the wider region can create localised tensions and road obstructions. A trained security driver with local knowledge materially reduces exposure to these risks.

Key Routes and Operational Areas

Core transport corridors include Kumasi Airport (KMS), the Kejetia Market and Ring Road commercial district, the Kumasi-Obuasi mining corridor, the University of Science and Technology (KNUST) research campus, and the principal hotels (Golden Tulip Kumasi City, Novatel Kumasi). Movements to Accra via the Accra-Kumasi highway are regularly covered.

Mining Sector Transport

The Kumasi-Obuasi corridor is the primary route for executives visiting gold-mining operations in the Ashanti Region. All movements on this corridor are conducted with a trained security driver using pre-assessed routing and timing. Current intelligence on road conditions and any reported incidents is reviewed before each departure. Coordination with on-site security teams at mine facilities is standard.

Vehicle Standards

We provide Toyota Land Cruiser or equivalent 4WD SUVs for Kumasi operations. These vehicles are suited to variable road conditions on mine-site access roads, carry first-aid equipment, and have satellite tracking as standard. For movements along the Kumasi-Obuasi corridor, hardened door-panel vehicles are available on request.

Accra-Kumasi Highway Cover

The Accra-Kumasi highway is one of Ghana's busiest national roads and connects the country's two largest cities. It carries both commercial and private traffic and is subject to road-safety incidents and occasional armed robbery. We provide security-driver cover for the full Accra-Kumasi route, with timing planned to avoid overnight travel and known high-risk segments.
