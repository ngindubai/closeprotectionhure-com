Juba is the capital of South Sudan, one of the world’s youngest and most challenging operating environments for security transport. The country’s oil sector draws corporate visitors, while the UN, NGO, and diplomatic community is large given South Sudan’s ongoing stabilisation requirements. The US State Department rates South Sudan at Level 3 (Reconsider Travel) as of 2026, and FCDO advises a high degree of caution throughout the country. Juba International Airport (JUB) is within the city limits, but even the short transfer requires vetted, pre-arranged transport given the high risk environment.

What this covers

Operational detail for Juba

Juba International Airport Transfer

Juba International Airport (JUB) is located within the city limits, making the transfer into the central districts relatively short. However, the high risk environment in South Sudan means that even a short airport transfer requires a pre-arranged, vetted driver holding appropriate authorisation. Inside-terminal collection is the standard protocol; kerbside at Juba airport carries robbery risk for informal transport users. The driver coordinates with an operations controller throughout the city transfer via GPS.

Route Intelligence: Juba

Juba's operating zone for corporate principals centres on the Hai Malakal and Juba Town areas where international organisations, diplomatic missions, and commercial operations are based. The city has experienced episodes of ethnic violence and armed clashes, including significant urban fighting in 2016. Road surfaces in Juba can be poor, particularly after the rainy season (April to October). Movement outside the established international organisation corridors requires specific assessment and enhanced protocols. FCDO advises a high degree of caution throughout South Sudan. Source: FCDO South Sudan travel advice (2026).

Vehicle Selection

Juba engagements use high-specification, unmarked 4WD SUVs suited to the road environment and the risk profile. Road conditions, particularly after rain, make a capable off-road vehicle a practical as well as security requirement. Armoured vehicles are available for principals assessed at elevated risk, including those attending sensitive negotiations or visiting locations outside the established international corridors. All vehicles have confirmed communications and GPS tracking.

South Sudan Driver Authorisation

South Sudan's private security regulatory framework is at an early stage of development compared with more established security markets. Security driver companies operating in Juba should hold current business authorisation from the relevant South Sudan authority and carry documentation confirming the individual driver's vetting and employment record. Due diligence on provider credentials is particularly important in Juba given the less developed regulatory environment.

Night Movement Protocols

Night movement in Juba carries substantially elevated risk compared with daytime movement. FCDO South Sudan travel advice (2026) and the US State Department Level 3 advisory (2026) both note that the after-dark environment in Juba increases exposure to armed robbery, carjacking, and other crime. Night movement protocol applies from 20:00: movements restricted to confirmed routes between pre-approved locations, locked doors, windows up, GPS tracking throughout. Movement after 22:00 is avoided unless operationally essential.

Oil, NGO, and Diplomatic Account Cover

Oil sector visitors connected with South Sudan's petroleum sector (Unity and Higleg oil fields), UN agency and NGO personnel, and diplomatic mission staff represent the primary security driver client profile in Juba. Standing account arrangements for recurring visitors include named drivers familiar with Juba conditions, updated route plans against current FCDO advisories and operator intelligence, and a single operations controller contact.
