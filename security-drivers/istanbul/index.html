Istanbul’s ground transport security picture combines a documented terrorism threat, a unique two-continent geography that creates routing constraints, and an intelligence environment that affects principals in specific sectors. An OGG-certified security driver with Istanbul operational knowledge addresses all three in the normal course of professional driving rather than as exceptional measures.

The ground transport context in Istanbul

The FCDO terrorism threat rating for Turkey is high. Previous Istanbul attacks have targeted crowded public spaces and transport hubs. For ground movement planning, this translates to route awareness around known high-target-density areas at high-risk times, rather than route-avoidance that makes the itinerary unworkable. Istanbul is a functioning major business city; security driving here is about calibrated awareness, not paralysis.

The Bosphorus geography is the single most distinctive operational planning element in Istanbul. Principals who cross regularly, particularly those moving between IST on the European side and meetings or accommodation on the Asian side, create predictable patterns at the crossing points. Route and timing variation is the standard mitigation.

The intelligence environment in Istanbul affects executives in defence, energy, and technology. Counter-surveillance awareness in ground movement, OGG-certified drivers who know what out-of-pattern following looks like, and clear contact protocols if surveillance is suspected add operational value for these client profiles.

OGG compliance

Turkey’s Law 5188 requires OGG certification for commercial security driving. Operating companies must also hold Ministry of Interior registration. The verification step is to ask for the OGG certificate number of the specific driver assigned and the operating company’s Ministry registration number.

Operational detail for Istanbul

OGG-Certified Drivers

All drivers we engage in Turkey hold a current Ozel Guvenlik Gorevlisi (OGG) certificate issued by the Ministry of Interior under Law 5188. OGG certification is the legal requirement for commercial security driving in Turkey.

Istanbul Airport (IST) and Sabiha Gokcen (SAW) Transfers

Pre-arranged collection at IST (European side) or SAW (Asian side). IST operates with upgraded security infrastructure following the 2016 Ataturk Airport attack. The E80 motorway from IST into central Istanbul has specific peak-hour and incident considerations.

Bosphorus Crossing Planning

Istanbul's two-continent geography means that cross-Bosphorus movement via the Bosphorus Bridge (Bogazici Koprusu) or Fatih Sultan Mehmet Bridge creates predictable timing patterns. Route variation across the crossing is part of the standard planning for principals who cross regularly.

Levent, Maslak, Nisantasi, Besiktas Coverage

Operational familiarity with Istanbul's primary corporate and accommodation areas on the European side, and Kadikoy and Atasehir on the Asian side. Separate route intelligence for both sides of the Bosphorus.

Counter-Surveillance Awareness

Istanbul is a regional intelligence hub. For executives in defence, energy, and technology sectors, OGG-certified drivers are briefed on basic counter-surveillance awareness: route variation, following-vehicle detection, and contact protocols if surveillance is suspected.

Bilingual Drivers

All drivers working with international clients in Istanbul are English/Turkish bilingual. Drivers working with clients from specific regions (German, Russian, Arabic) are available through specialist providers with advance notice.
