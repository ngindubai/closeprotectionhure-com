Pretoria has no meaningful international airport, so every security driver assignment here starts at OR Tambo International Airport, the same arrival point used for Johannesburg. From there, the choice is a road transfer of roughly 50 to 60 minutes or the Gautrain rail link, which requires a transfer at Marlboro station before reaching Hatfield in Pretoria, a total journey of around an hour to an hour and a quarter. Neither option is a default; the right one depends on traffic conditions, the principal’s schedule, and whether continuous vehicle security matters more than platform-to-platform speed.

Once in the city, drivers work two very different environments. Brooklyn and Waterkloof, the diplomatic quarter that hosts most of Pretoria’s foreign embassies, offer straightforward, well-policed routing. The Union Buildings and the surrounding government precinct add a layer that Johannesburg’s financial district doesn’t: FCDO’s 2026 advice notes that protests in South Africa can escalate quickly, and route planning near administrative buildings accounts for that possibility as a matter of course, not an exception.

None of this changes the underlying provincial risk. Gauteng, the province containing both Pretoria and Johannesburg, accounts for more than half the country’s carjackings according to SAPS Q3 2025/26 statistics, so closed-window, locked-door discipline at every intersection applies here exactly as it does across the rest of the province. PSIRA registration under the Private Security Industry Regulation Act is the baseline licensing check for any driver working the route.

Operational detail for Pretoria

South African Licensing Requirements

Security drivers operating in Pretoria hold current registration with the Private Security Industry Regulatory Authority (PSIRA), mandatory nationwide under the Private Security Industry Regulation Act. Drivers taking on protection-linked work beyond standard chauffeuring generally hold additional close protection and anti-hijack driving qualifications, and this applies across Gauteng, not just in Pretoria itself.

Route Planning: Airport, Gautrain, and Road

Pretoria has no significant international airport. OR Tambo International Airport (JNB), serving greater Johannesburg, is the arrival point for every visit. Drivers offer two practical routes onward: a road transfer of roughly 50 to 60 minutes depending on traffic, or the Gautrain rail link, which runs from OR Tambo via a transfer at Marlboro station to Hatfield in Pretoria, a journey of around an hour to an hour and a quarter. Road transfer keeps the principal under continuous vehicle security; the Gautrain option suits time-pressed itineraries when platform and station security are pre-checked.

Fleet and Vehicle Standards

Executive saloons and, where the threat assessment warrants it, armoured B4 vehicles are standard for the OR Tambo to Pretoria corridor and for movement within the city's government and diplomatic precincts. Central locking discipline and closed-window policy at intersections apply as a baseline across Gauteng, given the carjacking pattern the province records.

Driver Training and Regional Knowledge

Drivers working Pretoria train on anti-hijack technique consistent with Gauteng's carjacking risk, and separately on the city's own geography: the Brooklyn and Waterkloof diplomatic quarter, the Union Buildings and government precinct, and Hatfield around the University of Pretoria. Awareness of scheduled protest activity near administrative buildings, flagged in FCDO's 2026 South Africa advice, feeds into daily route selection.

Airport Transfers

Drivers meet principals airside at OR Tambo and manage the onward leg to Pretoria directly, rather than handing off at a station. For principals prioritising speed over vehicle continuity, a driver can also meet the Gautrain at Hatfield station for the final leg into the city, keeping ground security in place from the platform onward.

Emergency Protocols

South Africa's emergency numbers are 10111 for police, 10177 for ambulance, and 112 free from any mobile phone. Steve Biko Academic Hospital in Capital Park is the reference tertiary facility for the city, and Netcare Pretoria East Hospital in Moreletapark serves as a private-sector alternative. Drivers carry load shedding schedules for the City of Tshwane, since scheduled outages affect traffic signal operation and require adjusted intersection driving practice.
