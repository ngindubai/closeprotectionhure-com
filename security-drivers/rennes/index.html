Operational detail for Rennes

Licensing and Vehicle Standards

Security drivers in Rennes operate under France's private security framework, principally Loi 83-629 as amended and the authority of the Conseil National des Activites Privees de Securite (CNAPS). The employing company must hold a CNAPS authorisation for private security activity in metropolitan France. Individual drivers performing a combined driver-security role must hold a carte professionnelle issued by CNAPS. The hire vehicle function requires a licence de transport de personnes (VTC licence) from the Prefecture d'Ille-et-Vilaine or the DREAL (Direction Regionale de l'Environnement, de l'Amenagement et du Logement) Bretagne. Security drivers are unarmed under the standard French private security framework. Principals should confirm CNAPS company authorisation and individual carte professionnelle before engagement.

Rennes is the capital of Brittany and a growing technology and agri-food industry hub in western France, situated approximately 350km west of Paris. The RNS airport and Rennes Gare (TGV station, 1h25 from Paris Montparnasse) are the two main arrival points. The N137 connects Rennes south to Nantes (1h10); the N12 and N164 provide the main Brittany peninsula corridors. France's national security alert system (Vigipirate), cited in FCDO France travel advice (2024), maintains an elevated terrorism threat level; security drivers apply event-location and public transport awareness accordingly. Demonstrations, noted in FCDO advice, have affected Rennes city centre periodically and are incorporated into route planning for principal movements near administrative buildings.

Many executives visiting Rennes from outside France arrive at Paris Charles de Gaulle (CDG) and travel onward by TGV; the 1h25 TGV journey from Paris Montparnasse to Rennes Gare is a standard executive arrival route.
