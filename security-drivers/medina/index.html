Medina is the second holiest city in Islam and a place of significant and accelerating infrastructure investment, driven by Saudi Vision 2030 and the expansion of pilgrim capacity around the Prophet’s Mosque. The international professional population in Medina is concentrated in the infrastructure and construction sector and in pilgrimage logistics companies that coordinate Hajj and Umrah travel operations. Security driver services in Medina are structured around these client categories, with a central operational constraint that does not exist in other Saudi cities: the Haram exclusion zone around the Prophet’s Mosque is closed to non-Muslims, and all route planning must account for this boundary from the outset.

Prince Mohammad bin Abdulaziz International Airport (MED), 12km from central Medina, is the primary arrival point. What this covers

Operational detail for Medina

Licensing and Vehicle Standards

Private security in Saudi Arabia operates under Saudi Council of Ministers Resolution No. 58. Only Saudi Arabian licensed security companies may operate; foreign personnel function as employees or consultants of licensed local firms. Security drivers must hold appropriate Saudi credentials. All vehicles are inspected before deployment. A critical operational restriction in Medina is that the Haram area around the Prophet's Mosque is closed to non-Muslims; security drivers and close protection personnel who are non-Muslim cannot enter the Haram zone and itinerary planning must account for this restriction from the outset.

Route Planning and Threat Assessment

Prince Mohammad bin Abdulaziz International Airport (MED) is 12km northwest of central Medina. The city presents a low-moderate risk profile for the categories of international professional most likely to require security driver services: infrastructure and construction sector expats, pilgrimage logistics company personnel, and corporate visitors to the development projects surrounding the Haramain High Speed Railway network. The FCDO Saudi Arabia travel advice (2024) maintains a terrorism risk for the Kingdom generally. Route planning accounts for the Haram exclusion zone and the significant crowd movement around the mosque area during pilgrimage seasons.

Fleet and Vehicle Security

Standard Medina deployments use high-specification saloon or SUV vehicles. During the Hajj and Umrah pilgrimage periods, vehicle and road access in Medina is significantly restricted and advance planning is essential. All vehicles carry operations-controller GPS tracking. Pilgrimage season deployments require coordination with Saudi authorities on permitted approach routes into and out of the city.

Driver Training and Certification

Security drivers covering Medina hold current Saudi credentials and are familiar with the city's Haram exclusion zone boundaries, the major hotel approaches (Anwar Al Madinah Movenpick, Marriott Madinah, Hilton Madinah), the Haramain Railway station, and the construction sector site access roads south and east of the city. Pilgrimage season crowd management awareness is part of the local driver competency for Medina deployments.

Airport Transfer Services

Prince Mohammad bin Abdulaziz International Airport (MED) is 12km northwest of central Medina. Transfer cover includes inside-terminal collection, route-planned transfer to the principal hotel or project site, and operations-controller tracking throughout. During Hajj season, airport transfer capacity is under severe pressure from the volume of pilgrimage traffic; principals travelling during this period should plan with substantially extended transfer time assumptions.

Emergency Protocols

All Medina security driver operations run against the Saudi emergency baseline: 999 for police, 911 for general emergency, 920000999 for ambulance. Consular contacts: British Embassy Riyadh +966 11 488 0077; US Embassy Riyadh +966 11 488 3800. An operations controller maintains continuous GPS tracking and check-in communications throughout every transfer.
