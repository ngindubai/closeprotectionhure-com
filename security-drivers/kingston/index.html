Kingston presents one of the Caribbean’s more demanding transport security environments. The Jamaica Constabulary Force (JCF) recorded substantial levels of gang-related violence across the capital in 2023, concentrated in inner-city communities but extending to transit routes used by international visitors, particularly those travelling to and from Norman Manley International Airport. The US State Department classifies Jamaica at Level 3 (Reconsider Travel) as of 2026, and the FCDO (2026) advises against complacency even within the New Kingston business district. Airport transfers represent the highest-risk movement for arriving executives, as the kerbside at KIN is a known observation point for criminal networks targeting international arrivals.

A PSRA-licensed security driver addresses these risks systematically. The driver is vetted under the Private Security Regulation Authority Act 2009, trained in defensive driving and route variation, and briefed on current JCF advisory zones before each movement. Vehicles are prepared at a secure compound and brought to the terminal only when the client is confirmed ready, eliminating unnecessary kerbside wait time. Routes are selected daily against current intelligence rather than fixed in advance, avoiding gang-active communities along Marcus Garvey Drive in favour of the Washington Boulevard corridor to New Kingston. Night movements follow a heightened protocol with no unscheduled stops and continuous communication with the coordinating contact. For corporate clients making repeat visits to Kingston, a standing driver arrangement provides itinerary continuity and pre-briefed route familiarity.

Operational detail for Kingston

Norman Manley International to City Transfer Protocol

Norman Manley International Airport (KIN) sits approximately 20km from New Kingston. The kerbside at KIN is the highest-risk window: unregistered taxi touts actively target arriving passengers, and the FCDO (2026) notes that gang networks observe international arrivals. A pre-arranged, PSRA-licensed driver meets clients inside the terminal, not at kerbside, to close that exposure window before the client reaches the public concourse. Transfers are timed to avoid peak gang-activity hours wherever the flight schedule permits.

Route Intelligence: Kingston

The preferred transfer route from KIN to New Kingston follows Washington Boulevard, avoiding the inner-city communities along Marcus Garvey Drive where gang activity is concentrated (JCF statistics, 2023). The Portmore Causeway offers an alternative southern approach but extends journey time. Route decisions are reviewed daily against JCF incident data. Clients in Liguanea or Half Way Tree benefit from a slightly different approach via Constant Spring Road. No route is treated as permanently safe; real-time intelligence drives each movement plan.

Vehicle Selection and Preparation

For standard corporate travel in Kingston, a high-specification saloon or SUV with tinted windows is the baseline. The vehicle is swept and prepared at a secure compound before client collection. Communications equipment includes a secondary handset pre-loaded with local emergency contacts (JCF 119) and the client's itinerary contact. For principals assessed at elevated risk, soft-skin B4-level vehicles are available through vetted Kingston partners. Vehicle plates and livery are kept low-profile throughout every movement.

Jamaica Driver Licensing Requirements

Security drivers in Jamaica must hold a valid PSRA licence under the Private Security Regulation Authority Act 2009. The PSRA requires background vetting, a clean criminal record, and periodic re-registration. An unregistered taxi driver has no PSRA licence, no security training, and no accountability to a regulated body. When selecting a provider, request PSRA licence numbers for both the driver and the company and verify them against the PSRA register before travel.

Night Movement Protocols

The FCDO (2026) advises that night risk in Kingston is substantially elevated across the city, including in New Kingston and the broader business district. Security drivers apply a strict after-dark protocol: no unnecessary stops, route deviations only when a confirmed threat is ahead, locked doors, windows up, and constant communication with the coordinating contact. Clients are strongly advised to schedule all evening restaurant and hotel transfers with the security driver rather than using local taxis.

Corporate Account and Recurring Cover

Organisations with regular Jamaica operations benefit from a standing driver arrangement: a named, vetted driver allocated to the account, pre-briefed on executive itineraries and preferences, and available for airport transfers, inter-city runs to Montego Bay, and meeting-to-meeting movement. Account billing is monthly with per-movement logging for travel security reporting. Recurring clients receive a pre-trip intelligence brief covering current JCF advisory zones before each visit.
