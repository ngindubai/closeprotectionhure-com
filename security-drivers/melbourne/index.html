Operational detail for Melbourne

Victoria Private Security Act 2004 and VSIRA Licensing

Private security in Victoria is governed by the Private Security Act 2004 (Vic) and administered by the Victorian Security Industry Regulatory Authority (VSIRA). Individual security personnel must hold a VSIRA licence in the appropriate class: the relevant categories for security driver roles include the Bodyguard licence class and the Security Guard licence class. VSIRA licence applications require a national police record check through the Australian Criminal Intelligence Commission (ACIC), identity verification, completion of a Certificate III in Security Operations from a Registered Training Organisation (RTO), and a fitness to hold a licence assessment. Security driver companies in Melbourne must hold a VSIRA Proprietary Security Organisation licence or a licensed operator arrangement. Clients should confirm the operating company's VSIRA licence and the individual driver's VSIRA licence class before engagement.

Australian National PROBABLE Terrorism Threat

Australia's national terrorism threat level is PROBABLE, meaning an attack is likely, as assessed by the Australian Security Intelligence Organisation (ASIO) and the National Terrorism Threat Advisory System (NTTАС). Victoria has a documented terrorism history that directly affects Melbourne security driver planning: the 2017 Bourke Street Mall vehicle attack (6 killed) was a deliberate vehicle ramming attack in the Melbourne CBD, and ASIO has assessed multiple foiled plots in Victoria. Melbourne CBD's pedestrianised high streets, crowded event venues, and major tourist precincts are the primary risk locations. Security drivers maintain terrorism awareness and have contingency protocols for crowd-evacuation and secure-extraction scenarios at primary principal venues.

Melbourne Airport (Tullamarine) Transfer Operations

Melbourne Airport (MEL), commonly known as Tullamarine, is approximately 22 kilometres northwest of the CBD, accessed via the Tullamarine Freeway (M2). Transfer times typically range from 30 to 50 minutes outside peak hours; peak-hour periods, particularly afternoon (16:00-18:30) and Friday evenings, can extend transfer times to 60 to 75 minutes. Melbourne Airport has four terminals (T1 International, T2, T3, T4 domestic); the majority of corporate and HNWI international arrivals use Terminal 1. Security driver collections from Terminal 1 use the ground-level arrivals pickup area; collection credentials and principal identification are confirmed before collection. The terminal has a dedicated VIP premium lounge service through the Qantas and Virgin Australia lounges used by many senior corporate arrivals.

Melbourne CBD and Principal District Coverage

Melbourne security driver operations cover the principal business and cultural districts: the CBD around Collins Street, Bourke Street, and Exhibition Street (the law firm, financial institution, and corporate headquarters corridor); Southbank for hotel-based engagements at the Crown Towers and InterContinental Melbourne The Rialto; Carlton and Fitzroy for creative sector and university-adjacent corporate meetings; South Yarra, Toorak, and Brighton for HNWI residential principals; and the Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre (MCEC) at South Wharf for major conference transfer logistics. The Grand Hyatt Melbourne, Adelphi Hotel, and Park Hyatt Melbourne are principal venues for senior corporate arrivals. Event period planning covers the Australian Open (January), Melbourne Grand Prix (March), and AFL Finals (September) as the primary demand peaks.

Melbourne Event Periods and Major Venue Coverage

Melbourne hosts major international events that create elevated security driver demand: the Australian Open (Melbourne Park, January), Melbourne Formula 1 Grand Prix (Albert Park, March), and AFL Finals (MCG, September) are the three highest-demand periods. Each event concentrates corporate hospitality, HNWI clients, and high-profile principals in specific Melbourne venues and corridors. The Australian Open's Melbourne Park precinct and the Grand Prix Albert Park venue both require security drivers with current Melbourne road and access credential knowledge. Security drivers plan departure timing around event-day road closures and venue-adjacent congestion; pre-planned alternative routes are confirmed for every event period engagement.
