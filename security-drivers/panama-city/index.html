Pre-arrange Your Panama City Ground Transport

Request Consultation

Panama City’s dual identity as Central America’s primary financial centre and the gateway to one of the world’s most important maritime corridors brings a consistent flow of senior executives, legal professionals and government principals through the city. The ground transport environment reflects the medium risk picture: carjacking and armed robbery are documented on certain corridors, civil unrest is a periodic operational reality, and the Darien province border represents a categorically different risk environment that standard security driving does not cover.

Operating corridors

The primary lower-risk zones for corporate ground transport in Panama City are Punta Pacifica, Punta Paitilla, Miraflores and San Francisco. These areas host the majority of international hotels, banking institutions and senior executive residences. Casco Viejo is manageable during daylight with appropriate route awareness, but requires additional planning after dark. Ground transport planning avoids corridors with higher carjacking exposure and does not use vehicles with a visible hire-car profile.

PTY transfers

Tocumen International Airport (PTY) is the region’s primary hub and arrivals periods carry the standard exposure associated with unverified transport. Pre-arranged collection includes the driver’s name and vehicle registration provided to the principal before departure, confirmed terminal identification, and counter-surveillance during the transition from arrivals to the vehicle. Routing to Punta Pacifica or the principal’s first appointment is confirmed before departure from the terminal.

Civil unrest preparedness

The 2022 and 2023 civil unrest that saw the Pan-American Highway blocked for extended periods remains an active planning reference. Ground transport operations in Panama maintain a current civil unrest calendar, with alternative routing prepared for all principal movements during known flashpoint periods. The lesson from those events is that itinerary flexibility is a structural requirement, not an improvised response.

What this covers

Operational detail for Panama City

DGSP-Licensed Drivers

Panama's Directorate of Private Security Services (DGSP), operating under the Ministry of Government, licenses all private security operators. Our Panama City security drivers hold current DGSP authorisation through locally licensed companies, providing clients with a clear legal compliance baseline in one of Central America's more developed regulatory environments.

PTY Airport Secure Transfers

Tocumen International Airport (PTY) is one of the region's busiest transit hubs. Pre-arranged collection at PTY includes named driver and vehicle registration provided in advance, terminal identification, counter-surveillance during the arrivals transition and confirmed routing to Punta Pacifica, Punta Paitilla or the principal's first appointment.

Carjacking Pattern Avoidance

Armed robbery and carjacking are documented risks across parts of Panama City. Ground transport planning identifies known high-risk corridors and time periods, selects vehicles without a visible hire-car profile, and avoids unnecessary stops in lower-lit areas after dark.

Finance and Canal Zone Routing

Panama City is Central America's primary financial centre. Our drivers are familiar with the operating environments of the banking district, canal administration facilities, and the Colon Free Zone corridor. Canal zone access and port authority coordination are handled as standard for relevant assignments.

Civil Unrest and Protest Route Management

The 2022 and 2023 civil unrest, which saw Pan-American Highway blockades disrupt ground transport across Panama, demonstrated that alternative routing is not optional for professional operations. Our teams carry the current civil unrest calendar and maintain alternative routes for all principal movements during known risk periods.

Darien Approach Warning

FCDO advises against all travel to Darien province. Ground transport to the border region, including movements along the Pan-American Highway towards the Colombian border, requires specialist assessment. Standard security driving protocols do not cover Darien approach routes without prior threat review.
