Yaounde, as Cameroon’s capital and political centre, presents a security environment shaped by the country’s broader instability. The US State Department rates Cameroon at Level 3 (Reconsider Travel) as of 2026, driven in part by the ongoing Anglophone crisis in the North-West and South-West regions, the threat from Boko Haram in the Far North, and general crime risk across the country. FCDO Cameroon (2026) advises against all travel to these three regions. Yaounde itself is not directly affected by these regional conflicts, but the overall advisory level reflects a national environment that warrants structured security planning for corporate visitors, including vetted transport arrangements from Nsimalen International Airport.

Nsimalen International to City Transfer Protocol

Nsimalen International Airport (NSI) is located approximately 25km south of Yaounde. The transfer into the capital along the Nsimalen highway is the primary exposure window for arriving executives. Inside-terminal collection by a MINAT-authorised security driver is standard: kerbside at Nsimalen carries robbery risk targeting international arrivals, particularly after dark. The driver confirms the transfer route is clear and coordinates with the operations controller via GPS throughout the 25km approach into the city. Transfer timing avoids late-night movement wherever the flight schedule permits.

Route Intelligence: Yaounde

Yaounde is Cameroon's capital and political centre. The central districts around the Plateau and the main diplomatic and commercial zones are the lower-risk operating areas for corporate principals. Peripheral areas and outskirts carry elevated opportunistic crime risk, particularly after dark (FCDO Cameroon, 2026). MINAT-authorised security drivers maintain current knowledge of local incident patterns and route their movements via the established commercial arterials. Route planning is also informed by awareness that FCDO advises against all travel to the North-West, South-West, and Far North regions of Cameroon: whilst these areas do not directly affect Yaounde, movement planning for visits that combine Yaounde with regional travel requires specific assessment.

Vehicle Selection and Preparation

For standard corporate visits to Yaounde, a high-specification, unmarked executive saloon or SUV is the appropriate vehicle. The risk profile in the capital centres on opportunistic robbery and petty crime rather than organised kidnapping, making profile management and route discipline the primary protective measures. Armoured vehicles are available for principals with elevated risk profiles, including those with regional operational exposure in FCDO-restricted areas. All vehicles are prepared at a secure location before collection, with confirmed communications, fuel, and route plans for every movement.

Cameroon Driver Licensing Requirements

Private security companies in Cameroon operate under the authority of MINAT (Ministere de l'Administration Territoriale). Companies providing security driver services must hold current MINAT authorisation. Individual security personnel must be registered and vetted by MINAT. Verify the company's MINAT authorisation number and the individual driver's registration status before engagement. Due diligence on provider credentials is particularly important in Yaounde, where the formal security sector operates alongside unregulated operators.

Night Movement Protocols

FCDO Cameroon (2026) and US State Department Cameroon Level 3 (2026) both note that robbery and petty crime risk in Yaounde is elevated after dark. Night movement protocol applies from 21:00: all movements are restricted to confirmed routes between pre-approved locations, no unplanned stops, locked doors, windows up, and continuous GPS tracking with the operations controller. Late-night arrivals at Nsimalen International require pre-arranged inside-terminal collection; kerbside transport at night should not be used.

Corporate Account and Recurring Cover

Corporate clients with regular Yaounde operations, particularly those in the energy, mining, development, and diplomatic sectors, benefit from a standing MINAT-authorised driver arrangement. Recurring cover includes a named driver familiar with the Yaounde operational environment, pre-approved route plans updated against current FCDO and US State Department advisories, and account management providing a single operations controller contact. Clients with operations combining Yaounde with regional travel also receive specific movement planning that accounts for the FCDO regional restriction areas.
