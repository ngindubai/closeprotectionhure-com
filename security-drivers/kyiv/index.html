Operational detail for Kyiv

FCDO Against All Travel Advisory

FCDO advises against all travel to Ukraine, including Kyiv. Security driver services in Kyiv are available only for principals and organisations that have made a documented, authorised decision to travel under FCDO's highest advisory level. Any organisation deploying personnel to Kyiv must formally assess and accept the wartime risk at the appropriate level of management authority, ensure comprehensive war-zone insurance, and confirm that the specific business requirement justifies travel given FCDO's current advisory status.

Armoured Vehicle Requirement for Kyiv Operations

Armoured vehicles are the baseline vehicle specification for all principal-level movements in Kyiv given the wartime threat environment. B6-specification vehicles provide protection against the fragmentation and blast overpressure associated with drone and missile attack effects in the environment of a city under active attack. Drivers operating armoured vehicles in Kyiv have current operational experience in the Ukrainian wartime environment, including familiarity with Kyiv's checkpoint network, current air alert protocols, and the shelter identification protocol that is a mandatory element of all vehicle movements.

Air Alert and Shelter-in-Place Vehicle Protocol

Russian drone and missile attacks on Kyiv occur regularly and with limited warning. The Kyiv air alert system provides advance warning for drone attacks; ballistic missiles may arrive with less warning time. The security driver protocol for air alerts during a vehicle movement requires immediate transition to the nearest identified hardened shelter: pre-identified shelter points are established for every route segment before the transfer departs. On air alert activation, the driver moves to the nearest safe stop, and the principal and team move to the shelter. Movement resumes only after the all-clear. All shelter points are verified before each route segment is used.

Borispil and Boryspil Wartime Airport Operations

Boryspil International Airport (KBP) is the principal international gateway to Kyiv, located approximately 35 kilometres south-east of the city centre. Wartime flight operations from KBP are limited and variable: commercial services have been partially restored on some routes, but the operational environment is fundamentally different from peacetime. For principals arriving by any available commercial route, inside-terminal collection at KBP and immediate transfer to armoured vehicle cover applies from the point of arrival. Overland entry from Poland via the Korczowa border crossing is an alternative used by some principals; border crossing waiting times and post-crossing land transfer arrangements are coordinated in advance.

Power Outage Driver Protocol

Ukraine's power grid has been systematically attacked, resulting in scheduled and unscheduled power outages of 8 to 16 hours or more in Kyiv. Security driver vehicles are maintained with sufficient fuel for the planned route regardless of grid status, with additional fuel reserves where itinerary length warrants it. Traffic signals fail during power outages, creating specific intersection management requirements. Vehicle communications equipment includes battery-backed and satellite-capable devices to maintain operations controller contact during extended power outages.

Kyiv Checkpoint and Mobilisation Protocol

Kyiv and the approach roads into the city have an active checkpoint network operated by Ukrainian Security Forces (ZSU) and territorial defence units. Security drivers are briefed on current checkpoint locations on the principal's planned route, carry operator documentation and vehicle registration for checkpoint presentation, and have pre-agreed protocols for checkpoint processing. FCDO Ukraine travel advice notes that foreign male nationals may be approached at Ukrainian checkpoints; the driver protocol includes the appropriate documentation and communication approach to confirm non-Ukrainian nationality.
